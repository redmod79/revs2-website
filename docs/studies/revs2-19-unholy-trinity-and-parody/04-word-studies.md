# Word Studies

## Question
Dragon/Beast/False Prophet as systematic parody of Father/Son/Spirit in Revelation 12-13. Document every verbal parallel. Trace Greek vocabulary links. Does the parody operate at verse, scene, or macro-structural level?

### G4969 — sphazō

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4969-sphazo.md`

**Occurrences:** 9

9 NT occurrences (10 tokens); 8 in Revelation (89%), 1 in 1 John 3:12. Perfect passive participle dominates — 6 of 8 uses in Revelation. Four categories: (1) Lamb's atoning death (Rev 5:6, 5:9, 5:12, 13:8); (2) martyrs slain (Rev 6:9, 18:24); (3) literal killing (1 Jn 3:12, Rev 6:4); (4) beast counterfeit (Rev 13:3). Concentrated almost entirely in Revelation.

**Semantic categories:** atoning death of the Lamb, martyr slaughter, literal killing, beast pseudo-death parody

**Key verses:** Rev 5:6, Rev 13:3, Rev 13:8, 1 Jhn 3:12

**Relevance:** The central lexical datum for the death-resurrection parody. Rev 5:6 ἐσφαγμένον (V-RPP-ASN neuter, arnion G721) vs Rev 13:3 ἐσφαγμένην (V-RPP-ASF feminine, kephalē G2776). Same verb, same perfect-passive aspect, different gender agreeing with modified noun. The parody operates at word level through identical morphological form with only gender changing to match the parodied noun.

### G4352 — proskuneō

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4352-proskyneo.md`

**Occurrences:** 65

54 unique verses (65 tokens) NT; 22 in Revelation (38%). Revelation density 5.4 per 100 verses — highest in NT. Six semantic categories: healing/petition, divine sonship recognition, true worship discourse, exclusive worship command, heavenly throne room, eschatological false worship. Top collocation: therion 8x.

**Semantic categories:** healing/petition, divine sonship recognition, true worship, exclusive worship command, heavenly throne-room worship, eschatological false worship

**Key verses:** Rev 4:10, Rev 5:14, Rev 13:4, Rev 13:8, Rev 13:12, Rev 14:9, Rev 19:10, Rev 22:8-9

**Relevance:** proskuneo structures the entire parody at the macro-structural level. True worship directed to God/Lamb in Rev 4-5, 7, 11, 19, 22 is systematically counter-directed to dragon + beast in Rev 13. The same verb marks both worship scenes, making the contrast explicit. therion collocates 8x — beast worship is a programmatic anti-worship structure.

### G3528 — nikaō

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3528-nikao.md`

**Occurrences:** 28

28 NT; 17 in Revelation (61%); overwhelmingly Johannine. Rev 13:7 and Rev 11:7: beast overcomes (hostile conquest). Rev 2:7, 2:11, 2:26, 3:5, 3:12, 3:21, 21:7: saints overcome (promise). Rev 12:11: saints overcome by blood of Lamb and word of testimony. Collocation: martyria (G3141) 2x.

**Semantic categories:** saints overcoming (promise), beast overcoming saints (temporary conquest), God/Lamb overcoming (ultimate victory)

**Key verses:** Rev 2:7, Rev 12:11, Rev 13:7, Rev 17:14

**Relevance:** nikao inversion: the same verb used for saints overcoming in the letters (2:7, 2:11, 2:26, 3:5, 3:12, 3:21) is used for the beast overcoming saints in 13:7. The beast's nikao is a granted (ἐδόθη) temporary conquest; the saints' nikao is through blood and testimony (12:11). Both uses share vocabulary; the direction and basis are inverted.

### G3664 — homoios

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3664-hómoios.md`

**Occurrences:** 47

47 NT; 22 in Revelation (38% — exceptional density). Four semantic categories: parabolic similitude, ethical equivalence, appearance description in apocalyptic, incomparability formula. Rev 13:4 is the ONLY NT occurrence in an explicit rhetorical incomparability formula. H4310 mi ('who?') maps to G3664 with PMI 2.76 — incomparability formula contexts. 1 Jn 3:2: positive contrast (we shall be like him).

**Semantic categories:** parabolic similitude, ethical equivalence, apocalyptic appearance, incomparability formula (Rev 13:4), eschatological likeness (1 Jn 3:2)

**Key verses:** Rev 13:4, Rev 13:11, Exod 15:11, Psa 35:10, 1 Jhn 3:2

**Relevance:** The incomparability formula 'who is like the beast?' (Rev 13:4) is the single most concentrated parody instance — it applies to a creature the exact rhetorical form reserved for YHWH's absolute uniqueness (mi khamocha, Exod 15:11, Ps 35:10, Ps 89:8). homoios also marks Rev 13:11 (false prophet 'like a lamb' = homoios + arnion dative), confirming the Lamb-parody structure of the second beast.

### G721 — arnion

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G721-arnion.md`

**Occurrences:** 30

30 tokens, 28 unique verses; 27 in Revelation (96%), 1 in John 21:15. Rev 13:11 is the sole exception to the Christological usage — false prophet's horns 'like a lamb' (homoios + arnion dative). Every other occurrence is the singular Arnion as a Christological title. Used exclusively in Rev for the Lamb who was slain and who rules.

**Semantic categories:** Christological title (Revelation), comparison target in false prophet description (Rev 13:11)

**Key verses:** Rev 5:6, Rev 13:11, Rev 14:1, Rev 17:14

**Relevance:** The arnion anomaly in Rev 13:11 is structurally significant: the sole non-Christological use appears in the false prophet description. The second beast is homoios (like) arnion — using dative construction (comparison) rather than predicate. It does not claim to be the Lamb; it presents as resembling a lamb while speaking as a dragon. The anomaly underscores the parody's precision.

### G1504 — eikōn

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1504-eikon.md`

**Occurrences:** 23

23 NT; 10 in Revelation (highest density 2.5/100). Categories: imperial coin, Christ as image of God, humanity bearing image, shadow vs true image, image of the beast. Revelation cluster dominates. Hebrew source: H6755/H6754 tselem (PMI 9.36/8.98 — strongest connection). Collocations: therion 10x, proskuneo 6x, charagma 6x.

**Semantic categories:** imperial coin, Christ as image of God, human image-bearing, shadow vs true image, image of the beast

**Key verses:** Col 1:15, 2 Cor 4:4, Rev 13:14, Rev 13:15, Rev 14:9

**Relevance:** The eikon chain: Col 1:15 (Christ = eikon of God, genitive of origin) → Rev 13:14 (eikona tō thēriō, dative = image made for/dedicated to the beast) → Rev 13:15 (eikoni tou thēriou, genitive = of the beast). The genitive-dative-genitive shift encodes the shift from authentic divine image to idol and back. The false image requires animation (pneuma) because it lacks inherent life; the authentic image (Christ) is the life-giving Word.

### G4151 — pneuma

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4151-pneuma.md`

**Occurrences:** 385

385 NT; 25 in Revelation. Categories include Holy Spirit (most uses), animating power (Rev 13:15), unclean/evil spirits (Rev 16:13), Spirit as seal and guarantee (Eph 1:13, Eph 4:30). Hebrew sources: H7307 ruach (PMI 5.92), H5397 neshamah (PMI 5.26).

**Semantic categories:** Holy Spirit, seven Spirits of God (Rev), animating power, unclean spirits, spirit of prophecy (Rev 19:10)

**Key verses:** Rev 1:4, Rev 5:6, Rev 13:15, Rev 16:13, Rev 19:10, Eph 1:13

**Relevance:** pneuma connects three levels of the parody: (1) Rev 5:6 seven Spirits of God (authentic Spirit bound to the Lamb) — (2) Rev 13:15 false prophet gives pneuma to the image (animated but lifeless counterfeit) — (3) Rev 16:13 three unclean pneumata from all three unholy trinity members. The same noun (G4151) marks both the Holy Spirit's activity and its parody, making the connection explicit.

### G5480 — charagma

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5480-charagma.md`

**Occurrences:** 9

9 NT verses; 8 in Revelation (89%), 1 in Acts 17:29. charagma does not appear in the Septuagint — no LXX Hebrew source. Revelation occurrences: 13:16, 13:17, 14:9, 14:11, 15:2, 16:2, 19:20, 20:4.

**Semantic categories:** beast's imposed allegiance mark

**Key verses:** Rev 13:16, Rev 13:17, Rev 14:9, Rev 20:4

**Relevance:** charagma is the anti-sphragis (G4973). Both appear on the forehead in Revelation, making them direct antitheses. sphragis = divine ownership and protection; charagma = beast allegiance and commerce. charagma's absence from the LXX underscores that it is a new, purpose-built term for the beast's counterfeit ownership system. Related to charakter (G5481, Heb 1:3 — authentic divine imprint) by shared etymology (charassō = to engrave) but opposite relational direction.

### G4973 — sphragis

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4973-sphragis.md`

**Occurrences:** 16

16 NT; 13 in Revelation. Categories: authentication/certification, divine concealment/authorization, sequential judgment unveiling, divine ownership/eschatological protection. Direct antithesis to charagma: 'Both appear on the forehead in Revelation, making them direct antitheses.'

**Semantic categories:** authentication, divine concealment, judgment unveiling, divine ownership and protection

**Key verses:** Rev 7:2-3, Rev 9:4, Rev 14:1, Eph 1:13, Eph 4:30

**Relevance:** sphragis is placed in servants' foreheads (Rev 7:3) as divine protection; charagma is placed in beast-worshippers' foreheads as allegiance marker. The spatial parallel (both in foreheads) makes the anti-structure visible. Spirit as seal (Eph 1:13) vs. false-prophet animating spirit (Rev 13:15) extends the parallel to the mechanism: authentic sealing is by the Spirit; parody sealing by a pneuma-given animation.

### G1238 — diadēma

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1238-diadema.md`

**Occurrences:** 3

3 NT verses, all in Revelation only. Dragon: 7 diadems on 7 heads (12:3). Beast: 10 diadems on 10 horns (13:1). Christ: many (polla) diadems on head (19:12).

**Semantic categories:** royal crown, cosmic authority marker

**Key verses:** Rev 12:3, Rev 13:1, Rev 19:12

**Relevance:** The diadem trajectory is a structural feature of the parody at macro level: Dragon (7) → Beast (10) → Christ (many, unlimited). The escalation of number in the unholy trinity is surpassed by the incalculable number of Christ's diadems. The parody overreaches in quantity (dragon→beast escalation) but is surpassed in quality by the original.

### G3841 — pantokratōr

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3841-pantokrator.md`

**Occurrences:** 10

10 NT verses; 9 in Revelation (1:8, 4:8, 11:17, 15:3, 16:7, 19:6, 21:22 nominative; 16:14, 19:15 genitive), 1 in 2 Cor 6:18. The title is the divine name's complement — Almighty expresses absolute dominion. The beast's incomparability formula in Rev 13:4 ('who is able to make war with him?') implicitly counters the Pantokrator claim.

**Semantic categories:** divine title — all-powerful, heavenly worship address, eschatological judgment authority

**Key verses:** Rev 1:8, Rev 4:8, Rev 11:17, Rev 19:6, Rev 19:15

**Relevance:** pantokrator frames the divine identity that the beast parodies. Rev 1:8 and 4:8 pair pantokrator with the IS/WAS/IS-TO-COME formula; Rev 17:8 pairs the beast with WAS/IS-NOT/WILL-COME. The beast's formula is structurally anti-pantokrator: it claims temporal existence (was) but lacks present being (is not) and faces destruction. Pantokrator = absolute power over all; beast = delegated (divine passive ἐδόθη) temporal power.

### G3141 — martyria

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3141-martyria.md`

**Occurrences:** 37

37 tokens, 33 unique verses; Johannine corpus 79%. Rev occurrences: 1:2, 6:9, 12:11, 19:10 — testimony of Jesus thread. Collocation: nikao appears in 12:11 (they overcame by the word of their testimony).

**Semantic categories:** legal testimony, Christian witness/proclamation, testimony of Jesus

**Key verses:** Rev 1:2, Rev 6:9, Rev 12:11, Rev 12:17, Rev 19:10

**Relevance:** martyria defines the saints who face beast persecution (12:11, 12:17, 20:4). The beast overcomes saints (nikao, 13:7) who hold the testimony of Jesus (martyria). But the saints overcome the beast (nikao, 12:11) by the blood of the Lamb and the word of their testimony (martyria). The same verb + noun pair appears on both sides of the conflict, demonstrating that the parody operates at the vocabulary level even in the saints-vs-beast dynamic.

### G1849 — exousia

**Occurrences:** 103

Authority/power. Key occurrences in the parody: Rev 13:2 ἐξουσίαν μεγάλην — great authority given by dragon to beast (active, ἔδωκεν). Rev 13:7 ἐξουσία — authority over every tribe given by divine passive (ἐδόθη). Rev 12:10: ἡ ἐξουσία τοῦ Χριστοῦ αὐτοῦ — the authority of his Christ proclaimed as the dragon is cast down.

**Semantic categories:** delegated authority, divine authority of Christ, beast's derived authority

**Key verses:** Rev 12:10, Rev 13:2, Rev 13:7, Matt 28:18

**Relevance:** exousia structures the authority transfer at the heart of the parody. Dragon gives beast exousia (13:2 active verb). God further delimits that authority via divine passives (13:5, 13:7). Christ's exousia is proclaimed when the dragon falls (12:10). Matt 28:18: all exousia given to Christ via divine passive ἐδόθη. The parody: beast's exousia is a secondhand imitation of Christ's all-authority.

### G1325 — didōmi

**Occurrences:** 430

Give. The active/passive contrast in Rev 13 is the grammatical backbone of the parody. Active ἔδωκεν (13:2, 13:4): dragon named subject, gives authority to beast. Passive ἐδόθη (13:5×2, 13:7×2, 13:14, 13:15): unnamed agent (divine passive), God's sovereign permission. Same verb appears 8 times in Rev 13 in this contrasting pattern.

**Semantic categories:** active gift by named agent, divine passive permission

**Key verses:** Rev 13:2, Rev 13:4, Rev 13:5, Rev 13:7, Rev 13:14, Rev 13:15

**Relevance:** The active/passive alternation of didōmi is the single most important grammatical datum in the study. Dragon's grant is active (ἔδωκεν) — he presents himself as the sovereign giver. But the beast's operational mandates (mouth, 42 months, war on saints, authority, signs, breath to image) are all given by divine passive — God is the unnamed but sovereign framer of the beast's every activity. The parody's imitation is enclosed within divine sovereignty.

### G12 — abyssos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G12-abyssos.md`

**Occurrences:** 9

9 NT verses; 7 in Revelation. Beast ascends from abyss: Rev 11:7, Rev 17:8. Hebrew source: H8415 tehom (PMI 9.16 — primary). The abyss is the underworld prison, the anti-heavenly realm.

**Semantic categories:** underworld, prison of evil spirits, beast's origin-point

**Key verses:** Rev 11:7, Rev 17:8, Luk 8:31

**Relevance:** The beast's pseudo-resurrection (Rev 13:3, 17:8) involves ascent from the abyss — the opposite of Christ's resurrection from the tomb via divine power. Christ ascends to the Father's right hand; the beast ascends from the abyss. The beast's 'coming' (μέλλει ἀναβαίνειν in Rev 17:8) is an ascent from below rather than a descent from above. Anti-ascension parody.

35 unique verses (39 tokens); 4 in Revelation (all chs. 13 and 16). Rev 13:6: blasphemy against God, his name, tabernacle, and heavenly dwellers. The beast's defining verbal activity within the 42-month mandate.

**Semantic categories:** slander of God/Christ, beast's mandate activity

**Key verses:** Rev 13:1, Rev 13:5, Rev 13:6, Rev 16:9

### G2362 — thronos

**Occurrences:** 61

Throne. Rev 4-5: God's throne (τοῦ θρόνου) is the seat of universal worship and authority. Rev 13:2: dragon gives beast τὸν θρόνον αὐτοῦ (his own throne). Rev 3:21: Christ grants overcomers to sit with him in his throne. The throne vocabulary structures the parody at the scene-level.

**Semantic categories:** divine throne (heavenly), beast's received throne, overcomer's promised throne-sharing

**Key verses:** Rev 4:2, Rev 5:6, Rev 13:2, Rev 3:21

**Relevance:** thronos connects the scene-level parody directly: Rev 4-5 throne room (God's throne, worship, Lamb receives scroll) is structurally paralleled by Rev 13 beast court (beast's throne from dragon, worship demanded, beast receives universal authority). The same noun (G2362) marks both scenes; the worshippers, the recipient, and the source of authority are inverted.

### G5481 — charaktēr

**Occurrences:** 1

1 NT occurrence, Heb 1:3 only. χαρακτήρ = exact representation, the impression that reproduces the original. Christ is the charakter of the Father's hypostasis. Shares etymology with charagma (G5480) via charassō (to engrave/imprint) but opposite relational direction.

**Semantic categories:** authentic divine representation

**Key verses:** Heb 1:3

**Relevance:** charakter (Heb 1:3) vs. charagma (Rev 13:16-17) forms a word-family parody at the etymological level: both derive from charassō (engraving/imprinting), but charakter describes Christ as the exact imprint of the Father's being, while charagma describes the beast's imposed mark on humanity. The direction is reversed: Father → Son (charakter); beast → humanity (charagma). Authentic imprint flows from origin; false imprint is imposed by coercion.

### G1411 — dunamis

**Occurrences:** 120

Power/might. Key occurrences in the parody: Rev 4:11 (God's creation power in doxology); Rev 5:12 (Lamb receives dunamis in sevenfold doxology); Rev 12:10 (Christ's dunamis proclaimed at dragon's fall); Rev 13:2 (dragon gives beast his dunamis — active ἔδωκεν). Also: 2 Thess 2:9 (lawless one comes with all dunamis after Satan's working).

**Semantic categories:** divine creative power, Lamb's received power (doxological), dragon's transferred power (parody), Satan's empowerment of lawless one

**Key verses:** Rev 4:11, Rev 5:12, Rev 12:10, Rev 13:2, 2 Thess 2:9

**Relevance:** dunamis structures the power-transfer parody: the sevenfold doxology gives the Lamb δύναμιν (Rev 5:12) by universal acclamation; the dragon gives the beast τὴν δύναμιν αὐτοῦ (Rev 13:2) by private active transfer. God's creative dunamis (Rev 4:11) is the source of genuine power; the dragon's delegated dunamis (itself bounded by divine sovereignty) is the parody source. Christ's authority proclamation (Rev 12:10) occurs precisely when the dragon's dunamis is terminated.

### G3359 — metopon

**Occurrences:** 8

Forehead. G3359 appears 8× in Revelation: 4× as divine seal location (7:3, 9:4, 14:1, 22:4), 3× as beast mark location (13:16, 14:9, 20:4), 1× as harlot's forehead (17:5). The forehead is the consistent spatial marker for both ownership systems operating in parallel throughout Revelation.

**Semantic categories:** divine seal location, beast mark location, harlot's identification

**Key verses:** Rev 7:3, Rev 9:4, Rev 13:16, Rev 14:1, Rev 14:9, Rev 20:4, Rev 22:4

**Relevance:** metopon is the spatial anchor of the seal/mark anti-structure. Both God's seal and the beast's mark are placed in the forehead — the same anatomical location. This spatial parallel makes the contrast explicit and deliberate: God's seal in forehead = ownership, protection, and identity; beast's mark in forehead = allegiance, commercial access, and condemnation (Rev 14:9-11). Rev 22:4 closes the arc: God's name in the foreheads of his servants — the ultimate counterpart to the beast's charagma.

### G1404 — drakōn

**Occurrences:** 13

Dragon. G1404 appears 13× in Revelation, exclusively. Identified in Rev 12:9 as 'that old serpent, called the Devil and Satan.' The dragon is the first member of the unholy trinity — the counterpart to the Father. He possesses a throne (Rev 2:13), gives his power/throne/authority to the beast (Rev 13:2), and is source of the three unclean spirits (Rev 16:13).

**Semantic categories:** cosmic adversary, first member of the unholy trinity, source of beast's authority, old serpent/devil/satan (Rev 12:9)

**Key verses:** Rev 12:3, Rev 12:9, Rev 13:2, Rev 13:4, Rev 16:13, Rev 20:2, Rev 20:10

**Relevance:** drakōn is the dragon-as-Father in the parody: (1) dragon gives beast dunamis/thronos/exousia (parodies Father giving authority to Son); (2) dragon is worshipped along with beast (Rev 13:4) — parodies Father being worshipped alongside the Lamb; (3) three unclean spirits proceed from dragon + beast + false prophet (parodies the Spirit proceeding from Father and Son). The dragon's defeat (12:7-9) precedes the beast's appearance — the parody rests on an already-fallen base.
