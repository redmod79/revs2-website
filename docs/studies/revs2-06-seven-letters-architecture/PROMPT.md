6. **Genre classification: prophetic edict, covenant treaty, or prophetic oracle?:**
The tade legei (G3004) formula appears in all seven letters and is the key to genre classification. In the OT, 'koh amar YHWH' (thus says the LORD) introduces prophetic oracles (over 400 occurrences). In the LXX, tade legei renders koh amar. Aune argues the form parallels ancient royal/imperial edicts (superscriptio = address, narratio = assessment, dispositio = commands, sanctio = rewards/punishments). Shea maps the five-fold suzerain treaty pattern. The prophetic oracle model connects to the covenant lawsuit (rib) tradition. READ `$WORD_STUDIES/G3004-lego.md` and `$WORD_STUDIES/G1125-grapho.md`. Run `semantic_grammar.py 'tade legei formula' --greek` to check grammar textbooks for the prophetic formula. The research agent should search for OT parallels to the tade legei formula and assess which genre model best accounts for all template elements including the dual closing (ear formula + overcomer promise) that has no exact parallel in edict or treaty structures.
**Verses:** Rev 2:1, Rev 2:8, Rev 2:12, Rev 2:18, Rev 3:1, Rev 3:7, Rev 3:14
