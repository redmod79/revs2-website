# Word Studies

## Question
How does Revelation construct its already/not-yet temporal framework through verb tense, vocabulary, and structural markers — and what does this reveal about the book's eschatological logic?

### G1451 — engys

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1451-engýs.md`

**Occurrences:** 30

Adverb meaning 'near, nigh, at hand' — spatial, temporal, relational, and eschatological range. 30 NT occurrences (John 11×, Matt 3×, Luke 3×, Acts 3×, Eph 2×, Heb 2×, Rom 1×, Phil 1×, Rev 2×). Translation distribution: 'nigh' 40%, 'at hand' 20%, 'near' 13.3%, 'nigh at hand' 13.3%. The two Revelation occurrences form the 'ho kairos engus' inclusio at 1:3 and 22:10. Hebrew source: H7138 (PMI 7.99), H7350 (PMI 5.86). Verbal cognate: G1448 eggizō (process vs. state distinction — eggizō = 'drawing near'; engys = 'is near').

**Semantic categories:** spatial nearness, temporal nearness, relational/covenantal nearness, eschatological imminence, moral nearness

**Key verses:** Rev 1:3, Rev 22:10, Php 4:5, Eph 2:13, Mat 24:32-33

**Relevance:** The two Revelation occurrences create the book's temporal framework inclusio — 'ho kairos engus' at both 1:3 (opening) and 22:10 (closing) frames the entire content as disclosure of imminent events. Contrast with Dan 12:4's 'many days' seal command.

### G3528 — nikao

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3528-nikao.md`

**Occurrences:** 29

Verb meaning 'to overcome, prevail, conquer, be victorious.' 28 NT occurrences — 17 in Revelation (61%). Key tense taxonomy: (1) Aorist indicative self-originated = Christ's completed cross-victory (Rev 3:21 enikesan, 5:5 enikesan; John 16:33 nenikēka perfect). (2) Present participle = believer's ongoing overcoming (Rev 3:21 nikōn; 21:7 nikōn). (3) Aorist infinitive after edothē = beast's permitted/dependent overcoming (13:7 nikēsai). (4) Future indicative = eschatological Lamb victory (17:14 nikēsei).

**Semantic categories:** eschatological victory (believers), cosmic victory (Christ), moral victory, military/hostile conquest (beast), legal/forensic vindication

**Key verses:** Rev 3:21, Rev 5:5, Rev 13:7, Rev 17:14, Rev 21:7, John 16:33

**Relevance:** The nikao tense system encodes the already/not-yet framework directly: Christ's past completed victory grounds the believer's present ongoing overcoming, while the beast's permitted overcoming operates within divine boundaries, and the future eschatological victory remains certain.

### G5281 — hypomone

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5281-hupomone.md`

**Occurrences:** 32

Noun meaning 'patient endurance, steadfast waiting, perseverance.' 32 NT occurrences — 7 in Revelation (highest density at 1.7 per 100 verses). Key Revelation formula: 'Here is the patience/endurance of the saints' at 13:10 and 14:12. Also at 1:9 (John co-partaker), 2:2-3 (Ephesus commended), 2:19 (Thyatira), 3:10 (Philadelphia's 'word of my patience'). Hebrew source: H4723 miqveh (PMI 9.98, hope/expectation vocabulary) and H8615 tiqvah (PMI 8.3).

**Semantic categories:** endurance through tribulation, patient persistence, eschatological waiting, virtue lists, communal testimony

**Key verses:** Rev 1:9, Rev 3:10, Rev 13:10, Rev 14:12, Rev 6:9-11

**Relevance:** Hypomonē is Revelation's structural response to the already/not-yet tension: the saints endure in the present because judgment is certain in the future. Its seven occurrences function as eschatological structural markers — calls to endurance embedded at key points in the tribulation narrative.

### G5550 — chronos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5550-chronos.md`

**Occurrences:** 49

Noun meaning 'time' (measured duration, elapsed time). 49 unique NT verses — 4 in Revelation (2:21, 6:11, 10:6, 20:3). All 4 Revelation occurrences = 'Brief Remaining Period / Space for Action' semantic category. At Rev 10:6: 'chronos ouketi estai' = 'time shall be no longer' — the cessation of the delay interval. Distinction: chronos = measured elapsed duration; kairos (G2540) = appointed/proper moment/season.

**Semantic categories:** measured elapsed time, brief remaining period, space for repentance, eschatological cessation of delay

**Key verses:** Rev 10:6, Rev 6:11, Rev 20:3, Rev 2:21

**Relevance:** Chronos at Rev 10:6 marks the cessation of the delay period — 'time no longer' means the interval between the already and the not-yet has run its course. At Rev 6:11 and 20:3, 'chronos mikron/oligon' marks the bounded waiting period that the not-yet occupies.

### G1096 — ginomai

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1096-ginomai.md`

**Occurrences:** 702

Verb meaning 'to become, to come to be, to happen, to come about.' 631 unique NT verses — 38 in Revelation. Dominant form: egeneto (aorist middle indicative 3sg) = 233 of 668 forms (34.9%). Primary LXX source: H1961 hayah (1,236×). Revelation completion arc: egeneto aorist (11:15, 12:10 — declared) → gegonen perfect singular (16:17 — completed) → gegonan perfect plural (21:6 — all things accomplished).

**Semantic categories:** event occurrence, state transition, prophetic completion, creative/eschatological fulfillment

**Key verses:** Rev 11:15, Rev 12:10, Rev 16:17, Rev 21:6

**Relevance:** Ginomai encodes the temporal arc from announcement (aorist) to completion (perfect singular, then plural). The shift from aorist to perfect in the ginomai arc marks the escalating stages of eschatological completion.

### G4098 — pipto

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4098-pipto.md`

**Occurrences:** 93

Verb meaning 'to fall.' 85 unique NT verses — 20 in Revelation (highest density at 5.0 per 100 verses). Aorist forms = 80% of all forms — punctiliar action verb. Semantic category 3 covers 'Destruction or Ruin of Cities and Powers': Rev 14:8, 18:2 (Babylon epesen doublet), 16:19, 17:10. Hebrew source: naphal H5307 (PMI 30.42, 239 LXX co-occurrences) — overwhelming alignment.

**Semantic categories:** physical falling, worship prostration, destruction of cities/powers, eschatological downfall

**Key verses:** Rev 14:8, Rev 18:2, Rev 16:19

**Relevance:** Pipto is the vehicle for the proleptic aorist Babylon doublet. The epesen epesen at 14:8 and 18:2 mirrors the Hebrew prophetic perfect doublet naphal naphal at Isa 21:9 — the most concentrated proleptic aorist example in Revelation.

### G4972 — sphragizo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4972-sphragizo.md`

**Occurrences:** 28

Verb meaning 'to seal, to set a seal upon.' 28 NT occurrences — 18 in Revelation (72%). Voice/tense distribution: Aorist Active (7), Aorist Middle (2), Aorist Passive (2), Perfect Passive Participle (4). At Rev 22:10: σφραγίσῃς [V-AAS-2S] = prohibitive aorist subjunctive ('seal NOT'). Hebrew source: chatham H2856 (PMI 9.53, 15 LXX co-occurrences). Rev 10:4 = only thing sealed in Revelation (seven thunders) — everything else is unsealed.

**Semantic categories:** physical sealing, authentication/ownership marking, apocalyptic sealing (prophetic concealment), protective sealing, prohibitive unsealing

**Key verses:** Rev 5:1, Rev 10:4, Rev 22:10, Dan 12:4

**Relevance:** The sphragizō arc tracks the temporal framework directly: Dan 12:4 commands sealing for distant fulfillment; Rev 5:1 presents the sealed scroll that only the Lamb can open; Rev 22:10 prohibits sealing because the time is near. The seal vocabulary itself encodes the Daniel-sealed/Revelation-unsealed contrast.

### G2064 — erchomai

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2064-erchomai.md`

**Occurrences:** 664

Verb meaning 'to come, to go.' 604 unique NT verses — 33 in Revelation. Suppletive paradigm: present erchomai, aorist ēlthon, perfect elēlytha. The form ēlthen [V-2AAI-3S] = the proleptic aorist 3S form appearing at Rev 6:17, 11:18, 14:7, 14:15, 18:10, 19:7. Hebrew source: H935 bō' (PMI 20.33, 654 LXX co-occurrences).

**Semantic categories:** physical movement/travel, prophetic arrival, eschatological coming, divine/angelic advent

**Key verses:** Rev 6:17, Rev 11:18, Rev 14:7, Rev 14:15, Rev 18:10, Rev 19:7

**Relevance:** Erchomai provides the ēlthen formula (hōra/orgē/basileia + ēlthen) that marks prophetic arrival announcements. Six Revelation occurrences of the same proleptic aorist announce the arrived state of judgment, wrath, the kingdom, harvest, and the marriage — all using ēlthen [V-2AAI-3S] to declare future events as accomplished.

### G5034 — tachos

**Occurrences:** 8

Noun meaning 'speed, quickness' used in the prepositional phrase 'en tachei' = 'shortly, quickly, soon.' Appears at Rev 1:1 and 22:6 (prologue-epilogue frame). BDAG glosses en tachei as 'soon, in a short time' at Rev 1:1. Scholarly debate: (1) temporal imminence reading (Mounce, BDAG, Farrar, Aland) = 'soon, within a short time'; (2) manner-of-action reading (Walvoord, Thomas) = 'swiftly when it comes.' The temporal reading is the majority BDAG and UBS position.

**Semantic categories:** temporal imminence, swiftness/rapidity of action

**Key verses:** Rev 1:1, Rev 22:6

**Relevance:** En tachei sets the temporal expectation for the entire book at 1:1 and confirms it at 22:6 in the closing frame. The semantic debate directly bears on whether Revelation presents its contents as historically imminent or manner-descriptive.

### G5035 — tachy

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5035-tachy.md`

**Occurrences:** 12

Adverb meaning 'quickly, soon.' 12 NT occurrences — 6 in Revelation (highest density). KJV: 'quickly' (11×), 'lightly' (1×). Revelation uses: (1) Conditional disciplinary at 2:5 and 2:16 (threat to non-repenting churches). (2) Transitional encouragement at 3:11 (Philadelphia, promise). (3) Eschatological epilogue at 22:7, 22:12, 22:20 (Christ's repeated 'I come quickly' declarations). Entirely absent from the epistles — G5030 tacheōs serves that function. G5035 is the form preferred in direct discourse and narrative commands.

**Semantic categories:** eschatological promise, eschatological event imminence, conditional/disciplinary imminence, transitional promise

**Key verses:** Rev 2:5, Rev 2:16, Rev 3:11, Rev 22:7, Rev 22:12, Rev 22:20

**Relevance:** Tachy drives the book's 'I come quickly' promises. The shift from conditional uses in the letters (2:5, 2:16) to unconditional eschatological promises in the epilogue (22:7,12,20) tracks the temporal framework. The final tachy at 22:20 elicits the community's marana tha response.

### G5030 — tacheōs

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5030-tacheōs.md`

**Occurrences:** 10

Adverb meaning 'shortly, quickly, soon, suddenly.' 10 NT occurrences — 0 in Revelation. Paul's preferred speed-adverb. Distribution: Luke 2×, John 1×, 1 Cor 1×, Gal 1×, Phil 2×, 2 Thess 1×, 1 Tim 1×, 2 Tim 1×. Translations: 'shortly' 40%, 'quickly' 20%, 'soon' 20%, 'suddenly' 10%, 'hastily' 10%. Distinct from G5035 tachy (used in direct discourse/Revelation) and G5034 tachos (noun, used in en tachei). All three belong to the same root family (G5036 tachus = swift).

**Semantic categories:** temporal urgency in apostolic plans, manner swiftness, imminence in pastoral contexts

**Key verses:** Rom 16:20, 2 Thess 2:2, 1 Tim 5:22

**Relevance:** Tacheōs establishes that speed vocabulary outside Revelation carries temporal imminence meaning (Rom 16:20 'bruise Satan shortly' = temporal). The absence from Revelation distinguishes Revelation's preferred forms (tachy, en tachei) while confirming the semantic field.

### G4973 — sphragis

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4973-sphragis.md`

**Occurrences:** 16

Noun meaning 'seal, signet.' 16 NT occurrences — 13 in Revelation (81%). Translations: 'seal' 56.2%, 'seals' 31.2%. Primary Revelation function: the seven seals on the scroll (Rev 5:1-8:1). The sealed scroll = divine content requiring authorized opening. Semantic categories: (1) Authentication/certification — circumcision seals prior faith (Rom 4:11). (2) Divine concealment/authorization — seven-sealed scroll (Rev 5:1). (3) Sequential judgment unveiling — each opened seal releases cosmic events. (4) Protective sealing — seal of God on foreheads (Rev 7:2). (5) Prohibition reversal — sphragizō used prohibitively at Rev 22:10 (do NOT seal).

**Semantic categories:** authentication and certification, divine concealment and authorization, sequential judgment unveiling, protective sealing, prohibition reversal

**Key verses:** Rev 5:1, Rev 5:2, Rev 5:5, Rev 7:2, Rev 22:10

**Relevance:** Sphragis (noun) and sphragizō (verb) track the sealed/unsealed arc that structures the temporal framework: Daniel seals → Christ unseals (Rev 5) → prohibition not to seal at end (Rev 22:10). The seven seals on the scroll represent the sealed Daniel-era revelation now being opened.

### H2856 — chatham

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2856-chatham.md`

**Occurrences:** 26

Hebrew verb meaning 'to seal, to close up.' 26 tokens, 24 unique verses, 11 books. Stem distribution: Qal 22×, Niphal 2×, Piel 1×, Hiphil 1×. KJV: 'seal/sealed' 75%, 'sealed up' 8%, 'seal up/to seal up' 8%. Distribution concentrated in legal/administrative (Esther, Nehemiah, Jeremiah) and prophetic literature (Daniel, Isaiah, Job). Daniel occurrences: Dan 8:26 (seal up vision, Qal Impv), Dan 12:4 (seal the book, Qal Impv), Dan 12:9 (sealed, Qal Ptcp). Greek equivalent G4972 sphragizō (PMI 9.53, 15 LXX co-occurrences).

**Semantic categories:** legal sealing of documents, royal authentication, prophetic concealment, divinely ordered closure

**Key verses:** Dan 8:26, Dan 12:4, Dan 12:9, Jer 32:10-11

**Relevance:** Chatham is the Hebrew verb behind the Daniel sealing commands (Dan 8:26, 12:4,9) that Rev 22:10 inverts. The LXX's sphragizō translation creates the direct Greek link between chatham and Rev 22:10's prohibition. Understanding chatham confirms the deliberate inversion: God commanded sealing for distant fulfillment → God commands NOT sealing for imminent fulfillment.

### G737 — arti

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G737-árti.md`

**Occurrences:** 36

Adverb meaning 'just now, this moment, at this instant.' 36 NT occurrences — 1 in Revelation (12:10). Translation distribution: 'now' 52.8%, 'henceforth' 8.3%, 'Hereafter' 5.6%, 'though now' 5.6%. Distribution: John 13×, Matthew 7×, 1 Cor 7×, Gal 3×, 1 Pet 2×, 2 Thess 1×, 1 Thess 1×, 1 John 1×, Rev 1×, 2 Thess 1×. Denotes present time with emphasis on immediacy and current moment — marks the 'this very moment' of an announcement. Contrast with nyn (G3568) = 'now, at this time' (broader present).

**Semantic categories:** punctiliar present — this exact moment, temporal contrast marker, eschatological now

**Key verses:** Rev 12:10, John 13:31-32, Mat 26:53

**Relevance:** At Rev 12:10 'Arti egeneto hē sōtēria' — 'NOW is come salvation' — arti marks the arrival of salvation as a present-moment realization from the heavenly vantage point. The single Revelation occurrence appears precisely at the heaven-perspective declaration following the dragon's casting. It marks the 'already' of the cosmic shift.

### G906 — ballo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G906-ballo.md`

**Occurrences:** 127

Verb meaning 'to throw, cast.' 106 verses / 127 tokens across 8 NT books. Distribution: Matthew 29×, Revelation 23× (highest density at 5.7 per 100 verses), Luke 16×, Mark 17×, John 14×, Acts 5×. KJV: 'cast' 24.5%, 'casting' 5.7%, 'to be cast' 4.7%, 'was cast' 4.7%. Revelation uses: five semantic categories including cosmic displacement (dragon arc), fire judgment, and seal/shut sequences. The three-stage Satan displacement arc (TM196): eblēthē (12:9, heaven→earth) + ebalen (20:3, earth→abyss) + eblēthē (20:10, abyss→lake of fire). Note: G1544 ekballō is a cognate but distinct root — used at John 12:31 (Satan 'cast out').

**Semantic categories:** throwing/casting objects, placing into a location, casting into judgment locations, cosmic displacement (dragon arc), angelic/divine expulsion

**Key verses:** Rev 12:9, Rev 20:3, Rev 20:10, Rev 12:13, Mat 3:10

**Relevance:** Ballo drives the three-stage dragon displacement arc that spans Rev 12-20. The same verb (G906) traces the dragon's progressive confinement: heaven → earth (12:9), earth → abyss (20:3), abyss → lake of fire (20:10). The arc embeds the temporal structure of the already/not-yet: the first casting (12:9) is the 'already'; the final casting (20:10) is the ultimate 'not yet.'

### G5610 — hora

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5610-hora.md`

**Occurrences:** 108

Noun meaning 'hour, time, moment, season.' 108 NT occurrences — 10 in Revelation (second-highest density at 2.5 per 100 verses, after John at 3.0). KJV: 'hour' 70.4%, 'time' 7.4%. Revelation uses: hōra tēs kriseōs (14:7), hōra therisai (14:15), mia hōra (18:10,17,19), oligon hōran (17:12), hōra tou peirasmou (3:10). Key pattern: hōra + ēlthen = 'the hour has come' formula (proleptic aorist). Distribution: John 26×, Matthew 23×, Luke 16×, Mark 12×, Acts 12×, Revelation 10×.

**Semantic categories:** literal clock hour, the decisive moment/christological turning point, eschatological judgment hour, bounded duration (one hour), appointed hour of testing

**Key verses:** Rev 14:7, Rev 14:15, Rev 18:10, Rev 3:10, Jhn 5:25

**Relevance:** Hora is the vehicle for the hōra + ēlthen proleptic formula — the judgment hour, harvest hour, and judgment of Babylon are all announced with 'the hour has come' (ēlthen hē hōra). The six Revelation uses of hora in temporal-arrival contexts form the VP176-180 structural pattern.

### G2540 — kairos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2540-kairos.md`

**Occurrences:** 84

Noun meaning 'appointed time, proper season, occasion.' 84 tokens in 79 unique verses, 19 NT books. KJV: 'time' 49%, 'times' 9%, 'season/seasons' 9%. Distribution by density: 1 Pet 3.8×, 2 Tim 3.6×, 1 Tim 2.7×, Eph 2.6×, Revelation 1.2× (5 occurrences). Revelation uses: ho kairos engus (1:3, 22:10 — inclusio); kairos tōn nekrōn (11:18 — time of the dead); oligon kairon (12:12 — dragon's short season); kairos ouketi (12:14 — times and half a time). Distinction: kairos = appointed/proper moment; chronos (G5550) = measured elapsed duration.

**Semantic categories:** appointed season/occasion, eschatological appointed time, prophetic fulfillment moment, bounded interval of evil

**Key verses:** Rev 1:3, Rev 22:10, Rev 11:18, Rev 12:12, Rev 12:14

**Relevance:** Kairos is the word in the book's temporal inclusio ('ho kairos engus' at 1:3 and 22:10). The distinction between kairos (appointed moment) and chronos (elapsed duration) is critical at Rev 10:6: chronos ceases, not kairos — the measured delay ends but appointed moments continue to unfold.
