# Verses

## Question
How does Revelation construct its already/not-yet temporal framework through verb tense, vocabulary, and structural markers — and what does this reveal about the book's eschatological logic?

### En Tachei — The Book's Opening Temporal Claim

> The Revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave unto him, to shew unto his servants things which must shortly come to pass; and he sent and signified [it] by his angel unto his servant John:

**Context:** Prologue. The entire book's mandate — 'things which must shortly come to pass' — uses en tachei (G5034, dative of tachos). Sets temporal expectation for everything that follows.

**Notes:** τάχει [N-DSN] G5034 dative of tachos. BDAG glosses en tachei as 'soon, in a short time' at this verse. Debate: does it mean temporal imminence (Mounce, BDAG, Farrar) or manner of execution — swiftness (Walvoord, Thomas)? The identical phrase recurs at Rev 22:6, forming a prologue-epilogue frame. LXX Dan 2:28 uses 'ha dei genesthai' (what must come to pass) — Rev 1:1 echoes this verbatim, linking Revelation's mandate to Daniel's.

### Kairos Engus — First Inclusio Marker

> Blessed [is] he that readeth, and they that hear the words of this prophecy, and keep those things which are written therein: for the time [is] at hand.

**Context:** First beatitude of Revelation. 'The time is at hand' = ho kairos engus. Opens the book's temporal inclusio bracket.

**Notes:** καιρὸς [N-NSM] G2540 + ἐγγύς [ADV] G1451. This exact phrase 'ho kairos engus' recurs at Rev 22:10, forming an inclusio that brackets the entire book. Engus from G1451 word study: 30 NT occurrences; Rev has 2 (1:3, 22:10) — both in this inclusio. Hebrew source H7138 (PMI 7.99). G1448 eggizō is the verbal cognate (process vs. state distinction).

### Three-Element Divine Formula — Full Form

> John to the seven churches which are in Asia: Grace [be] unto you, and peace, from him which is, and which was, and which is to come; and from the seven Spirits which are before his throne;

**Context:** Epistolary prescript. The three-element divine self-description — ho ōn kai ho ēn kai ho erchomenos — appears here in its full form.

**Notes:** ὢν [V-PAP-NSM] G1510 Pres Act Ptcp — 'which is' (present). ἦν [V-IAI-3S] G1510 Impf Act Ind — 'which was' (past). ἐρχόμενος [V-PNP-NSM] G2064 Pres M/P Ptcp — 'which is to come' (future-oriented). Three-element formula: present + past + coming. Recurs at 1:8 and 4:8 in full form. Reduced to two elements at 11:17 and 16:5 (dropping ho erchomenos) — the reduction signals that 'the coming' has been realized.

### Three-Element Divine Formula with Pantokrator

> I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the ending, saith the Lord, which is, and which was, and which is to come, the Almighty.

**Context:** Divine self-declaration. Full three-element formula + Pantokrator (Almighty/all-sovereign).

**Notes:** Same three-element formula as 1:4. Παντοκράτωρ [N-NSM] G3841 added. This is the full form — all three elements present. Compare to reduced form at 11:17 and 16:5 where ho erchomenos is absent.

### Hypomonē as Covenant Condition — Philadelphia

> Because thou hast kept the word of my patience, I also will keep thee from the hour of temptation, which shall come upon all the world, to try them that dwell upon the earth.

**Context:** Letter to Philadelphia. The church's past hypomonē (keeping 'my patience') conditions a future divine keeping during the hour of testing.

**Notes:** G5281 hypomonē = 'my patience/endurance.' The church has matched Christ's own hypomonē (see Rev 1:9 where John is co-partaker). Note tense structure: kept (aorist) → I will keep (future). Endurance in the present tribulation connects to future preservation.

### Nikao Aorist/Present Contrast — Christ's Victory Grounds Believer's Ongoing Overcoming

> To him that overcometh will I grant to sit with me in my throne, even as I also overcame, and am set down with my Father in his throne.

**Context:** Letter to Laodicea, seventh and climactic overcomer promise. Christ's completed victory grounds the promise to ongoing overcomers.

**Notes:** νικῶν [V-PAP-NSM] G3528 Pres Act Ptcp — ongoing 'he that overcometh.' ἐνίκησα [V-AAI-1S] G3528 Aor Act Ind 1S — Christ's completed 'I overcame.' The tense contrast is structural: Christ's aorist (past completed) grounds the believer's present participle (ongoing process). This is TM195 nikao taxonomy: Christ's self-originated aorist vs. believer's present participle.

### Three-Element Divine Formula in Throne Room

### Mighty Angel, Open Book, and Chronos No Longer

> And I saw another mighty angel come down from heaven, clothed with a cloud: and a rainbow [was] upon his head, and his face [was] as it were the sun, and his feet as pillars of fire: And he had in his hand a little book open: and he set his right foot upon the sea, and [his] left [foot] on the earth, [3] And cried with a loud voice, as [when] a lion roareth: and when he had cried, seven thunders uttered their voices. [4] And when the seven thunders had uttered their voices, I was about to write: and I heard a voice from heaven saying unto me, Seal up those things which the seven thunders uttered, and write them not. [5] And the angel which I saw stand upon the sea and upon the earth lifted up his hand to heaven, [6] And sware by him that liveth for ever and ever, who created heaven, and the things that therein are, and the earth, and the things that therein are, and the sea, and the things which are therein, that there should be time no longer: [7] But in the days of the voice of the seventh angel, when he shall begin to sound, the mystery of God should be finished, as he hath declared to his servants the prophets.

**Context:** Interlude between trumpets 6 and 7. The little book is open (contrast sealed scroll of Rev 5). The oath declares chronos ouketi estai. Rev 10:4 seals the seven thunders — the ONLY thing sealed in Revelation (per G4972 word study).

**Notes:** χρόνος [N-NSM] G5550 + οὐκέτι [ADV-N G3765] + ἔσται [V-FDI-3S] G1510 Fut Mid Ind 3S. 'Time shall be no longer' — chronos = measured elapsed duration (not kairos = appointed season). The open book contrasts with the sealed scroll of Dan 12:4 and Rev 5:1. The mystery to be 'finished' (G5055 teleō) at the seventh trumpet links to Rev 11:15 kingdom announcement. The oath structure (lifting hand to heaven) echoes Dan 12:7.

### Seventh Trumpet — Kingdom Announcement as Proleptic Aorist

> The second woe is past; and, behold, the third woe cometh quickly. And the seventh angel sounded; and there were great voices in heaven, saying, The kingdoms of this world are become [the kingdoms] of our Lord, and of his Christ; and he shall reign for ever and ever.

**Context:** Third woe / seventh trumpet. The kingdom announcement in v.15 uses aorist egeneto ('are become') while the future reign is expressed as basileusei (future). Tachu in v.14 marks the rapid sequence.

**Notes:** ταχύ [ADV] G5035 at v.14 — 'quickly' (manner-of-action or temporal imminence). ἐγένοντο [V-2ADI-3P] Aor Mid Ind 3P — the 'voices' became/arose. Ἐγένετο [V-2ADI-3S] G1096 Aor Mid Ind 3S — 'are become' (kingdoms). This is the proleptic aorist: the kingdoms' transfer is announced as accomplished fact in heaven before its earthly realization. βασιλεύσει [V-FAI-3S] G936 Fut Act Ind 3S — 'he shall reign' (future). OT background: Dan 2:44, 7:18,27. NT parallel: Rev 12:10 (ēlthen hē basileia) — same announcement.

### Two-Element Divine Formula — Ho Erchomenos Absent

> Saying, We give thee thanks, O Lord God Almighty, which art, and wast, and art to come; because thou hast taken to thee thy great power, and hast reigned.

**Context:** 24 elders' response to kingdom announcement. KJV translates with three elements but the Greek has ONLY TWO — the coming element (ho erchomenos) is absent.

**Notes:** ὁ ὢν [V-PAP-NSM] + ὁ ἦν [V-IAI-3S] — TWO-ELEMENT formula. ho erchomenos is ABSENT. The KJV 'art to come' is a translation addition not in the Greek. Significance: by dropping ho erchomenos, the formula signals that the 'coming' has been realized in the context of this proclamation. εἴληφας [V-2RAI-2S] G2983 Perf Act Ind 2S lambanō — 'hast taken' (perfect = completed with ongoing result). ἐβασίλευσας [V-AAI-2S] G936 Aor Act Ind 2S — 'hast reigned' (ingressive aorist or prophetic aorist). Two-element formula also at 16:5.

**Context:** Heavenly proclamation following the expulsion (Rev 12:9). The heaven perspective (v.10-11) and the earth-danger perspective (v.12) show the dual already/not-yet reality.

**Notes:** Ἄρτι [ADV] G737 — 'Now' (present-moment marker). ἐγένετο [V-2ADI-3S] G1096 — 'is come' salvation (proleptic/prophetic aorist). ἐβλήθη [V-API-3S] G906 — accuser 'is cast down' (aorist passive). κατηγορῶν [V-PAP-NSM] G2723 — 'which accused' (PRESENT participle — ongoing activity now terminated). v.12: κατέβη [V-2AAI-3S] G2597 — devil 'is come down' (aorist). ἔχων [V-PAP-NSM] G2192 — 'having' great wrath (present participle). εἰδὼς [V-RAP-NSM] G1492 — 'knoweth' (PERFECT participle — knowing with certainty). ἔχει [V-PAI-3S] G2192 — 'hath' but a short time (present indicative). ὀλίγον καιρὸν — 'oligon kairon' = 'a short time/season' (kairos G2540, not chronos). Beast's conquest is real but bounded and permitted — this is the NOT YET that operates under divine sovereignty.

Even so, come, Lord Jesus' = marana tha — the Aramaic liturgical prayer (cf. 1 Cor 16:22). This is the eschatological amen that closes the entire book's temporal framework: en tachei (1:1) → ho kairos engus (1:3) → tachy × 6 (2:5,16; 3:11; 22:7,12,20) → community response. The already/not-yet framework closes with petition for the not-yet to arrive.

### Ha Dei Genesthai — Daniel's LXX Source for Rev 1:1

> But there is a God in heaven that revealeth secrets, and maketh known to the king Nebuchadnezzar what shall be in the latter days. Thy dream, and the visions of thy head upon thy bed, are these;

**Context:** Daniel interpreting Nebuchadnezzar's dream (Dan 2). The LXX of this verse contains 'ha dei genesthai' — the exact phrase repeated in Rev 1:1.

**Notes:** LXX Dan 2:28: 'ha dei genesthai ep' eschatōn tōn hēmerōn' = 'what must come to pass in the last days.' Rev 1:1: 'ha dei genesthai en tachei' = 'what must shortly come to pass.' Rev 1:1 echoes Dan 2:28 verbatim except for the time modifier: 'in the last days' → 'shortly.' This substitution is the key temporal shift — Daniel's distant 'latter days' becomes Revelation's imminent 'shortly.'

### Time Times and Half a Time — Parallel to Rev 12:14

> And he shall speak [great] words against the most High, and shall wear out the saints of the most High, and think to change times and laws: and they shall be given into his hand until a time and times and the dividing of time.

**Context:** Daniel's vision of the fourth beast (Dan 7). The little horn's 3.5-year period — the same time formula as Rev 12:14.

**Notes:** Aramaic: 'iddan v'iddanin u'pelug iddan' = 'a time and times and the dividing of time' = 3.5 years. This exact formula appears at Rev 12:14 ('a time, and times, and half a time') — same temporal period as the 42 months (Rev 13:5) and 1,260 days (Rev 12:6). The saints 'given into his hand' echoes the edothē passives of Rev 13:7.

### Naphal Naphal — Hebrew Prophetic Perfect Doublet Source

> And, behold, here cometh a chariot of men, [with] a couple of horsemen. And he answered and said, Babylon is fallen, is fallen; and all the graven images of her gods he hath broken unto the ground.

**Context:** Isaiah's 'burden of Babylon' oracle. The doublet 'is fallen, is fallen' = Hebrew prophetic perfect (qatal used for future certainty).

**Notes:** Hebrew: naphal naphal Babel = 'fallen, fallen is Babylon' (qatal doublet — prophetic perfect). This is the direct OT source for Rev 14:8 and 18:2's Greek epesen epesen. The LXX renders naphal with epesen (G4098) — the doublet structure is preserved. The repetition in the Hebrew marks the certainty of the announcement. Rev 14:8 declares Babylon's fall prolepticaly before Rev 17-18 narrate it.

### Prophetic Perfect in Isaiah — OT Precedent

> Surely he hath borne our griefs, and carried our sorrows: yet we did esteem him stricken, smitten of God, and afflicted. But he [was] wounded for our transgressions, [he was] bruised for our iniquities: the chastisement of our peace [was] upon him; and with his stripes we are healed.

**Context:** Isaiah's fourth Servant Song. All Hebrew verbs are past-tense qatal forms describing the Servant's future suffering as accomplished fact.

**Notes:** Hebrew qatal (perfect) throughout vv.4-5 describes future events as completed — the prophetic perfect in its most concentrated OT form. 'He was wounded' (chilal, Pual perfect), 'he was bruised' (daka, Pual perfect), 'by his stripes we are healed' (niphal perfect). The LXX translates with aorists — the same Greek aorist tense that Rev 14:8 and 18:2 use for the proleptic doublet. This establishes the OT precedent for prophetic aorists that announce future events as accomplished.

### Pauline Prophetic Aorist — Glorified as Past

> Moreover whom he did predestinate, them he also called: and whom he called, them he also justified: and whom he justified, them he also glorified.

**Context:** Romans 8:29-30 golden chain. 'Glorified' is aorist — future glorification stated as already accomplished.

**Notes:** ἐδόξασεν [V-AAI-3S] G1392 Aor Act Ind 3S — 'glorified' (aorist). This is the Pauline precedent for the prophetic/proleptic aorist: future glorification stated in past tense because it is so certain in God's purpose as to be counted done. Confirms that proleptic aorist use is not unique to Revelation — it is a recognizable Koine/LXX grammatical-rhetorical device.

### En Tachei in Paul — Temporal Imminence Outside Revelation

> And the God of peace shall bruise Satan under your feet shortly. The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ [be] with you. Amen.

**Context:** Romans closing. 'Shortly' = en tachei — same phrase as Rev 1:1 and 22:6.

**Notes:** ἐν τάχει [G5034, N-DSN] — 'shortly' (en tachei). Pauline use in a clear temporal-imminence context: the Satan-bruising is promised to happen soon. This supports the temporal-imminence reading of en tachei at Rev 1:1 — in Paul's usage, the phrase clearly means 'soon, within a short time,' not 'swiftly when it comes.' Alludes to Gen 3:15 (seed bruising serpent's head).

### Michael and the Devil — Contention Over Moses' Body

> Yet Michael the archangel, when contending with the devil he disputed about the body of Moses, durst not bring against him a railing accusation, but said, The Lord rebuke thee.

**Context:** Jude's citation of a conflict between Michael and the devil. Michael appears here as agent in cosmic conflict — parallel to his role in Rev 12:7.

**Notes:** Michael = the archangel who contends with the devil (Jude) and leads the war in heaven (Rev 12:7). The devil is the 'accuser' (katēgorōn at Rev 12:10) — his role as prosecutor/accuser spans both Jude and Revelation. The Michael-devil conflict background illuminates Rev 12:7-9 as part of a larger pattern of cosmic legal/military contest.

### Destroy Works of Devil — Cross-Event Already

> He that committeth sin is of the devil; for the devil sinneth from the beginning. For this purpose the Son of God was manifested, that he might destroy the works of the devil.

**Context:** 1 John 3. The Son of God's purpose in incarnation: to destroy devil's works.

**Notes:** ἐφανερώθη [V-API-3S] G5319 Aor Pass Ind 3S — 'was manifested' (aorist = completed event). λύσῃ [V-AAS-3S] G3089 Aor Act Subj 3S — 'might destroy' (aorist subjunctive = purpose). The cross-event 'already' of 1 John 3:8 parallels John 16:33 (nenikēka) and John 12:31 (ekblēthēsetai future). The devil's works are being destroyed — the 'already' of manifestation grounds the ongoing destruction.

### Destroy Devil Through Death — Cross Already

> Forasmuch then as the children are partakers of flesh and blood, he also himself likewise took part of the same; that through death he might destroy him that had the power of death, that is, the devil;

**Context:** Hebrews 2. Christ's incarnation specifically to destroy the devil through death.

**Notes:** καταργήσῃ [V-AAS-3S] G2673 Aor Act Subj 3S — 'might destroy/render inoperative.' The perfect understanding: Christ's death has already accomplished the defeat of the one holding the power of death. The devil 'had' (past tense) the power of death — but Christ's death changed that. The 'already' of the cross: the devil's power of death is destroyed (even while death itself continues until the final resurrection).

### Tachu in Vision — Speed/Urgency Meaning

> And saw him saying unto me, Make haste, and get thee quickly out of Jerusalem: for they will not receive thy testimony concerning me.

**Context:** Paul's recounting of his vision in the temple. 'Quickly' = tachu — instruction to depart with speed.

**Notes:** ταχύ [ADV] G5035 — 'quickly' (speed/urgency use, not eschatological imminence). 'Make haste and get quickly out of Jerusalem' is a manner-of-action use — the urgency is about speed of departure, not temporal nearness of an event. This establishes that tachy has a non-eschatological semantic range alongside its eschatological uses in Revelation. The word can mean 'quickly/swiftly' (manner) or 'soon' (temporal). Context determines which.

### Satan Fall as Lightning — Jesus's Pre-Cross Declaration

> And he said unto them, I beheld Satan as lightning fall from heaven.

**Context:** Jesus's response to the 70 returning from ministry. He sees Satan's defeat in the disciples' successful exorcisms.

**Notes:** ἐθεώρουν [G2334, imperfect] — 'I was beholding' (imperfect = ongoing action). Σατανᾶν [G4567] + πεσόντα [G4098, aorist participle] — 'Satan having fallen.' The imperfect of viewing + aorist participle = Jesus watched as Satan fell. The NT parallel tool confirmed this as the highest NT parallel for Rev 12:9 (0.349). The pre-cross declaration connects to Rev 12:9's casting out — the cosmic ejection Jesus witnessed in the disciples' exorcisms is the same pattern magnified in Rev 12.
