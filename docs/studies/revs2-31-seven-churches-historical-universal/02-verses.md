# Verses

## Question
What do the seven churches of Revelation 2-3 represent? Do they map to church-history periods? Evaluate the historical-prophetic model, local-historical model, universal-representative model, and hybrid models. Engage the structural evidence from Part 1: the letter chiasm A-B-C-D-C'-B'-A' with Thyatira at center, the 3+4 ear/overcomer reversal, the overcomer arc escalating to New Jerusalem fulfillment, the 7-element template, and the Rev 1:12-20 vision connections.

**Context:** Letter 4 — chiastic center (D). Longest letter (12 verses). ONLY use of 'Son of God' title. ONLY letter with agape commendation. ONLY letter showing growth ('last more than first'). Thyatira commendation is the strongest objection to a purely apostate-era reading in the historical-prophetic model.

### Lion/Lamb Paradox — Christ's Completed Victory

> Revelation 5:5	And one of the elders saith unto me, Weep not: behold, the Lion of the tribe of Juda, the Root of David, hath prevailed to open the book, and to loose the seven seals thereof.
Revelation 5:6	And I beheld, and, lo, in the midst of the throne and of the four beasts, and in the midst of the elders, stood a Lamb as it had been slain, having seven horns and seven eyes, which are the seven Spirits of God sent forth into all the earth.

**Context:** Nikao tense pivot: enikesen (aorist active indicative, 5:5) = Christ's COMPLETED victory. But the Lion announced becomes a Lamb slain — victory redefined as sacrificial death. Seven horns (complete power) and seven eyes (= seven Spirits, complete knowledge).

**Notes:** Aorist enikesen contrasts with present participle ho nikon in the letter promises. Christ overcame (completed) so believers can be overcoming (ongoing). The Lion/Lamb paradox redefines victory.
