5. **The fifth seal altar scene: spatial identification and the sphazo chain:**
READ $WORD_STUDIES/G2379-thysiastērion.md for altar occurrences (23x NT). READ $WORD_STUDIES/G4969-sphazo.md for the slaughter chain. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G5590-psyche.md for 'souls' under the altar. READ TR-mizbeach-thysiasterion.md for the Hebrew-to-Greek altar translation relationship. The prior study (revs-14) documented AN027 (Strong): Rev 6:9's unqualified thysiasteriou differs from the qualified 'golden altar' (to thysiastērion to chrysoun) at Rev 8:3 and 9:13. The spatial term hypokatō ('underneath') corresponds to OT blood poured at the base of the burnt offering altar (Lev 4:7, 18, 25, 30, 34). The sphazo chain uses the identical perfect passive participle esphagmenon for both the Lamb (Rev 5:6, 'as it had been slain') and the martyrs (Rev 6:9, 'slain for the word of God').
