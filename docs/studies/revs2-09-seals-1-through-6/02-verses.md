# Verses

## Question
What is the internal pattern of the four horsemen (seals 1-4)? Document the formula each horseman follows (living creature says 'Come,' horse appears, rider described, effect stated). How does the fifth seal's altar cry ('How long, O Lord?') introduce the theodicy question that drives the rest of the book? What role does the interlude between seals 6 and 7 (the 144,000 and the great multitude, Rev 7) play — how does it answer the question of 6:17 ('who shall be able to stand?')? What is the structural relationship between the seals and the Olivet Discourse (Matt 24)?

**Context:** The Lamb who was found worthy to open the sealed scroll (Rev 5) now opens the first seal. The first living creature (lion, per Rev 4:7) summons the horseman with 'Come' (Erchou). The formula opens with unique thunder element absent from subsequent seals.

### Second seal: red horse, power to take peace, given great sword

> And when he had opened the second seal, I heard the second beast say, Come and see. And there went out another horse [that was] red: and [power] was given to him that sat thereon to take peace from the earth, and that they should kill one another: and there was given unto him a great sword.

**Context:** The second living creature (calf/ox, per Rev 4:7) summons the second horseman. The thunder element is absent. Two divine passives (edothe x2): power given to take peace, and a great sword given.

**Notes:** Formula elements: (1) Lamb opens seal, (2) second living creature says 'Come,' (3) red horse (hippos pyrrhos — pyrrhos G4450 only 2x NT: here and Rev 12:3 red dragon), (4) rider given authority (double edothe), (5) effect — peace removed from earth, mutual slaughter (sphazo G4969 future active: sphaxousin). The great sword is machaira megale (G3162), distinct from the rhomphaia (G4501) of Christ's mouth in Rev 19:15.

### Third seal: black horse with balances, famine pricing, oil and wine spared

> And when he had opened the third seal, I heard the third beast say, Come and see. And I beheld, and lo a black horse; and he that sat on him had a pair of balances in his hand. And I heard a voice in the midst of the four beasts say, A measure of wheat for a penny, and three measures of barley for a penny; and [see] thou hurt not the oil and the wine.

**Context:** The third living creature (human face, per Rev 4:7) summons the third horseman. Unique element: a voice from the midst of the four living creatures announces famine pricing. The divine passive edothe is ABSENT from this seal.

**Notes:** Formula elements: (1) Lamb opens seal, (2) third living creature says 'Come,' (3) black horse (hippos melas — melas G3189, 3x NT: Rev 6:5, 6:12, Matt 5:36), (4) rider has balances/scales (zygos G2218, normally 'yoke,' here 'balance'), (5) unique element — voice announces scarcity prices but protects oil and wine. The edothe pattern is absent — the rider's authority is implied by the pricing voice rather than stated via divine passive.

### Fourth seal: pale horse named Death, Hades follows, authority over 1/4 of earth

> And when he had opened the fourth seal, I heard the voice of the fourth beast say, Come and see. And I looked, and behold a pale horse: and his name that sat on him was Death, and Hell followed with him. And power was given unto them over the fourth part of the earth, to kill with sword, and with hunger, and with death, and with the beasts of the earth.

**Context:** The fourth living creature (eagle, per Rev 4:7) summons the fourth horseman. This is the only seal where the rider is named (Thanatos/Death, G2288) and has a companion (Hades, G86). The fourfold killing means match Ezekiel 14:21 (AN024 Strong).

**Notes:** Formula elements: (1) Lamb opens seal, (2) fourth living creature says 'Come,' (3) pale horse (hippos chloros — chloros G5515, normally 'green/verdant' but here 'sickly pallor'), (4) rider NAMED (onoma auto ho Thanatos, TM065), companion Hades follows (akoloutheo imperfect — continuous following), (5) edothe to them (plural — both Death and Hades) authority over 1/4 of earth. Killing means: en rhomphaia (sword, note: Greek uses rhomphaia G4501 here, not machaira), en limo (famine), en thanato (death/pestilence), hypo ton therion (by beasts). This matches Ezek 14:21: sword, famine, noisome beast, pestilence (AN024). Fraction 1/4 begins the escalation pattern SP036 (1/4 seals → 1/3 trumpets → total bowls).

### Fifth seal: altar scene, martyrs' cry, white robes, little season

> And when he had opened the fifth seal, I saw under the altar the souls of them that were slain for the word of God, and for the testimony which they held: And they cried with a loud voice, saying, How long, O Lord, holy and true, dost thou not judge and avenge our blood on them that dwell on the earth? And white robes were given unto every one of them; and it was said unto them, that they should rest yet for a little season, until their fellowservants also and their brethren, that should be killed as they [were], should be fulfilled.

**Context:** Clear 4+3 structural break (SP025 Strong). Every element of the horseman formula is abandoned: no living creature command, no horse, no rider. Instead: altar scene in heavenly sanctuary. The martyrs are described with the same sphazo vocabulary as the Lamb (esphagmenon, perfect passive participle). The 'how long' cry echoes OT lament tradition (Ps 6:3, 13:1, 79:5, 94:3, Hab 1:2, Zech 1:12). Three temporal markers: (a) esphagmenon — perfect participle, past completed martyrdom; (b) chronon mikron — accusative of duration, future waiting period; (c) mellontes apoktennesthai — present participle + present passive infinitive, future martyrdom. The vindication arc (VP076 Strong) begins here with ekdikeis (present active indicative) and resolves at Rev 19:2 with exedikesen (aorist active indicative).

### Sixth seal: great earthquake, sun black, moon as blood, stars fall, heaven departs as scroll

> And I beheld when he had opened the sixth seal, and, lo, there was a great earthquake; and the sun became black as sackcloth of hair, and the moon became as blood; And the stars of heaven fell unto the earth, even as a fig tree casteth her untimely figs, when she is shaken of a mighty wind. And the heaven departed as a scroll when it is rolled together; and every mountain and island were moved out of their places.

**Context:** Cosmic disruption at the sixth seal. VP208 (Strong, 4 shared terms) with Matt 24:29: helios (sun), selene (moon), aster (stars), pipto (fall). OT sources: Joel 2:10,31 (sun/moon darkened), Isa 13:10-13 (cosmic judgment), Isa 34:4 (heavens rolled as scroll). The seismos megas (great earthquake, G4578) connects to the Sinai-theophany formula established in revs2-03.

**Notes:** Seven cosmic elements: (1) seismos megas, (2) sun black as sackcloth (melas G3189 reappears), (3) moon as blood, (4) stars fall, (5) fig-tree simile, (6) heaven departs as scroll, (7) mountains/islands moved. Rev 6:14 'heaven departed as a scroll when it is rolled together' parallels Isa 34:4 'heavens shall be rolled together as a scroll' — verbal source. Mountains/islands language also appears at Rev 16:20 in the seventh bowl, creating shared endpoint language (SP092 per hist-14).

### Sixth seal response: seven social classes hide, 'who shall be able to stand?'

> And the kings of the earth, and the great men, and the rich men, and the chief captains, and the mighty men, and every bondman, and every free man, hid themselves in the dens and in the rocks of the mountains; And said to the mountains and rocks, Fall on us, and hide us from the face of him that sitteth on the throne, and from the wrath of the Lamb: For the great day of his wrath is come; and who shall be able to stand?

**Context:** Seven social classes form a merism of all humanity — no one exempted. The cry to mountains parallels Hos 10:8 ('say to the mountains, Cover us; and to the hills, Fall on us'). 'Wrath of the Lamb' (orge tou arniou) is a paradoxical phrase — the slain Lamb who redeems also judges. The question 'who shall be able to stand?' (tis dynatai stathenai) uses the aorist passive infinitive of histemi (G2476) and is answered in Rev 7:9 where the multitude is 'standing' (hestotes, perfect active participle of the same verb).

**Notes:** The seven classes: basileis (kings), megistanes (great men), plousioi (rich), chiliarchoi (chief captains/commanders), dynatoi (mighty), doulos (bondman), eleutheros (free). This forms a merism covering all social strata. orge (G3709) — wrath — appears 6x in Revelation. The question in 6:17 drives the narrative into the interlude of Rev 7.

### Four angels holding four winds, sealing angel with seal of the living God

> And after these things I saw four angels standing on the four corners of the earth, holding the four winds of the earth, that the wind should not blow on the earth, nor on the sea, nor on any tree. And I saw another angel ascending from the east, having the seal of the living God: and he cried with a loud voice to the four angels, to whom it was given to hurt the earth and the sea, Saying, Hurt not the earth, neither the sea, nor the trees, till we have sealed the servants of our God in their foreheads.

**Context:** The interlude between the sixth and seventh seals (SP031 Moderate, interlude pattern). Four angels restrain destructive judgment (winds) while the sealing angel marks God's servants. The sealing parallels Ezek 9:4-6 (AN020 Strong): mark on foreheads, protection before judgment, faithfulness criterion, angelic agents.

**Notes:** meta tauta (after these things) opens a new vision unit. The seal of the living God (sphragis theou zontos) — sphragis G4973 and sphragizo G4972. Foreheads (metopon G3359, 8x Revelation only, always marking allegiance — TM054). The verb 'hurt' (adikeo G91) connects to the restraining function — judgment is held back until sealing is complete. This establishes the sealing as preparation for subsequent judgments.

### 144,000 sealed from 12 tribes — tribal listing with anomalies

> And I heard the number of them which were sealed: [and there were] sealed an hundred [and] forty [and] four thousand of all the tribes of the children of Israel. Of the tribe of Juda [were] sealed twelve thousand. Of the tribe of Reuben [were] sealed twelve thousand. Of the tribe of Gad [were] sealed twelve thousand. Of the tribe of Aser [were] sealed twelve thousand. Of the tribe of Nepthalim [were] sealed twelve thousand. Of the tribe of Manasses [were] sealed twelve thousand. Of the tribe of Simeon [were] sealed twelve thousand. Of the tribe of Levi [were] sealed twelve thousand. Of the tribe of Issachar [were] sealed twelve thousand. Of the tribe of Zabulon [were] sealed twelve thousand. Of the tribe of Joseph [were] sealed twelve thousand. Of the tribe of Benjamin [were] sealed twelve thousand.

**Context:** John HEARS the number 144,000 (ekousa, aorist of akouo G191). The tribal list is unique in Scripture (TM052): Judah first (not Reuben the firstborn — 1 Chr 5:2 explains Judah prevailed, Rev 5:5 identifies Jesus as Lion of Judah), Dan absent (idolatry association, Jdg 18, 1 Ki 12:28-29), Levi included (dissolving priestly/non-priestly distinction), Joseph replaces Ephraim (Ephraim had idolatry associations, Hos 4:17).

**Notes:** Arithmetic: 12 × 12 × 1,000 = 144,000 (TM053). The structure 12 × 12 × 1,000 connects to Revelation's pervasive use of 12: 12 gates, 12 foundations, 12 apostles, 24 (2×12) elders, 12,000 stadia. The 1,000 multiplier suggests completeness. VP066 (Strong, 4 shared elements) links Rev 7:3-4 with Rev 14:1 (sealing, foreheads, 144,000, servants/Father's name).

### Throne service, skenosei dwelling, no hunger/thirst/heat, Lamb leads to living fountains

> Therefore are they before the throne of God, and serve him day and night in his temple: and he that sitteth on the throne shall dwell among them. They shall hunger no more, neither thirst any more; neither shall the sun light on them, nor any heat. For the Lamb which is in the midst of the throne shall feed them, and shall lead them unto living fountains of waters: and God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes.

**Context:** The interlude's conclusion answers 6:17 comprehensively: those who 'stand' are those who serve before God's throne. The verb skenosei (future of skenoo G4637, 'shall dwell/tabernacle') combines with the palm branches of 7:9 to create a Feast of Tabernacles vocabulary cluster. VP067 (Moderate): Rev 7:17 and Rev 21:4 share the identical phrase 'God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes.'

**Notes:** skenosei (G4637) — cognate of skene (tabernacle). The Tabernacles vocabulary cluster: palms (phoinix G5404, 7:9) + tabernacle verb (skenosei, 7:15) = feast-specific allusion. Tabernacles is the post-harvest celebration feast — placing the great multitude's worship in a post-judgment context. Rev 7:16 'They shall hunger no more, neither thirst any more' reverses the famine/thirst imagery of the horsemen judgments. 'Living fountains of waters' (pegas zoson hydaton) — pege G4077 (spring/fountain). Rev 7:17 and 21:4 verbal parallel: VP067 Moderate.

### Golden altar: incense with prayers of saints, fire cast to earth — vindication arc stage 2

> And another angel came and stood at the altar, having a golden censer; and there was given unto him much incense, that he should offer [it] with the prayers of all saints upon the golden altar which was before the throne. And the smoke of the incense, [which came] with the prayers of the saints, ascended up before God out of the angel's hand. And the angel took the censer, and filled it with fire of the altar, and cast [it] into the earth: and there were voices, and thunderings, and lightnings, and an earthquake.

**Context:** The golden altar (to thysiastērion to chrysoun) is explicitly qualified here, unlike the unqualified altar (tou thysiasteriou) in Rev 6:9 — AN027 Strong. This is stage 2 of the altar vindication arc: the prayers that ascended in 6:10 ('how long?') are now mixed with incense and returned to earth as judgment (fire). The Sinai-theophany formula escalates: Rev 4:5 had 3 elements; Rev 8:5 adds earthquake (4 elements).

**Notes:** Vindication arc stage 2: martyrs' prayers (6:10) → incense-prayer mixture at golden altar (8:3-4) → fire cast to earth (8:5) → judgment begins (trumpets). The altar receives prayers and transforms them into judgment instruments. Same altar later personified in Rev 16:7.

### Vindication arc midpoint: altar personified speaks — 'true and righteous are thy judgments'

> And I heard the angel of the waters say, Thou art righteous, O Lord, which art, and wast, and shalt be, because thou hast judged thus. For they have shed the blood of saints and prophets, and thou hast given them blood to drink; for they are worthy. And I heard another out of the altar say, Even so, Lord God Almighty, true and righteous [are] thy judgments.

**Context:** The altar itself speaks (ek tou thysiasteriou, 'out of the altar'). This is the same altar under which the martyrs cried in 6:9 and at which their prayers were offered in 8:3. Now the altar CONFIRMS the justice of God's judgments: 'true and righteous' (alethinai kai dikaiai) — vocabulary shared with 6:10 (ho hagios kai alethinos) and 19:2 (alethinai kai dikaiai). VP076/VP077.

**Notes:** Vindication arc stage 3: the altar that received the martyrs' blood now vindicates God's justice. The blood-for-blood reciprocity: 'they have shed blood of saints... thou hast given them blood to drink.' The altar personification transforms it from passive receptacle to active witness.

### Vindication arc resolution: 'true and righteous are his judgments... hath avenged'

> And after these things I heard a great voice of much people in heaven, saying, Alleluia; Salvation, and glory, and honour, and power, unto the Lord our God: For true and righteous [are] his judgments: for he hath judged the great whore, which did corrupt the earth with her fornication, and hath avenged the blood of his servants at her hand.

**Context:** The vindication arc resolves. The verb exedikesen (aorist active indicative of ekdikeo G1556) marks completed vindication — tense shift from the present ekdikeis at 6:10. The vocabulary 'true and righteous' (alethinai kai dikaiai) echoes both 6:10 and 16:7. VP076 Strong, 5 links: Rev 6:10 → 16:7 → 19:2.

**Notes:** Vindication arc resolution: ekdikeis (present, 6:10 — 'dost thou not avenge?') → exedikesen (aorist, 19:2 — 'hath avenged'). Tense shift from present to aorist signals the arc's completion. The blood of his servants (haima ton doulon autou) — same vocabulary as 6:10 (haima hemon). The phrase 'at her hand' (ek cheiros autes) — Babylon held accountable.
