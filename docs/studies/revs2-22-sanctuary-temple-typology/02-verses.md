# Verses

## Question
How does sanctuary/temple typology run through the entire book of Revelation? Trace every sanctuary element, identify its OT compartment (Exod 25-40), and determine whether the progression outer court → Holy Place → Most Holy Place correlates with the judgment sequences. How does DOA typology (Lev 16) function as a structural marker?

### Seven Golden Lampstands — Christ among Holy Place Furniture

> And I turned to see the voice that spake with me. And being turned, I saw seven golden candlesticks; And in the midst of the seven candlesticks [one] like unto the Son of man, clothed with a garment down to the foot, and girt about the paps with a golden girdle.

**Context:** Opening vision of Revelation; John turns to see the speaker and encounters Christ ministering among seven golden lampstands. Priestly garment (podērē + golden girdle) identifies Christ as the heavenly high priest.

**Notes:** ἑπτὰ λυχνίας χρυσᾶς (seven golden lampstands, G3087) — the OT menorah placed in the Holy Place (Exod 25:31-40; 26:35). Christ as priest in the midst of the lampstands establishes the HP as Revelation's opening sanctuary setting. Paulien's septet-introduction pattern: lampstand scene (1:12-20) precedes the seven letters just as throne room (4-5) precedes the seals.

### Lampstand Identification — Seven Churches

> The mystery of the seven stars which thou sawest in my right hand, and the seven golden candlesticks. The seven stars are the angels of the seven churches: and the seven candlesticks which thou sawest are the seven churches.

**Context:** Christ interprets the opening vision: the seven stars = angels of the seven churches; the seven lampstands = the seven churches themselves.

**Notes:** αἱ ἑπτὰ λυχνίαι αἱ ἑπτὰ ἐκκλησίαι εἰσίν — lampstands = churches (explicit equation). The churches occupy the position of the Holy Place lampstand in the heavenly sanctuary; their lamp is removable (Rev 2:5 threat). G3087 lychnia: 6 Revelation occurrences, all in the lampstand/church context.

### Naos Occurrence — Overcomer as Pillar

> Him that overcometh will I make a pillar in the temple of my God, and he shall go no more out: and I will write upon him the name of my God, and the name of the city of my God, [which is] new Jerusalem, which cometh down out of heaven from my God: and [I will write upon him] my new name.

**Context:** Letter to Philadelphia; overcomer promise at letter 6 of 7. The promise is permanent installation as a structural element of the heavenly naos.

**Notes:** στῦλον ἐν τῷ ναῷ τοῦ Θεοῦ μου — pillar in the naos of God. Permanent stability: he shall go no more out (οὐ μὴ ἐξέλθῃ ἔτι). Three names inscribed: God's name, New Jerusalem's name, Christ's new name. Context does not specify HP vs MHP — naos here = inner sanctuary in general.

### Seven Lamps (HP) and Sea of Glass (OC) — All-Compartment Throne Scene

> And out of the throne proceeded lightnings and thunderings and voices: and [there were] seven lamps of fire burning before the throne, which are the seven Spirits of God. And before the throne [there was] a sea of glass like unto crystal: and in the midst of the throne, and round about the throne, [were] four beasts full of eyes before and behind.

**Context:** Throne room vision of Rev 4; the establishing scene before the seal series begins. All major sanctuary compartments are present simultaneously.

**Notes:** ἑπτὰ λαμπάδες πυρὸς καιόμεναι (seven torches of fire = seven Spirits, G2985 lampas not G3087 lychnia — distinct term). θάλασσα ὑαλίνη ὁμοία κρυστάλλῳ (sea of glass like crystal) = bronze laver (Exod 30:18-21; 1 Kgs 7:23-26 outer court placement). Theophany: 3-element (lightning + voices + thunder) = IC077 Level 1. This establishing scene conflates all compartments before sequential progression begins at 6:9.

Twenty-four elders = priestly courses (1 Chr 24); white raiment = priestly garb. Rainbow (Rev 4:3) echoes Ezek 1:28.

### Golden Bowls of Incense — Vessel Transformation Origin

> And when he had taken the book, the four beasts and four [and] twenty elders fell down before the Lamb, having every one of them harps, and golden vials full of odours, which are the prayers of saints.

**Context:** The Lamb receives the sealed scroll; the four living creatures and twenty-four elders fall in worship holding harps and golden bowls.

**Notes:** φιάλας χρυσᾶς γεμούσας θυμιαμάτων (golden bowls full of incense, G5357 phiale + G2368 thymiama) = prayers of saints. This is the intercession phase of the vessel transformation arc. The same grammatical construction recurs at Rev 15:7 with wrath (θυμοῦ τοῦ Θεοῦ) replacing incense — identical φιάλας χρυσᾶς γεμούσας + genitive content. HP incense altar function.

### Souls Under the Bronze Altar — Outer Court

> And when he had opened the fifth seal, I saw under the altar the souls of them that were slain for the word of God, and for the testimony which they held:

**Context:** Fifth seal opened; John sees martyred souls beneath the altar crying for vindication.

**Notes:** ὑποκάτω τοῦ θυσιαστηρίου (G5270 hypokatō + G2379 thysiastērion, genitive) — souls UNDER the altar; no χρυσοῦν qualifier = outer court bronze altar (Exod 27:1-8). Blood of sin offering poured at base of bronze altar (Lev 4:7) — martyrs' blood = sacrifice at OC altar. Initiates the altar vindication arc: 6:10 (ekdikeis, present) → 16:7 (ekdikeo, altar speaks) → 19:2 (exedikēsen, aorist, resolved).

### Naos Occurrence — Priestly Service Before the Throne

> Therefore are they before the throne of God, and serve him day and night in his temple: and he that sitteth on the throne shall dwell among them.

**Context:** The great multitude who came out of the great tribulation are described in their heavenly state before the throne.

**Notes:** λατρεύουσιν αὐτῷ ἡμέρας καὶ νυκτός ἐν τῷ ναῷ αὐτοῦ (serve him day and night in his naos). σκηνώσει ἐπ' αὐτούς (G4637 skēnoō, future active) — God will tabernacle over them. The perpetual day-and-night service before the throne suggests the naos = inner sanctuary in general (not MHP exclusively, since MHP access was once-yearly in the earthly type). Context test for Bohr's claim: this is more naturally inner sanctuary than MHP-exclusive.

### Golden Altar of Incense — Holy Place; DOA Typology

> And when he had opened the seventh seal, there was silence in heaven about the space of half an hour. And I saw the seven angels which stood before God; and to them were given seven trumpets. And another angel came and stood at the altar, having a golden censer; and there was given unto him much incense, that he should offer [it] with the prayers of all saints upon the golden altar which was before the throne. And the smoke of the incense, [which came] with the prayers of the saints, ascended up before God out of the angel's hand. And the angel took the censer, and filled it with fire of the altar, and cast [it] into the earth: and there were voices, and thunderings, and lightnings, and an earthquake.

**Context:** Seventh seal opened with half-hour silence; incense offering at the golden altar precedes the trumpet series.

**Notes:** Two epi constructions: (1) ἐστάθη ἐπὶ τοῦ θυσιαστηρίου [gen] = stood at the altar (positional); (2) ἐπὶ τὸ θυσιαστήριον τὸ χρυσοῦν [acc] = motion onto the golden altar (directional). λιβανωτὸν χρυσοῦν (golden censer, G3031) — only 2 NT occurrences, both in Rev 8. DOA parallel: Lev 16:12-13 (censer with coals from altar + incense → before LORD). Theophany at v.5: 4-element (IC077 Level 2). Censer reversal: incense ascending (8:3-4) → fire cast to earth (8:5).

### Triple Genitive — Explicit HP Golden Altar Identification

> And the sixth angel sounded, and I heard a voice from the four horns of the golden altar which is before God,

**Context:** Sixth trumpet; a voice from the horns of the golden altar releases the four angels bound at the Euphrates.

**Notes:** Triple genitive chain: τοῦ θυσιαστηρίου τοῦ χρυσοῦ τοῦ ἐνώπιον τοῦ Θεοῦ (the altar + the golden one + the before-God one). Most explicit HP golden altar identification in Revelation. Four horns = incense altar (Exod 30:3; Lev 16:18). Voice from the horns = altar speaks (cf. Rev 16:7). HP identification confirmed: chrysou + enopion tou Theou + four horns = triple qualifier.

### Three Sanctuary Zones Named — Naos Measured, Outer Court Excluded

> And there was given me a reed like unto a rod: and the angel stood, saying, Rise, and measure the temple of God, and the altar, and them that worship therein. But the court which is without the temple leave out, and measure it not; for it is given unto the Gentiles: and the holy city shall they tread under foot forty [and] two months.

**Context:** Between the sixth and seventh trumpets; John is given a measuring reed to measure three zones of the sanctuary.

**Notes:** Three zones: (1) τὸν ναὸν τοῦ Θεοῦ (naos, G3485, acc) — inner sanctuary measured; (2) τὸ θυσιαστήριον (altar, G2379, acc) — measured; (3) τοὺς προσκυνοῦντας ἐν αὐτῷ — worshippers. τὴν αὐλὴν τὴν ἔξωθεν τοῦ ναοῦ (G833 aulē, court outside the naos) — explicitly excluded. This is the only passage naming all three zones in sequence. aulē = outer court (Exod 27:9); its exclusion confirms naos ≠ outer court.

### Naos Opened — Ark Seen — MHP Access (Janus Verse)

> And the temple of God was opened in heaven, and there was seen in his temple the ark of his testament: and there were lightnings, and voices, and thunderings, and an earthquake, and great hail.

**Context:** Seventh trumpet climax; after the kingdom announcement (11:15-18), the naos is opened and the ark is revealed.

**Notes:** ἠνοίγη ὁ ναὸς (aor pass, agentless divine passive) + ὤφθη ἡ κιβωτός (aor pass, agentless) — both passives: the naos opened itself (divinely), the ark was seen. κιβωτὸς τῆς διαθήκης (G2787, only Revelation occurrence) = MHP furniture (Exod 26:34; Heb 9:4). Theophany: 5-element (lightning + voices + thunder + earthquake + great hail) = IC077 Level 3. Janus verse: closes the trumpet sequence, opens toward the MHP bowl prelude (15:5).

### Naos Occurrence — Beast Blasphemes God's Sanctuary

> And he opened his mouth in blasphemy against God, to blaspheme his name, and his tabernacle, and them that dwell in heaven.

**Context:** The beast from the sea opens his mouth in blasphemy; the target is God himself, his naos/skēnē, and the heavenly inhabitants.

**Notes:** τὴν σκηνὴν αὐτοῦ καὶ τοὺς ἐν τῷ οὐρανῷ σκηνοῦντας — beast blasphemes God's skēnē (G4633) and those skēnoō-ing (G4637, 4th use) in heaven. Some MSS read 'his name and his naos, even his tabernacle' — textual variant where naos and skēnē are coordinated terms. Anti-sanctuary polemic as mark of the beast's character; the sanctuary is the target precisely because it is the seat of divine authority.

### Angel from the Naos — First Harvest Command

> And another angel came out of the temple, crying with a loud voice to him that sat on the cloud, Thrust in thy sickle, and reap: for the time is come for thee to reap; for the harvest of the earth is ripe.

**Context:** First harvest scene; an angel exits the naos to command the harvest of the earth.

**Notes:** ἐκ τοῦ ναοῦ (out of the naos) — the harvest command originates inside the inner sanctuary. Naos as the source of judicial/harvest commands. No compartment qualifier given; the naos here = command center from which divine judgment issues. Cf. Rev 16:1 (voice from naos commands bowl pouring).

### Angel from the Naos — Second Harvest (Vintage)

> And another angel came out of the temple which is in heaven, he also having a sharp sickle.

**Context:** Second harvest scene (vintage/wrath); another angel exits the naos in heaven carrying a sharp sickle for the vine harvest.

**Notes:** ἐκ τοῦ ναοῦ τοῦ ἐν τῷ οὐρανῷ — from the naos which is in heaven (same articulated phrase as 11:19). The vine harvest = wrath trampling (14:19-20). Two consecutive angels exit the naos (14:15 and 14:17), establishing the naos as the origin of both grain harvest and vintage judgment commands.

### Angel from the Altar — Fire Authority (Outer Court)

> And another angel came out from the altar, which had power over fire; and cried with a loud cry to him that had the sharp sickle, saying, Thrust in thy sharp sickle, and gather the clusters of the vine of the earth; for her grapes are fully ripe.

**Context:** Third angel in the harvest sequence; this angel comes from the altar (thysiastērion) and has authority over fire.

**Notes:** ἐκ τοῦ θυσιαστηρίου — from the thysiastērion (no chrysoun qualifier). ἐξουσίαν ἐπὶ τοῦ πυρός (authority over fire) = bronze altar identification (fire of burnt offering, not incense altar). Consistent with Rev 8:5 (fire from the same altar cast to earth). No golden qualifier = outer court bronze altar, not HP golden altar.

### Sea of Glass Mingled with Fire — Laver Transformation

### Divine Warrior — Possible DOA Priest-Emergence Typology

> And I saw heaven opened, and behold a white horse; and he that sat upon him [was] called Faithful and True, and in righteousness he doth judge and make war. His eyes [were] as a flame of fire, and on his head [were] many crowns; and he had a name written, that no man knew, but he himself. And he [was] clothed with a vesture dipped in blood: and his name is called The Word of God. And the armies [which were] in heaven followed him upon white horses, clothed in fine linen, white and clean. And he hath on [his] vesture and on his thigh a name written, KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS.

**Context:** The Divine Warrior emerges from heaven on a white horse for the final battle; he is called Faithful and True, the Word of God.

**Notes:** Heaven opened (ἠνεῳγμένον τὸν οὐρανόν) — the warrior emerges from the opened heaven. Vesture dipped in blood (βεβαμμένον αἵματι) — judgment imagery. Fine linen, white and clean (βύσσινον λευκὸν καθαρόν) = same linen as the bowl-angels (Rev 15:6). Possible DOA typology: the high priest emerged from the MHP after completing atonement (Lev 16:24 — 'he shall come out, and offer his burnt offering'). The warrior-priest emergence from the opened heaven after the bowl series (DOA typology) parallels the high priest's post-atonement emergence. Confidence: Moderate/Weak — the primary reading is Divine Warrior, not priest-emergence.
