# Verse-by-Verse Analysis
# revs2-22 — Sanctuary/Temple Typology in Revelation

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

---

### Rev 1:12-13
**Context:** John's inaugural vision — he turns to see the speaker and finds Christ walking among seven golden lampstands, dressed in a priestly robe with a golden sash.

**Direct statement:** Christ is minister among the lampstands. The seven lampstands are identified at 1:20 as the seven churches. The priestly garment (podērē = long robe + chrysas zōnas = golden sash) marks Christ as heavenly high priest.

**Original language:** ἑπτὰ λυχνίας χρυσᾶς (seven golden lampstands, G3087 lychnia). The OT menorah (H4501 menōrāh) is specifically placed in the Holy Place (Exod 26:35; 40:24-25; Heb 9:2). The LXX renders H4501 with lychnia, making the identification explicit. G3087 lychnia has 12 NT occurrences, 6 of them in Revelation.

**Cross-references:** Exod 25:31-40 (lampstand design; HP placement); Exod 26:35 (explicit HP location); Zech 4:2-11 (golden lampstand as Spirit empowerment); Heb 9:2 (lampstand in the first tabernacle = HP).

**Relationship to other evidence:** The lampstand is the first sanctuary element introduced in the book. By placing Christ among HP furniture in the opening vision, Revelation establishes the HP as the starting sanctuary context. This is the first point in Paulien's four-introduction pattern: lampstand scene (1:12-20) introduces the seven letters.

---

### Rev 1:20
**Context:** Christ interprets the inaugural vision — lampstands = churches, stars = angels of the churches.

**Direct statement:** The equation lampstands = churches is explicit and authoritative (Christ himself gives it).

**Original language:** αἱ ἑπτὰ λυχνίαι αἱ ἑπτὰ ἐκκλησίαι εἰσίν. The churches occupy the position of the HP menorah in the heavenly sanctuary.

**Cross-references:** Rev 2:5 (threat to remove the lampstand); the churches function in the HP as mediating lights — the menorah's role was to illuminate the HP for priestly ministry.

**Relationship to other evidence:** Confirms that the lampstands belong in the HP zone. The removability of the lampstand (2:5) implies it occupies a specific place — consistent with the fixed HP location in the blueprint.

---

### Rev 3:12
**Context:** Letter to Philadelphia; the overcomer promise: "a pillar in the temple (naos) of my God."

**Direct statement:** The overcomer will be permanently installed as a structural element inside the naos. Three names will be inscribed: God's name, New Jerusalem's name, Christ's new name.

**Original language:** στῦλον ἐν τῷ ναῷ τοῦ Θεοῦ μου (pillar in the naos of God). οὐ μὴ ἐξέλθῃ ἔτι (shall go no more out) — double negative emphatic = permanent stability. The naos here is used in its general inner-sanctuary sense; no compartment qualifier is given. The promise of permanence looks forward to Rev 21:22 where God himself IS the naos.

**Cross-references:** Rev 21:22 (naos transcended); Rev 21:3 (God dwelling with his people); the pillar promise is an overcomer fulfillment anticipating the New Jerusalem.

**Relationship to other evidence:** This is one of several naos occurrences where no HP/MHP distinction is signaled. The naos = inner sanctuary generally. Bohr's MHP-exclusive claim would require that the overcomer is installed as a pillar specifically in the MHP — possible, but the text does not mark it. The general inner-sanctuary reading is more natural here.

---

### Rev 4:1-4 (Supporting)
**Context:** Throne room establishing scene; John is summoned through a door opened in heaven.

**Direct statement:** The door opened in heaven (θύρα ἠνεῳγμένη) gives John access to the heavenly throne room. This is a vocabulary anchor: compare 11:19 (naos opened) and 15:5 (naos of testimony opened).

**Original language:** The opening vocabulary (anoigō) connects this scene to the progressive sanctuary-opening sequence: 4:1 (door) → 11:19 (naos opened, ark visible) → 15:5 (naos of testimony opened). The twenty-four elders (white raiment, golden crowns) parallel the priestly courses (1 Chr 24).

**Cross-references:** Exod 24:10 (elders of Israel see God on the mountain); Dan 7:9-10 (thrones, Ancient of Days, judgment set).

**Relationship to other evidence:** The throne room vision is the establishing scene that conflates all compartments. It is not the first step of OC→HP→MHP progression; it is the structural context within which that progression unfolds.

---

### Rev 4:5
**Context:** Throne room scene; the theophany formula originates "out of the throne," accompanied by seven lamps of fire.

**Direct statement:** The seven lamps of fire = the seven Spirits of God. These are distinct from the seven golden lampstands (lychnia, 1:12) — here the term is lampas (G2985 lampas = torch/burning lamp) not lychnia.

**Original language:** ἑπτὰ λαμπάδες πυρὸς καιόμεναι ἐνώπιον τοῦ θρόνου (seven torches of fire burning before the throne, G2985 lampas). This is the HP lampstand imagery but with a different term, identifying the lights as divine spirit activity rather than the lampstand itself. Theophany formula: astrapai + phōnai + brontai = 3 elements (IC077 Level 1, baseline).

**Cross-references:** Zech 4:10 (seven eyes of the LORD); Isa 11:2 (seven-fold Spirit); Exod 25:37 (seven lamps of the menorah).

**Relationship to other evidence:** The 3-element theophany here is the structural baseline for the escalation sequence (4:5 / 8:5 / 11:19 / 16:18). Its origin "out of the throne" anchors each subsequent theophany recurrence back to the throne room.

---

### Rev 4:6 (and Rev 4:5-6 combined)
**Context:** Before the throne — the sea of glass like crystal, and the four living creatures around it.

**Direct statement:** "Before the throne there was a sea of glass like unto crystal" (G5193 hualinos, ὁμοία κρυστάλλῳ). The four living creatures with eyes all around are positioned in the midst and around the throne.

**Original language:** θάλασσα ὑαλίνη ὁμοία κρυστάλλῳ (sea of glass like crystal). Position: ἐνώπιον τοῦ θρόνου = before the throne. The OT laver (bronze, Exod 30:18-21) was placed in the outer court between the tabernacle entrance and the bronze altar. The bronze sea of Solomon's temple (1 Kgs 7:23-26) was similarly in the outer court (1 Kgs 7:39). The four living creatures echo Ezek 1:22 (crystal firmament above the living creatures, throne above the firmament). Revelation may draw on both traditions — the laver typology (outer-court function: purification) and the Ezekiel crystal expanse (cosmic throne-room backdrop).

**Cross-references:** Exod 30:17-21 (bronze laver, outer court); 1 Kgs 7:23-26 (bronze sea, outer court); Ezek 1:22-28 (crystal firmament under the divine throne).

**Relationship to other evidence:** The sea of glass in the throne room is a transferred element: it originates in the outer court by function (laver/purification) but appears in the inner sanctuary context in Revelation's heavenly vision. The four living creatures around the throne = cherubim of the MHP (Exod 25:18-20). This confirms the all-compartment character of the establishing scene: OC (sea of glass/laver), HP (seven lamps/lampstand), MHP (living creatures/cherubim), all simultaneously present in the throne room.

### Rev 5:8
**Context:** The Lamb receives the scroll; the four living creatures and elders fall in worship holding harps and golden bowls full of incense.

**Direct statement:** φιάλας χρυσᾶς γεμούσας θυμιαμάτων, αἵ εἰσιν αἱ προσευχαὶ τῶν ἁγίων — golden bowls full of incense, which are the prayers of the saints. The incense = prayers equation is explicit.

**Original language:** G5357 phiale (bowl), all 12 NT occurrences are in Revelation. G2368 thymiama (incense), 6 NT occurrences. The grammatical construction phialas chrysas gemousas thymiamaton is exactly replicated at Rev 15:7 with contents changed to wrath (tou thymou tou Theou). This is the vessel transformation arc's origin point.

**Cross-references:** Ps 141:2 ("Let my prayer be set forth before thee as incense"); Exod 30:1-10 (golden incense altar; HP); Lev 16:12-13 (incense cloud before the mercy seat at DOA).

**Relationship to other evidence:** This is the intercession phase of the vessel transformation arc (5:8 prayers/incense → 15:7 wrath). It establishes the HP function (incense altar, prayer mediation) as the mode of ministry from the throne room scene through the seal and trumpet sequences. The reversal to judgment (15:7) marks the transition from HP-intercession to MHP-judgment phase.

### Rev 6:9
**Context:** Fifth seal; martyred souls are seen under the altar.

**Direct statement:** ὑποκάτω τοῦ θυσιαστηρίου (under the altar, G5270 + G2379). No chrysoun (golden) qualifier. The souls are those slain for the word of God. They cry "How long?" for vindication.

**Original language:** The critical syntactic markers: (1) hypokatō + genitive = under/below the altar; (2) no chrysoun qualifier = not the golden incense altar. Lev 4:7 requires that blood of the sin offering be poured at the base (literally under the horns) of the bronze altar of burnt offering. The martyrs beneath the altar recapitulate the animal sacrifice position at the OC bronze altar — their blood echoes both Abel's blood (Gen 4:10, crying from the ground) and the sphagmenon (slain, same root) of the Lamb (5:6).

**Cross-references:** Exod 27:1-8 (bronze altar, outer court); Lev 4:7 (blood at base of altar); Gen 4:10 (Abel's blood crying from the ground); Heb 12:24 (blood of Jesus that speaks better than Abel's).

**Relationship to other evidence:** This is the first new sanctuary element after the establishing scene of Rev 4-5. It is an outer court element (bronze altar = OC). This is the starting point of the sequential OC→HP→MHP progression. The altar vindication arc begins here: ekdikeis present tense (6:10 — still waiting) → 16:7 (altar affirms) → 19:2 (exedikēsen, completed).

### Rev 6:12-17 (Supporting)
**Context:** Sixth seal — cosmic signs; the great day of wrath arrives.

**Direct statement:** The sixth seal climaxes in universal terror: "the great day of his wrath is come; and who shall be able to stand?"

**Cross-references:** Joel 2:31-32; Isa 2:19; Mal 4:1. The cosmic-unraveling imagery marks an eschatological boundary.

**Relationship to other evidence:** No sanctuary furniture named at the sixth seal. The progression from 6:9 (OC bronze altar) to the seven trumpets (HP golden altar) passes through the cosmic upheaval of the sixth seal, confirming that the sanctuary-element sequence is not simply a furniture catalog but a structural narrative.

### Rev 7:15
**Context:** The great multitude from the tribulation; they stand before the throne and serve in his naos day and night.

**Direct statement:** λατρεύουσιν αὐτῷ ἡμέρας καὶ νυκτός ἐν τῷ ναῷ αὐτοῦ (serve him day and night in his naos). God will σκηνώσει ἐπ' αὐτούς (tabernacle over them, G4637, future).

**Original language:** ἐν τῷ ναῷ αὐτοῦ = in his naos (inner sanctuary). The phrase "day and night" implies continuous access — not annual once-per-year entry. In the earthly type, the HP was entered daily (Heb 9:6 — priests went always into the first tabernacle); the MHP was entered only once yearly (Heb 9:7). Perpetual day-and-night ministry is more consistent with the HP continuous-ministry model than with the MHP's once-yearly exclusivity. This is the strongest counter-evidence to Bohr's MHP-exclusive claim. The context also adds skēnoō (G4637, first of the skēnē arc occurrences in the churches/seals section) — God's tabernacling over the redeemed anticipates 21:3.

**Cross-references:** Heb 9:6 (priests always in the first tabernacle); Isa 4:5-6 (divine tabernacling as protective covering); Rev 21:3 (skēnoō arc climax).

**Relationship to other evidence:** The naos here is best understood as the inner sanctuary generally — the continuous service before the throne. Bohr's MHP-exclusive claim would require that perpetual day-and-night ministry happens in the MHP, which contradicts the earthly typology where the MHP was accessed once yearly. The naos = inner sanctuary (HP + MHP) reading is more coherent here.

### Rev 8:1-2
**Context:** Seventh seal opens with half-hour silence; the seven trumpet angels receive their trumpets.

**Direct statement:** Silence in heaven (sige, G4602 — only 2 NT occurrences) for half an hour (hemiorion, G2256, NT hapax). The seven trumpet angels stand before God.

**Cross-references:** Hab 2:20 (LORD in his holy temple, all earth silent); Zeph 1:7 (silence before the LORD, day of the LORD is at hand); Zech 2:13 (hasah before the LORD).

**Relationship to other evidence:** The silence itself is a sanctuary-associated phenomenon. All three OT background texts link cosmic silence to divine sanctuary presence. The half-hour silence positions the HP incense prelude (8:3-5) as a solemn sanctuary act before the trumpets begin.

### Rev 8:3-5 (and combined Rev 8:1-5)
**Context:** An angel stands at the golden altar with a golden censer; incense with prayers ascends; then the censer is filled with altar fire and cast to earth.

**Direct statement:** Two distinct altar references in one pericope: (1) "stood at the altar" (epi tou thysiastēriou, epi + genitive = positional, the altar beside which the angel stands); (2) "upon the golden altar" (epi to thysiastērion to chrysoun, epi + accusative = directional, motion toward the golden altar). The smoke of incense with prayers ascends. Then the censer is reversed.

**Original language:** The Greek parsing confirms two epi constructions: ἐστάθη ἐπὶ τοῦ θυσιαστηρίου [gen] = positional (stood at the altar) and ἐπὶ τὸ θυσιαστήριον τὸ χρυσοῦν [acc] = directional (onto the golden altar). τὸ χρυσοῦν = the golden one (adjective, acc sg neu) — the chrysoun qualifier unmistakably marks the HP incense altar (Exod 30:3: overlaid with pure gold). λιβανωτὸν χρυσοῦν (golden censer, G3031) — both NT occurrences of G3031 are in Rev 8 (vv. 3 and 5). This extreme concentration (2/2 NT occurrences in one pericope) is itself a structural signal. Theophany at v.5: brontai + phonai + astrapai + seismos = 4 elements (IC077 Level 2).

**DOA parallel:** Lev 16:12-13 — the high priest takes a censer with burning coals from the altar before the LORD, hands full of incense, brings it within the veil, puts incense on the fire before the LORD, so the cloud of incense covers the mercy seat. AN022 (Strong confidence, 5-element DOA correspondence established in revs2-10).

**Cross-references:** Exod 30:1-10 (golden incense altar, HP); Lev 16:12-13 (DOA censer act); Rev 6:9 (OC bronze altar, contrasting unqualified form); Ps 141:2 (prayer as incense).

**Relationship to other evidence:** This is the HP element that follows the OC element (6:9). The chrysoun qualifier + epi + accusative = the most explicit HP identification through Greek syntax alone. The censer reversal (incense up, 8:3-4 → fire down, 8:5) prefigures the vessel transformation (prayers, 5:8 → wrath, 15:7) at the macro level. The DOA parallel here anticipates the fuller DOA typology at 15:5-8.

### Rev 9:13
**Context:** Sixth trumpet; a voice from the four horns of the golden altar commands judgment.

**Direct statement:** Voice from "the four horns of the golden altar which is before God" — the most explicit HP altar identification in the book.

**Original language:** Triple genitive chain: τοῦ θυσιαστηρίου τοῦ χρυσοῦ τοῦ ἐνώπιον τοῦ Θεοῦ — the altar + the golden one + the before-God one. Three stacked genitive qualifiers leave no ambiguity: (1) gold = HP material; (2) four horns = incense altar horns (Exod 30:3); (3) enopion tou Theou = before God = inner sanctuary position. This triple genitive system is the grammatical high point of altar identification in Revelation.

**Cross-references:** Exod 30:3 (horns of the incense altar); Rev 8:3 (same altar, chrysoun qualifier); Rev 16:7 (altar speaks again, but unqualified = OC altar).

**Relationship to other evidence:** The triple genitive confirms that by the sixth trumpet, the HP golden altar is explicitly operational. This closes the trumpet sequence's HP phase. The voice from the horns of the HP altar releases the Euphrates judgment — the HP intercession function gives way to command.

### Rev 11:1-2
**Context:** Between the sixth and seventh trumpets; John is commanded to measure the naos, altar, and worshippers, but exclude the outer court.

**Direct statement:** All three sanctuary zones are named in one pericope: (1) τὸν ναὸν τοῦ Θεοῦ (the naos of God, measured); (2) τὸ θυσιαστήριον (the altar, measured); (3) τοὺς προσκυνοῦντας ἐν αὐτῷ (worshippers, measured); (4) τὴν αὐλὴν τὴν ἔξωθεν τοῦ ναοῦ (the court outside the naos, G833, excluded).

**Original language:** G833 (the outer court term) = court/courtyard — the only word for outer court in Revelation. The phrase τὴν αὐλὴν τὴν ἔξωθεν τοῦ ναοῦ (the court outside the naos) explicitly marks the spatial distinction: naos = inner; the outer court term = outer, excluded. This is the only passage in the NT where G833 clearly means the outer court of the temple (as opposed to a courtyard/palace). The distinction confirms: naos ≠ outer court, the outer court term ≠ inner sanctuary.

**Cross-references:** Exod 27:9 (the outer court/aulē of the tabernacle); Ezek 40:17-23 (the outer court of the visionary temple).

**Relationship to other evidence:** The aulē/naos contrast here directly answers the naos-compartment question: naos never refers to the outer court. The aulē is explicitly excluded from measurement and given to the Gentiles — it is a zone of vulnerability and trampling, while the naos is a zone of divine protection. This gives Bohr's claim a partial foundation: naos in Revelation at minimum excludes the outer court and refers only to the inner sanctuary. But the verse does not force naos = MHP only, since both the altar (which could be HP) and the naos are measured together.

### Rev 11:15-19 (including Rev 11:19 as primary)
**Context:** Seventh trumpet — kingdom announcement, wrath declared, judgment of the dead, and the climactic opening of the naos with the ark visible.

**Direct statement:** "And the temple of God was opened in heaven, and there was seen in his temple the ark of his testament: and there were lightnings, and voices, and thunderings, and an earthquake, and great hail."

**Original language:** ἠνοίγη ὁ ναὸς (aor pass ind, agentless divine passive = "was opened," no human agent). ὤφθη ἡ κιβωτός (aor pass ind, agentless = "was seen"). κιβωτὸς τῆς διαθήκης αὐτοῦ (G2787 kibotos, only Revelation occurrence) = the ark of the covenant. Heb 9:4 explicitly places the kibotos tēs diathēkēs in the MHP (after the second veil). Therefore: naos opened + ark visible = MHP access. This is the most unambiguous MHP marker in the book. Theophany: astrapai + phonai + brontai + seismos + chalaza megalē = 5 elements (IC077 Level 3). The addition of chalaza (G5464, great hail) marks the highest point yet in the formula's escalation.

**Janus function:** Rev 11:19 closes the trumpet sequence and simultaneously opens toward the bowl prelude (15:5). The same naos-opening vocabulary appears at both points (anoigō + naos). The two verses form the transition bracket for the MHP phase.

**Cross-references:** Exod 25:10-22 (ark design; MHP placement); Heb 9:4 (ark explicitly in MHP); Lev 16:2 (access to MHP restricted to DOA); Rev 15:5 (naos of testimony opened = matching vocabulary).

**Relationship to other evidence:** This is the structural hinge from HP to MHP. The 5-element theophany (IC077 Level 3) confirms a major structural transition. The ark's appearance is the MHP furniture revelation that complements the HP furniture (lampstand, golden altar) in earlier sections. Prior study revs2-12 cataloged this as a Janus verse; the present study confirms it with the theophany formula data.

### Rev 12:12 (Supporting)
**Context:** Heaven and its inhabitants rejoice; earth is warned of the devil's wrath.

**Direct statement:** Those "dwelling" (skēnountes, G4637 participle) in heaven rejoice.

**Original language:** G4637 (divine-dwelling participle) — fourth NT use of this word (after John 1:14, Rev 7:15, Rev 13:6). The heavenly dwellers are those who tabernacle in the sanctuary context.

**Relationship to other evidence:** Part of the skēnē/skēnoō arc. The beastly polemic of 13:6 targets these same "skēnoō-ing" ones and God's skēnē itself, establishing the sanctuary as the contested space throughout the cosmic war narrative.

### Rev 13:6
**Context:** The beast blasphemes God, his naos/skēnē, and those dwelling in heaven.

**Direct statement:** βλασφημῆσαι τὸ ὄνομα αὐτοῦ καὶ τὴν σκηνὴν αὐτοῦ, τοὺς ἐν τῷ οὐρανῷ σκηνοῦντας — to blaspheme God's name and his tabernacle (G4633) and those who tabernacle in heaven (G4637).

**Original language:** Some MSS read "his name and his naos, even his tabernacle" — coordinating naos and skēnē. The majority text reads skēnē autou (his tabernacle), making the blasphemy target the divine dwelling/tabernacling presence rather than the structural building. G4637 (fourth use): those in heaven who participate in the divine dwelling are also targeted.

**Cross-references:** Rev 7:15 (God tabernacles over the redeemed); Rev 15:5 (naos tēs skēnēs tou martyriou); Rev 21:3 (skēnē of God with men).

**Relationship to other evidence:** The beast's anti-sanctuary program mirrors Satan's original rebellion. The sanctuary is the seat of divine authority, making it the target of the counterfeit system. The skēnē arc passes through this verse as the hostile middle (the beast attacks the tabernacle) before reaching the triumphant conclusion (21:3: the tabernacle of God is with men).

### Rev 14:15, 14:17, 14:18
**Context:** Harvest sequence — three angels: one exits the naos (grain harvest command), one exits the naos (vintage harvest), one exits from the altar with fire authority.

**Direct statement:** Two angels exit from the naos (14:15: ἐκ τοῦ ναοῦ; 14:17: ἐκ τοῦ ναοῦ τοῦ ἐν τῷ οὐρανῷ). One angel exits from the altar (14:18: ἐκ τοῦ θυσιαστηρίου) with fire authority.

**Original language:** The naos here is the command center from which judgment angels emerge. No compartment qualifier (HP vs MHP) is given in these verses. The altar angel at 14:18 has "authority over fire" (ἐξουσίαν ἐπὶ τοῦ πυρός) — fire authority is associated with the bronze altar (Lev 6:12-13, perpetual fire on the altar). No chrysoun qualifier = OC bronze altar, consistent with 6:9 and 8:5 (fire cast from the same altar).

**Cross-references:** Rev 8:5 (fire from the altar cast to earth); Lev 6:12 (perpetual fire on the burnt offering altar = OC).

**Relationship to other evidence:** The naos as judicial command center (14:15, 14:17) is consistent with 16:1 (voice from naos commands the bowls). The altar with fire authority (14:18) continues the OC altar thread that began at 6:9. Three sanctuary agents (2 from naos + 1 from altar) give orders in the harvest sequence — the sanctuary is the origin of both harvest commands.

### Rev 15:1-4 (including Rev 15:2)
**Context:** Seven angels with last plagues; the sea of glass mingled with fire; victors stand on the sea singing.

**Direct statement:** "I saw as it were a sea of glass mingled with fire" (θάλασσα ὑαλίνη μεμιγμένη πυρί, G5193 hualinos). Victors stand on it having harps. They sing the song of Moses and the Lamb.

**Original language:** The sea of glass first appeared at 4:6 (like crystal, transparent, serene). Here it is "mingled with fire" (memigmenē pyri) — the fire of judgment is now present in the same element. The victors stand on the sea (epi + accusative = standing on top of) having overcome the beast. This echoes Exod 15:1 (Moses and Israel singing on the far shore after the Egyptian army was swallowed by the sea). The sea of glass has transformed from a purification surface (laver typology) to a victory platform.

**Cross-references:** Rev 4:6 (sea of glass, crystal, before the throne); Exod 30:17-21 (bronze laver, outer court); 1 Kgs 7:23-26 (bronze sea, outer court); Exod 15:1-3 (song of Moses at the sea).

**Relationship to other evidence:** The fire added to the sea at 15:2 may signal the transition from the intercession phase (sea = laver, purification) to the judgment phase (sea = fire). The victors standing ON the sea suggests they have passed through it (purity demonstrated) and stand above it in victory. The OC laver typology is maintained, but its function is transformed from ritual washing to eschatological vindication.

### Rev 15:5-8
**Context:** DOA structural hinge — the naos of the tabernacle of testimony is opened; bowl angels emerge; the naos is filled with smoke and sealed shut.

**Direct statement:** Five DOA indicators cluster in four verses: (1) naos tēs skēnēs tou martyriou opened (15:5); (2) seven angels clothed in pure linen with golden girdles (15:6); (3) seven golden bowls full of the wrath of God distributed (15:7); (4) naos filled with smoke from God's glory and power (15:8); (5) no man able to enter until the seven plagues are completed (15:8).

**Original language:** v.5: ὁ ναὸς τῆς σκηνῆς τοῦ μαρτυρίου (triple genitive, unique NT construction). naos (N-NSM, inner sanctuary) + tēs skēnēs (N-GSF, of the tabernacle) + tou martyriou (N-GSN, of the testimony) = the inner sanctuary of the tabernacle of the testimony. The word martyrion links to the wilderness tabernacle (LXX: skēnē tou martyriou = tent of the testimony/meeting = the tabernacle). v.6: ἐνδεδυμένοι λινοῦν καθαρὸν λαμπρόν (clothed in pure bright linen; perfect passive = established state) + χρυσᾶς ζώνας (golden girdles) = Lev 16:4 DOA vestments (white linen coat, linen girdle, linen breeches, linen turban). v.8: ἐγεμίσθη ὁ ναὸς καπνοῦ (aor pass = naos was filled with smoke; G2586 kapnos) + ἐκ τῆς δόξης τοῦ Θεοῦ (from the glory of God) — smoke from glory + power. οὐδεὶς ἐδύνατο εἰσελθεῖν εἰς τὸν ναόν (imperfect = ongoing inability to enter) ἄχρι τελεσθῶσιν (until they should be completed, aor pass subj) = temporal bound. G2586 kapnos: 13 NT occurrences; 12 are in Revelation.

**DOA parallels with Lev 16:** (a) v.5: naos tēs skēnēs tou martyriou ↔ Lev 16's "ohel moed" (the tabernacle of the congregation/testimony) — same sanctuary; (b) v.6: linen garments ↔ Lev 16:4 (special linen DOA vestments); (c) v.8 smoke fills naos ↔ Lev 16:13 (cloud of incense covers mercy seat); (d) v.8 no one can enter ↔ Lev 16:17 ("no man shall be in the tent of meeting"); (e) v.8 until the plagues are completed ↔ Lev 16:17 ("until he come out") — temporal bound on exclusion.

**Cross-references:** Lev 16:2-17 (DOA ritual); Exod 40:34-35 (cloud fills tabernacle, Moses excluded); 1 Kgs 8:10-11 (cloud fills temple, priests excluded); Isa 6:4 (house filled with smoke at seraphim's cry); Rev 11:19 (naos opened, ark visible — the preceding MHP-access event).

**Relationship to other evidence:** This is the structural centerpiece of the sanctuary progression. Five independent indicators all converge on a single four-verse passage. No alternative OT source accounts for all five simultaneously: Exod 40:34-35 lacks the temporal bound; 1 Kgs 8:10-11 lacks the temporal bound and is an inauguration; Isa 6:4 lacks the exclusion clause (Isaiah remains). Only Lev 16 accounts for all five: the tabernacle of the testimony, linen vestments, smoke/cloud, exclusion, and temporal limit ("until he come out"). The access-reversal (4-5: open access → 15:8: sealed) proves this is not an inauguration but a DOA hinge.

### Rev 16:1
**Context:** A great voice from the naos commands the seven bowl angels.

**Direct statement:** φωνῆς μεγάλης ἐκ τοῦ ναοῦ — a great voice from the naos commands: "Pour out the vials of the wrath of God upon the earth."

**Original language:** ἐκ τοῦ ναοῦ = from within the inner sanctuary. The naos access exclusion (15:8) is in force — no man can enter — yet commands issue from within. God himself, inaccessible to human approach during the DOA judgment, is the source of the bowl commands.

## Patterns Identified

### Pattern 1: The Three-Zone Vocabulary System
Revelation uses three sanctuary-zone terms, never confusing them:
- naos (G3485, 46 NT occurrences) = inner sanctuary (HP + MHP); 16 occurrences in Revelation; 0 in non-Revelation NT books when used for the inner shrine; hieron (G2411, 71 NT occurrences) = 0 in Revelation
- The outer court term (G833, 12 NT occurrences) = 1 in Revelation (11:2) = outer court, explicitly excluded
- The altar term (G2379, 23 NT occurrences) = functions as the HP/OC distinguisher through the chrysoun qualifier

John used hieron 11 times in his Gospel (outer courts, public areas). In Revelation he uses it zero times and naos exclusively. This is a deliberate lexical shift that restricts every "temple" reference in the book to the inner sanctuary zone.

### Pattern 2: The Two-Altar Grammar
The chrysoun (golden) qualifier separates the two altars with grammatical precision:
- Without chrysoun = OC bronze altar (Rev 6:9, 14:18, 16:7)
- With chrysoun = HP golden altar (Rev 8:3 explicit; Rev 9:13 triple genitive)
- Prepositional case confirms: epi + accusative = motion toward golden altar (8:3); hypokatō + genitive = under/below bronze altar (6:9)

### Pattern 3: The Compartmental Progression
The sequential emergence of sanctuary elements:
- Rev 1:12-13: Lampstands (HP) — Revelation's opening sanctuary context
- Rev 4-5: All compartments simultaneously (establishing scene)
- Rev 6:9: Souls under OC bronze altar → first sequential element
- Rev 8:3, 9:13: HP golden altar → second sequential zone
- Rev 11:19: Ark seen when naos opened → MHP revealed
- Rev 15:5-8: DOA typology at naos of the tabernacle of testimony → MHP judgment phase
- Rev 21:22: No naos; God/Lamb ARE the naos → transcendence

### Pattern 4: The Theophany Escalation (IC077)
3 elements (4:5) → 4 elements (8:5) → 5 elements (11:19) → 5+ amplified (16:18-21). Each escalation point corresponds to a major sanctuary transition. This provides an independent structural index for the compartmental progression.

### Pattern 5: The Vessel Transformation Arc
phialas chrysas gemousas thymiamaton (5:8, prayers/intercession) → phialas chrysas gemousas tou thymou tou Theou (15:7, wrath/judgment). Same construction, same vessels, inverted contents. Marks the transition from HP intercessory ministry to MHP judgment execution.

### Pattern 6: The Skēnē/Skēnoō Arc
John 1:14 (Christ tabernacled among us) → Rev 7:15 (God will tabernacle over them) → Rev 12:12 (those tabernacling in heaven) → Rev 13:6 (beast attacks the tabernacle) → Rev 15:5 (naos of the tabernacle of testimony) → Rev 21:3 (tabernacle of God with men). The arc moves from incarnate tabernacling through conflict to eschatological fulfillment.

### Pattern 7: The Altar Vindication Arc
6:10 (ekdikeis, present — "when will you avenge?") → 16:7 (altar affirms righteousness of judgment) → 19:2 (exedikēsen, aorist — vindication complete). Three-point arc from OC altar (6:9) through bowls (DOA phase) to resolution.

## Word Study Integration

**G3485 naos (46 NT occurrences):** In Revelation, naos = inner sanctuary generally (HP + MHP zone), never the outer court. The outer court uses G833 exclusively. The 16 Revelation occurrences span all contexts: general inner-sanctuary service (7:15), command-center function (14:15, 14:17, 16:1), MHP-access events (11:19, 15:5), DOA-sealed-naos (15:8), and transcendence (21:22). Bohr's claim that all 16 refer specifically to the MHP is supported for passages where MHP furniture (ark) or DOA indicators appear, but Rev 7:15 (perpetual day-and-night service, more consistent with HP continuous ministry per Heb 9:6) and Rev 3:12 (pillar installed in the naos, without compartment marker) suggest naos operates as the general inner-sanctuary term.

**G2411 hieron (71 NT occurrences, 0 in Revelation):** The deliberate exclusion of hieron from Revelation restricts all sanctuary references to the inner shrine. This is the most fundamental lexical finding of the study.

**G2379 (23 NT occurrences):** The two-altar grammar is embedded in the word's usage patterns. Its 8 Revelation occurrences divide cleanly by compartment through the chrysoun qualifier.

**G3087 lychnia (12 NT occurrences):** 6 of 12 are in Revelation. The lampstand = HP furniture through the Exod 25:31-40 anchor. Its appearance in the opening scene (1:12-13) establishes the HP as Revelation's starting sanctuary zone.

**G3031 libanotos (2 NT occurrences):** Both in Rev 8 (vv. 3 and 5). This extreme concentration — 100% of NT occurrences in a single four-verse pericope — marks the DOA censer scene as a uniquely weighted structural moment.

**G5357 phiale (12 NT occurrences):** All 12 in Revelation. The vessel transformation arc (5:8 → 15:7) is embedded in a word that Revelation owns entirely. No other NT book uses the word at all — the bowls of incense/wrath are Revelation's unique symbol.

**G2586 kapnos (13 NT occurrences):** 12 of 13 are in Revelation. Smoke in Revelation has dual function: divine presence/glory (15:8: ek tēs doxēs tou Theou = from the glory of God) and judgment/destruction (9:2-3: smoke of the pit darkens the sun; 19:3: smoke ascends forever from Babylon). The smoke of 15:8 draws on the divine-presence function (from God's glory), paralleling Exod 40:34-35 and Lev 16:13, while also marking the judgment-completion phase.

**G5068 tetragonos (1 NT occurrence):** NT hapax at Rev 21:16. The cube designation for New Jerusalem confirms the MHP typology: the only OT sanctuary feature described with perfectly equal three-dimensional measurements is the MHP (1 Kgs 6:20: 20 × 20 × 20).

**G4637 (5 NT occurrences):** The tabernacle-dwelling arc spans John 1:14 → Rev 7:15 → 12:12 → 13:6 → 21:3. All five occurrences are theological landmarks in the divine-dwelling-with-humanity narrative.

**G5132 trapeza (15 NT occurrences):** 0 in Revelation. The showbread table's total absence is confirmed. G5132 appears in the Gospels (money-changers' tables; the table of the Last Supper) and Paul (table of demons; table of the Lord in 1 Cor 10:21). Never in Revelation.

**Heb 9:6-8 → the two-phase progression:** The HP continuous/MHP annual structure of the earthly sanctuary provides the typological grid for the seal-trumpet phase (HP intercession) and the bowl-judgment phase (DOA/MHP).

## Difficult or Complicating Passages

### Difficulty 1: Rev 7:15 and Bohr's MHP-Exclusive Claim
Bohr argues all 16 Revelation naos occurrences refer exclusively to the MHP. The strongest counter-case is Rev 7:15: "serve him day and night in his naos." The earthly type forbade perpetual day-and-night access to the MHP (once yearly only, Heb 9:7). If naos = MHP exclusively, then the great multitude serves in the MHP perpetually — a pattern the earthly type never anticipated. Alternatively, the heavenly MHP operates without the typological limitations of the earthly (the veil is already removed at the cross, Heb 10:19-20). The more natural reading: naos = inner sanctuary generally at 7:15; the MHP-specific contexts (11:19, 15:5-8) are marked by additional MHP indicators (ark, DOA vocabulary). The conclusion: naos = inner sanctuary, with MHP-specificity indicated by contextual furniture and ritual elements.

### Difficulty 2: The Sea of Glass — OC or Inner Sanctuary?
The laver was in the outer court (Exod 30:18-21; 40:7). The sea of glass is "before the throne" in the inner sanctuary context. Revelation appears to have repositioned the laver-typology element from the outer court to the inner sanctuary zone. This may reflect: (a) the throne room's all-compartment establishing character (all elements, including OC, are present before the sequential progression); (b) a different OT background — Ezekiel's crystal firmament (1:22) beneath the divine throne, not the earthly laver; (c) the sea of glass as a typological composite that draws on both traditions without belonging strictly to one compartment. The compartment question for the sea of glass may be unanswerable — it functions like the laver but sits in the throne room context. The fire added at 15:2 suggests transformation from purification-function to judgment-fire, consistent with the transition to the DOA/bowl phase.

### Difficulty 3: The Showbread Gap
The table of showbread (Exod 25:23-30; Heb 9:2) has no counterpart in Revelation. The lampstand (HP), golden altar (HP), and bronze altar (OC) all appear. The ark (MHP) appears. Only the showbread table is absent. Forced identifications (seals = showbread; Rev 19:9 = marriage supper as bread-substitute) have no textual support. The gap is real. Possible explanations: (a) the showbread table's function (bread of presence, 12 loaves renewed weekly) is transcended in the New Jerusalem where God himself is present face-to-face; (b) John's sanctuary catalog emphasizes altar/incense (prayer-and-judgment) and lampstand (witness/church) functions over the showbread (priestly sustenance) function. The absence is noted but not resolved by the text.

### Difficulty 4: Rev 11:19 vs. Rev 15:5 as the MHP-Entry Point
Two verses mark the naos opening with MHP significance: 11:19 (ark visible = MHP furniture) and 15:5 (naos of the tabernacle of testimony opened = DOA initiation). Which is the structural MHP-entry marker? Answer: both, at different levels. Rev 11:19 is the Janus verse that reveals MHP access is available — the ark is seen, the way into the holiest is open. Rev 15:5 is the DOA-initiation verse that formally begins the antitypical Day of Atonement — the sanctuary is explicitly identified as the tabernacle of testimony and the bowl angels emerge. Rev 11:19 establishes the MHP as accessible; Rev 15:5-8 describes the DOA process occurring within it. They are sequential stages, not alternatives.

## Preliminary Synthesis

The compartmental progression OC → HP → MHP holds with high confidence across Revelation's judgment sequences. It is supported by seven independent lines of evidence: (1) compartmental distribution of sanctuary elements, (2) vessel transformation arc, (3) theophany formula escalation, (4) fraction escalation (1/4 → 1/3 → total), (5) altar vindication arc, (6) DOA mapping, and (7) feast calendar mapping. The progression does not break down — it simply operates at two levels simultaneously: the strict compartmental sequence of sanctuary elements, and the septet-introduction pattern (each sequence introduced by a sanctuary scene progressing from HP to all compartments to HP to MHP). The naos/hieron distinction is the lexical foundation; the DOA typology at Rev 15:5-8 is the structural centerpiece; and the New Jerusalem as cosmic MHP (Rev 21:16, 22) is the typological endpoint. The showbread gap and the sea-of-glass compartment question are genuine open issues that the text does not resolve with certainty.
