## Question
How does sanctuary/temple typology run through the entire book of Revelation? Trace every sanctuary element, identify its OT compartment (Exod 25-40), and determine whether the progression outer court → Holy Place → Most Holy Place correlates with the judgment sequences. How does DOA typology (Lev 16) function as a structural marker?

## Prior Research Summary
Prior revs2 studies (03, 09, 10, 11, 12, 13) and dedicated studies sanc-28 and sanc-29 have established the core framework: naos (G3485) 16x in Revelation, hieron (G2411) 0x — a deliberate lexical choice. Compartmental progression documented: OC (6:9 thysiastērion unqualified) → HP (8:3 thysiastēriou tou chrysou + golden altar + incense) → MHP (11:19 kibotos, 15:5-8 tabernacle of testimony). Five DOA indicators at 15:5-8 (triple-genitive 15:5; linen garments 15:6; smoke 15:8; access exclusion 15:8; 'until' clause 15:8). Vessel transformation: phiale with prayers (5:8) → phiale with wrath (15:7). Altar vindication arc: 6:10 → 16:7 → 19:2. Theophany escalation 3→4→5→5+ at 4:5/8:5/11:19/16:18. Feast calendar: Trumpets→DOA→Tabernacles. Showbread table gap confirmed real. New Jerusalem cube = MHP typology (21:16, 22; 1 Kgs 6:20). Exo 25:8 → Rev 21:3 → 21:22 theological spine. PRIMARY OPEN QUESTION: Does naos carry compartmental specificity (Bohr: naos = MHP exclusively) or does naos mean 'inner sanctuary' generally with compartmental progression emerging from context and associated elements?

### Focus Areas

1. **The naos/hieron distinction and what naos means in Revelation:**
The naos/hieron distinction governs the entire study. READ G3485-naos.md and G2411-hieron.md from the library — these canonical entries will have occurrence counts, translation distributions, and the lexical argument. The key claim to verify: John used hieron 11x in his Gospel (referring to Herod's temple complex where crowds gather, money-changers work, etc.) but uses it zero times in Revelation. Instead, he uses naos exclusively, which in Greek (Josephus, Philo, Trench) refers to the inner sanctuary building containing the HP and MHP. This means every naos reference in Revelation points to the inner shrine — never the courts or outer areas. Run greek_parser.py on Rev 11:1-2 to confirm aulē (G833) is used for the outer court (excluded from measurement), while naos is used for the measured inner sanctuary. Then catalog all 16 naos occurrences: Rev 3:12, 7:15, 11:1, 11:2, 11:19, 13:6, 14:15, 14:17, 15:5, 15:6, 15:8 (×2), 16:1, 16:17, 21:22 (×2). For each: what activity is happening, who enters/exits, what is accessed? Bohr claims naos = MHP exclusively in all 16 occurrences — test this against contexts like Rev 7:15 (perpetual service before the throne, which is more naturally the entire sanctuary) and Rev 13:6 (blasphemy against God's naos and skēnēn, where skēnē is added as a second term). Note also Rev 21:22 where God and Lamb ARE the naos — if naos = MHP only, this says God/Lamb are the MHP, which may actually be consistent with the cube typology at 21:16.
**Verses:** Rev 3:12, Rev 7:15, Rev 11:1-2, Rev 11:19, Rev 13:6, Rev 14:15, Rev 14:17, Rev 15:5, Rev 15:6, Rev 15:8, Rev 16:1, Rev 16:17, Rev 21:22, Heb 9:1-5

2. **The two-altar problem: distinguishing the bronze altar (OC) from the golden altar (HP):**
The two-altar problem is the crux of the OC vs HP compartment distinction. READ G2379-thysiastērion.md — the library entry should document all 8 Revelation occurrences with their syntactic context. Prior studies (revs2-09, revs2-10) resolved the syntax: at 6:9, hypokatō tou thysiastēriou places souls UNDER the altar (Lev 4:7 — blood of sin offering poured at base of bronze altar = Outer Court); at 8:3, epi to thysiastērion to chrysoun with epi + accusative = motion toward the golden altar = Holy Place. The triple genitive at 9:13 (thysiastēriou tou chrysou tou enopion tou Theou) is the most explicit identification in the book. Run greek_parser.py on each of these verses to copy the exact Greek forms and confirm the prepositional constructions. Note that Rev 14:18 says 'another angel who had authority over fire' — this angel comes from the thysiastērion, consistent with the fire-from-the-altar motif of Rev 8:5 (the same vessel that carried incense/prayers casts fire to earth). The thysiastērion at 16:7 that 'speaks' is the voice of the altar vindication arc (6:10 ekdikeis → 16:7 ekdikeo → 19:2 exedikēsen). Verify whether the syntax in 16:7 identifies which altar is speaking — the arc context (starting at 6:9 souls under the OC altar) suggests the bronze altar, though 9:13 had the HP golden altar speaking.

7. **The sea of glass — laver typology and compartment assignment:**
READ G5193-hualinos.md for the 3 NT occurrences (Rev 4:6; 15:2; 21:18 — the city like pure gold, clear as glass). The sea of glass in Rev 4:6 is described as 'before the throne' (enōpion tou thronou). In the earthly tabernacle, the laver stood between the tent of meeting and the altar (Exod 40:7 — 'between the tent of meeting and the altar'), which locates it in the outer court. The bronze sea in Solomon's temple was also in the outer court (1 Kgs 7:39). However, in Revelation the sea of glass is in the inner sanctuary context — before the throne, surrounded by the four living creatures (= cherubim = MHP). This suggests Revelation may have 'moved' the laver typologically, or that the sea of glass draws on Ezek 1:22 (raqia hakkatel, the crystal firmament under the divine throne) rather than the outer-court laver. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-ot on Rev 4:6 to identify which OT passage the parallel engine finds strongest. The Chaffart source (web research) identified the functional parallel (washing/purity) but not the compartmental placement. Rev 15:2 (sea mingled with fire) is where the overcomers stand having conquered the beast — standing on the glassy sea as the bowls are poured. Compare with Exod 15:1 (Moses and Israel singing on the far shore after the sea consumed the Egyptian army). Note: whatever compartment the sea of glass occupies, it does NOT use naos or aule — it is its own imagery category.
