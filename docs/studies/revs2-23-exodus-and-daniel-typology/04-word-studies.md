# Word Studies

## Question
How does Revelation draw on Exodus plague typology in the trumpet and bowl sequences, how does Rev 13:2 composite the four Danielic beasts in reverse order with Dan 7 as the explicit decoder, and how does Dan 7:9-10 serve as the structural template for the great white throne judgment in Rev 20:11-13?

### G1668 — ἕλκος (helkos)

**Source:** `G1668-hélkos.md`

**Occurrences:** 3

Ulcer/sore. NT: 3 occurrences — Luk 16:21 (Lazarus), Rev 16:2 (Bowl 1), Rev 16:11 (referenced again). LXX primary bridge H7822 shechin (11 co-occ, PMI 10.64) — the sixth plague boils of Exo 9:9-11. Rev 16:2 helkos kakon kai poneron is the direct lexical echo of Exo 9:9 shechin parach = 'boil breaking forth.' Strongest single LXX vocabulary chain between bowls and plagues.

**Semantic categories:** bowl judgment plague, LXX plague 6 echo, Exodus typology

**Key verses:** Rev 16:2, Rev 16:11, Exo 9:9, Exo 9:10, Exo 9:11

**Relevance:** Bowl 1 / Plague 6 direct LXX bridge. The word helkos appears only in Revelation among all bowl descriptions with a direct Exodus precedent.

### G3173 — μέγας (megas)

**Source:** `G3173-megas.md`

Great/large/loud. 200 unique NT verses. Core vocabulary of Revelation's superlative register. stoma laloun megala (Rev 13:5) — megala (A-APN, accusative plural neuter object of laloun): the great things the beast speaks. thronon megan leukon (Rev 20:11) — megan (A-ASM, agrees with thronon masculine): the great white throne. Megala kai thaumasta (Rev 15:3) — megala (A-NPN, nominative neuter plural): great and marvelous works. The Aramaic equivalent in Dan 7:8 is millin rabberevan (great words) = Greek megala.

**Semantic categories:** stoma laloun megala, great white throne, superlative register, Dan 7 echo

**Key verses:** Rev 13:5, Rev 20:11, Rev 15:3, Rev 16:21, Dan 7:8

**Relevance:** megala in Rev 13:5 (stoma laloun megala) is the direct LXX echo of LXX Dan 7:8 stoma laloun megala — verbatim match. The word appears in four study-critical contexts: beast's great-speaking (13:5), great white throne (20:11), Song of Moses great works (15:3), great plague of hail (16:21).

### G2980 — λαλέω (laleo)

**Source:** `G2980-laleo.md`

**Occurrences:** 308

To speak/talk/utter. 271 NT unique verses. In Rev 13:5: laloun (V-PAP-NSN, present active participle nominative singular neuter) — agrees with stoma (neuter) as a participial predicate: 'a mouth speaking great things.' This is the critical parsing: the present tense (ongoing action) combined with nominative neuter agreement with stoma (not with a person) makes the mouth itself the subject of the speaking — the beast's very mouth has continuous great-speaking as its function. LXX Dan 7:8,20: stoma laloun megala (exactly the same construction). The Theodotion LXX renders Dan 7:8's little horn as having a stoma laloun megala — verbatim match to Rev 13:5.

**Semantic categories:** stoma laloun megala, Dan 7 echo, blasphemy, present active participle

**Key verses:** Rev 13:5, Rev 13:6, Dan 7:8, Dan 7:11, Dan 7:20

**Relevance:** laloun (present active participle nominative singular neuter, Rev 13:5) agrees with stoma neuter — 'mouth speaking.' The LXX Dan 7:8,20 stoma laloun megala is verbatim: same construction. This is the strongest single grammatical link between Daniel and Revelation.

### A4437 — מַלְכוּ (malkuw)

**Source:** `A4437-malkuw.md`

**Occurrences:** 43

Aramaic kingdom/dominion. 43 occurrences in Aramaic portions of OT (primarily Daniel). This is the explicit decoder word in Dan 7:23: cheyevata reviyayta = malkuw reviyayta ('the fourth beast = the fourth kingdom'). Also Dan 7:17 (malkin = kings, the beast-king equivalence). The Aramaic malkuw is the within-text hermeneutical key for reading Rev 13:2's composite beast as a composite of world kingdoms. LXX bridge: malkuw → G932 basileia (PMI 23.90, 41 co-occ). Dan 7:14 ('there was given him malkuw, all people shall serve him') → Rev 5:9; 7:9 (all nations). Dan 7:27 (malkuw given to saints) → Rev 20:4,6.

**Semantic categories:** Dan 7 decoder, beast = kingdom, Aramaic, corporate solidarity

**Key verses:** Dan 7:17, Dan 7:23, Dan 7:27, Dan 7:14, Rev 13:2

**Relevance:** Dan 7:17,23 provide the within-text decoder: beast = king/kingdom. This within-text authorial decoder is the basis for reading Rev 13:2's composite beast as a composite of Danielic kingdoms. malkuw → G932 basileia is the primary Aramaic-Greek bridge.

### H1818 — דָּם (dam)

**Source:** `H1818-dam.md`

**Occurrences:** 391

Hebrew blood. 295 OT unique verses. Primary LXX bridge to G129 haima (269 co-occ, PMI 6.2 (score 34.72) — highest single-word PMI score in the tool). Exodus P1 (Exod 7:17-21): kol-hamayim asher ba-yeor nehepku ledam (all the waters in the Nile were turned to blood). The haima vocabulary chain from Exodus P1 flows through: Rev 8:7 (T1: hail fire mingled with haima), Rev 8:8 (T2: sea became haima), Rev 16:3 (B2: sea became haima nekrou), Rev 16:4 (B3: rivers became haima). The blood theme is the most frequently recurring plague vocabulary in the trumpet-bowl sequence.

**Semantic categories:** Plague 1 blood, LXX bridge to haima, trumpet and bowl judgments, Exodus typology

**Key verses:** Exod 7:17, Exod 7:20, Exod 7:21, Rev 8:7, Rev 8:8, Rev 16:3, Rev 16:4

**Relevance:** The H1818 dam → G129 haima chain (PMI 6.2 (score 34.72)) is the strongest single LXX vocabulary bridge in the study. Blood vocabulary recurs across T1, T2, B2, and B3 — Plague 1 is the most extensively echoed of all ten Exodus plagues in Revelation.
