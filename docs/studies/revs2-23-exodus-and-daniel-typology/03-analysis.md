# Verse-by-Verse Analysis
# revs2-23: Exodus Plague Typology and Daniel's Beast Visions

---

## The Ten Exodus Plagues

### Plague 1 — Blood (Exod 7:14-25)
**Context:** YHWH's first judgment on Egypt: the Nile turns to blood, fish die, Egyptians cannot drink. This is the opening plague and sets the pattern for all that follow.
**Direct statement:** Water-to-blood is the primary Exodus plague vocabulary entering Revelation. The LXX renders H1818 dam as G129 haima (PMI 34.72 — the highest single-word PMI bridge in the study data).
**Original language:** Hebrew dam (H1818) → Greek haima (G129). G129 occurs 99 times in the NT. Exod 7:17 "they shall be turned to blood" = LXX "kai strphetai eis haima."
**Cross-references:** Rev 8:7 (T1: hail, fire, blood), Rev 8:8-9 (T2: sea becomes haima), Rev 16:3-4 (B2-B3: sea and rivers become haima). The blood-water transformation is the most frequently echoed plague in the entire trumpet-bowl sequence.
**Relationship to other evidence:** Plague 1 generates the broadest Revelation echo — both trumpet and bowl sequences pick it up. The bowl treatment (B2: sea; B3: rivers and springs) extends the scope far beyond the original Nile targeting, demonstrating Revelation's universalizing tendency.

### Plague 2 — Frogs (Exod 8:1-15)
**Context:** Frogs cover all Egypt. LXX: H6854 tsephardea → G944 batrachos (13 co-occurrences, PMI 10.77). Perfect one-to-one correspondence — tsephardea has no other LXX translation.
**Direct statement:** The NT has exactly one occurrence of batrachos — Rev 16:13, Bowl 6. Three unclean spirits like frogs emerge from the mouths of the dragon, beast, and false prophet.
**Original language:** G944 batrachos is an NT hapax. The word's sole NT use as a deliberate Exodus allusion cannot be doubted — the word was available only from the LXX plague narrative, where it was fixed vocabulary.
**Cross-references:** Rev 16:13 is the only echo. No trumpet carries frog imagery.
**Relationship to other evidence:** Bowl 6 frog-spirits and trumpet 5's Goshen exemption (Rev 9:4) are the two clearest structural demonstrations of how Revelation transforms Exodus: the frogs shift from a physical pest to demonic spirits emerging from the mouths of the antitypical Pharaoh-trinity.

### Plague 3 — Lice/Gnats (Exod 8:16-19)
**Context:** Lice from dust. Magicians acknowledge "the finger of God." No Revelation counterpart.
**Direct statement:** No vocabulary chain to any trumpet or bowl.
**Relationship to other evidence:** Plague 3 is the first of three consecutive absent plagues (P3, P4, P5). The magicians' breakdown here (v.19) foreshadows how Revelation's false prophet can perform great signs (Rev 13:13) while the Plagues 3-5 simply drop from the typological repertoire. Their absence is principled: all three operate at domestic or agricultural scale rather than cosmic scale.

### Plague 4 — Flies (Exod 8:20-32)
**Context:** Flies sent on Egypt; Goshen explicitly exempted (Exod 8:22-23). This is the first plague with explicit divine severance language: "I will put a division between my people and thy people."
**Direct statement:** No fly vocabulary echoes in Revelation. But Exod 8:22-23 is the foundational Goshen text — the theological principle that structures both trumpet and bowl protective exemptions.
**Cross-references:** Rev 7:3 (seal before trumpets), Rev 9:4 (locusts cannot harm the sealed), Rev 16:2 (sores fall only on mark-bearers). The protection mechanism migrates from Exod 8:22-23 through these three passages.
**Relationship to other evidence:** Plague 4 contributes the Goshen principle to Revelation without contributing its specific plague content. The flies themselves are absent; the discrimination structure is everywhere.

### Plague 5 — Murrain on Cattle (Exod 9:1-7)
**Context:** Disease on Egyptian livestock; Israel's cattle untouched (Exod 9:4,6). Goshen applied to animals.
**Direct statement:** No Revelation echo. No vocabulary chain.
**Relationship to other evidence:** Like Plague 3 and Plague 4's flies, the cattle disease operates at agricultural/economic scale. Revelation's judgments target people, political powers, water systems, and the cosmos — not livestock. The three consecutive absent plagues (P3-P5) cluster at this agricultural register.

### Plague 6 — Boils (Exod 9:8-12)
**Context:** Ashes from the furnace produce boils on man and beast throughout Egypt. Magicians cannot stand before Moses because of their own boils.
**Direct statement:** H7822 shechin → G1668 helkos (11 co-occurrences, PMI 10.64). Rev 16:2 Bowl 1: helkos kakon kai poneron falls on those with the mark of the beast.
**Original language:** helkos has only 3 NT occurrences: Luke 16:21 (Lazarus), Rev 16:2, Rev 16:11. Its appearance at Bowl 1 is the strongest single LXX vocabulary chain between any individual plague and any individual bowl.
**Cross-references:** Rev 16:11 references "their sores" (helkon) still active during Bowl 5 — the bowl plagues accumulate rather than replace each other.
**Relationship to other evidence:** The boils fall on Egyptians; Bowl 1 falls on those with the mark of the beast. The anti-Goshen inversion is clear: in Exodus, the plague spared Israel in Goshen; in Revelation, the bowl spares those with God's seal (Rev 9:4) and strikes only those with the beast's mark. The principle of divine discrimination is preserved; its polarity (who is protected) depends on whose side you are on.

### Plague 7 — Hail/Fire (Exod 9:18-34)
**Context:** Hail mingled with fire — unprecedented in Egypt's history. Goshen explicitly exempt (Exod 9:26). H1259 barad → G5464 chalaza (21 co-occurrences, PMI 9.58).
**Direct statement:** Rev 8:7 Trumpet 1: chalaza + pyr + haima. Rev 16:21 Bowl 7: chalaza of talent-weight. Both echo Plague 7 via the same LXX word.
**Original language:** chalaza has 4 NT occurrences, all in Revelation (Rev 8:7; 11:19; 16:21 — three verses). Its exclusive concentration in Revelation marks every use as a deliberate Exodus allusion.
**Cross-references:** Exod 9:24 "hail, and fire mingled with the hail" = Rev 8:7 "hail and fire mingled with blood." Rev 16:21's "plague of the hail" (plege tes chalazas) is the only occurrence in Revelation where a bowl explicitly self-identifies as "the plague of" its content word — both G4127 and G5464 appear together.
**Relationship to other evidence:** Plague 7 is the only Exodus plague with both a trumpet AND a bowl echo (T1 and B7). The bowl treatment escalates to talent-weight hail; the trumpet treatment combines Plague 7 hail with Plague 1 blood. Both demonstrate Revelation's compositional technique: not mechanical reproduction but amplified synthesis.

### Plague 8 — Locusts (Exod 10:1-20)
**Context:** Locusts cover all Egypt and eat every herb. H697 arbeh → G200 akris (19 co-occurrences, PMI 9.52).
**Direct statement:** Rev 9:3-7 Trumpet 5: akris from the bottomless pit. Critical inversion: Exodus locusts ate vegetation (Exod 10:15); Trumpet 5 locusts are commanded NOT to harm vegetation but only those without God's seal (Rev 9:4).
**Original language:** akris has 4 NT occurrences: Matt 3:4 and Mark 1:6 (John the Baptist's diet), Rev 9:3 and 9:7 (Trumpet 5). The Revelation locusts are cosmically transformed — horse-shaped, crowned, with human faces, scorpion tails, lion teeth (Rev 9:7-9). Joel 1:4-7 and 2:4-10 mediate the transformation from agricultural pest to cosmic army.
**Cross-references:** Joel 1:6 (locusts with lion teeth) → Rev 9:8 (locusts with lion teeth). Joel 2:4 (horse appearance) → Rev 9:7 (locusts like battle horses). Joel is the prophetic lens through which Revelation re-reads Plague 8.
**Relationship to other evidence:** No bowl carries locust imagery. Plague 8 echoes only in the trumpet sequence. The Goshen principle appears in Trumpet 5 via Rev 9:4 — the seal functions as the Exodus Goshen severance. This is one of the most theologically rich transformations: the locust mandate itself is inverted (from "eat everything" to "harm only the unsealed"), demonstrating that Revelation's locusts are not natural but divine agents of discrimination.

### Plague 9 — Darkness (Exod 10:21-23)
**Context:** Three days of palpable darkness over all Egypt; Israel had light in their dwellings (Exod 10:23). LXX: skotia/skotos vocabulary.
**Direct statement:** Rev 8:12 Trumpet 4: one-third of sun, moon, and stars darkened. Rev 16:10 Bowl 5: beast's kingdom full of darkness.
**Original language:** LXX verb skotoo (G4654) and noun skotos (G4655) for the darkness. Rev 16:10 eskotomene (from skotoo) is the direct LXX echo.
**Cross-references:** Trumpet 4 is partial (to triton, one-third); Bowl 5 is total (no fraction, the beast's entire kingdom). Exod 10:23's Goshen light (Israel had light while Egypt was dark) is echoed structurally in Bowl 5 falling specifically on "the seat of the beast" — kingdom-targeted, as Plague 9 targeted Egypt specifically.
**Relationship to other evidence:** Plague 9 has both trumpet and bowl echoes. The trumpet treatment is mediated through Joel 2:10 (sun and moon dark, stars withdraw); the bowl treatment carries direct LXX vocabulary (skotoo). The Goshen light principle (Exod 10:23) is not explicitly repeated in Rev 16:10 — the text does not state God's people have light during Bowl 5. This is an honest complication: Bohr's claim that Bowl 5 extends the Goshen light pattern to God's people goes beyond what Rev 16:10-11 explicitly states.

### Plague 10 — Death of Firstborn (Exod 11:4-7; 12:29-30)
**Context:** The culminating plague: death of all firstborn in Egypt, from Pharaoh to the mill-maid to beasts. Exod 11:7: "the LORD doth put a difference between the Egyptians and Israel" — the most comprehensive theological articulation of the Goshen principle.
**Direct statement:** No Revelation trumpet or bowl carries direct LXX vocabulary from the firstborn-death narrative. Rev 18:8 ("death and mourning and famine in one day") is the closest conceptual echo — sudden death as judgment — but without shared vocabulary.
**Cross-references:** Passover blood on doorposts (Exod 12:7-13) is the household-level protection during P10 that prefigures Rev 7:3 (seal on foreheads) and Rev 13:16 (mark on forehead/hand). The protection-token migrates from household door to individual forehead.
**Relationship to other evidence:** Plague 10 is absent from both trumpet and bowl sequences by LXX vocabulary. Its theological contribution comes through the Passover institution (1 Cor 5:7 connects Passover Lamb to Christ) and through Rev 15:3's Song of Moses (which celebrated the victory culminating in P10's aftermath). The absence reinforces that Revelation's plague typology is selective, not mechanical.

## Song of Moses (Exod 15:1-11; Rev 15:2-4)

### Exod 15:1-11
**Context:** The victory song sung by Moses and Israel after crossing the Red Sea and Pharaoh's drowning. The climactic incomparability question: "Who is like unto thee, O LORD, among the gods?" (mi khamocha, Exod 15:11).
**Direct statement:** Rev 15:3 explicitly names this as "the song of Moses the servant of God, and the song of the Lamb" — the most direct programmatic statement in Revelation that the bowl sequence should be read through the Exodus lens.
**Cross-references:** Rev 13:4 "who is like unto the beast?" (tis homoios to therio) is the beast-parody of Exod 15:11's incomparability formula, established in revs2-19 as the mi khamocha inversion (VP313).
**Relationship to other evidence:** The Song of Moses in Rev 15:3 is not incidental positioning. The sequence mirrors Exod: Red Sea crossing (Exod 14) → Song of Moses (Exod 15) → wilderness plagues → Sinai. Revelation: sea of glass mingled with fire (Rev 15:2, transformed Red Sea) → Song of Moses (Rev 15:3) → bowl plagues (Rev 16). The structural echo of the Exodus narrative sequence is deliberate.

### Rev 15:2-4
**Context:** Victors standing on the sea of glass sing the Song of Moses and the Lamb before the seven bowls. adousin (present active indicative) — continuous singing.
**Direct statement:** "Great and marvellous are thy works, Lord God Almighty; just and true are thy ways, thou King of saints." Rev 15:4 "Who shall not fear thee?" echoes Exod 15:11's incomparability form while restoring the correct direction (God, not the beast, is incomparable).
**Cross-references:** pantokrator (G3841) appears here — the same title the beast's worshippers implicitly deny when they ask "who is like the beast?" (Rev 13:4). Deut 32:4 supplies "just and true are thy ways" — the song synthesizes Exod 15 and Deut 32.
**Relationship to other evidence:** Rev 15:3 is the explicit narrative anchor that frames the bowl sequence as antitypical Exodus. The positioning — immediately before the seven bowls are poured — is the strongest structural evidence that the bowls correspond to the Exodus plagues rather than the trumpets. The trumpets have no equivalent Song of Moses framing.

## The Trumpet Sequence

### Rev 8:7 — Trumpet 1 (Hail, Fire, Blood)
**Context:** chalaza + pyr + haima. Three Exodus elements combined: Plague 7 (chalaza/barad), Plague 1 (haima/dam), and fire from Exod 9:23.
**Direct statement:** The 1/3 fraction (to triton) appears here — trees and grass affected partially.
**Cross-references:** Exod 9:23-24 (hail + fire). The trumpet adds haima (blood) to the Exodus combination.
**Relationship to other evidence:** This is the trumpet most directly connected to an Exodus plague by LXX vocabulary. The addition of blood synthesizes two plagues (P1 and P7) in one trumpet judgment.

### Rev 8:8-9 — Trumpet 2 (Sea to Blood)
**Context:** Mountain of fire cast into sea; 1/3 of sea becomes haima; 1/3 of sea creatures die.
**Direct statement:** Plague 1 echo (haima) with Goshen principle absent — no protection stated for those at sea.
**Relationship to other evidence:** The scope is partial (1/3). Bowl 2 (Rev 16:3) will universalize: all the sea becomes blood of a dead man, every living soul dies. The trumpet is the warning; the bowl is the execution.

### Rev 8:10-11 — Trumpet 3 (Wormwood)
**Context:** Star named Wormwood poisons 1/3 of rivers and springs. Many die.
**Direct statement:** No Exodus plague vocabulary. Source tradition is Jer 9:13-15 (wormwood as covenant-curse judgment for unfaithfulness).
**Relationship to other evidence:** T3's non-Exodus origin is the clearest evidence that the trumpet sequence is not a mechanical Exodus replay. Revelation draws on multiple OT judgment traditions. The wormwood tradition (Jer 9:15; Lam 3:15; Amos 5:7) supplements the Exodus framework.

### Rev 8:12 — Trumpet 4 (Darkened Sun/Moon/Stars)
**Context:** to triton appears five times in this single verse — the highest concentration in the trumpet sequence. One-third of sun, moon, and stars darkened.
**Direct statement:** Plague 9 echo (darkness) but partial (1/3 vs. total in Exod 10:22, "thick darkness in all the land"). Joel 2:10 mediates: "the sun and the moon shall be dark, and the stars shall withdraw their shining."
**Relationship to other evidence:** T4 vs. B5 contrast is structurally significant. T4 darkens one-third; B5 (Rev 16:10) plunges the beast's entire kingdom into total darkness. The escalation from 1/3 to total mirrors the Exodus plague's own totality ("thick darkness," Exod 10:22).

### Rev 9:1-11 — Trumpet 5 (Locusts)
**Context:** akris (G200) from the bottomless pit. Key inversion: Exod locusts eat vegetation; these locusts are commanded NOT to harm vegetation (Rev 9:4) but only those without God's seal.
**Direct statement:** The Goshen principle operates here explicitly. Rev 9:4 names the criterion: "those men which have not the seal of God in their foreheads."
**Original language:** Joel 1:6 (lion teeth) and Joel 2:4 (horse appearance) mediate between the agricultural locusts of Exod 10 and Revelation's militarized cosmic army. The akris vocabulary chain (H697 arbeh → G200 akris, PMI 9.52) connects to Exodus; the military transformation comes through Joel.
**Relationship to other evidence:** This is the only Exodus plague whose Revelation counterpart explicitly inverts the Exodus mandate. The locusts in Exodus ate everything; these locusts are forbidden from eating anything. The inversion is the mechanism for expressing the Goshen principle in the trumpet sequence.

### Rev 9:13-19 — Trumpet 6 (Four Angels, 200 Million Cavalry)
**Context:** Four angels released at the Euphrates; 200 million cavalry with lion-headed horses.
**Direct statement:** No direct Exodus plague echo. The lion-heads (v.17) connect to Dan 7:4 and Rev 13:2 rather than to any Exodus plague.
**Relationship to other evidence:** Trumpet 6's lack of Exodus connection contributes to the count of 5/7 trumpets with Exodus parallels (T1, T2, T4, T5 have clear parallels; T3 is Jeremiah; T6 is non-Exodus; T7 is cosmic completion).

### Rev 9:20-21 — Retroactive Plague Label
**Context:** "The rest of the men which were not killed by these plagues yet repented not." plegais (G4127, dative plural) retroactively applies the plague label to the entire trumpet sequence.
**Direct statement:** The author himself identifies the trumpets as "plagues" — the Exodus typology is authorial intent, not modern scholarly inference.
**Cross-references:** Rev 11:6 two witnesses "smite the earth with all plagues (pasais tais pleges), as often as they will" — Moses/plague typology actively deployed in the prophetic witness narrative.
**Relationship to other evidence:** AN185 (TM350) established: this internal labeling is the textual basis for reading the trumpet sequence as intentionally Exodus-typological rather than coincidentally similar. Pharaoh's pattern of hardening (Exod 7:22; 8:19; 9:12; 9:34; 10:20) is exactly mirrored: each refusal follows the previous plague, escalating to the next.

## The Bowl Sequence

### Rev 16:2 — Bowl 1 (Helkos Sores)
**Context:** helkos kakon kai poneron on those with the mark of the beast.
**Direct statement:** H7822 shechin → G1668 helkos (PMI 10.64). Exact LXX vocabulary chain. The Goshen inversion: the plague falls on mark-bearers, not on God's people.
**Cross-references:** Exod 9:9-11. The magicians who could not stand before Moses at Plague 6 (Exod 9:11) correspond to those who chose the beast's mark — both groups face the same plague from which God's people are exempt.
**Relationship to other evidence:** Bowl 1 provides the clearest individual plague-bowl vocabulary match. Leithart's 3+7=10 proposal acknowledges that the bowls function as a discrete plague unit, with Bowl 1's helkos providing the anchor to Plague 6.

### Rev 16:3-7 — Bowls 2-3 (Blood Universalized)
**Context:** Bowl 2: entire sea becomes blood of a dead man, every living soul dies. Bowl 3: all rivers and springs become blood. Angel of waters declares lex talionis (v.6): they shed saints' blood, so receive blood.
**Direct statement:** haima (G129) appears in both bowls. No fraction qualifier — total scope.
**Cross-references:** Exod 7:17-21 (Nile blood, Plague 1). The scope expands: Nile → sea → all rivers and springs. Trumpet 2 had 1/3 of sea become blood; Bowl 2 is total. Trumpet 3 had 1/3 of rivers become wormwood; Bowl 3 has all rivers become blood.
**Relationship to other evidence:** Bowls 2-3 together complete what Trumpet 2-3 began partially. The totality matches Exodus scope: Exod 7:21 "blood throughout all the land of Egypt" — no fraction. The bowl sequence reproduces Exodus totality; the trumpet sequence does not.

### Rev 16:8-9 — Bowl 4 (Scorching Sun)
**Context:** Sun scorches men with fire. kaumatizo (G2739) — 4 NT occurrences, 2 in Revelation. Not in LXX; no Hebrew bridge. No Exodus plague equivalent.
**Direct statement:** The blasphemy-and-no-repentance refrain appears: "they blasphemed the name of God... and they repented not." plege appears here — self-referencing the bowls as a plague-series.
**Cross-references:** Mal 4:1 (the day that burns as an oven), Joel 2:30-31 (before the great day of the Lord). Bowl 4's tradition is Day-of-Lord fire, not Exodus.
**Relationship to other evidence:** Bowl 4 is the honest counter-evidence to any simple "bowls = Exodus plagues" formula. It inverts rather than echoes — Plague 9 was darkness (reduced sun); Bowl 4 is intensified sun. This structural inversion (T4 darkness → B5 beast-darkness / B4 scorching) suggests Revelation uses the Exodus template creatively, not mechanically. The bowl sequence is closer to Exodus than the trumpet sequence, but Bowl 4 prevents the relationship from being classified as "Strong."

### Rev 16:10-11 — Bowl 5 (Darkness on Beast's Throne)
**Context:** Beast's kingdom full of darkness (eskotomene). Tongue-gnawing for pain. The accumulation from Bowl 1 continues: "their sores" (helkon) still active.
**Direct statement:** LXX skotoo vocabulary echoes Plague 9. The target is the beast's basileia — kingdom-specific, like Plague 9 targeting Egypt specifically.
**Cross-references:** Exod 10:21-23 — Plague 9 was darkness on Egypt while Israel had light. Bowl 5 targets "the seat of the beast" — the center of rival authority.
**Relationship to other evidence:** The Goshen light principle (Exod 10:23) is structurally present in Bowl 5 — the darkness falls specifically on the beast's kingdom — but the text does not explicitly state God's people have light during Bowl 5. Bohr's claim that Bowl 5 preserves the Goshen light pattern is structurally plausible but goes beyond what Rev 16:10-11 explicitly states. This is an honest distinction between what the text says and what a type implies (per MEMORY.md guideline on typological overreach).

### Rev 16:12-16 — Bowl 6 (Euphrates Dried; Frog-Spirits)
**Context:** Euphrates dried for kings of the east; three frog-like unclean spirits emerge from the dragon, beast, and false prophet.
**Direct statement:** batrachos (G944) — NT hapax, Plague 2 echo. The frog-spirits come from the mouths of the unholy trinity — a demonic emission, not an agricultural pest.
**Cross-references:** Exod 8:2-6 (Plague 2 frogs). The frogs in Exodus came from the water; these frog-spirits come from mouths. The source shifts from natural to supernatural, from water to speech.
**Relationship to other evidence:** The NT hapax status of batrachos makes the Exodus allusion certain. But the transformation is total: Plague 2 was physical frogs; Bowl 6 frogs are demonic spirits that gather the kings of the earth for Armageddon. The word is preserved; everything about its deployment is eschatological.

### Rev 16:17-21 — Bowl 7 (Great Hail; Gegonen)
**Context:** Seventh bowl into the air; "It is done" (gegonen); talent-weight hail.
**Direct statement:** chalaza (G5464) = Plague 7 hail (barad/chalaza, PMI 29.60). "Plague of the hail" (plege tes chalazas) self-identifies as a plague. Talent-weight hail = eschatological escalation.
**Cross-references:** Exod 9:23-24 (Plague 7 hail + fire). The Exodus superlative ("such as there was none like it") = Rev 16:21 superlative ("exceeding great"). Rev 11:19 uses chalaza for the theophany marker between seventh trumpet and bowl prelude.
**Relationship to other evidence:** Bowl 7 closes the sequence with the same vocabulary (plege tes chalazas) that connects Bowl 7 and Trumpet 1 to Plague 7. The escalation from Trumpet 1 hail (mixed with fire and blood) to Bowl 7 hail (talent-weight) tracks the overall pattern: partial warning → total completion.

## The Goshen Protection Texts

### Exod 8:22-23 (First Goshen Exemption)
**Context:** Plague 4 flies: first explicit divine severance. "I will put a division between my people and thy people."
**Direct statement:** H6395 palah (to distinguish) establishes the theological foundation for all subsequent Goshen protections.
**Cross-references:** Rev 7:2-3 (sealing before trumpets), Rev 9:4 (locusts cannot harm sealed), Rev 16:2 (sores fall only on mark-bearers).
**Relationship to other evidence:** The Goshen principle — divine discrimination during judgment — is present in both plague sequences. Its mechanism transforms from geographic (land of Goshen) to personal (individual seal), but the structural logic is identical.

### Exod 9:26; 10:23 (Goshen from Hail; Light in Darkness)
**Context:** No hail in Goshen during Plague 7; Israel had light during Plague 9's darkness.
**Direct statement:** Geographic exemption explicitly stated at both verses. The geographic model is the prototype; Rev 7:3 and 9:4 transform it to personal seal.
**Relationship to other evidence:** The explicit Goshen-light text (Exod 10:23) does not have an explicit counterpart in Rev 16:10-11. The transformation from geographic to personal protection means that in Revelation, the protection is implied through the seal system (Rev 9:4) rather than stated at each bowl.

### Rev 7:2-3 (Sealing Before Trumpets)
**Context:** Seal of the living God applied to servants' foreheads before judgment falls. Hurt not earth, sea, trees until sealing is complete.
**Direct statement:** The seal functions as the personal equivalent of Goshen — distinguishing God's servants before the judgments come.
**Cross-references:** Exod 8:22-23 (geographic distinction), Exod 12:7-13 (Passover blood on doorposts). The sequence moves from territory (Goshen) → household (Passover door) → individual (forehead seal).
**Relationship to other evidence:** VP309 documents the three-stage arc: geographic Goshen → personal seal → mark-inversion. The VP is confirmed by the data across all three stages.

### Rev 9:4 (Locusts Cannot Harm the Sealed)
**Context:** Trumpet 5 locusts instructed not to harm those with God's seal. Exact Goshen parallel: Israel in Goshen was exempt from the flies; the sealed are exempt from the locusts.
**Direct statement:** sphragi-da (G4973) = seal of God. The sealed/unsealed distinction here anticipates the mark/seal contrast at Rev 13:16-17 and Rev 16:2.
**Relationship to other evidence:** This is the most explicit trumpet-sequence application of the Goshen principle. The inversion of the locust mandate (eat everything → cannot harm vegetation, only the unsealed) is simultaneously an Exodus echo and a Goshen application.

### Rev 16:2 (Sores on Mark-Bearers)
**Context:** Bowl 1 falls only on those who had the mark and worshipped the beast's image.
**Direct statement:** The anti-Goshen: those who accepted the beast's mark receive the bowl that those with God's seal do not.
**Relationship to other evidence:** This is the most direct reversal of the Goshen principle: Plague 6 (boils) fell on Egyptians while Israel in Goshen was exempt; Bowl 1 (helkos) falls on mark-bearers while those with God's seal are exempt. The targeting language ("upon the men which had the mark") mirrors the Goshen-severance language of Exod 8:22-23.

## Daniel 7 — The Composite Beast

### Dan 7:3-8 (Four Beasts)
**Context:** Lion with eagle wings (Dan 7:4), bear raised on one side with three ribs (Dan 7:5), leopard with four wings and four heads (Dan 7:6), terrible fourth beast with ten horns and a little horn with a great-speaking mouth (Dan 7:7-8).
**Direct statement:** Head count: lion (1) + bear (1) + leopard (4) + terrible (1) = 7. Ten horns on beast 4. Rev 13:1 composite beast: 7 heads and 10 horns — matching the cumulative Daniel totals exactly.
**Original language:** Aramaic cheyva (H2423) for each beast → G2342 therion (PMI 7.78). Dan 7:8 "mouth speaking great things" = LXX stoma laloun megala = Rev 13:5 verbatim.
**Cross-references:** Rev 13:1-2 (composite beast). Dan 7:8 little horn's mouth → Rev 13:5 beast's mouth.
**Relationship to other evidence:** SP212 (from the research agent's catalog): the 7-head cumulative count from Dan 7 is structurally exact. The 10 horns come from the terrible beast only. Rev 13:1's seven heads and ten horns are not independent symbolic invention — they are the mathematical result of adding up the Daniel beast anatomy.

### Dan 7:9-10 (Ancient of Days Judgment Scene)
**Context:** Thrones set, Ancient of Days enthroned (white garment, pure-wool hair, fiery throne), river of fire, vast attendant multitude, judgment sits, books opened.
**Direct statement:** Four sequential elements: (1) throne set, (2) one seated, (3) assembly stands before, (4) books opened for judgment. These four elements recur in Rev 20:11-13 in the same order.
**Original language:** Aramaic kursawan remiw (Peil 3mp, thrones were placed — divine passive). sifrin petichuw (Peil 3mp, books were opened — divine passive). The Peil is the Aramaic equivalent of the Greek divine passive (agent unnamed = God acts). "Hair like pure wool" — H6015 Aramaic amar → G2053 erion (PMI 10.30). LXX Theodotion: "hōsei erion katharon" (like pure wool).
**Cross-references:** Rev 20:11-13 (great white throne). Rev 1:14 (Christ's white-wool hair = Dan 7:9 Ancient of Days description applied to the Son of Man). Rev 4:2 (throne in heaven) + Rev 5:11 (ten thousand times ten thousand = Dan 7:10 multitude formula). Rev 20:12 (biblia enoichthesan = divine passive books opened).
**Relationship to other evidence:** Dan 7:9-10 is a distributed template in Revelation. The throne scene establishes in Rev 4-5; the books-opened judicial function executes in Rev 20:11-13. The attendant multitude formula ("thousand thousands...ten thousand times ten thousand") appears at Rev 5:11 — in the throne-room scene, not the Great White Throne. Revelation deliberately separates what Daniel presents as a unified scene. VP310 documents this distribution across Rev 4-5 and Rev 20:11-13.

### Dan 7:11-14 (Son of Man Receives Kingdom)
**Context:** Beast slain and burned (Dan 7:11). Son of Man comes with clouds to the Ancient of Days and receives everlasting kingdom (Dan 7:13-14). yehibh (Peil passive, was given) — the kingdom is divinely bestowed.
**Direct statement:** The Son of Man's kingdom reception (Dan 7:14) = the Lamb's scroll reception (Rev 5:5-7). Dan 7:14 "all people, nations, and languages shall serve him" = Rev 5:9; 7:9; 14:6 (every tribe, tongue, people, nation).
**Cross-references:** Rev 1:7 (coming with clouds), Rev 14:14 (Son of Man on cloud), Rev 11:15 (kingdoms become God's and his Christ's).
**Relationship to other evidence:** The Christological merger in Rev 1:14 (the glorified Christ bears the Ancient of Days' white-wool hair while being identified as the Son of Man) means that the figure who receives the kingdom in Dan 7:13-14 and the figure who renders judgment in Dan 7:9-10 are the same person in Revelation. IC165 (Strong rating) establishes this merger as structurally significant.

### Dan 7:17-18; 7:23-25 (Explicit Decoders)
**Context:** Dan 7:17: "These great beasts which are four are four kings" (malkin). Dan 7:23: "The fourth beast shall be the fourth kingdom" (malkuw). Dan 7:25: "He shall speak great words against the Most High" (yemallel millin).
**Direct statement:** Dan 7:17 and 7:23 are the within-text authorial decoders: beast = king/kingdom. The text's own angel explains what the beasts mean. Dan 7:23 provides the specific decoder for the fourth beast as the fourth kingdom.
**Original language:** Aramaic malkin (kings, Dan 7:17) and malkuw (kingdom, Dan 7:23) both apply to the same beasts — confirming the king/kingdom interchangeability (corporate solidarity). malkuw → G932 basileia (PMI 23.90). Dan 7:25 yemallel millin (Pael impf, he shall speak words) → Rev 13:5 stoma laloun megala.
**Cross-references:** Rev 13:1-2 (composite beast = composite of all four kingdoms). Rev 13:5 (42 months = Dan 7:25 "time, times, half a time"). Rev 16:10 (beast's basileia falls under Bowl 5).
**Relationship to other evidence:** Dan 7:17,23 are the hermeneutical keys for Rev 13:2. The within-text decoder means that reading Rev 13's composite beast as a composite of Danielic kingdoms is not an external interpretive imposition — it is what the Daniel text itself tells the reader to do. AN182 and AN186 establish this as the strongest reason for the "Strong" rating on IC163.

## Rev 13:1-2 — Composite Beast

### Analysis
**Context:** Beast rises from sea with 7 heads, 10 horns, 10 crowns. Leopard body + bear feet + lion mouth. Dragon gives beast dynamis + thronos + exousia megale.
**Direct statement:** The three animals are listed in reversed Dan 7 order: Dan 7 = lion → bear → leopard (chronological, future-facing from Babylon). Rev 13:2 = leopard → bear → lion (reverse order, reading from body to origin).
**Original language:** Rev 13:2 key parsings: homoion pardali (dative of comparison, leopard), hōs arkou (genitive of description, bear), hōs stoma leontos (lion's mouth). G3917 pardalis (NT hapax, Rev 13:2 only; PMI 11.88 from H5246 namer). G715 arkos (NT hapax, Rev 13:2 only; PMI 10.62 from H1677 dob). G3023 leon (9 NT occurrences; PMI 7.75 from H738 ari).
**Cross-references:** Dan 7:4-7 (lion, bear, leopard). Hos 13:7-8 (lion, leopard, bear as divine judgment triad in a single OT passage — pre-Daniel). The Hosea text establishes that the three-animal combination was established OT judgment imagery before Daniel.
**Relationship to other evidence:** VP311 (reversed-order construction) documents the reversal as a textual fact. The explanation that John looks backward from Rome through the empires in reverse order is an inferential argument consistent with the text's perspective. Both must be distinguished: the reversal is certain; the directional explanation is plausible but not stated in the text.

### Rev 13:5-6 — Stoma Laloun Megala
**Context:** Edothe double-passive: "was given to him a mouth speaking great things... was given to him authority for 42 months." Both grants are divine passive.
**Direct statement:** stoma laloun megala is verbatim from LXX Dan 7:8 (stoma laloun megala), Dan 7:11 (stoma to laloun megala), Dan 7:20 (stoma laloun megala). The participle laloun (G2980, V-PAP-NSN, present active nominative singular neuter) agrees with stoma (neuter) — the mouth itself is the continuous speaking agent.
**Original language:** G4750 stoma (mouth), G2980 laleo (to speak), G3173 megas (great). Revelation has 18 occurrences of stoma at 4.5/100 verses — highest NT density. The 42 months of Rev 13:5 = Dan 7:25 "a time, times, and half a time."
**Cross-references:** Dan 7:25 (speaks great words against the Most High). Rev 13:6 (blasphemy against God's name, tabernacle, and those who dwell in heaven).
**Relationship to other evidence:** TM343 establishes this as the strongest single textual link between Daniel and Revelation — a verbatim three-word LXX quotation embedded in the beast narrative. The double-divine-passive structure (edothe × 2) means that even the beast's blasphemous speaking authority is divinely permitted, not self-generated.

## Rev 20:11-13 — Great White Throne

### Analysis
**Context:** Great white throne seen; one sitting on it; earth and heaven flee; dead small and great stand before God; books opened; another book (book of life) opened; dead judged kata ta erga.
**Direct statement:** Four-element sequence paralleling Dan 7:9-10: (1) throne (thronon megan leukon) → (2) one sitting (kathemenos) → (3) dead stand before God → (4) books opened (biblia enoichthesan).
**Original language:** Rev 20:12 biblia enoichthesan (G975 + G455, V-API-3P, aorist passive indicative 3rd plural — books were opened) = Dan 7:10 Aramaic sifrin petichuw (Peil 3mp = books were opened — divine passive). Both constructions: books as subject, divine passive agent unnamed, third plural, judicial function. This grammatical parallel is the strongest link between Dan 7:9-10 and Rev 20:11-13.
**Cross-references:** Dan 7:9 (white garment/wool hair of Ancient of Days → Rev 1:14 Christ's wool-white hair → Rev 20:11 white throne). Dan 7:10 (ten thousand times ten thousand → Rev 5:11, not Rev 20). Rev 20:4 (thrones, judgment given to saints = Dan 7:22).
**Relationship to other evidence:** SP210 documents the four-element sequential parallel. The Dan 7:9-10 multitude formula ("thousand thousands ministered; ten thousand times ten thousand stood") appears in Rev 5:11 (throne room scene), not in Rev 20. Revelation intentionally distributes Dan 7:9-10's elements across two scenes: Rev 4-5 receives the throne-setting and attendant multitude; Rev 20:11-13 receives the books-opened judicial execution. This distribution serves the book's narrative structure — the throne room is established at the beginning of the sealed-scroll narrative; the forensic judgment executes at the end of the millennium.

## Rev 1:13-14 — Christological Merger

### Analysis
**Context:** Christ described as "like unto the Son of man" (Dan 7:13), clothed in long garment with golden girdle (Dan 7:9 Ancient of Days and high priest combined). "Head and hairs were white like wool, as white as snow."
**Direct statement:** erion (G2053) — 2 NT occurrences: Heb 9:19 (ritual wool) and Rev 1:14 (Christ's wool-white hair). LXX Dan 7:9 Theodotion: "hōsei erion katharon" (like pure wool). Rev 1:14: "leukai hōs erion" (white like wool).
**Cross-references:** Dan 7:9 (Ancient of Days' wool hair) + Dan 7:13 (Son of Man figure). Rev 20:11 (great white throne — the same Christ who is both Son of Man and Ancient of Days performs the judgment).
**Relationship to other evidence:** TM347 (erion as Christological merger marker) and IC165 (Strong rating for the merger) both confirm: Rev 1:14 deliberately applies the Ancient of Days' defining physical description to the Son of Man. The result is that Revelation's Christ combines both Daniel figures. This has structural implications: the judgment that in Dan 7:9-10 is performed by the Ancient of Days is performed by Christ in Rev 20:11, because Christ has already been identified as the Ancient of Days in Rev 1:14.

## Supporting Passages

### Rev 11:6 (Two Witnesses and All Plagues)
Two witnesses with authority "to smite the earth with all plagues (pasais tais pleges), as often as they will." Moses typology (water to blood) + Elijah typology (no rain). The Exodus plague tradition is actively operative as a live prophetic authority in Revelation's narrative, not merely typological background.

### Rev 11:8 (Jerusalem as Egypt)
The great city "spiritually is called Sodom and Egypt" — pneumatikos (G4152, adverb) explicitly signals that Egypt is a typological designation. TM351 establishes this as the authorial signal validating the entire Exodus-plague typological reading.

### Rev 11:15 (Kingdom Announcement)
"The kingdoms of this world are become the kingdoms of our Lord, and of his Christ" — egeneto (aorist, proleptic completion). Dan 7:14 kingdom transferred to Christ + Dan 7:27 kingdom given to saints = fulfilled here.

### Rev 19:11-16 (Christ as Warrior-Judge)
"King of Kings and Lord of Lords" fulfills Dan 7:14's everlasting dominion over all people, nations, languages. The same Christ who received the kingdom (Dan 7:13-14) executes judgment (Rev 19:11-16) and sits on the great white throne (Rev 20:11). The Christological merger of Rev 1:14 is the structural link between all three.

### Rev 20:4 (Millennial Thrones — Dan 7:22)
"Judgment was given unto them" (krima edothe autois, divine passive) = Dan 7:22 "judgment was given to the saints of the Most High" (dina yehib le-qadishei, Peil divine passive). Those who refused the mark of the beast (the inverse of Rev 16:2's mark-recipients) are specifically those who receive the millennial judgment/reign.

### Psalm 78:43-51; Psalm 105:28-36 (OT Plague Recitations)
Both Psalms recite the plagues in non-Exodus order; both omit Plagues 3 (gnats) and 5 (livestock). Psalm 105 begins with darkness (P9) then blood (P1) — completely rearranged. This OT precedent establishes that selective, non-sequential plague use is standard Israelite literary practice, not Revelation's innovation. SP098 (revs-40) established this as Strong.

### Rev 12:3; Rev 5:5-7; Dan 7:26-28 (Supporting Context)
Dragon's 7 heads and 10 horns (Rev 12:3) = source of composite beast's anatomy (Rev 13:1) with the same cumulative Dan 7 count. The Lamb's scroll reception (Rev 5:5-7) = Dan 7:13-14 Son of Man approaching the Ancient of Days. Dan 7:26-28 (judgment removes beast's dominion; kingdom given to saints) = Rev 20:4 + Rev 11:15 combined.

## Patterns Identified

**Pattern 1 — Fraction Escalation:** Seals (1/4) → Trumpets (1/3, 11× in Rev 8) → Bowls (no fraction, zero to triton in Rev 16). This quantitative pattern corresponds to: warning (seals) → graduated judgment (trumpets) → final judgment (bowls). Exodus plagues operated at total scope (no fraction); the bowl sequence matches this totality.

**Pattern 2 — Goshen-to-Seal Transformation Arc:** Geographic Goshen (Exod 8:22-23 through Exod 11:7) → Personal Seal (Rev 7:3; 9:4) → Mark-Inversion (Rev 16:2). Five Exodus texts establish the Goshen principle; Revelation transforms the mechanism while preserving the theological structure.

**Pattern 3 — Song of Moses as Explicit Frame:** Rev 15:3 names "the song of Moses" as the programmatic framework for the bowl sequence. No equivalent Exodus frame brackets the trumpet sequence. The explicit naming establishes the bowls (not the trumpets) as the primary Exodus antitype.

**Pattern 4 — LXX Vocabulary Chains:** Bowls have direct LXX vocabulary chains for 6 of 7: helkos/shechin (B1/P6), haima/dam (B2-3/P1), skotoo/choshek (B5/P9), batrachos/tsephardea (B6/P2), chalaza/barad (B7/P7). Trumpets have LXX chains for 3-4 of 7: chalaza/barad (T1/P7), haima/dam (T2/P1), akris/arbeh (T5/P8), and partial darkness (T4/P9 via Joel). The bowl vocabulary chains are more direct and more numerous.

**Pattern 5 — Dan 7 Distributed Template:** Dan 7:9-10's four elements are distributed across Revelation: throne (Rev 4:2), attendant multitude (Rev 5:11), books opened for judgment (Rev 20:12). Revelation intentionally separates the Dan 7 elements to serve different narrative functions at different points in the book.

**Pattern 6 — Christological Merger:** Rev 1:14 merges the Ancient of Days (Dan 7:9, white wool) with the Son of Man (Dan 7:13), so that all Dan 7 judgment and kingdom functions converge in Christ. This merger connects the throne-room establishment (Rev 4-5), the bowl framing (Rev 15:3), the warrior-judge (Rev 19:11-16), and the great white throne (Rev 20:11) into a single Christological trajectory.

**Pattern 7 — Three-Beast NT Hapax Cluster:** G3917 pardalis (leopard), G715 arktos (bear), and G944 batrachos (frog) are all NT hapaxes — words that appear exactly once in the NT, exclusively in Revelation. All three echo Daniel or Exodus LXX vocabulary directly. The clustering of NT hapaxes in this study's key passages is not coincidental; it marks these terms as deliberate, once-deployed allusions.

## Word Study Integration

**G1668 helkos (3 NT occurrences):** The restriction of helkos to three NT verses (Luke 16:21; Rev 16:2; 16:11) makes its Bowl 1 appearance a high-signal LXX bridge to Plague 6. The accumulation (still active at Rev 16:11) demonstrates that bowl plagues are cumulative.

**G5464 chalaza (4 NT occurrences, all Revelation):** Exclusive Revelation distribution marks every use as an Exodus 7 allusion. The Trumpet 1 chalaza (Rev 8:7) combines with haima; the Bowl 7 chalaza (Rev 16:21) self-names as plege tes chalazas. The word brackets both sequences.

**G944 batrachos (1 NT occurrence):** NT hapax at Rev 16:13. When a word has one NT occurrence and that occurrence reproduces the LXX's vocabulary for an Exodus plague, the allusion is structurally certain.

**G2053 erion (2 NT occurrences):** The restriction to Heb 9:19 and Rev 1:14 makes the Rev 1:14 echo of Dan 7:9 precise. The Christological merger depends on this single vocabulary link.

**G4127 plege (21 NT occurrences, 14 in Revelation):** The self-referential use at Rev 9:20 (retroactive trumpet label) and Rev 16:9 (bowl self-identification) confirms the author's intentional plague-framework for both sequences.

**A4437 malkuw (43 Aramaic occurrences):** Dan 7:17,23 are the within-text decoder texts. malkuw → basileia (G932, PMI 23.90) is the Aramaic-Greek bridge that makes the beast = kingdom equation operative in Revelation's language.

## Cross-Testament Connections

**Exod 15:11 → Rev 13:4 → Rev 15:3-4:** The incomparability formula (mi khamocha → tis homoios soi) is parodied at Rev 13:4 (tis homoios to therio) and restored at Rev 15:4 before the bowls. The three-verse arc (Exodus hymn → beast parody → restored hymn) frames the bowl sequence as antitypical Exodus.

**Dan 7:10 siprin petichuw → Rev 20:12 biblia enoichthesan:** The divine passive books-opened construction crosses from Aramaic (Peil) to Greek (aorist passive) with identical function: books opened by divine action for judgment. This is the strongest grammatical cross-testament link between Daniel and Revelation in this study.

**Dan 7:9 amar neka → Rev 1:14 erion:** The wool-white hair of the Ancient of Days (Aramaic amar, G2053 erion via LXX) applied to Christ creates the Christological merger. The LXX bridge is essential: the same Greek word used in Theodotion Dan 7:9 appears in Rev 1:14.

**H697 arbeh → G200 akris → Joel 1:4-7 → Rev 9:3-7:** The Exodus locust (arbeh) reaches Revelation's trumpet 5 (akris) through Joel's militarizing transformation. The LXX bridge is real (PMI 9.52), but Joel reshapes what the locusts do and become.

**H7822 shechin → G1668 helkos → Rev 16:2:** Direct single-step LXX bridge. No prophetic intermediary. The strongest Exodus-to-bowl vocabulary chain in the study.

## Difficult or Complicating Passages

**Bowl 4 and the Missing Exodus Parallel:** Bowl 4 (Rev 16:8-9) has no direct Exodus plague equivalent. kaumatizo (G2739) is not in the LXX; its source tradition is Day-of-Lord fire (Mal 4:1; Joel 2:30-31). More significantly, Bowl 4 inverts rather than echoes the darkness tradition: Plague 9 was reduced light; Bowl 4 is intensified light. This prevents SP099 from receiving a Strong rating despite the strength of the other six bowl-plague connections. The bowl sequence is not a mechanical Exodus replay.

**Trumpet 3 (Wormwood) and Non-Exodus Tradition:** T3's source is Jeremiah (Jer 9:15), not Exodus. This confirms that the trumpet sequence draws on multiple OT judgment traditions beyond Exodus — Joel for locusts, Jeremiah for wormwood, Exodus for hail and blood. The trumpet sequence is an OT synthesis; only the bowl sequence is Exodus-primary.

**Rev 16:10-11 and the Absent Goshen Light:** Exod 10:23 explicitly states Israel had light in Goshen during the darkness plague. Rev 16:10-11 does not explicitly state God's people have light during Bowl 5. The Goshen analogy is structurally present (the bowl targets the beast's kingdom specifically) but not verbally explicit. Bohr's CPNE p.57 claim that God's people have light during Bowl 5 is a typological inference, not a stated textual parallel.

**Dan 7:9-10 — One Event or Two?:** Dan 7:9-10 presents the Ancient of Days judgment scene as a unified vision. Revelation distributes its elements: throne room (Rev 4-5) receives the enthronement and attendant multitude; Great White Throne (Rev 20:11-13) receives the books-opened judicial execution. The question of whether Dan 7:9-10 depicts the pre-millennial investigative judgment (Rev 4-5 / Dan 8:14 connection, as in EGW tradition) or the post-millennial Great White Throne (Rev 20:11-13) or both is a valid hermeneutical question. The structural analysis of this study documents both parallel connections without imposing a single-event resolution. The text of Dan 7:10 says "judgment was set and the books were opened" — the forensic function matches Rev 20:12 most precisely. But the enthroned figure's description (white wool, fiery flames, vast multitude) most closely matches Rev 4-5 and Rev 1:14.

**Reversed Beast Order — Explanation vs. Datum:** The reversed order in Rev 13:2 (leopard → bear → lion) is a textual datum. Bohr's explanation (Daniel looks forward; John looks backward) is an inferential argument. The reversal is certain; the directional explanation is plausible but not stated in either text. This distinction matters: VP311 documents the reversal as a fact; the interpretation that Rev 13:2 reads back through completed history is an inference that fits the evidence without being demanded by it.

**Leithart's 3+7=10 — Attractive but Imprecise:** The claim that three trumpet plagues + seven bowls = ten mirrors the Exodus count. The problem: the text labels all trumpets as "plagues" (Rev 9:20, plegais), not just three. And the trumpet that most clearly echoes an Exodus plague in vocabulary (T1 = P7 hail) is not one of the three woe-trumpets that Leithart identifies. The 3+7=10 structure is an attractive observation but imposes a count the text does not itself make explicit. The first three Exodus plagues (gnats, flies, livestock) — which Leithart associates with pre-Goshen-distinction plagues — are precisely the three absent from Revelation's plague typology. The parallel is structurally appealing; the textual basis is not as firm as the bowl-closer claim (SP208).

## Preliminary Synthesis

**Bowls are closer to Exodus than trumpets, but the relationship is not a one-to-one replay.** Six of seven bowls carry direct LXX vocabulary chains to Exodus plagues; only four to five of seven trumpets do, and some trumpet connections are mediated through Joel or Isaiah. The bowls operate at total scope (no fraction); the trumpets operate at one-third. Exodus plagues operated at total scope ("all the land of Egypt," "thick darkness in all the land"). The bowl-Exodus match in scope is exact; the trumpet-Exodus match is partial. The Song of Moses (Rev 15:3) explicitly frames the bowl sequence as Exodus antitype; no equivalent frame exists for the trumpets.

**The composite beast of Rev 13:2 is the four Daniel kingdoms combined, read in reverse order.** Dan 7:17,23 provide the authorial decoder: beast = kingdom. The cumulative head count from Dan 7 (1+1+4+1=7) exactly matches Rev 13:1. The three NT hapaxes (pardalis, arktos, and the near-exclusive leon in this context) confirm the deliberateness of the Daniel allusion.

**Dan 7:9-10 serves as a distributed template for Rev 4-5 (throne and multitude) and Rev 20:11-13 (books opened for judgment).** The strongest grammatical parallel is the divine passive books-opened construction (siprin petichuw = biblia enoichthesan). The Christological merger in Rev 1:14 (erion/wool) means that the Ancient of Days who judges in Dan 7:9-10 is Christ in Revelation, resolving the Dan 7 distinction between Ancient of Days and Son of Man.
