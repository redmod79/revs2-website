# Word Studies

## Question
How do the numbers 12 (and its multiples: 24 elders, 144 cubits, 12,000 stadia, 144,000 sealed) and 4 (living creatures, horsemen, angels at corners, winds, universal formulas like 'every kindred, tongue, people, and nation') function as structural and symbolic numbers in Revelation? Document every occurrence of each number. Does 12 consistently represent God's people and 4 represent creation/universality? How do these secondary numbers relate to the primary structural number 7?

### G1427 — dodeka

**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G1427-dodeka.md`

**Occurrences:** 72

The number twelve. Indeclinable noun appearing 72 times in the NT, with 22 occurrences in Revelation (highest density at 3.5 per 100 verses). In Revelation, dodeka modifies concrete architectural nouns (gates, foundations, pearls, fruits) and identity structures (tribes, apostles, stars). Rev 21:12 and 21:14 each contain dodeka three times per verse — the densest NT concentration.

**Semantic categories:** Twelve Apostles, Twelve Tribes of Israel, Structural Completeness in Revelation, Numeric Quantity in Narrative

**Key verses:** Rev 21:12, Rev 21:14, Rev 22:2, Rev 7:5-8, Rev 12:1, Mat 19:28

**Relevance:** Core term for this study. The number 12 in Revelation appears exclusively in contexts related to God's people (tribes, apostles) or sacred architecture (gates, foundations, fruits). No negative or demonic uses exist, supporting Kuykendall's claim.

### G5064 — tessares

**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G5064-téssares.md`

**Occurrences:** 42

The number four. Adjective appearing 42 times in the NT, with 30 occurrences in Revelation (71% concentration, 3.1 per 100 verses). Categories: living creatures (16 collocations with zoon), angels at corners/winds, horsemen, twenty-four elders, and spatial totality. Rev 7:1 contains tessares three times in one verse — the densest NT concentration.

**Semantic categories:** Four living creatures, Four angels/horsemen, Four corners/winds/directions, Twenty-four elders, 144,000 and measurement

**Key verses:** Rev 4:6, Rev 7:1, Rev 6:1-8, Rev 9:14, Rev 20:8, Rev 15:7

**Relevance:** Core term for this study. The number 4 in Revelation consistently denotes creation scope, universal judgment, and geographic totality. The four living creatures represent creation categories; the four corners/winds represent geographic completeness.

### G721 — arnion

**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G721-arnion.md`

**Occurrences:** 30

The Lamb — Revelation's distinctive christological title. 30 total occurrences (tokens) across 28 unique verses in 2 books (John 21:15 + 27 Revelation verses). Bauckham's claim of 28 Revelation occurrences needs qualification: 29 tokens appear in 27 Revelation verses (some verses have 2 tokens: Rev 14:4, 17:14). If counting unique verses, there are 28 total (27 Rev + 1 John). The 7x4=28 claim works for unique verse count but not token count.

**Semantic categories:** Enthroned Lamb, Slain Lamb, Object of Worship, Warrior-Judge, Bridegroom, New Jerusalem

**Key verses:** Rev 5:6, Rev 5:12, Rev 7:17, Rev 14:1, Rev 17:14, Rev 21:22-23

**Relevance:** Tests Bauckham's 7x4=28 structural claim. The arnion count is a key data point for the interaction of 7 and 4. At unique-verse level, 28 = 7x4 is confirmed (28 verses across the entire NT). At token level, it's 30.

### G4245 — presbyteros

**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G4245-presbyteros.md`

**Occurrences:** 67

Elder. Comparative adjective appearing 67 times in NT across 13 books, with 12 occurrences in Revelation (3.0 per 100 verses). All 12 Revelation occurrences refer to the 24 elders in the heavenly throne room. Co-occurs with tessares (G5064) 9 times, thronos (G2362) 8 times, and zoon (G2226) 7 times.

**Semantic categories:** Jewish Religious Authority, Church Leadership, Apostolic Council, Twenty-Four Heavenly Elders, Age/Generation, Epistolary Self-Designation, Ancestors of Faith

**Key verses:** Rev 4:4, Rev 4:10, Rev 5:8, Rev 7:11, Rev 11:16, Rev 19:4

**Relevance:** Presbyteros appears exactly 12 times in Revelation — always of the 24 elders. The number of occurrences itself is 12, a possible intentional numerical pattern. The elders co-occur with the four creatures exactly 7 times (Kuykendall's claim verified).

### G2226 — zoon

**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G2226-zoon.md`

**Occurrences:** 23

Living creature. Neuter noun appearing 20 unique verses (23 tokens) in NT, with 17 Revelation verses (85%). Three structural functions: (1) worship at throne (4:6-9, 5:8-9, 19:4), (2) summoning horsemen (6:1-7), (3) distributing wrath (15:7). Co-occurs with tessares (G5064) 11 times, presbyteros (G4245) 7 times.

**Semantic categories:** Four Heavenly Living Creatures, Sacrificial/Ritual Animals, Brute/Irrational Animals

**Key verses:** Rev 4:6-8, Rev 5:8, Rev 6:1, Rev 15:7, Rev 19:4

**Relevance:** The four living creatures are the primary 4-entity in Revelation. Their three roles (worship, summoning judgment, distributing wrath) demonstrate that the number 4 is functionally connected to creation's relationship with God.

### G5443 — phyle

**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G5443-phylē.md`

**Occurrences:** 31

Tribe, kindred. Feminine noun appearing 31 times in NT, with 21 occurrences in Revelation (67.7% concentration). Appears in both the fourfold universal formula (5 times with all four components) and the tribal lists (Rev 7:4-8, 21:12). Collocates with glossa, laos, and ethnos 5 times each.

**Key verses:** Rev 5:9, Rev 7:4-8, Rev 7:9, Rev 21:12, Rev 14:6

**Relevance:** Component of the fourfold formula and the 12-tribe structure. Phyle bridges the 4-symbolism (fourfold formula) and the 12-symbolism (twelve tribes) — it participates in both number systems.

### G1484 — ethnos

**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G1484-ethnos.md`

**Occurrences:** 164

Nation, Gentile. Neuter noun appearing 164 times (152 unique verses) in NT across 17 books, with 22 occurrences in Revelation. Dual function: neutral national/ethnic term and technical Jew-Gentile contrast. In Revelation, appears in the fourfold formula, in eschatological judgment, and in the New Jerusalem.

**Key verses:** Rev 5:9, Rev 7:9, Rev 11:18, Rev 14:6, Rev 21:24, Rev 22:2

**Relevance:** Component of the fourfold formula. Ethnos represents the national/political dimension of human diversity. Rev 21:24 and 22:2 show nations in the eschatological positive context — walking in the city's light and healed by the tree of life.

### G2992 — laos

**Relevance:** Component of the fourfold formula. Laos bridges the people-of-God theology (12-symbolism) with the universal-humanity scope (4-symbolism), appearing in both the 12-tribe covenant language and the fourfold universal formula.

### G2033 — hepta

**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G2033-heptá.md`

**Occurrences:** 87

Seven. Indeclinable noun appearing 87 times in NT, with 54 occurrences in Revelation (62% concentration, 55.1 per 100 verses — the highest density of any book). Seven structures nearly every major symbol: churches, spirits, seals, trumpets, bowls, thunders, heads, horns, eyes. Unlike 12, the number 7 serves both divine and demonic symbolism (seven heads on dragon/beast).

**Semantic categories:** Completeness and divine fullness, Apocalyptic structuring, Narrative patterns, Leadership and institutional order, OT covenant and ritual

**Key verses:** Rev 1:4, Rev 4:5, Rev 5:6, Rev 8:2, Rev 12:3, Rev 15:1

**Relevance:** The primary structural number against which 12 and 4 operate as secondary numbers. Key observation: 7 can serve both divine (seven spirits, seven seals) and demonic (seven heads, seven mountains) symbolism, while 12 is exclusively positive.

### G5507 — chilioi

**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G5507-chilioi.md`

**Occurrences:** 11

Thousand (cardinal adjective). 11 occurrences in NT: 9 in Revelation, 2 in 2 Peter. In Revelation, 6 cluster in chapter 20 (the millennium — chilia ete 'thousand years'). Also appears in prophetic time periods (1,260 days in 11:3, 12:6; 1,600 furlongs in 14:20). Distinct from G5505 chilias (noun form).

**Semantic categories:** The millennium, Prophetic time periods, God's timescale vs human

**Key verses:** Rev 20:2-7, Rev 11:3, Rev 12:6, Rev 14:20

**Relevance:** Chilioi is the cardinal adjective; it modifies nouns directly (chilia ete 'a thousand years'). Revelation uses chilioi exclusively for the millennium and time/distance periods. The multiplier in 12,000 and 144,000 uses the noun form chilias (G5505) instead.

### G5505 — chilias

**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G5505-chiliás.md`

**Occurrences:** 23

A thousand (collective noun). Feminine noun appearing 23 times in NT, with 19 in Revelation. Denotes a grouped block of one thousand — a counting unit, not a bare number. The plural chiliades in Rev 7:4-8 (hekatontessarakonta tessares chiliades = 'one hundred forty-four thousand-groups') emphasizes organized, counted units. Rev 7:5-8 repeats dodeka chiliades 12 times (once per tribe).

**Semantic categories:** Collective counting unit, Military/census enumeration, Innumerable multitude, Structured symbolic numbering

**Key verses:** Rev 7:4-8, Rev 5:11, Rev 14:1, Rev 14:3, Rev 21:16

**Relevance:** The noun form used in 144,000. The plural chiliades supports Bohr's claim that the Greek emphasizes '144 thousand-groups' rather than a conglomerate number. Also appears in the 12,000 stadia measurement (Rev 21:16) and 7,000 killed (Rev 11:13).

### G1429 — dodekaphylon

**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G1429-dōdekaphylon.md`

**Occurrences:** 1

Twelve tribes (compound word). Neuter noun hapax legomenon appearing only in Acts 26:7. Fuses dodeka (twelve) and phylon (tribe) into a single compound, emphasizing the corporate unity of all twelve tribes as one indivisible entity. Paul uses it before Agrippa to argue that the entire twelve-tribe nation shares one hope — the resurrection promise.

**Semantic categories:** National corporate identity, Eschatological hope of Israel

**Key verses:** Acts 26:7

**Relevance:** The compound form emphasizes that the twelve tribes are not twelve separate entities but one unified commonwealth. This supports the interpretation that 12 represents the wholeness of God's people as a single body.

### G4972 — sphragizo

**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G4972-sphragizo.md`

**Occurrences:** 28

To seal. Verb appearing 28 times (15 distinct verse locations) in NT. In Revelation, the sealing of the 144,000 (7:3-8) marks them as belonging to God — parallel to the beast's mark (charagma, G5480) on the forehead. The perfect passive participle esphragismenon/esphragismenoi appears in Rev 7:4 for those 'having been sealed.'

**Semantic categories:** Authentication, Divine concealment, Ownership/protection

**Key verses:** Rev 7:3-4, Rev 7:5-8, Rev 5:1, Eph 1:13, Eph 4:30

**Relevance:** The sealing verb connects the 144,000 (12-number system) to the seal of God. The contrast with charagma (mark of the beast) shows the 12-structure (sealed people) opposing the beast's system.

### G4973 — sphragis

**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G4973-sphragis.md`

**Occurrences:** 16

Seal (noun). Feminine noun appearing 16 times in NT, with 13 in Revelation. Functions: seven seals on the scroll (5:1-2, 5:5, 5:9, 6:1-12, 8:1), seal of the living God marking God's servants (7:2, 9:4). **Semantic categories:** Authentication, Divine concealment, Sequential judgment, Divine ownership/protection

**Key verses:** Rev 5:1-2, Rev 6:1-12, Rev 7:2, Rev 8:1

**Relevance:** Bridges the 7-system (seven seals on the scroll) and the 12-system (seal of God on the 144,000). The four living creatures announce the opening of each of the first four seals, creating a 4+3 structure within the seven.

### G4735 — stephanos

**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G4735-stephanos.md`

In Revelation: crowns on the 24 elders (4:4, 4:10), crown on the woman (12:1 — twelve stars), crown on the locusts (9:7), crown on the rider on the white horse (6:2, 14:14).

**Relevance:** The elders' gold crowns (stephanos) connect to the overcomer promises in the letters. The woman's stephanos of twelve stars specifically ties the crown to the 12-tribal identity. The elders casting their crowns before the throne (4:10) emphasizes derived authority.

### G935 — basileus

**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G935-basileus.md`

**Occurrences:** 118

King. Masculine noun appearing 118 times in NT, with 23 in Revelation (5.7 per 100 verses). In the fourfold formula context, basileusin (kings) substitutes for phylai (tribes) in Rev 10:11 — the only formula occurrence where a non-standard term replaces one of the four components.

**Key verses:** Rev 10:11, Rev 17:14, Rev 19:16

**Relevance:** The substitution of basileus for phyle in Rev 10:11's fourfold formula adds a political-authority dimension. The Lamb is 'King of kings' (17:14, 19:16), connecting the 7x4=28 Lamb-count to royal imagery.

### G3841 — pantokrator

**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G3841-pantokrator.md`

**Occurrences:** 10

Almighty. Masculine noun appearing 10 times in NT, with 9 in Revelation (90%). Used exclusively as a divine title. Appears in the living creatures' trisagion (4:8), in doxologies (11:17, 15:3, 16:7), and in theophanic announcements (19:6, 21:22). The 9-of-10 concentration in Revelation is the highest for any NT vocabulary item.

**Semantic categories:** Divine sovereignty title

**Key verses:** Rev 4:8, Rev 11:17, Rev 15:3, Rev 19:6, Rev 21:22

**Relevance:** The four living creatures declare God 'pantokrator' in 4:8, connecting creation-representing entities (4) with the all-sovereign title. In 21:22, 'the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are the temple' — pantokrator appears in the 12-saturated New Jerusalem context.
