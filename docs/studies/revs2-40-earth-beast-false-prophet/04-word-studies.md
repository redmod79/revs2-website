# Word Studies

## Question
What does the structural and typological evidence identify about the earth beast of Revelation 13:11-18?

### G721 — arnion

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G721-arnion.md`

**Occurrences:** 30

Diminutive of aren G704; a lambkin. 30 NT occurrences: 29 Christological (28 in Revelation + John 21:15), 1 sole exception at Rev 13:11 (earth beast horns like arnion). Functions virtually as a proper name for Christ in Revelation. The arnion anomaly is the sharpest single-verse parody identifier in the book — John uses Christ's own Lamb-title for the counterfeit.

**Semantic categories:** Enthroned Lamb — Sovereign Authority, Slain Lamb — Sacrificial Redemption, Lamb as Object of Worship, Lamb as Bridegroom/Victor, Parody — Earth Beast with Lamb Horns

**Key verses:** Rev 5:6, Rev 5:12, Rev 13:8, Rev 13:11, John 21:15, Rev 7:14, Rev 17:14

**Relevance:** The sole non-Christological use of arnion at Rev 13:11 is the strongest lexical evidence for the earth beast as a Christ-counterfeit. John deliberately uses arnion (not amnos) to create a Revelation-internal echo between the authentic Lamb and the false lamb.

### G5578 — pseudoprophetes

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G5578-pseudoprophetes.md`

**Occurrences:** 11

Compound of pseudos + prophetes = spurious prophet / religious impostor. 11 NT occurrences across 7 books. In Revelation, shifts to singular with definite article ('the false prophet') referring to the earth beast. Title first appears at Rev 16:13 — withheld until the unholy trinity acts in concert.

**Semantic categories:** Warning Against Deceptive Teachers, Eschatological Warning: Signs and Wonders, Historical Precedent, Individual Instance — Bar-Jesus, Eschatological Triad: Dragon, Beast, False Prophet

**Key verses:** Matt 7:15, Matt 24:24, Mark 13:22, Rev 16:13, Rev 19:20, Rev 20:10, 1 John 4:1

**Relevance:** The retrospective title pseudoprophetes (first at 16:13) confirms the earth beast's identity as the false prophet. The compound pseudo+prophetes = counterfeit spokesman — structurally paralleling the Spirit's role as God's spokesman.

### G4160 — poieo

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G4160-poieo.md`

**Occurrences:** 597

Broadest Greek action verb: to make, do, cause. 597 tokens across 26 NT books. In Rev 13:12-16, poieo dominates the earth beast section in causative constructions: poiei + hina + subjunctive = 'causes that they should.' The causative pattern grammatically encodes compulsion.

**Semantic categories:** General Action/Doing, Creative Making, Causative Construction, Moral Conduct, Sign-Working

**Key verses:** Rev 13:12, Rev 13:13, Rev 13:14, Rev 13:15, Rev 13:16, Rev 19:20

**Relevance:** Poieo's causative use in the earth beast section is distinctive: the poiei+hina pattern encodes compulsion, not invitation. The earth beast CAUSES worship, CAUSES mark reception. This grammar distinguishes coercion from persuasion.

### G4105 — planao

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G4105-planao.md`

**Occurrences:** 51

From the verbal base meaning 'to roam/wander.' Properly, to cause to roam from safety/truth/virtue; passively, to go astray. 51 NT occurrences. Deception verb chain in Revelation: dragon (12:9) → earth beast (13:14) → false prophet (19:20) → devil (20:10). Planao traces the deception trajectory through all four passages.

**Semantic categories:** Literal/Pastoral — Straying Sheep, Satanic/Cosmic Deception, End-Times Deception, Self-Deception/Error, Institutional/Doctrinal Deception

**Key verses:** Rev 12:9, Rev 13:14, Rev 19:20, Rev 20:3, Rev 20:10, Matt 24:24, 2 Thess 2:10

**Relevance:** Planao creates the deception trajectory linking all unholy trinity members. The earth beast inherits the dragon's planao function (12:9 → 13:14), confirming the Spirit-parody identification: as the Spirit leads into truth, the false prophet leads into error.

### G4151 — pneuma

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G4151-pneuma.md`

**Occurrences:** 385

Current of air, breath, spirit. 385 NT occurrences. Three-tier in Revelation: (1) Seven Spirits of God (1:4; 5:6) = authentic; (2) false prophet gives pneuma to image (13:15) = counterfeit animation; (3) three unclean pneumata (16:13) = Pentecost parody. John's choice of pneuma (not zoe, psyche, bios, or pnoe) for the image signals functional animation, not genuine life.

**Semantic categories:** Holy Spirit, Human Spirit, Angelic/Demonic Spirits, Breath/Wind, Counterfeit Animation

**Key verses:** Rev 1:4, Rev 5:6, Rev 13:15, Rev 16:13, John 16:13, Eph 1:13

**Relevance:** Pneuma in Rev 13:15 is the lexical bridge between the earth beast and Spirit-parody function #3 (giving life/animation). The three-tier pneuma structure in Revelation maps the authentic-counterfeit-demonic progression.

### G4352 — proskyneo

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G4352-proskyneo.md`

**Occurrences:** 65

To prostrate oneself in worship; to do homage. 65 NT tokens, 54 unique verses. Highest concentration in Revelation (22 occurrences). Forms the book's worship binary: beast/dragon proskyneo vs. God/Lamb proskyneo. The earth beast's primary function is to cause (poiei) proskyneo toward the first beast.

**Semantic categories:** Worship of God/Christ, Idolatrous Worship, Beast/Dragon Worship, Worship Prohibited/Redirected

**Key verses:** Rev 13:4, Rev 13:8, Rev 13:12, Rev 13:15, Rev 14:7, Rev 14:9, Rev 19:10, Rev 22:8-9

**Relevance:** Proskyneo is the central verb of the earth beast's program: it CAUSES worship (13:12) directed toward the first beast. The worship binary (true proskyneo vs. beast proskyneo) is the defining conflict of Revelation.

### G2342 — therion

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G2342-therion.md`

**Occurrences:** 46

Diminutive of ther; a wild beast, dangerous animal. 46 NT occurrences; 38 in Revelation (84%). Distinct from zoon G2226 (living creatures around throne). Prophetic beast designation for both sea beast and earth beast. The earth beast is allo therion (another beast of the same kind).

**Semantic categories:** Literal Wild/Dangerous Animal, Prophetic Beast Symbol — Sea Beast, Prophetic Beast Symbol — Earth Beast/False Prophet, Generic Wild Animals

**Key verses:** Rev 13:1, Rev 13:11, Rev 13:14-15, Rev 16:13, Rev 19:20, Rev 20:10, Dan 7:3

**Relevance:** Both beasts are therion — same prophetic category. Allos therion (13:11) = another of the same kind (not heteros = different kind). Both belong to the same class of symbolic power.

### G4592 — semeion

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G4592-semeion.md`

**Occurrences:** 77

A sign, miracle, token — an indication that signifies something beyond itself. 77 NT tokens. Classified under Communication (Louw-Nida 33) — semiotic function. The earth beast performs semeia megala (13:13) and is identified retrospectively by signs done enopion autou (19:20). Signs function as both authentication and deception.

**Semantic categories:** Divine Authentication, Messianic Signs, Requested/Demanded Signs, Deceptive/Counterfeit Signs, Eschatological Signs

**Key verses:** Rev 13:13, Rev 13:14, Rev 16:14, Rev 19:20, 2 Thess 2:9, Matt 24:24, Heb 2:4

**Relevance:** Semeion bridges the authentic (Heb 2:4) and counterfeit (Rev 13:13-14, 2 Thess 2:9) authentication patterns. The earth beast appropriates the sign-authentication mechanism for deceptive purposes.

### G5059 — teras

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G5059-teras.md`

**Occurrences:** 16

A prodigy or omen; a wonder. 16 NT occurrences; ALWAYS plural and ALWAYS paired with semeia (signs). 2 Thess 2:9 terasin pseudous (wonders of falsehood) parallels Rev 13:13-14 sign-working.

**Semantic categories:** Divine Authentication Paired with Signs, Apostolic Authentication, Counterfeit/Lying Wonders

**Key verses:** 2 Thess 2:9, Matt 24:24, Mark 13:22, Acts 2:22, Heb 2:4

**Relevance:** Teras in 2 Thess 2:9 with pseudous genitive (wonders OF falsehood) provides the verbal parallel to the earth beast's sign-working. The lying qualifier confirms the counterfeit nature.

### G5480 — charagma

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G5480-charagma.md`

**Occurrences:** 9

A scratch, etching, stamp — badge of servitude. 9 NT occurrences (8 in Revelation, 1 in Acts). Counterfeits sphragis (seal). The earth beast causes all to receive charagma on right hand or forehead — forehead OR hand (disjunction with e) vs. seal's forehead ONLY.

**Semantic categories:** Engraved/Sculptured Artwork, Mark Compulsorily Imposed, Mark Linked to Worship, Mark as Basis for Judgment

**Key verses:** Rev 13:16, Rev 13:17, Rev 14:9, Rev 14:11, Rev 16:2, Rev 19:20, Rev 20:4

**Relevance:** Charagma is the enforcement mechanism of the earth beast's program. Spirit-parody function #5: counterfeiting the Spirit's sealing (sphragis, Eph 1:13). The hand option reveals the beast accepts mere compliance without conviction.

### G4973 — sphragis

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G4973-sphragis.md`

**Occurrences:** 16

A signet, seal — stamp of authentication, privacy, or genuineness. 16 NT occurrences (13 in Revelation). Counterpart to charagma. Seal = forehead only (4 passages, zero exceptions); mark = forehead or hand. Mutually exclusive binary.

**Semantic categories:** Authentication and Certification, Divine Concealment and Authorization, Sequential Judgment Unveiling, Sealing of God's People

**Key verses:** Rev 7:2-4, Rom 4:11, Eph 1:13, Eph 4:30, 2 Tim 2:19, Rev 5:1

**Relevance:** Sphragis as divine seal counterparts and opposes charagma as beast mark. The seal-mark binary is mutually exclusive: one bears either the seal of God (forehead only, conviction required) or the mark of the beast (forehead or hand, compliance sufficient).

### G1325 — didomi

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G1325-didomi.md`

**Occurrences:** 430

Primary verb: to give. 430 NT tokens. In Rev 13, edothe (aorist passive = divine passive) marks God as the unnamed permitter. Eight edothe occurrences in Rev 13 establish sovereign permission. The double-didomi at 13:15 (edothe auto dounai) is unique: God permits the earth beast to give pneuma to the image.

**Semantic categories:** Simple Transfer/Gift, Delegated Authority, Divine Permission (Passive), Causative/Agent Role

**Key verses:** Rev 13:5, Rev 13:7, Rev 13:14, Rev 13:15, Rev 13:16

**Relevance:** Edothe (divine passive of didomi) in Rev 13 establishes that God sovereignly permits the beast system. The edoken/edothe alternation distinguishes dragon's active empowerment (edoken, 13:2) from God's passive permission (edothe, 13:5-15). The double-didomi at 13:15 encodes three levels of derived authority.

### G1849 — exousia

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G1849-exousia.md`

**Occurrences:** 103

Authority, delegated power, right, jurisdiction. 103 NT tokens. The earth beast exercises exousia of the first beast (13:12) — derivative, never autonomous. Distinguished from dynamis (raw power): exousia = vested right/legitimate authority.

**Semantic categories:** Delegated Authority, Autonomous Authority, Supernatural Powers, Legitimate Right

**Key verses:** Rev 13:2, Rev 13:4, Rev 13:5, Rev 13:7, Rev 13:12

**Relevance:** The earth beast's authority is entirely derivative — ten exousian tou protou theriou pasan (all the authority of the first beast). It never exercises independent authority, always acting enopion autou (in his presence). This reinforces the Spirit-parody: the Spirit's authority is also derivative (from Christ).

### G2768 — keras

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G2768-keras.md`

**Occurrences:** 11

Horn — literal or figurative. 11 NT tokens, concentrated in Revelation. Symbolizes power/authority in apocalyptic literature. Earth beast: two horns (13:11) vs. Lamb's seven (5:6) vs. sea beast's ten (13:1). Two without crowns = incomplete, uncrowned authority.

**Semantic categories:** Literal Horn/Altar Corner, Symbolic Power — Apocalyptic, Messianic Horn — Salvation

**Key verses:** Rev 5:6, Rev 13:1, Rev 13:11, Rev 12:3, Rev 17:12, Luke 1:69

**Relevance:** The horn contrast: Lamb = 7 (complete power), sea beast = 10 with crowns (political authority), earth beast = 2 without crowns (limited, uncrowned — possibly civil and religious principles). The absence of crowns on earth beast's horns may signal republican rather than monarchical authority.

### G243 — allos

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G243-allos.md`

**Occurrences:** 160

Another of the same kind (classical distinction from heteros = another of different kind). 160 NT tokens. At Rev 13:11, allo therion = another beast of the SAME KIND as the sea beast. Both beasts belong to the same prophetic category.

**Semantic categories:** Numerical Addition — Same Kind, Qualitative Distinction — Same Category, Sequential/Temporal Another

**Key verses:** Rev 13:11, John 14:16, Gal 1:7

**Relevance:** Allo therion at 13:11 establishes that the earth beast belongs to the same prophetic category as the sea beast — both are theria, both exercise political-religious authority. Cf. John 14:16 allon parakleton = another Comforter of the same kind (the Spirit as another like Jesus).

### G3664 — homoios

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G3664-homoios.md`

**Occurrences:** 47

Similar, resembling — appearance resemblance, not identity. 47 NT occurrences (22 in Revelation). At 13:11, homoia arnio = like/similar to a lamb (dative of comparison). Signals appearance resemblance: the earth beast LOOKS like a lamb but IS NOT the Lamb.

**Semantic categories:** Parabolic Similitude, Apocalyptic Description, Appearance Resemblance, Character Comparison

**Key verses:** Rev 13:11, Rev 13:2, Rev 1:15, Rev 9:7, Matt 13:31

**Relevance:** Homoios + dative at 13:11 encodes appearance-only resemblance: the earth beast has horns SIMILAR TO a lamb's but speaks as a dragon. The resemblance is external and deceptive, not internal or genuine.

### G1093 — ge

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G1093-gē.md`

**Occurrences:** 252

Earth, land, ground, soil, region. 252 NT occurrences (82 in Revelation = highest density). Translates Hebrew eretz. At 13:11, ek tes ges = origin of the second beast. Sea (thalassa) = peoples, nations (17:15); earth = by contrast, newer/less populated territory.

**Semantic categories:** Physical Earth/Ground, Geographic Territory/Land, Inhabited World, Earth vs. Heaven Contrast

**Key verses:** Rev 13:11, Rev 12:16, Rev 13:12, Rev 17:15

**Relevance:** Ge as origin of the earth beast contrasts with thalassa (sea) as origin of the first beast. If sea = densely populated, contested civilizations (17:15), then earth = newer, less populated, more stable territory. This is the only beast in prophetic literature with an earth origin.

### G1799 — enopion

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G1799-enopion.md`

**Occurrences:** 97

Before, in the presence of, in the sight of. 97 NT occurrences (36 in Revelation). Marks subordination/liturgical function. Used three times for the earth beast: 13:12 (exercises authority enopion autou), 13:14 (signs done enopion tou theriou), 19:20 (signs enopion autou confirming identity).

**Semantic categories:** Divine Presence/Before God, Subordination/Before Authority, Liturgical/Priestly Function, Public Visibility

**Key verses:** Rev 13:12, Rev 13:14, Rev 19:20, Luke 1:15

**Relevance:** Enopion autou marks the earth beast as never autonomous: always acting in the presence of, before, or on behalf of the first beast. This parallels the Holy Spirit's self-effacing role before Christ (John 16:14). The triple enopion (13:12, 13:14, 19:20) is the verbal chain confirming earth beast = false prophet.

### G500 — antichristos

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G500-antichristos.md`

**Occurrences:** 5

Opponent of the Messiah / one who stands against or in place of Christ. 5 tokens in 4 verses (1 John, 2 John only). Dual force of anti: both opposition and substitution. 1 John 2:18 distinguishes singular expected antichrist from many already present.

**Semantic categories:** Expected Eschatological Figure, Many Present Antichrists, Doctrinal Test — Denial of Incarnation

**Key verses:** 1 John 2:18, 1 John 2:22, 1 John 4:3, 2 John 1:7

**Relevance:** John's own usage of antichristos (many already present) resists purely singular identification of the false prophet. Cross-linked with pseudoprophetes and christos. The institutional/plural dimension supports system-level identification.

### G1504 — eikon

**Occurrences:** 23

Image, likeness, visible representation. 23 NT occurrences. From eido (to see) — a visible likeness. Distinct from eidolon G1497 (empty idol): eikon stresses genuine visible likeness, eidolon stresses emptiness. Eikon chain: Gen 1:26 LXX (imago Dei) → Col 1:15 (Christ IS eikon of God) → Rev 13:14-15 (manufactured beast-image). In Rev 13:14, poiesai eikona to therio = to make an image FOR the beast — a structural replica of the beast's authority pattern.

**Semantic categories:** Imago Dei — Original, Christological — Christ as God's Image, Imperial/Civic Image, Beast Image — Manufactured Counterfeit

**Key verses:** Gen 1:26-27, Col 1:15, 2 Cor 4:4, Rev 13:14, Rev 13:15, Rev 14:9, Rev 19:20, Rev 20:4

**Relevance:** Eikon (not eidolon) = genuine likeness, not empty idol. The beast's image is a structural replica of the beast's church-state authority pattern. Rev 13:14 uses eikon deliberately: the image genuinely resembles the beast's institutional structure. This makes the image an institutional phenomenon, not a conventional idol.

### G1753 — energeia

**Occurrences:** 8

Working, operation, efficiency ('energy'). 8 NT occurrences. At 2 Thess 2:9, kat energeian tou Satana = by the working OF Satan (subjective genitive: Satan is the agent). At 2 Thess 2:11, energeian planes = working of delusion. The energeia + subjective genitive construction shows the same authority-transfer structure as Rev 13:2/4 (dragon gives authority to beast).

**Semantic categories:** Divine Working/Operation, Satanic Working/Operation, Effective Power in Believers

**Key verses:** 2 Thess 2:9, 2 Thess 2:11, Eph 1:19, Eph 3:7, Eph 4:16, Phil 3:21, Col 1:29, Col 2:12

**Relevance:** Energeia in 2 Thess 2:9 provides the verbal parallel to the dragon's authority transfer in Rev 13:2. The satanic energeia working through the man of lawlessness parallels the dragon empowering the beast system. The grammar entry (energeia+subjective genitive) confirms Satan as agent.

### G2087 — heteros

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G2087-heteros.md`

**Occurrences:** 99

Another of a different kind (classical distinction from allos = same kind). 99 NT tokens. John does NOT use heteros for the earth beast — he uses allos (G243). This choice signals the earth beast is another beast of the SAME kind as the sea beast, not a fundamentally different category.

**Semantic categories:** Numerical/Qualitative Distinction, Different Kind/Category, Temporal Sequence

**Key verses:** Gal 1:6-7, Acts 4:12, Luke 23:32

**Relevance:** The absence of heteros at Rev 13:11 is significant: John chose allos (same kind) over heteros (different kind). Both beasts belong to the same prophetic category — both are institutional powers, not one system and one individual.

### G4442 — pyr

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G4442-pyr.md`

**Occurrences:** 74

Fire — literal or figurative. 74 NT tokens across 14 books. Fire from heaven authenticates both true (1 Kings 18:38; Acts 2:3) and counterfeit (Rev 13:13) claims. The earth beast makes fire descend from heaven as Elijah/Pentecost parody.

**Semantic categories:** Literal Fire, Judgment/Purification Fire, Authentication Fire, Lake of Fire

**Key verses:** Rev 13:13, Rev 19:20, Rev 20:10, Acts 2:3, 1 Kings 18:38

**Relevance:** Pyr ek tou ouranou at Rev 13:13 counterfeits the Elijah fire (1 Kings 18:38) and Pentecost fire (Acts 2:3). Spirit-parody function #2: fire from heaven for false authentication.

### G1497 — eidolon

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G1497-eidolon.md`

**Occurrences:** 11

Idol, image for worship — from eidos (form/appearance). 11 NT occurrences. Distinguished from eikon: eidolon stresses emptiness, eikon stresses genuine likeness. Rev 9:20 uses eidolon for old-style idols; Rev 13:14 deliberately uses eikon — the beast image is NOT a conventional empty idol.

**Semantic categories:** Pagan Idol/False God, Idol Food/Offering, Empty/Nothing Representation

**Key verses:** Rev 9:20, 1 Cor 8:4, 1 Cor 10:19, Acts 7:41

**Relevance:** The eikon/eidolon distinction confirms the beast image is NOT a conventional idol. Rev 9:20 uses eidolon for traditional idols; Rev 13:14 uses eikon = genuine structural replica. The image has institutional capacity (pneuma, speech, enforcement) that no eidolon ever possessed.

### G3667 — homoioma

**Source:** `bible-studies/word-studies/G3667-homoioma.md`

**Occurrences:** 6

Likeness, similitude — the concrete embodiment of a resemblance. Cross-linked with eikon and eidolon. Distinguished from homoios (adjective = 'similar') and eikon (visible representation): homoioma emphasizes the resulting form that embodies the resemblance.

**Semantic categories:** Concrete Likeness/Form, Theological Likeness — Incarnation, Idol Likeness

**Key verses:** Rom 1:23, Rom 8:3, Phil 2:7

**Relevance:** Cross-reference for the image vocabulary cluster. Homoioma fills the gap between abstract similarity (homoios) and visual representation (eikon), informing the nature of the beast image as a genuine structural likeness.
