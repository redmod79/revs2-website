## Question

## Prior Research Summary
Seven prior studies provide directly relevant findings. revs-03 documented the threefold division of Rev 1:19 (TM012, TM013, TM016, SP008 Moderate validation) and concluded IC007: Rev 1:19 provides a general content-scope description rather than a strict structural outline. revs-04 documented 9 verbal parallels between prologue and epilogue (SP009 Strong validation) including ha dei genesthai (TM015), ho kairos eggys (TM017), Alpha-Omega title expansion (TM018), sevenfold makarios (TM019), tachy triple (TM020), charis grace-frame (TM021), martyreo word family (TM022), and the Dan 12:4 seal reversal (TM023). revs2-01 established the fourfold en pneumati division with two verb-pairs (ginomai vs. apophero), kai eidon catalog (~35 structural uses), meta tauta catalog (9 occurrences), and the Sinai-theophany formula. Web research identified three competing scholarly camps: traditional threefold (McLean), eclectic idealist (Beale — all three clauses describe the whole book), and chiastic/concentric (Strand, Schussler Fiorenza). Key unresolved question: Michaels' epexegetical reading of ha eisin — if it means 'what they are/mean' rather than 'things which are now,' the threefold-division reading is fundamentally undermined.

### Focus Areas

1. **The Greek clause structure of Revelation 1:19:**
This is the foundational linguistic question. Run `greek_parser.py --verse 'REV 1:19'` and `greek_parser.py --clause 'REV 1:19'` to get the exact morphological data. Three verb forms create the temporal framework: aorist eides ('you saw'), present eisin ('are'), and present mellei + aorist infinitive genesthai ('about to happen'). READ `$WORD_STUDIES/G3195-mello.md` for mello's semantic range — does mello + infinitive always denote futurity, or can it denote imminence or inevitability? READ `$WORD_STUDIES/G1096-ginomai.md` for ginomai's range. The critical question from Michaels is whether ha eisin can be read as 'what they are' (epexegetical, explaining what was seen) rather than 'things which are now' (temporal, referring to present realities). If epexegetical, 1:19 collapses from threefold to twofold: 'write what you saw — namely what they mean — and what is about to happen.' Compare Rev 1:20's immediate use of eides to explain the mystery of the lampstands — does 1:20 function as the fulfillment of the middle clause?
**Verses:** Rev 1:19, Rev 1:20, Rev 4:1
**Strong's:** G1125, G3195, G1096, G1510
