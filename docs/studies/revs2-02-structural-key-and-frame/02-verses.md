# Verses

## Question
Does Revelation 1:19 ("write the things which thou hast seen, and the things which are, and the things which shall be hereafter") provide a structural key for the entire book, and how does each clause map to sections of the text? How does the prologue-epilogue inclusio (Rev 1:1-8 / Rev 22:6-21) frame the visions — what verbal parallels connect the opening and closing, and how do they create a literary bracket? What is the relationship between the 1:19 threefold division and the fourfold en pneumati division from study 01?

### Prologue opening — transmission chain and ha dei genesthai en tachei

> The Revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave unto him, to shew unto his servants things which must shortly come to pass; and he sent and signified [it] by his angel unto his servant John:

**Context:** Opening verse of the book establishing the revelatory chain: God -> Christ -> angel -> John -> servants. Contains ha dei genesthai en tachei, the Daniel 2:28 allusion, and esemainen (signified) indicating the mode of communication is through signs/symbols.

**Notes:** Five key vocabulary items: apokalypsis (G602), deixai (G1166, show), doulois (G1401, servants), dei genesthai (G1163+G1096, must come to pass), en tachei (G5034, shortly/quickly), esemainen (G4591, signified), angelou (G32, angel). The transmission chain reappears at 22:6 and 22:16 creating an inclusio. The verbal link to Dan 2:28 LXX (ha dei genesthai) transforms 'in the latter days' to 'en tachei' (shortly).

### John's testimony — martyria vocabulary chain

> Who bare record of the word of God, and of the testimony of Jesus Christ, and of all things that he saw.

**Context:** John's credentials as witness — he bore record (emartyesen, from martyreo G3140) of the word of God and the testimony (martyria G3141) of Jesus Christ, and all things he saw (eiden, aorist of horao G3708).

**Notes:** The martyreo/martyria word family appears here at the start and returns in the epilogue (22:16, 18, 20). The aorist eiden (he saw) parallels the aorist eides (thou hast seen) in 1:19. Three objects of testimony: word of God, testimony of Jesus, all things he saw.

### First beatitude — makarios with ho kairos eggys

> Blessed [is] he that readeth, and they that hear the words of this prophecy, and keep those things which are written therein: for the time [is] at hand.

**Context:** First of seven makarios beatitudes in Revelation (1:3, 14:13, 16:15, 19:9, 20:6, 22:7, 22:14). Three present participles: ho anaginoskon (the one reading), hoi akouontes (those hearing), terountes (those keeping). Ho kairos eggys (the time is near) echoed at 22:10.

**Notes:** Parallels with 22:7 — both have makarios + keeping the words/sayings of prophecy. The phrase 'words of this prophecy' (logous tes propheteias) recurs at 22:7, 10, 18. Ho kairos eggys at 1:3 and ho kairos gar eggys estin at 22:10 form an inclusio element.

### Epistolary greeting — charis, temporal title, seven Spirits

> John to the seven churches which are in Asia: Grace [be] unto you, and peace, from him which is, and which was, and which is to come; and from the seven Spirits which are before his throne;

**Context:** Epistolary prescript matching Pauline convention (charis hymin kai eirene). Contains the temporal divine title: ho on (present participle of eimi, 'the one who is') kai ho en (imperfect of eimi, 'the one who was') kai ho erchomenos (present participle of erchomai, 'the coming one'). Seven Spirits (hepta pneumaton) before the throne.

**Notes:** The temporal title order here is present-past-future. At 4:8 the order reverses to past-present-future (ho en kai ho on kai ho erchomenos). The charis opening at 1:4 matches the charis closing at 22:21, creating the epistolary frame (SP010).

### Christ as faithful witness — martys pistos; doxology

> And from Jesus Christ, [who is] the faithful witness, [and] the first begotten of the dead, and the prince of the kings of the earth. Unto him that loved us, and washed us from our sins in his own blood, And hath made us kings and priests unto God and his Father; to him [be] glory and dominion for ever and ever. Amen.

**Context:** Christ described with three titles: ho martys ho pistos (the faithful witness, G3144), ho prototokos ton nekron (firstborn of the dead), ho archon ton basileon tes ges (ruler of kings of the earth). Followed by doxology.

**Notes:** Martys (G3144) occurs 5 times in Revelation (1:5, 2:13, 3:14, 11:3, 17:6). The 'faithful witness' title appears also at 3:14. The phrase lysanti hemas ek ton hamartion hemon en to haimati autou uses en + dative instrumental (by his blood).

### Coming with clouds — composite Dan 7:13 + Zech 12:10 allusion

> Behold, he cometh with clouds; and every eye shall see him, and they [also] which pierced him: and all kindreds of the earth shall wail because of him. Even so, Amen.

**Context:** Composite OT allusion: 'cometh with clouds' from Dan 7:13 (one like the Son of man came with the clouds of heaven) + 'they shall look upon me whom they have pierced' from Zech 12:10. The phrase 'every eye shall see him' is unique to Revelation.

**Notes:** The erchomai here is present tense (erchetai, third person — 'he comes'). In the epilogue, erchomai shifts to first person (erchomai, 'I come' at 22:7, 12, 20). This person-shift from 3rd to 1st is a documented structural feature (TM020 area). The parallels tool confirms Dan 7:13 as the top OT parallel (0.411 hybrid score).

### Alpha and Omega self-declaration with temporal title

> I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the ending, saith the Lord, which is, and which was, and which is to come, the Almighty.

**Context:** First Alpha-Omega occurrence. Speaker is 'the Lord God' (Kyrios ho Theos). Contains the temporal title (ho on kai ho en kai ho erchomenos) plus ho Pantokrator (the Almighty). The Greek text reads: Ego eimi to Alpha kai to O.

**Notes:** This is the first of four Alpha-Omega occurrences (1:8, 1:11 TR only, 21:6, 22:13). The expansion pattern: 1:8 has Alpha/Omega + beginning/ending + temporal title; 22:13 adds first/last (protos/eschatos). The NT parallel to 22:13 confirms 1:8 as the top match (0.469 hybrid). The OT source for 'first and last' is in Isaiah (41:4, 44:6, 48:12).

### First en pneumati marker — I John, Patmos, Lord's day

> I John, who also am your brother, and companion in tribulation, and in the kingdom and patience of Jesus Christ, was in the isle that is called Patmos, for the word of God, and for the testimony of Jesus Christ. I was in the Spirit on the Lord's day, and heard behind me a great voice, as of a trumpet,

**Context:** First en pneumati marker: egenomyn en pneumati (I became in the Spirit). John identifies himself ('I John'), his location (Patmos), and the occasion (Lord's day). The great voice 'as of a trumpet' (hos salpingos) echoed at 4:1 ('the first voice which I heard was as it were of a trumpet').

**Notes:** The verb egenomyn (G1096, aorist middle indicative 1st sg) is used for the first two en pneumati markers (1:10, 4:2) with John as the grammatical subject. At 17:3 and 21:10 the verb shifts to apenengken (G667, apophero, aorist active) with the angel as subject. This verb-pair distinction was documented in revs2-01. The phrase 'for the word of God and for the testimony of Jesus Christ' uses the same martyria vocabulary from 1:2.

### First graphon command — write in a book; seven churches

> Saying, I am Alpha and Omega, the first and the last: and, What thou seest, write in a book, and send [it] unto the seven churches which are in Asia; unto Ephesus, and unto Smyrna, and unto Pergamos, and unto Thyatira, and unto Sardis, and unto Philadelphia, and unto Laodicea.

**Context:** First write command. The KJV includes 'I am Alpha and Omega, the first and the last' which is present in the Textus Receptus but ABSENT from the N1904/Nestle-Aland critical text. The text comparison confirms this is a textual variant — TR has ego to alpha kai to omega, ho protos kai ho eschatos, while N1904 begins directly with ho blepeis grapson eis biblion.

**Notes:** The TR/N1904 variant is significant: if the Alpha-Omega title is original at 1:11, there are four occurrences; if it is a TR addition, there are three. The text comparison shows 31 words in N1904 vs 36 in TR, with Strong's numbers unique to TR including G1 (alpha), G5598 (omega), G4413 (protos), G2078 (eschatos). The graphon command 'write in a book' (grapson eis biblion) begins the series of 12 write commands documented in TM014.

### THE crux text — threefold temporal command

> Write the things which thou hast seen, and the things which are, and the things which shall be hereafter;

**Context:** The central verse of this study. Three clauses with distinct verb forms: (1) ha eides — 'things which thou hast seen' (aorist active indicative 2nd sg of horao G3708); (2) ha eisin — 'things which are' (present active indicative 3rd pl of eimi G1510); (3) ha mellei genesthai meta tauta — 'things which shall be hereafter' (mellei: present active indicative 3rd sg of mello G3195 + genesthai: aorist middle infinitive of ginomai G1096 + meta tauta: after these things).

**Notes:** Greek text: grapson oun ha eides kai ha eisin kai ha mellei genesthai meta tauta. The graphon is aorist active imperative 2nd sg (V-AAM-2S). The critical question is whether ha eisin (R-NPN + V-PAI-3P) functions temporally ('things which now are') or epexegetically ('what they mean/signify'), as Michaels argues. The grammar reference on epexegetical clauses shows that ha eisin is syntactically identical to Revelation's symbol-identification clauses (4:5, 5:8, 17:9, 17:12, 17:15) where eisin decodes rather than temporalizes. Rev 1:20 immediately follows with the same eides + eisin construction used to decode the mystery of the stars and lampstands, supporting the epexegetical reading.

### Mystery decoded — eides echo and explanatory eisin

> The mystery of the seven stars which thou sawest in my right hand, and the seven golden candlesticks. The seven stars are the angels of the seven churches: and the seven candlesticks which thou sawest are the seven churches.

**Context:** Immediate continuation of 1:19 — the mysterion (G3466) is decoded. Uses eides (aorist of horao, 'thou sawest') echoing 1:19, and eisin (present of eimi, V-PAI-3P) twice in the identifications: 'the seven stars ARE the angels' and 'the seven candlesticks ARE the seven churches.'

**Notes:** The eisin here clearly functions as a copula of identification, not a temporal marker. The stars ARE (= represent) the angels; the lampstands ARE (= represent) the churches. This is the epexegetical use documented in the grammar reference. The fact that 1:20 immediately follows 1:19 and uses the same verb (eisin) in an explanatory function supports reading 1:19's ha eisin as 'what they mean' rather than 'things which are now.' The boundary between 'things seen' (eides) and 'things which are' (eisin) is blurred — 1:20 uses eides to refer to the very objects that eisin then explains.

### Major structural transition — meta tauta + dei genesthai + first voice echo

> After this I looked, and, behold, a door [was] opened in heaven: and the first voice which I heard [was] as it were of a trumpet talking with me; which said, Come up hither, and I will shew thee things which must be hereafter.

**Context:** Strongest structural transition in the book. Meta tauta eidon (after this I looked) + 'the first voice' (he phone he prote) echoing the voice of 1:10. The final clause 'I will shew thee things which must be hereafter' (deixo soi ha dei genesthai meta tauta) uses the same dei genesthai formula from 1:1 and 1:19, but substitutes meta tauta for en tachei.

**Notes:** This is the ONLY point where meta tauta eidon and en pneumati (4:2) co-occur at the same boundary, making it the strongest structural marker in Revelation (documented in revs2-01). The verbal chain: 1:1 ha dei genesthai en tachei / 1:19 ha mellei genesthai meta tauta / 4:1 ha dei genesthai meta tauta / 22:6 ha dei genesthai en tachei. The 4:1 version blends elements from both 1:1 and 1:19. The phrase 'the first voice which I heard' (he phone he prote hen ekousa) uses protos (G4413) explicitly linking back to the trumpet voice of 1:10.

### Second en pneumati marker — eutheos egenomyn en pneumati

> And immediately I was in the spirit: and, behold, a throne was set in heaven, and [one] sat on the throne.

**Context:** Second en pneumati marker. Eutheos (immediately) + egenomyn en pneumati (I became in the Spirit) — same verb form as 1:10 (egenomyn, V-2ADI-1S, aorist middle indicative 1st singular of ginomai). This is the only verse where meta tauta (from 4:1) and en pneumati co-occur at the same boundary.

**Notes:** The eutheos (G2112, immediately) adds urgency not present at 1:10. The throne-room vision that follows (4:2-11) sets the scene for the seal sequence. Under the threefold scheme, this is the beginning of 'things which shall be hereafter.' Under the fourfold scheme, this is Vision 2 (4:1-16:21).

### Third en pneumati marker — apenengken me en pneumati (wilderness)

> And there came one of the seven angels which had the seven vials, and talked with me, saying unto me, Come hither; I will shew unto thee the judgment of the great whore that sitteth upon many waters: With whom the kings of the earth have committed fornication, and the inhabitants of the earth have been made drunk with the wine of her fornication. So he carried me away in the spirit into the wilderness: and I saw a woman sit upon a scarlet coloured beast, full of names of blasphemy, having seven heads and ten horns.

**Context:** Third en pneumati marker. The verb shifts from egenomyn (1:10, 4:2) to apenengken (G667, apophero, aorist active indicative 3rd sg) — the angel carries John, rather than John becoming in the Spirit. Location: wilderness (eremon). This passage has extensive verbal parallels with 21:9-10.

**Notes:** Shared elements with 21:9-10 documented in revs2-01: (1) 'one of the seven angels which had the seven vials' (both), (2) 'talked with me, saying' (both), (3) 'Come hither, I will shew unto thee' (both), (4) apenengken me en pneumati (both), (5) location (wilderness vs mountain), (6) woman figure (harlot vs bride). These form VP001 (Strong, 6+ shared elements). Under the threefold scheme, 17:1 falls within 'things which shall be hereafter' with no 1:19 signal at this boundary.

### Fourth en pneumati marker — apenengken me en pneumati (mountain)

> And there came unto me one of the seven angels which had the seven vials full of the seven last plagues, and talked with me, saying, Come hither, I will shew thee the bride, the Lamb's wife. And he carried me away in the spirit to a great and high mountain, and shewed me that great city, the holy Jerusalem, descending out of heaven from God,

**Context:** Fourth en pneumati marker. Same verb apenengken (G667) as 17:3, same agent (one of the seven bowl angels), same invitation formula ('Come hither, I will shew thee'). Location: great and high mountain (oros mega kai hypselon). The antithetical parallel: wilderness/harlot/Babylon vs. mountain/bride/Jerusalem.

**Notes:** The verbal parallel with 17:1-3 is documented as VP001 (Strong validation). The en pneumati is confirmed by parsing: apenengken (V-AAI-3S from apophero G667) me en Pneumati (PREP + N-DSN). The deiknymi verb (edeixen, V-AAI-3S from deiknymi G1166) appears at 21:10 and connects to the transmission chain verb at 1:1 (deixai, V-AAN) and 22:6 (deixai, V-AAN).

### Epilogue opening — faithful and true; transmission chain echo

> And he said unto me, These sayings [are] faithful and true: and the Lord God of the holy prophets sent his angel to shew unto his servants the things which must shortly be done.

**Context:** Epilogue opening echoing 1:1. Key shared elements: (1) apestellen ton angelon autou (sent his angel) — 1:1 aposteilas dia tou angelou autou; (2) deixai tois doulois autou (to shew unto his servants) — identical infinitive and recipients; (3) ha dei genesthai en tachei (things which must shortly come to pass) — verbatim five-word match with 1:1.

**Notes:** The five-word Greek phrase ha dei genesthai en tachei is identical in both 1:1 and 22:6, confirmed by parser output. The phrase 'faithful and true' (pistoi kai alethinoi) connects to the 'faithful witness' (martys pistos) at 1:5 and 'Faithful and True' (Pistos kai Alethinos) at 19:11. The transmission chain in 22:6 is: Lord God -> angel -> servants. Parsing confirms: dei (V-PAI-3S), genesthai (V-2ADN), en (PREP), tachei (N-DSN from tachos G5034).

### Second epilogue beatitude — I come quickly + keeping prophecy

> Behold, I come quickly: blessed [is] he that keepeth the sayings of the prophecy of this book.

**Context:** First of three 'I come quickly' (erchomai tachy) statements in the epilogue (22:7, 12, 20). Contains the second epilogue beatitude (makarios G3107). Echoes 1:3 — both have makarios + keeping + words/sayings of prophecy.

**Notes:** Parsing confirms: erchomai (V-PNI-1S, present middle/passive indicative 1st sg) — first person, contrasting with 1:7 erchetai (3rd person). Tachy (G5035, ADV). Makarios (A-NSM). The 1:3/22:7 parallel: 1:3 has 'words of this prophecy' (logous tes propheteias) + terountes (keeping, V-PAP-NPM); 22:7 has 'sayings of the prophecy of this book' (logous tes propheteias tou bibliou toutou) + teron (keeping, V-PAP-NSM). Shared elements: makarios, tereo, logous tes propheteias = 3 shared vocabulary items (Moderate VP).

### I John self-identification; worship refusal parallel to 19:10

> And I John saw these things, and heard [them]. And when I had heard and seen, I fell down to worship before the feet of the angel which shewed me these things. Then saith he unto me, See [thou do it] not: for I am thy fellowservant, and of thy brethren the prophets, and of them which keep the sayings of this book: worship God.

**Context:** 'I John' (kago Ioannes) echoes 1:9 (ego Ioannes). Worship refusal parallels 19:10 — both end with 'worship God' (to theo proskyneson). The angel identifies himself as 'thy fellowservant' (syndoulos).

**Notes:** The 'I John' self-identification appears at 1:9 and 22:8, forming an inclusio element within the vision frame. The worship refusal at 19:10 and 22:8-9 creates an internal pair (documented by Bauckham) — both occur at structural boundaries (19:10 at the end of Babylon vision, 22:8-9 in the epilogue). Shared vocabulary between 19:10 and 22:8-9: proskyneso (worship), prosthes (fell down), syndoulos (fellowservant), to theo proskyneson (worship God).

### Seal not the sayings — Daniel 12:4 reversal; ho kairos eggys

> And he saith unto me, Seal not the sayings of the prophecy of this book: for the time is at hand.

**Context:** Reversal of Dan 12:4 ('shut up the words and seal the book'). The prohibitive subjunctive me sphragises (V-AAS-2S, aorist active subjunctive 2nd sg from sphragizo G4972) prohibits the specific act of sealing. 'The time is at hand' (ho kairos gar eggys estin) echoes 1:3 (ho gar kairos eggys).

**Notes:** Parsing confirms the prohibitive subjunctive construction: me (PRT-N, G3361) + sphragises (V-AAS-2S from sphragizo G4972). The grammar reference on prohibitive subjunctive documents this as prohibiting the initiation of a specific act. Dan 12:4 commands sealing; Rev 22:10 prohibits it — an explicit reversal. The kairos eggys echo between 1:3 and 22:10 is documented as TM017. Parallels tool confirms Dan 12:4 as a match (0.337 hybrid, with 'seal, time' as shared keywords).

### I come quickly + reward; expanded Alpha/Omega/first/last/beginning/end title

> And, behold, I come quickly; and my reward [is] with me, to give every man according as his work shall be. I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the end, the first and the last.

**Context:** Second 'I come quickly' + the fully expanded divine title combining all three pairs: Alpha/Omega (to Alpha kai to O), beginning/end (he arche kai to telos, G746+G5056), first/last (ho protos kai ho eschatos, G4413+G2078). This is the maximum title expansion in Revelation.

**Notes:** Parser confirms: protos (A-NSM-S from protos G4413), eschatos (A-NSM-S from eschatos G2078), arche (N-NSF from arche G746), telos (N-NSN from telos G5056). The title expansion pattern: 1:8 Alpha/Omega only; 1:17 first/last only; 21:6 Alpha/Omega + beginning/end; 22:13 all three pairs combined. The 'first and last' is an YHWH self-identification from Isa 41:4, 44:6, 48:12. NT parallels confirm 1:8 (0.469) and 21:6 (0.434) as the strongest matches.

### Sixth beatitude — commandments, tree of life, gates

> Blessed [are] they that do his commandments, that they may have right to the tree of life, and may enter in through the gates into the city.

**Context:** Sixth of seven makarios beatitudes. 'Tree of life' (to xylon tes zoes) connects to the church promise at 2:7 (eat of the tree of life in the paradise of God). 'Gates into the city' connects to New Jerusalem description at 21:12-13.

**Notes:** This verse creates a chiastic link between the church letters (2:7 tree of life promise) and the epilogue. The seven makarios distribution: 1:3 (prologue), 14:13 (Vision 2), 16:15 (Vision 2), 19:9 (boundary), 20:6 (Vision 2/3), 22:7 (epilogue), 22:14 (epilogue). Three of seven (43%) fall in the prologue-epilogue frame.

### I Jesus — transmission chain + ekklesia return

> I Jesus have sent mine angel to testify unto you these things in the churches. I am the root and the offspring of David, [and] the bright and morning star.

**Context:** The only verse outside chapters 1-3 (and the rare 22:16) that uses ekklesia (G1577). Jesus speaks in first person: 'I have sent mine angel to testify (martyresai, aorist infinitive of martyreo G3140) unto you these things in the churches (tais ekklesiais).'

**Notes:** This verse echoes the transmission chain of 1:1 but with Christ as the explicit sender: 'I Jesus have sent mine angel' (ego Iesous epempsa ton angelon mou). The return of ekklesia in the epilogue after its complete absence from Visions 2-4 (chs. 4-22:5) is structurally significant — it creates a literary bracket with the church letters. The martyreo verb (martyresai) connects to the witness vocabulary chain (1:2, 1:5, 22:18, 22:20).

### Spirit and bride invitation — Come; water of life

> And the Spirit and the bride say, Come. And let him that heareth say, Come. And let him that is athirst come. And whosoever will, let him take the water of life freely.

**Context:** The pneuma (G4151, Spirit) appears in the epilogue alongside the bride. The imperative erchou (come, from erchomai G2064) shifts to invitation — not the coming of Christ, but the invitation to the thirsty. The 'water of life' (to hydor zoes) connects to 21:6 and 22:1.

**Notes:** The erchomai usage here completes the person-shift documented in the study: 1:7 erchetai (3rd person, 'he comes'), 22:7/12/20 erchomai (1st person, 'I come'), 22:17 erchou (imperative/invitation, 'Come!'). The pneuma in the epilogue connects to the en pneumati formula of the four visions and the 'seven Spirits' of 1:4, creating pneuma as a vocabulary thread spanning the entire book.

### Canon warning — adding/taking away from words of prophecy

> For I testify unto every man that heareth the words of the prophecy of this book, If any man shall add unto these things, God shall add unto him the plagues that are written in this book: And if any man shall take away from the words of the book of this prophecy, God shall take away his part out of the book of life, and out of the holy city, and [from] the things which are written in this book.

**Context:** Canon-closing warning using martyreo (G3140, 'I testify'). The phrase 'words of the prophecy of this book' (logous tes propheteias tou bibliou toutou) echoes 22:7 and 22:10, and ultimately 1:3 ('words of this prophecy'). Adding/taking away echoes Deut 4:2 and 12:32.

**Notes:** The martyreo verb (summartyro, I testify) connects to the witness vocabulary chain. The biblion (G975, book) in 'book of this prophecy' and 'book of life' creates a double reference — the scroll of Revelation's prophecy and the eschatological book of life. The warning frames the text as complete and inviolable.

### He which testifieth — third I come quickly + Maranatha response

> He which testifieth these things saith, Surely I come quickly. Amen. Even so, come, Lord Jesus.

**Context:** Third and final 'I come quickly' (erchomai tachy). The martyron (present participle of martyreo G3140, 'the one testifying') identifies the speaker. John's response 'Even so, come, Lord Jesus' (erchou kyrie Iesou) echoes the Maranatha formula (1 Cor 16:22).

**Notes:** The triple tachy (G5035) in the epilogue (22:7, 12, 20) is documented as TM020. The progression: 22:7 connects to beatitude, 22:12 connects to reward/judgment, 22:20 is the final promise with liturgical response. The erchou (imperative, 'Come!') from John is the same form used in 22:17 by the Spirit and bride.

### Epistolary grace benediction — charis closing

> The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ [be] with you all. Amen.

**Context:** Final verse. Epistolary closing matching Pauline convention. Charis (G5485) at 22:21 forms an inclusio with charis at 1:4. The N1904 text reads: he charis tou kyriou Iesou meta panton — shorter than the KJV/TR version which adds 'Christ' and 'Amen.'

**Notes:** Parsing confirms: charis (N-NSF from charis G5485), meta (PREP from meta G3326), panton (A-GPM from pas G3956). The 1:4/22:21 charis frame is documented as TM021 and contributes to SP010 (epistolary frame, Moderate validation). The structural implication: Revelation opens and closes as a letter, despite its apocalyptic content, creating a genre hybrid (letter + prophecy + apocalypse).

### OT source for ha dei genesthai — 'what shall be in the latter days'

> But there is a God in heaven that revealeth secrets, and maketh known to the king Nebuchadnezzar what shall be in the latter days. Thy dream, and the visions of thy head upon thy bed, are these; As for thee, O king, thy thoughts came [into thy mind] upon thy bed, what should come to pass hereafter: and he that revealeth secrets maketh known to thee what shall come to pass.

**Context:** The source passage for Revelation's ha dei genesthai formula. Dan 2:28 LXX: ha dei genesthai ep' eschatwn ton hemeron ('what must happen in the last days'). Rev 1:1 transforms the temporal frame from 'in the latter days' to 'en tachei' (shortly/quickly). Dan 2:29 adds 'what should come to pass hereafter' (meta tauta), providing the source for Rev 4:1's meta tauta.

**Notes:** The verbal chain: Dan 2:28 LXX ha dei genesthai ep' eschatwn ton hemeron -> Rev 1:1 ha dei genesthai en tachei -> Rev 4:1 ha dei genesthai meta tauta -> Rev 22:6 ha dei genesthai en tachei. The three shared Greek words (dei, genesthai + the relative pronoun ha) create a Strong allusion. The temporal shift from 'in the latter days' to 'shortly' indicates John views himself as living within the eschatological fulfillment period.

### What shall come to pass hereafter — meta tauta echo

> Forasmuch as thou sawest that the stone was cut out of the mountain without hands, and that it brake in pieces the iron, the brass, the clay, the silver, and the gold; the great God hath made known to the king what shall come to pass hereafter: and the dream [is] certain, and the interpretation thereof sure.

**Context:** Daniel's conclusion echoing 2:28-29. 'What shall come to pass hereafter' uses the same prophetic disclosure formula. The 'great God hath made known' parallels Rev 1:1's God gave/showed. The phrase 'the dream is certain, and the interpretation thereof sure' parallels Rev 22:6 'these sayings are faithful and true.'

### Prohibition of writing — graphon negated

**Context:** Reversal of the writing command pattern. ### Temporal title variation — was, is, is to come (order differs)

> And the four beasts had each of them six wings about [him]; and [they were] full of eyes within: and they rest not day and night, saying, Holy, holy, holy, Lord God Almighty, which was, and is, and is to come.

**Context:** Living creatures' Trisagion with temporal title. Order differs from 1:4,8: here 'which was (ho en), and is (ho on), and is to come (ho erchomenos)' — past-present-future rather than present-past-future.

**Notes:** Parsing confirms the reversed order: ho en (V-IAI-3S, imperfect) kai ho on (V-PAP-NSM, present participle) kai ho erchomenos (V-PNP-NSM, present middle participle). At 1:4 and 1:8 the order is ho on kai ho en kai ho erchomenos (present-past-future). This variation suggests the formula describes an attribute of God rather than being a rigid structural template. Part of TM016 (5 temporal title instances).

### Temporal title — which art and wast (drops 'is to come' in some MSS)

> Saying, We give thee thanks, O Lord God Almighty, which art, and wast, and art to come; because thou hast taken to thee thy great power, and hast reigned.

**Context:** Temporal title in the seventh trumpet. Some manuscripts omit 'and art to come' (kai ho erchomenos), leaving only ho on kai ho en. If original, this two-element version (dropping the future component) is significant — at the seventh trumpet, the 'coming' has arrived.

**Notes:** The possible omission of ho erchomenos at 11:17 is theologically loaded: if God's 'coming' has been realized at the seventh trumpet, the future element drops from the title. This would make 11:17 a three-to-two reduction, contrasting with 17:8's beast parody. Part of TM016.

### Temporal title variation — which art and wast and shalt be

> And I heard the angel of the waters say, Thou art righteous, O Lord, which art, and wast, and shalt be, because thou hast judged thus.

**Context:** Temporal title during the bowl judgments. The KJV reads 'shalt be' rather than 'is to come' — some textual traditions have ho hosios (the holy one) rather than ho esomenos (the one who will be) or ho erchomenos.

**Notes:** Textual variant — the third element varies across manuscripts. The KJV 'shalt be' differs from the standard 'is to come' (erchomenos). Part of TM016's five temporal title instances.

### Beast's anti-temporal formula — was, is not, yet is

> The beast that thou sawest was, and is not; and shall ascend out of the bottomless pit, and go into perdition: and they that dwell on the earth shall wonder, whose names were not written in the book of life from the foundation of the world, when they behold the beast that was, and is not, and yet is.

**Context:** The beast's temporal parody: 'was, and is not, and yet is' (en kai ouk estin kai parestai). This inverts the divine title pattern — where God 'is, was, is to come,' the beast 'was, is not, yet is.' The parody confirms the temporal triad is a characterological device, not unique to 1:19's structure.

**Notes:** Part of TM016. The beast's parody demonstrates that temporal triads in Revelation characterize entities rather than organize the book's structure. The divine title uses positive being (is/was/coming); the beast uses negative being (is not). Also connects to 17:10 'five are fallen, one is, the other is not yet come' — another temporal triad applied to a non-structural entity (seven kings).
