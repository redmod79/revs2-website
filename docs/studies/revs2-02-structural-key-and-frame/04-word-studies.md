# Word Studies

## Question
Does Revelation 1:19 ("write the things which thou hast seen, and the things which are, and the things which shall be hereafter") provide a structural key for the entire book, and how does each clause map to sections of the text? How does the prologue-epilogue inclusio (Rev 1:1-8 / Rev 22:6-21) frame the visions — what verbal parallels connect the opening and closing, and how do they create a literary bracket? What is the relationship between the 1:19 threefold division and the fourfold en pneumati division from study 01?

### G1125 — grapho

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1125-grapho.md`

**Occurrences:** 216

Primary verb meaning 'to write.' 197 occurrences in 26 NT books. Revelation has 28 occurrences (6.9 per 100 verses), the highest density of any book. The dominant form is the perfect passive gegrapai ('it is written,' 52% of all uses). In Revelation, the aorist active imperative grapson ('Write!') appears 12 times: 2 general commands (1:11, 1:19), 7 church-specific (2:1,8,12,18; 3:1,7,14), 1 prohibition (10:4 me grapses), 2 later (14:13, 21:5). The distribution spans all proposed structural divisions.

**Semantic categories:** written revelation, prophetic inscription, scriptural authority

**Key verses:** Rev 1:11, Rev 1:19, Rev 10:4, Rev 14:13, Rev 21:5

**Relevance:** The graphon distribution (TM014) is critical evidence that writing commands span the entire book, cross-cutting the threefold division of 1:19. If the writing activity covers all visions, the writing mandate of 1:19 cannot be confined to one section.

### G3195 — mello

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3195-mello.md`

**Occurrences:** 109

Verb meaning 'to be about to, to intend, to be destined.' 106 unique verses, 109 tokens across 18 NT books. Revelation has 12 occurrences (3.0 per 100 verses). Forms: present active indicative (33%), present active participle (47%), imperfect active indicative (18%). The extreme translation variety (60+ renderings) reflects mello's function as an auxiliary verb. In Revelation: 1:19 (mellei genesthai, structural), 2:10 (melleis paschein, Smyrna), 3:10 (mellouses erchesthai, Philadelphia), 3:16 (mello se emesai, Laodicea), 10:4 (emellon graphein), and others.

**Semantic categories:** imminence, futurity, divine necessity, intention

**Key verses:** Rev 1:19, Rev 2:10, Rev 3:10, Rev 3:16, Rev 10:4

**Relevance:** The presence of mello within the church letters (2:10, 3:10, 3:16 — TM013) is one of the strongest arguments against a strict threefold structural reading of 1:19. Future-oriented vocabulary within the 'present' section makes the temporal boundaries porous.

### G1096 — ginomai

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1096-ginomai.md`

**Occurrences:** 702

Primary verb meaning 'to become, to happen, to come into being.' 631 occurrences (668 parsed forms) — one of the most common NT verbs. The aorist dominates (73%). In Revelation's structural formulas: genesthai (aorist middle infinitive) appears in ha dei genesthai en tachei (1:1, 22:6) and ha mellei genesthai meta tauta (1:19, 4:1). Also egenomyn en pneumati (1:10, 4:2) uses the aorist middle indicative 1st sg. The verb serves dual structural functions: the dei genesthai formula (Danielic allusion) and the en pneumati formula (vision markers).

**Semantic categories:** becoming, happening, transition, narrative marker

**Key verses:** Rev 1:1, Rev 1:10, Rev 1:19, Rev 4:1, Rev 4:2, Rev 22:6

**Relevance:** Ginomai appears in both the ha dei genesthai chain (linking prologue-epilogue) and the en pneumati markers (creating the fourfold division). Its dual structural role makes it a key lexical thread connecting the two structural systems this study examines.

### G1510 — eimi

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1510-eimi.md`

**Occurrences:** 155

The verb 'to be.' In Rev 1:19, eisin (V-PAI-3P, present active indicative 3rd plural) is the crux of the epexegetical vs. temporal debate. The grammar reference documents that ha eisin is syntactically identical to Revelation's symbol-identification clauses (4:5, 5:8, 17:9, 17:12, 17:15) where eisin functions as a timeless copula of identification. In the temporal divine title, eimi appears as ho on (present participle, 'the one who is') and ho en (imperfect, 'the one who was').

**Semantic categories:** existence, identity, copula, divine being

**Key verses:** Rev 1:4, Rev 1:8, Rev 1:19, Rev 1:20, Rev 4:8

**Relevance:** The eisin in 1:19 is the linguistic crux of the entire study — whether it functions temporally ('things which are now') or epexegetically ('what they mean'). The grammar reference shows both readings are syntactically valid.

### G602 — apokalypsis

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G602-apokalypsis.md`

**Occurrences:** 18

Noun meaning 'revelation, unveiling, disclosure.' Opening word of the book (Apokalypsis Iesou Christou). The genitive 'of Jesus Christ' is ambiguous — objective ('revelation about Jesus Christ') or subjective ('revelation from/by Jesus Christ'). Rev 1:1 favors the subjective reading: God gave the revelation to Christ to show to his servants.

**Semantic categories:** divine disclosure, unveiling, apocalyptic genre

**Key verses:** Rev 1:1

**Relevance:** The opening word establishes the book's genre and purpose. The transmission chain (God -> Christ -> angel -> John -> servants) embedded in 1:1 reappears at 22:6 and 22:16, forming the inclusio's structural spine.

### G1163 — dei

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1163-dei.md`

**Occurrences:** 105

Impersonal verb meaning 'it is necessary, one must.' Expresses divine necessity. In Revelation: appears in the ha dei genesthai formula (1:1, 4:1, 22:6) — always V-PAI-3S (present active indicative 3rd singular). The formula echoes Dan 2:28 LXX, where dei genesthai introduces God's disclosure of future events. The shift from Daniel's 'in the latter days' to Revelation's 'en tachei' (shortly) signals inaugurated eschatology.

**Semantic categories:** divine necessity, prophetic disclosure, eschatological compulsion

**Key verses:** Rev 1:1, Rev 4:1, Rev 22:6, Dan 2:28

**Relevance:** The dei genesthai formula creates a three-point structural thread: 1:1 (prologue) -> 4:1 (major transition) -> 22:6 (epilogue). This chain, rooted in Daniel 2:28, may be more structurally significant than 1:19's threefold scheme.

### G3107 — makarios

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3107-makarios.md`

**Occurrences:** 50

Adjective meaning 'blessed, happy, fortunate.' Occurs exactly 7 times in Revelation (1:3, 14:13, 16:15, 19:9, 20:6, 22:7, 22:14) — the sevenfold beatitude pattern. Three of seven (43%) fall in the prologue-epilogue frame (1:3, 22:7, 22:14). TM019 documents this as a structural pattern spanning the entire book.

**Semantic categories:** blessedness, beatitude, eschatological promise

**Key verses:** Rev 1:3, Rev 14:13, Rev 22:7, Rev 22:14

**Relevance:** The sevenfold makarios distribution with concentration in the frame (3 of 7) supports the inclusio structure. The 1:3/22:7 pair shares vocabulary (makarios + keeping + words of prophecy), forming a VP element.

### G5035 — tachy

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5035-tachy.md`

**Occurrences:** 12

Adverb meaning 'quickly, shortly, without delay.' 12 NT occurrences, 6 in Revelation (50% — highest concentration). Used in the 'I come quickly' (erchomai tachy) formula at Rev 2:16, 3:11, 22:7, 22:12, 22:20. Also at 11:14 ('the third woe cometh quickly'). The epilogue triple (22:7, 12, 20) is documented as TM020.

**Semantic categories:** eschatological imminence, divine promise, temporal urgency

**Key verses:** Rev 22:7, Rev 22:12, Rev 22:20, Rev 2:16, Rev 3:11

**Relevance:** The triple tachy in the epilogue (TM020) creates escalating eschatological urgency. Distinct from G5034 tachos (en tachei in 1:1, 22:6) — tachy is used for Christ's personal coming promise, while tachos describes the broader scope of events.

### G5485 — charis

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5485-charis.md`

**Occurrences:** 156

Noun meaning 'grace, favor, kindness.' In Revelation: 1:4 (charis hymin kai eirene, 'grace to you and peace') and 22:21 (he charis tou kyriou Iesou, 'the grace of the Lord Jesus'). These two occurrences create the epistolary frame (SP010, Moderate validation). The opening formula matches Pauline convention (cf. Rom 1:7, 1 Cor 1:3). The closing matches 2 Thess 3:18.

**Semantic categories:** divine favor, epistolary convention, blessing

**Key verses:** Rev 1:4, Rev 22:21

**Relevance:** The charis frame (TM021) is the strongest evidence for Revelation's epistolary character. The grace opening/closing wraps apocalyptic and prophetic content in an epistolary frame, establishing the book's genre hybridity.

**Relevance:** The en pneumati fourfold division is one of the two primary structural systems this study examines. The relationship between the fourfold en pneumati system and the threefold 1:19 system is the central unresolved question.

### G5034 — tachos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5034-tachos.md`

Neuter noun meaning 'speed, swiftness.' 7 NT occurrences, all in the fixed idiom en tachei (in speed = shortly/quickly). In Revelation: 1:1 and 22:6 both contain ha dei genesthai en tachei. The idiom functions adverbially — 'what must shortly come to pass.'

### G4592 — semeion

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4592-semeion.md`

**Occurrences:** 77

Neuter noun meaning 'sign, miracle, token.' 77 tokens in 69 verses across 11 books. Root of semaino (G4591, 'to signify'). In Revelation: 7 occurrences. Critically, Rev 1:1 uses esemainen (aorist of semaino) — 'he signified it' — indicating the mode of revelation is through signs/symbols. Revelation's content is sign-mediated communication, not direct literal description.

**Semantic categories:** sign, meaning-bearing act, semiotic communication

**Key verses:** Rev 1:1, Rev 12:1, Rev 12:3, Rev 13:13-14, Rev 15:1, Rev 16:14, Rev 19:20

**Relevance:** The esemainen at 1:1 establishes the hermeneutical principle for the entire book: the content is 'signified' (communicated through signs/symbols). This connects to the eisin as identification (explaining what the signs mean) in the epexegetical reading of 1:19.

**Occurrences:** 415

Verb meaning 'to see, to perceive, to behold.' 415 NT occurrences. In Revelation: eides (aorist active indicative 2nd sg, 'you saw') appears in 1:19 (ha eides, 'things which thou hast seen') and 1:20 (hous eides, 'which thou sawest'). The aorist eidon (1st sg, 'I saw') is the kai eidon formula (~35 structural uses in Revelation). The verb idou (imperative, 'Behold!') appears 30 times.

**Semantic categories:** vision, perception, prophetic sight, apocalyptic seeing

**Key verses:** Rev 1:2, Rev 1:19, Rev 1:20, Rev 4:1

**Relevance:** The eides in 1:19 is the aorist that defines the first clause ('things thou hast seen'). The same form at 1:20 (hous eides, 'which thou sawest') uses the vision vocabulary to introduce the explanation, blurring the boundary between 'seen' and 'are.'

### G1401 — doulos

**Semantic categories:** servant, slave, covenant relationship

**Key verses:** Rev 1:1, Rev 22:6

**Relevance:** The identical phrase tois doulois autou at 1:1 and 22:6 is part of the verbatim transmission chain echo contributing to the prologue-epilogue inclusio.

### G4396 — prophetes

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4396-prophetes.md`

**Occurrences:** 149

Noun meaning 'prophet.' In Revelation: 22:6 'the Lord God of the holy prophets' (ho kyrios ho theos ton pneumaton ton propheton — lit. 'the God of the spirits of the prophets'). Also 22:9 'thy brethren the prophets.' The prophetes vocabulary in the epilogue connects to 1:3's 'words of this prophecy.'

**Semantic categories:** prophet, prophetic office, divine spokesperson

**Key verses:** Rev 22:6, Rev 22:9

**Relevance:** Part of the prophetic vocabulary thread in the epilogue, connecting to the 'words of this prophecy' (logous tes propheteias) formula at 1:3, 22:7, 22:10, 22:18.
