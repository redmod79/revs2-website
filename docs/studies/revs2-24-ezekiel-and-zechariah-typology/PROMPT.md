3. **Temple Measuring: Outer Court Exclusion Transformation (Ezek 40-42 / Rev 11:1-2):**
Ezekiel 40:3-5 introduces a man with a measuring rod (Hebrew qaneh, 'reed') making detailed measurements of the temple complex — including inner court, outer court (Ezek 40:6-16 measures the outer court east gate explicitly), all gates, walls, and chambers. The LXX renders qaneh as kalamos (G2563). Revelation 11:1 gives John 'a reed like a rod' (kalamos homoios rabdō) — read G2563-kalamos.md to confirm occurrence pattern and LXX source. Read G3354-metreo.md for the measuring verb distribution across Rev 11 and 21. The critical transformation is in Rev 11:2: 'the court which is without the temple leave out, and measure it not; for it is given unto the Gentiles.' This aulē (outer court, G833) exclusion has NO counterpart in Ezekiel 40-42 — Ezekiel measures everything. Revelation transforms the measuring into a theologically charged act: what is measured is protected/preserved (naos and altar = worshippers); what is excluded is given over to Gentile trampling (42 months, 3.5 years). Also note the contrast within Revelation: Rev 11:1 uses a mortal reed with minimal specification vs. Rev 21:15 where an ANGEL uses a golden reed (chrysoun kalamon) and produces glorious, detailed measurements of the New Jerusalem.
