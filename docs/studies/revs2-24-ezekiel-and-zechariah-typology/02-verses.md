# Verses

## Question
How does Ezekiel serve as the most extensive OT structural template for Revelation? Map the parallel sequence: throne vision (Ezek 1 / Rev 4), scroll eating (Ezek 2:9-3:3 / Rev 10:8-10), temple measuring (Ezek 40-42 / Rev 11:1-2), Gog and Magog (Ezek 38-39 / Rev 20:7-10), river of life (Ezek 47:1-12 / Rev 22:1-2). What does Zechariah contribute? Map: horses and colors (Zech 1:8, 6:1-8 / Rev 6:1-8), lampstand (Zech 4:2 / Rev 1:12, 4:5), olive trees (Zech 4:3,11-14 / Rev 11:4), stone with seven eyes (Zech 3:9 / Rev 5:6), measuring line (Zech 2:1-2 / Rev 11:1). Where does Revelation transform rather than simply repeat its source?

### The Throne-Chariot Vision (Full Chapter): Four Living Creatures, Wheels, Firmament, Divine Glory

> See sub-entries Ezek 1:4-6, Ezek 1:7-14, Ezek 1:15-21, Ezek 1:22-28 for detailed verse analysis.

**Context:** Ezekiel 1 — Ezekiel's inaugural merkavah/throne-chariot vision by the river Chebar in Babylon (593 BC). Full chapter overview entry.

**Notes:** VP187 Strong (8 elements shared with Rev 4). See sub-entries for detailed per-section analysis. Sirach 49:8 confirms merkavah status. Source merger for Rev 4: Ezek 1 + Isa 6 = composite.

### Opening Theophany: Stormy Wind, Cloud, Fire; Four Living Creatures Appear

> Ezekiel 1:4 And I looked, and, behold, a whirlwind came out of the north, a great cloud, and a fire infolding itself, and a brightness [was] about it, and out of the midst thereof as the colour of amber, out of the midst of the fire. 1:5 Also out of the midst thereof [came] the likeness of four living creatures. And this [was] their appearance; they had the likeness of a man. 1:6 And every one had four faces, and every one had four wings.

**Context:** Ezekiel 1 — Opening of the merkavah vision. Theophanic storm from the north; four living creatures (chayyot) emerge. The foundational throne-chariot vision (593 BC, by river Chebar).

**Notes:** H2416 chay root: chayyot (living creatures) — the term identifying these beings before Ezek 10:20 names them kerubim. Four creatures with four faces (v.6) — face-count is the key transformation marker for Rev 4:7 (one face each). LXX renders chayyot as zōa (G2226) — Revelation adopts this term, deliberately bypassing the cherub identity confirmed in Ezek 10.

### Creatures' Feet, Hands, Faces; Fire and Lightning Between Them

> Ezekiel 1:7 And their feet [were] straight feet; and the sole of their feet [was] like the sole of a calf's foot: and they sparkled like the colour of burnished brass. 1:8 And [they had] the hands of a man under their wings on their four sides; and they four had their faces and their wings. 1:9 Their wings [were] joined one to another; they turned not when they went; they went every one straight forward. 1:10 As for the likeness of their faces, they four had the face of a man, and the face of a lion, on the right side: and they four had the face of an ox on the left side; they four also had the face of an eagle. 1:11 Thus [were] their faces: and their wings [were] stretched upward; two [wings] of every one [were] joined one to another, and two covered their bodies. 1:12 And they went every one straight forward: whither the spirit was to go, they went; [and] they turned not when they went. 1:13 As for the likeness of the living creatures, their appearance [was] like burning coals of fire, [and] like the appearance of lamps: it went up and down among the living creatures; and the fire was bright, and out of the fire went forth lightning. 1:14 And the living creatures ran and returned as the appearance of a flash of lightning.

**Context:** Ezekiel 1 — Creature morphology: calf-foot, burnished brass, four faces (man/lion/ox/eagle), four wings. Fire and lightning circulate among them.

**Notes:** 1:10 face order: man/lion (right) / ox (left) / eagle — four faces per creature simultaneously. Rev 4:7 assigns ONE face per creature (lion/calf/man/eagle) — the ox becomes calf (moschos G3448). H6440 paniym (faces) is the morphological key: each creature has a paniym-set of four orientations. 1:13 fire/lightning → Rev 4:5 lightnings from the throne. 4 wings here; Rev 4:8 has 6 wings (source: Isa 6:2, not Ezek 1).

### Wheels Full of Eyes Alongside the Creatures

> Ezekiel 1:15 Now as I beheld the living creatures, behold one wheel upon the earth by the living creatures, with his four faces. 1:16 The appearance of the wheels and their work [was] like unto the colour of a beryl: and they four had one likeness: and their appearance and their work [was] as it were a wheel in the middle of a wheel. 1:17 When they went, they went upon their four sides: [and] they turned not when they went. 1:18 As for their rings, they were so high that they were dreadful; and their rings [were] full of eyes round about them four. 1:19 And when the living creatures went, the wheels went by them: and when the living creatures were lifted up from the earth, the wheels were lifted up. 1:20 Whithersoever the spirit was to go, they went, thither [was their] spirit to go; and the wheels were lifted up over against them: for the spirit of the living creature [was] in the wheels. 1:21 When those went, [these] went; and when those stood, [these] stood; and when those were lifted up from the earth, the wheels were lifted up over against them: for the spirit of the living creature [was] in the wheels.

**Context:** Ezekiel 1 — The ophanim/wheels: beryl-colored, wheel-within-a-wheel, rings full of eyes. The wheels move in perfect synchrony with the creatures because the spirit of the creature is in the wheels.

**Notes:** 1:18 eye-location: the WHEELS (rings/rims) are full of eyes — NOT the creature bodies. Hebrew: gabbot (rims/rings) + mele'ot einayim (full of eyes). This is the critical eye-location difference from Rev 4:6 where eyes are on creature bodies ('full of eyes before and behind'). Revelation RELOCATES eyes from the wheels to the creatures themselves — a deliberate transformation. Hebrew parser: gabbot from gab (G-N-mp-Abs) = rims/curves; mele'ot (Qal Ptcp fp) = being full.

### Crystal Firmament Above Creatures; Divine Throne; Rainbow of Glory

> Ezekiel 1:22 And the likeness of the firmament upon the heads of the living creature [was] as the colour of the terrible crystal, stretched forth over their heads above. 1:23 And under the firmament [were] their wings straight, the one toward the other: every one had two, which covered on this side, and every one had two, which covered on that side, their bodies. 1:24 And when they went, I heard the noise of their wings, like the noise of great waters, as the voice of the Almighty, the voice of speech, as the noise of an host: when they stood, they let down their wings. 1:25 And there was a voice from the firmament that [was] over their heads, when they stood, [and] had let down their wings. 1:26 And above the firmament that [was] over their heads [was] the likeness of a throne, as the appearance of a sapphire stone: and upon the likeness of the throne [was] the likeness as the appearance of a man above upon it. 1:27 And I saw as the colour of amber, as the appearance of fire round about within it, from the appearance of his loins even upward, and from the appearance of his loins even downward, I saw as it were the appearance of fire, and it had brightness round about. 1:28 As the appearance of the bow that is in the cloud in the day of rain, so [was] the appearance of the brightness round about. This [was] the appearance of the likeness of the glory of the LORD. And when I saw [it], I fell upon my face, and I heard a voice of one that spake.

**Context:** Ezekiel 1 — The raqia (firmament) above the creatures like terrible crystal; the divine throne above the firmament; the kavod surrounded by rainbow. This is the climax of the merkavah vision.

**Notes:** 1:22 raqia (firmament/crystal expanse) is ABOVE the creatures. Rev 4:6 has the sea of glass like crystal BEFORE/BELOW the throne — positional transformation: Ezek firmament above → Rev sea below. 1:28 rainbow = Ezek 1:28 → Rev 4:3 rainbow around the throne, Rev 10:1 rainbow on the angel's head. 1:24 'voice like the noise of great waters' → Ezek 43:2 repeats this phrase for the returning kavod; Rev 1:15 applies it to Christ. VP187 Strong (8 elements). Sirach 49:8: 'It was Ezekiel who saw the glorious vision, which was shewed him upon the chariot of the cherubims' — confirms merkavah identity.

### The Scroll Written on Front and Back

> Ezekiel 2:9 And when I looked, behold, an hand [was] sent unto me; and, lo, a roll of a book [was] therein; 2:10 And he spread it before me; and it [was] written within and without: and [there was] written therein lamentations, and mourning, and woe.

**Context:** Ezekiel 2 — The scroll-eating commission. Before eating, Ezekiel is shown the scroll. Its content: lamentations, mourning, and woe.

**Notes:** Scroll morphology identical to Rev 5:1 ('a book written within and on the backside'). Content of Ezek's scroll is pure judgment (lamentations, mourning, woe); Rev 5:1 scroll has seven seals. The two-sided writing is the lexical link. This precedes the eating command in Ezek 3:1-3.

### Eat the Scroll — Sweet as Honey

> Ezekiel 3:1 Moreover he said unto me, Son of man, eat that thou findest; eat this roll, and go speak unto the house of Israel. 3:2 So I opened my mouth, and he caused me to eat that roll. 3:3 And he said unto me, Son of man, cause thy belly to eat, and fill thy bowels with this roll that I give thee. Then did I eat [it]; and it was in my mouth as honey for sweetness.

**Context:** Ezekiel 3 — The eating command. Honey sweetness is the ONLY taste experience. No bitterness is mentioned here.

**Notes:** AN037 Strong — core parallel to Rev 10:9-10. Key finding: Ezekiel's scroll is purely sweet ('as honey for sweetness'). No bitterness in the scroll itself or its eating. The transformation in Rev 10:10 adds intrinsic belly bitterness. Hebrew: debash (honey) — LXX uses glykasmos/meli. G1099 glykys chain confirmed.

### Bitterness of Spirit After Eating

> Ezekiel 3:14 So the spirit lifted me up, and took me away, and I went in bitterness, in the heat of my spirit; but the hand of the LORD was strong upon me.

**Context:** Ezekiel 3 — After eating the scroll and receiving his commission, Ezekiel describes his emotional state as he goes to the captives.

**Notes:** CRITICAL for transformation analysis: bitterness here is (1) EMOTIONAL (bitterness of spirit/ruach), (2) SEQUENTIAL — comes AFTER the eating, not during, (3) describes the PROPHET'S STATE not the scroll's property. Hebrew: mar/chemah (bitterness/heat). Mede (745) correctly identified: 'no reference, or but an obscure one, to bitterness in the belly' in Ezekiel. Rev 10:10 relocates bitterness from the prophet's emotional aftermath INTO the physical act of digestion — a transformation from external emotion to intrinsic scroll property.

### Mark on Foreheads Before Judgment

> Ezekiel 9:4 And the LORD said unto him, Go through the midst of the city, through the midst of Jerusalem, and set a mark upon the foreheads of the men that sigh and that cry for all the abominations that be done in the midst thereof.

**Context:** Ezekiel 9 — Vision of judgment on Jerusalem. Before the destroying angels act, a man in linen marks the faithful on their foreheads. Mark is 'tav' (last Hebrew letter, paleo-Hebrew = X/cross shape).

**Notes:** VP188 Strong (5 elements: forehead mark/seal, before judgment, protective function, destroying agents delayed, judgment follows) → Rev 7:3 parallel. Pattern: mark/seal → protective function → judgment follows. Tav in paleo-Hebrew = X or cross shape. Continuous Niphal participles for 'sighing and crying' (ha-ne'enakim ve-ha-ne'enaqim) indicate ongoing grief, not one-time act.

### Cherubim Revisited: Wheels, Eyes on Whole Body, Cherub Morphology Confirmed

> Ezekiel 10:9 And when I looked, behold the four wheels by the cherubims, one wheel by one cherub, and another wheel by another cherub: and the appearance of the wheels [was] as the colour of a beryl stone. 10:10 And [as for] their appearances, they four had one likeness, as if a wheel had been in the midst of a wheel. 10:11 When they went, they went upon their four sides; they turned not as they went, but to the place whither the head looked they followed it; they turned not as they went. 10:12 And their whole body, and their backs, and their hands, and their wings, and the wheels, [were] full of eyes round about, [even] the wheels that they four had. 10:13 As for the wheels, it was cried unto them in my hearing, O wheel. 10:14 And every one had four faces: the first face [was] the face of a cherub, and the second face [was] the face of a man, and the third the face of a lion, and the fourth the face of an eagle. 10:15 And the cherubims were lifted up. This [is] the living creature that I saw by the river of Chebar. 10:16 And when the cherubims went, the wheels went by them: and when the cherubims lifted up their wings to mount up from the earth, the same wheels also turned not from beside them. 10:17 When they stood, [these] stood; and when they were lifted up, [these] lifted up themselves [also]: for the spirit of the living creature [was] in them.

**Context:** Ezekiel 10 — Second merkavah vision. 10:12 eyes on the whole body and wheels — eyes are dispersed through the entire form. 10:14 face order differs from Ezek 1: cherub replaces ox.

**Notes:** 10:12 'whole body, backs, hands, wings, and wheels full of eyes' — eye coverage expanded to the entire body, not just the wheel rims of Ezek 1:18. 10:14 face order: cherub/man/lion/eagle — OX (Ezek 1:10) replaced by CHERUB. This face-order shift within Ezekiel itself shows the vision is not mechanically repeated. H3742 kerub named explicitly here. LXX: H3742 → cheroubim (G5502), NOT zōon (G2226) — Rev 4:6 uses zōon, departing from LXX's cherub-term.

### Explicit Identification: Ezek 10 Creatures Are the Same as Ezek 1 Cherubim

> Ezekiel 10:20 This [is] the living creature that I saw under the God of Israel by the river of Chebar; and I knew that they [were] the cherubims. 10:21 Every one had four faces apiece, and every one four wings; and the likeness of the hands of a man [was] under their wings. 10:22 And the likeness of their faces [was] the same faces which I saw by the river of Chebar, their appearances and themselves: they went every one straight forward.

**Context:** Ezekiel 10 — Ezekiel's own interpretive statement confirming the identity of the Ezek 1 creatures as kerubim. The two visions are explicitly linked.

**Notes:** 10:20 'I knew that they were the cherubims' — explicit retroactive identification. The chayyot (living creatures) of Ezek 1 = the kerubim of Ezek 10. H3742 kerub = the same beings. Revelation adopts the chayyot/zōon terminology rather than kerubim — a deliberate choice to avoid anchoring the heavenly beings to the earthly ark-cherub image. 10:21 four faces, four wings again — confirming the morphological data of Ezek 1.

### Fourfold Judgment Formula

> Ezekiel 14:21 For thus saith the Lord GOD; How much more when I send my four sore judgments upon Jerusalem, the sword, and the famine, and the noisome beast, and the pestilence, to cut off from it man and beast?

**Context:** Ezekiel 14 — God's fourfold judgment formula: sword, famine, beast, pestilence. Primary source for Rev 6:8 four horsemen death toll.

**Notes:** VP074 Strong — source for Rev 6:8 death toll (kill with sword, hunger, death, beasts of the earth). The four horsemen's destruction mechanism draws on Ezek 14:21 more directly than on Zechariah. Judgment content of Rev 6:8 = Ezekiel, not Zechariah.

### Guardian Cherub in Eden — Figurative Use of Cherub Identity

> Ezekiel 28:14 Thou [art] the anointed cherub that covereth; and I have set thee [so]: thou wast upon the holy mountain of God; thou hast walked up and down in the midst of the stones of fire. 28:15 Thou [wast] perfect in thy ways from the day that thou wast created, till iniquity was found in thee. 28:16 By the multitude of thy merchandise they have filled the midst of thee with violence, and thou hast sinned: therefore I will cast thee as profane out of the mountain of God: and I will destroy thee, O covering cherub, from the midst of the stones of fire.

**Context:** Ezekiel 28 — The lament over the king of Tyre, understood as figurative language for the covering cherub in Eden. The 'guardian/covering cherub' (kerub hammisach) in the garden of God, amid the stones of fire.

**Notes:** FIGURATIVE use of H3742 kerub — the Nave's Cherubim entry lists Ezek 28:14-16 under 'Figurative.' The covering/anointed cherub is cast from Eden for iniquity (v.15-16). This connects to the stones of fire imagery in Ezek 1 (fire/lightning among the creatures). The Cherubim Nave's topic confirms this figurative category alongside the symbolic use in Ezek 1 and 10. Background context: cherub identity as guardian/covering figure in sacred space, which explains why Revelation avoids the cherub term and uses zōon instead — the cherub term carries too much freight from Ezek 28.

### Gog of the Land of Magog — Named Northern Coalition

> Ezekiel 38:2 Son of man, set thy face against Gog, the land of Magog, the chief prince of Meshech and Tubal, and prophesy against him, 38:3 And say, Thus saith the Lord GOD; Behold, I [am] against thee, O Gog, the chief prince of Meshech and Tubal: 38:4 And I will turn thee back, and put hooks into thy jaws, and I will bring thee forth, and all thine army, horses and horsemen, all of them clothed with all sorts [of armour, even] a great company [with] bucklers and shields, all of them handling swords: 38:5 Persia, Ethiopia, and Libya with them; all of them with shield and helmet: 38:6 Gomer, and all his bands; the house of Togarmah of the north quarters, and all his bands: [and] many people with thee.

**Context:** Ezekiel 38 — The oracle against Gog. Named coalition of nations: Meshech, Tubal, Persia, Ethiopia, Libya, Gomer, Togarmah.

**Notes:** Highly specific named coalition vs. Rev 20:8 universalization. Gog = individual ruler; Magog = territory. Compare Rev 20:8: 'Gog and Magog' become a paired collective name for nations of the four corners of the earth. Geographic specificity (named nations, north) → cosmic universality (four quarters of the earth).

### Fire on Magog — Defeat and Death

> Ezekiel 39:1 Therefore, thou son of man, prophesy against Gog, and say, Thus saith the Lord GOD; Behold, I [am] against thee, O Gog, the chief prince of Meshech and Tubal: 39:2 And I will turn thee back, and leave but the sixth part of thee, and will cause thee to come up from the north parts, and will bring thee upon the mountains of Israel: 39:3 And I will smite thy bow out of thy left hand, and will cause thine arrows to fall out of thy right hand. 39:4 Thou shalt fall upon the mountains of Israel, thou, and all thy bands, and the people that [is] with thee: I will give thee unto the ravenous birds of every sort, and [to] the beasts of the field to be devoured. 39:5 Thou shalt fall upon the open field: for I have spoken [it], saith the Lord GOD. 39:6 And I will send a fire on Magog, and among them that dwell carelessly in the isles: and they shall know that I [am] the LORD.

**Context:** Ezekiel 39 — The defeat oracle. Fire on Magog (39:6) directly paralleled in Rev 20:9. Birds devour fallen (39:4) — this element appears in Rev 19:17-18 BEFORE the millennium.

**Notes:** 39:4 bird-feast element: 'I will give thee unto the ravenous birds' → this appears in Rev 19:17-18, not Rev 20. Revelation SPLITS the Gog-Magog material: bird feast (Ezek 39:17-20) → Rev 19:17-18 (pre-millennium); fire-from-God defeat + Gog/Magog name (Ezek 38-39) → Rev 20:7-10 (post-millennium). This deliberate split is the most structurally significant finding.

### Bird Feast on the Fallen — Sacrificial Supper

> Ezekiel 39:17 And, thou son of man, thus saith the Lord GOD; Speak unto every feathered fowl, and to every beast of the field, Assemble yourselves, and come; gather yourselves on every side to my sacrifice that I do sacrifice for you, [even] a great sacrifice upon the mountains of Israel, that ye may eat flesh, and drink blood. 39:18 Ye shall eat the flesh of the mighty, and drink the blood of the princes of the earth, of rams, of lambs, and of goats, of bullocks, all of them fatlings of Bashan. 39:19 And ye shall eat fat till ye be full, and drink blood till ye be drunken, of my sacrifice which I have sacrificed for you. 39:20 Thus ye shall be filled at my table with horses and chariots, with mighty men, and with all men of war, saith the Lord GOD.

**Context:** Ezekiel 39 — Bird-feast oracle. Birds summoned to eat flesh of the fallen. This element appears in Revelation BEFORE the Gog-Magog battle (Rev 19:17-18).

**Notes:** VP166 Strong — connects to Rev 19:17-18. The bird-feast vocabulary: 'feathered fowl,' 'eat flesh,' 'flesh of the mighty / princes' → Rev 19:17-18 'eat the flesh of kings, captains, mighty men.' Revelation presents this element PRE-millennium while Gog-Magog names appear POST-millennium. Riddlebarger's same-event reading is undermined by this split: if the bird-feast is Ezek 39:17-20 and it appears in Rev 19, then Ezek 38-39 material is distributed across TWO separate Revelation scenes.

### Man with Measuring Rod (Qaneh/Kalamos) — Primary OT Source for Rev 11:1

> Ezekiel 40:3 And he brought me thither, and, behold, [there was] a man, whose appearance [was] like the appearance of brass, with a line of flax in his hand, and a measuring reed; and he stood in the gate. 40:4 And the man said unto me, Son of man, behold with thine eyes, and hear with thine ears, and set thine heart upon all that I shall shew thee; for to the intent that I might shew [them] unto thee [art] thou brought hither: declare all that thou seest to the house of Israel. 40:5 And behold a wall on the outside of the house round about, and in the man's hand a measuring reed of six cubits [long] by the cubit and an hand breadth: so he measured the breadth of the building, one reed; and the height, one reed.

**Context:** Ezekiel 40 — Introduction of the angelic surveyor with a measuring reed (qaneh hammiddah) of six cubits. The primary OT source for Rev 11:1's 'reed like a rod' (kalamos homoios rabdo).

**Notes:** AN092 Strong — lexical chain: H7070 qaneh → LXX G2563 kalamos (PMI 9.09, 16 co-occurrences) → Rev 11:1 kalamos homoios rabdo. SAME Greek word confirmed. The measuring reed here is a functional instrument used by an angelic figure. Contrast: Zech 2:1 uses a measuring LINE (chevel/cord), not a rod — confirming Ezek 40 is the primary lexical source for Rev 11:1, not Zechariah.

### Detailed Measurement of East Gate and Outer Court — Everything Measured

> Ezekiel 40:6 Then came he unto the gate which looketh toward the east, and went up the stairs thereof, and measured the threshold of the gate, [which was] one reed broad; and the other threshold [of the gate, which was] one reed broad. 40:7 And [every] little chamber [was] one reed long, and one reed broad; and between the little chambers [were] five cubits; and the threshold of the gate by the porch of the gate within [was] one reed. 40:8 He measured also the porch of the gate within, one reed. 40:9 Then measured he the porch of the gate, eight cubits; and the posts thereof, two cubits; and the porch of the gate [was] inward. 40:10 And the little chambers of the gate eastward [were] three on this side, and three on that side; they three [were] of one measure: and the posts had one measure on this side and on that side. 40:11 And he measured the breadth of the entry of the gate, ten cubits; [and] the length of the gate, thirteen cubits. 40:12 The space also before the little chambers [was] one cubit [on this side], and the space [was] one cubit on that side: and the little chambers [were] six cubits on this side, and six cubits on that side. 40:13 He measured then the gate from the roof of [one] little chamber to the roof of another: the breadth [was] five and twenty cubits, door against door. 40:14 He made also posts of threescore cubits, even unto the post of the court round about the gate. 40:15 And from the face of the gate of the entrance unto the face of the porch of the inner gate [were] fifty cubits. 40:16 And [there were] narrow windows to the little chambers, and to their posts within the gate round about, and likewise to the arches: and windows [were] round about inward: and upon [each] post [were] palm trees.

**Context:** Ezekiel 40 — The east gate and outer court measured in exhaustive detail. The outer court (aule) is fully surveyed — it is NOT excluded.

**Notes:** KEY CONTRAST with Rev 11:2: Ezekiel measures the outer court extensively; Rev 11:2 explicitly EXCLUDES the outer court ('the court which is without the temple leave out, and measure it not; for it is given unto the Gentiles'). This outer-court exclusion is the critical transformation. Ezekiel's comprehensive measurement (inner + outer + gates) becomes theologically selective in Revelation: naos + altar + worshippers protected; aule given to Gentiles for 42-month trampling.
