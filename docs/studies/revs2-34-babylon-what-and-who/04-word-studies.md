### G4204 — porne

**Source:** `word-studies/G4204-porne.md`

**Occurrences:** 12

Harlot/prostitute; 12 NT uses, 4 in Revelation's Babylon section. Figurative use for idolatrous/apostate community dominates Revelation usage. LXX translates zanah (H2181) consistently as porneia-group terms.

**Semantic categories:** Covenant infidelity/spiritual harlotry, Literal prostitution, Apocalyptic anti-church figure

**Key verses:** Rev 17:1, Rev 17:5, Rev 19:2

**Relevance:** The harlot of Revelation 17 — figurative use for idolatrous/apostate community. The covenant-unfaithfulness dimension is foundational.

### G897 — Babylon

**Source:** `word-studies/H1101-balal.md`

To mix/confuse; 44 OT uses; 88% = mixing flour with oil in priestly offerings. Etymological root of Babel (Gen 11:9). Babylon's essential character is deliberate mixture.

**Semantic categories:** Priestly mixing/blending, Linguistic confusion, Deliberate mixture

**Key verses:** Gen 11:9, Lev 2:4, Lev 14:10

**Relevance:** Etymology of Babylon. The dominant usage (mixing flour with oil) suggests Babylon's character is deliberate MIXTURE of sacred and profane, truth and error.

### G3735 — oros

**Source:** `word-studies/G3735-óros.md`

**Occurrences:** 65

Mountain; 65 NT uses. Critical for seven mountains/hills debate at Rev 17:9. Never translated 'hill' in Revelation. OT: mountains = kingdoms in prophetic imagery.
