# 03 — Verse-by-Verse Analysis by Focus Area

## FA01: Babylon-Bride Mirror (IC182)

### Rev 17:1-3 — Angel Introduction and Spirit-Transport to Wilderness

The bowl-angel introduces the harlot vision with a formula repeated verbatim at 21:9-10 for the bride: kai elthen heis ek ton hepta angelon ton echonton tas hepta phialas / kai elalesen met emou legon / Deuro deixo soi. The verb apophero (G667, 7 NT uses total) for Spirit-transport occurs at only two passages in the entire NT: 17:3 and 21:10 (VP001). This exclusivity is not accidental. The author signals that these two visions are architecturally twinned — one shows what the covenant community becomes when it abandons God; the other shows what the covenant community becomes when God perfects it.

The destinations are antithetical: eis eremon (wilderness) for the harlot, epi oros mega kai hypselon (great high mountain) for the bride. Wilderness in OT theology is the place of testing and judgment; the high mountain is the place of divine revelation and kingdom establishment (Ezek 40:2; Dan 2:35).

**Interpretive force:** Whatever category of entity the bride represents, the harlot must represent the same category in its corrupted form. The bride is not a literal city but a symbolic community — the redeemed church (19:7-8; 21:2). The harlot is therefore a symbolic community — an apostate religious system. This structural constraint eliminates the literal-city reading and the purely political reading.

### Rev 21:9-10 — Mirror Angel Introduction for Bride

Verbatim parallels: (1) one of the seven angels having the seven bowls, (2) spoke with me saying, (3) Come I will show you, (4) he carried me away in the Spirit. The destination is a great high mountain; the object is the bride, the Lamb's wife. The structural bracket closes with matching angel-worship refusals at 19:10 and 22:8-9 (VP022).

### Rev 17:4 / Rev 21:18 — Gilded vs. Intrinsic Gold

The harlot is kechrusomene chrysio (perfect passive participle of chrysoo G5558, plated/gilded with gold). chrysoo occurs only twice in the NT — both for Babylon (17:4; 18:16). New Jerusalem IS chrysion katharon (pure gold, 21:18). Same material, opposite relationship (VP023). Babylon wears gold on the surface; New Jerusalem is gold through and through. This is the difference between appearance and reality, between a system that imitates true worship and one that embodies it.

### Rev 18:2 / Rev 21:3 — Demon Habitation vs. Divine Tabernacle

Babylon becomes katoiketerion daimonion (habitation of demons, 18:2). New Jerusalem is he skene tou theou meta ton anthropon (the tabernacle of God with men, 21:3). Same dwelling vocabulary, opposite inhabitants (VP030). One house is given over to demons; the other to God himself.

### Rev 18:23 / Rev 21:23 — Extinguished Lamp vs. Eternal Light

In Babylon, phos lychnou ou me phane en soi eti (the light of a lamp shall shine no more at all in you). In New Jerusalem, ho lychnos autes to arnion (its lamp is the Lamb). Same word lychnos (G3088, 14 NT uses), antithetical fates (VP026). The lamp — representing witness, truth, spiritual illumination — is permanently extinguished in Babylon but eternally sustained in the bride.

### Rev 17:6 / Rev 22:1 — Blood vs. Water of Life

The harlot is drunk with haima (blood, G129) of the saints. New Jerusalem has potamon hydatos zoes (river of water of life, 22:1). VP025. Death-liquid versus life-liquid.

### Rev 19:10 / Rev 22:8-9 — Angel-Worship Refusal Bracket

Near-identical scenes close Visions 3 and 4 (VP022). This structural bracket confirms the architectural twinning is deliberate, not coincidental. The twelvefold mirror documented in SP013 leaves no reasonable doubt: the harlot and bride are the same type of entity — a community of worshipers — in antithetical spiritual conditions.


## FA02: OT Babylon Allusions — Isa 21:9, Jer 50-51, Isa 47

### Isa 21:9 — Fall Formula (AN085)

naphlah naphlah bavel — fallen, fallen is Babylon. naphal (H5307, Qal perfect 3fs) doubled for emphasis. The LXX renders this as epesen epesen Babyloon, which Rev 14:8 and 18:2 quote verbatim. This is an explicit allusion (AN085). The doubled verb emphasizes certainty and completeness. The OT context includes the breaking of Babylon's idol-gods — the fall is religious, not merely political.

### Jer 51:6-7 — Come-Out Call and Golden Cup

Two elements from this single passage generate major Revelation themes. First, the come-out call: nusuw mittowk bavel umalletuw ish napshow (flee out of the midst of Babylon and deliver every man his soul). This becomes Rev 18:4. Second, the golden cup: kos-zahav bavel beyad-YHWH — Babylon is a golden cup in the LORD's hand. In Jeremiah, the cup is in God's hand — God uses Babylon as an instrument of judgment on the nations. In Rev 17:4, the golden cup is in the harlot's hand. The agency has shifted. This transformation shows John reading the OT typologically: the original Babylon was God's instrument; Revelation's Babylon is a self-directing system of corruption that has taken the cup of influence into its own hands.

### Jer 51:13 — Many Waters

shokhaneti al-mayim rabbim — O you who dwell upon many waters. This refers literally to Babylon's location on the Euphrates canal system. Rev 17:15 universalizes it: the waters are peoples, multitudes, nations, and tongues. The geographic becomes global. The transformation from literal to symbolic is the angel's own interpretation, not the reader's inference.

### Jer 51:45 — My People, Go Out

tseu mittokah ammi — go out from her midst, my people. Direct source for Rev 18:4. God calls people ammi (my people, H5971) while they are still inside the system.

### Jer 51:48 / Rev 18:20 — Heaven Rejoices

werinnu al-bavel shamayim wa'arets — heaven and earth shall sing for Babylon. Rev 18:20: euphrainou ep auten, ourane. Same theme, same participants.

### Jer 51:63-64 / Rev 18:21 — Stone into Water

Jeremiah ties a stone to a scroll and casts it into the Euphrates as a symbolic action: thus shall Babylon sink. Rev 18:21 transforms this into a mighty angel casting a great millstone into the sea. Same symbol, intensified in scale.

### Isa 47:7-9 / Rev 18:7 — Queen Boast

Three parallels with near-verbatim force: (1) "I shall be a lady forever" → "I sit a queen"; (2) "I shall not sit as a widow" → "am no widow"; (3) "in a moment in one day" → "in one day." Also: "multitude of thy sorceries" (keshaphayik) → "by thy sorceries (pharmakeia) were all nations deceived" (18:23).

**Summary of OT transformation:** John does not reproduce his OT sources mechanically. He transforms them: geographic references become universal symbols, God's instrument becomes a self-directing system, Jeremiah's Babylon-specific language is applied to a composite entity that draws from Babylon, Tyre, Sodom, and Egypt simultaneously. This composite method demonstrates that Revelation's Babylon is not a single historical city but a type — a category of anti-God system that manifests across history.


## FA03: Ezek 27 Tyre Oracle and Rev 18 Cargo List

### Ezek 27:12-25 — Tyre Cargo List as Source Quarry

The Tyre oracle is a qinah lamentation (sa qinah al-Tsor, Ezek 27:2). Rev 18 borrows both the cargo-list structure and the threefold lament pattern (kings 18:9-10, merchants 18:11-17, shipmasters 18:17-19) from this oracle.

### Rev 18:12-13 — 28-Item Cargo List

The list moves from luxury goods (gold, silver, precious stones) through textiles and aromatics to food and livestock, climaxing in somaton kai psychas anthropon (bodies and souls of men). Ezek 27:13 LXX has only psychas anthropon (souls of men). The addition of somata (G4983, bodies) intensifies the exploitation — total human commodification, physical and spiritual.

### Interpretive Significance

The composite nature of the cargo list — drawn from Tyre, not from any Babylon-specific oracle — demonstrates that John is building a symbolic type. By grafting Tyre's commercial profile onto Babylon's religious-political profile, Revelation creates a figure encompassing both economic exploitation and spiritual corruption. No single historical entity perfectly matches both profiles simultaneously, which is precisely the point: Babylon is a composite symbol, not a zip code.


## FA04: Ezek 16 / Hosea Harlotry = Covenant Unfaithfulness

### Ezek 16:15-16 — Jerusalem as Foundling Bride Turned Harlot

wattizbni (zanah H2181, Qal wayyiqtol 2fs) — and you played the harlot. The metaphor in Ezekiel 16 requires prior covenant relationship: YHWH found Jerusalem as an abandoned infant (16:4-6), washed her, clothed her in royal garments, and married her (16:8-14). She then took those very garments and used them in harlotry. This is the OT template for Rev 17's harlot imagery.

### Ezek 16:37-41 / Rev 17:16 — Punishment of the Harlot

Four precise parallels: (1) lovers/nations gathered → horns and beast; (2) nakedness exposed → gymnen; (3) stripping of garments → eremomenen; (4) burning with fire → katakausosin en pyri. John applies the OT harlot-judgment template precisely. The punishment pattern belongs to covenant unfaithfulness, not to the destruction of a pagan city.

### Hos 1:2 — Wife of Whoredoms

eshet zenunim — wife of whoredoms. The marital covenant is foundational: YHWH as husband, Israel as unfaithful wife. zanah is applied to the covenant people for departing from the LORD.

### Isa 1:21 — Faithful City Become a Harlot

eikah hayethah lezonah qiryah ne'emanah — How the faithful city has become a harlot. Key verb: hayethah (became). The city was once faithful and then became a harlot. This egeneto pattern matches Rev 18:2's egeneto exactly — progressive moral deterioration of a once-faithful entity.

### Isa 23:15-17 / Nah 3:4 — Exceptions to Covenant-Only Usage

Tyre and Nineveh receive harlot language despite being pagan cities. But these are illuminating exceptions: Tyre's harlotry is commercial seduction (luring nations into trade dependency); Nineveh's is political domination through sorcery. These secondary uses confirm the primary domain: covenant infidelity. When no covenant exists, zanah/porneia vocabulary describes a different mechanism — seduction, manipulation — not marital betrayal.

### The Zanah Constraint

zanah (H2181) appears 107 times in the OT. The overwhelming majority of uses (Ezek 16, Hos 1-4, Jer 2-3, Isa 1:21, Judg 2:17, etc.) apply to entities that were in covenant relationship with YHWH and became unfaithful. The LXX consistently maps zanah to the porneia word family (TR-zanah-porne), and Revelation inherits this semantic domain. When Revelation calls Babylon "the great harlot" (he porne he megale), it invokes the entire covenant-unfaithfulness tradition. This vocabulary is the decisive structural constraint: it eliminates any identification that lacks a prior covenant relationship with God. Pagan Rome never married YHWH. Neither did literal Babylon in Mesopotamia. Only a religious system that once stood in covenantal relationship with God can be called a harlot in this full OT sense.


## FA05: Rev 17:5 MYSTERY Title and Mysterion Vocabulary

### Rev 17:5 — Forehead Inscription

mysterion (G3466, N-NSN nominative) appears on the harlot's forehead as part of the written name. At Rev 1:20 — the only other Revelation occurrence — mysterion explicitly introduces symbolic interpretation: "the mystery of the seven stars... the seven stars ARE the angels of the seven churches." This sets the precedent: when Revelation labels something a mysterion, what follows is symbolic, not literal. MYSTERY BABYLON THE GREAT is a symbolic designation.

### Priestly-Garment Parody (Rev 17:4 / Exod 28:5-6)

The harlot's attire: gold (chrysion), purple (porphyroun), scarlet (kokkinon), precious stones (litho timio), pearls (margaritais). The high priest's attire: gold (zahav), blue (tekeleth), purple (argaman), scarlet (tola'at shani), fine linen (shesh). Every element matches except blue. Blue (tekeleth) is the law-faithfulness color: Num 15:38-39 commands a blue ribbon on garment borders "that ye may remember all the commandments of the LORD." The concluding phrase uses zanah: "after which ye use to go a whoring." The harlot wears priestly garments but lacks the element representing God's commandments.

### Forehead Inscription Inversion (Rev 17:5 / Exod 28:36)

The high priest bore a gold plate on his forehead inscribed qodesh la-YHWH (HOLINESS TO THE LORD). The harlot bears MYSTERY BABYLON THE GREAT MOTHER OF HARLOTS on hers. Same location, inverted content. If the harlot parodies the high priest, she represents a corrupted religious system wearing the vestments of true worship without the law-faithfulness that gives them meaning.

### METER TON PORNON — Productive Genitive

meter (G3384, N-NSF nominative) + ton pornon (G4204, N-GPF genitive plural) = mother OF the harlots. This is a productive genitive: not "the greatest harlot" (superlative) but "the source/generator of other harlots." A mother produces offspring carrying her character. This means no single historical institution IS Babylon — they can be daughters of the mother system, but the mother is the generative principle itself. Ezek 23:2-4 (Oholah and Oholibah) anticipates this: two sisters, daughters of one mother, both engaging in harlotry.


## FA06: Rev 17:9-11 Seven Mountains / Kings

### Rev 17:9 — hepta ore

oros (G3735, N-NPN nominative plural neuter) = seven mountains. oros means "mountain" in every other Revelation occurrence: 6:14,15,16 (mountains moved); 8:8 (burning mountain); 14:1 (Mount Zion); 16:20 (mountains not found); 21:10 (great high mountain). It never means "hill" in Revelation. The Greek word for hill is bounos (G1015), used at Luke 3:5 and 23:30. John knows the difference and consistently uses oros for mountain.

### kai basileis hepta eisin — Epexegetical or Additive?

The text transitions immediately: "they are also seven kings" (kai basileis hepta eisin). The kai eisin could be epexegetical (explaining the mountains as kings) or additive (introducing a second feature). The flow of the text favors the epexegetical reading: the mountains ARE the kings. Mountains = kingdoms in OT prophetic vocabulary: Jer 51:25 calls Babylon a "destroying mountain" (har hammashchith); Dan 2:35 — the stone becomes a great mountain filling the earth, interpreted as God's kingdom (2:44); Zech 4:7 addresses a "great mountain" as an obstacle/kingdom.

### The Seven-Hills Problem

The identification of Rev 17:9 with Rome's seven hills faces multiple difficulties. (1) Lexical: oros means mountain, not hill. (2) Circularity: if "seven hills" is an obvious code for Rome, why encode it when Rome's identity as the city of seven hills was universally known? A code only works if it is not immediately transparent. (3) Rome-rides-Rome: if both beast and harlot are Rome, then Rome rides Rome (17:3), Rome hates Rome (17:16), and Rome burns Rome (17:16). The text presents two distinct interacting entities — the woman and the beast — not a single entity in self-contradiction.

### Rev 17:10-11 — Five Fallen, One Is, One to Come

The five-one-one sequence with the eighth who is "of the seven" has generated numerous identification schemes. The text itself does not name the kings. What the text does establish is that the beast is a composite entity spanning multiple historical manifestations — consistent with Daniel's four-kingdom sequence (Dan 2; Dan 7) being compressed into a single composite beast.


## FA07: Rev 17:18 — "That Great City" — Every Great City in Revelation

### Rev 17:18 — Explicit Identification

he gyne hen eides estin he polis he megale he basileuousa epi ton basileon tes ges — the woman whom you saw is that great city which reigns over the kings of the earth. The angel explicitly identifies the woman as a city.

### Rev 11:8 — Great City = Jerusalem

he polis he megale hetis kaleitai pneumatikos Sodoma kai Aigyptos hopou kai ho kyrios auton estaurothoe — the great city which is spiritually called Sodom and Egypt, where also their Lord was crucified. Same phrase he polis he megale. Unambiguously Jerusalem. But the adverb pneumatikos (G4153) — spiritually — signals that city names in Revelation are symbolic designations. If Jerusalem can be "spiritually" called Sodom and Egypt (cities with which it shares no geography), then the name "Babylon" could function the same way: a spiritual designation applied to a system sharing Babylon's character.

### Rev 16:19 — Great City Divided

he polis he megale egeneto eis tria mere — the great city became three parts. Distinguished from "the cities of the nations." The tripartite division may correspond to the dragon/beast/false prophet triad of 16:13.

### Mapping Summary

The phrase he polis he megale at 11:8 = Jerusalem. At 17:18 = the woman/Babylon. At 16:19 = possibly both or a composite. At 18:10,16,18,19,21 = Babylon in judgment. The phrase does not consistently identify a single geographic location. It identifies a type — the great anti-God city that manifests differently at different points in Revelation's narrative.


## FA08: Rev 18:4 — "Come Out of Her My People" — OT Come-Out Tradition

### Rev 18:4 — The Call

Exelthate ho laos mou ex autes (aorist active imperative of exerchomai G1831). Decisive, urgent command. ho laos mou (G2992, N-NSM) = my people — the covenant-people term, not ochlos (crowd) or ethnos (nation). God claims these people as covenant members while they are still inside Babylon.

### Dual Hina-Me Clause

(1) hina me synkoinonesete tais hamartiais autes (aorist active subjunctive of sygkoinoneo G4790) — that you not co-participate in her sins. (2) hina me labete ek ton plegon autes (aorist active subjunctive of lambano G2983) — that you not receive of her plagues. Both verbs are aorist subjunctive — unrealized purpose. The people addressed have NOT YET fully participated in her sins. There is still time to leave. This grammar presupposes sincere believers inside the system who have not yet crossed the line of full complicity.

### OT Come-Out Tradition

The pattern runs through Scripture: Abraham from Ur (Gen 12:1), Lot from Sodom (Gen 19:17), Israel from Egypt (Exod 8:23), the faithful from Korah (Num 16:26), Isaiah's six imperatives (52:11), Jeremiah's Babylon calls (51:6,45). The Korah precedent is the closest parallel to Rev 18:4 — separation from rebellion within the covenant community itself.

### John 10:16 — Sheep in the Wrong Fold

aule (G833, fold/pen) vs poimne (G4167, flock). God's sheep can be in the wrong fold (Babylon's organizational structure) while belonging to the right flock (Christ's people). The come-out call is the shepherd's voice reaching his own sheep inside someone else's pen. This presupposes genuine believers inside Babylon who need to hear and respond — which requires Babylon to be a religious entity where sincere worship can be offered, not a pagan empire where no one sincerely serves God.


## FA09: Mother/Daughters Relationship (Rev 17:5)

### Productive Genitive

METER TON PORNON (G3384 + G4204, genitive plural feminine) = mother of the harlots. This is not a superlative but a productive designation: a mother who generates daughters carrying her character. Ezek 23's Oholah and Oholibah illustrate the principle: two sisters (Samaria and Jerusalem), daughters of one mother, both engaging in harlotry. Unfaithfulness is reproductive — a system generates offspring who inherit its characteristics.

### BDELYGMATON TES GES — Abominations of the Earth

bdelygma (G946, N-GPN genitive plural neuter) = abominations. OT use of shiqquts/bdelygma is consistently for idolatrous worship objects (1 Kings 11:5,7; Dan 9:27; 12:11). The cup is full of religious abominations, not political corruption.

### Interpretive Implication

The mother-daughter language means no single historical institution can BE Babylon exhaustively. Individual institutions can be daughters — manifestations of the mother system — but the mother is the generative principle of apostate worship itself. The mother is separate from her daughters (EGW: "the mother was not the daughters, but separate and distinct from them").


## FA10: Evidence For/Against Each Identification Model

### Model 1: Rome/Roman Empire
**For:** City of seven hills (if oros = hills); ruled over kings of the earth (17:18); persecuted the early church; 1 Pet 5:13 already uses Babylon as cipher for Rome; purple and scarlet worn by Roman elite.
**Against:** oros means mountain, not hill; Rome-rides-Rome problem (if beast = Rome AND woman = Rome); no covenant relationship with YHWH (zanah constraint fails); Rome was pagan, never a bride who became a harlot; the come-out call presupposes God's people inside the system.

### Model 2: Apostate Religious System
**For:** Harlot-bride mirror requires same category (symbolic community); zanah/porneia covenant presupposition met (system was once in covenant relationship); mother-daughter productive genitive fits (generates offspring); MYSTERY signals symbolic interpretation; priestly-garment parody points to corrupted religious system; come-out call to God's people fits (genuine believers inside the system); egeneto (became) indicates progressive deterioration of a once-pure entity; missing blue = abandoned commandments.
**Against:** Requires reading later church history into first-century text (but so does any historicist reading); does not fully account for commercial details of Rev 18 (but Rev 18 borrows from Tyre, showing composite construction).

### Model 3: World System
**For:** Universal scope of "all nations drunk" and "all slain upon the earth"; composite construction from multiple OT city-oracles; MOTHER OF HARLOTS as trans-historical principle.
**Against:** Too vague to explain specific textual details; does not account for the covenant-unfaithfulness vocabulary; does not explain the come-out call (how do you "come out" of civilization itself?); does not explain why the beast destroys the harlot (political power can turn against religion, but the "world system" destroying itself is incoherent).

### Model 4: Literal Rebuilt Babylon
**For:** Geographic consistency argument; "many waters" fits the Euphrates canal system; unfulfilled OT prophecies about Babylon's destruction.
**Against:** Babylon was not a functioning city when John wrote; ignores apocalyptic genre; Rev 17:15 explicitly interprets "many waters" as peoples/nations (angel-interpreted, not reader-inferred); MYSTERY signals symbolic interpretation; the composite cargo list draws from Tyre, not Babylon; the harlot-bride mirror requires a symbolic community, not a literal city.

### Model 5: Jerusalem
**For:** Rev 11:8 identifies "the great city" as Jerusalem; harlot language is primarily applied to Israel/Judah in OT; blood of prophets echoes Matt 23:34-37; beast destroying the woman could describe Rome's AD 70 destruction of Jerusalem; Staples' Rev 12 → Rev 17 continuity argument.
**Against:** Jerusalem never reigned over the kings of the earth (17:18); Jerusalem's commercial profile does not match Rev 18; the mother-daughter language does not fit first-century Jerusalem (she did not generate daughter institutions); the come-out tradition in Rev 18:4 draws from Jer 51 (come out of Babylon), not from any Jerusalem-specific oracle; the egeneto (became) progressive deterioration language at 18:2 better fits a long historical arc than a sudden first-century fall.


## FA11: Blood of Prophets and Saints (Rev 17:6, 18:24)

### Rev 17:6 — Drunk with Blood

methyousan (present active participle of methyo G3184) ek tou haimatos ton hagion kai ek tou haimatos ton martyron Iesou — drunk from the blood of the saints and from the blood of the witnesses of Jesus. Present participle: the intoxication is ongoing, not a single past event. Dual source: saints (hagion) AND witnesses/martyrs of Jesus (martyron Iesou). This persecuting agency is systematic.

### Rev 18:24 — Blood Found in Her

haima propheton kai hagion kai panton ton esphagmenon epi tes ges — blood of prophets and of saints and of all who were slain upon the earth. Three categories expanding in scope. The universal clause "all who were slain upon the earth" transcends any single city. Matt 23:34-37 assigns prophet-killing to Jerusalem specifically. Rev 18:24 extends the scope universally, suggesting systemic rather than geographic bloodguilt — every system that kills truth-tellers participates in the same spirit, and Babylon as the "mother" bears responsibility for all her daughters' violence.

### SP037 — Altar Vindication Arc

The souls under the altar at 6:9-10 cry: "How long?" The answer comes at 19:2: ekrinen ten pornen ten megalen, exedikesen to haima ton doulon autou ek cheiros autes — he judged the great harlot and avenged the blood of his servants at her hand. The persecutor is identified. The arc spans the entire book.


## FA12: Wine/Fornication Vocabulary Network

### Rev 14:8 — Genitive Chain

tou oinou tou thumou tes porneias autes — the wine of the wrath/passion of her fornication. Three stacked genitives. oinos (G3631, wine) characterized by thumos (G2372, passion/fury) characterized by porneia (G4202, fornication). Babylon's intoxicating influence is her corrupting false teaching that drives the nations into spiritual unfaithfulness.

### Rev 14:10 — Akratos

tou oinou tou thumou tou theou tou kekerasmenou akratou — the wine of the wrath of God mixed unmixed. akratos (G194) is a NT hapax legomenon (1 occurrence) — used only here. It means undiluted, without any water or mixture. God's judgment wine has no dilution. The paradox of "mixed unmixed" intensifies: the cup that was supposed to be mixed (diluted, moderated) is poured out akratos — full strength.

### Rev 19:15 — Maximum Genitive Stacking

ten lenon tou oinou tou thumou tes orges tou theou — the winepress of the wine of the fury of the wrath of God. Four genitive nouns. thumos (passion/fury) + orge (settled wrath) together = complete divine judgment. This is the climax of the wine/wrath vocabulary network.

### Dual Intoxication

The harlot intoxicates the nations with her wine (false teaching, 14:8; 17:2; 18:3). But the harlot herself is drunk — not on wine, but on the blood of the saints (17:6). She intoxicates others with doctrine; she intoxicates herself with violence. The two intoxications mirror each other: false teaching produces persecution, and persecution becomes its own addiction.
