### Beast existence parody: was, is not, yet is

> And the angel said unto me, Wherefore didst thou marvel? I will tell thee the mystery of the woman, and of the beast that carrieth her, which hath the seven heads and ten horns. The beast that thou sawest was, and is not; and shall ascend out of the bottomless pit, and go into perdition: and they that dwell on the earth shall wonder, whose names were not written in the book of life from the foundation of the world, when they behold the beast that was, and is not, and yet is.

**Context:** FA06 parody structure from revs2-19. Beast existence formula inverts divine formula (Rev 1:4,8; 4:8). Beast goes to apoleia (G684), same term for Judas (John 17:12) and man of sin (2 Thess 2:3).

**Notes:** en (V-IAI-3S imperfect) kai ouk estin (V-PAI-3S present) kai parestai (V-FDI-3S future of pareimi). The tense sequence: imperfect (was) + present (is not) + future (shall be present). Divine formula: ho on (present IS) kai ho en (imperfect WAS) kai ho erchomenos (coming). Parody: God starts with present IS; beast starts with past WAS. apoleian (N-ASF accusative of apoleia G684) = perdition/destruction.
