4. **The jewel contrast: adorned with vs. composed of precious stones:**
The harlot is described as kechryomene chrysio kai litho timio kai margaritais (17:4) — 'having been gilded with gold and precious stone and pearls.' The perfect passive participle kechryomene (from chrysoo, G5558) means she has been plated/overlaid — external decoration. By contrast, NJ's wall is jasper (21:18), the city itself is pure gold like clear glass (21:18), the 12 foundations are 12 different precious stones (21:19-20), and the 12 gates are 12 pearls (21:21). The city IS these materials; they are not applied to its surface. Run search_strongs.py --lookup on G2393 (iaspis/jasper) — it appears at Rev 4:3, 21:11, 21:18, 21:19 and connects the NJ description to the throne-room vision. Compare the 12 NJ foundation stones (Rev 21:19-20: jasper, sapphire, chalcedony, emerald, sardonyx, sardius, chrysolite, beryl, topaz, chrysoprasus, jacinth, amethyst) with the 12 breastplate stones (Exod 28:17-20) and the 9 stones of Ezek 28:13. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-ot on Rev 21:19.

7. **The kings-of-the-earth contrast: fornication vs. bringing glory:**

8. **The angel-worship bracket (VP022): 19:10 and 22:8-9:**
The angel-worship refusal occurs at the structural conclusion of each city-woman section: 19:10 closes the Babylon section, 22:8-9 closes the NJ section. Both share near-identical vocabulary: John falls to worship (epesa proskunesai), the angel says 'see thou do it not' (hora me), identifies himself as 'thy fellowservant' (sundoulos sou eimi), and commands 'worship God' (to Theo proskuneson). Run greek_parser.py --verse on Rev 19:10 and Rev 22:8-9 to identify exact differences. The revs-06 study documented this as VP022 (Strong, 6 links).
