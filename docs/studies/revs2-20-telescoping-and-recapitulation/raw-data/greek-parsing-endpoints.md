# Greek Parsing Endpoints

======================================================================
  GREEK PARSING — SEQUENCE ENDPOINTS AND KEY STRUCTURAL VERBS
======================================================================

=== Rev 6:17 — ēlthen (erchomai aorist) ===
Greek: ὅτι ἦλθεν ἡ ἡμέρα ἡ μεγάλη τῆς ὀργῆς αὐτῶν, καὶ τίς δύναται σταθῆναι;
ēlthen: G2064, V-2AAI-3S, Aor Act Ind Sg 3rd — "the great day of his wrath IS COME" (aorist arrival)
orgē: G3709, N-GSF — wrath (genitive of specification)
stathēnai: G2476, V-APN, Aor Pass Inf — "to stand" (ability question: who is able to stand?)

=== Rev 11:15 — egenonto (ginomai aorist plural) ===
Greek: Ἐγένετο ἡ βασιλεία τοῦ κόσμου τοῦ Κυρίου ἡμῶν καὶ τοῦ Χριστοῦ αὐτοῦ
egeneto: G1096, V-2ADI-3S, Aor Mid Ind Sg 3rd — "has become" (proleptic aorist: kingdom declared accomplished)
basileusei: G936, V-FAI-3S, Fut Act Ind Sg 3rd — "he shall reign" (future tense — the reigning is still future!)
Note: The declaration is aorist (accomplished), but the reigning is future — internal tension

=== Rev 11:17 — divine title truncation ===
Greek: Κύριε ὁ Θεός ὁ Παντοκράτωρ, ὁ ὢν καὶ ὁ ἦν
Title: ho ōn (present ptcp) + ho ēn (imperfect) = "who IS and who WAS"
ABSENT: "kai ho erchomenos" (and who is to come) — compare Rev 1:4, 1:8, 4:8 which have all three
ebas​ileus​as: G936, V-AAI-2S, Aor Act Ind 2S — "you HAVE REIGNED/BEGUN TO REIGN" (ingressive aorist)

=== Rev 11:18 — ēlthen orgē (same as 6:17) ===
Greek: καὶ ἦλθεν ἡ ὀργή σου
ēlthen: G2064, V-2AAI-3S, Aor Act Ind Sg 3rd — IDENTICAL FORM as Rev 6:17
orgē: G3709, N-NSF — "your wrath" (nominative subject)
VP174 CONFIRMED: Same verb form ēlthen + orgē at both Rev 6:17 and Rev 11:18

5-element programmatic statement at 11:18:
1. ōrgisthēsan (G3710) — nations were angry — Aor Pass Ind 3P
2. ēlthen hē orgē sou — your wrath came — Aor Act Ind 3S
3. kairos tōn nekrōn kri​thē​nai — time of dead to be judged — Aor Pass Inf
4. dounai ton misthon — to give reward — Aor Act Inf
5. diaphtheirai tous diaphtheirontas — to destroy destroyers — Aor Act Inf

=== Rev 16:17 — gegonen (ginomai PERFECT) ===
Greek: Γέγονεν.
gegonen: G1096, V-2RAI-3S, Perf Act Ind Sg 3rd — "It IS DONE / It HAS HAPPENED"
PERFECT TENSE: stative resultant state — the action has completed and the completion is present
CONTRAST with ēlthen (aorist, arrival of a period) vs. gegonen (perfect, completed state of affairs)

=== Rev 10:7 — etelesthe (teleō aorist passive) ===
Greek: καὶ ἐτελέσθη τὸ μυστήριον τοῦ Θεοῦ
etelesthe: G5055, V-API-3S, Aor Pass Ind Sg 3rd — "the mystery of God SHOULD BE finished"
Context: en tais hēmerais (in the DAYS of) — plural hēmerais implies temporal duration, not instant

=== Rev 11:7 — telesōsin (teleō aorist subjunctive) ===
Greek: καὶ ὅταν τελέσωσιν τὴν μαρτυρίαν αὐτῶν
telesōsin: G5055, V-AAS-3P, Aor Act Subj Pl 3rd — "when they SHALL HAVE FINISHED" (aorist subj of completion)

=== Rev 15:1 — etelesthe thumos (teleō aorist passive) ===
Greek: ὅτι ἐν αὐταῖς ἐτελέσθη ὁ θυμὸς τοῦ Θεοῦ
etelesthe: G5055, V-API-3S, Aor Pass Ind Sg 3rd — "is FILLED UP / COMPLETED" (same form as 10:7)
thumos: G2372, N-NSM — FIERCE wrath (not orgē — note: 15:1 uses thumos, not orgē)

=== Rev 9:12 — woe temporal markers ===
Greek: Ἠ Οὐαὶ ἡ μία ἀπῆλθεν· ἰδοὺ ἔρχεται ἔτι δύο Οὐαὶ μετὰ ταῦτα.
apēlthen: G565, V-2AAI-3S, Aor Act Ind — "the first woe HAS PASSED" (completed past)
erchetai: G2064, V-PNI-3S, Pres M/P Ind — "there COME two more woes" (present tense, ongoing approach)
meta tauta: "after these things" — explicit temporal sequence marker

=== Rev 11:14 — second woe past ===
Greek: Ἠ Οὐαὶ ἡ δευτέρα ἀπῆλθεν· ἰδοὺ ἡ Οὐαὶ ἡ τρίτη ἔρχεται ταχύ.
apēlthen: same Aor Act Ind — "the second woe HAS PASSED"
erchetai: same Pres M/P Ind — "the third woe IS COMING quickly" (tachu — quickly)
Note: Third woe (7th trumpet) never declared "past" — asymmetry (TM071 confirmed)

=== Rev 8:1 — silence ===
Greek: ἐγένετο σιγὴ ἐν τῷ οὐρανῷ ὡς ἡμιώριον.
ēnoixen: G455, V-AAI-3S, Aor Act Ind — "when he HAD OPENED" (temporal clause)
egeneto: G1096, V-2ADI-3S, Aor Mid Ind — "there WAS silence"
sige: G4602, N-NSF — silence (hapax relative to theophany contexts; only 2 NT uses)
hēmiōrion: G2256, N-ASN, Acc Sg N — "half an hour" (hapax legomenon; only occurrence in NT)

=== Rev 8:2 — kai eidon ===
Greek: καὶ εἶδον τοὺς ἑπτὰ ἀγγέλους
eidon: G3708, V-2AAI-1S, Aor Act Ind — "and I SAW the seven angels"
eidothēsan: G1325, V-API-3P, Aor Pass Ind — "and were GIVEN to them seven trumpets"
STRUCTURAL NOTE: kai eidon at 8:2 is the same vision-introduction formula as elsewhere
Whether this continues the 7th seal scene OR begins a new unit determines the telescoping vs. sequential argument

=== Rev 7:3 — adikeō prohibition ===
Greek: Μὴ ἀδικήσητε τὴν γῆν μήτε τὴν θάλασσαν μήτε τὰ δένδρα
adikēsēte: G91, V-AAS-2P, Aor Act Subj — "DO NOT HURT earth / sea / trees" (prohibitive aorist subjunctive)
sphragisamem: G4972, V-AAS-1P, Aor Act Subj — "UNTIL we have sealed" (temporal boundary)

=== Rev 9:4 — seal protection ===
Greek: ἵνα μὴ ἀδικήσουσιν... εἰ μὴ τοὺς ἀνθρώπους οἵτινες οὐκ ἔχουσιν τὴν σφραγῖδα τοῦ Θεοῦ
adikēsousin: G91, V-FAI-3P, Fut Act Ind — "they should NOT HURT" (command via hina + future indicative)
echousin: G2192, V-PAI-3P, Pres Act Ind — "those who DO NOT HAVE the seal" (present continuous)
sphragida: G4973, N-ASF — seal (same vocabulary as Rev 7:3 sealing command)
CONFIRMATION: Rev 7:3 adikeō prohibition + Rev 9:4 same vocabulary = cross-reference confirmed

=== Rev 8:7 — to triton (1/3 fraction) ===
Greek: καὶ τὸ τρίτον τῆς γῆς κατεκάη, καὶ τὸ τρίτον τῶν δένδρων κατεκάη, καὶ πᾶς χόρτος χλωρὸς κατεκάη.
tritos: G5154, A-NSN — "the THIRD PART" appears twice in one verse
Note: "all green grass burnt up" — trumpet 1 destroys 1/3 of trees but ALL green grass (mixed fractions)
Rev 6:8 (seals): tetarton (1/4) — "power over the FOURTH PART of the earth"
Rev 16: zero fraction language — totality (confirmed)
