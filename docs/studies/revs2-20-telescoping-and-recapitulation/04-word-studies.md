# Word Studies

## Question
Does the text of Revelation support telescoping (7th seal contains trumpets; 7th trumpet contains bowls), recapitulation (all three sequences cover overlapping time), or linear progression? Present the strongest textual evidence for each model. Document the intensifying theophany formula (4:5 → 8:5 → 11:19 → 16:18). Do multiple sequences end with Second Coming language? What do the transition passages 8:1-5 and 11:15-19 indicate?

### G796 — astrapē

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G796-astrapē.md`

**Occurrences:** 9

Lightning, flashing light. 9 NT occurrences: Matthew (2), Luke (3), Revelation (4). All 4 Revelation occurrences are exclusively in the four theophany-formula passages (4:5, 8:5, 11:19, 16:18). This exclusivity makes astrapē the most distinctive theophany-formula marker in Revelation — its presence identifies a theophanic passage with certainty. Hebrew source: H1300 (baraq, lightning) with PMI 10.08. Common collocations: brontē (4x), phōnē (4x), seismos (3x), chalaza (2x).

**Semantic categories:** theophanic manifestation, suddenness of Christ's coming, supernatural radiance

**Key verses:** Rev 4:5, Rev 8:5, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:18, Matt 24:27

**Relevance:** Astrapē's exclusive appearance in the four theophany passages makes it the anchor term for the escalating series. Its presence at all four sites (4:5, 8:5, 11:19, 16:18) is the non-negotiable structural marker of the theophany formula. The occurrence count (9 total, 4 in Revelation) confirms prior study finding TM057.

### G1027 — brontē

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1027-bronte.md`

**Occurrences:** 12

Thunder. 12 NT tokens in 11 verses: Mark (1), John (1), Revelation (9). Present in all four theophany-formula passages. Also appears in the seven thunders of Rev 10:3-4 (sealed, not written). Forms fixed triad with astrapē and phōnē in the theophany formula. Hebrew sources: H7482 (ra'am, thunder, PMI 18.61) and H6963 (qol, voice/sound).

**Semantic categories:** throne-room theophanic formula, seven thunders (sealed), voice of divine majesty

**Key verses:** Rev 4:5, Rev 8:5, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:18, Rev 10:3-4

**Relevance:** Thunder's presence at all four theophany occurrences confirms the persistent triad structure (astrapē + phōnē + brontē). The seven thunders of Rev 10 that John was forbidden to write represent a sealed sub-sequence within the interlude — potentially relevant to the epistemological-unsealing model.

### G4578 — seismos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4578-seismos.md`

**Occurrences:** 14

Earthquake, tempest. 14 tokens in 5 books. In Revelation: 5 occurrences (6:12; 8:5; 11:13; 11:19; 16:18). Added to the theophany formula at Rev 8:5 (4 elements). Appears at the superlative formula in 16:18 ('such as was not since men were upon the earth'). Important: Rev 11:13 has its own earthquake that is NOT part of the theophany formula (different literary function). Primary Hebrew source: H7494 (ra'ash, PMI 10.23).

**Semantic categories:** apocalyptic theophany, eschatological warning sign, divine deliverance

**Key verses:** Rev 6:12, Rev 8:5, Rev 11:13, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:18

**Relevance:** Seismos marks the escalation from 4:5 (no earthquake) to 8:5 (earthquake added) to 11:19 (earthquake + hail) to 16:18 (superlative earthquake). The 16:18 superlative formula 'such as was not since men were upon the earth' parallels Dan 12:1's 'time of trouble such as never was since there was a nation' — a linked superlative tradition.

### G5464 — chalaza

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5464-chalaza.md`

**Occurrences:** 4

Hail. 4 NT tokens, all in Revelation (8:7; 11:19; 16:21). Exclusively a Revelation word — the only NT book with this term. Added to the theophany formula at Rev 11:19 (5th element). Intensified at Rev 16:21 (each stone a talent weight, ~75-100 lbs). In Rev 8:7, hail initiates trumpet 1 but is not part of the theophany formula. Hebrew source: H1259 (barad, hail, PMI 29.60 — highest in study).

**Semantic categories:** theophanic judgment, extreme divine wrath

**Key verses:** Rev 8:7, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:21

**Relevance:** Chalaza's first theophany-formula appearance is at 11:19 (not 8:5), then intensified at 16:21. Rev 8:7 has hail but not as part of the theophany formula. The chalaza inclusio: Rev 8:7 (trumpet 1) → 11:19 (theophany formula) → 16:21 (bowl 7 climax) brackets the trumpet-bowl arc.

### G4602 — sigē

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4602-sige.md`

**Occurrences:** 2

Silence. Only 2 NT occurrences: Acts 21:40 (Paul's defense before the crowd) and Rev 8:1 (heaven silent for half an hour after 7th seal). The silence is structural — it follows the 7th seal's opening and precedes the appearance of the seven angels with trumpets. OT parallels: Hab 2:20 ('let all the earth keep silence before him'), Zeph 1:7 ('hold thy peace at the presence of the LORD'). Both OT parallels connect silence with theophanic presence and judgment.

**Semantic categories:** theophanic solemnity, pre-judgment silence, heavenly reverence

**Key verses:** Rev 8:1, Acts 21:40, Hab 2:20, Zeph 1:7

**Relevance:** The half-hour silence at Rev 8:1 is a structural hinge point. Its hapax-legomenon character in the theophany/apocalyptic context makes it structurally unique. The silence separates the 7th seal opening from the trumpet preparation — whether it belongs to the seal's 'content' or marks a transition point is central to the telescoping vs. sequential debate.

### G2256 — hēmiōrion

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2256-hemiorion.md`

**Occurrences:** 1

Half an hour. Hapax legomenon — only NT occurrence is Rev 8:1. The temporal precision of 'half an hour' is unusual in apocalyptic literature (where round numbers are typical). Various interpretive traditions: (1) Tamid liturgical silence during incense burning (Aune); (2) eschatological solemn pause before judgment; (3) the silence represents the absence of intercession as the 7th seal takes effect.

**Semantic categories:** unique temporal marker, theophanic pause

**Key verses:** Rev 8:1

**Relevance:** As a hapax legomenon, the half-hour silence is structurally isolated and unique within Revelation's vocabulary. Its placement between the 7th seal opening and the trumpet sequence makes it a pivot term for any structural analysis.

### G5055 — teleō

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5055-teleo.md`

**Occurrences:** 30

To complete, finish, bring to an end. 30 NT tokens in 26 verses. In Revelation: Rev 10:7 (mystery finished, etelesthe, aorist passive), 11:7 (testimony finished, telesōsin, aorist subjunctive), 15:1 (wrath filled up, etelesthe, aorist passive), 20:3, 20:7. The aorist passive form etelesthe appears identically at 10:7 and 15:1, creating a verbal bracket. John 19:30 (tetelestai — 'it is finished') is the perfect passive form — more complete than the aorist. Hebrew source: H3615 (kalah, to end/finish, PMI 5.49).

**Semantic categories:** completion of mission/course, eschatological completion, fulfillment of Scripture

**Key verses:** Rev 10:7, Rev 11:7, Rev 15:1, Rev 20:7, John 19:30

**Relevance:** The three Revelation uses of teleō map to three structural moments: (1) Rev 10:7 — mystery completed at/during 7th trumpet (plural 'days'); (2) Rev 11:7 — witness testimony completed (within trumpet interlude); (3) Rev 15:1 — wrath completed in the bowls (eschatai = 'last'). The bowls are explicitly called 'the last plagues' and their wrath 'filled up' — strongest terminal language for sequential/telescoping position.

### G1096 — ginomai

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1096-ginomai.md`

**Occurrences:** 702

To become, happen, come to be. 702 NT occurrences. Most diversely translated word in NT (218 unique KJV translations). Key forms in this study: (1) egeneto (Aor Mid Ind 3S) — narrative transition 'it came to pass'; (2) egenonto (Aor Mid Ind 3P) — plural 'there were' (theophany formula trigger); (3) gegonen (Perf Act Ind 3S) — 'it is done' (Rev 16:17, stative perfect); (4) gegonan (Perf Act Ind 3P) — 'it is done/they are done' (Rev 21:6, plural). Primary Hebrew source: H1961 (hayah, 1,236 LXX co-occurrences).

**Semantic categories:** narrative transition, change of state, completion declarations

**Key verses:** Rev 11:15, Rev 16:17, Rev 16:18, Rev 21:6, John 1:3

**Relevance:** The aspectual distinction between aorist egenonto/egeneto (punctiliar event, change) and perfect gegonen (stative resultant state) is central to the endpoint debate. The proleptic aorist egeneto at Rev 11:15 ('has become') vs. the perfect gegonen at 16:17 ('it is done') marks different levels of completion. Gegonen signals irreversible consummation; egeneto signals accomplished change.

### G2064 — erchomai

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2064-erchomai.md`

**Occurrences:** 664

To come, arrive. 664 verses. Suppletive paradigm: present erchomai, aorist ēlthen/ēlthon, perfect elēlytha. In Revelation: 33 occurrences. Key uses for this study: (1) ēlthen (Aor Act Ind 3S) at Rev 6:17 ('the great day of his wrath is come') and Rev 11:18 ('thy wrath is come') — VP174 IDENTICAL FORMS; (2) erchomai (Pres Ind 1S) at Rev 16:15 ('I come as a thief' — present tense); (3) ēlthen at Rev 19:7 ('the marriage of the Lamb is come' — SC context). Primary Hebrew source: H935 (bow', PMI 20.33).

**Semantic categories:** physical arrival, SC/eschatological return, coming of wrath/judgment

**Key verses:** Rev 6:17, Rev 11:18, Rev 16:15, Rev 19:7, Rev 22:20

**Relevance:** VP174: ēlthen at 6:17 and 11:18 are IDENTICAL forms of the same verb — the strongest textual evidence for the recapitulation position (same wrath-arrival language at two different sequence endpoints). Counter-argument: ēlthen describes ARRIVAL of a period; gegonen (perfect, 16:17) describes COMPLETION. The ēlthen at Rev 19:7 ('the marriage of the Lamb is come') uses the same form in an SC context — potentially supporting the argument that ēlthen at 6:17 and 11:18 also marks SC-arrival, not just the Day's onset.

### G3709 — orgē

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3709-orge.md`

**Occurrences:** 36

Settled divine indignation/wrath. Used at Rev 6:17 (genitive: 'of their wrath') and Rev 11:18 (nominative: 'your wrath'). Contrast with thumos (G2372, fierce outbursting wrath). The combination tou thymou tēs orgēs ('the fierce wrath of his wrath') appears for the first time at Rev 16:19 — the only combined occurrence in Revelation (TM097 confirmed by cross-check).

**Semantic categories:** settled divine judgment, wrath of God and Lamb

**Key verses:** Rev 6:17, Rev 11:18, Rev 16:19

**Relevance:** The VP174 argument rests on orgē: the exact construction ēlthen [hē] orgē (wrath HAS COME) appears at both endpoints. The unique combination thumos + orgē at 16:19 signals the climactic bowl endpoint is categorically different from the earlier wrath-arrival declarations.

### G2372 — thumos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2372-thumos.md`

**Occurrences:** 18

Fierce, outbursting wrath. Appears in Revelation at 12:12, 14:8, 14:10, 14:19, 15:1, 15:7, 16:1, 16:19, 18:3. Used at Rev 15:1 ('in them is filled up the thumos of God') and Rev 16:1 ('pour out the thumos of God'). Combined with orgē for the first time at Rev 16:19 (tou thymou tēs orgēs autou). The bowl sequence uses thumos consistently while the earlier sequences use orgē.

**Semantic categories:** fierce divine wrath, outbursting judgment, wrath vocabulary distinction

**Key verses:** Rev 14:10, Rev 15:1, Rev 16:1, Rev 16:19

**Relevance:** The thumos vs. orgē distinction tracks the escalation: earlier sequences use orgē (settled wrath); bowls use thumos (fierce outburst); 16:19 combines both for the first time. This vocabulary progression supports an escalating rather than recapitulative model.

### G5154 — tritos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5154-tritos.md`

**Occurrences:** 57

Third, one-third. SP036 Strong: to triton (the third part) saturates trumpets 1-4 with 11+ occurrences in Rev 8:7-12, peaking at 5 occurrences in 8:12. Zero occurrences in the bowl sequence. The fraction escalation: seals (tetarton, 1/4 at 6:8) → trumpets (to triton, 1/3, 11+ occurrences) → bowls (no fraction, totality).

**Semantic categories:** fraction marker, partial judgment

**Key verses:** Rev 6:8, Rev 8:7, Rev 8:8-12, Rev 9:15, Rev 9:18

**Relevance:** The zero-tritos pattern in the bowl sequence (TM107) is one of the strongest structural arguments against the bowls being a recapitulation of the trumpets. If both covered the same events, the same fraction language would appear. The escalation from 1/4 → 1/3 → total implies progressive intensification.

### G5357 — phialē

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5357-phiale.md`

**Occurrences:** 12

Shallow bowl/vial. The vessel for the final wrath. Kurschner notes: the shallow phialē suggests rapid, sweeping judgment — contrasting with the trumpet sequence's extended duration (trumpet 5 lasts 'five months', Rev 9:5). The verb ekcheō (to pour out) used for bowls implies swift emptying.

**Semantic categories:** final judgment vessel, rapid execution of wrath

**Key verses:** Rev 5:8, Rev 15:7, Rev 16:1-21

**Relevance:** The phialē's rapid-sweeping connotation vs. the trumpet sequence's duration (trumpet 5 = five months) supports sequential distinction between the sequences. The same vessels are introduced in Rev 5:8 as holding incense (prayers of saints) — the vessel transformation arc (SP119): incense → wrath.

### G455 — anoigō

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G455-anoigo.md`

**Occurrences:** 78

To open. Present at Rev 11:19 (naos opened) and Rev 15:5 (temple opened before bowls). Also used for scroll seals being opened (6:1, 6:3, etc.) and heaven opened at the SC (Rev 19:11). The progressive sanctuary opening arc: door (4:1) → heaven split (6:14) → naos opened, ark visible (11:19) → testimony-filled/closed (15:5-8) → no naos (21:22).

**Semantic categories:** sanctuary access, divine disclosure, DOA imagery

**Key verses:** Rev 11:19, Rev 15:5, Rev 19:11

**Relevance:** The open/closed temple sequence: naos OPENED at 11:19 (highest access point, ark visible = DOA) → naos CLOSED at 15:8 (no one could enter until plagues complete). This before/after distinction textually separates the trumpet and bowl phases. SP058 (intercession contrast) confirmed.

### G5456 — phōnē

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5456-phōnḗ.md`

**Occurrences:** 141

Voice, sound. 141 NT occurrences: Revelation (55), Acts (27), John (15), Luke (15), others. Appears in the theophany formula as phōnai (voices/thunderings) at all four occurrences (4:5, 8:5, 11:19, 16:18). phōnē megalē (great/loud voice) appears 29 times in Revelation alone — the register of authoritative heavenly announcement. Key uses: Rev 16:17 (phōnē megalē from naos and throne saying 'it is done') and Rev 11:15 (phōnai megalai in heaven announcing kingdom). From G5456 library entry: collocation with megas (G3173) = 52 co-occurrences; with astrapē = 4 co-occurrences (all four theophany passages).

**Semantic categories:** divine voice and theophany, eschatological proclamation, shepherd/sheep recognition, voice at resurrection

**Key verses:** Rev 4:5, Rev 8:5, Rev 11:15, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:17, Rev 16:18

**Relevance:** Phōnē is present in all four theophany-formula passages as phōnai (voices/thunderings). The voice from the naos at Rev 16:17 (phōnē megalē) and at Rev 11:15 (phōnai megalai) share the naos/heaven origin pattern (VP096 confirmed). The 55 occurrences in Revelation (7.1 per 100 verses — highest density of any NT book) reflects the book's concern with authoritative divine speech.
