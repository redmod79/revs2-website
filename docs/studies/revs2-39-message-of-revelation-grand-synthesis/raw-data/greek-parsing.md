## Rev 11:15

Greek Text: Καὶ ὁ ἕβδομος ἄγγελος ἐσάλπισεν· καὶ ἐγένοντο φωναὶ μεγάλαι ἐν τῷ οὐρανῷ, λέγοντες Ἐγένετο ἡ βασιλεία τοῦ κόσμου τοῦ Κυρίου ἡμῶν καὶ τοῦ Χριστοῦ αὐτοῦ, καὶ βασιλεύσει εἰς τοὺς αἰῶνας τῶν αἰώνων.

Key forms:
- Ἐγένετο (Egeneto): V-2ADI-3S γίνομαι G1096 — Aorist Middle Indicative — PROLEPTIC AORIST: the kingdom 'became' — prophetic certainty treating future event as completed. Ginomai arc Station 1
- ἐγένοντο: V-2ADI-3P — same verb, plural, narrative frame ('there came great voices')
- βασιλεύσει: V-FAI-3S βασιλεύω G936 — Future Active Indicative ('he shall reign') — future tense alongside the proleptic aorist shows the text is aware the kingdom claim is anticipatory
- τοῦ Κυρίου ἡμῶν καὶ τοῦ Χριστοῦ αὐτοῦ: 'of our Lord and of his Christ' — dual genitive: kingdom belongs to both Father and Son

## Rev 13:5

## Rev 20:4

## Rev 20:5

Key forms:
- ἔζησαν: V-AAI-3P ζάω G2198 — SAME form as 20:4. If ezesan = bodily at 20:4, it must = bodily at 20:5. 'The rest of the dead did not live' = did not experience bodily resurrection

## Rev 21:22

Greek Text: Καὶ ναὸν οὐκ εἶδον ἐν αὐτῇ· ὁ γὰρ Κύριος ὁ Θεὸς ὁ Παντοκράτωρ ναὸς αὐτῆς ἐστιν, καὶ τὸ Ἀρνίον.

Key forms:
- ναὸν οὐκ εἶδον: 'temple not I-saw' — fronted accusative naon (N-ASM G3485) for emphasis. The negation is dramatic: after 16 previous occurrences, naos is denied
- ναὸς (second occurrence): N-NSM — ANARTHROUS predicate nominative. Per Harner/Wallace analysis: qualitative force. God does not merely HAVE a temple; God IS temple-quality. The nature/quality of 'templeness' inheres in the divine persons
- ὁ Κύριος ὁ Θεὸς ὁ Παντοκράτωρ: triple-articulated divine name — full sovereign designation
- ἐστιν: V-PAI-3S εἰμί G1510 — copulative present: identity statement
- καὶ τὸ Ἀρνίον: 'and the Lamb' — coordinate subject (N-NSN ἀρνίον G721). God and Lamb jointly and equally constitute the naos. Christological and ecclesiological implication: the building becomes the persons
