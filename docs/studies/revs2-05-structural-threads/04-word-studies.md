# Word Studies

## Question
What recurring vocabulary threads run through the entire book of Revelation — 'faithful and true' (pistos kai alethinos), 'kings and priests' (basileis kai hiereis), the overcomer (nikao) arc, 'testimony of Jesus' (he martyria Iesou), 'word of God' (ho logos tou theou), 'Almighty' (pantokrator) — and how do they create structural cohesion across the four visions?

### G4103 — pistos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4103-pistos.md`

**Occurrences:** 67

Adjective meaning 'faithful' or 'trustworthy' (passive: reliable) or 'believing' (active: one who trusts). 67 tokens in 62 unique NT verses across 19 books. 8 Revelation occurrences: 1:5, 2:10, 2:13, 3:14, 17:14, 19:11, 21:5, 22:6. Applied to Christ as title (1:5, 3:14, 19:11), commanded of believers (2:10), attributed to believers (2:13, 17:14), and applied to words/sayings (21:5, 22:6). The Revelation distribution spans prologue, Vision 1, Vision 3, and epilogue.

**Semantic categories:** faithful character quality, divine attribute, faithful sayings formula, believing/believers

**Key verses:** Rev 1:5, Rev 2:10, Rev 2:13, Rev 3:14, Rev 17:14, Rev 19:11, Rev 21:5, Rev 22:6

**Relevance:** Core word in the 'faithful and true' thread. 8 Revelation occurrences trace a progression from Christological title (1:5, 3:14) through believer characteristic (2:10, 2:13, 17:14) to word-descriptor (21:5, 22:6). The person-to-words shift is a key structural observation.

### G228 — alethinos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G228-alethinos.md`

**Occurrences:** 27

Adjective meaning 'true' in the sense of genuine/authentic/real (vs. counterfeit or shadow). 27 tokens in 25 unique NT verses across 6 books. 10 Revelation occurrences: 3:7, 3:14, 6:10, 15:3, 16:7, 19:2, 19:9, 19:11, 21:5, 22:6. Heavily concentrated in Johannine literature (80% of all NT occurrences). In Revelation: applied to Christ (3:7, 3:14, 19:11), to God's character (6:10), to judgments (15:3, 16:7, 19:2), and to words (19:9, 21:5, 22:6).

**Semantic categories:** genuine vs. copy, God as true God, divine character faithful and true, true judgments and sayings, inner genuineness

**Key verses:** Rev 3:7, Rev 3:14, Rev 6:10, Rev 15:3, Rev 16:7, Rev 19:2, Rev 19:9, Rev 19:11, Rev 21:5, Rev 22:6

**Relevance:** Core word in the 'faithful and true' thread. 10 Revelation occurrences — more than pistos. Appears with and without pistos: solo at 3:7, 6:10, 15:3, 16:7, 19:2, 19:9; combined at 3:14, 19:11, 21:5, 22:6. The combined pistos kai alethinos appears only 4 times and traces the person-to-words progression.

### G3528 — nikao

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3528-nikao.md`

**Occurrences:** 28

Verb meaning 'to overcome, conquer, prevail.' 28 NT occurrences (17 in Revelation = 61%). Overwhelmingly a Johannine word (Revelation + John + 1 John = 86%). Revelation distribution: seven overcomer promises (2:7, 2:11, 2:17, 2:26, 3:5, 3:12, 3:21), theological pivot (5:5), two adversarial uses (11:7, 13:7), conflict-victory uses (6:2x2, 12:11, 15:2, 17:14), comprehensive inheritance (21:7). Tense distribution: present participle (letters — ongoing), aorist indicative (5:5, 12:11 — completed), future (11:7, 17:14 — predicted), aorist infinitive (13:7), aorist subjunctive (6:2).

**Semantic categories:** eschatological victory — believers, cosmic victory — Christ, moral victory, military/hostile conquest, legal/forensic vindication

**Key verses:** Rev 2:7, Rev 5:5, Rev 12:11, Rev 17:14, Rev 21:7, John 16:33, 1 John 5:4-5

**Relevance:** Core verb of the overcomer arc. 17 Revelation occurrences form a structured chain: promises (V1) -> pivot (V2) -> conflict/victory (V2-V3) -> culmination (V3). Tense distribution correlates with structural position. The arc spans three of four visions (present in V1, V2, V3; the comprehensive statement at 21:7 is in V3 near the V3/V4 boundary).

### G3141 — martyria

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3141-martyria.md`

**Occurrences:** 37

Feminine noun meaning 'testimony, witness, record.' 37 tokens in 33 unique NT verses across 9 books. 8 Revelation occurrences: 1:2, 1:9, 6:9, 11:7, 12:11, 12:17, 19:10 (x2). Concentrated in Johannine literature (79% of all occurrences). In Revelation, the specific phrase martyria Iesou appears at 1:2, 1:9, 12:17, 19:10 (x2), 20:4. Paired with logos tou theou at 1:2, 1:9, 6:9, 20:4.

**Semantic categories:** legal/judicial testimony, Johannine self-testimony debate, apostolic and divine witness, testimony of Jesus Christ in Revelation, character reference

**Key verses:** Rev 1:2, Rev 1:9, Rev 6:9, Rev 12:11, Rev 12:17, Rev 19:10, Rev 20:4

**Relevance:** Core noun of the testimony thread. The specific formula martyria Iesou creates a structural chain across the book. The logos tou theou + martyria pairing at four locations (1:2, 1:9, 6:9, 20:4) creates LaRondelle's 'indivisible unit.' Rev 19:10 provides the interpretive key: testimony of Jesus = spirit of prophecy.

### G3140 — martyreo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3140-martyreo.md`

**Occurrences:** 86

Verb meaning 'to testify, bear witness.' Cognate verb of martyria (G3141). In Revelation appears at 1:2 (emartyesen — John testified), 22:16 (martyresai — Jesus sent angel to testify), 22:18 (martyro — I testify), 22:20 (ho martyron — the one testifying). The verb form is concentrated in the prologue (1:2) and epilogue (22:16, 18, 20), creating a testimony-verb frame around the book.

**Semantic categories:** active testifying, divine self-testimony, prologue-epilogue framing

**Key verses:** Rev 1:2, Rev 22:16, Rev 22:18, Rev 22:20

**Relevance:** Extends the testimony thread via the cognate verb. The concentration in prologue and epilogue (with the noun form dominant in the visionary body) suggests a structural division of labor: martyreo frames, martyria permeates.

### G3144 — martys

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3144-martys.md`

**Occurrences:** 34

Masculine noun meaning 'witness' (and by extension 'martyr'). In Revelation: 1:5 (ho martys ho pistos — Christ), 2:13 (ho martys mou ho pistos — Antipas), 3:14 (ho martys ho pistos kai alethinos — Christ), 11:3 (two witnesses), 17:6 (martyron Iesou — martyrs of Jesus). The noun intersects directly with the pistos thread at 1:5, 2:13, 3:14 — the 'faithful witness' title combines two vocabulary threads.

**Semantic categories:** Christological witness title, believer witness-martyr, two witnesses

**Key verses:** Rev 1:5, Rev 2:13, Rev 3:14, Rev 11:3, Rev 17:6

**Relevance:** Intersects the pistos and martyria threads. The 'faithful witness' (martys pistos) title combines pistos + martys at 1:5, 2:13, 3:14 — three shared nodes between the two threads.

### G3142 — martyrion

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3142-martyrion.md`

**Occurrences:** 20

Neuter noun meaning 'testimony, evidence, witness.' The variant testimony noun. In Revelation, the word appears at 15:5 in the phrase 'the temple of the tabernacle of the testimony' (tes skenes tou martyriou). This is the only Revelation occurrence and uses the OT tabernacle terminology rather than the Johannine testimony formula.

**Semantic categories:** OT tabernacle terminology, testimony as evidence

**Key verses:** Rev 15:5

**Relevance:** Minor thread variant. The single Revelation occurrence at 15:5 uses martyrion in its OT sense (tabernacle of testimony) rather than the Johannine martyria Iesou formula. This distinguishes two testimony vocabulary streams in Revelation.

### G3056 — logos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3056-logos.md`

**Occurrences:** 329

Masculine noun meaning 'word, speech, saying, message.' 329 tokens across 314 verses in 24 NT books. 17 Revelation occurrences. In Revelation: logos tou theou paired with martyria (1:2, 1:9, 6:9, 20:4), logos tes martyrias (12:11), Christological title ho Logos tou Theou (19:13), applied to sayings/words (1:3, 17:17, 19:9, 21:5, 22:6, 22:7, 22:9, 22:10, 22:18, 22:19). The word functions in three registers: content (word OF God), testimony formula (paired with martyria), and Christological title (19:13).

**Semantic categories:** divine message/communication, testimony pairing formula, Christological title, prophetic sayings, words of the book

**Key verses:** Rev 1:2, Rev 1:9, Rev 6:9, Rev 12:11, Rev 19:13, Rev 20:4, Rev 21:5, Rev 22:6

**Relevance:** Core noun of the 'word of God' thread. The triple function (content, testimony pairing, Christological title) creates a progression parallel to pistos kai alethinos: from descriptor to person-name. The logos tou theou + martyria pairing is the backbone of the testimony/word thread.

### G3841 — pantokrator

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3841-pantokrator.md`

**Occurrences:** 10

Masculine noun meaning 'almighty, all-sovereign.' Compound of pas (all) + kratos (dominion). 10 NT occurrences (9 in Revelation, 1 in 2 Cor 6:18). Revelation occurrences: 1:8, 4:8, 11:17, 15:3, 16:7, 16:14, 19:6, 19:15, 21:22. Appears in the fixed formula Kyrios ho Theos ho Pantokrator (8 of 9 times). Two genitive forms (16:14, 19:15); rest are nominative. Distribution spans all four visions: V1 prologue (1:8), V2 (4:8, 11:17, 15:3, 16:7, 16:14), V3 (19:6, 19:15), V4 (21:22).

**Semantic categories:** liturgical doxology, self-identification title, eschatological sovereignty, divine presence replacing temple

**Key verses:** Rev 1:8, Rev 4:8, Rev 11:17, Rev 15:3, Rev 16:7, Rev 16:14, Rev 19:6, Rev 19:15, Rev 21:22

**Relevance:** Core noun of the Almighty thread. 9 Revelation occurrences spanning all four visions — potentially the most comprehensive thread. The formula varies: full (4:8 with temporal clause), shortened (11:17 drops 'coming one'), genitive (16:14, 19:15), temple-replacement (21:22). Formulaic variation pattern supports Bauckham's 'stock phrases with minor variations.'

### G935 — basileus

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G935-basileus.md`

**Occurrences:** 118

Masculine noun meaning 'king, sovereign.' 118 NT occurrences across 10 books. 23 Revelation occurrences — tied with Matthew for most in any book. In Revelation: Christological titles (basileus basileon at 17:14, 19:16; basileus ton ethnon at 15:3), believers made kings (1:6, 5:10 via basileia), earthly/eschatological kings (6:15, 16:12, 16:14, 17:2, 17:10, 17:12, 17:18, 18:3, 18:9, 19:18, 19:19, 21:24), demonic king (9:11).

**Semantic categories:** human monarchs, Jesus as messianic King, God/Christ as supreme sovereign, believers as kings, eschatological symbolic kings, parabolic kings, Melchizedek priest-king

**Key verses:** Rev 15:3, Rev 17:14, Rev 19:16, Rev 1:6, Rev 5:10

**Relevance:** Extended reigning vocabulary. The basileus basileon title at 17:14 and 19:16 represents the culmination of the kingly thread. Combined with hiereus at 1:6 and 5:10 to form the kings-and-priests formula. The 23 Revelation occurrences make this a major vocabulary presence.

### G2409 — hiereus

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2409-hiereus.md`

**Occurrences:** 32

Masculine noun meaning 'priest.' 32 tokens in 30 unique NT verses across 7 books. 3 Revelation occurrences: 1:6, 5:10, 20:6. All three are in the kings-and-priests formula. Hebrews dominates by density (12 of 30 occurrences). Revelation's use is exclusively figurative/eschatological — believers as priests to God.

**Semantic categories:** priestly certification, Levitical officials, priestly privilege, Christ as eternal priest, believers as priests

**Key verses:** Rev 1:6, Rev 5:10, Rev 20:6

**Relevance:** Core noun in the kings-and-priests formula. Only 3 Revelation occurrences but all three are in the structural formula. Bohr's claim that Revelation emphasizes kingship over priesthood is supported by the count: basileus 23x vs. hiereus 3x.

### G936 — basileuo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G936-basileuo.md`

**Occurrences:** 23

Verb meaning 'to reign as king.' In Revelation: 5:10 (basileusousin — shall reign, future), 11:15 (basileusei — shall reign, future), 11:17 (ebasileusas — hast reigned, aorist), 19:6 (ebasileusen — has reigned, aorist), 20:4 (ebasileusan — reigned, aorist), 20:6 (basileusousin — shall reign, future), 22:5 (basileusousin — shall reign, future). The verb traces a tense progression: future promise (5:10) -> inaugurated reign (11:17, 19:6) -> realized reign (20:4) -> eternal reign (22:5).

**Semantic categories:** future promised reign, inaugurated divine reign, millennial reign, eternal reign

**Key verses:** Rev 5:10, Rev 11:17, Rev 19:6, Rev 20:4, Rev 20:6, Rev 22:5

**Relevance:** Verbal form of the reigning thread. The tense progression from future to aorist to eternal-future traces the narrative arc of the kingdom: promised -> inaugurated -> realized -> eternalized.

### G2406 — hierateuma

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2406-hierateuma.md`

**Occurrences:** 2

Neuter noun meaning 'priesthood.' Used at 1 Pet 2:5 (hierateuma hagion — holy priesthood) and 2:9 (basileion hierateuma — royal priesthood). NOT used in Revelation — Revelation uses hiereis (G2409, the individual priest noun) rather than hierateuma (the collective priesthood noun). This lexical difference between 1 Peter and Revelation for the same OT allusion (Exo 19:6) is structurally significant.

**Semantic categories:** collective priesthood, royal priesthood

**Key verses:** 1 Pet 2:5, 1 Pet 2:9

**Relevance:** The absence of hierateuma in Revelation (which uses hiereis instead) shows Revelation independently renders the Exo 19:6 allusion rather than depending on the LXX or 1 Peter's vocabulary. Different Greek words for the same Hebrew source.

### G3529 — nike

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3529-nike.md`

**Occurrences:** 1

Feminine noun meaning 'victory.' Cognate noun of nikao (G3528). Used at 1 John 5:4 (haute estin he nike he nikesasa ton kosmon, he pistis hemon — 'this is the victory that overcomes the world, our faith'). Not used in Revelation itself but the 1 John connection links victory to faith (pistis), bridging the nikao arc and the pistos thread.

**Semantic categories:** victory through faith

**Key verses:** 1 John 5:4

**Relevance:** The cognate noun bridges nikao and pistos/pistis: victory (nike) IS faith (pistis). This lexical link means the overcomer arc and the faithfulness thread are semantically unified in the Johannine corpus.
