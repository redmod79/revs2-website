# Verse-by-Verse Analysis

## Question
What recurring vocabulary threads run through the entire book of Revelation — "faithful and true" (pistos kai alethinos), "kings and priests" (basileis kai hiereis), the overcomer (nikao) arc, "testimony of Jesus" (he martyria Iesou), "word of God" (ho logos tou theou), "Almighty" (pantokrator) — and how do they create structural cohesion across the four visions?

---

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Rev 1:1-2 — Prologue: logos tou theou + martyria Iesou first paired occurrence
**Context:** Opening verse of Revelation. God gives the revelation to Jesus Christ, who sends it through his angel to John. John "bare record" (emartyesen, G3140, aorist active indicative) of the word of God (ton logon tou Theou, G3056) and the testimony of Jesus Christ (ten martyrian Iesou Christou, G3141).
**Direct statement:** Two vocabulary threads are introduced in the book's first two verses: logos tou theou and martyria Iesou. The verb emartyesen (from martyreo, G3140) governs both objects, binding them grammatically under a single act of witness-bearing.
**Original language:** Both objects are accusative, coordinated by kai. The pairing logos + martyria is grammatically tight — one verb governs both. This is the first of four paired occurrences (1:2, 1:9, 6:9, 20:4).
**Cross-references:** The transmission chain (God -> Christ -> angel -> John) recurs at 22:6, 16, creating an inclusio.
**Relationship to other evidence:** Establishes the logos-martyria pairing that runs through the book. LaRondelle's claim that these form "one indivisible unit" is supported by the single-verb construction here.

### Rev 1:3 — Prologue beatitude: logoi tes propheteias
**Context:** First of seven beatitudes. Uses logoi (G3056, plural) in "the words of this prophecy."
**Direct statement:** Extends the logos vocabulary beyond the specific logos tou theou formula. The phrase hoi logoi tes propheteias recurs at 22:7, 10, 18, creating a prologue-epilogue frame for the logos thread.
**Relationship to other evidence:** The prophetic-words formula connects to 19:10 (testimony of Jesus = spirit of prophecy), linking the logos and martyria threads through the concept of prophecy.

### Rev 1:5-6 — Prologue: ho martys ho pistos + basileis kai hiereis
**Context:** Prologue doxology. Christ receives three titles: ho martys ho pistos (the faithful witness), ho prototokos ton nekron (firstborn of the dead), ho archon ton basileon tes ges (ruler of the kings of the earth). Immediately followed by the first kings-and-priests formula.
**Direct statement:** Five vocabulary threads converge: pistos (G4103), martys (G3144), basileus (G935, in archon ton basileon), hiereus (G2409), and basileia (G932, in basileian at 1:6). This is the densest thread convergence in the prologue.
**Original language:** Rev 1:5: ho martys (N-NSM) ho pistos (A-NSM) — article-noun-article-adjective, nominative. Pistos appears WITHOUT alethinos. Rev 1:6: epoiesen (G4160, V-AAI-3S, aorist) hemas basileian (G932, N-ASF) hiereis (G2409, N-APM). The N1904 text reads basileian (kingdom, singular) not basileis (kings, plural).
**Cross-references:** The faithful-witness title reappears at 2:13 (Antipas) and 3:14 (with alethinos added). The kings-and-priests formula recurs at 5:10, 20:6. The Exo 19:6 allusion provides the OT source.
**Relationship to other evidence:** The convergence of five threads in two verses creates the book's first major intersection node.

### Rev 1:8 — Prologue: first pantokrator occurrence
**Context:** Divine self-declaration closing the prologue. Full formula: Kyrios ho Theos, ho on kai ho en kai ho erchomenos, ho Pantokrator (G3841, N-NSM).
**Direct statement:** First of nine Revelation occurrences of pantokrator. The three-part temporal clause (the one being, the one who was, the one coming) accompanies it.
**Original language:** Pantokrator is appositive to the preceding titles, nominative singular masculine. The temporal formula here has the order is/was/coming; at 4:8 it reverses to was/is/coming.
**Cross-references:** 2 Cor 6:18 is the only other NT pantokrator occurrence. The temporal formula contracts at 11:17 (drops "the coming one").
**Relationship to other evidence:** Establishes the pantokrator thread in the prologue. Its appearance before Vision 1 begins means the thread is operative from the book's opening.

### Rev 1:9 — Vision 1 opening: logos tou theou + martyria Iesou second paired occurrence
**Context:** John on Patmos "for the word of God, and for the testimony of Jesus Christ." Second paired occurrence. Also contains basileia (G932) — "in the kingdom and patience of Jesus Christ."
**Direct statement:** Three threads present: logos, martyria, basileia.
**Original language:** dia ton logon tou Theou kai ten martyrian Iesou — the preposition dia + accusative governs both objects, creating the same grammatical binding as 1:2.
**Relationship to other evidence:** Confirms the logos-martyria pairing pattern. The dia + accusative construction (causal: "on account of") appears again at 6:9 and 20:4, where the pairing describes the reason for suffering/death.

### Rev 1:10 — First en pneumati marker
**Context:** Supporting verse. First of four en pneumati markers. Vision 1: 1:9-3:22.
**Direct statement:** Structural boundary marker.

### Rev 2:2 — Ephesus: faithfulness context
**Context:** Supporting verse. Ephesus commended for testing false apostles. Faithfulness context preceding the first overcomer promise.
**Direct statement:** Establishes the pistos theme within the letters.

### Rev 2:7 — First overcomer promise: tree of life
**Context:** First of seven overcomer promises. to nikounti (G3528, V-PAP-DSM) — present active participle dative.
**Direct statement:** Begins the nikao arc in the letters. The ear formula precedes the overcomer in letters 1-3.
**Original language:** Present participle indicates ongoing overcoming.
**Cross-references:** Fulfillment at 22:2, 14 (tree of life in New Jerusalem). VP: 2:7/22:2 share xylon tes zoes.

### Rev 2:10-11 — Smyrna: faithfulness + second overcomer promise
**Context:** ginou pistos achri thanatou (G4103) — "be faithful unto death." Second overcomer promise: second death immunity.
**Direct statement:** Thread intersection: pistos + nikao. This is the only Revelation occurrence where pistos is commanded of believers rather than attributed.
**Cross-references:** VP: 2:11/20:6,14 share deuteros thanatos.

### Rev 2:13 — Pergamos: Antipas ho martys mou ho pistos
**Context:** Antipas receives the same title structure as Christ at 1:5 — ho martys mou ho pistos — with the possessive mou added.
**Direct statement:** Thread intersection: pistos + martys. The faithful-witness title transfers from Christ to a believer. The word martys carries its double sense: witness and martyr.
**Original language:** Same article-noun-article-adjective construction as 1:5, nominative.

### Rev 2:17 — Third overcomer promise: hidden manna, new name
**Context:** nikao as present active participle dative. Third promise (Pergamos).
**Direct statement:** VP: 2:17/19:12 share onoma + oudeis oiden — Minimal VP.

### Rev 2:19 — Thyatira: pistin (faith/faithfulness)
**Context:** Supporting verse. pistin (G4102, cognate of pistos G4103) in Thyatira's commendation.
**Direct statement:** The pistis/pistos vocabulary family extends into the letter body.

### Rev 2:26-28 — Fourth overcomer promise: authority over nations + morning star
**Context:** First letter where overcomer precedes ear formula (reversal at letter 4). nikao combined with authority over nations — extended reigning vocabulary.
**Direct statement:** Thread intersection: nikao + basileia (co-regency over nations).
**Cross-references:** VP: 2:27/19:15 share poimanei + rhabdo + sidera — Moderate VP. VP: 2:28/22:16 share aster proinos — Minimal VP.

### Rev 3:5 — Fifth overcomer promise: white garments, book of life
**Context:** ho nikon (G3528, V-PAP-NSM) — present active participle nominative.
**Cross-references:** VP: 3:5/19:8 share white garment imagery. VP: 3:5/20:12-15 share biblos tes zoes.

### Rev 3:7 — Philadelphia title: ho alethinos
**Context:** Supporting verse. alethinos (G228) appears without pistos: ho hagios, ho alethinos.
**Direct statement:** Solo-alethinos form. Paired with hagios rather than pistos.

### Rev 3:12 — Sixth overcomer promise: pillar in temple, new Jerusalem
**Context:** VP: 3:12/21:2 share kaine Ierousalem + katabaino ek tou ouranou + apo tou Theou — Moderate VP.
**Direct statement:** Temple imagery finds ironic fulfillment at 21:22 where pantokrator and the Lamb ARE the temple.

### Rev 3:14 — Laodicea: ho martys ho pistos kai alethinos
**Context:** First occurrence of the FULL combined title: ho Amen, ho martys ho pistos kai alethinos.
**Direct statement:** Thread intersection: pistos + alethinos + martys. The baseline form of pistos kai alethinos applied to a PERSON.
**Original language:** ho Amen (G281), ho martys (G3144, N-NSM) ho pistos (G4103, A-NSM) kai alethinos (G228, A-NSM) — all nominative singular masculine.
**Relationship to other evidence:** Subsequent occurrences at 19:11 (rider), 21:5 (words), 22:6 (words) trace the person-to-words progression.

### Rev 3:21 — Seventh overcomer promise: throne sharing
**Context:** Climactic seventh promise. Contains both ho nikon (present participle) and kago enikesa (V-AAI-1S, aorist — Christ's completed victory).
**Direct statement:** Thread intersection: nikao + basileia (throne). Two nikao tenses in one verse: believers' ongoing overcoming (present) and Christ's completed victory (aorist).
**Cross-references:** VP: 3:21/20:4 share thronos + kathizo — Minimal VP.

### Rev 4:2 — Second en pneumati marker
**Context:** Supporting verse. Vision 2 begins: 4:1-16:21.

### Rev 4:8 — Vision 2 opening: trisagion with Pantokrator
**Context:** Throne room trisagion. Full pantokrator formula with reversed temporal order from 1:8.
**Direct statement:** Second pantokrator occurrence. The formula in Vision 2 is the fullest form: Kyrios ho Theos ho Pantokrator, ho en kai ho on kai ho erchomenos.
**Original language:** All nominative: Kyrios (N-NSM), Theos (N-NSM), Pantokrator (G3841, N-NSM).

### Rev 5:5 — Lion of Judah enikesen: nikao pivot
**Context:** Central pivot of the nikao arc. enikesen (G3528, V-AAI-3S) — aorist active indicative.
**Direct statement:** Christ's completed victory as the Lion of Judah. Immediately reinterpreted as Lamb (5:6) — victory through sacrifice.
**Original language:** Aorist enikesen: punctiliar, completed — distinct from the present participle of the letters.
**Cross-references:** John 16:33 (perfect nenikeka) provides the foundational declaration. Gen 49:9-10 provides the Lion of Judah OT allusion.

### Rev 5:10 — Second kings-and-priests formula
**Context:** New song of the redeemed. epoiesas autous basileian kai hiereis, kai basileusousin epi tes ges.
**Direct statement:** Thread intersection: basileia + hiereus + basileuo. Second kingly-priestly formula.
**Original language:** N1904 reads basileian (kingdom) + hiereis (priests) — same as 1:6. basileusousin (G936, V-FAI-3P) is future active indicative.
**Cross-references:** Exo 19:6 allusion. Note the person shift from 1:6 (hemas = us) to 5:10 (autous = them).

### Rev 6:2 — First seal: nikon kai hina nikese
**Context:** Two nikao forms: nikon (V-PAP-NSM, present participle) and nikese (V-AAS-3S, aorist subjunctive with hina).
**Direct statement:** Two distinct nikao forms in one clause. The identity of this rider is debated.
**Original language:** Present participle nikon ("conquering") + hina + aorist subjunctive nikese ("in order to conquer").

### Rev 6:9-10 — Fifth seal: logos + martyria + alethinos convergence
**Context:** Third paired occurrence of logos tou theou + martyria. Also contains alethinos in the martyrs' cry: ho despotes ho hagios kai alethinos.
**Direct statement:** Thread intersection: logos + martyria + alethinos. Three threads converge.
**Original language:** Rev 6:9: dia ton logon tou Theou kai dia ten martyrian hen eichon — two parallel dia + accusative phrases. Note: the martyria here is NOT explicitly martyrian Iesou but "the testimony which they held" (relative clause). Rev 6:10: ho Despotes (G1203) ho hagios (G40) kai alethinos (G228) — alethinos paired with hagios, not pistos.
**Relationship to other evidence:** The parallel dia construction at 6:9 matches the pattern at 1:9 and 20:4.

### Rev 9:11 — Apollyon: demonic king
**Context:** Supporting verse. basileus (G935) applied to the demonic king — contrast with Christ's basileus basileon title.

### Rev 11:3 — Two witnesses: martyrsin
**Context:** Supporting verse. martyrsin (G3144, N-DPM) — the noun "witnesses" applied to the two prophetic figures. Extends the martys vocabulary into Vision 2.

### Rev 11:7 — Beast overcomes witnesses: adversarial nikao
**Context:** nikesei (G3528, V-FAI-3S, future active indicative) — the beast overcomes the witnesses. Also contains martyrian auton (G3141) — "their testimony."
**Direct statement:** Thread intersection: nikao (adversarial) + martyria. Inverts the nikao thread's positive valence.
**Original language:** Future tense contrasts with aorist of 5:5 and 12:11.

### Rev 11:15-17 — Seventh trumpet: basileia + basileuo + pantokrator
**Context:** Three threads converge: basileia (G932, kingdoms become God's), basileusei (G936, shall reign), Pantokrator (G3841, third occurrence).
**Direct statement:** The pantokrator formula CONTRACTS — drops ho erchomenos. The "coming one" element is removed because the coming has been realized.
**Original language:** ebasileusas (G936, V-AAI-2S) — aorist, treating the eschatological event as accomplished. Pantokrator formula shortened to two-part temporal clause.

### Rev 12:11 — enikisan: three-thread convergence
**Context:** Believers' completed victory. Three threads: nikao + logos + martyria.
**Direct statement:** enikisan (G3528, V-AAI-3P, aorist) — completed. dia to haima tou Arniou kai dia ton logon tes martyrias auton.
**Original language:** logos tes martyrias — a variant of the testimony formula, not the standard logos tou Theou + martyria Iesou pairing but logos + martyria combined in a genitive construction.

### Rev 12:17 — Remnant: echonton ten martyrian Iesou
**Context:** Remnant defined by two characteristics: keeping God's commandments + having the testimony of Jesus.
**Direct statement:** martyria Iesou thread. The genitive Iesou is morphologically ambiguous (subjective or objective).
**Original language:** Both participles (terounton, echonton) are present active genitive plural — ongoing characteristics. The parallel with entolas tou Theou (commands OF God — subjective genitive) favors the subjective reading of martyrian Iesou.

### Rev 13:7 — Beast overcomes saints: second adversarial nikao
**Context:** nikesai (G3528, V-AAN, aorist active infinitive). edothe (divine passive) indicates God permits the beast's victory.
**Direct statement:** Second adversarial nikao. The divine passive contrasts with Christ's sovereign enikesen at 5:5.

### Rev 13:9 — Migrated ear formula without overcomer
**Context:** Supporting verse. Ear formula appears in chapter 13 WITHOUT the overcomer promise. The formula migrates but the nikao component drops out.

### Rev 14:12 — Saints: pistin Iesou + entolas tou Theou
**Context:** Supporting verse. Parallels 12:17 but substitutes pistin (G4102) for martyrian — functional equivalence between faith of Jesus and testimony of Jesus.

### Rev 15:2-3 — Victors over beast + Pantokrator + alethinos + basileus
**Context:** Four threads converge: nikao (nikountas, V-PAP-APM) + pantokrator (G3841) + alethinos (G228, dikaiai kai alethinai hai hodoi sou) + basileus (G935, ho Basileus ton ethnon).
**Direct statement:** Four-thread convergence node. The densest within Vision 2.
**Original language:** nikountas is present active participle accusative plural. Textual variant at 15:3: basileus ton ethnon / basileus ton hagion / basileus ton aionon.

### Rev 16:7 — Bowl judgment affirmation: pantokrator + alethinos
**Context:** Fifth pantokrator occurrence. Kyrie ho Theos ho Pantokrator, alethinai kai dikaiai hai kriseis sou.
**Direct statement:** Thread intersection: pantokrator + alethinos. Parallels 15:3 but applies to kriseis (judgments) rather than hodoi (ways).

### Rev 16:14 — Battle of the great day: genitive pantokrator
**Context:** Sixth pantokrator occurrence. Genitive form: tou Theou tou Pantokratoros (G3841, N-GSM). Also contains basileis (G935) tes oikoumenes.
**Direct statement:** Thread intersection: pantokrator + basileus (earthly kings gathered against God).

### Rev 17:3 — Third en pneumati marker
**Context:** Supporting verse. Vision 3 begins: 17:1-21:8.

### Rev 17:6 — Blood of the martyrs of Jesus
**Context:** Supporting verse. ton martyron (G3144, N-GPM) Iesou — variant form of the testimony thread using martys rather than martyria.

### Rev 17:14 — Lamb nikesei + pistoi + basileus basileon
**Context:** Three threads converge: nikao (nikesei, V-FAI-3S, future) + pistos (pistoi, A-NPM, believers are "faithful") + basileus (Basileus basileon, King of kings).
**Direct statement:** Three-thread convergence node.
**Original language:** The title order at 17:14 is Kyrios kyrion kai Basileus basileon — reverses at 19:16 to BASILEUS BASILEON KAI KYRIOS KYRION.

### Rev 19:2 — True and righteous judgments: alethinos
**Context:** alethinai kai dikaiai hai kriseis autou — alethinos applied to God's judgments. Parallels 16:7.
**Direct statement:** alethinos vocabulary in judicial-affirmation register.

### Rev 19:4 — Elders worship
**Context:** Supporting verse. Throne room worship preceding the pantokrator declaration at 19:6.

### Rev 19:6 — Pantokrator ebasileusen: Almighty reigns
**Context:** Seventh pantokrator occurrence. ebasileusen (G936, V-AAI-3S, aorist) Kyrios ho Theos hemon ho Pantokrator.
**Direct statement:** Thread intersection: pantokrator + basileuo. The aorist treats the reign as inaugurated.
**Original language:** KJV translates pantokrator as "omnipotent" here — unique rendering.

### Rev 19:8 — Fine linen: overcomer garment fulfillment
**Context:** Supporting verse. VP: 3:5/19:8 share white garment imagery. Overcomer promise fulfillment.

### Rev 19:9 — True sayings of God: logoi alethinoi tou Theou
**Context:** houtoi hoi logoi alethinoi tou Theou — alethinos applied to words. Transitional occurrence.
**Direct statement:** The alethinos vocabulary shifts from persons to words. logos + alethinos converge.

### Rev 19:10 — Testimony of Jesus = spirit of prophecy
**Context:** he martyria Iesou estin to pneuma tes propheteias. Two occurrences of martyria Iesou.
**Direct statement:** Interpretive key for the martyria Iesou thread. The copulative estin establishes identification.
**Original language:** he gar martyria (N-NSF) Iesou (N-GSM) estin (V-PAI-3S) to pneuma (N-NSN) tes propheteias (N-GSF). The gar introduces an explanatory ground.

**Context:** Second occurrence of the full pistos kai alethinos phrase. Applied as the rider's name.

**Context:** logos tou theou as a Christological title: keklemenon to onoma autou ho Logos tou Theou.

**Original language:** kekletai (V-RPI-3S, perfect passive) — naming formula. ho Logos (G3056, N-NSM) is nominative with definite article.

### Rev 19:15-16 — Pantokrator + basileus basileon
**Context:** Eighth pantokrator occurrence (genitive: tou Theou tou Pantokratoros) + BASILEUS BASILEON title.
**Direct statement:** Thread intersection: pantokrator + basileus. The title order reverses from 17:14.

### Rev 20:4-6 — Judgment throne: four-thread convergence

## Patterns Identified

### Pattern 1: Person-to-Words Progression (pistos kai alethinos)
The combined phrase pistos kai alethinos appears four times in Revelation, tracing a progression: applied to Christ as witness (3:14, person) -> applied to the rider as warrior-judge (19:11, person) -> applied to words/sayings (21:5, 22:6, words). The shift from nominative masculine singular (person) to nominative masculine plural (agreeing with logoi) is grammatically encoded.

### Pattern 2: Nikao Tense Distribution Correlates with Structural Position
Present participle (ho nikon, 2-3) in Vision 1 letters — ongoing overcoming. Aorist indicative (enikesen 5:5, enikisan 12:11) in Vision 2 — completed victories. Future indicative (nikesei 11:7, 13:7, 17:14) in Visions 2-3 — predicted outcomes. Present participle returns at 21:7 for the comprehensive inheritance.

### Pattern 3: Logos-Martyria Pairing with Shared Prepositional Construction
The four paired occurrences (1:2, 1:9, 6:9, 20:4) all use tight grammatical binding: a single verb or shared preposition governing both objects. This is consistent with LaRondelle's "indivisible unit" claim.

### Pattern 4: Pantokrator Formula Contraction
The temporal formula accompanying pantokrator contracts across the book: full three-part at 1:8 and 4:8, shortened to two-part at 11:17 (drops "the coming one"), then the temporal clause disappears entirely for later occurrences. This contraction signals progressive eschatological realization.

### Pattern 5: Kings-and-Priests Tense Progression
The basileuo verb traces: aorist epoiesen at 1:6 (made), future basileusousin at 5:10 (shall reign on earth), aorist ebasileusan at 20:4 (reigned), future basileusousin at 20:6 (shall reign 1000 years), future basileusousin at 22:5 (shall reign forever). The thread achieves temporal infinity.

### Pattern 6: Thread Intersection Density at Structural Boundaries
Multiple threads converge at or near structural boundaries: prologue (1:1-8, five threads), seventh trumpet (11:15-17, three threads), Babylon-judgment aftermath (19:6-16, five threads), New Jerusalem (21:5-7, three threads).

## Word Study Integration

The Greek data reveals structural distinctions invisible in English translation:

- **pistos** (G4103, 67 NT occurrences, 8 in Revelation) functions in three registers: Christological title, believer characteristic, word-descriptor. The shift between registers correlates with structural position.
- **alethinos** (G228, 27 NT occurrences, 10 in Revelation) has a wider distribution than pistos. Six of ten occurrences appear without pistos, in judicial contexts (6:10, 15:3, 16:7, 19:2).
- **nikao** (G3528, 28 NT occurrences, 17 in Revelation) — 61% in Revelation alone. Distinctively Johannine (86% across Revelation, John, 1 John). nike (G3529) bridges nikao to pistis at 1 John 5:4.
- **martyria** (G3141, 37 NT occurrences, 8+ in Revelation) forms the noun backbone of the testimony thread. martyreo (G3140) frames prologue/epilogue. martys (G3144) intersects with pistos at 1:5, 2:13, 3:14.
- **logos** (G3056, 329 NT occurrences, 17 in Revelation) operates in three registers: content (word OF God), testimony formula, Christological title (19:13). The triple function parallels the pistos progression.
- **pantokrator** (G3841, 10 NT occurrences, 9 in Revelation) — 90% in Revelation. Essentially unique to this book in the NT. The formulaic variation supports Bauckham's Leitwort model.
- **hiereus** (G2409, 32 NT occurrences, 3 in Revelation) — limited to the three kings-and-priests formula occurrences. basileus (G935, 118 NT occurrences, 23 in Revelation) has a far wider distribution — Bohr's observation that Revelation emphasizes kingship over priesthood is supported by the count (23 vs. 3).

## Cross-Testament Connections

- **Exo 19:6 -> Rev 1:6, 5:10, 20:6:** mamleketh kohanim rendered independently from the Hebrew rather than following the LXX's basileion hierateuma. Revelation and 1 Peter use different Greek vocabulary for the same OT allusion.
- **Jer 42:5 -> Rev 3:14, 19:11:** The only OT passage combining true + faithful + witness provides the lexical source for the pistos kai alethinos + martys combination.
- **Isa 65:16 -> Rev 3:14:** Elohey amen (God of the Amen) provides OT background for the Amen title.
- **Dan 7:18, 27 -> Rev 20:4, 22:5:** Saints receive and possess the kingdom forever — the reigning thread.
- **John 16:33 -> Rev 5:5, 3:21:** Christ's perfect-tense nenikeka grounds the Revelation nikao arc.
- **1 John 5:4 -> nikao + pistos threads:** Victory (nike) IS faith (pistis) — the two threads are semantically unified in the Johannine corpus.

## Difficult or Complicating Passages

### Rev 6:2 — Identity of the First Seal Rider
The white horse rider goes forth "conquering and to conquer" (nikon kai hina nikese). Whether this is Christ, the gospel, or a counterfeit conqueror affects how the nikao thread functions here. If adversarial, it creates a third use of nikao against God's purposes (alongside 11:7 and 13:7). If Christ or gospel, it extends the positive nikao chain into the seals. The text does not resolve the identity; both readings have structural implications.

### Rev 12:17 / 19:10 — Subjective vs. Objective Genitive
Is martyria Iesou the testimony FROM Jesus (subjective) or ABOUT Jesus (objective)? The parallel with entolas tou Theou at 12:17 favors subjective. The martyreo verb forms in the epilogue (22:16, 18, 20) show Jesus as the grammatical subject of testimony. Rev 19:10's copulative estin identifies this testimony with the spirit of prophecy. The grammatical evidence favors the subjective reading but does not eliminate the objective possibility.

### Rev 21:7 — Vision Boundary Placement
The comprehensive overcomer statement at 21:7 falls within Vision 3 (17:1-21:8) under the en pneumati framework, not Vision 4. Yet thematically it points forward to the New Jerusalem description that follows in Vision 4. This passage illustrates the transition zone identified in revs2-01 where the Vision 3/4 boundary is not a sharp line but a zone.

### Rev 15:3 Textual Variant — basileus ton ethnon / hagion / aionon
The title "King of nations/saints/ages" varies across manuscripts. All variants use basileus (G935), so the thread observation holds regardless. But the specific form affects whether the kingly vocabulary here refers to nations (political sovereignty), saints (spiritual rulership), or ages (temporal sovereignty).

## Preliminary Synthesis

The six vocabulary threads create a network of intratextual connections that spans the four-vision structure established in revs2-01. The distribution matrix shows:

- **Pantokrator** is the only thread with occurrences in all four visions: Prologue (1:8), V2 (4:8, 11:17, 15:3, 16:7, 16:14), V3 (19:6, 19:15), V4 (21:22).
- **Pistos kai alethinos** (the combined phrase) appears in V1 (3:14), V3 (19:11, 21:5), and Epilogue (22:6) — absent from V2 and V4 as combined form, though pistos and alethinos each appear separately in V2.
- **Nikao** concentrates in V1 (seven promises) and V2 (pivot + conflict), with the culminating statement at 21:7 in V3.
- **Martyria Iesou/logos tou theou** pairing appears in Prologue (1:2), V1 (1:9), V2 (6:9), and V3 (20:4), with the Christological title at 19:13 in V3. Absent from V4 in specific formula.
- **Kings-and-priests** formula appears in Prologue (1:6), V2 (5:10), V3 (20:6), V4 (22:5). The thread spans all four when the basileuo verb is included.
- **Witness family** (martyreo/martyria/martys combined) spans Prologue, V1, V2, V3, and Epilogue through cognate forms.

The threads do not independently span all four visions — most concentrate in V1 and V2-V3 with the prologue and epilogue providing framing occurrences. The convergence nodes (where multiple threads intersect) cluster at the prologue (1:1-9), the throne room (5:5-10), the cosmic conflict (12:11), the seal/trumpet scenes (15:2-3), the Babylon aftermath (17:14, 19:6-16), and the New Jerusalem transition (20:4-6, 21:5-7). This clustering at structural boundaries and transition points is consistent with deliberate literary design.
