# Verses

## Question
What are the primary structural markers in the book of Revelation? Trace the four "in the Spirit" (en pneumati) markers at Rev 1:10, 4:2, 17:3, and 21:10 — how do they divide the book into four major visions? Trace the "and I saw" (kai eidon) transitions — where do they occur, what do they introduce, and how do they subdivide the visions into scenes? Trace the "after these things" (meta tauta) indicators. How do these three marker systems work together to create Revelation's structural skeleton? For each marker, document the Greek form, every occurrence, and what structural boundary it signals. Note where the markers' structural signals converge and where they create ambiguity.

### Prologue: Book's self-identification and transmission chain

> The Revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave unto him, to shew unto his servants things which must shortly come to pass; and he sent and signified [it] by his angel unto his servant John: Who bare record of the word of God, and of the testimony of Jesus Christ, and of all things that he saw. Blessed [is] he that readeth, and they that hear the words of this prophecy, and keep those things which are written therein: for the time [is] at hand.

**Context:** Opening of the book. Establishes the transmission chain: God → Christ → angel → John → servants. Self-identifies as 'prophecy' and 'things which must shortly come to pass.' Part of the Prologue-Epilogue inclusio.

**Notes:** Verbal parallels with Rev 22:6 (angel, servants, things which must shortly be done). The word 'signified' (esēmanen, G4591 from sēmaion) connects to the semeion markers at 12:1, 12:3, 15:1.

### Prologue greeting: seven churches, seven Spirits, Alpha and Omega

> John to the seven churches which are in Asia: Grace [be] unto you, and peace, from him which is, and which was, and which is to come; and from the seven Spirits which are before his throne; And from Jesus Christ, [who is] the faithful witness, [and] the first begotten of the dead, and the prince of the kings of the earth. Unto him that loved us, and washed us from our sins in his own blood, And hath made us kings and priests unto God and his Father; to him [be] glory and dominion for ever and ever. Amen. Behold, he cometh with clouds; and every eye shall see him, and they [also] which pierced him: and all kindreds of the earth shall wail because of him. Even so, Amen. I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the ending, saith the Lord, which is, and which was, and which is to come, the Almighty.

**Context:** Prologue greeting addressed to the seven churches. Contains 'Alpha and Omega' (inclusio with 22:13), 'seven Spirits' (structural marker: Spirits before the throne), 'Behold, he cometh' (parallels 22:7, 12, 20).

**Notes:** Multiple verbal elements shared with the Epilogue (22:6-21): Alpha/Omega, seven churches, testimony, faithful witness, coming quickly.

### Space/time referent: Patmos setting, trumpet voice

> I John, who also am your brother, and companion in tribulation, and in the kingdom and patience of Jesus Christ, was in the isle that is called Patmos, for the word of God, and for the testimony of Jesus Christ. I was in the Spirit on the Lord's day, and heard behind me a great voice, as of a trumpet, Saying, I am Alpha and Omega, the first and the last: and, What thou seest, write in a book, and send [it] unto the seven churches which are in Asia; unto Ephesus, and unto Smyrna, and unto Pergamos, and unto Thyatira, and unto Sardis, and unto Philadelphia, and unto Laodicea.

**Context:** First en pneumati marker. Establishes the single visionary episode from Patmos. The trumpet voice recurs at 4:1. Korner's 'single space/time referent' — the en pneumati at 1:10 inaugurates a vision continuing through 22:5.

**Notes:** egenomen en Pneumati (V-2ADI-1S of ginomai G1096 + PREP G1722 + N-DSN of pneuma G4151). Anarthrous pneumati. Trumpet voice (salpinx G4536) parallels 4:1.

### First vision content: seven lampstands, Son of Man description

> And I turned to see the voice that spake with me. And being turned, I saw seven golden candlesticks; And in the midst of the seven candlesticks [one] like unto the Son of man, clothed with a garment down to the foot, and girt about the paps with a golden girdle. His head and [his] hairs [were] white like wool, as white as snow; and his eyes [were] as a flame of fire; And his feet like unto fine brass, as if they burned in a furnace; and his voice as the sound of many waters. And when I saw him, I fell at his feet as dead. And he laid his right hand upon me, saying unto me, Fear not; I am the first and the last: [I am] he that liveth, and was dead; and, behold, I am alive for evermore, Amen; and have the keys of hell and of death. **Context:** Vision 1 content begins. Sanctuary imagery: seven golden lampstands (menorah). Rev 1:17 contains 'when I saw him' (kai hote eidon auton) — eidon with intervening words, classified as concurrent description by Korner. Rev 1:19 contains meta tauta in temporal framework: past/present/future division.

**Notes:** Rev 1:19 Greek: graphon oun ha eides kai ha eisin kai ha mellei genesthai meta tauta. The meta tauta here is part of the temporal framework (what shall be hereafter), not a structural transition formula. Paulien identifies this as a sanctuary introduction scene (lampstands = Holy Place furniture).

### Spirit saith to the churches formula (first of seven)

> He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith unto the churches; To him that overcometh will I give to eat of the tree of life, which is in the midst of the paradise of God.

**Context:** First occurrence of the 'Spirit saith unto the churches' formula. Uses articular to Pneuma (the Spirit, G4151 with definite article G3588 T-NSN) — contrasting with the anarthrous en Pneumati of the visionary-state formula. Occurs identically at 2:11, 2:17, 2:29, 3:6, 3:13, 3:22.

- τὸ (G3588) — T-NSN — Nom Sg N
- Πνεῦμα (G4151) — N-NSN — Nom Sg N
- λέγει (G3004) — V-PAI-3S — Pres Act Ind Sg thirdPers
- ἐκκλησίαις (G1577) — N-DPF — Dat Pl F

**Notes:** Greek: to Pneuma legei tais ekklesiais. The Spirit (articular, nominative) is the subject speaking. This is functionally different from en Pneumati (anarthrous, dative) where pneuma describes John's visionary state. Seven occurrences confined to Vision 1.

### Throne room scene: inaugural vision of Vision 2

> And he that sat was to look upon like a jasper and a sardine stone: and [there was] a rainbow round about the throne, in sight like unto an emerald. And round about the throne [were] four and twenty seats: and upon the seats I saw four and twenty elders sitting, clothed in white raiment; and they had on their heads crowns of gold. And before the throne [there was] a sea of glass like unto crystal: and in the midst of the throne, and round about the throne, [were] four beasts full of eyes before and behind. And the first beast [was] like a lion, and the second beast like a calf, and the third beast had a face as a man, and the fourth beast [was] like a flying eagle. **Context:** Throne room scene. Rev 4:5 contains the first Sinai-theophany formula: astrapai kai phonai kai brontai (lightnings and voices and thunderings) — 3 elements. This is the inaugural scene for Vision 2. Paulien identifies it as a sanctuary introduction (throne room).

### Sealed book and Lamb: kai eidon scene openers in the throne room

> And I saw in the right hand of him that sat on the throne a book written within and on the backside, sealed with seven seals. And I saw a strong angel proclaiming with a loud voice, Who is worthy to open the book, and to loose the seals thereof? And no man in heaven, nor in earth, neither under the earth, was able to open the book, neither to look thereon. And I wept much, because no man was found worthy to open and to read the book, neither to look thereon. And one of the elders saith unto me, Weep not: behold, the Lion of the tribe of Juda, the Root of David, hath prevailed to open the book, and to loose the seven seals thereof. **Context:** Three kai eidon occurrences: 5:1 (kai eidon — sealed book), 5:2 (kai eidon — strong angel), 5:6 (kai eidon kai idou — Lamb). The double formula kai eidon kai idou at 5:6 marks elevated structural significance. The Lamb is introduced with a perfect passive participle (esphagmenon — 'having been slain,' resultant state).

**Notes:** Rev 5:6 kai eidon kai idou is one of 9 occurrences of the double formula. Rev 5:11 has the twinned visual-auditory formula: kai eidon kai ekousa.

### First seal: kai eidon opens the seal sequence

> And I saw when the Lamb opened one of the seals, and I heard, as it were the noise of thunder, one of the four beasts saying, Come and see. And I saw, and behold a white horse: and he that sat on him had a bow; and a crown was given unto him: and he went forth conquering, and to conquer.

**Context:** Kai eidon opens the seal sequence (6:1). Rev 6:2 has kai eidon kai idou (double formula) — the white horse. Thunder (bronte G1027) connects to the Sinai-theophany vocabulary. The twinned eidon-ekousa formula marks each seal opening.

**Notes:** Progressive intensity begins here: seals use 1/4 fraction (6:8). Kai eidon kai idou at 6:2, 6:5, 6:8 marks each horse-rider revelation.

### Fourth seal: pale horse, 1/4 fraction — progressive intensity marker

> And I looked, and behold a pale horse: and his name that sat on him was Death, and Hell followed with him. And power was given unto them over the fourth part of the earth, to kill with sword, and with hunger, and with death, and with the beasts of the earth.

**Context:** Kai eidon kai idou — double formula for elevated significance. 'The fourth part' (tetarton G5067) establishes the first fraction in the progressive intensity pattern: seals = 1/4, trumpets = 1/3, bowls = total.

**Notes:** The fraction escalation (1/4 → 1/3 → all) is a structural marker supporting either progressive sequence or telescoping.

### Meta touto eidon: singular demonstrative variant

> And after these things I saw four angels standing on the four corners of the earth, holding the four winds of the earth, that the wind should not blow on the earth, nor on the sea, nor on any tree.

**Context:** Uses meta TOUTO (singular, neuter — D-ASN of houtos G3778) instead of the usual meta TAUTA (plural — D-APN). This grammatical variation is structurally significant: singular touto may reference a single preceding event, while plural tauta references a series of events.

- ἀγγέλους (G32) — N-APM — Acc Pl M

**Notes:** Greek: Meta touto eidon (PREP G3326 + D-ASN G3778 + V-2AAI-1S G3708). Korner includes this as one of 5 meta tauta eidon occurrences. Bandy excludes it (counting only 4). The singular vs. plural distinction needs resolution.

### First four trumpets: 1/3 fraction — progressive intensity escalation

> The first angel sounded, and there followed hail and fire mingled with blood, and they were cast upon the earth: and the third part of trees was burnt up, and all green grass was burnt up. And the second angel sounded, and as it were a great mountain burning with fire was cast into the sea: and the third part of the sea became blood; And the third part of the creatures which were in the sea, and had life, died; and the third part of the ships were destroyed. And the third angel sounded, and there fell a great star from heaven, burning as it were a lamp, and it fell upon the third part of the rivers, and upon the fountains of waters; And the name of the star is called Wormwood: and the third part of the waters became wormwood; and many men died of the waters, because they were made bitter. And the fourth angel sounded, and the third part of the sun was smitten, and the third part of the moon, and the third part of the stars; so as the third part of them was darkened, and the day shone not for a third part of it, and the night likewise.

**Context:** Trumpets use 1/3 fraction (triton G5154) — escalation from seals' 1/4. The 'third part' recurs 12 times in the first four trumpets. 100% domain correspondence with bowls: earth, sea, rivers, sun — in identical sequential order.

**Notes:** Progressive intensity: seals 1/4 (6:8) → trumpets 1/3 (8:7-12) → bowls total (16:3-4). The domain correspondence (earth/sea/rivers/sun) supports either recapitulation or progressive parallel sequences.

### Disputed meta tauta: woe announcement

> One woe is past; [and], behold, there come two woes more hereafter.

**Context:** Meta tauta appears here but functions as a simple temporal reference ('two woes more hereafter'), not as a structural-transition formula. No eidon follows. Larsen includes this in his count of 9 meta tauta occurrences; others exclude it.

- ἀπῆλθεν (G565) — V-2AAI-3S — Aor Act Ind Sg thirdPers
- ἔρχεται (G2064) — V-PNI-3S — Pres M/P Ind Sg thirdPers
- μετὰ (G3326) — PREP — ταῦτα οὗτος G3778 [D-APN] Acc Pl N

**Notes:** Greek: He ouai he mia apelthen; idou erchetai eti duo ouai meta tauta. The meta tauta here means 'hereafter/after this' as a temporal adverb, not a vision-transition formula. Classification: non-formulaic temporal reference.

### Kai eidon: mighty angel with little book (6th-7th trumpet interlude)

> And I saw another mighty angel come down from heaven, clothed with a cloud: and a rainbow [was] upon his head, and his face [was] as it were the sun, and his feet as pillars of fire:

**Context:** Kai eidon introduces the mighty angel interlude between the 6th and 7th trumpets. This interlude (10:1-11:14) parallels the sealing interlude between the 6th and 7th seals (7:1-17). The 4+1+(interlude)+1 pattern in both seals and trumpets supports structural parallel.

**Notes:** The intercalation between 6th and 7th elements is structurally significant. Present in seals and trumpets but ABSENT in bowls — supporting Bauckham's observation that bowls have a 4+3 rather than 4+1+int+1 pattern.

### Seventh trumpet: kingdom declaration, theophany formula occurrence 3, ark revealed

> And the seventh angel sounded; and there were great voices in heaven, saying, The kingdoms of this world are become [the kingdoms] of our Lord, and of his Christ; and he shall reign for ever and ever. And the four and twenty elders, which sat before God on their seats, fell upon their faces, and worshipped God, Saying, We give thee thanks, O Lord God Almighty, which art, and wast, and art to come; because thou hast taken to thee thy great power, and hast reigned. And the nations were angry, and thy wrath is come, and the time of the dead, that they should be judged, and that thou shouldest give reward unto thy servants the prophets, and to the saints, and them that fear thy name, small and great; and shouldest destroy them which destroy the earth. And the temple of God was opened in heaven, and there was seen in his temple the ark of his testament: and there were lightnings, and voices, and thunderings, and an earthquake, and great hail.

**Context:** Major structural convergence point. Rev 11:18 elthen he orge ('thy wrath is come') — identical declaration to 6:17 (seal climax), supporting recapitulation. Rev 11:19 Sinai-theophany formula: 5 elements (astrapai, phonai, brontai, seismos, chalaza megale). Ark of covenant visible = Most Holy Place opened = Day of Atonement transition.

**Notes:** Sinai-theophany formula occurrence 3: 5 elements (astrapai G796, phonai G5456, brontai G1027, seismos G4578, chalaza megale G5464+G3173). Progression from 4 at 8:5 to 5 here. The ophthe (V-API-3S, aorist passive of horao) for the ark = 'was seen/appeared' — same passive construction as semeion ophthe at 12:1, 12:3. Neither en pneumati nor meta tauta marks 11:19 as a division, yet sanctuary progression and theophany formula place a major transition here.

### Semeion ophthe: woman sign, chronological regression proof

> And there appeared a great wonder in heaven; a woman clothed with the sun, and the moon under her feet, and upon her head a crown of twelve stars: And she being with child cried, travailing in birth, and pained to be delivered. And there appeared another wonder in heaven; and behold a great red dragon, having seven heads and ten horns, and seven crowns upon his heads. And his tail drew the third part of the stars of heaven, and did cast them to the earth: and the dragon stood before the woman which was ready to be delivered, for to devour her child as soon as it was born. And she brought forth a man child, who was to rule all nations with a rod of iron: and her child was caught up unto God, and [to] his throne.

**Context:** Rev 12:1 semeion mega ophthe (V-API-3S) — passive voice: 'a great sign appeared/was seen.' Distinct from kai eidon (active: 'I saw'). Rev 12:3 kai ophthe allo semeion — second sign appeared. Rev 12:5 narrates Christ's birth AFTER the 7th trumpet's kingdom declaration at 11:15 — the single most decisive proof that kai eidon does NOT always signal forward chronological movement.

- σημεῖον (G4592) — N-NSN — Nom Sg N
- μέγα (G3173) — A-NSN — Nom Sg N
- ὤφθη (G3708) — V-API-3S — Aor Pass Ind Sg thirdPers
- γυνὴ (G1135) — N-NSF — Nom Sg F
- περιβεβλημένη (G4016) — V-RPP-NSF — Perf Pass Ptcp Nom Sg F

**Notes:** Semeion ophthe at 12:1, 12:3 uses aorist PASSIVE indicative 3rd singular of horao. Agency shifted: the sign presents itself rather than John actively seeing it. The perfect passive participle peribeblemene (clothed) at 12:1 = summary tableau over the whole pericope (apocalyptic narrative prolepsis per grammar reference entry).

### Kai eidon contemporaneous beasts: chronological neutrality test case

> And I stood upon the sand of the sea, and saw a beast rise up out of the sea, having seven heads and ten horns, and upon his horns ten crowns, and upon his heads the name of blasphemy. [13:1] | And I beheld another beast coming up out of the earth; and he had two horns like a lamb, and he spake as a dragon. [13:11]

**Context:** Both verses use kai eidon (13:1, 13:11). But the second beast operates CONTEMPORANEOUSLY with the first (13:12: 'exerciseth all the power of the first beast before him'). This demonstrates that kai eidon does NOT require chronological sequence between scenes — SP006 from revs-02.

**Notes:** Key test case for chronological neutrality. kai eidon at 13:11 introduces a beast operating at the SAME TIME as the beast introduced at 13:1. The aorist tense describes completed action without specifying temporal relationship between events.

### Kai eidon kai idou: Lamb on Mount Zion — elevated double formula

> And I looked, and, lo, a Lamb stood on the mount Sion, and with him an hundred forty [and] four thousand, having his Father's name written in their foreheads.

### Kai eidon kai idou: Son of Man on white cloud — harvest

> Afterwards the spirit took me up, and brought me in a vision by the Spirit of God into Chaldea, to them of the captivity. So the vision that I had seen went up from me.

**Context:** Ezekiel's Spirit-transport. The spirit 'took me up' (nasa') and 'brought me' (bo') in a vision by the Spirit of God. Location-specific transport (to Chaldea). The vision concluding formula ('went up from me') marks the end of the visionary experience.

**Notes:** Ezekiel Spirit-transport parallel. Double Spirit reference: 'the spirit took me up' and 'by the Spirit of God' — the ruach functions both as transport agent and as the visionary medium. Parallels the en pneumati formula in Revelation where pneuma serves both functions.

### Ezekiel Spirit-transport: carried me out in the spirit of the LORD

> The hand of the LORD was upon me, and carried me out in the spirit of the LORD, and set me down in the midst of the valley which [was] full of bones,

**Context:** Valley of dry bones vision. 'The hand of the LORD was upon me' + 'carried me out in the spirit of the LORD' — combines the 'hand of the LORD' formula with Spirit-transport. Location: valley full of bones.

**Notes:** Ezekiel Spirit-transport parallel. 'Carried me out in the spirit of the LORD' (hotsi'ani beruach YHWH) — the carrying verb + in the spirit construction parallels Revelation's apophero + en pneumati at 17:3 and 21:10. Location-specific transport to a vision scene.
