# Verses

## Question
What are Christ's "I Am" (ego eimi) declarations in Revelation, and how do they form a structural frame — particularly the Alpha/Omega declarations in the prologue and epilogue? Document every ego eimi statement, its position, and its thematic content. How do the hymns, doxologies, and worship scenes function as structural markers throughout the book? Document every hymn/doxology, identify who sings, what the content is, and where it falls in the narrative structure. Do the hymns mark structural transitions?

### Prologue frame — grace formula, doxology, Alpha/Omega declaration

> The Revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave unto him, to shew unto his servants things which must shortly come to pass; and he sent and signified [it] by his angel unto his servant John: Who bare record of the word of God, and of the testimony of Jesus Christ, and of all things that he saw. Blessed [is] he that readeth, and they that hear the words of this prophecy, and keep those things which are written therein: for the time [is] at hand. John to the seven churches which are in Asia: Grace [be] unto you, and peace, from him which is, and which was, and which is to come; and from the seven Spirits which are before his throne; And from Jesus Christ, [who is] the faithful witness, [and] the first begotten of the dead, and the prince of the kings of the earth. Unto him that loved us, and washed us from our sins in his own blood, And hath made us kings and priests unto God and his Father; to him [be] glory and dominion for ever and ever. Amen. Behold, he cometh with clouds; and every eye shall see him, and they [also] which pierced him: and all kindreds of the earth shall wail because of him. Even so, Amen. I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the ending, saith the Lord, which is, and which was, and which is to come, the Almighty.

**Context:** Opening of Revelation. Prologue establishes the book's authority chain (God -> Christ -> angel -> John). Verses 4-5a contain the grace formula with temporal formula ('him which is, and which was, and which is to come'). Verses 5b-6 contain the first doxology. Verse 7 is the parousia announcement. Verse 8 is the Alpha/Omega ego eimi declaration with pantokrator title.

**Notes:** The speaker at 1:8 is identified as 'the Lord' (kyrios ho theos) with pantokrator — language typically associated with the Father in Revelation. But v.7 just described Christ's coming. The temporal formula appears in its full three-part form. First occurrence of Alpha/Omega title. Prologue doxology at 1:5b-6 is the first hymnic unit in Revelation.

### Inaugural vision — en pneumati marker, Christophany, ego eimi: First/Last and Living One

> I was in the Spirit on the Lord's day, and heard behind me a great voice, as of a trumpet, Saying, I am Alpha and Omega, the first and the last: and, What thou seest, write in a book, and send [it] unto the seven churches which are in Asia; unto Ephesus, and unto Smyrna, and unto Pergamos, and unto Thyatira, and unto Sardis, and unto Philadelphia, and unto Laodicea. And I turned to see the voice that spake with me. And being turned, I saw seven golden candlesticks; And in the midst of the seven candlesticks [one] like unto the Son of man, clothed with a garment down to the foot, and girt about the paps with a golden girdle. His head and [his] hairs [were] white like wool, as white as snow; and his eyes [were] as a flame of fire; And his feet like unto fine brass, as if they burned in a furnace; and his voice as the sound of many waters. And he had in his right hand seven stars: and out of his mouth went a sharp twoedged sword: and his countenance [was] as the sun shineth in his strength. And when I saw him, I fell at his feet as dead. And he laid his right hand upon me, saying unto me, Fear not; I am the first and the last: [I am] he that liveth, and was dead; and, behold, I am alive for evermore, Amen; and have the keys of hell and of death.

**Context:** First en pneumati marker at v.10. Christ appears amid the seven lampstands. V.11 contains Alpha/Omega in KJV/TR but this is ABSENT from N1904 critical text (textual variant confirmed via greek_text_compare.py). V.17-18 contain two ego eimi declarations: 'I am the first and the last' and 'I am he that liveth, and was dead.'

**Notes:** Greek text compare confirms Rev 1:11 Alpha/Omega is NOT in N1904 — only in TR. Strong's in N1904 only: G3004, G1125, G3992, G991, G3739, G1577, G4667. Strong's in TR only: G4413, G1473, G1, G5598, G2078 — these are the ego eimi and Alpha/Omega terms. The N1904 text of 1:11 simply reads 'saying, What you see write in a book and send to the seven churches.' The ego eimi at 1:17 uses explicit ego eimi (G1473 + G1510) with ho protos kai ho eschatos. At 1:18, ho zon (the Living One) + egenomen nekros (I became dead) + zon eimi eis tous aionas ton aionon (I am alive unto the ages of the ages).

### Smyrna letter opening — indirect ego eimi formula

> And unto the angel of the church in Smyrna write; These things saith the first and the last, which was dead, and is alive;

**Context:** Opening self-identification of Christ to the Smyrna church. Uses 'tade legei' (these things says) formula followed by First/Last title from 1:17 plus death/resurrection from 1:18.

**Notes:** This is an indirect ego eimi — the 'tade legei' formula introduces Christ's titles without the explicit ego eimi verbal construction. The self-identification draws directly on 1:17-18, applying the inaugural vision Christology to the specific church situation (persecution, poverty).

### Thyatira letter — Son of God self-identification and YHWH heart-searcher appropriation

> And unto the angel of the church in Thyatira write; These things saith the Son of God, who hath his eyes like unto a flame of fire, and his feet [are] like fine brass; I know thy works, and charity, and service, and faith, and thy patience, and thy works; and the last [to be] more than the first. Notwithstanding I have a few things against thee, because thou sufferest that woman Jezebel, which calleth herself a prophetess, to teach and to seduce my servants to commit fornication, and to eat things sacrificed unto idols. And I gave her space to repent of her fornication; and she repented not. Behold, I will cast her into a bed, and them that commit adultery with her into great tribulation, except they repent of their deeds. And I will kill her children with death; and all the churches shall know that I am he which searcheth the reins and hearts: and I will give unto every one of you according to your works.

**Context:** Only letter where Christ identifies as 'the Son of God.' V.23 contains explicit ego eimi: 'ego eimi ho eraunon nephrous kai kardias' (I am he who searches minds and hearts). This appropriates YHWH's self-description from Jer 17:10.

**Notes:** Greek confirms ego (G1473) eimi (G1510) followed by ho eraunon (the one searching, G2045 present active participle) nephrous (kidneys/reins, G3510) kai kardias (and hearts, G2588). Jer 17:10 reads 'I the LORD search the heart, I try the reins' — four vocabulary parallels: YHWH/ego, search/eraunon, heart/kardia, reins/nephros. Christ claims YHWH's prerogative directly.

### Thyatira overcomer promise — morning star linking to 22:16 epilogue

> And he that overcometh, and keepeth my works unto the end, to him will I give power over the nations: And he shall rule them with a rod of iron; as the vessels of a potter shall they be broken to shivers: even as I received of my Father. And I will give him the morning star.

**Context:** Overcomer promise includes 'the morning star' (ton astera ton proinon). Proinos (G4407) occurs only twice in the entire NT: here at 2:28 and at 22:16 where Christ identifies himself AS the morning star.

**Notes:** The promise to give the overcomer the morning star at 2:28 is fulfilled by the ego eimi declaration at 22:16 where Christ IS the bright morning star. This creates a structural link from the central letters to the epilogue.

### Trisagion and creation worthiness hymn — first worship scene

> And the four beasts had each of them six wings about [him]; and [they were] full of eyes within: and they rest not day and night, saying, Holy, holy, holy, Lord God Almighty, which was, and is, and is to come. And when those beasts give glory and honour and thanks to him that sat on the throne, who liveth for ever and ever, The four and twenty elders fall down before him that sat on the throne, and worship him that liveth for ever and ever, and cast their crowns before the throne, saying, Thou art worthy, O Lord, to receive glory and honour and power: for thou hast created all things, and for thy pleasure they are and were created.

**Context:** Trisagion (v.8) + three-fold attribute doxology (v.9, glory/honour/thanks) + worthiness hymn (v.11, creation, glory/honour/power). First two participants: living creatures and elders.

**Notes:** Three attributes at 4:9 (doxa, time, eucharistia) and three at 4:11 (doxa, time, dynamis) — different third term. The trisagion modifies Isa 6:3.

**Context:** The Lamb is introduced (v.5-6), takes the scroll (v.7), five-step worship expansion, four hymnic units: new song (v.9-10), seven-fold angel acclamation (v.12), four-fold universal doxology (v.13), amen response (v.14).

**Notes:** This is the subset of Rev 5 focused on the Lamb's introduction and the worship expansion pattern.

### Scroll crisis — weeping at the sealed scroll, dramatic tension before Lamb introduction

> And I saw in the right hand of him that sat on the throne a book written within and on the backside, sealed with seven seals. And I saw a strong angel proclaiming with a loud voice, Who is worthy to open the book, and to loose the seals thereof? And no man in heaven, nor in earth, neither under the earth, was able to open the book, neither to look thereon. And I wept much, because no man was found worthy to open and to read the book, neither to look thereon.

**Context:** The scroll crisis creates dramatic tension. The 'who is worthy' question (axios at v.2,4) sets up the worthiness hymns that follow.

**Notes:** Axios at 5:2 and 5:4 are interrogative/negative — 'who is worthy?' and 'no man was found worthy.' These prepare for the positive declarations at 5:9 and 5:12.

### Lamb on Zion — 144,000 singing exclusive new song

> And I looked, and, lo, a Lamb stood on the mount Sion, and with him an hundred forty [and] four thousand, having his Father's name written in their foreheads. And I heard a voice from heaven, as the voice of many waters, and as the voice of a great thunder: and I heard the voice of harpers harping with their harps: And they sung as it were a new song before the throne, and before the four beasts, and the elders: and no man could learn that song but the hundred [and] forty [and] four thousand, which were redeemed from the earth.

**Context:** The 144,000 sing a new song (ode kainos) that only they can learn. Ode (G5603) designation marks this as one of three explicitly 'sung' passages.

**Notes:** The exclusivity of this song — only the 144,000 can learn it — distinguishes it from the other hymnic units. The redeemed singing contrasts with the throne-room creatures and elders of earlier scenes.

### Everlasting gospel — worship imperative proskynesate to Creator

> And I saw another angel fly in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel to preach unto them that dwell on the earth, and to every nation, and kindred, and tongue, and people, Saying with a loud voice, Fear God, and give glory to him; for the hour of his judgment is come: and worship him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters.

**Context:** The worship imperative: proskynesate (aorist imperative, G4352) directed to the Creator. This is the positive command against beast worship (v.9-11).

**Notes:** The only aorist imperative of proskyneo in Revelation. Creator language echoes Exo 20:11.

### Throne room vision — second en pneumati, trisagion, creation worthiness hymn

> After this I looked, and, behold, a door [was] opened in heaven: and the first voice which I heard [was] as it were of a trumpet talking with me; which said, Come up hither, and I will shew thee things which must be hereafter. And immediately I was in the spirit: and, behold, a throne was set in heaven, and [one] sat on the throne. And he that sat was to look upon like a jasper and a sardine stone: and [there was] a rainbow round about the throne, in sight like unto an emerald. And round about the throne [were] four and twenty seats: and upon the seats I saw four and twenty elders sitting, clothed in white raiment; and they had on their heads crowns of gold. And out of the throne proceeded lightnings and thunderings and voices: and [there were] seven lamps of fire burning before the throne, which are the seven Spirits of God. And before the throne [there was] a sea of glass like unto crystal: and in the midst of the throne, and round about the throne, [were] four beasts full of eyes before and behind. And the first beast [was] like a lion, and the second beast like a calf, and the third beast had a face as a man, and the fourth beast [was] like a flying eagle. And the four beasts had each of them six wings about [him]; and [they were] full of eyes within: and they rest not day and night, saying, Holy, holy, holy, Lord God Almighty, which was, and is, and is to come. And when those beasts give glory and honour and thanks to him that sat on the throne, who liveth for ever and ever, The four and twenty elders fall down before him that sat on the throne, and worship him that liveth for ever and ever, and cast their crowns before the throne, saying, Thou art worthy, O Lord, to receive glory and honour and power: for thou hast created all things, and for thy pleasure they are and were created.

**Context:** Second en pneumati marker at v.2. Opens Vision 2 (4:1-16:21). The throne room introduces the heavenly worship setting. V.8 contains the trisagion (three-fold holy) modifying Isa 6:3 — replaces 'the whole earth is full of his glory' with the temporal formula 'which was, and is, and is to come.' V.11 contains the first worthiness hymn (creation). Three-fold attribute formula at 4:9 (glory, honour, thanks) and 4:11 (glory, honour, power).

**Notes:** Greek confirms temporal formula at 4:8 in three-part form: ho en kai ho on kai ho erchomenos. NOTE: the N1904 text has the unusual order ho en (was) THEN ho on (is) THEN ho erchomenos (coming) — contrasting with 1:4 and 1:8 which have ho on first. At 4:11, axios (G514) is applied to 'ho kyrios kai ho theos hemon' (our Lord and God) — directed to the Father/Creator. Three attributes: doxa (G1391), time (G5092), dynamis (G1411).

### Scroll crisis and Lamb — new song, angel acclamation, universal doxology, progressive participant expansion

> And I saw in the right hand of him that sat on the throne a book written within and on the backside, sealed with seven seals. And I saw a strong angel proclaiming with a loud voice, Who is worthy to open the book, and to loose the seals thereof? And he came and took the book out of the right hand of him that sat upon the throne. And when he had taken the book, the four beasts and four [and] twenty elders fell down before the Lamb, having every one of them harps, and golden vials full of odours, which are the prayers of saints. And every creature which is in heaven, and on the earth, and under the earth, and such as are in the sea, and all that are in them, heard I saying, Blessing, and honour, and glory, and power, [be] unto him that sitteth upon the throne, and unto the Lamb for ever and ever. And the four beasts said, Amen. And the four [and] twenty elders fell down and worshipped him that liveth for ever and ever.

**Context:** Five-step participant expansion: (1) four living creatures (4:8), (2) 24 elders (4:10-11), (3) creatures + elders (5:8-10), (4) myriads of angels + creatures + elders (5:11-12), (5) every creature in creation (5:13). The new song at 5:9 uses ado (G103) + ode (G5603) + kainos (G2537). Seven-fold attribute formula at 5:12: dynamis, ploutos, sophia, ischys, time, doxa, eulogia. Four-fold formula at 5:13: eulogia, time, doxa, kratos. V.14 amen response from living creatures.

**Notes:** Greek 5:9 confirms aidousin (G103, present active indicative) oden (G5603, accusative) kainen (G2537). Axios at 5:9 applied to the Lamb for redemption, paralleling axios at 4:11 for the Father/Creator. 5:12 seven attributes: dynamis (G1411), ploutos (G4149), sophia (G4678), ischys (G2479), time (G5092), doxa (G1391), eulogia (G2129). 5:13 four attributes addressed to BOTH 'him that sitteth upon the throne AND the Lamb' — co-recipients. Single dative kai to arnio confirms God and the Lamb receive the same worship.

### Great multitude worship — salvation acclamation, seven-fold angel doxology, Tabernacles imagery

> After this I beheld, and, lo, a great multitude, which no man could number, of all nations, and kindreds, and people, and tongues, stood before the throne, and before the Lamb, clothed with white robes, and palms in their hands; And cried with a loud voice, saying, Salvation to our God which sitteth upon the throne, and unto the Lamb. And all the angels stood round about the throne, and [about] the elders and the four beasts, and fell before the throne on their faces, and worshipped God, Saying, Amen: Blessing, and glory, and wisdom, and thanksgiving, and honour, and power, and might, [be] unto our God for ever and ever. Amen.

**Context:** Interlude between seals 6 and 7. Palm branches (v.9) evoke Tabernacles imagery (Zec 14:16-18). V.10 salvation cry directed to God AND the Lamb. V.12 seven-fold doxology: eulogia (G2129), doxa (G1391), sophia (G4678), eucharistia (G2169), time (G5092), dynamis (G1411), ischys (G2479). Framed by double amen.

**Notes:** Seven attributes at 7:12 share 5 terms with 5:12: eulogia, doxa, sophia, time, dynamis, ischys — but 7:12 has eucharistia (thanksgiving) where 5:12 has ploutos (wealth). The sixth term differs. Both have 7 attributes. The structural position is at the seal interlude — between seals 6 and 7.

### Seventh trumpet — kingdom proclamation, elder thanksgiving hymn, temporal formula contraction

> And the seventh angel sounded; and there were great voices in heaven, saying, The kingdoms of this world are become [the kingdoms] of our Lord, and of his Christ; and he shall reign for ever and ever. And the four and twenty elders, which sat before God on their seats, fell upon their faces, and worshipped God, Saying, We give thee thanks, O Lord God Almighty, which art, and wast, and art to come; because thou hast taken to thee thy great power, and hast reigned. And the nations were angry, and thy wrath is come, and the time of the dead, that they should be judged, and that thou shouldest give reward unto thy servants the prophets, and to the saints, and them that fear thy name, small and great; and shouldest destroy them which destroy the earth.

**Context:** Seventh trumpet marks a major structural boundary. V.15 announces the kingdom transfer. V.17 contains the temporal formula in contracted two-part form: 'ho on kai ho en' — dropping 'ho erchomenos' (the coming one). This contraction signals narrative realization: He has come to reign (ebasileusen, aorist). Followed by theophany formula at v.19 (lightnings, voices, thunderings, earthquake, hail).

**Notes:** Greek 11:17 confirms: ho on (present participle, 'the one being') kai ho en (imperfect indicative, 'the one who was') — two-part only, NO ho erchomenos. The aorist ebasileusen (G936) may be proleptic/prophetic aorist — the reign is declared as accomplished. Pantokrator (G3841) appears in the address formula. The KJV 'and art to come' is a TR addition not in N1904.

### Cosmic victory hymn — salvation/kingdom/authority/power acclamation, non-throne-room setting

> And I heard a loud voice saying in heaven, Now is come salvation, and strength, and the kingdom of our God, and the power of his Christ: for the accuser of our brethren is cast down, which accused them before our God day and night. And they overcame him by the blood of the Lamb, and by the word of their testimony; and they loved not their lives unto the death. Therefore rejoice, [ye] heavens, and ye that dwell in them. Woe to the inhabiters of the earth and of the sea! for the devil is come down unto you, having great wrath, because he knoweth that he hath but a short time.

**Context:** This hymn occurs within the cosmic conflict narrative (ch. 12), NOT at a structural boundary. One of two non-throne-room hymns identified by Smith. Four attributes at v.10: soteria (salvation), dynamis (strength), basileia (kingdom), exousia (power/authority). The speaker is an unnamed loud voice in heaven.

**Notes:** This hymn functions as a pivot within the cosmic conflict section. It celebrates Satan's expulsion but also announces woe to the earth — a double-edged proclamation. The four-fold attribute pattern (12:10) matches the 4-fold at 5:13 in count but uses different terms: soteria, dynamis, basileia, exousia vs. eulogia, time, doxa, kratos.

### Lamb on Zion — 144,000 singing new song, everlasting gospel worship imperative

> And I looked, and, lo, a Lamb stood on the mount Sion, and with him an hundred forty [and] four thousand, having his Father's name written in their foreheads. And I heard a voice from heaven, as the voice of many waters, and as the voice of a great thunder: and I heard the voice of harpers harping with their harps: And they sung as it were a new song before the throne, and before the four beasts, and the elders: and no man could learn that song but the hundred [and] forty [and] four thousand, which were redeemed from the earth. These are they which were not defiled with women; for they are virgins. These are they which follow the Lamb whithersoever he goeth. These were redeemed from among men, [being] the firstfruits unto God and to the Lamb. And in their mouth was found no guile: for they are without fault before the throne of God. And I saw another angel fly in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel to preach unto them that dwell on the earth, and to every nation, and kindred, and tongue, and people, Saying with a loud voice, Fear God, and give glory to him; for the hour of his judgment is come: and worship him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters.

**Context:** V.3 uses ode (G5603) for the exclusive new song only the 144,000 can learn. V.7 contains the worship imperative: proskynesate (aorist imperative of proskyneo, G4352) directed to the Creator — the positive counterpart to the beast worship warning in v.9-11.

**Notes:** The worship imperative at 14:7 is the only aorist imperative of proskyneo in Revelation — a decisive command. The Creator-language ('him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters') echoes Exodus 20:11 (Sabbath commandment). This positions true worship against beast worship in the defining binary.

### Song of Moses and the Lamb — sea of glass, explicit ode designation, creation/nations themes

> And I saw as it were a sea of glass mingled with fire: and them that had gotten the victory over the beast, and over his image, and over his mark, [and] over the number of his name, stand on the sea of glass, having the harps of God. And they sing the song of Moses the servant of God, and the song of the Lamb, saying, Great and marvellous [are] thy works, Lord God Almighty; just and true [are] thy ways, thou King of saints. Who shall not fear thee, O Lord, and glorify thy name? for [thou] only [art] holy: for all nations shall come and worship before thee; for thy judgments are made manifest.

**Context:** Bowl prelude. Victors over the beast sing. Uses ado (G103) + ode (G5603) twice — 'the song of Moses' and 'the song of the Lamb.' Pantokrator (G3841) in address. Nominative-for-vocative at 'Kyrie ho Theos ho Pantokrator.'

### OT background — 'I the LORD, the first, and with the last; I am he' (ani hu)

> Who hath wrought and done [it], calling the generations from the beginning? I the LORD, the first, and with the last; I [am] he.

**Context:** Isaiah's monotheistic polemic against Babylonian idols. God challenges the nations to demonstrate any deity who controls history. 'I YHWH rishon we-et acharonim ani hu' — the first of three First/Last declarations in Isaiah 41-48.

### OT background — 'I am the first, and I am the last; beside me there is no God'

> Thus saith the LORD the King of Israel, and his redeemer the LORD of hosts; I [am] the first, and I [am] the last; and beside me [there is] no God.

**Notes:** Hebrew: ani (I) rishon (H7223, first) wa-ani (and I) acharon (H314, last, Adj.ms.Abs) u-mibaladi (and besides me) ein (there is not) elohim (God). When Christ claims 'the first and the last' in Rev 1:17 and 22:13, he claims a title that in Isaiah is YHWH's exclusive property in a monotheistic polemic context.

Wherefore God also hath highly exalted him, and given him a name which is above every name: That at the name of Jesus every knee should bow, of [things] in heaven, and [things] in earth, and [things] under the earth; And [that] every tongue should confess that Jesus Christ [is] Lord, to the glory of God the Father.

**Context:** Pre-Revelation Christ hymn. Structure: pre-existence (v.6) -> humiliation (v.7-8) -> exaltation (v.9-11). Universal worship in v.10-11 parallels Rev 5:13 (every creature). 'Every knee shall bow' echoes Isa 45:23.

**Notes:** Comparative hymnody: Phil 2 has a narrative arc (descent-ascent) while Revelation's hymns are acclamatory (responding to completed events). Phil 2 culminates in worship 'to the glory of God the Father' — Christ's worship glorifies the Father. Revelation's throne room has God and the Lamb as co-recipients.

### Comparative NT Christ-hymn — creation stanza and reconciliation stanza

> Who is the image of the invisible God, the firstborn of every creature: For by him were all things created, that are in heaven, and that are in earth, visible and invisible, whether [they be] thrones, or dominions, or principalities, or powers: all things were created by him, and for him: And he is before all things, and by him all things consist. And he is the head of the body, the church: who is the beginning, the firstborn from the dead; that in all [things] he might have the preeminence. For it pleased [the Father] that in him should all fulness dwell; And, having made peace through the blood of his cross, by him to reconcile all things unto himself; by him, [I say], whether [they be] things in earth, or things in heaven.

**Context:** Two-stanza structure: creation (v.15-17) and reconciliation (v.18-20). 'The arche' (beginning) at v.18 connects to Rev 3:14 and 22:13. 'Firstborn from the dead' connects to Rev 1:5 'first begotten of the dead.'

**Notes:** Shared vocabulary with Revelation hymns: arche (beginning), prototokos (firstborn), ktizo (create), haima (blood), thronos (throne). Col 1 hymn has a systematic theological structure; Revelation hymns are more liturgical/responsive.

### Comparative NT confessional hymn fragment — six-line creedal formula

> And without controversy great is the mystery of godliness: God was manifest in the flesh, justified in the Spirit, seen of angels, preached unto the Gentiles, believed on in the world, received up into glory.

**Context:** Brief confessional/hymnic fragment in six parallel lines. No narrative arc — declarative creedal form. No worship language per se, but summarizes Christ's work in a liturgical-sounding formula.

**Notes:** The most compact of the NT hymnic fragments. Revelation's hymns are longer and more varied in form than this compressed creedal statement.

### OT ego eimi background — 'I AM THAT I AM'

> And God said unto Moses, I AM THAT I AM: and he said, Thus shalt thou say unto the children of Israel, I AM hath sent me unto you.

**Context:** The foundational divine self-declaration. 'Ehyeh asher ehyeh' — I AM THAT I AM. LXX renders as ego eimi ho on (I am the being one). Background for both Johannine and Revelation ego eimi usage.

**Notes:** The LXX rendering ego eimi ho on directly connects to Revelation's 'ho on' (the One who is) in the temporal formula at 1:4, 1:8, 4:8.

### Johannine ego eimi parallel — 'Before Abraham was, I am'

> Jesus said unto them, Verily, verily, I say unto you, Before Abraham was, I am.

**Context:** Jesus' most explosive ego eimi in John's Gospel. The Jews attempt to stone him (v.59), recognizing the divine claim. Relevant as Johannine background for the same author's use of ego eimi in Revelation.

**Notes:** Same author (John) uses ego eimi as a divine self-declaration in both the Gospel and Revelation. In the Gospel, ego eimi is a claim to deity that provokes violent reaction. In Revelation, the ego eimi declarations carry the same weight but in a visionary/apocalyptic context.

### Third en pneumati marker — wilderness transport, no hymn at this boundary (structural absence)

> So he carried me away in the spirit into the wilderness: and I saw a woman sit upon a scarlet coloured beast, full of names of blasphemy, having seven heads and ten horns.

**Context:** Third en pneumati marker. Opens Vision 3 (17:1-21:8). Angel carries John to the wilderness. Notable: NO hymn accompanies this structural boundary. The structural ABSENCE of worship at this transition is significant — the Babylon vision begins without heavenly endorsement.

**Notes:** The absence of a hymn at the third en pneumati marker contrasts with the second (4:2 leads immediately to throne room worship). This structural silence is itself a marker.

### Fourth en pneumati marker — mountain transport, parallel angel-introduction formula

> And there came unto me one of the seven angels which had the seven vials full of the seven last plagues, and talked with me, saying, Come hither, I will shew thee the bride, the Lamb's wife. And he carried me away in the spirit to a great and high mountain, and shewed me that great city, the holy Jerusalem, descending out of heaven from God,

**Context:** Fourth en pneumati marker. Opens Vision 4 (21:9-22:5). Parallels 17:1-3 with the angel-introduction formula (6+ identical Greek phrases). No hymn at this boundary either — the worship elements come later within the vision.

**Notes:** The parallel between 17:1-3 and 21:9-10 is one of the strongest structural indicators in Revelation. Both begin with 'one of the seven angels with the seven vials' + 'come hither, I will show you' + en pneumati transport. But the destinations contrast: wilderness (Babylon/harlot) vs. mountain (New Jerusalem/bride).

### Throne room establishment — 'one sat on the throne' identification

> And immediately I was in the spirit: and, behold, a throne was set in heaven, and [one] sat on the throne. And he that sat was to look upon like a jasper and a sardine stone: and [there was] a rainbow round about the throne, in sight like unto an emerald.

**Context:** Establishes 'ho kathemenos epi ton thronon' (he who sits on the throne) as a reference for the Father/God throughout Revelation. This identification matters for the speaker question at 21:5-6.

**Notes:** The phrase 'ho kathemenos epi tou thronou' appears at 4:2, 4:9, 5:1, 5:7, 5:13, 6:16, 7:10, 7:15, 19:4, 20:11, 21:5. In every case up to 21:5, it refers to God the Father. At 22:1,3, the throne becomes 'the throne of God AND of the Lamb' — convergence.

### River and throne — 'throne of God and of the Lamb' singular, kings-and-priests fulfillment

> And he shewed me a pure river of water of life, clear as crystal, proceeding out of the throne of God and of the Lamb. In the midst of the street of it, and on either side of the river, [was there] the tree of life, which bare twelve [manner of] fruits, [and] yielded her fruit every month: and the leaves of the tree [were] for the healing of the nations. And there shall be no more curse: but the throne of God and of the Lamb shall be in it; and his servants shall serve him: And they shall see his face; and his name [shall be] in their foreheads. And there shall be no night there; and they need no candle, neither light of the sun; for the Lord God giveth them light: and they shall reign for ever and ever.

**Context:** The culmination of the throne imagery. 'The throne of God and of the Lamb' (singular throne, two occupants) appears at v.1 and v.3. 'His servants shall serve him' — singular pronoun for God-and-the-Lamb together. 'They shall reign for ever and ever' — fulfillment of 'kings and priests' from 1:6 and 5:10.

**Notes:** Bauckham's observation verified: 'his servants shall serve HIM' (singular autou) and 'they shall see HIS face' (singular autou) — God and the Lamb are treated grammatically as one. John avoids plural pronouns for God-and-Christ. Pantokrator at 21:22 nearby: 'the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are the temple of it.'
