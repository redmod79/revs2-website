# Word Studies

## Question
What are Christ's "I Am" (ego eimi) declarations in Revelation, and how do they form a structural frame — particularly the Alpha/Omega declarations in the prologue and epilogue? Document every ego eimi statement, its position, and its thematic content. How do the hymns, doxologies, and worship scenes function as structural markers throughout the book? Document every hymn/doxology, identify who sings, what the content is, and where it falls in the narrative structure. Do the hymns mark structural transitions?

### G5598 — omega

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5598-ō.md`

**Occurrences:** 4

The last letter of the Greek alphabet, occurring 4 times in the NT — all in Revelation (1:8, 1:11, 21:6, 22:13). Always paired with Alpha as a divine self-designation expressing exhaustive sovereignty. Forms a structural inclusio around the book.

**Semantic categories:** divine self-designation, structural inclusio, christological identity, eschatological completion

**Key verses:** Rev 1:8, Rev 21:6, Rev 22:13

**Relevance:** Core to the Alpha/Omega structural frame. The four occurrences (including the TR variant at 1:11) create the inclusio that frames Revelation.

### G239 — allelouia

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G239-allēlouïa.md`

**Occurrences:** 4

Hebrew loanword (halelu-Yah, 'praise ye the LORD'). All 4 NT occurrences concentrated in Rev 19:1-6 — the ONLY NT hallelujah passage. Functions as liturgical exclamation in the climactic worship scene after Babylon's fall.

**Semantic categories:** heavenly doxology, Hebrew psalmic praise tradition

**Key verses:** Rev 19:1, Rev 19:3, Rev 19:4, Rev 19:6

**Relevance:** The concentration of all NT allelouias in one scene (19:1-6) marks this as the climactic worship event in Revelation. Hebrew loanword connects back to OT Hallel psalms.

### G514 — axios

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G514-axios.md`

**Occurrences:** 41

Adjective meaning 'worthy, deserving.' 41 NT occurrences across 10 books. 7 occurrences in Revelation tracing creation (4:11) -> redemption (5:9,12) -> judgment irony (16:6). Kou claims axios applied to God 'nowhere outside Revelation' in the NT — the library entry shows the doxological acclamation category is indeed concentrated in Revelation.

**Semantic categories:** doxological acclamation, moral-ethical fitness, legal-judicial assessment, trustworthy saying formula

**Key verses:** Rev 4:11, Rev 5:2, Rev 5:4, Rev 5:9, Rev 5:12, Rev 16:6

**Relevance:** The axios acclamation establishes Christological parity — God is worthy for creation (4:11), the Lamb is worthy for redemption (5:9,12). The judgment-ironic use at 16:6 inverts the positive acclamation.

### G5603 — ode

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5603-ōidḗ.md`

**Occurrences:** 7

Noun meaning 'song, ode, chant.' 7 NT occurrences: 5 in Revelation, 1 each in Ephesians and Colossians. In Revelation, designates specific heavenly songs: 'new song' (5:9, 14:3), 'song of Moses' and 'song of the Lamb' (15:3). Cognate of ado (G103).

**Semantic categories:** heavenly liturgical song, congregational worship expression, exclusive or restricted song

**Key verses:** Rev 5:9, Rev 14:3, Rev 15:3

**Relevance:** Only 3 Revelation passages explicitly use the word 'song' (ode): 5:9, 14:3, 15:3. This means most hymnic units are 'said' (lego) rather than explicitly 'sung' — structural distinction.

### G5214 — humneo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5214-humneo.md`

**Occurrences:** 4

Verb meaning 'to hymn, sing a religious ode.' 4 NT occurrences: Matt 26:30, Mark 14:26, Acts 16:25, Heb 2:12. ABSENT from Revelation — Revelation uses ado (G103) and lego (G3004) for hymnic utterances, never humneo.

**Semantic categories:** liturgical worship after sacred meals, praise amid suffering, messianic declaration

**Key verses:** Matt 26:30, Acts 16:25, Heb 2:12

**Relevance:** The ABSENCE of humneo from Revelation is notable — the author uses different vocabulary (ado, lego) for hymnic speech. This suggests Revelation's hymns are distinct in form from traditional 'hymn-singing.'

### G103 — ado

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G103-aido.md`

**Occurrences:** 5

Primary verb 'to sing.' 5 NT occurrences: 3 in Revelation (5:9, 14:3, 15:3), 1 each in Ephesians and Colossians. Cognate of ode (G5603). In Revelation, always co-occurs with ode — 'they sing a song.'

**Semantic categories:** heavenly worship and praise, congregational spiritual expression

**Key verses:** Rev 5:9, Rev 14:3, Rev 15:3

**Relevance:** The three Revelation passages using ado match the three using ode — these are the explicitly 'sung' hymnic units. The remaining hymnic units use lego (say), creating a distinction between sung songs and spoken doxologies.

### G281 — amen

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G281-amḗn.md`

**Occurrences:** 152

Hebrew particle meaning 'firm, trustworthy, surely.' 152 NT occurrences across 24 books, 10 in Revelation. Functions as solemn affirmation (Jesus' 'verily'), liturgical response closing doxologies, epistolary closing, Christological title (Rev 3:14), and heavenly worship acclamation.

**Semantic categories:** solemn affirmation by Jesus, liturgical response, epistolary closing, Christological title, heavenly worship acclamation

**Key verses:** Rev 1:6, Rev 1:7, Rev 3:14, Rev 5:14, Rev 7:12, Rev 19:4, Rev 22:20

**Relevance:** Amen functions as a structural marker in Revelation — closing doxologies (1:6, 7:12), responding to worship (5:14, 19:4), and as a Christological title (3:14). The double amen at 7:12 frames the seven-fold doxology.

### H1984 — halal

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1984-halal.md`

**Occurrences:** 193

Hebrew verb 'to praise, shine, boast.' 193 occurrences, 104 in Psalms. The imperative plural 'hallelu' combined with 'Yah' (H3050) produces the compound 'hallelujah' (praise ye the LORD), which is transliterated into Greek as allelouia (G239) in Rev 19.

**Semantic categories:** praise and worship of God, boasting and self-glory, shining and light, commendation and renown

**Key verses:** Psa 113:1, Psa 150:6, Psa 22:23

**Relevance:** Root of hallelujah compound. The OT Hallel psalms (113-118) and final doxology psalms (146-150) provide the worship tradition background for Rev 19's allelouia concentration.

### H7223 — rishon

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7223-riʼshôwn.md`

**Occurrences:** 183

Hebrew adjective 'first, former.' 183 occurrences. In Isaiah's divine self-declarations (41:4, 44:6, 48:12), God identifies as 'the first' (rishon) paired with 'the last' (acharon). LXX maps to protos (G4413) in 104 co-occurrences.

**Semantic categories:** temporal ordinal, temporal priority (former things), rank/status, divine self-designation

**Key verses:** Isa 41:4, Isa 44:6, Isa 48:12

**Relevance:** Source word for Greek protos in Revelation's 'First and Last' title. The Isaiah divine self-designation usage is the specific category relevant to this study.

### H314 — acharon

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H314-ʼachărôwn.md`

**Occurrences:** 53

Hebrew adjective 'last, latter, hinder.' 53 occurrences. Paired with rishon (H7223) in divine self-declarations and in the Chronicler's formula. LXX maps to eschatos (G2078) in 33 co-occurrences — the direct bridge to Revelation's 'First and Last.'

**Semantic categories:** temporal last in sequence, Chronicler formula, future generations, spatial rear/western position

**Key verses:** Isa 41:4, Isa 44:6, Isa 48:12

**Relevance:** Source word for Greek eschatos in the 'First and Last' title. The rishon/acharon pair in Isaiah flows directly into protos/eschatos in Revelation via LXX.

### G3841 — pantokrator

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3841-pantokrator.md`

**Occurrences:** 10

Masculine noun 'all-ruler, Almighty.' 10 NT occurrences: 9 in Revelation, 1 in 2 Cor 6:18. LXX renders YHWH Sabaoth (LORD of hosts) as kyrios pantokrator. Appears in the ego eimi declaration at 1:8 and in hymns at 4:8, 11:17, 15:3, 16:7, 19:6. The ONLY vocabulary thread spanning all four en pneumati visions.

**Semantic categories:** liturgical doxology, self-identification / Christological title, eschatological sovereignty, divine presence replacing temple

**Key verses:** Rev 1:8, Rev 4:8, Rev 11:17, Rev 15:3, Rev 16:7, Rev 19:6, Rev 21:22

**Relevance:** The lexical bridge between ego eimi declarations and hymns. Appears at 1:8 (ego eimi context) and in six hymnic passages. The only thread spanning all four en pneumati visions.

### G4352 — proskyneo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4352-proskyneo.md`

**Occurrences:** 65

Verb 'to worship, bow down, do homage.' 65 NT occurrences (tokens) across 54 unique verses. Revelation has 22 occurrences (41% of all NT uses) — highest concentration. The worship binary: positive worship (God/Lamb) vs. negative worship (beast/dragon).

**Semantic categories:** prostration before Jesus, recognition of divine Sonship, true worship discourse, exclusive worship command, heavenly throne room worship, eschatological false worship

**Key verses:** Rev 4:10, Rev 7:11, Rev 11:16, Rev 13:4, Rev 14:7, Rev 19:4, Rev 19:10, Rev 22:9

**Relevance:** The defining verb for worship in Revelation (37% of NT occurrences). The aorist imperative proskynesate at 14:7 is the positive command against beast worship.

### G721 — arnion

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G721-arnion.md`

**Occurrences:** 30

Diminutive neuter noun 'lamb.' 30 tokens in 28 verses: 27 in Revelation, 1 in John 21:15. Central Christological designation in Revelation — enthroned, slain, worshipped, warrior-judge, bridegroom. Appears in 6 hymnic passages (5:8-14, 7:10, 14:1-4, 15:3, 19:7-9, 21:22-23).

**Semantic categories:** enthroned Lamb, slain Lamb, object of worship, warrior-judge, bridegroom, New Jerusalem

**Key verses:** Rev 5:6, Rev 5:12, Rev 7:10, Rev 15:3, Rev 19:7, Rev 21:22

**Relevance:** The Lamb is the central Christological figure in the hymns — appearing as co-recipient of worship with God. Six hymnic appearances. The 'throne of God and of the Lamb' (22:1,3) is the culmination.

### G1391 — doxa

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1391-doxa.md`

**Occurrences:** 168

Feminine noun 'glory.' 168 NT occurrences (tokens) across 151 unique verses. 17 in Revelation. Primary attribute in doxological ascription formulas. Appears in every attribute-count pattern: 2-fold (1:6), 3-fold (4:9, 4:11, 19:1), 4-fold (5:13, 12:10), and 7-fold (5:12, 7:12).

**Semantic categories:** divine luminous presence, Christ's inherent glory, doxological ascription, human honor/status

**Key verses:** Rev 1:6, Rev 4:9, Rev 4:11, Rev 5:12, Rev 5:13, Rev 7:12, Rev 19:1, Rev 21:23

**Relevance:** Doxa is the most frequent attribute term across all doxological formulas in Revelation — it appears in every pattern (3-fold, 4-fold, 7-fold). The universal presence of doxa creates continuity across the attribute-count progression.

### G2316 — theos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2316-theos.md`

**Occurrences:** 1343

Masculine noun 'God, deity.' 1343 NT occurrences (tokens) across 1172 unique verses. 90 in Revelation (22.3 per 100 verses). Central to speaker identification in ego eimi passages: ho kyrios ho theos at 1:8 points to the Father; ego Iesous at 22:16 points to Christ.

**Semantic categories:** supreme deity, God the Father, Son of God / Christ as God, kingdom of God

**Key verses:** Rev 1:8, Rev 4:8, Rev 4:11, Rev 7:12, Rev 19:6, Rev 21:3, Rev 21:22

**Relevance:** Critical for speaker identification throughout the ego eimi passages and hymns. The progression from 'the Lord God' (1:8) to 'the throne of God AND of the Lamb' (22:1,3) traces the Christological convergence.

### G40 — hagios

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G40-hagios.md`

**Occurrences:** 229

Adjective 'holy, sacred, set apart.' 229 NT occurrences (tokens) across 219 unique verses. 25 in Revelation. The triple hagios at 4:8 (trisagion) is the most concentrated use in a single verse. Also used for 'saints' (martyrs, persecuted faithful) and 'holy city.'

**Semantic categories:** Holy Spirit, the saints, sacred places, divine attribute, Holy One

**Key verses:** Rev 4:8, Rev 6:10, Rev 15:4, Rev 21:2

**Relevance:** The trisagion at 4:8 (triple hagios) anchors the throne-room worship scene. Modified from Isa 6:3 — the temporal formula replaces the spatial glory claim.

### G746 — arche

Feminine noun 'beginning, origin, ruler, principality.' 58 NT occurrences. Part of the Beginning/End merism pair in the Alpha/Omega title complex. At Rev 21:6 and 22:13, arche designates Christ as the beginning/origin of all things.

**Semantic categories:** temporal beginning, origin/source, ruler/principality, divine self-designation

**Key verses:** Rev 3:14, Rev 21:6, Rev 22:13

**Relevance:** Part of the Beginning/End merism pair. Combined with telos at 21:6 and 22:13. At 3:14, Christ is 'the arche of the creation of God.'

### G5056 — telos

Neuter noun 'end, completion, goal, tribute.' 42 NT occurrences. Paired with arche (G746) in the Beginning/End merism at Rev 21:6 and 22:13.

**Key verses:** Rev 21:6, Rev 22:13

### G1510 — eimi

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1510-eimi.md`

**Occurrences:** 155

Copulative verb 'to be, I am.' 155 occurrences in NT (as eimi specifically). The verb in the ego eimi self-declaration formula. In Rev 1:8 and 22:16, the full ego eimi form appears; in 21:6 and 22:13, the ego alone functions without the verb.

**Semantic categories:** divine self-declaration formula, existential copula, identity statement

**Key verses:** Rev 1:8, Rev 1:17, Rev 1:18, Rev 2:23, Rev 22:16

**Relevance:** The verb component of ego eimi. Its presence or absence in the self-declarations matters: 1:8, 1:17, 1:18, 2:23, 22:16 have explicit eimi; 21:6 and 22:13 use ego alone (verbless self-identification).

### G1473 — ego

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1473-ego.md`

**Occurrences:** 370

First person pronoun 'I.' 370 NT occurrences. The emphatic pronoun in the ego eimi formula. In Greek, the pronoun is not required (the verb form already indicates person), so its explicit use signals emphasis — 'I myself am.'

**Semantic categories:** emphatic self-reference, divine self-declaration, apostolic authority

**Key verses:** Rev 1:8, Rev 1:17, Rev 2:23, Rev 21:6, Rev 22:13, Rev 22:16

**Relevance:** Present in every ego eimi declaration. The emphatic pronoun ego transforms an ordinary copula into a solemn self-declaration. At 22:16, 'ego Iesous' (I, Jesus) definitively identifies the speaker.

### G4407 — proinos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4407-proinos.md`

**Occurrences:** 1

Adjective 'morning, early.' Only 1 occurrence in the N1904 text (Rev 22:16 — 'the morning star'). The TR also has it at Rev 2:28. Links the Thyatira overcomer promise to the epilogue ego eimi declaration.

**Semantic categories:** temporal (morning), christological title (morning star)

**Key verses:** Rev 2:28, Rev 22:16

**Relevance:** The extreme rarity (1-2 NT occurrences, both in Revelation) creates a lexical link between the Thyatira letter (2:28) and the epilogue ego eimi (22:16). Christ promises the morning star, then identifies himself AS the morning star.

### G792 — aster

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G792-aster.md`

**Occurrences:** 24

Masculine noun 'star.' 24 NT occurrences. In Revelation: seven stars of the churches (1:16,20), star from heaven (8:10, 9:1), twelve-star crown (12:1), and the bright morning star as Christ's title (22:16).

**Semantic categories:** celestial body, symbolic identification (churches), christological title (morning star), apocalyptic imagery

**Key verses:** Rev 1:16, Rev 1:20, Rev 2:28, Rev 22:16

**Relevance:** The morning star title at 22:16 connects to the Num 24:17 'Star out of Jacob' prophecy and to the seven stars of the churches in the inaugural vision.
