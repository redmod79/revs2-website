### Focus Areas

charagma-vocabulary. **Charagma (G5480) — Mark Vocabulary and Every Occurrence:**
The charagma investigation begins with etymology. Both charagma and charakter derive from charasso (to engrave), but they point in opposite directions: charakter in Hebrews 1:3 describes Christ as the authentic divine imprint — the exact representation of God's nature. Charagma in Revelation describes a counterfeit imprint imposed by a counterfeit system. The charasso root creates a spectrum from authentic (charakter) through protective (sphragis) to counterfeit (charagma). The nine occurrences must be mapped exhaustively because the pattern of collocations (which words always accompany charagma) reveals whether the mark is ever separable from worship and image — or whether they form an inseparable triad.
**Verses:** Rev 13:16, Rev 13:17, Rev 14:9, Rev 14:11, Rev 15:2, Rev 16:2, Rev 19:20, Rev 20:4, Acts 17:29
**Strong's:** G5480, G5481, G4742, G4593

sphragis-charagma-competition. **Sphragis (G4973) vs. Charagma — Seal/Mark Binary Competition:**
The seal/mark binary is the structural backbone of this study. Sphragis appears 16 times in the NT (13 in Revelation, mostly in the seven-seal sequence of chs. 5-8 plus the sealing of ch. 7). Charagma appears 9 times (8 in Revelation, concentrated in chs. 13-14 and 16-20). The two words never overlap — they are mutually exclusive markers of allegiance. When John distributes them across Revelation's scenes, he builds a sustained either/or: every person in the narrative bears one or the other. The LXX mapping from chowtham (H2368) to sphragis achieves a PMI of 10.71, the highest relevance score in the study's Hebrew-Greek connections. This means sphragis is the overwhelmingly dominant Greek choice for Hebrew seal/signet vocabulary. The absence of any LXX antecedent for charagma reinforces that it was coined as a direct anti-sphragis.
**Verses:** Rev 7:2, Rev 7:3, Rev 9:4, Rev 14:1, Rev 22:4, Rev 5:1-2, Eph 1:13, Eph 4:30, 2 Cor 1:22, Rom 4:11
**Strong's:** G4973, G4972, G5480, H2368, H2856

metopon-allegiance-binary. **Metopon (G3359) — Forehead as the Allegiance Site:**
The metopon investigation reveals that Revelation has engineered a closed anatomical allegiance system. The forehead is the exclusive contest site — no one receives either seal or mark elsewhere. The asymmetry is theologically loaded: God's seal appears on the forehead alone because divine allegiance requires conviction, the engagement of the mind and will. The beast's mark appears on forehead OR hand because the counterfeit system accepts either genuine belief or mere outward compliance. This maps directly onto the Deuteronomic sequence where God's instructions must be in the heart (Deut 6:6) before they become a sign on hand and frontlets between eyes (Deut 6:8). The beast reverses this: external action (hand) is sufficient without internal conviction.
**Verses:** Rev 7:3, Rev 9:4, Rev 13:16, Rev 14:1, Rev 14:9, Rev 17:5, Rev 20:4, Rev 22:4, Deut 6:6-8, Deut 11:18
**Strong's:** G3359, H226, H2903

eikon-beast-dan3-parallel. **Eikon (G1504) — Image of the Beast and Daniel 3 Parallel:**
The eikon investigation traces a degradation chain. In Genesis 1:26 LXX, humanity is created according to God's eikon — the authentic divine image. In Colossians 1:15, Christ IS the eikon of the invisible God — the image in its fullest expression. In Revelation 13:14, the earth beast commands humanity to make an eikon FOR the beast — a manufactured likeness of a counterfeit system. The chain runs: God's image in humanity → Christ as God's image → beast's image as parody of both. The dative-to-genitive shift between verses 14 and 15 reveals a transformation: the image begins as something made FOR the beast (dative of dedication, an instrument) but becomes something OF the beast (genitive of possession, an extension of the beast's own authority). The Daniel 3 parallel confirms the pattern: state-mandated image, compulsory worship, death penalty, faithful remnant refusal, divine vindication. But Revelation escalates beyond Daniel 3 by giving the image pneuma — it speaks and enforces. Daniel's image was silent; the beast's image has institutional voice.
**Verses:** Rev 13:14, Rev 13:15, Rev 14:9, Rev 14:11, Rev 15:2, Rev 16:2, Rev 19:20, Rev 20:4, Gen 1:26-27, Col 1:15, Dan 3:1-6, Dan 3:14-18
**Strong's:** G1504, G1497, G4151, G2936

proskuneo-worship-distribution. **Proskuneo (G4352) — Worship Vocabulary and the Allegiance Conflict:**
The proskuneo investigation reveals that Revelation is fundamentally a worship book. Proskuneo occurs 22 times in Revelation (highest NT density per chapter). The distribution divides cleanly: true worship flows toward God and the Lamb in throne-room scenes (4:10, 5:14, 7:11, 11:16), while false worship flows toward the dragon and beast in the parody scenes (13:4, 13:8, 13:12, 13:15). The corrective scenes (19:10, 22:8-9) where John is told NOT to worship angels reinforce that proskuneo belongs to God alone. The mark-and-image system exists to redirect proskuneo away from God toward the beast. This is why Rev 14:9 treats worship and mark-receiving as one compound condition — the mark is the mechanism that enforces false worship. The first angel's counter-command (14:7) uses proskuneo + poieo (the one who made heaven and earth and sea) rather than ktizo, John's usual creation verb. This verb switch aligns with Exod 20:11 LXX (poieo), linking the first angel's worship command directly to the fourth commandment.
**Verses:** Rev 4:10, Rev 5:14, Rev 7:11, Rev 11:16, Rev 13:4, Rev 13:8, Rev 13:12, Rev 13:15, Rev 14:7, Rev 14:9, Rev 14:11, Rev 15:4, Rev 16:2, Rev 19:4, Rev 19:10, Rev 19:20, Rev 20:4, Rev 22:8-9
**Strong's:** G4352, G4160, G2936

deut-hand-forehead-covenant. **Deut 6:6-8 / 11:18 / Exod 13:9,16 — Hand/Forehead as Covenant Loyalty Markers:**
The four OT hand/forehead texts (Exod 13:9, 13:16, Deut 6:8, 11:18) establish the theological grammar that Revelation assumes. In each case, God's covenant instructions become a visible sign on the hand (actions/works) and between the eyes/on the forehead (mind/convictions). The critical detail is the Deut 6:6-8 sequence: the words must be IN THE HEART first (v.6), diligently taught (v.7), and THEN bound as external signs (v.8). Heart comes before hand. Internal conviction before external display. This sequence explains Revelation's asymmetry: God's seal appears only on the forehead because genuine divine allegiance requires the engagement of mind and will. The beast's mark appears on forehead OR hand because the counterfeit system is satisfied with either genuine belief or mere outward conformity. The Hebrew word owth (H226) used in these passages is the same word used four times for the Sabbath sign (Exod 31:13, 31:17, Ezek 20:12, 20:20) — a vocabulary link that connects the hand/forehead loyalty system to the Sabbath.
**Verses:** Deut 6:6-8, Deut 11:18, Exod 13:9, Exod 13:16, Rev 13:16, Rev 7:3
**Strong's:** H226, H2146, H2903, H3027

sabbath-sign-seal. **Exod 31:13-17 — Sabbath as Sign/Seal and the Owth Vocabulary:**
The Sabbath-seal composite argument rests on eight independent lines of evidence converging on a single conclusion. First, the Sabbath alone among the Ten Commandments receives the owth (sign) designation — and receives it four times (Exod 31:13, 31:17, Ezek 20:12, 20:20). No other commandment is called God's sign even once. Second, Romans 4:11 establishes the precedent that a sign (semeion) can simultaneously function as a seal (sphragis): Paul calls circumcision both. Third, the fourth commandment uniquely contains the three elements of an ancient Near Eastern seal: the ruler's name (the LORD thy God), title (Creator, who made), and domain (heaven, earth, sea). Fourth, Rev 14:7's worship command uses poieo (not ktizo) to match Exod 20:11 LXX, linking the first angel directly to the fourth commandment. At the mode level, the seal is voluntarily received through conviction; the mark is imposed through economic coercion. At the economic level, the mark enables buying and selling (Rev 13:17); the seal has no economic component — it operates on trust in God. At the eschatological level, the mark promises economic survival but delivers divine wrath (Rev 16:2); the seal appears to mark for persecution but delivers eternal protection (Rev 7:3, 9:4).
