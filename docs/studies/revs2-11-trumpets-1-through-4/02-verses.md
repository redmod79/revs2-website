# Verses

## Question
What is the structural pattern of trumpets 1-4 — the '1/3 destruction' pattern? Document what is struck, what fraction is affected, and what the result is for each trumpet. How do trumpets 1-4 parallel the Exodus plagues? Use Exodus 7-12 as the source text and document the element-by-element mapping. What structural distinction separates the first four trumpets from the three woes (trumpets 5-7)?

### Incense prelude — sanctuary context for the trumpet sequence

> And I saw the seven angels which stood before God; and to them were given seven trumpets.
And another angel came and stood at the altar, having a golden censer; and there was given unto him much incense, that he should offer [it] with the prayers of all saints upon the golden altar which was before the throne.
And the smoke of the incense, [which came] with the prayers of the saints, ascended up before God out of the angel's hand.
And the angel took the censer, and filled it with fire of the altar, and cast [it] into the earth: and there were voices, and thunderings, and lightnings, and an earthquake.

**Context:** After the seventh seal opening (8:1), John sees the incense prelude establishing the sanctuary context. The same censer (libanotos G3031) carries prayers upward (8:3-4) then fire downward (8:5), pivoting from intercession to judgment. The Sinai-theophany formula at 8:5 (voices, thunderings, lightnings, earthquake — 4 elements) signals that the trumpet judgments emanate from the sanctuary.

**Notes:** This passage was analyzed in revs2-10. The DOA censer parallel (AN022), Sinai-theophany escalation (TM057), and censer reversal are established findings. The 4-element theophany formula here will escalate to 5 elements at 11:19 (adding great hail).

### Formulaic introduction — seven angels prepare to sound

> And the seven angels which had the seven trumpets prepared themselves to sound.

**Context:** Transitional verse between the incense prelude and the first trumpet. The seven angels with seven trumpets (introduced at 8:2) now prepare to sound, establishing the sevenfold sequence.

**Notes:** The verb 'prepared themselves' (hetoimazo, G2090) marks the deliberate, structured nature of the sequence — these are not random calamities but ordered judgments.

### Plague 7 — hail and fire; source for first trumpet parallel

> And the LORD said unto Moses, Stretch forth thine hand toward heaven, that there may be hail in all the land of Egypt, upon man, and upon beast, and upon every herb of the field, throughout the land of Egypt.
And Moses stretched forth his rod toward heaven: and the LORD sent thunder and hail, and the fire ran along upon the ground; and the LORD rained hail upon the land of Egypt.
So there was hail, and fire mingled with the hail, very grievous, such as there was none like it in all the land of Egypt since it became a nation.
And the hail smote throughout all the land of Egypt all that [was] in the field, both man and beast; and the hail smote every herb of the field, and brake every tree of the field.
Only in the land of Goshen, where the children of Israel [were], was there no hail.

**Context:** The seventh plague of Egypt — hail and fire. The verbal parallels with Rev 8:7 include: barad (H1259, hail) = chalaza (G5464), esh (H784, fire) = pyr (G4442), 'fire mingled with the hail' (Exo 9:24) = 'fire mingled with blood' (Rev 8:7), vegetation destruction (Exo 9:25 'smote every herb of the field, brake every tree') = Rev 8:7 '1/3 trees burnt up, all green grass burnt up.' The Goshen exemption (9:26) establishes the divine discrimination pattern.

**Notes:** The LXX of Exo 9:24 uses chalaza and pyr together — the identical Greek words appearing in Rev 8:7. The Hithpael participle mitlaqqachat ('catching itself,' 'flashing') in 9:24 describes fire intermingled with hail — a unique phenomenon. Exo 9:31-32 specifies selective destruction: flax and barley smitten but wheat and rye not — partial, not total.

> Thus saith the LORD, In this thou shalt know that I [am] the LORD: behold, I will smite with the rod that [is] in mine hand upon the waters which [are] in the river, and they shall be turned to blood.

And Moses and Aaron did so, as the LORD commanded; and he lifted up the rod, and smote the waters that [were] in the river, in the sight of Pharaoh, and in the sight of his servants; and all the waters that [were] in the river were turned to blood.

**Context:** The first plague of Egypt — water to blood. Shared elements with the second trumpet (Rev 8:8-9): dam (H1818, blood) = haima (G129), fish/creatures died (Exo 7:21 = Rev 8:9), water made unusable. **Notes:** The blood connection between Plague 1 and Trumpet 2 is thematic rather than verbal — the vocabulary chains differ (dam/haima for the substance, but different verbs and contexts). Rev 8:7 also contains blood (haima), creating a composite allusion where blood from Plague 1 appears in Trumpet 1.

### Plague 9 — three days of darkness; source for fourth trumpet parallel

> And the LORD said unto Moses, Stretch out thine hand toward heaven, that there may be darkness over the land of Egypt, even darkness [which] may be felt.
And Moses stretched forth his hand toward heaven; and there was a thick darkness in all the land of Egypt three days:
They saw not one another, neither rose any from his place for three days: but all the children of Israel had light in their dwellings.

**Context:** The ninth plague of Egypt — total darkness. The Hebrew choshek (H2822) = Greek skotos (G4655). The Exodus darkness is total ('thick darkness,' choshek-aphelah double noun construction), while Revelation's is partial (1/3 darkened). The Goshen exemption persists: 'all the children of Israel had light in their dwellings.' The phrase 'darkness which may be felt' (veyamesh choshek — Hiphil of mashash H4959, to grope) emphasizes the tangible quality of this darkness.

**Notes:** The prophetic intermediary tradition connects here: Joel 2:2 ('a day of darkness and of gloominess'), Isa 13:10, Ezek 32:7-8 use similar vocabulary to describe eschatological darkness, mediating between the Exodus event and Revelation's trumpet.

### Tripartite division of hair — fire, sword, scattering; source for 1/3 fraction

> And thou, son of man, take thee a sharp knife, take thee a barber's rasor, and cause [it] to pass upon thine head and upon thy beard: then take thee balances to weigh, and divide the [hair].
Thou shalt burn with fire a third part in the midst of the city, when the days of the siege are fulfilled: and thou shalt take a third part, [and] smite about it with a knife: and a third part thou shalt scatter in the wind; and I will draw out a sword after them.
Thou shalt also take thereof a few in number, and bind them in thy skirts.
Then take of them again, and cast them into the midst of the fire, and burn them in the fire; [for] thereof shall a fire come forth into all the house of Israel.

**Context:** Ezekiel's prophetic sign-act. The Hebrew shliyshith (noun form of the tripartite division) appears three times in 5:2 — each 'third part' assigned a different mode of destruction: fire, sword, wind/scattering. This is the primary OT source for the 1/3 fraction concept in Revelation's trumpets (Beale). Note: Ezekiel's three thirds = comprehensive destruction (all are destroyed, each by a different mode).

**Notes:** Critical distinction: in Ezekiel, three thirds = total destruction (each third is destroyed differently). In Revelation, one third is destroyed while two thirds remain — a significant transformation of the source concept. The small remnant saved 'in thy skirts' (5:3) is then also cast into fire (5:4), leaving no survivors in Ezekiel's version.

### Explicit 'a third part shall die with pestilence' — direct OT background for to triton pattern

> A third part of thee shall die with the pestilence, and with famine shall they be consumed in the midst of thee: and a third part shall fall by the sword round about thee; and I will scatter a third part into all the winds, and I will draw out a sword after them.

**Context:** The interpretation of the sign-act: shliyshith appears three times again, now applied to Jerusalem's population. The three-mode destruction pattern (pestilence/famine, sword, scattering) parallels the three-mode division in 5:2. The Hebrew construction shliyshitheykh (your third parts, with 2fs suffix) personalizes the fraction. The LXX translates shliyshith with to triton — the same construction used in Rev 8:7-12.

**Notes:** The one-third-trumpets study confirmed that Revelation synthesizes both Ezekiel (comprehensive destruction) and Zechariah (surviving remnant) traditions. H7992 shliyshiy has 109 OT occurrences, but only ~13% are partitive ('third part'); the majority are ordinal.

### Inverted 1/3 pattern — 2/3 cut off, surviving 1/3 refined

> And it shall come to pass, [that] in all the land, saith the LORD, two parts therein shall be cut off [and] die; but the third shall be left therein.
And I will bring the third part through the fire, and will refine them as silver is refined, and will try them as gold is tried: they shall call on my name, and I will hear them: I will say, It [is] my people: and they shall say, The LORD [is] my God.

**Context:** Zechariah inverts the Ezekiel pattern: two-thirds are destroyed, the surviving one-third is refined through fire and restored to covenant faithfulness. The verbs tsaraph (H6884, refine) and bachan (H974, try/test) are consistently remedial, not punitive. This creates a hopeful counterpart to Ezekiel's total destruction.

**Notes:** Revelation synthesizes both: destroys 1/3 (echoing Ezekiel's judgment vocabulary) while leaving 2/3 as potential remnant (echoing Zechariah's hope). The tragedy: the 2/3 survivors 'repented not' (Rev 9:20-21), failing to fulfill the Zechariah 13 refinement pattern.

### Wormwood (laanah) as judgment for forsaking God's law — source for third trumpet

> And the LORD saith, Because they have forsaken my law which I set before them, and have not obeyed my voice, neither walked therein;
But have walked after the imagination of their own heart, and after Baalim, which their fathers taught them:
Therefore thus saith the LORD of hosts, the God of Israel; Behold, I will feed them, [even] this people, with wormwood, and give them water of gall to drink.

**Context:** Jeremiah's first wormwood passage. The cause is explicit: forsaking God's law and following Baalim. The punishment: being fed with laanah (H3939, wormwood) and given 'water of gall' (me-rosh) to drink. This connects wormwood specifically to apostasy — the OT source for the third trumpet's wormwood imagery.

**Notes:** The LXX translates laanah here with different Greek words than apsinthos — it uses chole (G5521, gall) or pikria (G4088, bitterness). Revelation's use of apsinthos (G894) is its own lexical choice, preserving the wormwood concept while using a word attested in Greek pharmacology.

### Wormwood fed to false prophets — second OT source for wormwood imagery

> And I have seen folly in the prophets of Samaria; they prophesied in Baal, and caused my people Israel to err.
I have seen also in the prophets of Jerusalem an horrible thing: they commit adultery, and walk in lies: they strengthen also the hands of evildoers, that none doth return from his wickedness: they are all of them unto me as Sodom, and the inhabitants thereof as Gomorrah.
Therefore thus saith the LORD of hosts concerning the prophets; Behold, I will feed them with wormwood, and make them drink the water of gall: for from the prophets of Jerusalem is profaneness gone forth into all the land.

**Context:** Jeremiah's second wormwood passage — specifically targeting false prophets. The prophets of Jerusalem commit adultery, walk in lies, and strengthen evildoers. The identical judgment formula: 'I will feed them with wormwood, and make them drink the water of gall.' The comparison to Sodom and Gomorrah intensifies the indictment.

**Notes:** Both Jeremiah passages link wormwood to a specific cause: apostasy and false teaching. This is the textual basis for Bohr's reading of the third trumpet as symbolizing corruption of pure teaching — the OT source text itself makes this connection.

### Day of the LORD — sun darkened, moon no light, stars no light; parallel to fourth trumpet

> For the stars of heaven and the constellations thereof shall not give their light: the sun shall be darkened in his going forth, and the moon shall not cause her light to shine.

**Context:** Isaiah's day-of-the-LORD oracle against Babylon. The celestial triad (kokab H3556/stars, shemesh H8121/sun, yareach H3394/moon) matches Revelation's trumpet 4 triad (aster G792, helios G2246, selene G4582). The Hebrew verb chashak (H2822 as verb root, Qal perfect 3ms) describes the sun being 'darkened' — the same concept as skotizo (G4654) in Rev 8:12.

**Notes:** Cross-testament parallels tool confirmed Isa 13:10 as the top OT match for Rev 8:12 (hybrid score 0.416, matching on moon, star, sun). The Joel passages (2:10, 2:31, 3:15) share the same celestial-darkening vocabulary.

### Trumpet blown in Zion — day of darkness and gloominess; trumpet-warning-darkness triad

> Blow ye the trumpet in Zion, and sound an alarm in my holy mountain: let all the inhabitants of the land tremble: for the day of the LORD cometh, for [it is] nigh at hand;
A day of darkness and of gloominess, a day of clouds and of thick darkness, as the morning spread upon the mountains: a great people and a strong; there hath not been ever the like, neither shall be any more after it, [even] to the years of many generations.

**Context:** Joel pairs the trumpet warning with the day-of-the-LORD darkness — a trumpet-darkness triad that Revelation separates into distinct trumpets (trumpets 1-4 for judgments, the overall trumpet concept as warning). The combination of showphar (trumpet) + choshek (darkness) in a single oracle provides the conceptual template for Revelation's trumpet-judgment-darkness sequence.

**Notes:** Wadholm (2015) identified Joel 2:1 as one of nine Joel-Revelation verbal parallels. The trumpet-in-Zion motif connects to the warning function of the OT trumpet (Ezk 33:3-6).

### Blood, fire, pillars of smoke; sun to darkness, moon to blood — verbal parallels with Rev 8:7-12

> And I will shew wonders in the heavens and in the earth, blood, and fire, and pillars of smoke.
The sun shall be turned into darkness, and the moon into blood, before the great and the terrible day of the LORD come.

**Context:** Joel combines blood (dam), fire (esh), and smoke (ashan) with celestial darkening (sun to darkness, moon to blood). This single passage contains verbal parallels to multiple trumpets: blood + fire (trumpet 1, Rev 8:7), sun darkened (trumpet 4, Rev 8:12), moon to blood (combining darkness and blood motifs). Acts 2:19-20 quotes this passage at Pentecost.

**Notes:** The prophetic intermediary tradition (SP101): Revelation may access plague imagery through Joel rather than directly from Exodus. Joel 2:30-31 creates a composite that Revelation distributes across multiple trumpets.
