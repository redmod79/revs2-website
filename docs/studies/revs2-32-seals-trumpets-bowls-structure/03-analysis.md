# Verse-by-Verse Analysis

## Question
SHORT STUDY: What does IC077 mean for readers? How do the three judgment sequences (seals, trumpets, bowls) relate?

---

## FA01. IC077 Hybrid Model Explained for Non-Specialists

### Rev 4:5 — Theophany Baseline
The throne room scene establishes the first theophany station with three elements: lightning, thunder, and voices proceeding from the throne. This is the baseline from which all subsequent theophany formulas grow. The Greek word astrape (G796) appears here for the first time in the NT. Every subsequent station at the juncture between judgment sequences will repeat and expand this formula, making it a structural signpost readers can track.

### Rev 8:1-2 — Seventh Seal Opens Into Trumpets
The seventh seal produces no judgment of its own. There is only silence — about half an hour — and then the seven trumpet angels appear. The text contains no closing formula separating the seal sequence from the trumpet sequence (TM058). This is the first telescoping transition: the 7th seal is not a terminal event but a container that opens into the next series. The silence draws on the OT pre-judgment tradition of Habakkuk 2:20 ("let all the earth keep silence before him") and Zephaniah 1:7 ("Hold thy peace at the presence of the Lord GOD"). For readers, this means the seals do not end — they open outward into the trumpets.

### Rev 6:17 — Wrath Arrival at Seal Endpoint
"For the great day of his wrath is come" — elthen he orge. This wrath-arrival formula appears identically at Rev 11:18 at the trumpet endpoint. Both sequences reach the same horizon: the day of wrath. This is the overlapping eschatological orientation component of IC077. For readers, it means these are not descriptions of two entirely different timelines; both sequences orient toward the same end.

### Rev 11:18 — Wrath Arrival at Trumpet Endpoint
"Thy wrath is come" — elthen he orge sou. The wording is practically identical to 6:17 (VP174). Both seal and trumpet endpoints use the same wrath-arrival language. This does not mean the events described are identical (the fraction escalation disproves that), but it does mean both sequences point to the same eschatological destination.

### Rev 6:8 — The 1/4 Fraction
Authority was given (edothe, divine passive of didomi G1325) over one-fourth of the earth. The Greek tetarton (G5067) appears only here in the seal sequence. This is the first point of the fraction escalation: 1/4 in seals, 1/3 in trumpets, total in bowls. The fractions cannot describe the same events because the same event cannot simultaneously destroy one-fourth, one-third, and everything.

### Rev 8:7-12 — The 1/3 Fraction Saturating the Trumpets
The fraction to triton (one-third) saturates the first four trumpets. The third part of trees burned up (8:7). The third part of the sea became blood (8:8). The third part of rivers became wormwood (8:10). The third part of the sun was struck (8:12). Rev 8:12 alone contains five "third part" references in a single verse (TM075). This saturation is deliberate — a structural signature marking the trumpet sequence.

### Rev 16:3 — Total Scope in the Bowls
"Every living soul died in the sea." No fraction. No qualifier. The language shifts to totalizing expressions. This pattern continues through 16:20 ("every island fled away"). The bowl sequence operates without fractional limitation.

### Rev 11:15-19 — Seventh Trumpet Opens Into Bowls
The seventh trumpet sounds, and heaven proclaims the kingdoms of this world as having become the kingdoms of the Lord (egeneto, proleptic aorist of ginomai G1096 — viewing the transfer as a completed whole from heaven's perspective). The naos opens and the ark becomes visible (11:19). The same naos-opening vocabulary (anoigo + naos, VP082) reappears at 15:5 when the bowl prelude begins. The same completion verb etelesthe (G5055) appears at both 10:7 ("mystery finished") and 15:1 ("wrath filled up"). These vocabulary bridges are the structural joints of the second telescoping transition.

## FA02. Functional Gradient: Seals vs. Trumpets vs. Bowls

### Rev 6:1-8 — Gospel First, Then Permitted Consequences
The OT source comparison resolves the first horseman question. Ezekiel 14:21 lists four covenant-curse judgments: sword, famine, noisome beast, pestilence. The fourth horseman at Rev 6:8 recaps all four ("sword, hunger, death, beasts of the earth"). Horsemen 2-4 map to Ezekiel's curses. But the first horseman has no Ezekiel counterpart — there is no "conquering" judgment in the four-curse list. Revelation prepends the gospel to Ezekiel's judgment framework. The nikao-stephanos-leukos chain confirms this: conquering (nikao G3528, positive throughout Revelation), victor's crown (stephanos G4735, used for believers and elders), white (leukos G3022, exclusively divine/pure in Revelation). The edothe ("crown was given") fits Christ receiving authority from the Father (Matt 28:18; Rev 5:7). The Olivet Discourse confirms the same elements: the gospel was already going forth in Jesus' own day (Matt 24:14), and wars, famines, pestilence appear alongside it — the seal sequence reflects the historical order in which these realities unfold.

For horsemen 2-4, the divine passive edothe (V-API-3S of didomi G1325) grants bounded authority within the Ezekiel covenant-curse framework. Power was given to take peace (6:4). A great sword was given (6:4). Authority was given over a fourth of the earth (6:8). These agents do not act by their own power. Their authority is granted and bounded by God.

### Rev 6:9-11 — The Altar Cry
The fifth seal reveals souls under the altar crying "How long, O Lord, holy and true, dost thou not judge and avenge our blood?" (heos pote). This is the theodicy question. The Greek ekdikeis (present tense of ekdikeo G1556) expresses an ongoing, unresolved demand. The altar vindication arc (SP037) begins here and does not resolve until Rev 19:2, threading across all three judgment sequences.

### Rev 8:7 — First Trumpet: Earth
Hail and fire mingled with blood, cast upon the earth. The third part of trees burned up. Domain 1 targeted at 1/3 scope. The Exodus plague typology is active (hail + fire = Exod 9:23-24). The trumpet operates as a warning — partial destruction with room for response.

### Rev 8:8-9 — Second Trumpet: Sea
A great mountain burning with fire cast into the sea. The third part of the sea became blood. The third part of sea creatures died. The third part of ships destroyed. Three "third part" references reinforce the fraction pattern. The bowl counterpart at 16:3 removes the fraction entirely: "every living soul died in the sea."

### Rev 8:10-11 — Third Trumpet: Rivers/Springs
A great star named Wormwood falls on the third part of rivers and fountains. Waters become bitter. Many men die. The bowl counterpart at 16:4-7 converts the same rivers to blood and adds the altar vindication speech.

### Rev 8:12 — Fourth Trumpet: Sun Darkened
The third part of the sun, moon, and stars is struck — darkened. Five "third part" references in a single verse climax the fraction saturation. The critical divergence from the bowl counterpart: the 4th trumpet darkens one-third of the sun, while the 4th bowl causes the sun to scorch with great heat (Rev 16:8-9). These are physically opposite effects on the same target. Darkness and scorching cannot describe the same event (SP060).

### Rev 9:1-2 — Fifth Trumpet: Abyss Darkness
A star falls from heaven and receives the key to the bottomless pit — edothe (divine passive again). Smoke from the abyss darkens the sun and air. The bowl counterpart at 16:10-11 targets the beast's throne with darkness, but from a different source.

### Rev 9:4 — Sealed Protection
The locusts are commanded not to hurt grass, green things, or trees — only those men who do not have the seal of God. This links to the sealing at 7:3 (achri temporal clause) and establishes sequential dependency: sealing must precede trumpet harm (SP186).

### Rev 9:13 — Sixth Trumpet: Voice From the Altar
A voice issues from the four horns of the golden altar before God. Station 3 of the altar vindication arc: the altar has moved from passive reception (6:9) through mediation (8:3) to active command. The altar now directs judgment.

### Rev 9:15 — Third Part of Men Killed
The four angels prepared for this hour kill the third part of men. The surviving two-thirds become the target audience for the warning.

### Rev 9:20-21 — Failed Warning
The Greek ou metanoesan (aorist of metanoeo G3340) appears twice — negated at both 9:20 (oude metanoesan) and 9:21 (ou metanoesan). The surviving two-thirds explicitly refuse repentance. They refused to abandon the works of their hands — idols of gold, silver, brass, stone, and wood that cannot see, hear, or walk (echoing Deut 4:28). Nor did they repent of murders, sorceries, fornication, or thefts. The trumpet sequence's purpose as warning is revealed precisely by its failure. This echoes the Amos 4:6-12 pattern: escalating judgments with repeated "yet have ye not returned unto me."

### Rev 15:1 — Bowl Introduction
Seven angels with the seven last plagues — eschatas (G2078), the only sequence explicitly designated as "last." In them the wrath of God is "filled up" (etelesthe, G5055 — the same completion verb as 10:7). The adjective eschatos marks the bowls as terminally final.

### Rev 16:2 — First Bowl: Earth (Sores)
Poured upon the earth — sores on those with the mark of the beast. No fraction. The trumpet counterpart hit vegetation (8:7); the bowl hits persons. Same domain, different target within the domain.

### Rev 16:3 — Second Bowl: Sea (Total Death)
"Every living soul died in the sea." Compare with 8:8-9 where one-third died. The escalation from 1/3 to total on the same domain is mathematically incompatible with identical events.

### Rev 16:4-7 — Third Bowl: Rivers to Blood + Altar Speaks
Rivers and fountains become blood. The angel of the waters affirms God's righteousness. Then the altar itself speaks: "Even so, Lord God Almighty, true and righteous are thy judgments" (16:7). The Greek shows a grammatical anomaly: the masculine participle legontos (present active participle, genitive singular masculine) modifies the neuter noun thysiasteriou (genitive singular neuter). This grammatical mismatch is deliberate personification — the altar is treated as a speaking person. Station 5 of the altar vindication arc.

### Rev 16:8-9 — Fourth Bowl: Sun Scorches
The sun is given power to scorch men with fire. Blasphemy is added to the refusal: "they blasphemed the name of God... and repented not." The impenitence escalation (SP056) advances beyond the trumpet's passive refusal to active blasphemy combined with refusal.

### Rev 16:10-11 — Fifth Bowl: Beast's Throne Darkened
The beast's kingdom is filled with darkness. They "blasphemed the God of heaven because of their pains and their sores, and repented not of their deeds." This is the last occurrence of metanoeo in Revelation. After 16:11, the word for repentance disappears entirely from the text.

### Rev 16:12 — Sixth Bowl: Euphrates Dried
The Euphrates is dried to prepare the way for the kings of the east. The trumpet counterpart released four angels at the Euphrates (9:14). Release versus drying — same geography, different action.

### Rev 16:17-21 — Seventh Bowl: Gegonen and Superlative Theophany
Theophany Station 4 with all five elements plus superlative intensifiers. The earthquake is unprecedented — "such as was not since men were upon the earth" (seismos oios ou egeneto). Hail weighs a talent (approximately 75 pounds). The voice from the naos and from the throne declares gegonen (perfect active indicative of ginomai G1096) — "It is done," with permanent resultant state. At 16:21, metanoeo is absent. Only blasphemy remains: "men blasphemed God because of the plague of the hail." Character fixation is complete.

## FA03. Fraction Escalation: 1/4 to 1/3 to Total

### Rev 6:8 — 1/4 (Seals)
The fraction tetarton (G5067) scopes only the fourth horseman's domain. It appears once in the seal sequence.

### Rev 8:7, 8:8-9, 8:10-11, 8:12 — 1/3 (Trumpets)
The fraction to triton saturates the first four trumpets with repeated occurrences, climaxing at 8:12 with five references in one verse. The scope doubles from 1/4 to 1/3.

### Rev 9:15 — 1/3 Extended to Persons
The third part of men killed by the sixth trumpet. The fraction now applies to human casualties.

### Rev 16:3, 16:20 — Total (Bowls)
"Every living soul died" (16:3). "Every island fled away, and the mountains were not found" (16:20). Zero fraction language in Revelation 16 (TM107). The fraction has been eliminated.

### Ezek 5:1-12 — OT Source
Ezekiel's tripartite destruction destroys all three thirds — total. Revelation transforms this by destroying only one-third, preserving a surviving majority with opportunity to respond. The tragedy at Rev 9:20-21 is that the surviving two-thirds refuse the repentance that Zechariah 13:8-9's oracle promised for the surviving remnant.

### Zech 13:8-9 — Surviving Remnant
Zechariah's pattern: two-thirds cut off, one-third refined through fire and called "my people." Revelation inverts: one-third destroyed, two-thirds survive — but refuse refinement.

## FA04. Theophany Formula Escalation

### Rev 4:5 — Station 1: 3 Elements
Lightning + thunder + voices from the throne. Baseline. Throne room introduction.

### Rev 8:5 — Station 2: 4 Elements (+ Earthquake)
Thunder + voices + lightning + earthquake. Earthquake (seismos G4578) added as the fourth element. This station marks the seal-to-trumpet transition. The censer reversal happens here: the same libanotos (G3031) that carried incense upward (8:3) now carries fire downward (8:5).

### Rev 11:19 — Station 3: 5 Elements (+ Great Hail)
Lightning + voices + thunder + earthquake + great hail. Hail (chalaza G5464) added as the fifth element. The naos opens and the ark of the testament is visible. Sanctuary compartmental transition to the Most Holy Place.

### Rev 16:18-21 — Station 4: 5+ Elements (Superlative)
All five elements return with unprecedented intensifiers. The earthquake is the greatest "since men were upon the earth." The hail weighs a talent. The element count never decreases — it can only grow. Each station sits at a major structural juncture.

## FA05. Telescoping Transitions

### Rev 8:1-2, 8:5-6 — Seal to Trumpet
The 7th seal opens with silence (8:1). No independent judgment. No closing formula. The trumpet angels appear within this opening (8:2). The censer scene (8:3-5) functions as the transition mechanism, bridging intercession to judgment. Then the trumpets begin to sound (8:6).

### Rev 10:7, 11:14-15, 11:19, 15:1, 15:5 — Trumpet to Bowl
Multiple vocabulary bridges connect the 7th trumpet to the bowl sequence. The etelesthe verbal bridge (teleo G5055) at both 10:7 ("mystery finished") and 15:1 ("wrath filled up"). The shared naos-opening formula (anoigo G455 + naos G3485) at both 11:19 and 15:5. The third woe is never formally declared past (TM071) — the first and second woes are closed at 9:12 and 11:14, but the third remains open. The plural "days" of the seventh angel's voice (10:7) suggests duration rather than a single moment (TM073).

### Rev 11:15, 16:17, 21:6 — The Ginomai Arc
The three-point ginomai arc constitutes Revelation's grammatical backbone. At 11:15, egeneto (aorist middle indicative, 3rd person singular) announces the kingdom as accomplished — a completed whole from heaven's vantage point. At 16:17, gegonen (perfect active indicative, 3rd person singular) declares judgment completed with permanent resultant state. At 21:6, gegonan (perfect active indicative, 3rd person plural) extends that permanence to the renewal of all things. The aspectual shift from aorist (viewed as a whole) through perfect singular (completed with lasting result) to perfect plural (all things completed) is a deliberate escalation.

## FA06. DOA Hinge at Rev 15:5-8

### Lev 16:2-17 — OT Source
The Day of Atonement ritual provides the typological framework. The high priest enters the Most Holy Place with incense, producing a cloud that covers the mercy seat. "No man shall be in the tabernacle of the congregation when he goeth in to make an atonement in the holy place, until he come out" (Lev 16:17). The Hebrew ad tsetho ("until he come out") establishes a temporal boundary that no other OT glory-cloud passage contains.

### Lev 16:12-13 — Incense Cloud
The high priest takes a censer (machtah) full of burning coals from the altar and handfuls of fine incense, bringing them within the veil. The incense cloud covers the mercy seat. This parallels the smoke filling the naos at Rev 15:8.

### Rev 15:5-8 — Five DOA Indicators
Five independent markers converge. (1) The triple-genitive sanctuary identification: "the temple of the tabernacle of the testimony" (ho naos tes skenes tou martyriou) — an NT hapax (TM100) mirroring the LXX rendering of ohel moed. (2) Priestly DOA vestments: pure and white linen with golden girdles, matching Lev 16:4. (3) Smoke fills the naos from God's glory, paralleling Lev 16:12-13. (4) "No man was able to enter into the temple" — matching Lev 16:17's exclusion. (5) "Until the seven plagues of the seven angels were fulfilled" (achri telesthosin) — matching Lev 16:17's "until he come out." The distinguishing "until" clause is shared only with Lev 16:17, not with Exod 40:34-35 or 1 Kings 8:10-11.

### Rev 8:3-5 — Intercession Active (Before Hinge)
Before the DOA hinge, intercession is active. The angel at the golden altar offers incense with the prayers of all saints. The smoke of the incense ascends before God with the prayers. The censer (libanotos G3031) mediates between earth and heaven.

### Rev 5:8 — Vessel Transformation Start
Golden bowls (phialas chrysas) hold incense equated with the prayers of the saints. The construction phialas chrysas gemousas thymiamaton will be precisely mirrored at 15:7 with thymos replacing thymiama — golden bowls full of wrath replacing golden bowls full of incense-prayers.

### 1 Kings 8:10-11 — Glory Cloud at Dedication (Contrast)
The glory cloud at Solomon's temple dedication is a positive inauguration context. Priests cannot stand to minister. But this lacks the "until" temporal clause. Rev 15:8 matches the DOA judgment context of Lev 16, not the inauguration context of 1 Kings 8.

### Exod 40:34-35 — Tabernacle Dedication (Contrast)
Moses cannot enter the tent because the cloud abode upon it and glory filled the tabernacle. Again, no "until" clause. The distinction is important: the "until" at Rev 15:8 aligns with Lev 16:17's DOA exclusion, not Exod 40's inauguration.

### Isa 6:4 — Judgment Vision Smoke
Isaiah's temple fills with smoke during a judgment vision — the closest OT parallel to Rev 15:8 in terms of context (judgment, not inauguration).

## FA07. Seven-Domain Trumpet-Bowl Correspondence

### Rev 8:7 / Rev 16:2 — Domain 1: Earth
Trumpet: 1/3 vegetation burned. Bowl: sores on persons with the mark of the beast. Same domain, different target within the domain. The divergence between vegetation and human bodies marks these as distinct events on the same ground.

### Rev 8:8-9 / Rev 16:3 — Domain 2: Sea
Trumpet: 1/3 sea to blood, 1/3 creatures died. Bowl: "every living soul died in the sea." The fraction escalation from 1/3 to total on the same domain.

### Rev 8:10-11 / Rev 16:4-7 — Domain 3: Rivers/Springs
Trumpet: wormwood poisoning the third part of rivers. Bowl: rivers to blood plus the altar vindication speech at 16:7. Different effects (bitterness vs. blood) on the same geographic target.

### Rev 8:12 / Rev 16:8-9 — Domain 4: Sun
Trumpet: 1/3 of the sun darkened. Bowl: sun scorches with heat. These are opposite effects on the same target — the clearest evidence against flat recapitulation. The same event cannot simultaneously darken and scorch.

### Rev 9:1-2 / Rev 16:10-11 — Domain 5: Darkness/Kingdom
Trumpet: abyss smoke darkens. Bowl: beast's throne darkened. Different sources of darkness, different targets.

### Rev 9:13 / Rev 16:12 — Domain 6: Euphrates
Trumpet: four angels released at the Euphrates. Bowl: Euphrates dried to prepare the way. Release versus drying on the same geographic feature.

### Rev 11:15-19 / Rev 16:17-21 — Domain 7: Cosmic Climax
Trumpet: kingdom announced (egeneto). Bowl: judgment completed (gegonen). Both end with theophany formulas, but the 7th bowl adds superlative intensifiers absent from the 7th trumpet.

## FA08. Proleptic Aorists and Already/Not-Yet

### Rev 11:15 — Egeneto (Aorist)
Egeneto he basileia tou kosmou tou kyriou hemon — "the kingdoms of this world are become the kingdoms of our Lord." The aorist middle indicative (V-2ADI-3S) views the kingdom transfer as a completed whole from heaven's perspective. Yet the bowl judgments, the fall of Babylon, and the second coming are all still future in the narrative. This is what the grammar reference identifies as a proleptic aorist: a past-tense verb describing a future event as already accomplished.

### Rev 16:17 — Gegonen (Perfect Singular)
Gegonen — "It is done." Perfect active indicative (V-2RAI-3S). The perfect tense marks the bowl judgments as completed with permanent resultant state. What the aorist at 11:15 announced as a completed whole, the perfect at 16:17 confirms as permanently accomplished.

### Rev 21:6 — Gegonan (Perfect Plural)
Gegonan — "They are done." Perfect active indicative, 3rd person plural (V-2RAI-3P). The completion announced at 11:15 and confirmed at 16:17 now extends to all things made new. The plural subject encompasses the entire renewal.

### Rev 12:10 — Salvation Come
"Now is come salvation" — egeneto he soteria. Another proleptic aorist paralleling 11:15. Heaven proclaims salvation as accomplished while the narrative continues to unfold on earth. This dual-plane structure is a genre convention of apocalyptic literature.

### Rev 12:1-5 — Chronological Regression
After the 7th trumpet at 11:15, the narrative rewinds to the woman, the dragon, and the birth of the male child — events centuries before the trumpet sequence. This chronological regression (TM192) refutes pure strict linearity. The visionary sequence does not mirror the chronological sequence.

## FA09. What IC077 Excludes

### Rev 6:17 / Rev 11:18 — Against Pure Linear (VP174)
The identical wrath-arrival formula (elthen he orge) at two different sequence endpoints means both sequences point to the same eschatological horizon. A purely linear reading where each sequence covers a different time period cannot account for this shared endpoint language.

### Rev 8:12 / Rev 16:8-9 — Against Pure Recapitulation (SP060)
The 4th trumpet darkens one-third of the sun. The 4th bowl causes the sun to scorch with heat. These are opposite effects on the same target. If these were the same event viewed from different perspectives, the same sun cannot simultaneously go dark and burn people.

### Rev 11:19 / Rev 15:8 — Against Coexisting States
The naos is opened at 11:19 (ark visible, access available). The naos is filled with smoke at 15:8 (no one can enter). These states cannot coexist — the sanctuary cannot be simultaneously open for viewing and barred from entry. They describe different moments in the narrative.

### Rev 12:1-5 — Against Pure Linearity (TM192)
The chronological regression to the birth of the male child after the 7th trumpet proves the narrative does not march forward in strict chronological order.

### Rev 6:9 / Rev 16:7 / Rev 19:2 — Against Complete Independence
The altar vindication arc threads the same vocabulary (ekdikeo, thysiasteriou), the same entity (the altar), and the same theological question (vindication of the martyrs' blood) across all three sequences. This continuous progression from demand (6:10 ekdikeis, present tense) to personified confirmation (16:7 legontos) to resolution (19:2 exedikesen, aorist) proves the sequences cannot be read as three unrelated series.

## FA10. Reader Implications

### Rev 6:9-10 / Rev 8:3-5 / Rev 9:13 / Rev 14:18 / Rev 16:7 / Rev 19:2 — The Altar Vindication Arc as Reading Guide
The altar provides a narrative thread connecting all three sequences. It progresses from passive reception of martyrs (6:9), through mediating intercession (8:3-5), to commanding judgment (9:13), to exercising authority over fire (14:18), to personified speech affirming divine justice (16:7), to resolved vindication (19:2). Readers can follow this arc as a single continuous story.

### Rev 4:5 / Rev 8:5 / Rev 11:19 / Rev 16:18 — Theophany Formula as Structural Markers
Whenever lightning, thunder, voices, earthquake, and hail appear together, the reader is at a major structural juncture between judgment sequences. These theophany formulas function like musical refrains that grow with each repetition. The element count (3, 4, 5, 5+) never decreases.

### Rev 5:8 / Rev 15:7 — Vessel Transformation as Theological Logic
The same golden bowls that hold incense-prayers of the saints (5:8) are refilled with wrath (15:7). The construction is grammatically parallel — phialas chrysas gemousas + content. The phonetic near-identity of thymiama (incense) and thymos (wrath) reinforces that judgment is not arbitrary destruction but the judicial consequence of rejected intercession. The bowls are not random weapons; they are the very vessels of prayer, transformed by the refusal of what they offered.

### Rev 7:3 / Rev 9:4 — Sealing Chain as Sequential Evidence
The sealing at 7:3 must precede the trumpet judgments that target the unsealed at 9:4. The achri temporal clause ("until we have sealed") establishes sequential dependency. Readers should understand that the sealing sequence is a genuine prerequisite, not a parallel retelling.

## OT Supporting Verses (Context Notes)

### Ezek 5:1-12
Ezekiel's tripartite destruction provides the OT source of the 1/3 fraction vocabulary but applies it to total destruction (all three thirds). Revelation transforms the pattern by destroying only one-third and preserving a two-thirds majority capable of response.

### Ezek 14:21
Four covenant-curse judgments (sword, famine, beast, pestilence) provide the framework for the four horsemen of the seal sequence. The seal judgments operate within this Ezekielian covenant-curse tradition.

### Zech 13:8-9
The surviving-remnant pattern: two-thirds cut off, one-third refined. Revelation inverts the proportion but preserves the refinement opportunity. The tragedy is that Rev 9:20-21's surviving majority refuses the refinement.

### Amos 4:6-12
Five escalating judgments with the refrain "yet have ye not returned unto me." This is the structural precedent for the trumpet sequence's warning-refusal pattern. God gives escalating warnings; the people refuse each one; final confrontation follows.

### Hab 2:20
"The LORD is in his holy temple: let all the earth keep silence before him." Pre-judgment silence tradition underlying Rev 8:1's half-hour of silence.

### Zeph 1:7
"Hold thy peace at the presence of the Lord GOD." Pre-judgment silence before the Day of the LORD. Combined with Hab 2:20, this tradition informs the transition between the 7th seal and the trumpets.

### Joel 2:1
"Blow ye the trumpet in Zion, and sound an alarm in my holy mountain." The trumpet's primary OT function is warning — sounding the alarm before divine judgment. This function underlies the trumpet sequence's purpose as divine warnings calling for repentance.

### Num 10:1-10
Silver trumpets serve dual functions: assembly/warning and war alarm that ensures "ye shall be remembered before the LORD." The memorial function parallels the trumpet prelude's intercession scene (Rev 8:3-5).

### Exod 40:34-35
Glory cloud at tabernacle dedication. Inauguration context. Lacks the "until" temporal clause that Rev 15:8 shares with Lev 16:17. This distinction supports reading Rev 15:5-8 as DOA rather than inauguration.

### Isa 6:4
Temple filled with smoke during a judgment vision. The closest OT contextual parallel to Rev 15:8 — both are judgment-context scenes where smoke fills the sanctuary.
