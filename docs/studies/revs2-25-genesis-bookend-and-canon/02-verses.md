# Verses

## Question
How does the Genesis-Revelation bookend structure work? Map the seven parallel pairs (tree of life, river, no more curse, face of God, very good/all things new, serpent, marriage) with LXX vocabulary chains. Does Matthew 24 provide a structural key to the seal sequence (Rev 6)? Does Paul's man of lawlessness (2 Thess 2:3-12) parallel Revelation's beast, using Daniel 7:25 as the common source?

### God's Verdict: Very Good

> And God saw every thing that he had made, and, behold, [it was] very good. And the evening and the morning were the sixth day.

**Context:** The concluding verdict of creation week. tov meod (very good) is the authoritative divine assessment before the fall. Bookend pair: Gen 1:31 original goodness -> Rev 21:5 eschatological renewal.

**Notes:** Hebrew: tov meod (very good). LXX: kalon lian. kalon (G2570) replaced by kainos (G2537) in Rev 21:5.

---

### Tree of Life in Eden

> And out of the ground made the LORD God to grow every tree that is pleasant to the sight, and good for food; the tree of life also in the midst of the garden, and the tree of knowledge of good and evil.

**Context:** The etz hachayyim (tree of life) at the center of Eden. LXX: xylon tes zoes (G3586 + G2222) - identical phrase at Rev 22:2 and 22:14. A-VERBAL IDENTITY.

**Notes:** Hebrew: etz hachayyim (H6086 + H2416). LXX: xylon tes zoes (G3586 + G2222). Appears at Rev 2:7, 22:2, 22:14. Classification: A-VERBAL IDENTITY.

### River Flowing from Eden

> And a river went out of Eden to water the garden; and from thence it was parted, and became into four heads.

**Context:** The nahar (river) flowing from Eden. Second bookend pair with Rev 22:1. LXX: potamos (G4215) for nahar (H5104).

**Notes:** Hebrew: nahar (H5104). LXX: potamos (G4215). PMI 32.49. Rev 22:1 adds hydatos zoes via Ezek 47 intermediary.

### The First Marriage: Adam and Eve

> And Adam said, This [is] now bone of my bones, and flesh of my flesh: she shall be called Woman, because she was taken out of Man. Therefore shall a man leave his father and his mother, and shall cleave unto his wife: and they shall be one flesh.

**Context:** Institution of marriage at creation. Seventh bookend pair: Gen 2:23-24 (first marriage) -> Rev 19:7-9 (marriage of the Lamb). Hebrew ishshāh (H802) -> LXX gynē (G1135).

**Notes:** Hebrew: ishshāh (H802), ish (H376). LXX: gynē (G1135). Eph 5:31-32 quotes Gen 2:24 LXX verbatim and calls it a mystery concerning Christ and the church.

### The Serpent's Entrance

**Context:** The nachash (H5175) makes its first appearance. Fifth bookend anchor: Gen 3:1 (serpent enters) -> Rev 12:9 and 20:2 (serpent identified as devil and Satan). LXX ophis (G3789).

**Notes:** Hebrew: nachash (H5175). LXX: ophis (G3789). PMI 31.32. Rev 12:9: ho ophis ho archaios ho kaloumenos Diabolos kai ho Satanas.

### The Temptation and Fall

> But of the fruit of the tree which [is] in the midst of the garden, God hath said, Ye shall not eat of it, neither shall ye touch it, lest ye die. And the serpent said unto the woman, Ye shall not surely die: For God doth know that in the day ye eat thereof, then your eyes shall be opened, and ye shall be as gods, knowing good and evil. And when the woman saw that the tree [was] good for food, and that it [was] pleasant to the eyes, and a tree to be desired to make [one] wise, she took of the fruit thereof, and did eat, and gave also unto her husband with her; and he did eat.

**Context:** The serpent's lie directly contradicts God's word. 2 Cor 11:3 connects this event to satanic deception of the church.

**Notes:** Hebrew: lo mut temutun (ye shall not surely die). Three-fold appeal (good for food, pleasant to eyes, desired for wisdom) anticipates 1 John 2:16. 2 Cor 11:3: exapataō (G1818, thoroughly deceive).

### Hiding from the Face of God

> And they heard the voice of the LORD God walking in the garden in the cool of the day: and Adam and his wife hid themselves from the presence of the LORD God amongst the trees of the garden.

**Context:** Fourth bookend anchor: Gen 3:8 (hiding from God's face) -> Rev 22:4 (his servants shall see his face). Hebrew panim (H6440) -> LXX prosopon (G4383).

**Notes:** Hebrew: panim (H6440), mithallekh (Hithpael participle). LXX: prosopon (G4383). Rev 22:4 opsetai to prosopon autou - direct reversal.

### Curse on the Serpent and Protoevangelium

> And the LORD God said unto the serpent, Because thou hast done this, thou [art] cursed above all cattle, and above every beast of the field; upon thy belly shalt thou go, and dust shalt thou eat all the days of thy life: And I will put enmity between thee and the woman, and between thy seed and her seed; it shall bruise thy head, and thou shalt bruise his heel.

**Context:** The protoevangelium: Gen 3:15 seed-of-the-woman language fulfilled in Rev 12:1-5 (woman gives birth to male child) and Rev 20:2 (serpent bound).

**Notes:** Hebrew: shuph (H7779, bruise/crush), rosh (H7218, head), aqev (H6119, heel), eybah (H342, enmity), zera (H2233, seed). Same root H7779 for both bruising occurrences - protoevangelium symmetry.

### The Curse on the Ground

> And unto Adam he said, Because thou hast hearkened unto the voice of thy wife, and hast eaten of the tree, of which I commanded thee, saying, Thou shalt not eat of it: cursed [is] the ground for thy sake; in sorrow shalt thou eat [of] it all the days of thy life; Thorns also and thistles shall it bring forth to thee; and thou shalt eat the herb of the field;

**Context:** Third bookend anchor: arur ha'adamah (H779 + H127) -> Rev 22:3 no more curse (katathema, G2652). LXX renders arur as epikataratos (G1944).

**Notes:** Hebrew: arur (H779, Qal passive participle), ha'adamah (H127), atzavon (H6093). LXX: epikataratos (G1944). Rev 22:3 uses katathema (G2652, NT hapax) - comprehensive reversal.

### Olivet Discourse: Beginning of Birth Pangs

> And Jesus answered and said unto them, Take heed that no man deceive you. For many shall come in my name, saying, I am Christ; and shall deceive many. And ye shall hear of wars and rumours of wars: see that ye be not troubled: for all these things must come to pass, but the end is not yet. For nation shall rise against nation, and kingdom against kingdom: and there shall be famines, and pestilences, and earthquakes, in divers places. All these are the beginning of sorrows.

**Context:** SP114 positions 1-4. False christs (seal 1), wars (seal 2), famines (seal 3), death (seal 4). Matt 24:8: archē ōdinōn (beginning of birth pangs) frames the sequence as preliminary.

**Notes:** Greek: planao (G4105) v.4; polemos (G4171) v.6; limoi (G3042), seismoi (G4578) v.7. archē (G746) ōdinōn (G5604, birth pangs, genitive pl) v.8. TM218: pseudochristos and planao ABSENT from Rev 6:2.
