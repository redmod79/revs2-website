# Verses

## Question
What are the seven overcomer promises (Rev 2:7, 2:11, 2:17, 2:26-28, 3:5, 3:12, 3:21), and how do they escalate across the letter sequence? Do they form a progression from individual blessing (tree of life) to cosmic enthronement (sitting on Christ's throne)? How does each promise find its fulfillment in Revelation 21-22's New Jerusalem vision? Does the overcomer arc create a structural bridge from the letters to the book's conclusion?

### OT source: Tree of life in Eden — origin of Promise 1 imagery

> And out of the ground made the LORD God to grow every tree that is pleasant to the sight, and good for food; the tree of life also in the midst of the garden, and the tree of knowledge of good and evil.

**Context:** Creation narrative. Tree of life (ets hayyim, LXX: xylon tes zoes) placed in the midst of the garden — same positional language as Rev 2:7 ('in the midst of the paradise of God'). First occurrence of tree of life in Scripture.

**Notes:** Hebrew: ets (H6086) hayyim (H2416). LXX renders as xylon tes zoes — identical to the Greek of Rev 2:7 and 22:2. 'In the midst of the garden' (betok haggan) parallels 'in the midst of the paradise of God' (en meso tou paradeisou tou theou, Rev 2:7). Genesis-Revelation inclusio: tree of life available (Gen 2:9) → barred (Gen 3:22-24) → restored (Rev 2:7; 22:2,14).

### Temple measured — naos active in prophetic section

> And there was given me a reed like unto a rod: and the angel stood, saying, Rise, and measure the temple of God, and the altar, and them that worship therein. But the court which is without the temple leave out, and measure it not; for it is given unto the Gentiles: and the holy city shall they tread under foot forty and two months.

**Context:** John measures the naos. The naos is active/present in the prophetic section (Rev 11). Inner naos measured; outer court given to Gentiles. Part of the naos progression: 3:12 (pillar in naos) → 7:15 (serve in naos) → 11:1-2 (naos measured) → 11:19 (naos opened, ark visible) → 15:8 (naos filled, inaccessible) → 21:22 (no naos — God IS the naos).
