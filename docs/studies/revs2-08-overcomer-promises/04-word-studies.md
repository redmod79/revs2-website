# Word Studies

## Question
What are the seven overcomer promises (Rev 2:7, 2:11, 2:17, 2:26-28, 3:5, 3:12, 3:21), and how do they escalate across the letter sequence? Do they form a progression from individual blessing (tree of life) to cosmic enthronement (sitting on Christ's throne)? How does each promise find its fulfillment in Revelation 21-22's New Jerusalem vision? Does the overcomer arc create a structural bridge from the letters to the book's conclusion?

### G3528 — nikao

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3528-nikao.md`

**Occurrences:** 28

To subdue, conquer, overcome, prevail. 28 NT occurrences (17 in Revelation = 61%). 86% Johannine. Present active participle (ho nikon) is the overcomer formula in all seven letters + Rev 21:7. Tense distribution: perfect for Christ's completed victory (John 16:33) and believers' shared victory (1 John 2:13-14; 4:4); aorist for Christ's bounded historical victory (Rev 3:21; 5:5) and saints overcoming Satan (12:11); present participle for believers' ongoing overcoming (Rev 2-3; 21:7); future for Lamb's final victory (17:14). Adversarial uses at 6:2, 11:7, 13:7.

**Semantic categories:** eschatological victory — believers overcoming, cosmic victory — Christ overcoming, moral victory — overcoming evil, military/hostile conquest, legal/forensic vindication

**Key verses:** Rev 2:7, Rev 2:11, Rev 3:21, Rev 5:5, Rev 12:11, Rev 17:14, Rev 21:7, John 16:33, 1 John 5:4

**Relevance:** Core overcomer verb — the structural skeleton of the entire arc. The ho nikon formula in all seven letter promises + Rev 21:7 creates the grammatical link that unifies the overcomer arc from beginning to end.

### G3534 — nikos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3534-nikos.md`

**Occurrences:** 4

Victory, conquest (neuter noun). 4 occurrences: 3 in 1 Cor 15:54-57 (death swallowed up in victory), 1 in Matt 12:20 (judgment unto victory). The noun form of the nikao word family, denoting the concrete result of conquest.

**Semantic categories:** eschatological victory over death, messianic triumph through gentleness

**Key verses:** 1 Cor 15:54, 1 Cor 15:55, 1 Cor 15:57, Matt 12:20

**Relevance:** Connects the overcomer's victory to death's defeat. 1 Cor 15:54-57 declares 'death is swallowed up in victory (nikos)' — the Promise 2 fulfillment (second death has no power) is the nikao/nikos word group completing its arc.

### G5245 — hypernikao

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5245-hupernikao.md`

**Occurrences:** 1

To more than conquer. Hapax legomenon (Rom 8:37). Compound of huper (beyond) + nikao (overcome). Pauline superlative: believers do not merely survive adversity but surpassingly conquer through Christ's love. The prefix huper- transforms ordinary victory into overwhelming surplus.

**Semantic categories:** superabundant victory through Christ's love

**Key verses:** Rom 8:37

**Relevance:** Pauline parallel to the Johannine overcomer language. Same root nikao with intensifying prefix. Shows the overcomer concept is cross-authorial, not unique to John. The means of overcoming ('through him that loved us') connects to Rev 12:11 ('by the blood of the Lamb').

### G2222 — zoe

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2222-zoe.md`

**Occurrences:** 134

Life. 134 tokens across 126 verses. Dominant Johannine usage. Forms key theological images: tree of life (xylon tes zoes, Rev 2:7; 22:2,14), book of life (biblos tes zoes, Rev 3:5; 20:12), water of life (hudor tes zoes, Rev 21:6; 22:1,17), crown of life (stephanos tes zoes, Rev 2:10; Jas 1:12). Primary antonym: thanatos (death).

**Semantic categories:** eternal/eschatological life, life as present possession in the Son, life contrasted with death, symbolic objects of life

**Key verses:** Rev 2:7, Rev 22:2, Rev 3:5, Rev 20:12, Rev 21:6, John 3:16

**Relevance:** Zoe appears in Promises 1 (tree of life), 5 (book of life), and the capstone (21:6 water of life). It is the life-substance that the overcomer receives, the record in which the overcomer is inscribed, and the water freely given.

### G3586 — xylon

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3586-xylon.md`

**Occurrences:** 19

Tree, wood, timber. 19 tokens. Two primary NT domains: (1) crucifixion/tree (Acts 5:30; 10:39; 13:29; Gal 3:13; 1 Pet 2:24 — Christ hanged on a 'tree'), (2) tree of life (Rev 2:7; 22:2,14). LXX renders Hebrew ets (H6086) as xylon. The dual usage connects the cross to the tree of life.

**Semantic categories:** crucifixion/hanging on a tree, tree of life (eschatological), wooden clubs/staves, building material

**Key verses:** Rev 2:7, Rev 22:2, Rev 22:14, Acts 5:30, Gal 3:13

**Relevance:** Promise 1 vocabulary. The tree of life (xylon tes zoes) creates the Genesis-Revelation inclusio: available (Gen 2:9) → barred (Gen 3:22-24) → restored (Rev 2:7; 22:2,14). The cross-tree connection (xylon for both) may suggest the cross is the instrument by which the tree of life is reopened.

### G2288 — thanatos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2288-thanatos.md`

**Occurrences:** 119

Death. 119 tokens. Six semantic categories: physical death, death as consequence of sin, death as reigning cosmic power, Christ's atoning death, spiritual death, death personified/second death. In Revelation: Death rides (6:8), saints overcome death through the Lamb (12:11), the second death has no power on overcomers (20:6), death itself is cast into the lake of fire (20:14), no more death (21:4).

**Semantic categories:** physical death, death as sin's consequence, death as cosmic power, Christ's atoning death, second death (personified)

**Key verses:** Rev 2:11, Rev 20:6, Rev 20:14, Rev 21:4, Rev 21:8

**Relevance:** Promise 2 vocabulary. The second death (thanatos deuteros) is the eschatological death that overcomers escape. The death arc: sin brings death (Rom 6:23) → Christ conquers death (1 Cor 15:26,54-57) → overcomers are immune to the second death (Rev 2:11; 20:6) → death itself is destroyed (20:14) → no more death (21:4).

### G3131 — manna

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3131-mánna.md`

**Occurrences:** 5

Manna. 5 NT occurrences: John 6:31,49,58 (bread from heaven, Christ as true bread), Heb 9:4 (golden pot of manna in the ark), Rev 2:17 (hidden manna as overcomer gift). Three semantic domains: wilderness provision, christological type (Jesus = true bread), hidden manna as eschatological reward.

**Semantic categories:** wilderness provision, christological type, hidden manna — eschatological reward

**Key verses:** Rev 2:17, John 6:49, Heb 9:4

**Relevance:** Promise 3 vocabulary. The hidden manna (to manna to kekrummenon) evokes the manna pot preserved in the ark (Heb 9:4, Exod 16:33-34). The manna hidden in the Most Holy Place becomes the eschatological sustenance for the overcomer. Participatory christology: Christ IS the bread of life (John 6:48-51), and the overcomer receives the hidden manna.

### G5586 — psephos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5586-psēphos.md`

**Occurrences:** 3

Pebble, voting stone. 3 occurrences: Acts 26:10 (Paul cast his vote/psephos against Christians), Rev 2:17 (white stone given to overcomer). The word reflects Greco-Roman judicial voting practice: white pebble for acquittal, black for condemnation.

**Semantic categories:** voting pebble — judicial verdict, white stone — eschatological gift

**Key verses:** Rev 2:17, Acts 26:10

**Relevance:** Promise 3 vocabulary. The white psephos (psephon leuken) in Rev 2:17 carries multiple cultural associations: (1) acquittal verdict (white = innocent), (2) tessera hospitalis (friendship/admission token), (3) victory token. The white color (leukos G3022) links to Promise 5 (white raiment) and the broader white imagery thread in Revelation.

### G4464 — rhabdos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4464-rhabdos.md`

**Occurrences:** 12

Rod, staff, sceptre. 12 NT occurrences across 6 books. Key categories: authority/rule as sceptre (Heb 1:8, Rev 2:27, 12:5, 19:15), Aaron's rod in the ark (Heb 9:4), traveler's staff, measurement rod (Rev 11:1). Collocates with sideros (iron, 3x) and poimaino (shepherd, 3x) in the 'rod of iron' formula.

**Semantic categories:** authority and rule (sceptre), discipline and correction, sacred object (Aaron's rod), traveler's staff

**Key verses:** Rev 2:27, Rev 12:5, Rev 19:15, Heb 1:8, Heb 9:4

**Relevance:** Promise 4 vocabulary. The rod of iron (rhabdos sidera) in Rev 2:27 alludes to Ps 2:9 and is fulfilled in Rev 19:15. The poimaino + rhabdos + sideros triple collocation creates a Strong VP between Rev 2:27 and 19:15.

### G4407 — proinos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4407-prōïnós.md`

**Occurrences:** 1

Pertaining to the dawn, morning. Only 1 occurrence in the N1904 mapped database (Rev 2:28). Also in Rev 22:16 (ho aster ho lampros ho proinos). Modifies aster (star) to form 'morning star.' Adjective from proi (G4404, early/dawn).

**Semantic categories:** dawn/daybreak, messianic morning star

**Key verses:** Rev 2:28, Rev 22:16

**Relevance:** Promise 4 vocabulary. The morning star (ho aster ho proinos) is the gift in Rev 2:28 and Christ's self-identification in 22:16. The overcomer RECEIVES what Christ IS — participatory christology. Only 2 shared elements between 2:28 and 22:16 (aster + proinos), but the theological significance is high.

### G2928 — krupto

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2928-krypto.md`

**Occurrences:** 16

To conceal, hide. 16 NT occurrences. Categories: physical concealment of persons, concealment of objects/treasure, divine concealment and revelation, inability to remain hidden, hidden from understanding, secrecy in action. In Rev 2:17 the hidden manna uses the perfect passive participle kekrummenon — the manna has been hidden and remains concealed.

**Semantic categories:** physical concealment, divine concealment and revelation, concealment of treasure

**Key verses:** Rev 2:17, Matt 13:44, Col 3:3

**Relevance:** Promise 3 vocabulary. Kekrummenon (perfect passive participle) in Rev 2:17 — the manna has been hidden and remains in that state. Connects to the divine concealment theme: God hides truths until the appointed time (Matt 11:25; 13:35). The hidden manna = divine provision concealed in the Most Holy Place, revealed only to the overcomer.

### G3686 — onoma

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3686-onoma.md`

**Occurrences:** 228

Name. Appears in Promises 3, 5, and 6, creating a name sub-thread that escalates across the letter sequence. Promise 3 (2:17): new name, private, known only to the receiver. Promise 5 (3:5): name confessed before Father and angels — public declaration. Promise 6 (3:12): triple name inscription — name of God, name of the city, Christ's new name. Fulfillment: 14:1 (Father's name on foreheads), 19:12 (unknowable name on Christ), 22:4 (his name in their foreheads).

**Semantic categories:** personal identity, divine name, new name as new identity

**Key verses:** Rev 2:17, Rev 3:5, Rev 3:12, Rev 14:1, Rev 19:12, Rev 22:4

**Relevance:** The name sub-thread across Promises 3, 5, 6 is a structural arc within the overcomer arc. Escalation: private (2:17) → public (3:5) → inscribed (3:12) → visible on forehead (22:4). This progression from hidden to visible supports the overall escalation thesis.

### G3485 — naos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3485-naos.md`

**Occurrences:** 46

Temple, shrine — specifically the inner sanctuary where deity dwells, distinguished from hieron (entire temple precinct). 16 of 40+ occurrences in Revelation (37%). Semantic categories: physical inner sanctuary, Christ's body as temple, believers as God's dwelling, heavenly temple, eschatological/prophetic temple. The naos progression in Revelation: present/active (3:12; 7:15; 11:1-2,19) → filled/inaccessible (15:8) → absent as building, absorbed into God (21:22).

**Semantic categories:** physical inner sanctuary, Christ's body as temple, believers as God's dwelling, heavenly temple, eschatological temple

**Key verses:** Rev 3:12, Rev 7:15, Rev 11:19, Rev 15:8, Rev 21:22

**Relevance:** Promise 6 vocabulary. The naos tension between 3:12 (pillar in my naos) and 21:22 (no naos therein: God and the Lamb ARE the naos) is the most structurally significant single observation in this study. The temple-to-Person transformation (SP104) means the overcomer's pillar-status is fulfilled not in a building but in God's own presence.

### G2362 — thronos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2362-thronos.md`

**Occurrences:** 61

Throne. 61 tokens across 50 verses. 72% in Revelation (36/50). Categories: God's throne in heaven, messianic/Davidic throne, throne of grace, judgment throne, delegated thrones (overcomers), Satan's seat and beast's throne. Rev 3:21 textually distinguishes two thrones: 'my throne' (en to throno mou) and 'my Father's throne' (en to throno autou). Rev 22:3 unifies them: 'the throne of God and of the Lamb' (singular).

**Semantic categories:** God's throne in heaven, messianic throne, throne of grace, judgment throne, delegated thrones, counterfeit throne

**Key verses:** Rev 3:21, Rev 4:2, Rev 20:4, Rev 20:11, Rev 22:1, Rev 22:3

**Relevance:** Promise 7 vocabulary. The throne promise is the climax of the overcomer arc: sharing Christ's throne. Rev 3:21 distinguishes two thrones; Rev 22:3 merges them into one. The throne arc: God's throne (4:2) → Lamb approaches throne (5:6-7) → overcomers seated on thrones (20:4) → throne of God and the Lamb (22:1,3) → overcomers reign from throne forever (22:5).

### G4165 — poimaino

### G976 — biblos

### G2816 — kleronomeo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2816-kleronomeo.md`

**Occurrences:** 22

To inherit. In Rev 21:7: 'he that overcometh shall inherit (kleronomēsei) all things.' This is the only Revelation use and marks a verb shift from doso (I will give, used in promises 1,3,4) to kleronomeo. Inheritance implies legal sonship — the overcomer is an heir, not merely a gift-receiver.

**Semantic categories:** eschatological inheritance, legal heir status

**Key verses:** Rev 21:7, Gal 3:29, Heb 1:14

**Relevance:** Capstone promise vocabulary. The shift from doso (gift) to kleronomeo (inheritance) at Rev 21:7 elevates the overcomer from recipient to heir. Combined with the sonship declaration ('he shall be my son'), this marks the highest relational status in the arc.

### G2537 — kainos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2537-kainos.md`

New (qualitatively, not merely recent). In the overcomer arc: new name (2:17; 3:12), new Jerusalem (3:12; 21:2), new heaven and earth (21:1), all things new (21:5). Kainos implies qualitative renewal, not just temporal freshness. The 'new' thread spans the entire book.

**Semantic categories:** qualitative newness, eschatological renewal

**Key verses:** Rev 2:17, Rev 3:12, Rev 21:1, Rev 21:2, Rev 21:5

**Relevance:** Cross-cutting vocabulary in Promises 3 (new name) and 6 (new Jerusalem, Christ's new name). The kainos thread ties the overcomer promises to the cosmic renewal: the overcomer receives new identity (name) and enters the new creation (new Jerusalem, new heaven/earth).

### G5207 — huios

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5207-huios.md`

**Occurrences:** 382

Son. In Rev 21:7: 'he shall be my son' (autos estai moi huios). Without article — qualitative, emphasizing the status of sonship. This is the covenant formula echoing 2 Sam 7:14 and Heb 1:5. Sonship is the ultimate relational fulfillment: the overcomer is not just a servant, priest, or king — but a son.

**Semantic categories:** divine sonship, covenant relationship, eschatological identity

**Key verses:** Rev 21:7, 2 Sam 7:14, Heb 1:5

**Relevance:** Capstone promise vocabulary. The sonship declaration at Rev 21:7 is the relational climax of the overcomer arc. All seven promises lead to this: the overcomer inherits all things and is declared God's son.

### G4735 — stephanos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4735-stephanos.md`

**Occurrences:** 18

Crown, wreath. 18 unique verses. Categories: crown of thorns (Passion narrative), athletic victor's wreath (1 Cor 9:25), eschatological crown of reward (2 Tim 4:8; Jas 1:12; Rev 2:10; 3:11), persons as crown (1 Thess 2:19), heavenly crowned beings (Rev 4:4; 6:2). Distinguished from diadema (G1238, royal crown of sovereignty). Rev 2:10: 'crown of life' (stephanos tes zoes) — related to overcomer but distinct from the overcomer promise itself (given in 2:11).

**Semantic categories:** crown of thorns, athletic victor's wreath, eschatological reward, heavenly crowned beings

**Key verses:** Rev 2:10, Rev 3:11, Rev 4:4, 2 Tim 4:8, Jas 1:12

**Relevance:** Related but distinct from overcomer promises. Rev 2:10 (crown of life) is in the exhortation to Smyrna, not the overcomer promise (which is 2:11, second death immunity). Rev 3:11 (hold fast your crown) is in the exhortation to Philadelphia, not the promise (which is 3:12, pillar/temple/names). The crown is the reward for faithfulness; the overcomer promise is the eschatological fulfillment.

### G1325 — didomi

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1325-didomi.md`

**Occurrences:** 430

To give. The standard verb formula in the overcomer promises: doso (future active indicative, 'I will give') in Promises 1 (2:7), 3 (2:17, twice), 4 (2:26,28), and 7 (3:21). Promise 5 uses different verbs (peribaleitai, exaleipso, homologeso). Promise 6 uses poieso. The capstone (21:7) shifts to kleronomeo. Also in 21:6 (doso to dipsōnti, 'I will give to the thirsting one').

**Semantic categories:** divine gift-giving, promise formula

**Key verses:** Rev 2:7, Rev 2:17, Rev 2:26, Rev 2:28, Rev 3:21, Rev 21:6

**Relevance:** The doso formula is the standard overcomer-promise verb, making the shift to kleronomeo at Rev 21:7 structurally significant. The verb pattern: doso (give as gift) → kleronomeo (inherit as right) represents an escalation in the overcomer's status from recipient to heir.

**Key verses:** Rev 3:21

### G3529 — nike

**Occurrences:** 1

Victory — 1 occurrence (1 John 5:4). The noun form of nikao. "This is the victory that overcometh the world, even our faith."

### G1208 — deuteros

**Occurrences:** 47

Second — 44 occurrences. Used for "second death" (Rev 2:11, 20:6, 20:14, 21:8), unique to Revelation.

**Semantic categories:** Ordinal number, Second death eschatological term

**Key verses:** Rev 2:11, Rev 20:6, Rev 20:14, Rev 21:8

**Relevance:** Second death is the negative counterpart to the overcomer promise in Rev 2:11: "He that overcometh shall not be hurt of the second death."
