# Verses

## Question
What is the structural significance of Revelation's time periods? Why does the 3.5-year period appear in three different expressions — 42 months (Rev 11:2, 13:5), 1,260 days (Rev 11:3, 12:6), and 'a time, and times, and half a time' (Rev 12:14)? Use Daniel 7:25 and 12:7 as the source texts. Do the three expressions map to different subjects or perspectives? What does the text of Revelation 20 explicitly state about the millennium — what happens before, during, and after the thousand years? Document the sequence of events in Rev 20:1-15 without importing external frameworks.

### 42 Months — Gentiles Trample the Holy City (Hostile Subject)

> But the court which is without the temple leave out, and measure it not; for it is given unto the Gentiles: and the holy city shall they tread under foot forty [and] two months.

**Context:** An angel instructs John to measure the temple but exclude the outer court. The Gentiles (ethne) are the grammatical subject of the trampling — hostile actors given (edothe, divine passive) the court, who trample (patesousin, V-FAI-3P, future active) the holy city for 42 months.

**Notes:** 42-month expression appears with hostile subject (Gentiles/nations). Key vocabulary: edothe (divine passive = God permits), pateo (trample — hostile-exclusive verb per G3961 study, all 5 NT uses connote hostile or authoritative treading), ethne (nations), menas tessarakonta dyo (accusative of duration). Verbal parallel to Luke 21:24 (pateo + kairos ethnon). This is 42-month Expression #1.

### 1260 Days — Two Witnesses Prophesy (Preservation Subject)

> And I will give [power] unto my two witnesses, and they shall prophesy a thousand two hundred [and] threescore days, clothed in sackcloth.

**Context:** God speaks in first person (doso, V-FAI-1S, future active indicative — NOT the divine passive edothe). The witnesses are the grammatical subject of prophesying. Same time period as 42 months in the immediately preceding verse, but expressed in days and with a preservation/faithful-witness vocabulary.

**Notes:** 1260-day expression appears with preservation/witness subject. Key vocabulary: doso (future active 1st person — God directly commissioning, NOT divine passive), propheteusousin (prophesy — faithful activity), martyrs (witness), hemeras chilias diakosias hexekonta (accusative of duration). Voice shift: edothe (passive) in 11:2 → doso (active) in 11:3, indicating different agency layers. This is 1260-day Expression #1.

### 1260 Days — Woman Nourished in Wilderness (Preservation Subject)

> And the woman fled into the wilderness, where she hath a place prepared of God, that they should feed her there a thousand two hundred [and] threescore days.

**Context:** Rev 12 cosmic woman narrative. The woman (gyne) is subject. She flees to a wilderness place prepared (hetoimasmenon, V-RPP) by God. The verb trephosin (V-PAS-3P, present active subjunctive, 3rd plural) — 'they nourish' — active voice with unspecified plural subject (angels? faithful? God's agents?).

**Notes:** 1260-day expression #2 with preservation subject. Key vocabulary: gyne (woman), eremos (wilderness), hetoimasmenon apo tou theou (prepared of God), trephosin (active voice — 'they nourish'). VP162 from revs-33: five shared elements with Rev 12:14 (woman, wilderness, trepho, time expression, divine provision). Active voice trephosin contrasts with passive trephetai in 12:14.

Recapitulation of 12:6 with voice shift and Daniel quotation formula.

### 42 Months — Beast Given Authority to Act (Hostile Subject)

> And there was given unto him a mouth speaking great things and blasphemies; and power was given unto him to continue forty [and] two months.

**Context:** The sea-beast receives authority. Double edothe (V-API-3S, divine passive): first grants a mouth speaking blasphemies, second grants exousia to 'act' (poiesai, V-AAN, aorist active infinitive) for 42 months. The poiesai + accusative of duration idiom (per grammar ref: aorist-infinitive-accusative-of-duration.md).

**Notes:** 42-month expression #2 with hostile subject. Key vocabulary: edothe (divine passive ×2), stoma laloun megala kai blasphemias (mouth speaking great things and blasphemies — verbal parallel to Dan 7:25 'speak great words against the most High'), exousia poiesai menas tessarakonta dyo (authority to act 42 months). The hostile vocabulary cluster: blasphemia, stoma, megala. Verbal allusion to Dan 7:25 confirmed by shared vocabulary: speak great things + time expression + authority given.

### Aramaic Source Text — Horn's Authority for Time-Times-Half

> And he shall speak [great] words against the most High, and shall wear out the saints of the most High, and think to change times and laws: and they shall be given into his hand until a time and times and the dividing of time.

**Context:** Daniel's vision of the fourth beast's little horn. Aramaic text. Contains TWO different Aramaic time-words: zimnin (zman, A2166 — 'appointed times' the horn thinks to change) and iddan (A5732 — 'a set time/year' in the prophetic formula). The formula ad-iddan ve-iddanin u-pelag iddan = 1 + 2 + 0.5 = 3.5 iddan.

**Notes:** Source text for all three Revelation expressions. Parser confirms: zimnin (zman, Noun.mp.Abs = 'times' — calendar-appointed times that are changed) vs. iddan (Noun.ms.Abs = 'time/year' — prophetic duration formula). BDB defines iddan as 'technically a year.' Dan 4:16,25 establish iddan = year (seven iddanin = seven years of Nebuchadnezzar's madness). The horn: (1) speaks against the Most High, (2) wears out saints, (3) thinks to change times and laws, (4) saints given into his hand for 3.5 iddan. Verbal parallels to Rev 13:5-7: speak great things = speak blasphemies, wear out saints = make war with saints, given into his hand = edothe autoi exousia.

### Hebrew Source Text — Oath Formula for Time-Times-Half

> And I heard the man clothed in linen, which [was] upon the waters of the river, when he held up his right hand and his left hand unto heaven, and sware by him that liveth for ever that [it shall be] for a time, times, and an half; and when he shall have accomplished to scatter the power of the holy people, all these [things] shall be finished.

**Context:** Hebrew text. The same time formula but with Hebrew moed (H4150) instead of Aramaic iddan. Moed = appointed time/feast/assembly — carries cultic/covenant calendar overtones that iddan does not. The oath formula (both hands raised, swearing by the eternal one) parallels Rev 10:5-6.

**Notes:** Parser confirms: moed (Noun.ms.Abs, 'appointment') + moadim (Noun.mp.Abs, 'appointments') + chatsi (half). Hebrew uses moed where Aramaic Dan 7:25 uses iddan. Moed carries feast/appointed-assembly connotations (150 of 229 OT uses = 'tabernacle of the congregation'). The endpoint: 'when he shall have accomplished to scatter the power (yad) of the holy people.' Rev 10:5-6 parallel: angel raises hand, swears by the eternal creator, declares chronos ouketi estai ('time/delay no longer').

### Horn Wars with Saints; Judgment Given TO Saints

> I beheld, and the same horn made war with the saints, and prevailed against them; Until the Ancient of days came, and judgment was given to the saints of the most High; and the time came that the saints possessed the kingdom.

**Context:** Dan 7 vision. The horn makes war with saints (cf. Rev 13:7) until the Ancient of Days comes and judgment is given TO the saints (not against them). This parallels Rev 20:4 krima edothe autois — judgment was given unto them.

**Notes:** Key parallel to Rev 20:4: 'judgment was given to the saints.' In Dan 7:22 the saints RECEIVE judgment (vindication/authority to judge), not receive judgment upon themselves. Same construction in Rev 20:4: krima edothe autois. The sequence: horn prevails → Ancient of Days intervenes → judgment given to saints → saints possess kingdom. This maps to: beast's 42 months → Christ returns → millennium judgment → saints reign.

### Pre-Millennium — Angel Binds Satan, Casts into Abyss, Seals

> And I saw an angel come down from heaven, having the key of the bottomless pit and a great chain in his hand. And he laid hold on the dragon, that old serpent, which is the Devil, and Satan, and bound him a thousand years, And cast him into the bottomless pit, and shut him up, and set a seal upon him, that he should deceive the nations no more, till the thousand years should be fulfilled: and after that he must be loosed a little season.

**Context:** Pre-millennium sequence. Five aorist verbs form a narrative chain: ekratesen (laid hold), edesen (bound), ebalen (cast), ekleisen (shut), esphragisen (sealed). Temporal boundary: achri telesthe ta chilia ete (until the thousand years should be fulfilled). Necessity: dei lythenai auton mikron chronon (he must be loosed a little time).

**Notes:** TM175 deo/luo antonym pair frames the millennium (edesen 20:2, lythenai/lythesetai 20:3,7). TM178 triple confinement (cast, shut, sealed — three escalating security verbs). TM179 fourfold Satan ID identical to 12:9. The ONLY stated purpose of binding: hina me planese ta ethne (prevent deception of nations). Text does NOT say 'confined to desolate earth' (EGW addition) — says abyssos (abyss/bottomless pit). Chronos at end: mikron chronon (little time) — contrast with kairos in 12:14. Chilia ete occurrence #1 (20:2, bound) and #2 (20:3, fulfilled).

Anastasis appears in Revelation ONLY here (20:5 and 20:6). Paired ordinals: first resurrection + second death → implies first death + second resurrection (SP083).

### Day-Year Formula — Yom Lashshanah (First Occurrence)

> After the number of the days in which ye searched the land, [even] forty days, each day for a year, shall ye bear your iniquities, [even] forty years, and ye shall know my breach of promise.

**Notes:** Parser confirms: yom (Noun.ms.Abs, 'day') + la-shanah (Prep + Art + Noun.fs.Abs, 'for the year'). The formula appears twice in this verse for emphasis. Context: specific judgment ratio applied to a specific situation (40 days → 40 years). The question is whether this declares a universal prophetic principle or describes a specific divine decree.

### Chronos — Delay No Longer (Chronos vs Kairos Distinction)

> And sware by him that liveth for ever and ever, who created heaven, and the things that therein are, and the earth, and the things that therein are, and the sea, and the things which are therein, that there should be time no longer:

**Context:** The mighty angel's oath. Uses chronos (G5550) — 'time/delay no longer.' Parallels Dan 12:7's oath formula. Chronos here means 'delay' or 'duration of waiting,' not the cessation of time itself.

**Notes:** Parser: chronos (N-NSM) ouketi (ADV-N, 'no longer') estai (V-FDI-3S, 'will be'). Rev 12:14 uses kairos (appointed prophetic time), Rev 10:6 uses chronos (delay/duration). The distinction: kairos = appointed season in God's plan, chronos = elapsed duration or delay. The oath parallels Dan 12:7 structurally but uses different vocabulary to make a different point.

### Stacked Time Units — Author CAN Use Year (Etos) in Non-Prophetic Context

> And the four angels were loosed, which were prepared for an hour, and a day, and a month, and a year, for to slay the third part of men.

**Context:** The four angels at the Euphrates. The verse stacks hour + day + month + year (horan kai hemeran kai mena kai eniauton). This proves the author knows and can use the word 'year' (eniauton, G1763) — but chooses NOT to use it in the prophetic time expressions (42 months, 1260 days, time-times-half).

**Notes:** Key evidence for Pfandl's point #5: the word 'year' (etos/eniautos) is deliberately absent from Daniel-Revelation prophetic time expressions. Rev 9:15 proves the author has the vocabulary. Rev 20 uses etos with chilioi (chilia ete) for the millennium — the only prophetic period in Revelation expressed in years. This exception may signal that the millennium is NOT subject to the same symbolic-time framework as the 3.5-year period.
