# Verses

## Question
How do trumpets 5-7 (the three "woes") differ structurally from trumpets 1-4? What is the role of the trumpet interlude (the little book and the two witnesses, Rev 10:1-11:14)? How does the seventh trumpet's kingdom announcement (Rev 11:15-18) function as both climax and transition? What is the relationship between the seventh trumpet and the bowls?

### Trumpets 1-4: Formulaic nature judgments with one-third fraction

> The first angel sounded, and there followed hail and fire mingled with blood, and they were cast upon the earth: and the third part of trees was burnt up, and all green grass was burnt up. And the second angel sounded, and as it were a great mountain burning with fire was cast into the sea: and the third part of the sea became blood; And the third part of the creatures which were in the sea, and had life, died; and the third part of the ships were destroyed. And the third angel sounded, and there fell a great star from heaven, burning as it were a lamp, and it fell upon the third part of the rivers, and upon the fountains of waters; And the name of the star is called Wormwood: and the third part of the waters became wormwood; and many men died of the waters, because they were made bitter. And the fourth angel sounded, and the third part of the sun was smitten, and the third part of the moon, and the third part of the stars; so as the third part of them was darkened, and the day shone not for a third part of it, and the night likewise.

**Context:** Trumpets 1-4 follow the seven-trumpet distribution scene (8:2,6). Brief, formulaic, nature-targeting, with the one-third fraction appearing 11 times. These serve as the structural baseline for comparison with trumpets 5-7.

**Notes:** TM075 documents the one-third fraction (to triton, G5154) appearing 11 times in these 6 verses. TM076 documents the formulaic trumpet-sounding formula. SP055 (Strong) documents that trumpets 1-4 and bowls 1-4 target the same domains in the same order: earth, sea, rivers, sun.

### Fifth trumpet: locust description and king Abaddon/Apollyon

> And the shapes of the locusts [were] like unto horses prepared unto battle; and on their heads [were] as it were crowns like gold, and their faces [were] as the faces of men. And they had hair as the hair of women, and their teeth were as [the teeth] of lions. And they had breastplates, as it were breastplates of iron; and the sound of their wings [was] as the sound of chariots of many horses running to battle. And they had tails like unto scorpions, and there were stings in their tails: and their power [was] to hurt men five months. And they had a king over them, [which is] the angel of the bottomless pit, whose name in the Hebrew tongue [is] Abaddon, but in the Greek tongue hath [his] name Apollyon.

**Context:** The locust description transforms Joel 2's divine army into a demonic force. The bilingual naming (Abaddon H11 / Apollyon G623) is unique in Revelation. Apollyon = active present participle of apollymi G622, 'the one who destroys.'

**Notes:** AN031 (Strong) documents the Joel 2 allusion. Key transformation: Joel 2:25 identifies the locusts as 'my great army which I sent among you' — God's army. Shared elements: horse-like appearance (Joel 2:4 / Rev 9:7), chariot noise (Joel 2:5 / Rev 9:9), battle array, fire. Rev 9:11 has the bilingual naming: Hebraisti Abaddon (G3, H11) and en te Hellenike Apollyon (G623).

### Sixth trumpet / Second woe: voice from golden altar, four angels at Euphrates

**Context:** The golden altar (thysiasteriou tou chrysou) at Rev 9:13 connects to the altar vindication arc (SP037): the same altar that received prayers (8:3-5) now issues a judgment command. The Euphrates boundary marks the eastern frontier of the Roman world.

### Sixth trumpet: horse description — lion heads, fire/smoke/brimstone, tail-power

### Mighty angel with open little book, seven thunders sealed

> And I saw another mighty angel come down from heaven, clothed with a cloud: and a rainbow [was] upon his head, and his face [was] as it were the sun, and his feet as pillars of fire: And he had in his hand a little book open: and he set his right foot upon the sea, and [his] left [foot] on the earth, And cried with a loud voice, as [when] a lion roareth: and when he had cried, seven thunders uttered their voices. And when the seven thunders had uttered their voices, I was about to write: and I heard a voice from heaven saying unto me, Seal up those things which the seven thunders uttered, and write them not.

**Context:** The trumpet interlude opens (episode 1). The angel's theophanic features (cloud, rainbow, sun-face, fire-feet) echo Dan 10:5-6 and Rev 1:13-16. biblaridion (G974) = diminutive scroll, distinct from biblion (G975). eneogmenon (V-RPP-ASN, perfect passive participle of anoigo G455) = permanently opened state.

**Notes:** TM078: biblaridion appears only in Rev 10:2,8,9,10. The seven thunders are sealed (sphragison, imperative of sphragizo G4972) — the only content in Revelation that is sealed rather than revealed. Scholarly proposal (Storms/Bauckham): the thunders represent cancelled warning judgments — earlier plagues proved ineffective (9:20-21), so no more warnings; bowls are unlimited and final.

### Eating the little book: sweet/bitter, prophetic recommission

> And the voice which I heard from heaven spake unto me again, and said, Go [and] take the little book which is open in the hand of the angel which standeth upon the sea and upon the earth. And I went unto the angel, and said unto him, Give me the little book. And he said unto me, Take [it], and eat it up; and it shall make thy belly bitter, but it shall be in thy mouth sweet as honey. And I took the little book out of the angel's hand, and ate it up; and it was in my mouth sweet as honey: and as soon as I had eaten it, my belly was bitter. And he said unto me, Thou must prophesy again before many peoples, and nations, and tongues, and kings.

**Context:** Ezek 2:9-3:3 allusion modified: both are sweet in the mouth, but Revelation adds bitterness INTRINSIC to the eating (Ezek's bitterness comes later at 3:14). The heavenly voice uses biblion (10:8) while John uses biblaridion (10:2,9,10) — this vocabulary alternation is significant for the scroll-identity debate.

**Notes:** propheteusai (V-AAN, aorist active infinitive of propheteuo G4395) — the ONLY aorist infinitive of propheteuo in the NT. dei (G1163, divine necessity) + palin (G3825, again) = must prophesy again. This recommission bridges episode 1 (prophetic authorization) to episode 2 (prophetic demonstration in 11:1-14).

**Context:** naos (G3485) = inner sanctuary, not the broader hieron. Measuring signifies protection/preservation (cf. Ezek 40-42). The outer court is 'given' (edothe, divine passive) to the Gentiles. 42 months = first of the 3.5-year period formulations in this chapter.

**Notes:** The 42 months (tessarakonta duo menas, G5062 + G1417 + G3376) = 3.5 years = 1260 days. TM079 tracks seven formulations of this period across Daniel and Revelation. The months formulation is associated with oppression/treading (11:2, 13:5).

### Twenty-four elders' worship: eilephas (perfect), ebasileusas (aorist)

> And the four and twenty elders, which sat before God on their seats, fell upon their faces, and worshipped God, Saying, We give thee thanks, O Lord God Almighty, which art, and wast, and art to come; because thou hast taken to thee thy great power, and hast reigned.

**Context:** eilephas (V-2RAI-2S, perfect of lambano G2983) = 'you have taken and now hold.' ebasileusas (V-AAI-2S, aorist of basileuo G936) = ingressive/proleptic aorist, 'you have begun to reign.' The divine title abbreviation: ho on kai ho en — MISSING ho erchomenos ('the coming one'), because from this vantage point God has already come.

**Notes:** The perfect-then-aorist sequence (eilephas + ebasileusas) is exegetically significant: the perfect establishes a state (God has seized and now holds power), the aorist marks the decisive exercise of that power. Rev 19:6 uses ebasileusen (V-AAI-3S) in the same ingressive-proleptic pattern. The missing ho erchomenos appears at 1:4, 1:8, 4:8 but is dropped at 11:17 and 16:5.

### Five-element programmatic announcement: nations, wrath, judgment, reward, destruction
