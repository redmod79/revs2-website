### Focus Areas

1. **The 4+3 structural division: nature-targeting vs. humanity-targeting trumpets:**
The prior study (revs2-11) established TM075 (one-third fraction, 11 occurrences in Rev 8:7-12) and TM076 (formulaic trumpet-sounding formula). This focus area extends that analysis into trumpets 5-7. Measure the textual length: trumpets 1-4 = ~6 verses, trumpet 5 alone = 12 verses, trumpet 6 = 9 verses. Document the shift from impersonal cosmic phenomena (hail, blood, wormwood, darkness) to personified demonic agents (locust-army with Abaddon as king, 200M cavalry with lion-headed horses). Note Rev 9:4's explicit prohibition against harming grass/trees — the very targets of trumpets 1-2 — confirming the domain shift. READ `$WORD_STUDIES/G5154-tritos.md` for the one-third fraction analysis. Run greek_parser.py --verse on Rev 8:13 to verify the triple ouai and the technical phrase tous katoikountas epi tes ges. Check whether the same earth-dweller formula appears at Rev 3:10, 6:10, 11:10, 13:8, 17:8 to establish it as a technical term.

4. **The trumpet interlude structure: parallel to the seal interlude and internal two-part design:**
SP042 (Moderate) identifies the two-episode structure: episode 1 (Rev 10:1-11) is prophetic commissioning (eating the book, receiving the command to prophesy again); episode 2 (Rev 11:1-14) is prophetic demonstration (the two witnesses prophesy, suffer, and are vindicated). The connecting thread is propheteuo — it appears at Rev 10:11 (commission) and Rev 11:3 (execution). SP043 (Moderate) documents the expanding pattern: seal interlude = Rev 7:1-17 (17 verses), trumpet interlude = Rev 10:1-11:14 (25 verses, ~47% longer). Both answer a preceding judgment question: 'who shall be able to stand?' (Rev 6:17) and the implicit 'who is protected from the woe-trumpets?' (answered by Rev 9:4's seal reference). Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-ot and --hybrid-nt on Rev 10:1 to find thematic parallels. Also verify whether Paulien's claim about Rev 9:4 referencing the sealed from Rev 7 is textually grounded — the phrase 'the seal of God in their foreheads' (Rev 9:4) matches Rev 7:3.

Run a search: grep for 'woe' across all of Revelation to map every occurrence.
