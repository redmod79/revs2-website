# Verse-by-Verse Analysis by Focus Area

## Question
What does Revelation's literary architecture establish about the new creation (Rev 21:1-22:5)?

---

## FA01. Rev 21:22 — No Naos: The Sanctuary Terminus

### Rev 21:22 — The Structural Climax

"And I saw no temple therein: for the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are the temple of it."

The Greek construction is chiastic: negation then affirmation. First half: naon (G3485, accusative singular) ouk eidon en aute — "a naos I did not see in her." The accusative naon is fronted before the verb, giving it emphatic position. This is not a passing observation but a deliberate declaration. Second half: ho gar kyrios ho theos ho pantokrator naos autes estin kai to arnion — naos appears again, now in the nominative as a predicate. God and the Lamb ARE the naos. The anarthrous predicate nominative (naos without the article) carries qualitative force: they possess the nature of the naos.

This is the endpoint of a 16-occurrence naos arc running through Revelation chapters 3 through 16. The word traces a compartmental progression: lampstands in the Holy Place (1:12-13), bronze altar imagery in the outer court (6:9), golden altar in the Holy Place (8:3, 9:13), MHP ark revealed (11:19), triple-genitive hapax naos tes skenes tou martyriou at the DOA hinge (15:5), and the final naos voice at 16:17 declaring gegonen (It is done). Then silence. For five chapters naos does not appear. When it returns at 21:22, it comes as negation followed by redefinition. The building yields to the Builder.

### Rev 15:5-8 — DOA Hinge

"The temple of the tabernacle of the testimony in heaven was opened."

The triple-genitive construction naos tes skenes tou martyriou is a New Testament hapax (TM100). It combines naos (G3485) and skene (G4633) in a single phrase — the only place where both temple and tabernacle vocabulary converge in the same genitive chain. Five structural indicators mark this as the DOA opening (IC156 Strong). The declaration that "no man was able to enter into the temple" (15:8) during the seven plagues signals the terminal sanctuary phase. Access is blocked for the duration of judgment.

### Rev 16:17 — Last Naos Occurrence

"And there came a great voice out of the temple of heaven, from the throne, saying, It is done."

The last naos mention before the 21:22 negation. The verb gegonen (G1096, perfect singular) — "It has been accomplished" — is the second station of the ginomai arc. The structural gap between 16:17 and 21:22 is significant: naos disappears from the narrative for the entire judgment-execution sequence, the millennium, and the great white throne. Its return as a negation completes the arc.

### Rev 11:19 — MHP Ark Revealed

"And the temple of God was opened in heaven, and there was seen in his temple the ark of his testament."

The naos opens to reveal the kibotos (ark). This is the MHP access moment — the compartmental progression reaches its deepest point. The ark, hidden behind the veil in Israel's tabernacle, is now visible. This structural precursor establishes that by the time the narrative reaches 21:22, the progression has moved through every sanctuary compartment. There is nowhere further to go except beyond the structure itself.

### Rev 3:12 — Overcomer Promise 6 (Philadelphia)

"Him that overcometh will I make a pillar in the temple of my God."

This promise creates a structural tension with 21:22. If there is no naos in the new creation, where does the pillar stand? The resolution lies in the redefinition: God and the Lamb ARE the naos. The promise of a pillar in the naos is not cancelled but fulfilled in a mode that exceeds the original language. The pillar stands in the divine presence itself, not in a building.

### Rev 7:15-17 — Proleptic Tabernacles Scene

"He that sitteth on the throne shall dwell among them."

The verb skenosei (G4637, future active indicative) appears here in the context of a scene that still includes the naos (7:15a: "serve him day and night in his temple"). Between 7:15 (naos present, skenoo used) and 21:22 (naos absorbed, skenoo used at 21:3), the temple undergoes transformation. Both passages use the same tabernacling verb, but the architectural frame has been absorbed into the divine persons.

## FA02. Ezek 47 to Rev 22: The River Source-Shift

### Rev 22:1 — River from the Throne

"A pure river of water of life, clear as crystal, proceeding out of the throne of God and of the Lamb."

The parsing: potamon (G4215, accusative) hydatos (G5204, genitive) zoes (G2222, genitive) — river of water of life. The present middle participle ekporeuomenon (G1607) — "proceeding" — describes continuous flowing from ek tou thronou tou Theou kai tou Arniou. The source is the throne — personal, not architectural. The single article tou before both Theou and Arniou binds God and the Lamb as a unity (Granville Sharp construction applied to the throne).

This verse is structurally inseparable from 21:22. If God and the Lamb ARE the naos (21:22), then what flowed from the temple threshold in Ezekiel now flows from the divine persons directly. The mediating architecture has been absorbed.

### Ezek 47:1 — River from the Temple Threshold

"Waters issued out from under the threshold of the house eastward."

The Hebrew miphtan habayit identifies the source as the architectural threshold of the temple structure. The waters flow eastward past the altar. In Ezekiel's vision, the river is tied to the building. It deepens progressively — ankle, knee, loins, swimming depth (47:3-5) — describing a process of increasing vitality.

### Rev 22:2 — Tree of Life with Healing Leaves

"The tree of life, which bare twelve manner of fruits, and yielded her fruit every month: and the leaves of the tree were for the healing of the nations."

This verse merges two OT sources. The tree of life (xylon zoes, G3586 + G2222) comes from Gen 2:9 via verbatim LXX identity. The monthly fruit and healing leaves come from Ezek 47:12 — "new fruit according to his months... the leaf thereof for medicine (terufah, H8644)." The Greek therapeia (G2322) at Rev 22:2 has only four NT occurrences; this is the only eschatological healing use.

The escalation from Ezekiel is in scope: Ezekiel's healing leaves serve the local environs along the river. Revelation's leaves serve the therapeia ton ethnon — the healing of the nations. Local has become universal.

### Ezek 47:12 — Monthly Fruit, Healing Leaves (Source Text)

"It shall bring forth new fruit according to his months... the leaf thereof for medicine."

The detail of monthly fruit (le-chadashav — new fruit by its months) is Ezekiel's contribution to the Rev 22:2 composite. Genesis provides the tree's name (tree of life); Ezekiel provides its productivity (monthly fruit) and purpose (healing leaves). Revelation merges both sources and universalizes the scope.

### Ezek 48:35 — YHWH Shammah

"The name of the city from that day shall be, The LORD is there."

The culmination of Ezekiel's temple vision. The city's identity is defined by divine presence. Rev 21:3 fulfills: God himself shall be with them. Rev 21:22 transcends: God IS the temple. The three words naon ouk eidon at 21:22 do not cancel Ezekiel's nine chapters of temple architecture through oversight. They cancel them through climactic completion. What Ezekiel named — YHWH shammah, the LORD is there — Revelation consummates: the LORD is not merely there but IS the sacred space itself.

### Gen 2:10 — River from Eden

"A river went out of Eden to water the garden."

The LXX uses potamos exeporeueto ek tou Eden — the river "was proceeding out of Eden." The parallel to Rev 22:1 (potamon ekporeuomenon ek tou thronou) is verbal: same noun, same verb root, different source. Eden's land yields to God's throne. The garden was a place; the new creation's river source is a Person.

## FA03. Gen 2:9 to Rev 22:2: The Tree of Life Bookend

### Gen 2:9 — First Tree of Life

"The tree of life also in the midst of the garden, and the tree of knowledge of good and evil."

The Hebrew: ets (H6086, noun masculine construct) hayyim (H2416, noun masculine plural absolute) — tree of life. The LXX renders this as xylon tes zoes. Two trees stand in Eden: the tree of life and the tree of knowledge. Both are named. In Rev 22:2, only one tree returns. The tree of knowledge has no parallel in the new creation. One tree was removed; one was restored and escalated.

### Gen 3:22-24 — Access Blocked

"So he drove out the man; and he placed at the east of the garden of Eden Cherubims, and a flaming sword which turned every way, to keep the way of the tree of life."

After the Fall, access to the tree of life is blocked — not because the tree is destroyed (it persists) but because humanity's condition has changed. The cherubim guard the way. This sets up the reversal: Rev 22:14 grants access ("that they may have right to the tree of life"), and 21:25 declares the gates never shut. The flaming sword gives way to permanently open gates.

### Rev 2:7 — Overcomer Promise 1 (Ephesus)

"To him that overcometh will I give to eat of the tree of life, which is in the midst of the paradise of God."

The phrase xylon tes zoes appears here, connecting the overcomer promise directly to both Gen 2:9 (origin) and Rev 22:2 (fulfillment). The tree of life is kept alive as a promise in the letters before it reappears physically in the new creation. This is overcomer promise 1, fulfilled at Rev 22:2.

### Rev 22:2 — Tree of Life Restored and Escalated

The escalation pattern is specific and measurable:
- **Number**: One tree in Eden becomes a grove straddling the river (enteuthen kai ekeithen — on this side and that side).
- **Fruit**: Seasonal in Eden; dodeka karpous kata mena hekaston — twelve fruits, one each month in New Jerusalem.
- **Scope**: Available to two people in a garden; leaves for therapeia ton ethnon — healing of nations.
- **Access**: Guarded by cherubim (Gen 3:24); gates never shut (Rev 21:25).

The bookend is not circular. It spirals upward at every point of contact.

### Prov 3:18 — Wisdom as Tree of Life (Interim Metaphor)

"She is a tree of life to them that lay hold upon her."

Between the blocking of access at Gen 3:24 and the restoration at Rev 22:2, the tree of life survives as a metaphor in wisdom literature. The phrase ets hayyim persists through Proverbs (3:18, 11:30, 13:12, 15:4), keeping the image alive during the long absence of the literal tree.

## FA04. The Ouk Eti Cluster: Systematic Fall-Reversal

### Rev 21:1 — Sea No More

"And there was no more sea."

Construction: he thalassa ouk estin eti — present tense of eimi (G1510) + ouk + eti (G2089). The sea (thalassa, G2281) carries chaos associations in ancient cosmology (cf. the sea beast of Rev 13:1). Its removal is the first ouk eti statement in the new creation passage.

### Rev 21:4 — Fourfold Removal

"There shall be no more death, neither sorrow, nor crying, neither shall there be any more pain: for the former things are passed away."

The Greek contains two ouk estai eti constructions bracketing a triple oute chain:
1. ho thanatos ouk estai eti — death shall be no more (G2288, future of eimi + ouk + eti)
2. oute penthos (G3997) — neither sorrow
3. oute krauge (G2906) — neither crying
4. oute ponos (G4192) ouk estai eti — neither pain shall be any more

The closing phrase ta prota apelthan uses the same verb aperchomai (G565, aorist 3rd plural) as 21:1 — "the former things passed away," identical grammar to "the first heaven and earth passed away." This verbal identity binds the two verses: the passing of the old creation (21:1) and the passing of suffering (21:4) are described in the same terms.

Each of the four items corresponds to a Genesis 3 consequence:
- Death — Gen 3:19 (unto dust shalt thou return)
- Sorrow (penthos) — Gen 3:16 (in sorrow thou shalt bring forth) and 3:17 (in sorrow shalt thou eat)
- Crying (krauge) — the outcry of pain's expression
- Pain (ponos) — Gen 3:16-17 (itstsabon — toil/pain)

### Rev 22:3 — Curse No More

"And there shall be no more curse."

Construction: pan katathema (G2652) ouk estai eti — every cursed thing shall be no more. Katathema is an NT hapax — it appears nowhere else in the NT. The pan (every/all) makes the removal comprehensive. This reverses Gen 3:17: epikataratos he ge — cursed is the ground. Different Greek compound but same root concept. The curse that entered at the Fall is not merely forgiven or transferred but ceases to exist.

### Rev 22:5 — Night No More

"And there shall be no night there."

Construction: nyx (G3571) ouk estai eti — night shall be no more. Night, the period of darkness and diminished communion, is abolished. This connects to the light-vs-darkness thread: Rev 21:23 (no need of sun — God's glory illuminates) and Gen 1:4-5 (evening and morning sequence). The new creation transcends the day-night cycle.

### Rev 21:25 — No Night (Parallel Statement)

"For there shall be no night there."

A parallel statement to 22:5: nyx ouk estai ekei. The gates are never shut because there is no night — the rationale connects permanent open access to the abolition of darkness.

### Rev 18:21-23 — Babylon's Sevenfold Removal (Structural Mirror)

"Shall be found no more at all... shall be heard no more at all... shall shine no more at all..."

Babylon's ou me...eti construction removes seven life-elements: the city itself, harpers/musicians, craftsmen, millstone, lamp-light, bridegroom/bride voices. Same negation pattern, opposite objects. Babylon loses life; the new creation loses suffering. The structural mirror confirms that the ouk eti cluster is deliberate literary architecture, not incidental phrasing.

### Rev 20:14 — Death Destroyed (Precondition)

"Death and hell were cast into the lake of fire."

The destruction of death and Hades at 20:14 is the precondition for the declaration at 21:4 that death shall be no more. Paul's identification of death as "the last enemy" (1 Cor 15:26) means everything after death's destruction is deathless reality.

### Isa 25:8 — Death Swallowed (OT Source)

"He will swallow up death in victory."

Billa hammavet lanetsach — God swallows up death forever. Quoted by Paul at 1 Cor 15:54. This is the OT promise that Rev 21:4 fulfills. The wiping of tears from all faces appears in the same Isaiah verse and at Rev 21:4.

## FA05. Babylon-Bride Mirror Antithesis (IC182)

### Rev 21:10 and Rev 17:3 — En Pneumati Transport (Binding Formula)

Both visions open with the same formula: apophero (G667) + en Pneumati (G4151). John is carried away in the Spirit — to the wilderness to see Babylon (17:3) and to a great high mountain to see New Jerusalem (21:10). These are the ONLY two NT occurrences of apophero with en Pneumati. The formula binds Visions 3 and 4 as the terminal pair of Revelation's four-vision structure.

The destinations mirror each other: wilderness (desolation) vs. great high mountain (elevation). The same angel group introduces both visions (one of the seven bowl angels).

### Rev 21:3 and Rev 18:2 — Tabernacle of God vs. Habitation of Demons

Babylon: katoiketerion (G2732) daimonion — habitation of demons. New Jerusalem: skene (G4633) tou theou — tabernacle of God. Different Greek word families describe each city's inhabitant. The katoiketerion/skene contrast is not accidental — Revelation selects distinct vocabulary for each city's indwelling presence.

### Rev 21:23 and Rev 18:23 — Lamb as Lamp vs. Lamp Extinguished

Babylon: phos lychnou (G3088) ou me phane en soi eti — light of lamp shall shine no more in you. New Jerusalem: ho lychnos autes to arnion — the Lamb is its lamp. Same lychnos vocabulary. In Babylon, the lamp is extinguished forever. In New Jerusalem, the Lamb IS the lamp — divine persons replace created light sources.

### Rev 22:1 and Rev 17:6 — River of Life vs. Blood of Saints

The harlot is drunk with the blood (haima) of saints (17:6). New Jerusalem offers the potamon hydatos zoes — river of water of life (22:1). Blood consumed vs. water freely given. Death absorbed vs. life flowing.

### Rev 22:4 and Rev 17:5 — God's Name vs. Babylon's Name on Foreheads

Babylon: name written on forehead — MYSTERY BABYLON THE GREAT (17:5). New Jerusalem: God's name on foreheads (22:4). The forehead inscription reverses. The OT source for the redeemed forehead is the high priest's gold plate: HOLINESS TO THE LORD (Exod 28:36). What was limited to one priest now extends to all inhabitants.

## FA06. Overcomer Promise Fulfillment Chain (SP029)

### Rev 21:7 — Overcomer Capstone

"He that overcometh shall inherit all things; and I will be his God, and he shall be my son."

Ho nikon (G3528, present active participle — the one presently overcoming) kleronomeo (G2816) tauta panta. The verb shift is significant: the seven letters use doso (future of didomi — I will give). The capstone at 21:7 uses kleronomeo — shall inherit. The overcomer moves from gift-receiver to heir. The sonship formula (I will be his God, he shall be my son) echoes 2 Sam 7:14 — the Davidic covenant promise now extended to every overcomer.

### The Seven Fulfillments

| Promise | Church | Content | Fulfillment |
|---------|--------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Ephesus (2:7) | Tree of life in paradise | Rev 22:2 — tree of life bearing twelve fruits |
| 2 | Smyrna (2:11) | Not hurt by second death | Rev 21:7-8 — overcomers inherit; non-overcomers face second death |
| 3 | Pergamos (2:17) | Hidden manna, new name | Rev 22:4 — God's name on foreheads |
| 4 | Thyatira (2:26-28) | Authority over nations, morning star | Rev 22:5 — reign forever; 22:16 — bright morning star |
| 5 | Sardis (3:5) | White garments, name in book of life | Rev 21:27 — only those in Lamb's book of life enter |
| 6 | Philadelphia (3:12) | Pillar in naos, New Jerusalem name | Rev 21:22 — God IS the naos; 21:2 — New Jerusalem descends |
| 7 | Laodicea (3:21) | Sit with me on my throne | Rev 22:3 — throne in the city; 22:5 — they shall reign |

Five of these seven converge within Rev 22:1-5 (promises 1, 3, 4, 6, 7). This is the densest structural convergence in the book.

### Rev 21:8 — Antithetical List

"But the fearful, and unbelieving, and the abominable... shall have their part in the lake which burneth with fire and brimstone: which is the second death."

The structural counterpart to 21:7. Eight categories of the excluded. The second death (ho thanatos ho deuteros) answers overcomer promise 2 (Smyrna, 2:11) from the negative side: the overcomer is protected from the second death; the non-overcomer faces it.

## FA07. Genesis-Revelation Bookend Pairs: Escalation Beyond Eden

### Seven Reversal Pairs

**Pair 1: Tree of Life** (A-Verbal Identity)
Gen 2:9 ets hayyim → LXX xylon tes zoes → Rev 22:2 xylon zoes.
Verbatim Greek phrase across the canon. But the tree escalates: one tree becomes a grove, seasonal fruit becomes monthly, leaves for two people become healing for nations.

**Pair 2: River** (C-Thematic)
Gen 2:10 potamos exeporeueto ek tou Eden → Rev 22:1 potamon ekporeuomenon ek tou thronou.
Same noun and verb root, different source. Eden's land yields to God's throne.

**Pair 3: Curse Reversal** (B-Verbal Echo)
Gen 3:17 epikataratos he ge → Rev 22:3 pan katathema ouk estai eti.
Different Greek compound (epikataratos vs. katathema) but same root concept (kata + curse). The reversal is total: pan (every) katathema.

**Pair 4: Face of God** (C-Thematic)
Gen 3:8 — hiding from God's prosopon → Rev 22:4 — they shall see His prosopon.
The direction reverses: fleeing becomes seeing. The face that drove fear now draws worship.

**Pair 5: Creation Assessment** (C-Thematic)
Gen 1:31 kalon lian (very good) → Rev 21:5 kaina poio panta (I make all things new).
Original creation assessed; new creation declared. The present tense poio (I am making) indicates ongoing creative renewal, not a single completed act.

**Pair 6: Serpent** (B-Verbal Echo)
Gen 3:1 ophis → Rev 12:9/20:2 ophis archaios — the ancient serpent explicitly identified.
The serpent that initiated the Fall is named and destroyed.

**Pair 7: Marriage** (C-Thematic)
Gen 2:23-24 → Rev 19:7-9/21:2,9.
Eden's first marriage bookends with the Lamb's marriage to the bride-city.

### Genuinely New Elements (No Genesis Parallel)

Several features of Rev 21-22 have no Eden counterpart:
- The Lamb on the throne (22:1,3)
- Twelve apostle foundations (21:14)
- Twelve gates with tribal names (21:12)
- Nations bringing glory (21:24,26)
- Cubic city dimensions (21:16)
- The number twelve saturating every measurement

These additions confirm that the new creation is not a return to Eden but an elevation beyond it. The spiral ascends.

## FA08. Cosmic MHP: The City as Most Holy Place

### Rev 21:16 — Tetragonos (NT Hapax)

"The city lieth foursquare, and the length is as large as the breadth... The length and the breadth and the height of it are equal."

Tetragonos (G5068) — foursquare — appears nowhere else in the NT. Equal length, breadth, and height produce a perfect cube: 12,000 stadia on each side.

### 1 Kgs 6:20 — Solomon's MHP

"Twenty cubits in length, and twenty cubits in breadth, and twenty cubits in the height thereof."

The MHP: 20 x 20 x 20 cubits — a perfect cube. This is the ONLY other perfect cube in Scripture. The MHP was the innermost room, entered only once a year by the high priest on Yom Kippur. God's presence dwelt between the cherubim above the ark.

### The Connection

When Rev 21:22 says there is no naos in the city, it does not mean sacred space has been eliminated. It means the entire city IS the sacred space. The cubic proportions declare that every location within New Jerusalem is Most Holy Place — every inhabitant stands in the immediate presence of God. The veil (Exod 26:33-34) that separated Holy from Most Holy no longer exists. Access is universal and permanent.

### Rev 21:12-14 — Twelve-Saturation

Twelve gates, twelve angels, twelve tribes, twelve foundations, twelve apostles. Add to this: twelve thousand stadia, 144 (12x12) cubits wall, twelve stones, twelve pearls, twelve monthly fruits. Twelve is the covenant number in Revelation, and it is exclusively positive — no hostile entity receives a twelve-association. The saturation marks New Jerusalem as the ultimate covenant space.

## FA09. Kainos vs Neos: New in Quality vs New in Time

### Rev 21:1 — Ouranon Kainon kai Gen Kainen

Kainos (G2537) appears twice in the opening verse: ouranon kainon (accusative masculine) and gen kainen (accusative feminine). Both modify their nouns with qualitative newness.

### Rev 21:5 — Kaina Poio Panta

"Behold, I make all things new."

The parsing is critical: kaina (G2537, accusative plural neuter) poio (G4160, present active indicative 1st singular) panta (G3956, accusative plural neuter). The present tense poio — "I am making" — describes an ongoing creative act, not a single completed event. The word is kaina, not nea. He is not making brand-new replacement things; he is making all things new in quality.

### Neos Absent from Revelation

Neos (G3501) does not appear anywhere in Revelation. Its 24 NT occurrences are concentrated in the Synoptics (new wine, new cloth) and Paul (new lump, new man in temporal sense). The complete absence from Revelation, combined with kainos appearing seven times (2:17, 3:12, 5:9, 14:3, 21:1, 21:2, 21:5), confirms the vocabulary selection is deliberate. The new creation is kainos — qualitatively renewed — not neos — temporally brand new.

### The Hebrew Background

Chadash (H2319) bridges to kainos in the LXX 38 of 48 times (PMI 8.73). Isa 65:17 uses both bara (H1254, divine creation verb) and chadash: hinni bore shamayim chadashim — I create new heavens. The LXX renders chadash as kainos, and Rev 21:1 follows suit.

### Aperchomai at 21:1 and 21:4

The same verb aperchomai (G565) governs both passages. At 21:1: ho protos ouranos kai he prote ge apelthan — the first heaven and earth passed away. At 21:4: ta prota apelthan — the former things passed away. Pain does not cease by being replaced with a different pain; it is transformed into its absence. The verbal identity suggests the passing of heaven/earth operates on the same principle: transformation rather than annihilation.

## FA10. Rev 21:3 Skene tou Theou: Permanent Tabernacles-Antitype

### Rev 21:3 — Double Sken- Root

"Behold, the tabernacle (skene, G4633) of God is with men, and he will dwell (skenosei, G4637) with them."

This is the ONLY NT verse where the noun skene and the verb skenoo co-occur. The double root is Tabernacles-specific vocabulary. The voice comes ek tou thronou (from the throne) — the announcement originates from God's own seat.

### The Five Skenoo Occurrences

All five are Johannine:
1. **John 1:14** — eskenosen (aorist): the Word tabernacled among us. Incarnation.
2. **Rev 7:15** — skenosei (future): He shall tabernacle over them. Proleptic Tabernacles.
3. **Rev 12:12** — skenountes (present participle): those tabernacling in heaven.
4. **Rev 13:6** — skenountas (present participle): the beast blasphemes those tabernacling.
5. **Rev 21:3** — skenosei (future): He shall tabernacle with them. Permanent dwelling.

The inclusio from John 1:14 (incarnation aorist) to Rev 21:3 (consummation future) brackets the entire Johannine corpus with the tabernacling motif.

### Hebrew Background

The phonetic pathway: shakan (H7931, to dwell) → Shekinah (the divine presence-glory) → skene/skenoo. The Feast of Tabernacles (sukkot, from sukkah H5521 → skene G4633 in LXX) commanded temporary dwelling in booths (Lev 23:42-43) as a memorial of the wilderness period. Rev 21:3 transforms this temporary memorial into permanent reality. The commanded seven days become eternity.

## FA11. Curse Removal: Katathema/Katara Reversal Chain

### The Three-Stage Chain

**Stage 1 — Curse Imposed (Gen 3:14, 17)**
Gen 3:14: arur (H779) — the serpent is cursed. LXX: epikataratos (G1944).
Gen 3:17: arur ha-adamah — cursed is the ground. LXX: epikataratos he ge.
The formal curse verb arur appears in the Qal passive participle. The curse falls on the serpent (3:14), the ground (3:17), and later Cain (4:11). It defines the human condition for the remainder of Scripture.

**Stage 2 — Curse Borne (Gal 3:13)**
"Christ hath redeemed us from the curse (katara, G2671) of the law, being made a curse for us."
Paul quotes Deut 21:23 connecting the xylon (tree/cross) to the curse. The intermediate stage: the curse is borne by Christ but not yet removed from creation.

**Stage 3 — Curse Removed (Rev 22:3)**
pan katathema (G2652) ouk estai eti — every cursed thing shall be no more.
Katathema is an NT hapax. Its rarity matches the uniqueness of the event it describes. The construction: pan (nominative neuter singular of pas — every) + katathema (nominative neuter singular) + ouk estai (future of eimi + negative) + eti (adverb — any longer). Permanent, comprehensive cessation.

### Gen 4:11 — Curse Extension

"And now art thou cursed from the earth."

The curse extends from the ground (3:17) to a person (4:11). The escalation of the curse through Genesis is reversed by the comprehensive pan katathema at Rev 22:3 — every cursed thing, without exception.

## FA12. Restoration vs Replacement: What the Structural Evidence Says

### Evidence for Restoration/Transformation

1. **Kainos not neos**: Consistent use of qualitative newness vocabulary throughout.
2. **Same aperchomai verb**: 21:1 and 21:4 use the same verb for both the passing of heaven/earth and the passing of pain/sorrow. Pain does not cease by replacement but by transformation into its absence.
3. **Tree of life persists**: A botanical entity from Genesis continues into the new creation, suggesting continuity.
4. **Nations bring glory**: Rev 21:24,26 — identities carried through the transition.
5. **Ginomai arc grammar**: The progressive perfect (egeneto → gegonen → gegonan) tracks becoming, not destroying-and-restarting.
6. **2 Cor 5:17 parallel**: Individual new creation (kaine ktisis) operates by transformation of the same person, not replacement with a different one.

### Evidence for Strong Discontinuity

1. **Pheugo at 20:11**: Earth and heaven "fled" (ephugen, G5343) — strong departure language.
2. **2 Pet 3:10**: Elements dissolving (luthesontai), earth burned up (katakasetai) — intense destruction language.

### Assessment

The structural evidence within Revelation tilts toward transformation. The vocabulary is consistently kainos, the verb governing the transition (aperchomai) operates the same way for both cosmic elements and experiential suffering, and the ginomai arc traces becoming rather than replacement. Peter's language (2 Pet 3:10) is the strongest counter-evidence, but Peter himself uses kainos for the resulting creation (3:13), suggesting the destruction language describes the process while kainos describes the outcome.

### Rev 21:5 — The Decisive Statement

"Behold, I make all things new."

Poio (present active) governs kaina panta. The verb is "make" (poieo) not "create from nothing" (a concept that would use ktizo or bara). And it is present tense — an ongoing making-new, not a single act of replacement. This is the seated one's own characterization of what is happening. The structural evidence aligns with it.

## Additional Verses

### Rev 21:2 — New Jerusalem Descending

"The holy city, new Jerusalem, coming down from God out of heaven, prepared as a bride adorned for her husband."

Kainen Ierousalem — the third kainos occurrence in two verses. The city-bride dual identity (nymphen kekosmemenen — bride adorned, perfect passive participle) establishes New Jerusalem as both architectural structure and covenantal relationship. It descends FROM God — the origin is heavenly, not earthly construction.

### Rev 21:6 — Gegonan (Ginomai Arc Terminus)

"It is done. I am Alpha and Omega."

Gegonan (G1096, perfect active indicative 3rd person plural) — the eschatological perfect declaring that the events have been completed. Third station of the ginomai arc: egeneto (proleptic aorist, 11:15) → gegonen (perfect singular, 16:17) → gegonan (perfect plural, 21:6). The grammar tracks progressive realization culminating in completed transformation. Alpha and Omega — the self-designation frames the entire arc as intentional from beginning to end.

### Rev 21:9 — Angel Introduction Mirror

"One of the seven angels which had the seven vials... Come hither, I will shew thee the bride."

Same introductory pattern as 17:1. Same angel group (from the seven bowls). "Come hither" formula. The structural mirror is exact: what 17:1 introduces (the harlot), 21:9 inverts (the bride).

### Rev 21:23 — Divine Light

"The glory of God did lighten it, and the Lamb is the light thereof."

The doxa tou theou provides illumination. The Lamb IS the lychnos (lamp). Same vocabulary as 18:23 (Babylon's extinguished lamp) with opposite outcome. In Babylon, the lamp goes dark forever. In New Jerusalem, the Lamb replaces all created light.

### Rev 21:24 — Nations Bring Glory

"The nations of them which are saved shall walk in the light of it: and the kings of the earth do bring their glory and honour into it."

A genuinely new element with no Eden parallel. Nations walking by divine light and bringing their glory into the city implies ongoing identity, activity, and purpose carried through the transition. This verse supports the restoration reading and demonstrates escalation beyond Eden (there were no nations in the garden).

### Rev 21:27 — Access Criterion

"There shall in no wise enter into it any thing that defileth... but they which are written in the Lamb's book of life."

The access criterion: book of life enrollment. Fulfills overcomer promise 5 (Sardis, 3:5) — name not blotted from the book of life. The double negative ou me (by no means) + negative list (defiles, works abomination, makes a lie) defines exclusion.

### Rev 22:4 — They Shall See His Face

"And they shall see his face; and his name shall be in their foreheads."

The prosopon (G4383) bookend: Gen 3:8 (hiding from God's face) → Rev 22:4 (seeing God's face). The reversal spans the entire canon. His name on their foreheads reverses Babylon's forehead inscription (17:5) and fulfills the high priest's gold plate (Exod 28:36) — priestly privilege universalized.

### Rev 22:5 — Eternal Reign

"They shall reign for ever and ever."

Basileusousin eis tous aionas ton aionon — future active indicative 3rd person plural. The millennial reign (20:4,6) extends into eternity (22:5). Five of seven overcomer promises converge in 22:1-5. This is the passage's culminating declaration: the redeemed do not merely inhabit the new creation but reign within it.

### Isa 65:17 — OT Source

"Behold, I create new heavens and a new earth."

Bara (H1254, Qal participle — divine creation verb) + chadash (H2319). The LXX renders chadash as kainos (G2537). Rev 21:1 follows the LXX vocabulary. Isaiah provides the promise; Revelation declares its fulfillment.

### 2 Pet 3:13 — Bridge Text

"New heavens and a new earth, wherein dwelleth righteousness."

Kainous ouranous kai gen kainen — same kainos vocabulary as Rev 21:1. Peter bridges Isa 65:17 to Rev 21:1, using kainos for the outcome even after describing the process in dissolution language (3:10).

### 2 Cor 5:17 — Individual New Creation Parallel

"If any man be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away; behold, all things are become new."

Kaine ktisis — new creation. Ta archaia parelthen — old things passed away (same verb root as Rev 21:1). Panta gegonen kaina — all things have become new (perfect of ginomai + kainos). The individual regeneration operates by transformation: the person is not replaced with a different person but made new in quality. This provides a hermeneutical template for the cosmic new creation.

### John 1:14 — Incarnation Tabernacling

"The Word was made flesh, and dwelt among us."

Eskenosen (G4637, aorist active indicative 3rd singular) en hemin — tabernacled among us. The incarnation use of skenoo. This is the beginning of the inclusio that closes at Rev 21:3 skenosei. The Johannine corpus opens with God tabernacling temporarily in flesh and closes with God tabernacling permanently with humanity.

### Lev 23:42-43 — Feast of Tabernacles Command

"Ye shall dwell in booths seven days... that your generations may know that I made the children of Israel to dwell in booths."

The sukkot (H5521) command: temporary dwelling as memorial. Rev 21:3 transforms the temporary memorial into permanent reality and the commanded seven days into eternity.

### Gal 3:13 — Intermediate Curse-Bearing

"Christ hath redeemed us from the curse of the law, being made a curse for us."

Katara (G2671) — the intermediate stage. Christ bore the curse. The cross (xylon, tree — quoting Deut 21:23) connects the curse to the tree, which connects to both the tree of the Fall (Gen 2:17) and the tree of life restored (Rev 22:2).

### Exod 26:33-34 — Veil Separating Holy from Most Holy

"The vail shall divide unto you between the holy place and the most holy."

In the earthly sanctuary, the veil separated HP from MHP. In New Jerusalem, the entire city has MHP dimensions (Rev 21:16). The veil is gone — access is universal.

### Rev 11:15 — Ginomai Arc Station 1

"The kingdoms of this world are become the kingdoms of our Lord."

Egeneto (G1096, aorist) — proleptic aorist declaring the kingdom transfer as already accomplished. First station of the three-station ginomai arc.

### Isa 66:22-23 — Monthly Worship

"From one new moon to another, and from one sabbath to another, shall all flesh come to worship before me."

The new-moon-to-new-moon worship cycle in the new creation. This may correspond to Rev 22:2's twelve monthly fruits — a monthly rhythm of worship and provision persisting into eternity.

### Gen 1:31 — Very Good

"God saw every thing that he had made, and, behold, it was very good."

The original creation assessment. Rev 21:5 responds: not merely "very good" but kaina — all things made new. The new creation exceeds the original.

### Rev 20:11 — Heaven and Earth Flee

"From whose face the earth and the heaven fled away."

Ephugen (G5343, aorist of pheugo) — fled. Strong departure language. Sets up the need for 21:1's new heaven and new earth. The strongest language for discontinuity in the immediate context.
