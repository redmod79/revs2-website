# Word Studies

## Question
How does the throne room vision of Revelation 4-5 function as the structural anchor for the entire book? What sanctuary furniture, worship elements, and throne-room language recur throughout the book, and how do these recurrences connect later passages back to this foundational scene? Use the sanctuary blueprint from Exodus 25-40 to identify which compartment each piece of furniture belongs to. How does the throne room establish the context for the seals, trumpets, and bowls? What is the structural relationship between the throne room and the final vision of New Jerusalem (Rev 21-22)?

### G2362 — thronos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2362-thronos.md`

**Occurrences:** 61

Throne — masculine noun from thrao (to sit). 61 tokens in 50 unique NT verses. Revelation accounts for 36 verses (72% of all NT uses). Semantic domains: divine sovereignty, Messianic/Davidic throne, throne of grace, judgment throne, adversarial throne (Satan/beast). The dominant structural term in Revelation.

**Semantic categories:** divine sovereignty, Messianic throne, throne of grace, judgment throne, adversarial throne

**Key verses:** Rev 4:2, Rev 4:5, Rev 5:6, Rev 5:13, Rev 7:15, Rev 16:17, Rev 20:11, Rev 22:1, Rev 22:3

**Relevance:** Central organizing principle of Revelation. Thronos distribution spans all major sections: prologue (1:4), churches (2:13, 3:21), throne room (4-5), seals (6:16), interlude (7:9-17), trumpets (8:3, 9:13 implicit), cosmic narrative (12:5, 14:3), bowls (16:10, 16:17), final worship (19:4-5), millennium (20:4, 20:11), New Jerusalem (21:5, 22:1,3). The throne is the structural anchor from which the entire book radiates.

### G721 — arnion

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G721-arnion.md`

**Occurrences:** 30

Lamb — neuter noun diminutive of aren (G704). 30 tokens in 28 unique verses across 2 books (Revelation 27 verses, John 1 verse). Nearly exclusive to Revelation (96%). Grammatical distribution: genitive 14, nominative 7, dative 5, accusative 3, accusative plural 1.

**Semantic categories:** enthroned Lamb sovereign authority, slain Lamb sacrificial redemption, Lamb as worship object, Lamb as warrior-overcomer, Lamb-beast parody contrast

**Key verses:** Rev 5:6, Rev 5:12, Rev 7:17, Rev 13:8, Rev 17:14, Rev 21:22, Rev 22:1, Rev 22:3

**Relevance:** The Lamb is the dominant christological title in Revelation, introduced at 5:6 in the throne room. Arnion threads through the entire book from introduction (5:6) through seals (6:1, 6:16), interlude (7:9-17), cosmic conflict (12:11, 13:8, 13:11), eschatological victory (14:1-4, 15:3, 17:14), marriage (19:7-9), and New Jerusalem (21:9-22:3). The Lamb's permanent throne-room identity (standing as slain) governs every subsequent appearance.

### G2226 — zoon

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2226-zoon.md`

**Occurrences:** 23

Living creature — neuter noun from zao (to live). 23 NT occurrences total, 20 in Revelation (87%). The four living creatures around the throne combine Ezekiel 1 (four faces, eyes) and Isaiah 6 (six wings, trisagion). They appear in worship (4:6-9, 5:8-14), seal announcements (6:1,3,5,7), interludes (7:11, 14:3), bowl distribution (15:7), and final worship (19:4).

**Semantic categories:** throne room worship entities, judgment sequence participants, Ezekiel-Isaiah composite beings

**Key verses:** Rev 4:6-9, Rev 5:8-14, Rev 6:1, Rev 15:7, Rev 19:4

**Relevance:** Living creatures span all three judgment sequences: they announce the horsemen (seals, 6:1-7), they are present at the altar scene (trumpets implicit), they distribute the bowls (15:7). This makes them a structural thread from the throne room through every judgment series, anchoring each sequence to the foundational scene.

### G4245 — presbyteros

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4245-presbyteros.md`

**Occurrences:** 67

Elder — comparative adjective used as noun. 67 NT occurrences, ~12 in Revelation. The 24 elders function as a recurring doxological thread: worship at throne room initiation (4:4,10), respond to Lamb's worthiness (5:5-14), participate in interlude worship (7:11-13), celebrate seventh trumpet (11:16-18), present at Lamb's throne (14:3), final combined worship (19:4).

**Semantic categories:** throne room worship participants, doxological boundary markers, priestly representatives

**Key verses:** Rev 4:4, Rev 4:10-11, Rev 5:5-14, Rev 7:11-13, Rev 11:16-18, Rev 14:3, Rev 19:4

**Relevance:** The 24 elders appear at every major structural boundary in Revelation. Their worship content shifts tracking the book's theological progression: creation (4:11) -> redemption (5:9-10) -> judgment (11:17-18) -> final vindication (19:4). They derive from the 24 priestly courses (1 Chr 24:1-19) and wear stephanos (G4735, victor's wreaths) not diadema (G1238, royal crowns).

### G2379 — thysiastErion

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2379-thysiastErion.md`

**Occurrences:** 23

Altar — neuter noun. 23 NT occurrences, 8 in Revelation. The ONLY sanctuary furniture item that spans all three judgment sequences: seals (6:9), trumpets (8:3-5, 9:13), bowls (16:7). The altar vindication arc moves from passive reception of blood (6:9) to active voice of vindication (16:7).

**Semantic categories:** bronze altar sacrifice, golden altar incense, altar vindication arc

**Key verses:** Rev 6:9, Rev 8:3, Rev 8:5, Rev 9:13, Rev 14:18, Rev 16:7

**Relevance:** Thysiastērion is the most structurally significant sanctuary furniture term because it alone appears in all three judgment sequences. The altar vindication arc (6:9-10 -> 8:3-5 -> 9:13 -> 14:18 -> 16:7 -> 19:2) creates a closed loop from blood cry to vindication, with the altar progressing from passive to active to personified.

### G3485 — naos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3485-naos.md`

**Occurrences:** 46

Temple/inner shrine — masculine noun from naio (to dwell). 46 BLB count, 16 in Revelation. Distinguished from hieron (G2411, temple precincts) which has 0 occurrences in Revelation. The reader is positioned INSIDE the inner sanctuary throughout. Naos undergoes a trajectory from opened (11:19, 15:5) to negated then predicated (21:22).

**Semantic categories:** physical inner sanctuary, Christ's body as temple, believers as God's dwelling, heavenly temple, eschatological temple resolution

**Key verses:** Rev 11:19, Rev 15:5, Rev 15:8, Rev 16:1, Rev 16:17, Rev 21:22

**Relevance:** The naos/hieron distinction is critical: hieron (G2411) has 0 Revelation occurrences while naos has 16 — the reader is positioned INSIDE the inner sanctuary, not in the outer precincts. The naos trajectory tracks from opened (11:19) to filled with glory (15:8) to no temple needed (21:22) — the progressive temple-opening sequence's terminus.

### G3087 — lychnia

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3087-lychnia.md`

**Occurrences:** 12

Lampstand — feminine noun from lychnos (G3088). 12 tokens in 11 NT verses, 6 in Revelation. The lampstand is HP furniture (Exo 25:31-40, Heb 9:2). In Revelation concentrated in the churches section: Rev 1:12,13,20(x2), 2:1, 2:5, and 11:4. First element of the light-word triad.

**Semantic categories:** household lamp stand, tabernacle HP furnishing, symbol of seven churches

**Key verses:** Rev 1:12, Rev 1:13, Rev 1:20, Rev 2:1, Rev 2:5, Rev 11:4

**Relevance:** First element of the light-word triad: lychnia (lampstand, fixed HP furniture, churches section) -> lampas (torch, burning before throne, Rev 4:5) -> lychnos (portable lamp, negated in New Jerusalem). The non-overlapping distribution across Revelation's major sections suggests deliberate lexical planning.

### G2985 — lampas

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2985-lampas.md`

**Occurrences:** 9

Torch/lamp — feminine noun related to lampo (to shine). 9 NT occurrences across 4 books: Matthew 5, Revelation 2, John 1, Acts 1. In Revelation: 4:5 (seven lamps of fire = seven Spirits) and 8:10 (star burning as a lamp). Second element of the light-word triad.

**Semantic categories:** handheld lamp for procession, heavenly apocalyptic fire-lamp, military torch

**Key verses:** Rev 4:5, Rev 8:10

**Relevance:** Second element of the light-word triad. Rev 4:5 'seven lampades pyros burning before the throne, which are the seven Spirits of God' — the lampas connects to the Zechariah 4 decoder (seven lamps = eyes of the LORD = Spirit). The burning lamps before the throne mediate God's presence — they will become unnecessary when God himself is the light (21:23, 22:5).

**Occurrences:** 6

Ark — feminine noun. In Revelation appears only at 11:19 (hē kibōtos tēs diathēkēs autou — the ark of his covenant). MHP furniture. The ark's single appearance at the structural pivot of the seventh trumpet marks the transition to the most holy place imagery.

**Key verses:** Rev 11:19

**Relevance:** The ark appears at 11:19 — the seventh trumpet climax and the structural pivot of the book. Its visibility signals that the naos is fully opened, revealing the MHP contents. This is the Paulien framework's fourth sanctuary introduction, pointing to the Day of Atonement phase.

### G5357 — phiale

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5357-phiale.md`

**Occurrences:** 12

Bowl/vial — feminine noun. All 12 NT occurrences are in Revelation, making it a Revelation-exclusive term. Used for golden bowls of incense/prayers (5:8) and golden bowls of wrath (15:7, 16:1-17). The vessel transformation arc: phialas chrysas gemousas thymiamatōn (5:8) -> phialas chrysas gemousas tou thymou (15:7).

**Semantic categories:** prayer vessel, wrath vessel, vessel transformation

**Key verses:** Rev 5:8, Rev 15:7, Rev 16:1, Rev 16:17, Rev 21:9

**Relevance:** The vessel transformation arc is the strongest evidence for deliberate sanctuary progression (sanc-29). The same golden bowls that held prayers (5:8) now hold wrath (15:7). The phonetic link thymiama/thymos reinforces the transformation: incense (thymiama) becomes wrath (thymos), both from the root thyō (to sacrifice/burn). Revelation OWNS this term — 12/12 NT occurrences.

**Relevance:** The incense-prayer identification (5:8 hai eisin hai proseuchai tōn hagiōn) connects the throne room to the altar scene of 8:3-4. The phonetic relationship thymiama (incense) / thymos (wrath) mirrors the vessel transformation: prayers offered become judgments poured.

### G3031 — libanotos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3031-libanotos.md`

**Occurrences:** 2

Censer — masculine noun. Both NT occurrences in Rev 8 (8:3 and 8:5). The golden censer (libanoton chrysoun) is the vessel for both intercession (incense with prayers, 8:3-4) and judgment (filled with fire and hurled earthward, 8:5). A transitional vessel.

**Semantic categories:** sanctuary vessel, intercession-to-judgment transition

**Key verses:** Rev 8:3, Rev 8:5

**Relevance:** Both NT occurrences in Rev 8 — Revelation OWNS this term entirely. The censer transitions from intercession to judgment within the same scene: receives incense with prayers (8:3-4), then is filled with altar fire and cast into the earth (8:5), triggering the Sinai-theophany formula. This is the physical mechanism connecting throne-room prayer to earthly judgment.

### G4352 — proskyneo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4352-proskyneo.md`

**Occurrences:** 65

Worship — from pros + kyneo (to kiss toward). 65 NT occurrences. Tracks the proper-vs-improper worship contrast through Revelation: proper worship directed at God/Lamb on the throne (4:10, 5:14, 7:11, 11:16, 19:4), improper worship directed at beast/dragon (13:4, 13:8, 14:9-11), and warnings against angel worship (19:10, 22:8-9).

**Key verses:** Rev 4:10, Rev 5:14, Rev 7:11, Rev 11:16, Rev 13:4, Rev 14:7, Rev 19:4, Rev 19:10, Rev 22:9

**Relevance:** Worship functions as structural punctuation in Revelation. The pipto + proskyneō pattern recurs at 4:10, 5:14, 7:11, 11:16, 19:4 — each a structural boundary. The contrast between proper worship (throne-directed) and improper worship (beast-directed) is a major structural argument of the book, all anchored to the foundational worship scene of 4:8-11.

### G4969 — sphazo

**Occurrences:** 10

Slay/slaughter — primary verb. 10 NT occurrences (8 in Revelation, 1 in 1 John). The perfect passive participle esphagmenon is the defining feature of the Lamb at 5:6, expressing the permanent ongoing state of having been slaughtered. The same root describes martyrs (6:9 esphagmenōn) and parodies the beast (13:3 esphagmenēn).

**Semantic categories:** christological sacrificial slaughter, martyr slaughter, beast parody wound

**Key verses:** Rev 5:6, Rev 5:9, Rev 5:12, Rev 6:9, Rev 13:3, Rev 13:8

**Relevance:** The perfect participle esphagmenon at 5:6 defines the Lamb's permanent identity — standing yet bearing the marks of slaughter. This same vocabulary connects martyrs (6:9, same root) to the Lamb and creates the beast parody (13:3, same root). The sphazō word links the throne room (5:6) to seals (6:9) to cosmic conflict (13:3,8).

### G3088 — lychnos

In Revelation: 18:23 (Babylon's lamp no more), 21:23 (Lamb is the light/lamp), 22:5 (no need of lamp). Third element of the light-word triad.

The three terms have non-overlapping distributions: lychnia in Rev 1-2 + 11:4; lampas at 4:5 + 8:10; lychnos at 18:23, 21:23, 22:5. The progression from fixed institutional light to portable personal light to negated light (direct divine presence) tracks the sanctuary-to-no-sanctuary arc.

### G1457 — enkainizo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1457-enkainizo.md`

**Occurrences:** 2

Inaugurate — verb. ABSENT from Revelation (0 occurrences). This absence is significant: Rev 4-5 is not an inauguration scene. The inauguration verb never appears because the throne room depicts the ongoing reality of the heavenly sanctuary, not a one-time inaugural event.

**Semantic categories:** inauguration, dedication, consecration

**Relevance:** The absence of enkainizo from Revelation is evidence that Rev 4-5 should not be read as an inauguration scene. ### G4735 — stephanos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4735-stephanos.md`

**Occurrences:** 18

Victor's wreath — masculine noun. Distinguished from diadema (G1238, royal crown). The 24 elders wear stephanous chrysous (golden wreaths, 4:4) and cast them before the throne (4:10) — a victory-acknowledgment gesture, not royal abdication.

**Semantic categories:** victor's wreath, priestly honor, eschatological reward

**Key verses:** Rev 2:10, Rev 3:11, Rev 4:4, Rev 4:10

**Relevance:** The stephanos/diadema distinction at 4:4 identifies the elders as victors, not royalty. Christ wears diadēmata (19:12), not stephanoi. The elders' crown-casting at 4:10 is worship — acknowledging God as the source of their victory. This priestly-victor identity connects to 1 Chr 24:1-19 (24 priestly courses).

### G1238 — diadema

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1238-diadema.md`

**Occurrences:** 3

Royal crown/diadem — neuter noun. Distinguished from stephanos (victor's wreath). In Revelation: dragon wears 7 diadēmata (12:3), beast wears 10 diadēmata (13:1), Christ wears many diadēmata (19:12). Marks sovereign royal authority, not athletic victory.

**Semantic categories:** royal sovereignty, adversarial royal authority, Christological kingship

**Key verses:** Rev 12:3, Rev 13:1, Rev 19:12

**Relevance:** The diadema/stephanos contrast reinforces the throne-room structure: elders wear stephanoi (4:4) as victors who acknowledge God's sovereignty; Christ alone wears diadēmata (19:12) as King of kings. The adversarial system uses diadēmata for dragon (12:3) and beast (13:1) — parodying royal authority.
