Edothē (divine passive ×2): 'it was given to him' and 'power was given him' — the beast's military authority over the saints and universal dominion are both bounded by divine permission. The beast nikao (overcomes) the saints temporarily — same verb as the overcomer promises in letters, inverted.

### Num 1:1-4

**Notes:** The wilderness census structure: 12 tribes, one leader per tribe, all males from 20 years old numbered. This census structure (12 × counted men) is the OT numerical background for Rev 7:4-8's 12 tribes × 12,000 = 144,000. Numbers 1 establishes the tribal military census pattern that Revelation eschatologizes: the 144,000 are the eschatological Israel in formation for spiritual warfare (Rev 14:4).

---

### Rev 17:10

**Notes:** Seven kings sequential enumeration: 5 (fallen, past) + 1 (is, present) + 1 (not yet come, future) = 7 total. The seven-king royal sequence uses the same temporal structure as the divine temporal formula (was/is/is-to-come) but applied to historical succession. The short duration of the seventh king precedes the eighth (17:11), who is 'of the seven' — deriving from the complete sequence.

---

### Rev 19:12

**Notes:** Polla diademata (many crowns) — diadema arc stage 3 (revs2-19). Dragon had 7 diadems, beast had 10 diadems, Christ has polla (many, uncountable). The diadema arc: 7 → 10 → polla. The parody system escalates in specific quantities; the original transcends quantity. Diadema (G1238): 3 NT occurrences, all in Revelation, all in this arc. Christ's diadems are on his head (singular, unified); dragon/beast diadems distributed across multiple heads/horns.

---

### Rev 11:16

**Notes:** 24 elders worship at the seventh trumpet (11:15-19). Fourth of the 7 joint appearances of 24 elders + 4 creatures (revs2-17). The 24 elders appear at every major structural boundary: throne room inauguration (4:4), Lamb's investiture (5:8), first seal interlude (7:11,13), seventh trumpet (11:16), harvest scene (14:3), Babylon's fall (19:4). Presbyteros (G4245) appears exactly 12 times in Revelation — all for the 24 elders.

### Ps 90:4

**Notes:** OT anchor for 1000 as divine time-perspective, not literal chronological duration. A thousand years to God = one day that has passed. This verse (along with 2 Pet 3:8) contextualizes the millennium as a divine time-unit rather than a strict 365,000-day calendar count. The theological point: the millennium is bounded and complete, not unending — it has both a beginning (Satan bound) and an end (Satan loosed).

---

### 2 Pet 3:8

**Notes:** NT application of Ps 90:4. Chilia etē (G5507 + G2094) here = thousand years (same compound as Rev 20). Peter uses this to contextualize divine timing: God's scale is not human chronology. The thousand-year millennium in Rev 20 is a divinely complete period — 10³ = fullness of time — not necessarily a literal 1000-year calendar. The chilia formula in 2 Pet 3:8 is the closest NT parallel to the Rev 20 formula.

---

### Luk 4:25

**Notes:** Tria etē kai menas hex = three years and six months (plain Greek expression). This is the 'plain form' deliberately avoided in all prophetic 3.5 expressions (revs2-18): Revelation never uses 'three years and six months' as a formula. Luke's plain form (historical Elijah drought period) distinguishes from prophetic usage which always uses 42 months, 1260 days, or time+times+half. The contrast confirms the prophetic forms are deliberate coded idioms.

---

### Jas 5:17

**Notes:** Second NT plain-form reference: three years and six months for Elijah's drought. James uses the same plain form as Luke 4:25. Both NT references to the 3.5-year Elijah drought use the plain grammatical form. Revelation's three prophetic 3.5 expressions (42 months, 1260 days, time+times+half) never use this form — the distinction is systematic and intentional, confirming the prophetic forms carry theological meaning beyond the bare duration.

---

### Ezek 1:5-10

**Notes:** OT source for Revelation's four living creatures. Ezekiel's four living creatures: four faces (man, lion, ox, eagle) and four wings each. Rev 4:6-8 adapts: four creatures with faces of lion, calf, man, eagle (same four faces, different order) and six wings (vs. Ezekiel's four). The four-faced structure = four-fold coverage of all creation categories. The Ezekiel creatures are the OT prototype of Revelation's tessares zoa.

### Rev 14:20

**Notes:** 1,600 stadia = 40² (forty squared). Hexakosioi (G1812) = 600 here; combined with 1,000 = 1,600. The mathematical structure: 40 = 4 × 10, so 40² = 4² × 10² = 16 × 100 = 1,600. The number 1,600 uses the square of 40 (the number of testing/judgment in biblical pattern) applied at the cosmic scale of final harvest judgment. It also = 4 × 400, where 4 is creation-universality. The spatial extent of the judgment harvest is encoded in a squared compound number.

---

### Rev 9:16

**Notes:** Two hundred million (dismyriades myriadōn = 2 × 10,000 × 10,000 = 200,000,000). G706 arithmos used twice in one verse (parallel to 13:18's triple arithmos). The army of the sixth trumpet: an incomprehensibly large force. This hyperbolic military number uses myriades (ten-thousands) as the base unit. Contrast with the 144,000 (precisely counted, sealed) — this army is also 'numbered' (John heard arithmon) but the scale points to created-order finality.

---

### Zech 3:9

**Notes:** Stone with seven eyes — OT seven-eyes motif. In Zech 3:9, the single stone has seven eyes (divine omniscience in sevenfold completeness). This feeds directly into Rev 5:6: the Lamb has seven eyes 'which are the seven Spirits of God sent forth into all the earth.' The seven-eyes symbolism is decoded in Zech 4:10 ('these seven — they are the eyes of the LORD, which run to and fro through the whole earth'). Rev 5:6 combines the seven eyes of Zech 3:9 with the seven-menorah lamp identification of Zech 4.
