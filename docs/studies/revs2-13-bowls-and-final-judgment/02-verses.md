# Verses

## Question
How does the bowl prelude (Rev 15:1-8) establish a Day of Atonement typology? How do the seven bowls compare systematically with the seven trumpets — shared target domains and escalation from 1/3 to total? What does the seventh bowl's gegonen ("It is done") declare? How does Rev 16:15 and the Armageddon gathering establish the bowls as pre-advent events?

### Opening sign: seven last plagues, wrath filled up (etelesthe)

> And I saw another sign in heaven, great and marvellous, seven angels having the seven last plagues; for in them is filled up the wrath of God.

**Context:** John sees a new heavenly sign introducing the bowl sequence. The word 'last' (eschatas) and 'filled up' (etelesthe, aorist passive of teleo) frame these plagues as the final, completing series of divine judgment.

**Notes:** etelesthe (V-API-3S, aorist passive indicative of teleo G5055) — 'was filled up/completed.' The wrath is viewed as already completed from the heavenly perspective. eschatas (G2078) qualifies these as the ultimate plagues. teleo root forms an inclusio with telesthosin at 15:8.

### Sea of glass mingled with fire — victors standing on it

> And I saw as it were a sea of glass mingled with fire: and them that had gotten the victory over the beast, and over his image, and over his mark, [and] over the number of his name, stand on the sea of glass, having the harps of God.

**Context:** The victorious saints stand on (epi with accusative) the sea of glass, which has gained fire since its first appearance in 4:6 (where it had no fire). The Red Sea crossing typology: Israelites standing on the far shore after Egypt's destruction.

**Notes:** The sea of glass gains fire between 4:6 and 15:2 (TM103). Victory fourfold: over beast, image, mark, number. Harps of God connect to the Song of Moses/Lamb in v.3.

### Song of Moses and of the Lamb — dual Exodus/Deuteronomy allusion

> And they sing the song of Moses the servant of God, and the song of the Lamb, saying, Great and marvellous [are] thy works, Lord God Almighty; just and true [are] thy ways, thou King of saints. Who shall not fear thee, O Lord, and glorify thy name? for [thou] only [art] holy: for all nations shall come and worship before thee; for thy judgments are made manifest.

**Context:** The dual genitive 'the song of Moses...and the song of the Lamb' presents one song belonging to two traditions. 'Great and marvellous' echoes Exo 15:11; 'just and true are thy ways' echoes Deut 32:4.

**Notes:** VP106 (Song of the Sea parallel, Strong, 5 links) and VP107 (Deut 32 parallel, Strong, 5 links) documented in revs-22. Cross-testament parallels found: Deut 32:44, 31:30, Exo 15:1 all score highly.

### Temple of the tabernacle of the testimony opened — unique triple genitive

> And after that I looked, and, behold, the temple of the tabernacle of the testimony in heaven was opened:

**Context:** The unique phrase naos tes skenes tou martyriou (triple genitive) appears nowhere else in the NT. It combines three sanctuary terms: naos (inner sanctuary), skene (tabernacle/tent), martyrion (testimony). The opening introduces the bowl prelude proper.

**Notes:** TM100 — naos (G3485, N-NSM) + skenes (G4633, N-GSF) + martyriou (G3142, N-GSN). Triple genitive chain identifies the heavenly sanctuary with maximum specificity. anoigo (G455, V-2API-3S aorist passive) — the temple is opened by divine action.

### Seven angels in priestly DOA attire — linen garments, golden girdles

> And the seven angels came out of the temple, having the seven plagues, clothed in pure and white linen, and having their breasts girded with golden girdles.

**Context:** Angels emerge from the naos wearing priestly garments. 'Pure and white linen' parallels Lev 16:4 DOA garments. 'Golden girdles' match Christ's description in Rev 1:13.

**Notes:** VP108 (DOA garment parallel with Lev 16:4, 4 shared elements). VP109 (golden girdle matching Rev 1:13 Christ description). The priestly attire marks these as sanctuary agents performing a ritual action.

### Golden bowls full of wrath — vessel transformation from 5:8

> And one of the four beasts gave unto the seven angels seven golden vials full of the wrath of God, who liveth for ever and ever.

**Context:** The same golden bowls (phialas chrysas) that held prayers/incense in 5:8 now hold wrath. The phonetic wordplay thymiamaton (incense, G2368) → thymou (wrath, G2372) signals the content transformation.

**Notes:** Parsed: phialas (G5357, N-APF) chrysas (G5552, A-APF) gemousas (G1073, V-PAP-APF) tou thymou (G2372, N-GSM) — exactly parallel to 5:8 phialas chrysas gemousas thymiamaton. SP119 vessel transformation arc. One of the four living creatures distributes the bowls, connecting judgment to the throne room.

### Temple filled with smoke, no entry — Lev 16:17 DOA parallel

> And the temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God, and from his power; and no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues of the seven angels were fulfilled.

**Context:** The key DOA typology verse. Four elements parallel Lev 16:17: (a) exclusion of persons (oudeis edynato eiselthein), (b) from the sanctuary space (eis ton naon), (c) during a specific ritual action (the seven plagues), (d) temporal limit (achri telesthosin). Competing parallels: Exo 40:34-35 (glory filling at inauguration) lacks the temporal 'until' clause; Isa 6:4 (smoke) lacks the exclusion clause.

**Notes:** telesthosin (G5055, V-APS-3P, aorist passive subjunctive) — 'were fulfilled/completed.' Forms inclusio with etelesthe at 15:1. naos appears twice (N-NSM subject, N-ASM object of eiselthein). The dual ek source (ek tes doxes + ek tes dynameōs) specifies the source of the smoke. Critical: Rev 4-5 has NO exclusion — the first exclusion comes at 15:8, supporting DOA rather than inauguration reading.

### Great voice from temple commanding bowl outpouring

> And I heard a great voice out of the temple saying to the seven angels, Go your ways, and pour out the vials of the wrath of God upon the earth.

**Context:** A great voice from the naos (since no one can enter, the voice is presumably divine) commands the sevenfold outpouring. ekcheo (G1632) is the characteristic bowl verb, parallel to salpizo (G4537) for trumpets.

**Notes:** The voice comes ek tou naou — from within the temple that no one can enter. This implies divine origin. The imperative 'pour out' (ekcheate) initiates the formulaic sequence. SP057 — characteristic verb correspondence: esalpisen for trumpets, execheen for bowls.

### Bowl 1 — sore/helkos on beast-worshippers, Exodus boils parallel

> And the first went, and poured out his vial upon the earth; and there fell a noisome and grievous sore upon the men which had the mark of the beast, and [upon] them which worshipped his image.

**Context:** First bowl targets the earth (ge, G1093), same domain as trumpet 1 (8:7). helkos (G1668, N-NSN) — 'sore/ulcer' parallels the Egyptian boils plague (Exo 9:8-12, LXX uses helkos for shechyin H7822). The plague is selective: only beast-worshippers.

**Notes:** Parsed: execheen (G1632, V-AAI-3S ekcheo), helkos (G1668, N-NSN), kakon (G2556, A-NSN) kai poneron (G4190, A-NSN). The adjectives kakon kai poneron ('noisome and grievous') intensify the plague. Target: eis ten gen — upon the earth. LXX Exo 9:9 uses helkos for the boils plague, confirming vocabulary link.

### Bowl 2 — sea becomes blood, every living soul dies (total vs. trumpet 1/3)

> And the second angel poured out his vial upon the sea; and it became as the blood of a dead [man]: and every living soul died in the sea.

**Context:** Second bowl targets the sea (thalassa, G2281), same domain as trumpet 2 (8:8-9). The critical escalation: trumpet 2 killed 1/3 of sea creatures; bowl 2 kills EVERY living soul. 'Blood of a dead man' — coagulated, putrid blood.

**Notes:** No to triton (G5154) — universalizing language replaces fractions. 'Every living soul died' (pasa psyche zosa apethanen) is absolute. TM107 — zero occurrences of 'third' in bowls. Exodus parallel: Exo 7:19-20 water-to-blood.

### Bowl 3 — rivers become blood, angel of waters' justice declaration, altar speaks

> And the third angel poured out his vial upon the rivers and fountains of waters; and they became blood. And I heard the angel of the waters say, Thou art righteous, O Lord, which art, and wast, and shalt be, because thou hast judged thus. For they have shed the blood of saints and prophets, and thou hast given them blood to drink; for they are worthy. And I heard another out of the altar say, Even so, Lord God Almighty, true and righteous [are] thy judgments.

**Context:** Third bowl targets rivers/fountains (potamos G4215 / pege G4077), same domain as trumpet 3 (8:10-11). The justice declaration (16:5-6) explains the lex talionis: they shed blood, now they drink blood. The altar speaks at 16:7, culminating the altar vindication arc (SP037).

**Notes:** The altar personified (16:7) answers the fifth seal's 'How long?' (6:10). SP037 altar arc: passive (6:9) → mediating (8:3-5) → commanding (9:13) → personified (16:7). 'Thou art righteous...because thou hast judged thus' — the judgment is declared just. 'They are worthy' (axioi eisin) — dark echo of 'thou art worthy' (5:9).

### Bowl 4 — sun scorches, blasphemy + non-repentance

> And the fourth angel poured out his vial upon the sun; and power was given unto him to scorch men with fire. And men were scorched with great heat, and blasphemed the name of God, which hath power over these plagues: and they repented not to give him glory.

**Context:** Fourth bowl targets the sun (helios, G2246), same domain as trumpet 4 (8:12). Critical divergence: trumpet 4 DARKENS the sun; bowl 4 SCORCHES through it. Opposite effects on the same domain (SP060). First appearance of the blasphemeo + metanoeo pattern.

**Notes:** Parsed: eblasphemesan (G987, V-AAI-3P) + ou metenoesan (G3340, V-AAI-3P). Both aorist active indicative 3rd plural. The blasphemy is directed at 'the name of God' (to onoma tou theou). SP056 impenitence escalation stage 2: blasphemeo AND metanoeo negated together.

### Bowl 5 — darkness on beast's throne, sores persist, blasphemy + non-repentance

> And the fifth angel poured out his vial upon the seat of the beast; and his kingdom was full of darkness; and they gnawed their tongues for pain, And blasphemed the God of heaven because of their pains and their sores, and repented not of their deeds.

**Context:** Fifth bowl targets the beast's throne/kingdom with darkness, same domain as trumpet 5 (9:1-2 abyssal darkness). The sores (helkon, G1668) from bowl 1 persist — cumulative effects (TM109). Same blasphemeo + metanoeo pattern as 16:9.

**Notes:** Parsed: eblasphemesan (G987, V-AAI-3P) + ou metenoesan (G3340, V-AAI-3P). helkon (G1668, N-GPN genitive plural) — the sores from the first bowl are still present at the fifth bowl. Exodus darkness parallel: Exo 10:21-23. The target is the beast's thronos — political/religious authority center.

### Bowl 6 — Euphrates dried up, way of kings of east

> And the sixth angel poured out his vial upon the great river Euphrates; and the water thereof was dried up, that the way of the kings of the east might be prepared.

**Context:** Sixth bowl targets the Euphrates (same domain as trumpet 6, 9:13-14). Trumpet 6 released angels BOUND at the Euphrates; bowl 6 dries the river. OT background: Isa 44:27-45:1 (Cyrus dries waters to conquer Babylon).

**Notes:** The Euphrates drying connects to the fall of historical Babylon (Cyrus diverted the river). 'Kings of the east' (basileon ton apo anatolon heliou) — literally 'kings from the rising of the sun.' This prepares for the Armageddon gathering (16:13-16).

### Three unclean frog-spirits — counterfeit trinity gathering for battle

> And I saw three unclean spirits like frogs [come] out of the mouth of the dragon, and out of the mouth of the beast, and out of the mouth of the false prophet. For they are the spirits of devils, working miracles, [which] go forth unto the kings of the earth and of the whole world, to gather them to the battle of that great day of God Almighty.

**Context:** The unholy trinity (dragon/beast/false prophet) produces three unclean (akathartos G169) frog-like spirits. Structural inversion of the three angels (14:6-12): angels proclaim truth from heaven; spirits emanate deception from mouths. batrachos (G944) — NT hapax, connecting to Exodus frog plague (Exo 8:1-15).

**Notes:** Paulien's structural inversion: three angels fly in heaven → three spirits come from mouths. Angels call to worship God → spirits gather for war against God. Dragon = counterfeit Father, Beast = counterfeit Son (received authority, 13:2), False Prophet = counterfeit Spirit (performs signs, 13:13-14). 'The battle of that great day of God Almighty' (ten polemon tes hemeras tes megales tou theou tou pantokratoros).

### Parenthetical thief warning — pre-advent marker, beatitude

> Behold, I come as a thief. Blessed [is] he that watcheth, and keepeth his garments, lest he walk naked, and they see his shame.

**Context:** Christ's own voice interrupts the sixth bowl narrative. erchomai (G2064, V-PNI-1S present indicative) — 'I am coming' (present tense, ongoing imminence). kleptēs (G2812, not kleptō G2813 — the noun 'thief' not the verb). makarios (G3107) — one of seven Revelation beatitudes.

**Notes:** Parsed: erchomai (G2064, V-PNI-1S present middle/passive indicative) — 'I come/am coming,' present tense indicating ongoing imminence at the time of the sixth bowl. kleptēs (G2812, N-NSM) — 'thief.' makarios (G3107, A-NSM) — 'blessed.' gregorōn (G1127, V-PAP-NSM) — 'watching,' present active participle. tērōn (G5083, V-PAP-NSM) — 'keeping,' present active participle. The thief-chain: Matt 24:43, 1 Thess 5:2, 2 Pet 3:10, Rev 3:3, Rev 16:15 — all pre-parousia warnings. The garment metaphor connects to priestly garments (15:6).

### Armageddon — gathering place named in Hebrew

> And he gathered them together into a place called in the Hebrew tongue Armageddon.

**Context:** Armageddon (G717) — NT hapax. Hebrew name in a Greek text: Har-Magedon ('mountain of Megiddo'). The emphasis is on GATHERING (synēgagen, G4863), not battle. The battle is resolved at 19:19-21.

**Notes:** G717 Armageddon — from H2022 har (mountain) + H4023 Megiddon (from gadad H1413, to assemble by troops). Megiddo: site of Josiah's death (2 Chr 35:22), Deborah's victory (Judg 5:19). The gathering here is pre-advent; the resolution at 19:19-21 is advent.

### Bowl 7 — gegonen ('It is done'), dual voice from naos and throne

> And the seventh angel poured out his vial into the air; and there came a great voice out of the temple of heaven, from the throne, saying, It is done.

**Context:** The seventh bowl poured into the air (aera, G109) — the final domain. gegonen (G1096, V-2RAI-3S, perfect active indicative 3rd singular of ginomai) — 'it has happened/come to be.' Dual source: ek tou naou AND apo tou thronou (TM093) — unique dual-origin formula.

**Notes:** Parsed: gegonen (G1096, V-2RAI-3S perfect active indicative) — 'it has come to be.' Different verb from tetelestai (G5055 teleo) at John 19:30. teleo = payment/fulfillment; ginomai = coming-into-being/occurrence. Dual prepositions: ek (out of) tou naou + apo (from) tou thronou. SP048 three completion declarations: tetelestai (cross), gegonen (judgment), gegonan (new creation).

### Superlative earthquake — theophany formula escalation climax

> And there were voices, and thunders, and lightnings; and there was a great earthquake, such as was not since men were upon the earth, so mighty an earthquake, [and] so great.

**Context:** The theophany formula reaches its climax. The earthquake is the only one in Revelation (or the NT) with a superlative qualifier: 'such as was not since men were upon the earth.' Escalation from 4:5 (3 elements) → 8:5 (4) → 11:19 (5) → 16:18-21 (5+ intensified).

**Notes:** TM094 — superlative earthquake. seismos (G4578) — the 'since the beginning' qualifier is unique. The theophany formula elements: voices (phonai), thunders (brontai), lightnings (astrapai), earthquake (seismos) — all present, plus the hail in v.21.

### Babylon remembered — combined wrath formula tou thymou tes orges

> And the great city was divided into three parts, and the cities of the nations fell: and great Babylon came in remembrance before God, to give unto her the cup of the wine of the fierceness of his wrath.

**Context:** The combined wrath formula: to poterion tou oinou tou thymou tes orges autou — 'the cup of the wine of the fierceness of his wrath.' thumos (G2372) and orge (G3709) merge in a single genitive chain (TM097). Babylon 'came in remembrance' — divine remembrance for judgment (inversion of OT remembrance for salvation).

**Notes:** Parsed: tou thymou (G2372, N-GSM) tes orges (G3709, N-GSF) — genitive chain. thumos is the intensity/heat; orge is the settled judicial disposition. Their merger is climactic. poterion (G4221, N-ASN) — cup. OT background: Jer 25:15-16 cup of wrath. SP051 divine remembrance inversion. Babylon announcement at 16:19 is programmatic — expanded in Rev 17-18 (IC049).

### Islands/mountains flee, talent-weight hail, final blasphemy

> And every island fled away, and the mountains were not found. And there fell upon men a great hail out of heaven, [every stone] about the weight of a talent: and men blasphemed God because of the plague of the hail; for the plague thereof was exceeding great.

**Context:** Mountain/island escalation: 'moved out of their places' (6:14) → 'fled away, were not found' (16:20). Displacement → annihilation (SP049). talantiaios (G5006) — NT hapax, talent-weight (~75-100 lbs). Final blasphemy WITHOUT metanoeo — repentance language disappears entirely.

Ezek 38:19-22 allusion (AN052, Strong, 5 elements): earthquake, mountains, hailstones, wrath, nations.

### DOA entry restriction — high priest's limited access behind veil

> And the LORD said unto Moses, Speak unto Aaron thy brother, that he come not at all times into the holy [place] within the vail before the mercy seat, which [is] upon the ark; that he die not: for I will appear in the cloud upon the mercy seat.

**Context:** Access restricted to the Day of Atonement only. The high priest cannot enter 'at all times' — access is limited to the annual ritual. The cloud upon the mercy seat signals divine presence.

**Notes:** The restriction establishes the DOA as the ONLY time the Most Holy Place is accessible. This makes the Rev 15:8 exclusion ('no man was able to enter') a DOA-specific marker.

### Holy linen garments for DOA — parallel to Rev 15:6

> He shall put on the holy linen coat, and he shall have the linen breeches upon his flesh, and shall be girded with a linen girdle, and with the linen mitre shall he be attired: these [are] holy garments; therefore shall he wash his flesh in water, and [so] put them on.

**Context:** Special linen garments for the DOA ritual. Four items: linen coat, linen breeches, linen girdle, linen mitre. All white/linen — not the colored high priestly garments. VP108 parallels Rev 15:6 angel attire.

**Notes:** VP108 shared elements: (1) linen/white, (2) girdle/belt, (3) priestly context, (4) sanctuary ritual setting. The DOA garments are distinct from regular priestly service garments — simpler, all-white.

### Censer with incense creating smoke screen — parallel to Rev 15:8

> And he shall take a censer full of burning coals of fire from off the altar before the LORD, and his hands full of sweet incense beaten small, and bring [it] within the vail: And he shall put the incense upon the fire before the LORD, that the cloud of the incense may cover the mercy seat that [is] upon the testimony, that he die not:

**Context:** The DOA incense creates a protective smoke cloud covering the mercy seat. The smoke prevents the priest from seeing God's glory directly. AN022 (Strong, 5 elements) documented in revs2-10: censer, coals, incense, 'before the LORD,' brought within.

**Notes:** Five DOA censer elements match Rev 8:3-5: (1) censer/libanotos, (2) coals from altar, (3) incense, (4) before the LORD, (5) brought into sacred space. The smoke serves protective purpose in Lev 16; in Rev 15:8 it serves exclusionary purpose.

### Sevenfold blood sprinkling — potential parallel to seven bowls

> And he shall take of the blood of the bullock, and sprinkle [it] with his finger upon the mercy seat eastward; and before the mercy seat shall he sprinkle of the blood with his finger seven times. Then shall he kill the goat of the sin offering, that [is] for the people, and bring his blood within the vail, and do with that blood as he did with the blood of the bullock, and sprinkle it upon the mercy seat, and before the mercy seat:

**Context:** Blood sprinkled seven times before the mercy seat. The sevenfold action in the DOA may parallel the seven bowls. Leithart's 'inverted atonement' thesis: DOA blood flows INWARD for cleansing; bowl judgments flow OUTWARD for judgment.

**Notes:** The sevenfold sprinkling is the central DOA ritual action. Blood goes INTO the sanctuary for cleansing in Lev 16; wrath flows OUT from the sanctuary in Rev 16. Direction reversal: inward cleansing → outward judgment.

### KEY SOURCE TEXT — no man in tabernacle during atonement

> And there shall be no man in the tabernacle of the congregation when he goeth in to make an atonement in the holy [place], until he come out, and have made an atonement for himself, and for his household, and for all the congregation of Israel.

**Context:** The primary OT source for Rev 15:8. Four structural parallels: (a) exclusion of persons — 'no man shall be in the tabernacle,' (b) from the sanctuary space — 'in the tabernacle of the congregation,' (c) during a specific ritual action — 'when he goeth in to make atonement,' (d) temporal limit — 'until he come out.' VP097 (Moderate) documented in revs-22.

**Notes:** The Hebrew 'ad tse'to ('until he come out') provides the explicit temporal boundary. Rev 15:8 achri telesthosin ('till the seven plagues were fulfilled') provides the corresponding temporal boundary. Both have UNTIL clauses — this is absent from Exo 40:35 and Isa 6:4.

### Seven sprinklings to cleanse altar — completing sevenfold action

> And he shall sprinkle of the blood upon it with his finger seven times, and cleanse it, and hallow it from the uncleanness of the children of Israel.

**Context:** A second sevenfold sprinkling, this time on the outer altar. The repetition of 'seven times' reinforces the sevenfold pattern that Rev 15-16 echoes with seven bowls.

**Notes:** Two sets of seven sprinklings in Lev 16: (1) v.14 on the mercy seat, (2) v.19 on the altar. Both serve cleansing purposes. The seven bowls may echo this dual sevenfold pattern.

### Competing source for Rev 15:8 — glory filling at inauguration

> Then a cloud covered the tent of the congregation, and the glory of the LORD filled the tabernacle. And Moses was not able to enter into the tent of the congregation, because the cloud abode thereon, and the glory of the LORD filled the tabernacle.

**Context:** The tabernacle inauguration. Glory fills the tabernacle and Moses cannot enter. Most traditional commentators connect Rev 15:8 to this passage. However, it lacks: (1) the temporal 'until' clause present in both Lev 16:17 and Rev 15:8, (2) occurs at INAUGURATION not judgment, (3) Rev 4-5 throne room scene has no such exclusion (revs2-03 finding).

**Notes:** The absence of exclusion in Rev 4-5 is critical. If the throne room scene were inauguration (Exo 40 parallel), the exclusion should appear THERE, not at 15:8. Its first appearance at 15:8 supports the DOA reading (Lev 16:17) over the inauguration reading (Exo 40:34-35).

### Trumpet prelude censer scene — intercession-to-judgment transition

> And another angel came and stood at the altar, having a golden censer; and there was given unto him much incense, that he should offer [it] with the prayers of all saints upon the golden altar which was before the throne. And the smoke of the incense, [which came] with the prayers of the saints, ascended up before God out of the angel's hand. And the angel took the censer, and filled it with fire of the altar, and cast [it] into the earth: and there were voices, and thunderings, and lightnings, and an earthquake.

**Context:** The trumpet prelude still has intercession: incense offered WITH prayers (8:3-4), THEN the censer filled with fire and cast to earth (8:5). The bowl prelude (15:5-8) has NO intercession element. SP058 marks the intercession-to-judgment transition. libanotos (G3031) appears only here and 8:5 in the entire NT.

**Notes:** Censer reversal: same libanotos carries prayers upward (8:3-4) then fire downward (8:5). SP119 triple-attested vessel transformation: (a) censer 8:3→8:5, (b) bowls 5:8→15:7, (c) smoke 8:4→15:8. The four theophany elements at 8:5 (voices, thunderings, lightnings, earthquake) are 4 elements — escalation from 4:5 (3 elements).

### Seventh trumpet kingdom announcement — structural predecessor

> And the seventh angel sounded; and there were great voices in heaven, saying, The kingdoms of this world are become [the kingdoms] of our Lord, and of his Christ; and he shall reign for ever and ever.

**Context:** The seventh trumpet announces what the seventh bowl declares completed. egeneto (G1096 aorist) — 'are become' (proleptic aorist, declaring as accomplished what is yet to be fully realized). Contrast with gegonen (G1096 perfect) at 16:17.

**Notes:** TM108 — tense distinction: egeneto (aorist, 11:15) vs. gegonen (perfect, 16:17). The aorist announces; the perfect declares completion. The seventh trumpet is the structural predecessor to the bowl sequence.

### Temple opened/ark visible — Janus verse, 5-element theophany

> And the temple of God was opened in heaven, and there was seen in his temple the ark of his testament: and there were lightnings, and voices, and thunderings, and an earthquake, and great hail.

**Context:** Five theophany elements: lightnings, voices, thunderings, earthquake, great hail. This is the 5-element stage in the theophany escalation (4:5 has 3, 8:5 has 4, 11:19 has 5, 16:18-21 has 5+ intensified). Functions as a Janus verse looking both backward (concluding trumpets) and forward (introducing bowls via temple opening).

**Notes:** The temple opening here reveals the ark; the temple opening at 15:5 releases the plague angels. Rev 11:19 introduces the ark (covenant/testimony); Rev 15:5 identifies it as 'temple of the tabernacle of the testimony.' Hail (chalaza G5464) appears for the first time outside Rev 8:7.

### tetelestai — completion declaration at the cross

> When Jesus therefore had received the vinegar, he said, It is finished: and he bowed his head, and gave up the ghost.

**Context:** tetelestai (G5055, V-RPI-3S, perfect passive indicative of teleo) — 'it has been accomplished/paid in full.' The first of three completion declarations. Different verb from gegonen (ginomai) used at Rev 16:17 and 21:6.

**Notes:** Parsed: Tetelestai (G5055, V-RPI-3S perfect passive indicative 3rd singular). teleo = bring to completion, fulfill, pay. Passive voice: the work has been completed upon/through Christ. SP048: tetelestai (cross) → gegonen (judgment) → gegonan (new creation). Different verbs at different nodes: teleo speaks of payment/fulfillment; ginomai speaks of coming-into-being.

### gegonan (plural) — completion declaration at new creation

> And he said unto me, It is done. I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the end. I will give unto him that is athirst of the fountain of the water of life freely.

**Context:** gegonan (G1096, V-2RAI-3P, perfect active indicative 3rd PLURAL of ginomai) — 'they have come to be.' Same verb (ginomai) as gegonen (16:17) but PLURAL. The plural signals that ALL things have been made new (21:5), not a single event.

**Notes:** Parsed: Gegonan (G1096, V-2RAI-3P perfect active indicative 3rd plural). The speaker identifies as Alpha and Omega — divine self-identification. **Context:** Trumpet 4 targets sun (helios G2246). to triton appears FIVE times in this single verse (TM075 climactic concentration). Bowl 4 also targets sun but with OPPOSITE effect: trumpet darkens, bowl scorches (SP060).

**Notes:** TM075 — 5-in-one-verse to triton concentration, the climax of the 1/3 pattern. SP055 pair 4 and SP060: same domain (sun), OPPOSITE effects. Not simple amplification — qualitative transformation. Trumpet: diminishment. Bowl: intensification.

### Trumpet 5 — darkness from abyss, comparison domain 5

> And the fifth angel sounded, and I saw a star fall from heaven unto the earth: and to him was given the key of the bottomless pit. And he opened the bottomless pit; and there arose a smoke out of the pit, as the smoke of a great furnace; and the sun and the air were darkened by reason of the smoke of the pit.

**Context:** Trumpet 5 produces darkness from the abyss. Bowl 5 produces darkness on the beast's throne (16:10). SP055 pair 5: both darkness, different sources (abyss vs. direct judgment on throne).

**Notes:** SP055 pair 5: same domain (darkness/kingdom), different mechanism. Trumpet 5 darkness comes from the abyss smoke; bowl 5 darkness is poured directly on the beast's throne. Exodus darkness parallel: Exo 10:21-23.
