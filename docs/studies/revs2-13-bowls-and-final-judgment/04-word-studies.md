# Word Studies

## Question
How does the bowl prelude (Rev 15:1-8) establish a Day of Atonement typology? How do the seven bowls compare systematically with the seven trumpets — shared target domains and escalation from 1/3 to total? What does the seventh bowl's gegonen ("It is done") declare? How does Rev 16:15 and the Armageddon gathering establish the bowls as pre-advent events?

### G5357 — phiale

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5357-phiale.md`

**Occurrences:** 12

Broad shallow bowl/vial used for ritual pouring. All 12 NT occurrences in Revelation. First appears at 5:8 (golden bowls full of prayers/incense), then seven judgment bowls (15:7, 16:1-17), and bowl angels as guides (17:1, 21:9). OT counterpart is mizraq (H4219, sprinkling bowl, PMI 31.39).

**Semantic categories:** golden vials of incense/prayers, seven golden vials of wrath, poured-out plague vessels, vial-bearing prophetic guides

**Key verses:** Rev 5:8, Rev 15:7, Rev 16:1, Rev 16:2, Rev 16:17, Rev 17:1, Rev 21:9

**Relevance:** Central vessel of the bowl judgments. The content transformation from prayers (5:8) to wrath (15:7) is the key structural observation — same golden bowls, transformed content. The thymiamaton/thymou phonetic wordplay signals the transformation.

### G2372 — thumos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2372-thumos.md`

**Occurrences:** 18

Passionate fury, boiling wrath. From thyo (to rush/breathe hard). 10 of 18 NT occurrences in Revelation. Distinguished from orge as the more passionate, turbulent eruption vs. settled judicial anger. Dominant in divine eschatological fury contexts (Rev 14:10, 15:1, 15:7, 16:1, 16:19, 19:15). Combined with orge at 16:19 and 19:15 for maximal intensity.

**Semantic categories:** divine eschatological fury, corrupting/intoxicating passion (Babylon's wine), satanic rage, human passionate outburst, vice/work of flesh

**Key verses:** Rev 14:10, Rev 15:1, Rev 15:7, Rev 16:1, Rev 16:19, Rev 19:15

**Relevance:** Content of the bowls (tou thymou tou theou). The phonetic near-identity with thymiama (G2368, incense) creates the wordplay signaling vessel transformation. Merges with orge at 16:19 (TM097) in the climactic combined wrath formula.

### G3709 — orge

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3709-orge.md`

**Occurrences:** 36

Settled judicial wrath, deliberate displeasure. 36 NT occurrences, concentrated in Romans (12) and Revelation (6). Dual Louw-Nida domains: anger (88.024) and punishment (38.001). Distinguished from thumos as the settled verdict vs. the passionate eruption. Combined with thumos at Rev 16:19 and 19:15.

**Semantic categories:** divine eschatological wrath, divine judicial wrath, wrath and the law, deliverance from wrath, human anger as vice

**Key verses:** Rev 6:16-17, Rev 11:18, Rev 16:19, Rev 19:15

**Relevance:** Appears in the climactic combined formula tou thymou tes orges (16:19). The thumos-orge merger represents the culmination of the wrath progression: thumos alone (passion, bowls content) + orge alone (judicial, seals/trumpets) → combined (total judgment, seventh bowl).

### G5055 — teleo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5055-teleo.md`

**Occurrences:** 30

To end, complete, execute, fulfill, pay. From telos (G5056, end/goal). 8 of 26 occurrences in Revelation. Key forms: etelesthe (15:1, aorist passive 'was filled up'), telesthosin (15:8, aorist passive subjunctive 'were fulfilled'), tetelestai (John 19:30, perfect passive 'it is finished'). The teleo forms create an inclusio framing Rev 15.

**Semantic categories:** narrative closure formula, fulfillment of Scripture, cross declaration, fulfilling the law, eschatological completion

**Key verses:** John 19:30, Rev 15:1, Rev 15:8, Rev 10:7, Rev 20:3

**Relevance:** Forms the inclusio around Rev 15 (etelesthe at 15:1 and telesthosin at 15:8). tetelestai at the cross (John 19:30) is a different structural node from gegonen at the seventh bowl — different verbs (teleo vs. ginomai) at different nodes (cross vs. judgment). SP048 three completion declarations.

### G1096 — ginomai

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1096-ginomai.md`

**Occurrences:** 702

To become, come into being, happen. Most versatile NT verb with 631 occurrences across 218 KJV translations. Key forms for this study: gegonen (16:17, perfect active indicative singular 'it has happened'), gegonan (21:6, perfect active indicative plural 'they have come to be'), egeneto (narrative transition marker). Different verb from teleo used at the cross.

**Semantic categories:** narrative transition, happening/occurring, becoming/change of state, being made/creation, emphatic denial

**Key verses:** Rev 16:17, Rev 21:6, Rev 11:15, John 1:3

**Relevance:** The verb of the seventh bowl declaration (gegonen) and the new creation declaration (gegonan). ginomai speaks of coming-into-being, distinguishing it from teleo which speaks of fulfillment/payment. SP048 structural arc: tetelestai (cross, teleo) → gegonen (judgment, ginomai singular) → gegonan (new creation, ginomai plural).

### G3485 — naos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3485-naos.md`

**Occurrences:** 46

Inner temple/sanctuary where deity dwells. Distinct from hieron (G2411, entire temple precinct). 16 of 40 NT occurrences in Revelation (37%). From naio (to dwell). In Revelation: heavenly temple (11:19, 15:5, 15:8), voice from temple (16:1, 16:17), no temple in new creation (21:22). LXX equivalent: heykal (H1964, PMI 30.9).

**Semantic categories:** physical inner sanctuary, Christ's body, believers as dwelling, heavenly temple, eschatological temple

**Key verses:** Rev 15:5, Rev 15:8, Rev 16:1, Rev 16:17, Rev 21:22

**Relevance:** Critical for the DOA typology. naos specifies the INNER sanctuary (not hieron). The unique triple genitive at 15:5 (naos tes skenes tou martyriou) identifies the heavenly sanctuary with maximum specificity. naos at 16:17 appears in the dual-source formula (ek tou naou apo tou thronou). The progression naos-present → naos-absent (21:22) structures Revelation's sanctuary theology.

### G4127 — plege

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4127-plege.md`

**Occurrences:** 21

Plague, stroke, wound. Technical term for the bowl judgments. eschatas (G2078) qualifies the plagues as 'last' at 15:1 — the final series. Used throughout Rev 16 for the bowl judgments and at 16:21 for the hail plague specifically.

**Semantic categories:** divine judgment plague, physical wound/stroke

**Key verses:** Rev 15:1, Rev 15:8, Rev 16:9, Rev 16:21

**Relevance:** The technical term connecting the bowl judgments. 'Seven last plagues' (hepta plegas tas eschatas) at 15:1 marks these as the completing/final series. The word links to the Exodus plague tradition (LXX uses plege for the Egyptian plagues).

### G987 — blasphemeo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G987-blasphemeo.md`

**Occurrences:** 39

To blaspheme, speak evil of, revile. In Revelation's judgment sequences, exclusive to the beast (13:6) and bowl-plague recipients (16:9, 16:11, 16:21). The impenitence escalation marker — appears only in the bowl section, never in the trumpet impenitence passage (9:20-21).

**Semantic categories:** active defiance against God, beast's characteristic action

**Key verses:** Rev 13:6, Rev 16:9, Rev 16:11, Rev 16:21

**Relevance:** Key impenitence escalation marker (SP056). Distribution: absent from trumpets (9:20-21) → present with metanoeo in bowls 4-5 (16:9, 16:11) → present without metanoeo at bowl 7 (16:21). The progression from passive refusal to active blasphemy to pure blasphemy tracks character fixation.

### G3340 — metanoeo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3340-metanoeo.md`

**Occurrences:** 36

To repent, change one's mind. Negated at both trumpets (9:20-21) and bowls (16:9, 16:11) but ABSENT from the final bowl (16:21). The disappearance of repentance language at the end of the bowl sequence signals that the possibility of repentance has passed.

**Semantic categories:** repentance called for, repentance negated/refused

**Key verses:** Rev 9:20, Rev 9:21, Rev 16:9, Rev 16:11

**Relevance:** Critical for the impenitence escalation (SP056). The progression: trumpets (metanoeo negated, no blasphemeo) → bowls (metanoeo negated WITH blasphemeo) → final bowl (metanoeo ABSENT, blasphemeo alone). The disappearance of metanoeo at 16:21 is structurally significant — repentance is no longer even mentioned.

### G1632 — ekcheo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1632-ekcheo.md`

**Occurrences:** 28

To pour out. The characteristic verb for the bowl judgments (execheen, aorist active indicative). Parallel to salpizo (G4537) as the formulaic verb for trumpets. Each bowl opens with the formula 'the Nth angel poured out (execheen) his vial.'

**Semantic categories:** bowl judgment formula, outpouring action

**Key verses:** Rev 16:1, Rev 16:2, Rev 16:3, Rev 16:4, Rev 16:8, Rev 16:10, Rev 16:12, Rev 16:17

**Relevance:** SP057 — characteristic verb correspondence. esalpisen (salpizo) opens each trumpet; execheen (ekcheo) opens each bowl. Identical formulaic positions in parallel judgment series.

### G5154 — tritos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5154-tritos.md`

**Occurrences:** 57

Third (ordinal). In Revelation 8:7-12, to triton ('the third part') appears 11+ times, with a climactic 5-in-one-verse concentration at 8:12 (TM075). ZERO occurrences in Rev 16 (TM107). The fraction pattern 1/3 appears exclusively in trumpets; bowls use universalizing language instead.

**Semantic categories:** fraction qualifier in trumpets, absent from bowls

**Key verses:** Rev 8:7, Rev 8:8, Rev 8:9, Rev 8:10, Rev 8:11, Rev 8:12

**Relevance:** The measurable escalation marker. to triton appears 11+ times in trumpets, 0 times in bowls. SP036 fraction escalation: 1/4 (seals) → 1/3 (trumpets) → total (bowls). TM107 — the ABSENCE of fractions in bowls is as structurally significant as their PRESENCE in trumpets.

### G796 — astrape

### G2362 — thronos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2362-thronos.md`

**Occurrences:** 61

Throne. At Rev 16:17, the voice comes from both the naos AND the throne (dual source formula, TM093). At 16:10, bowl 5 targets the beast's throne. The throne connects the judgment authority to the divine sovereignty established in Rev 4-5.

**Semantic categories:** divine authority seat, beast's authority seat, dual source formula

**Key verses:** Rev 4:5, Rev 16:10, Rev 16:17

**Relevance:** TM093 — the dual source at 16:17 (ek tou naou apo tou thronou) is unique in Revelation. The voice comes from BOTH the sanctuary and the throne, merging the liturgical and royal dimensions. Bowl 5 targets the beast's thronos — opposing authority to God's thronos.

### G3529 — nike

Victory. At Rev 15:2, the victors (nikao, G3528 verb form) stand on the sea of glass. Their victory is fourfold: over the beast, image, mark, and number.

### G1093 — ge

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1093-gē.md`

**Occurrences:** 252

Earth, land. The first target domain for both trumpet 1 (8:7, earth/vegetation) and bowl 1 (16:2, earth-dwellers). SP055 pair 1: same domain (ge), different effect (vegetation burning vs. sores on people).

**Semantic categories:** creation domain, judgment target, dwelling place

**Key verses:** Rev 8:7, Rev 16:2, Rev 14:7

**Relevance:** SP055 pair 1 domain vocabulary. ge appears in the creation formula (14:7) and as the first target in both judgment series, confirming the systematic domain correspondence.

### G2537 — kainos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2537-kainos.md`

**Occurrences:** 44

New (qualitatively). At Rev 21:5 'I make all things new' (kaina) precedes the gegonan declaration at 21:6. The new creation is the culmination after the bowl judgments.

**Semantic categories:** new creation, qualitative newness

**Key verses:** Rev 21:5, Rev 21:6

**Relevance:** The gegonan (plural, 'they have come to be') at 21:6 follows the kaina ('all things new') declaration. The completion arc: bowls complete judgment (gegonen, singular) → all things renewed (gegonan, plural).

### G4098 — pipto

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4098-pipto.md`

**Occurrences:** 93

To fall. At Rev 16:19, the cities of the nations 'fell' (epesan). At Rev 14:8, 'Babylon is fallen, is fallen.' The fall vocabulary connects the bowl judgments to the Babylon fall sequence.

**Semantic categories:** judgment fall, structural collapse

**Key verses:** Rev 14:8, Rev 16:19

**Relevance:** The fall of Babylon announced at 14:8 and executed at 16:19 demonstrates the programmatic nature of the seventh bowl — it announces what chapters 17-18 expand.
