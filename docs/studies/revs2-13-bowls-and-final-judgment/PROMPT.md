This is the central typological question. READ `$WORD_STUDIES/G3485-naos.md` for the naos vs. hieron distinction — naos specifies the inner sanctuary. Parse Rev 15:5 for the unique triple genitive naos tes skenes tou martyriou (TM100) — this phrase never appears elsewhere in the NT. Compare Rev 15:8 'no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues...were fulfilled' with Lev 16:17 'there shall be no man in the tabernacle of the congregation when he goeth in to make an atonement...until he come out.' Note the structural parallel: both have (a) exclusion of persons, (b) from the sanctuary space, (c) during a specific ritual action, (d) with a temporal limit (until). Then compare with Exo 40:35 'Moses was not able to enter' — this lacks the temporal 'until' clause and occurs at inauguration, not judgment. Check Isa 6:4 'the house was filled with smoke' — this has smoke but no exclusion clause. Run greek_parser.py on Rev 15:5 and 15:8 to verify the constructions. The revs2-03 finding that Rev 4-5 has NO exclusion is critical — if the throne room scene were inauguration, the exclusion should appear there, not at 15:8. Cross-reference with revs2-10's DOA censer parallel (AN022, Strong, 5 elements). Leithart's 'inverted atonement' thesis (blood flows outward as judgment rather than inward as cleansing) should be verified against Lev 16:14-15 mechanics.
