    Angel introduction formula + en pneumati transport to wilderness¶

    And there came one of the seven angels which had the seven vials, and talked with me, saying unto me, Come hither; I will shew unto thee the judgment of the great whore that sitteth upon many waters: With whom the kings of the earth have committed fornication, and the inhabitants of the earth have been made drunk with the wine of her fornication. So he carried me away in the spirit into the wilderness: and I saw a woman sit upon a scarlet coloured beast, full of names of blasphemy, having seven heads and ten horns.

    Context: FA01 Babylon-Bride Mirror (IC182). Angel introduction architecturally paired with 21:9-10. apophero (G667) exclusive to these two passages for Spirit-transport. Wilderness location = judgment/desolation, contrasting with mountain of bride vision.

    Notes: Bowl-angel introduces both visions using same formula. The verb apophero (G667) for transport occurs only here and at Rev 21:10 in NT. Destination: eis eremon (wilderness) vs epi oros mega (great high mountain). VP001 structural element.

    Beast existence parody: was, is not, yet is¶

    And the angel said unto me, Wherefore didst thou marvel? I will tell thee the mystery of the woman, and of the beast that carrieth her, which hath the seven heads and ten horns. The beast that thou sawest was, and is not; and shall ascend out of the bottomless pit, and go into perdition: and they that dwell on the earth shall wonder, whose names were not written in the book of life from the foundation of the world, when they behold the beast that was, and is not, and yet is.

    Context: FA06 parody structure from revs2-19. Beast existence formula inverts divine formula (Rev 1:4,8; 4:8). Beast goes to apoleia (G684), same term for Judas (John 17:12) and man of sin (2 Thess 2:3).

    Notes: en (V-IAI-3S imperfect) kai ouk estin (V-PAI-3S present) kai parestai (V-FDI-3S future of pareimi). The tense sequence: imperfect (was) + present (is not) + future (shall be present). Divine formula: ho on (present IS) kai ho en (imperfect WAS) kai ho erchomenos (coming). Parody: God starts with present IS; beast starts with past WAS. apoleian (N-ASF accusative of apoleia G684) = perdition/destruction.

    Seven mountains / seven kings interpretive crux¶

    And here [is] the mind which hath wisdom. The seven heads are seven mountains, on which the woman sitteth. And there are seven kings: five are fallen, and one is, [and] the other is not yet come; and when he cometh, he must continue a short space.

    Context: FA06 seven mountains/kings. oros (G3735) = mountain in every other Revelation use, never hill. Text transitions immediately to kings, suggesting mountains ARE kings. OT: mountains = kingdoms (Jer 51:25, Dan 2:35,44).
