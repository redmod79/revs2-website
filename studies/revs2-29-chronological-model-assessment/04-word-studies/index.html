    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    After 28 studies cataloging 1,230 structural elements, what does the cumulative evidence say about Revelation's chronological structure?

    G1096 — ginomai¶

    Source: D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G1096-ginomai.md

    Occurrences: 702

    702 NT occurrences. Dominant aorist form egeneto (34.9%). Tense distribution: Aorist 72.9%, Present 14.2%, Perfect 10.0%. Revelation's ginomai arc spans three distinct forms: egeneto (V-2ADI-3S proleptic aorist) at 11:15 declares kingdoms transferred as certain future; gegonen (V-2RAI-3S intensive perfect) at 16:17 announces terminal completion of bowl judgments; gegonan (V-2RAI-3P perfect plural) at 21:6 extends completion to new creation. This three-stage progression (aorist → perfect singular → perfect plural) constitutes Revelation's temporal backbone and is incompatible with flat recapitulation.

    Semantic categories: temporal backbone, prophetic completion, eschatological arrival

    Key verses: Rev 11:15, Rev 16:17, Rev 21:6

    Relevance: Ginomai arc: egeneto (V-2ADI-3S proleptic aorist at 11:15) → gegonen (V-2RAI-3S perfect stative at 16:17) → gegonan (V-2RAI-3P perfect plural at 21:6). Revelation's temporal backbone establishing progressive completion levels.

    G796 — astrapē¶

    Source: D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G796-astrapē.md

    Occurrences: 9

    9 total NT occurrences; 4 in Revelation exclusively at theophany formula passages (4:5, 8:5, 11:19, 16:18). Collocations: brontē (G1027) 4x, phōnē (G5456) 4x, seismos (G4578) 3x, chalaza (G5464) 2x. Lightning is the structural marker of the theophany formula whose element count escalates: 3 elements at 4:5 (baseline), 4 at 8:5 (+ earthquake), 5 at 11:19 (+ hail), 5+ superlative at 16:18 (with unprecedented geopolitical consequences). The escalation is directional and non-repeating.

    Semantic categories: theophany formula, divine presence, eschatological marker

    Key verses: Rev 4:5, Rev 8:5, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:18

    Relevance: Lightning component of theophany formula. Exclusively marks four formula passages in Revelation. Element-count escalation (3→4→5→5+) at SP054.

    G455 — anoigo¶

    Source: D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G455-anoigo.md

    Occurrences: 78

    78 NT tokens; 25 in Revelation (highest density at 6.2/100v). Passive voice 36%; aorist passive = divine passive. Key instances: ēnoigē (V-2API-3S divine passive aorist) at Rev 11:19 (naos opens after 7th trumpet, revealing ark) and Rev 15:5 (naos opens for bowl angels). These two divine-passive openings frame the bowl sequence and establish VP082 telescoping link from 7th trumpet to bowls. Distinct from active openings (seals opened by Lamb, scroll opened). The divine passive construction indicates God himself opens the sanctuary at each transition point.

    Semantic categories: sanctuary access, divine revelation, eschatological opening

    Key verses: Rev 11:19, Rev 15:5

    Relevance: Naos ēnoigē (divine passive aorist) at 11:19 — temple OPENS at 7th trumpet. VP082 telescoping link to 15:5. Contrasts with naos closure/inaccessibility at 15:8.

    G3485 — naos¶

    Source: D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G3485-naos.md

    Occurrences: 46

    40 NT occurrences; 16 in Revelation (37%), exclusively inner sanctuary (not outer court/temple precinct). Naos accessible at 7:15 (sealed servants worship), 14:15 (angel emerges), 14:17; naos opens at 11:19 (ēnoigē, divine passive, ark visible); naos closes at 15:8 (oudeis edunato eiselthein, achri clause). This sequential pattern — accessible → opened → sealed — is irreconcilable with flat recapitulation (naos cannot be simultaneously open and closed in the same event). SP184/IC149 is built on this contrast.

    Semantic categories: inner sanctuary, heavenly temple, eschatological access

    Key verses: Rev 11:19, Rev 15:8, Rev 7:15, Rev 14:15-17

    Relevance: 16 occurrences in Revelation, exclusively inner sanctuary. Open/closed contrast at 11:19 vs. 15:8 is SP184/IC149 central L-evidence conflict point.

    G891 — achri¶

    Source: D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G891-achri.md

    Occurrences: 49

    49 NT occurrences; Revelation highest density (2.5/100v). Temporal terminus function: achri + subjunctive = 'until X is completed'. Critical instances: Rev 7:3 (achri hou sphragidzōmen = 'until we have sealed') making the harm-prohibition explicitly conditional on sealing completion; Rev 15:8 (achri telesthōsin = 'until the seven plagues be fulfilled') making naos inaccessibility explicitly conditional on bowl completion. Both achri clauses convert what could be atemporal symbolic statements into sequential temporal dependencies that resist recapitulationist flattening.

    Semantic categories: temporal boundary, until clause, sequential marker

    Key verses: Rev 7:3, Rev 15:8

    Relevance: Temporal boundary marker making sealing chain (7:3) and naos closure (15:8) explicitly sequential. achri hou at 7:3 = 'until sealing complete'. This makes SP186 sealing chain resistant to atemporal recapitulationist reading.

    G2064 — erchomai¶

    Source: D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G2064-erchomai.md

    Occurrences: 664

    604 NT verse occurrences. ēlthen (aorist 3rd sg) at Rev 6:17 and 11:18 = VP174 identical wrath-arrival declaration — the strongest single R-model evidence item. Greek at 6:17: ὅτι ἦλθεν ἡ ἡμέρα ἡ μεγάλη τῆς ὀργῆς αὐτῶν (parsed: V-2AAI-3S Aor Act Ind Sg). Greek at 11:18: καὶ ἦλθεν ἡ ὀργή σου (same form). R interprets identical form as proof of same eschatological moment; L/T interprets both as proleptic aorists where certainty of future fulfillment is declared as accomplished fact.

    Semantic categories: arrival, eschatological coming, wrath declaration

    Key verses: Rev 6:17, Rev 11:18

    Relevance: ēlthen (aorist active indicative 3rd singular) at 6:17 and 11:18 = VP174 identical wrath-arrival declaration. Strongest single R-evidence item. L/T reads ēlthen as proleptic aorist in both cases.

    G3709 — orgē¶

    Source: D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G3709-orge.md

    Occurrences: 36

    36 NT occurrences. Dual-domain: anger (88.024) + punishment (38.001). 'Wrath' dominant KJV translation (63.6%). orgē = settled, principled wrath; distinct from thumos (G2372) = passionate, acute anger. At Rev 6:17 (tēs orgēs autōn, Gen Sg F) and Rev 11:18 (hē orgē sou, Nom Sg F) = VP174. At Rev 15:7 and 16:19 orgē appears in bowl context, establishing wrath theme through all three sequences. The thumos/orgē distinction matters: thumos dominates trumpets (G2372 at 14:10, 14:19), orgē appears at eschatological culmination points.

    Semantic categories: divine wrath, eschatological judgment, Day of Wrath

    Key verses: Rev 6:17, Rev 11:18

    Relevance: orgē appears with ēlthen at 6:17 and 11:18 forming VP174. Distinct from thumos (G2372) — orgē = settled, principled wrath; thumos = passionate anger.

    G5055 — teleo¶

    Source: D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G5055-teleo.md

    Occurrences: 30

    26 unique verses, 30 tokens. etelesthe (aorist passive indicative) at Rev 10:7 ('the mystery of God should be finished') and Rev 15:1 ('in them is filled up the wrath of God') = VP084 verbal bridge. telesthōsin (aorist passive subjunctive 3rd plural) at Rev 15:8 = achri clause completion marker. The passive voice throughout indicates divine completion. VP084 establishes a direct verbal link between the 7th trumpet's announcement (10:7) and the bowls' execution (15:1), which the T-model interprets as the 7th trumpet containing the bowl sequence.

    Semantic categories: completion, fulfillment, eschatological finish

    Key verses: Rev 10:7, Rev 15:1

    Relevance: etelesthe (aorist passive) at Rev 10:7 (mystery should be finished) and 15:1 (wrath filled up) = VP084 verbal bridge. Connects 7th trumpet announcement to bowl execution (T-model).

    G5154 — tritos¶

    Source: D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G5154-tritos.md

    Occurrences: 57

    49 unique verses; Revelation = 15 occurrences (highest book concentration, 3.7/100v). 'Third part' (to triton) saturates the trumpet sequence: 11+ occurrences in Rev 8-9 alone. Zero occurrences confirmed in the bowl sequence (TM323). The stark contrast — to triton omnipresent in trumpets, entirely absent in bowls — establishes SP036 fraction escalation: seals = 1/4 scope (tetarton, G5067 at 6:8), trumpets = 1/3 scope (to triton), bowls = total scope (no fraction limiting language). This directional escalation is incompatible with the sequences describing the same events.

    Semantic categories: fraction, scope limitation, trumpet sequence

    Key verses: Rev 8:7, Rev 8:8, Rev 8:9, Rev 8:10, Rev 8:12

    Relevance: to triton saturates trumpet sequence (11+ occurrences in Rev 8-9). Zero occurrences confirmed in bowl sequence (TM323). SP036 fraction escalation: 1/4 seals → 1/3 trumpets → total bowls.

    G91 — adikeō¶

    Source: D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G91-adikeo.md

    Occurrences: 35

    23 unique verses, 35 tokens in NT. Revelation = 38% of occurrences (9 of ~24 verses) — highest concentration of any NT book. SP186/VP289 sealing chain: (1) mē adikēsēte (prohibitive aorist subjunctive V-AAS-2P) at Rev 7:3 — harm prohibited until sealing complete, with achri temporal clause; (2) harm executed on same domains (earth, sea, trees) in Rev 8:7-9; (3) adikēsousin (future active indicative V-FAI-3P) at Rev 9:4 — sealed servants explicitly protected. This three-link chain with achri temporal boundary constitutes the strongest single L-evidence item. Active voice dominates (82%), indicating deliberate agents executing judgment.

    Semantic categories: harm, sequential causality, sealing chain

    Key verses: Rev 7:3, Rev 8:7, Rev 9:4

    Relevance: SP186/VP289 strongest single L-evidence item. mē adikēsēte (prohibitive aorist subjunctive at 7:3) → harm executed in 8:7-9 on same domains → adikēsousin (future active indicative at 9:4) respects the seal. Sequential causality chain with achri temporal clause.

    G5067 — tetartos¶

    Occurrences: 10

    tetarton (accusative form of G5067) at Rev 6:8 = 'the fourth part of the earth'. Establishes the 1/4 scope limitation of seal judgments. In context with to triton (1/3) saturating the trumpet sequence and total scope (no fraction) in the bowls, tetarton sets the baseline for SP036 fraction escalation argument: 1/4 → 1/3 → whole. The directional increase in scope across the three sequences is evidence against flat recapitulation (the same event cannot be described as affecting both 1/4 and 1/3 of the earth).

    Semantic categories: fraction, seal scope, escalation baseline

    Key verses: Rev 6:8

    Relevance: tetarton (G5067) at Rev 6:8 = 1/4 scope in seal sequence. SP036/SP185 fraction escalation baseline. Contrasts with to triton (1/3) in trumpets and total scope in bowls.

    G946 — bdelygma¶

    Source: D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G946-bdelygma.md

    Occurrences: 6

    Abomination language at Rev 17:4-5 connected to naos defilement and temple-violation themes from OT (H8251 shiqquwts in Dan 9:27, 11:31). Provides thematic background for naos access restriction theology — the sanctuary is protected from defilement by the closure at 15:8. Naos inaccessibility (IC149) functions as the inverse of bdelygma violation.

    Semantic categories: abomination, naos violation, defilement

    Key verses: Rev 17:4, Rev 17:5

    Relevance: Naos language context; temple-violation theme connected to naos access restriction.

    H8251 — shiqquwts¶

    Source: D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\H8251-shiqquwts.md

    Occurrences: 29

    OT counterpart to G946 bdelygma. Temple defilement in Dan 9:27 and Dan 11:31 provides the OT precedent for sanctuary access restriction. The abomination that maketh desolate sets the typological background for Rev 15:8's naos closure — where instead of defilement by an enemy, the naos is sealed by divine command during the time of judgment execution.

    Semantic categories: abomination, defilement, OT antetype

    Key verses: Dan 9:27, Dan 11:31

    Relevance: OT counterpart to G946; temple defilement background for naos access restriction theology.

    G5281 — hupomone¶

    Source: D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G5281-hupomone.md

    Occurrences: 32

    Patient endurance placed at maximum eschatological tension points in Revelation (Rev 14:12: 'Here is the patience of the saints'). Positioned at the cusp between trumpets and bowls — after programmatic 7th trumpet but before bowl execution — confirming that the interval between announcement and fulfillment is real, extended, and demanding endurance. The T-model's 'not-yet' interval is not instantaneous; hupomone language indicates the community must persevere through real time.

    Semantic categories: patient endurance, eschatological interval, not-yet

    Key verses: Rev 14:12

    Relevance: Placed at maximum eschatological tension points (Rev 14:12). Confirms not-yet interval is real and extended — the time between 7th trumpet announcement and bowl completion is not instantaneous.

    G906 — ballō¶

    Source: D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G906-ballo.md

    Occurrences: 127

    Dragon displacement arc VP294: cast to earth (12:9, eblēthē aorist passive) → cast into abyss (20:3, ebalen aorist active) → cast into lake of fire (20:10, eblēthē aorist passive). Three discrete spatial displacements to three distinct locations constitute a linear progression across three clearly sequential events. This arc is incompatible with pure recapitulation because the dragon cannot be simultaneously in all three locations — the progression marks genuine narrative advance.

    Semantic categories: displacement, dragon arc, linear progression

    Key verses: Rev 12:9, Rev 20:3, Rev 20:10

    Relevance: Dragon displacement arc VP294: cast to earth (12:9) → cast into abyss (20:3) → cast into lake of fire (20:10). Linear progression across three distinct events.

    G3772 — ouranos¶

    Source: D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G3772-ouranos.md

    Occurrences: 284

    Heaven as locus of theophany formula occurrences and major eschatological declarations. Theophany formula proceeds 'out of the throne' (4:5), follows the altar fire cast 'into the earth' (8:5), opens the naos 'in heaven' (11:19), and follows the bowl poured 'into the air' (16:18). The sequential movement from throne-centered theophany to altar/earth theophany to naos-opening theophany to final superlative theophany traces eschatological progression.

    Semantic categories: heaven, theophany locus, eschatological arena

    Key verses: Rev 4:1, Rev 8:1, Rev 11:15, Rev 16:17

    Relevance: Heaven as locus of theophany formula occurrences and eschatological declarations.

    G4602 — sigē¶

    Source: D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G4602-sige.md

    Occurrences: 2

    Half-hour silence at 7th seal (Rev 8:1). Only 2 NT occurrences (8:1 and Acts 21:40). Kline's recapitulationist argument reads the silence as eschatological completion motif (citing Hab 2:20; Zec 2:13). The T-model reads the silence as transition awe before the opening of a new trumpet series — analogous to the cosmic hush before divine action (Zep 1:7). Both readings are grammatically possible; the T-reading is supported by what follows (appearance of seven trumpet angels rather than concluding judgment imagery).

    Semantic categories: silence, 7th seal, cosmic awe

    Key verses: Rev 8:1

    Relevance: Half-hour silence at 7th seal. TM058 context. Kline reads as completion marker (R); T-model reads as silence before new series opens.

    G1632 — ekcheō¶

    Source: D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G1632-ekcheo.md

    Occurrences: 28
