    Verses¶

    Question¶

    What are Christ's "I Am" (ego eimi) declarations in Revelation, and how do they form a structural frame — particularly the Alpha/Omega declarations in the prologue and epilogue? Document every ego eimi statement, its position, and its thematic content. How do the hymns, doxologies, and worship scenes function as structural markers throughout the book? Document every hymn/doxology, identify who sings, what the content is, and where it falls in the narrative structure. Do the hymns mark structural transitions?

    Prologue frame — grace formula, doxology, Alpha/Omega declaration¶

    The Revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave unto him, to shew unto his servants things which must shortly come to pass; and he sent and signified [it] by his angel unto his servant John: Who bare record of the word of God, and of the testimony of Jesus Christ, and of all things that he saw. Blessed [is] he that readeth, and they that hear the words of this prophecy, and keep those things which are written therein: for the time [is] at hand. John to the seven churches which are in Asia: Grace [be] unto you, and peace, from him which is, and which was, and which is to come; and from the seven Spirits which are before his throne; And from Jesus Christ, [who is] the faithful witness, [and] the first begotten of the dead, and the prince of the kings of the earth. Unto him that loved us, and washed us from our sins in his own blood, And hath made us kings and priests unto God and his Father; to him [be] glory and dominion for ever and ever. Amen. Behold, he cometh with clouds; and every eye shall see him, and they [also] which pierced him: and all kindreds of the earth shall wail because of him. Even so, Amen. I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the ending, saith the Lord, which is, and which was, and which is to come, the Almighty.

    Context: Opening of Revelation. Prologue establishes the book's authority chain (God -> Christ -> angel -> John). Verses 4-5a contain the grace formula with temporal formula ('him which is, and which was, and which is to come'). Verses 5b-6 contain the first doxology. Verse 7 is the parousia announcement. Verse 8 is the Alpha/Omega ego eimi declaration with pantokrator title.

    Notes: The speaker at 1:8 is identified as 'the Lord' (kyrios ho theos) with pantokrator — language typically associated with the Father in Revelation. But v.7 just described Christ's coming. The temporal formula appears in its full three-part form. First occurrence of Alpha/Omega title. Prologue doxology at 1:5b-6 is the first hymnic unit in Revelation.

    Inaugural vision — en pneumati marker, Christophany, ego eimi: First/Last and Living One¶

    I was in the Spirit on the Lord's day, and heard behind me a great voice, as of a trumpet, Saying, I am Alpha and Omega, the first and the last: and, What thou seest, write in a book, and send [it] unto the seven churches which are in Asia; unto Ephesus, and unto Smyrna, and unto Pergamos, and unto Thyatira, and unto Sardis, and unto Philadelphia, and unto Laodicea. And I turned to see the voice that spake with me. And being turned, I saw seven golden candlesticks; And in the midst of the seven candlesticks [one] like unto the Son of man, clothed with a garment down to the foot, and girt about the paps with a golden girdle. His head and [his] hairs [were] white like wool, as white as snow; and his eyes [were] as a flame of fire; And his feet like unto fine brass, as if they burned in a furnace; and his voice as the sound of many waters. And he had in his right hand seven stars: and out of his mouth went a sharp twoedged sword: and his countenance [was] as the sun shineth in his strength. And when I saw him, I fell at his feet as dead. And he laid his right hand upon me, saying unto me, Fear not; I am the first and the last: [I am] he that liveth, and was dead; and, behold, I am alive for evermore, Amen; and have the keys of hell and of death.

    Context: First en pneumati marker at v.10. Christ appears amid the seven lampstands. V.11 contains Alpha/Omega in KJV/TR but this is ABSENT from N1904 critical text (textual variant confirmed via greek_text_compare.py). V.17-18 contain two ego eimi declarations: 'I am the first and the last' and 'I am he that liveth, and was dead.'

    Notes: Greek text compare confirms Rev 1:11 Alpha/Omega is NOT in N1904 — only in TR. Strong's in N1904 only: G3004, G1125, G3992, G991, G3739, G1577, G4667. Strong's in TR only: G4413, G1473, G1, G5598, G2078 — these are the ego eimi and Alpha/Omega terms. The N1904 text of 1:11 simply reads 'saying, What you see write in a book and send to the seven churches.' The ego eimi at 1:17 uses explicit ego eimi (G1473 + G1510) with ho protos kai ho eschatos. At 1:18, ho zon (the Living One) + egenomen nekros (I became dead) + zon eimi eis tous aionas ton aionon (I am alive unto the ages of the ages).

    Smyrna letter opening — indirect ego eimi formula¶

    And unto the angel of the church in Smyrna write; These things saith the first and the last, which was dead, and is alive;

    Context: Opening self-identification of Christ to the Smyrna church. Uses 'tade legei' (these things says) formula followed by First/Last title from 1:17 plus death/resurrection from 1:18.

    Notes: This is an indirect ego eimi — the 'tade legei' formula introduces Christ's titles without the explicit ego eimi verbal construction. The self-identification draws directly on 1:17-18, applying the inaugural vision Christology to the specific church situation (persecution, poverty).

    Thyatira letter — Son of God self-identification and YHWH heart-searcher appropriation¶

    And unto the angel of the church in Thyatira write; These things saith the Son of God, who hath his eyes like unto a flame of fire, and his feet [are] like fine brass; I know thy works, and charity, and service, and faith, and thy patience, and thy works; and the last [to be] more than the first. Notwithstanding I have a few things against thee, because thou sufferest that woman Jezebel, which calleth herself a prophetess, to teach and to seduce my servants to commit fornication, and to eat things sacrificed unto idols. And I gave her space to repent of her fornication; and she repented not. Behold, I will cast her into a bed, and them that commit adultery with her into great tribulation, except they repent of their deeds. And I will kill her children with death; and all the churches shall know that I am he which searcheth the reins and hearts: and I will give unto every one of you according to your works.

    Context: Only letter where Christ identifies as 'the Son of God.' V.23 contains explicit ego eimi: 'ego eimi ho eraunon nephrous kai kardias' (I am he who searches minds and hearts). This appropriates YHWH's self-description from Jer 17:10.

    Notes: Greek confirms ego (G1473) eimi (G1510) followed by ho eraunon (the one searching, G2045 present active participle) nephrous (kidneys/reins, G3510) kai kardias (and hearts, G2588). Jer 17:10 reads 'I the LORD search the heart, I try the reins' — four vocabulary parallels: YHWH/ego, search/eraunon, heart/kardia, reins/nephros. Christ claims YHWH's prerogative directly.

    Thyatira overcomer promise — morning star linking to 22:16 epilogue¶

    And he that overcometh, and keepeth my works unto the end, to him will I give power over the nations: And he shall rule them with a rod of iron; as the vessels of a potter shall they be broken to shivers: even as I received of my Father. And I will give him the morning star.

    Context: Overcomer promise includes 'the morning star' (ton astera ton proinon). Proinos (G4407) occurs only twice in the entire NT: here at 2:28 and at 22:16 where Christ identifies himself AS the morning star.

    Notes: The promise to give the overcomer the morning star at 2:28 is fulfilled by the ego eimi declaration at 22:16 where Christ IS the bright morning star. This creates a structural link from the central letters to the epilogue.

    Prologue frame — grace formula, temporal formula, doxology, Alpha/Omega declaration¶

    John to the seven churches which are in Asia: Grace [be] unto you, and peace, from him which is, and which was, and which is to come; and from the seven Spirits which are before his throne; And from Jesus Christ, [who is] the faithful witness, [and] the first begotten of the dead, and the prince of the kings of the earth. Unto him that loved us, and washed us from our sins in his own blood, And hath made us kings and priests unto God and his Father; to him [be] glory and dominion for ever and ever. Amen. Behold, he cometh with clouds; and every eye shall see him, and they [also] which pierced him: and all kindreds of the earth shall wail because of him. Even so, Amen. I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the ending, saith the Lord, which is, and which was, and which is to come, the Almighty.

    Context: Grace formula (v.4), temporal formula first occurrence (v.4,8), prologue doxology (v.5b-6), parousia announcement (v.7), Alpha/Omega ego eimi (v.8).

    Notes: This is a subset of 1:1-8 focused on the structural elements: temporal formula, doxology, ego eimi.

    Prologue opening — ha dei genesthai chain, beatitude, parallel to 22:6-7¶

    The Revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave unto him, to shew unto his servants things which must shortly come to pass; and he sent and signified [it] by his angel unto his servant John: Who bare record of the word of God, and of the testimony of Jesus Christ, and of all things that he saw.     Notes: Greek 22:13 confirms all three pairs: Alpha/Omega (G1 + G5598), protos/eschatos (G4413 + G2078), arche/telos (G746 + G5056). This is the ONLY verse combining all three. Greek 22:16 has explicit ego eimi (G1473 + G1510): he rhiza (G4491, root) kai to genos (G1085, offspring) Daueid, ho aster (G792) ho lampros (G2986) ho proinos (G4407). The 'ego Iesous' at 22:16 definitively identifies the speaker as Christ for the entire v.12-16 block, retroactively claiming the Alpha/Omega declaration at v.13.

    Beast temporal formula — parody inversion of divine temporal formula¶

    The beast that thou sawest was, and is not; and shall ascend out of the bottomless pit, and go into perdition: and they that dwell on the earth shall wonder, whose names were not written in the book of life from the foundation of the world, when they behold the beast that was, and is not, and yet is.

    Context: Third en pneumati vision (17:3). The beast's temporal formula 'was, and is not, and shall ascend/yet is' (en kai ouk estin kai mellei anabainein / en kai ouk estin kai parestai) inverts the divine formula 'which is, and which was, and which is to come.'

    Notes: Greek confirms: en (imperfect, G1510 'was') kai ouk estin (present, 'is not') kai parestai (future middle of pareimi, G3918, 'shall be present'). The divine formula uses erchomai (coming); the beast uses pareimi (being present) — similar but different verb. The inversion: divine is positive (is, was, is coming); beast is negative (was, is NOT, shall ascend from abyss). Structural significance: the beast's parody confirms the divine formula's importance.

    Prologue doxology — first hymnic unit in Revelation¶

    Unto him that loved us, and washed us from our sins in his own blood, And hath made us kings and priests unto God and his Father; to him [be] glory and dominion for ever and ever. Amen.

    Context: The first doxological ascription in Revelation, embedded in the prologue greeting. Directed to Christ. Two attributes: doxa (glory) and kratos (dominion). 'Kings and priests' language echoes Exo 19:6 and becomes a structural thread.

    Notes: This is the prologue doxology — the first hymnic element in the book. Two attributes only (doxa + kratos), making it the simplest ascription formula. Directed to Christ (the antecedent is 'Jesus Christ' in v.5a).

    Fifth seal martyrs' cry — 'How long?' altar lament initiating cry-answer arc¶

    And when he had opened the fifth seal, I saw under the altar the souls of them that were slain for the word of God, and for the testimony which they held: And they cried with a loud voice, saying, How long, O Lord, holy and true, dost thou not judge and avenge our blood on them that dwell on the earth?

    Context: The martyrs' cry initiates the altar cry-answer arc that completes at 16:5-7. 'Holy and true' (ho hagios kai alethinos) echoes the pistos kai alethinos thread. The altar becomes a structural link between seals and bowls.

    Notes: The cry 'How long?' connects to the judgment hymns at 16:5-7 where the altar itself responds 'Even so, Lord God Almighty, true and righteous are thy judgments.' Shared vocabulary: blood (haima), judge/judgment (krino/krisis), altar (thysiastèrion).

    OT background — 'I the LORD, the first, and with the last; I am he' (ani hu)¶

    Who hath wrought and done [it], calling the generations from the beginning? I the LORD, the first, and with the last; I [am] he.

    Context: Isaiah's monotheistic polemic against Babylonian idols. God challenges the nations to demonstrate any deity who controls history. 'I YHWH rishon we-et acharonim ani hu' — the first of three First/Last declarations in Isaiah 41-48.

    Notes: Hebrew: ani (I) YHWH (LORD) rishon (H7223, first, Adj.ms.Abs) we-et acharonim (H314, last ones, Adj.mp.Abs) ani hu (I am he). The 'ani hu' formula is the Hebrew equivalent of Greek ego eimi. Note the plural 'acharonim' (last ones) rather than singular — 'with the last ones.' The polemic context is crucial: this is an exclusive monotheistic claim made against idols.

    OT background — 'I am the first, and I am the last; beside me there is no God'¶

    Thus saith the LORD the King of Israel, and his redeemer the LORD of hosts; I [am] the first, and I [am] the last; and beside me [there is] no God.

    Context: Second of three First/Last declarations. Speaker identified as both 'YHWH King of Israel' and 'his redeemer YHWH Sabaoth (LORD of hosts).' The monotheistic exclusion clause 'beside me there is no God' makes this the strongest exclusivity claim.

    Notes: Hebrew: ani (I) rishon (H7223, first) wa-ani (and I) acharon (H314, last, Adj.ms.Abs) u-mibaladi (and besides me) ein (there is not) elohim (God). The compound title 'YHWH melek Yisrael we-goalo YHWH tsabaoth' — 'LORD King of Israel and his redeemer LORD of hosts.' YHWH tsabaoth becomes kyrios pantokrator in LXX. When Christ claims 'the first and the last' in Rev 1:17 and 22:13, he claims a title that in Isaiah is YHWH's exclusive property in a monotheistic polemic context.

    OT background — 'I am he; I am the first, I also am the last'¶

    Hearken unto me, O Jacob and Israel, my called; I [am] he; I [am] the first, I also [am] the last.

    Context: Third First/Last declaration. 'Ani hu' (I am he) explicitly combined with 'ani rishon af ani acharon' (I am the first, I also am the last).

    Notes: Three-fold self-declaration: ani hu (I am he), ani rishon (I am the first), af ani acharon (I also am the last). The 'af' (also) emphasizes totality — the same God who is the first is ALSO the last.

    OT trisagion source — seraphim 'Holy, holy, holy' modified in Rev 4:8¶

    In the year that king Uzziah died I saw also the Lord sitting upon a throne, high and lifted up, and his train filled the temple. Above it stood the seraphims: each one had six wings; with twain he covered his face, and with twain he covered his feet, and with twain he did fly. And one cried unto another, and said, Holy, holy, holy, [is] the LORD of hosts: the whole earth [is] full of his glory. And the posts of the door moved at the voice of him that cried, and the house was filled with smoke.

    Context: The source for Rev 4:8's trisagion. Isaiah has 'Holy, holy, holy is YHWH Sabaoth; the whole earth is full of his glory.' Revelation modifies: replaces 'the whole earth is full of his glory' with the temporal formula 'which was, and is, and is to come.' Both have six-winged creatures.

    Notes: The modification from Isaiah to Revelation is theologically significant: Isaiah's trisagion emphasizes spatial universality ('the whole earth'); Revelation's emphasizes temporal eternality ('was, is, is to come'). Both use YHWH Sabaoth / Kyrios ho Theos ho Pantokrator. Six wings in both. Isaiah has seraphim; Revelation has zoa (living creatures).

    OT background — YHWH source for Rev 2:23 heart-searcher appropriation¶

    I the LORD search the heart, [I] try the reins, even to give every man according to his ways, [and] according to the fruit of his doings.

    Context: YHWH declares himself the searcher of hearts and trier of reins (kidneys). Rev 2:23 has Christ claiming: 'I am he which searcheth the reins and hearts: and I will give unto every one of you according to your works.'

    Notes: Four vocabulary parallels between Jer 17:10 and Rev 2:23: (1) I/ego = YHWH/Christ, (2) search = choqer/eraunon, (3) heart = lev/kardia, (4) reins = kelayot/nephros. Plus the reward formula: 'give every man according to his ways/works.' Christ directly claims YHWH's prerogative.

    OT ego eimi complex — 'I am he' with Savior exclusion¶

    Ye [are] my witnesses, saith the LORD, and my servant whom I have chosen: that ye may know and believe me, and understand that I [am] he: before me there was no God formed, neither shall there be after me. I, [even] I, [am] the LORD; and beside me [there is] no saviour.

    Context: The 'ani hu' (I am he) formula in the context of exclusive monotheistic claims and exclusive savior claims. 'Before me there was no God formed, neither shall there be after me.'

    Notes: The combination of 'ani hu' (ego eimi equivalent) + 'beside me no savior' intensifies the Christological implications when Christ uses the ego eimi formula in Revelation.

    Laodicea opening — 'the Amen, the faithful and true witness, the beginning of the creation of God'¶

    And unto the angel of the church of the Laodiceans write; These things saith the Amen, the faithful and true witness, the beginning of the creation of God;

    Context: Christ's self-identification as 'the Amen' — transforming the liturgical response into a personal title. 'The arche (G746) of the creation of God' — arche here meaning origin/source/ruler, not first created being.

    Notes: The 'Amen' as Christological title connects to Isa 65:16 where God is called 'the God of Amen/truth.' The arche (G746) title at 3:14 connects to the arche in the merism pair at 21:6 and 22:13.

    Beast worship — negative worship binary¶

    And they worshipped the dragon which gave power unto the beast: and they worshipped the beast, saying, Who [is] like unto the beast? who is able to make war with him?

    Context: Double proskyneo: they worship both the dragon AND the beast. 'Who is like unto the beast?' parodies 'Who is like unto the LORD?' (Exo 15:11, Psa 35:10). Negative side of the worship binary.

    Notes: Two uses of proskyneo (G4352) in one verse — dragon and beast both receive worship. This is the negative counterpart to the positive worship scenes in chs. 4-5, 7, 11, 15, 19.

    Warrior Christ — Faithful and True, densest thread convergence zone¶

    And I saw heaven opened, and behold a white horse; and he that sat upon him [was] called Faithful and True, and in righteousness he doth judge and make war. His eyes [were] as a flame of fire, and on his head [were] many crowns; and he had a name written, that no man knew, but he himself. And he [was] clothed with a vesture dipped in blood: and his name is called The Word of God. And the armies [which were] in heaven followed him upon white horses, clothed in fine linen, white and clean. And out of his mouth goeth a sharp sword, that with it he should smite the nations: and he shall rule them with a rod of iron: and he treadeth the winepress of the fierceness and wrath of Almighty God. And he hath on [his] vesture and on his thigh a name written, KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS.

    Context: Warrior Christ emerges immediately after the hymnic climax of 19:1-8. Pantokrator at v.15. Five vocabulary threads converge in eleven verses — the densest convergence zone per revs2-05.

    Notes: Pantokrator at 19:15 in genitive form (Pantokratoros). This is not a hymnic passage but the narrative that the hymns of 19:1-8 anticipated.

    No temple — Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are the temple/light¶

    And I saw no temple therein: for the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are the temple of it. And the city had no need of the sun, neither of the moon, to shine in it: for the glory of God did lighten it, and the Lamb [is] the light thereof.

    Context: The pantokrator + arnion co-identification. God Almighty AND the Lamb together ARE the temple — the distinction between God and Lamb collapses into shared function.

    Notes: Pantokrator at 21:22 is the ninth and final Revelation occurrence. The Lamb as the light of the city fulfills the doxa-glory motif.

    Lamb slain from foundation — worship binary, book of life¶

    And all that dwell upon the earth shall worship him, whose names are not written in the book of life of the Lamb slain from the foundation of the world.

    Context: Universal beast worship — the negative side of the worship binary. 'The Lamb slain from the foundation of the world' connects the Lamb's sacrifice to cosmic origins.

    Notes: Proskyneo (G4352) directed to the beast. The 'book of life of the Lamb' links worship to the Lamb's redemptive work.

    OT Hallelujah psalm — 'Praise ye the LORD' pattern underlying Rev 19¶

    Praise ye the LORD. O give thanks unto the LORD; for [he is] good: for his mercy [endureth] for ever.

    Context: Opening of a Hallel psalm with 'Praise ye the LORD' (halelu-Yah). Provides OT liturgical background for the allelouia concentration in Rev 19.

    Notes: The halelu-Yah formula at the opening of OT psalms becomes the allelouia exclamation in Rev 19.

    Hallelujah psalm — praise framework¶

    Praise ye the LORD. I will praise the LORD with [my] whole heart, in the assembly of the upright, and [in] the congregation.

    Context: Another Hallel psalm opening. The congregational setting ('assembly of the upright') parallels the heavenly assembly in Revelation's worship scenes.

    Notes: The pattern of communal praise in the OT assembly prefigures the heavenly worship assembly in Revelation.

    OT 'new song' concept — background for Rev 5:9 and 14:3¶

    Sing unto him a new song; play skilfully with a loud noise.

    Context: The 'new song' (shir chadash) concept appears in multiple OT psalms (33:3, 40:3, 96:1, 98:1, 144:9, 149:1). Revelation adopts this as ode kaine.

    Notes: The 'new song' in the Psalms celebrates new acts of God. In Revelation, the new song celebrates the Lamb's redemptive work (5:9) and is exclusive to the redeemed (14:3).

    OT 'new song' — 'He put a new song in my mouth'¶

    And he hath put a new song in my mouth, [even] praise unto our God: many shall see [it], and fear, and shall trust in the LORD.

    Context: God puts the new song in the worshiper's mouth. The divine origin of the song parallels the heavenly origin of worship in Revelation.

    Notes: OT background for the new song concept. Cross-testament parallel tool confirmed Psa 40:3 as the top OT match for Rev 5:9.

    Song of Moses at Red Sea — background for 'Song of Moses and the Lamb' (Rev 15:3)¶

    Then sang Moses and the children of Israel this song unto the LORD, and spake, saying, I will sing unto the LORD, for he hath triumphed gloriously: the horse and his rider hath he thrown into the sea. The LORD [is] my strength and song, and he is become my salvation: he [is] my God, and I will prepare him an habitation; my father's God, and I will exalt him. The LORD [is] a man of war: the LORD [is] his name. ... The LORD shall reign for ever and ever.

    Context: The original Song of Moses celebrating deliverance at the Red Sea. Rev 15:3 explicitly titles the victors' song as 'the song of Moses the servant of God AND the song of the Lamb.'

    Notes: Exo 15:1 is one of the oldest poems in the OT. Its celebration of deliverance from oppressive power parallels Rev 15's celebration of victory over the beast. The theophanic language ('The LORD is a man of war') and the reign declaration ('The LORD shall reign for ever and ever' at v.18) both echo in Revelation's hymns.

    Root of Jesse/David — background for Rev 22:16 'root and offspring of David'¶

    And there shall come forth a rod out of the stem of Jesse, and a Branch shall grow out of his roots: ... And in that day there shall be a root of Jesse, which shall stand for an ensign of the people; to it shall the Gentiles seek: and his rest shall be glorious.

    Context: Isaiah's Davidic prophecy. 'Rod out of the stem of Jesse' (v.1) and 'root of Jesse' (v.10). Rev 22:16 has 'I am the root AND the offspring of David' — combining pre-existence (root/source) with incarnation (offspring/descendant).

    Notes: Christ's title at 22:16 combines rhiza (root/source) and genos (offspring/descendant) — both source and product of the Davidic line.

    Star from Jacob prophecy — OT background for 'bright morning star' (Rev 22:16)¶

    I shall see him, but not now: I shall behold him, but not nigh: there shall come a Star out of Jacob, and a Sceptre shall rise out of Israel, and shall smite the corners of Moab, and destroy all the children of Sheth.

    Context: Balaam's prophecy of the star from Jacob. The star is a messianic figure associated with royal power (sceptre). Background for Christ's self-designation as 'the bright and morning star' at Rev 22:16.

    Notes: The star-as-messianic-figure tradition from Num 24:17 underlies both Rev 22:16 (Christ IS the star) and Rev 2:28 (Christ GIVES the morning star to overcomers).

    Feast of Tabernacles — background for palm branches and worship in Rev 7:9-12¶

    And it shall come to pass, [that] every one that is left of all the nations which came against Jerusalem shall even go up from year to year to worship the King, the LORD of hosts, and to keep the feast of tabernacles. And it shall be, [that] whoso will not come up of [all] the families of the earth unto Jerusalem to worship the King, the LORD of hosts, even upon them shall be no rain. And if the family of Egypt go not up, and come not, that [have] no [rain]; there shall be the plague, wherewith the LORD will smite the heathen that come not up to keep the feast of tabernacles.

    Context: Zechariah's eschatological vision of universal worship at the Feast of Tabernacles. Palm branches in Rev 7:9 connect to Tabernacles imagery. 'The King, the LORD of hosts' = YHWH Sabaoth = kyrios pantokrator.

    Notes: The Tabernacles connection to Rev 7:9-12 is through the palm branches and the universal worship of all nations. YHWH Sabaoth in Zechariah = pantokrator in Revelation.

    Son of Man receives dominion — all peoples serve him, divine worship language¶

    And there was given him dominion, and glory, and a kingdom, that all people, nations, and languages, should serve him: his dominion [is] an everlasting dominion, which shall not pass away, and his kingdom [that] which shall not be destroyed.

    Context: Daniel's vision of the Son of Man receiving universal dominion. The Aramaic pelach ('serve') carries worship connotations. Three attributes given: dominion, glory, kingdom. 'All people, nations, and languages' parallels Rev 5:9 (every kindred, tongue, people, nation).

    Notes: The universal worship/service of Dan 7:14 parallels the universal doxology of Rev 5:13. Three attributes (dominion, glory, kingdom) compare with Revelation's attribute patterns.

    Comparative NT Christ-hymn — kenosis, exaltation, universal worship¶

    Who, being in the form of God, thought it not robbery to be equal with God: But made himself of no reputation, and took upon him the form of a servant, and was made in the likeness of men: And being found in fashion as a man, he humbled himself, and became obedient unto death, even the death of the cross. Wherefore God also hath highly exalted him, and given him a name which is above every name: That at the name of Jesus every knee should bow, of [things] in heaven, and [things] in earth, and [things] under the earth; And [that] every tongue should confess that Jesus Christ [is] Lord, to the glory of God the Father.

    Context: Pre-Revelation Christ hymn. Structure: pre-existence (v.6) -> humiliation (v.7-8) -> exaltation (v.9-11). Universal worship in v.10-11 parallels Rev 5:13 (every creature). 'Every knee shall bow' echoes Isa 45:23.

    Notes: Comparative hymnody: Phil 2 has a narrative arc (descent-ascent) while Revelation's hymns are acclamatory (responding to completed events).     Comparative NT confessional hymn fragment — six-line creedal formula¶

    And without controversy great is the mystery of godliness: God was manifest in the flesh, justified in the Spirit, seen of angels, preached unto the Gentiles, believed on in the world, received up into glory.

    Context: Brief confessional/hymnic fragment in six parallel lines. No narrative arc — declarative creedal form. No worship language per se, but summarizes Christ's work in a liturgical-sounding formula.

    Notes: The most compact of the NT hymnic fragments. Revelation's hymns are longer and more varied in form than this compressed creedal statement.

    OT ego eimi background — 'I AM THAT I AM'¶

    And God said unto Moses, I AM THAT I AM: and he said, Thus shalt thou say unto the children of Israel, I AM hath sent me unto you.

    Context: The foundational divine self-declaration. 'Ehyeh asher ehyeh' — I AM THAT I AM. LXX renders as ego eimi ho on (I am the being one). Background for both Johannine and Revelation ego eimi usage.

    Notes: The LXX rendering ego eimi ho on directly connects to Revelation's 'ho on' (the One who is) in the temporal formula at 1:4, 1:8, 4:8.

    Johannine ego eimi parallel — 'Before Abraham was, I am'¶

    Jesus said unto them, Verily, verily, I say unto you, Before Abraham was, I am.

    Context: Jesus' most explosive ego eimi in John's Gospel. The Jews attempt to stone him (v.59), recognizing the divine claim. Relevant as Johannine background for the same author's use of ego eimi in Revelation.

    Notes: Same author (John) uses ego eimi as a divine self-declaration in both the Gospel and Revelation. In the Gospel, ego eimi is a claim to deity that provokes violent reaction. In Revelation, the ego eimi declarations carry the same weight but in a visionary/apocalyptic context.

    Third en pneumati marker — wilderness transport, no hymn at this boundary (structural absence)¶

    So he carried me away in the spirit into the wilderness: and I saw a woman sit upon a scarlet coloured beast, full of names of blasphemy, having seven heads and ten horns.

    And he shewed me a pure river of water of life, clear as crystal, proceeding out of the throne of God and of the Lamb.
