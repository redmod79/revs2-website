    Question¶

    What are Christ's "I Am" (ego eimi) declarations in Revelation, and how do they form a structural frame — particularly the Alpha/Omega declarations in the prologue and epilogue? Document every ego eimi statement, its position, and its thematic content. How do the hymns, doxologies, and worship scenes function as structural markers throughout the book? Document every hymn/doxology, identify who sings, what the content is, and where it falls in the narrative structure. Do the hymns mark structural transitions?

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Three absorbed/dependent prior studies provide a comprehensive foundation: revs-28 (7 ego eimi declarations in critical text, title accumulation pattern at 22:13, OT allusion network to Isa 41:4/44:6/48:12), revs-30 (17 hymnic units across 10 major worship scenes, 8 of 10 at structural seams, participant expansion pattern, thematic progression from creation to consummation, allelouia concentration in Rev 19), revs2-05 (pantokrator all-four-visions thread, temporal formula contraction, thread convergence at Rev 19:6-16). Prior prologue-epilogue work (revs-04, revs2-02) confirms 9 verbal parallels and Strong inclusio. The worship-theology study 3am-14 establishes proskyneo (24x in Revelation, 37% of NT) as the dominant verb and worship as the defining binary. Web research provides scholarly catalogs from Smith (15 hymns), Aune (16 compositions in 7 antiphonal units), and Kou (structural mapping of hymns to narrative events). Gallusz argues Alpha/Omega forms a structural inclusio with merism rhetoric. Bauckham notes Christ shares divine titles, receives worship alongside God, and John avoids plural verbs for God-and-Christ. Bohr claims each hymn responds to a specific historical event that has 'just transpired.' EGW corpus provides leads on Alpha/Omega Christology and speaker identification debate (Smith distinguishes Father at 1:8 vs. Christ at 22:13).

    Primary Verses (31 — full analysis required)¶

    Ego eimi declarations: All explicit and implicit ego eimi / self-declaration passages in Revelation - Rev 2:8 — Smyrna letter opening — Christ's self-identification as 'the First and the Last, who was dead and came to life' (indirect ego eimi formula)

    Hymns at structural boundaries: Hymns and doxologies at key structural transition points - Rev 7:9-12 — Great multitude worship — salvation acclamation (v.10), seven-fold angel doxology (v.12), Tabernacles imagery - Rev 11:15-18 — Seventh trumpet — kingdom proclamation (v.15), elder thanksgiving hymn (v.16-18), temporal formula contraction ('which art and wast' — drops future) - Rev 15:2-4 — Song of Moses and the Lamb — sea of glass, explicit ode designation, creation/nations themes - Rev 19:1-8 — Fourfold allelouia — great multitude (v.1-3), elder/creature amen (v.4), throne voice (v.5), great multitude again (v.6-8), climactic worship, marriage supper announcement

    Non-boundary hymns: Hymns within narrative sections rather than at sequence boundaries - Rev 12:10-12 — Heavenly voice — salvation/kingdom/authority/power acclamation, non-throne-room hymn, cosmic conflict pivot - Rev 16:5-7 — Angel of waters + altar response — justice hymn within bowl sequence, non-throne-room hymn, altar cry-answer arc completion

    Prologue-epilogue inclusio: The opening and closing frames with Alpha/Omega and verbal parallels - Rev 22:6-21 — Epilogue — 'I come quickly' threefold (v.7,12,20), Alpha/Omega + First/Last + Beginning/End triple convergence (v.13), root/offspring/morning star (v.16), worship prohibition redux (v.8-9), maranatha invitation (v.17,20)

    Beast worship parody: Beast temporal formula inversion and negative worship binary - Rev 17:8 — Beast temporal formula 'was, is not, shall ascend' — parody inversion of divine temporal formula
