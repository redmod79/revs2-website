    Question¶

    Who or what are the "seven Spirits of God" (Rev 1:4, 3:1, 4:5, 5:6)? Use Zechariah 3:9 (seven eyes on the messianic stone) and 4:2-10 (seven lamps = eyes of the LORD = "my Spirit") as the OT source texts. How does the identification seven lamps → seven eyes → Spirit of the LORD work as a verbal-parallel chain? What role do the sealed thunders (Rev 10:3-4) play as the only hidden seven in an otherwise unsealed book (Rev 22:10)? How does the number seven function as the book's organizing principle — how many distinct heptads does Revelation contain?

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Eight prior studies provide substantial groundwork. revs2-03 (throne-room anchor) documented Rev 4:5 lampas/lychnia distinction and the sanctuary-compartment conflation. revs-25 (seven Spirits) established the progressive revelation pattern across four occurrences, Trinitarian greeting placement (TM088), Spirit-angel distinction at Rev 3:1 (TM091), singular-plural alternation (SP047), and classified the Zechariah verbal chain as Strong (VP099, 5 elements). revs-29 (sealed thunders) identified the three-layer inverse sealing pattern (SP071), the laleo speech-act (TM131), sphragizo contrast between Rev 10:4 and 22:10 (TM132), and the Dan 12 parallel (VP090). revs-43 traced the systematic Zechariah-to-Revelation transformation pattern (SP102). revs-27 documented the seven beatitudes. Web research identified the Bauckham vs. Aune/Koester scholarly debate, Felix Just's heptad catalog (15-20 explicit groups), and Bousset's Leviticus 26 theory. EGW/pioneer writers uniformly identify seven Spirits as Holy Spirit in fullness. Clarke provides the sole dissenting voice among historical commentators, arguing for seven angels.

    Focus Areas¶

    1. The Zechariah verbal-parallel chain: lamps to eyes to Spirit: This is the decisive chain. In Zechariah 3:9, the stone (H68 eben) has seven eyes (H5869 ayin) — READ library entry $WORD_STUDIES/H68-eben.md if available. In Zech 4:2, the lampstand has seven lamps (H5216 ner/nerah) with seven pipes. In Zech 4:10, these become 'the eyes of the LORD which run to and fro through the whole earth.' In Zech 4:6, the angel declares 'not by might nor by power but by my Spirit (H7307 ruach).' READ $WORD_STUDIES/H7307-ruach.md for the Spirit term. Revelation picks up the chain: Rev 4:5 uses lampas (G2985) for seven lamps = seven Spirits — READ $WORD_STUDIES/G2985-lampas.md. Rev 5:6 uses ophthalmos (G3788) for seven eyes = seven Spirits sent (apestalmenoi G649, perfect passive participle) into all the earth — READ $WORD_STUDIES/G649-apostello.md. Run greek_parser.py --verse on Rev 4:5 and Rev 5:6 to get full morphological data. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-ot on both verses. Verify whether the LXX uses identical terms for the Hebrew originals. Check PMI values between the key terms across the chain. Verses: Zech 3:9, Zech 4:2, Zech 4:6, Zech 4:10, Rev 4:5, Rev 5:6 Strong's: H68, H5869, H5216, H7307, G2985, G3788, G649

    2. Trinitarian greeting structure at Rev 1:4-5: The greeting formula at Rev 1:4-5 reads 'Grace to you and peace from (apo) him who is and was and is to come, and from (apo) the seven Spirits which are before his throne, and from (apo) Jesus Christ.' The research agent should run greek_parser.py --verse on Rev 1:4 and Rev 1:5 to verify that the same preposition apo (G575) governs all three sources. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G4151-pneuma.md for the Spirit term. Compare this structure with standard Pauline greetings (Rom 1:7, 1 Cor 1:3, 2 Cor 1:2, Gal 1:3, Eph 1:2, Phil 1:2) which use 'from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ' — two sources, no angels. If the seven Spirits are angels, Rev 1:4-5 uniquely inserts created beings as a co-equal source of divine grace. The grammar reference entry on genitive-of-quality-apposition ($GRAMMAR_REF/greek/genitive-of-quality-apposition.md) may be relevant for the genitive construction 'Spirits of God.' Verses: Rev 1:4-5, Rom 1:7, 1 Cor 1:3, 2 Cor 1:2 Strong's: G575, G4151, G5485, G1515

    3. The Spirit-angel distinction: Rev 3:1 and Rev 8:2: Rev 3:1 has Christ holding (echon G2192, present active participle) two distinct objects: 'the seven Spirits of God AND (kai) the seven stars.' Run greek_parser.py --verse on Rev 3:1 to confirm the conjunction structure. Separately, Rev 8:2 introduces 'the seven angels which stood before God' (hoi hepta angeloi hoi enopion tou theou hestekotes) — an entirely different phrase from 'the seven Spirits which are before his throne' (Rev 1:4).
