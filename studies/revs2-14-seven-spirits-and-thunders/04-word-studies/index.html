    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    Who or what are the "seven Spirits of God" (Rev 1:4, 3:1, 4:5, 5:6)? Use Zechariah 3:9 (seven eyes on the messianic stone) and 4:2-10 (seven lamps = eyes of the LORD = "my Spirit") as the OT source texts. How does the identification seven lamps → seven eyes → Spirit of the LORD work as a verbal-parallel chain? What role do the sealed thunders (Rev 10:3-4) play as the only hidden seven in an otherwise unsealed book (Rev 22:10)? How does the number seven function as the book's organizing principle — how many distinct heptads does Revelation contain?

    G4151 — pneuma¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4151-pneuma.md

    Occurrences: 385

    Spirit/spirit/Ghost/wind. 385 NT occurrences. Dominant usage is Holy Spirit/Spirit of God as person, agent, and gift. In Revelation: 25 occurrences, distributed between singular 'the Spirit' (to Pneuma — 7x in church letters + 14:13 + 22:17) and plural 'the seven Spirits' (ta hepta Pneumata — 4x at 1:4, 3:1, 4:5, 5:6). Also includes unclean/evil spirits and human spirit/disposition.

    Semantic categories: Holy Spirit / Spirit of God, Spirit as seal and guarantee, unclean/evil spirits, human spirit/disposition, wind/breath

    Key verses: Rev 1:4, Rev 3:1, Rev 4:5, Rev 5:6, Rev 2:7, Rev 14:13, Rev 22:17

    Relevance: Core term. The singular-plural alternation pattern (SP047) is the key to understanding the seven Spirits: same entity in two reference modes. The seven Spirits appear in visionary/symbolic contexts; the singular Spirit speaks directly.

    G2985 — lampas¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2985-lampas.md

    Occurrences: 9

    Lamp/torch. 9 NT occurrences across 4 books (Matthew 5, Revelation 2, John 1, Acts 1). In Revelation: Rev 4:5 (seven lamps of fire = seven Spirits) and Rev 8:10 (star burning as a lamp). Distinguished from lychnia (G3087, lampstand) and lychnos (G3088, portable lamp).

    Semantic categories: handheld lamp for procession, lamp going out/requiring fuel, military/official torch, heavenly/apocalyptic fire-lamp

    Key verses: Rev 4:5, Rev 8:10, Mat 25:1-8

    Relevance: Rev 4:5 identifies lampades pyros (lamps of fire) as the seven Spirits. The LXX reportedly uses lampas for the menorah lamps in Exodus 25, creating lexical continuity between the tabernacle lampstand and Rev 4:5.

    G3087 — lychnia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3087-lychnia.md

    Occurrences: 11

    Lampstand/candlestick. 11 NT occurrences, concentrated in Revelation (6 of 11). The stand that holds the lamp (lychnos). In Revelation: used exclusively for the seven churches (1:12, 1:13, 1:20, 2:1, 2:5) and the two witnesses (11:4). Never used for the seven Spirits.

    Semantic categories: lampstand holding a lamp, church as light-bearer, two witnesses as lampstands

    Key verses: Rev 1:12, Rev 1:20, Rev 2:5, Rev 11:4

    Relevance: Distinguished from lampas — lychnia represents the church/witnesses, not the Spirit. The non-overlapping distribution confirms the light-word triad in Revelation.

    G3088 — lychnos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3088-lychnos.md

    Occurrences: 14

    Portable lamp/candle. 14 NT occurrences. LXX's primary rendering of Hebrew ner (H5216). In Revelation: appears in eschatological resolution passages — Rev 21:23 (the Lamb is the light/lychnos of the city) and Rev 22:5 (they need no candle/lychnos).

    Semantic categories: household lamp on a stand, eye as lamp of the body, prophetic word as lamp, Christ as lamp of the city

    Key verses: Rev 21:23, Rev 22:5, Mat 5:15, 2 Pet 1:19

    Relevance: Third light-word completing the triad: lychnia (churches), lampas (Spirits), lychnos (eschatological resolution). The distribution is non-overlapping in Revelation.

    G4972 — sphragizo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4972-sphragizo.md

    Occurrences: 28

    Seal verb. 28 NT occurrences (15 unique verses), 72% in Revelation. Forms: aorist active (seal a document/tomb), aorist passive (believers sealed by God), perfect passive participle (the sealed ones). In Rev 10:4: Sphragison (aorist active imperative, 'seal up!'). In Rev 22:10: me sphragises (aorist active subjunctive with prohibitive me, 'do NOT seal').

    Semantic categories: physical sealing/security, divine sealing of believers, sealing of divine content, divine ownership marking

    Key verses: Rev 10:4, Rev 22:10, Rev 7:3, Eph 1:13, Eph 4:30, Mat 27:66

    Relevance: Central to the three-layer inverse sealing pattern (SP071). Same verb, contradictory commands at Rev 10:4 (seal) and Rev 22:10 (do not seal).

    G2696 — katasphragizo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2696-katasphragizo.md

    Occurrences: 1

    Seal completely/thoroughly. NT hapax at Rev 5:1. Intensified form of sphragizo (G4972) with kata- prefix. Perfect passive participle katesphragismenon — 'having been completely sealed.' Used for the scroll in God's right hand sealed with seven seals.

    Semantic categories: intensive/thorough sealing

    Key verses: Rev 5:1

    Relevance: First layer of the three-layer sealing pattern. The kata- intensifier emphasizes the thoroughness of the scroll's sealing — yet it is progressively OPENED through chs. 6-8.

    G4973 — sphragis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4973-sphragis.md

    Occurrences: 16

    Seal noun. 16 NT occurrences, 81% in Revelation (13 of 16). Used for: authentication/certification (Rom 4:11, 1 Cor 9:2), divine concealment (the seven seals on the scroll), sequential judgment unveiling (seal openings), and divine ownership marking (Rev 7:2).

    Semantic categories: authentication/certification, divine concealment/authorization, sequential judgment unveiling, divine ownership/protection

    Key verses: Rev 5:1, Rev 5:2, Rev 6:1, Rev 7:2, Rev 9:4

    Relevance: The seven seals (sphragides) on the scroll form one of Revelation's major heptads. Their progressive opening contrasts with the thunders' permanent sealing.

    G1027 — bronte¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1027-bronte.md

    Occurrences: 12

    Thunder. 12 tokens across 11 verses, 82% in Revelation (9 of 11). Four semantic categories: (1) throne-room theophanic formula (Rev 4:5, 8:5, 11:19, 16:18), (2) seven thunders personified/sealed (Rev 10:3-4), (3) thunder as voice of majesty (Rev 6:1, 14:2, 19:6), (4) epithet 'Boanerges, sons of thunder' (Mark 3:17).

    Semantic categories: throne-room theophanic formula, seven thunders personified/sealed, thunder as voice of majesty, epithet Boanerges

    Key verses: Rev 10:3, Rev 10:4, Rev 4:5, John 12:29, Mark 3:17

    Relevance: The seven thunders are unique: the only place in Revelation where thunder is the grammatical SUBJECT of intelligible speech (laleo). Connected to Psalm 29's sevenfold qol YHWH.

    G2980 — laleo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2980-laleo.md

    Occurrences: 308

    Speak/talk. 308 tokens across 271 verses and 23 books. Overwhelmingly active voice (93%). In Rev 10:3-4: elalisan (aorist active indicative 3rd plural) with the seven thunders as subject — they 'spoke' intelligible content. In 2 Cor 12:4: lalesai (aorist active infinitive) — words 'not lawful to speak.'

    Semantic categories: divine speech, prophetic utterance, general communication, prohibited speech

    Key verses: Rev 10:3, Rev 10:4, 2 Cor 12:4

    Relevance: The verb connecting the thunders to intelligible speech. The thunders laleo — they do not merely make noise but speak understandable content that John was about to record.

    G649 — apostello¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G649-apostellō.md

    Occurrences: 144

    Send forth/set apart and send. 144 NT occurrences. In Rev 5:6: apestalmenoi (perfect passive participle nominative plural masculine) — 'having been sent forth.' Describes the seven Spirits/eyes as permanently in the state of being sent into all the earth.

    Semantic categories: divine commissioning, authoritative sending, permanent state of mission

    Key verses: Rev 5:6, John 20:21, Heb 3:1

    Relevance: The perfect passive participle apestalmenoi at Rev 5:6 indicates a completed action with continuing effect — the seven Spirits have been sent and remain sent. This describes a permanent operative condition, not a single historical sending (contra Bohr's Pentecost-only reading).

    H7307 — ruach¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7307-ruach.md

    Occurrences: 434

    Spirit/wind/breath. 434 OT occurrences. Primary categories: Spirit of God/divine Spirit (Gen 1:2, Isa 11:2, Joel 2:28), wind/meteorological force, breath/life-force, human spirit/disposition. In Zech 4:6: be-ruchi ('by my Spirit') — the interpretive key linking the lamps/eyes to the divine Spirit.

    Semantic categories: Spirit of God/divine Spirit, wind/meteorological force, breath/life-force, human spirit/disposition, divine empowerment

    Key verses: Zech 4:6, Isa 11:2, Gen 1:2, Joel 2:28, Eze 36:27

    Relevance: The OT counterpart to pneuma. Zech 4:6 'my Spirit' (ruchi) is the key that unlocks the lampstand vision: the seven lamps = eyes of the LORD = the Spirit of God.

    H6963 — qowl¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6963-qowl.md

    Occurrences: 543

    Voice/sound/noise. 543 tokens across 436 verses and 35 books. Dominant translation: 'voice' (78%). In Psalm 29:3-9: qol YHWH appears exactly 7 times — the sevenfold 'voice of the LORD' equated with thunder via the Hiphil of ra'am (H7481) at v.3.

    Semantic categories: divine voice/theophany, thunder as divine speech, human voice/cry, sound/noise

    Key verses: Psa 29:3-9, Exo 19:16, 1 Sam 12:17-18, Deu 4:12

    Relevance: The sevenfold qol YHWH in Psalm 29 is the OT background for the seven thunders of Rev 10. Thunder = God's voice is an established OT pattern.

    H2856 — chatham¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2856-chatham.md

    Occurrences: 26

    Seal/close up. 26 tokens across 24 verses. Primarily Qal stem (22x). In Dan 12:4: chatom (Qal imperative 2ms, 'seal') the book — the Hebrew sealing command with temporal limit ('until the time of the end'). Compare Greek sphragizo (G4972) used in the same conceptual role in Revelation.

    Semantic categories: royal/legal authentication, divine concealment with temporal limit, cosmic closure, personal marking

    Key verses: Dan 12:4, Dan 12:9, Dan 9:24, Isa 8:16, Job 9:7

    Relevance: The Hebrew seal vocabulary. Dan 12:4 chatom has a temporal limit; Rev 10:4 sphragison does not. This distinction is critical for understanding the uniqueness of the thunders' sealing.

    G5456 — phone¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5456-phone.md

    Occurrences: 141

    Voice/sound. 141 tokens across 129 verses. Revelation has the highest concentration: 44 occurrences (10.9 per 100 verses). In Rev 10:4: phonen ek tou ouranou — 'a voice from heaven' commanding the sealing. In the theophanic formula: phones kai brontai (voices and thunderings).

    Semantic categories: divine voice/heavenly command, theophanic sound, human speech, angelic proclamation

    Key verses: Rev 10:4, Rev 4:5, Rev 1:10, John 12:28-29

    Relevance: The heavenly voice that commands the sealing of the thunders. Also part of the theophanic formula at Rev 4:5 (lightnings, thunderings, and voices).

    G602 — apokalypsis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G602-apokalypsis.md

    Occurrences: 18

    Revelation/disclosure/unveiling. 18 NT occurrences. Etymologically: apo (away from) + kalypto (to cover) = uncovering. In Rev 1:1: 'The Revelation (Apokalypsis) of Jesus Christ' — the book's title declares it an unveiling. The sealed thunders stand in deliberate tension with this title.

    Semantic categories: disclosure of divine truth, eschatological unveiling, Christ's appearing

    Key verses: Rev 1:1, Rom 16:25, 1 Cor 2:10, Gal 1:12

    Relevance: The book's very title (Apokalypsis = unveiling) creates a structural tension with the sealed thunders (Rev 10:4). In a book named 'Unveiling,' the thunders remain the sole veiled content.

    G2768 — keras¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2768-keras.md

    Occurrences: 11

    Horn. 11 tokens across 10 verses, 90% in Revelation. Symbol of power/strength/dominion. In Rev 5:6: the Lamb has seven horns (power) and seven eyes (omniscience) = seven Spirits (omnipresence).

    Semantic categories: metaphor for power/salvation, apocalyptic symbol of political power, altar horns

    Key verses: Rev 5:6, Rev 12:3, Rev 13:1, Rev 17:12, Luk 1:69

    Relevance: The seven horns at Rev 5:6 represent complete/perfect power, complementing the seven eyes (complete knowledge/perception) and seven Spirits (complete divine operation).

    H5869 — ayin¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5869-ayin.md

    Occurrences: 918

    Eye/fountain. 918 OT occurrences across 240 unique translations. Primary meaning: physical eye (literal) and 'in the sight of' (figurative). In Zech 3:9: shib'ah einayim — seven eyes on the messianic stone. In Zech 4:10: einei YHWH — eyes of the LORD running to and fro.

    Semantic categories: physical eye, divine omniscience (eyes of the LORD), appearance/sight, fountain/spring

    Key verses: Zech 3:9, Zech 4:10, 2 Chr 16:9, Prov 15:3

    Relevance: Central to the verbal-parallel chain. The seven 'eyes' (einayim) on the stone (Zech 3:9) become 'the eyes of the LORD' (einei YHWH, Zech 4:10) which become 'seven eyes which are the seven Spirits' (Rev 5:6, ophthalmous = G3788).

    H68 — eben¶

    Occurrences: 315

    Stone. 315 OT occurrences. In Zech 3:9: the stone (ha'eben) laid before Joshua with seven eyes. Connected to messianic stone imagery (Isa 28:16, Psa 118:22, Dan 2:34-35). In Rev 5:6, the stone becomes the Lamb — the messianic figure bearing the seven eyes/Spirits.

    Semantic categories: building material, boundary/legal marker, messianic stone, weaponry/judgment

    Key verses: Zech 3:9, Isa 28:16, Psa 118:22, Dan 2:34-35

    Relevance: The messianic stone with seven eyes (Zech 3:9) is appropriated by Revelation as the Lamb with seven eyes/Spirits (Rev 5:6). Stone → Lamb, seven eyes → seven Spirits, sin-removal → Lamb-as-slain.

    G3788 — ophthalmos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3788-ophthalmós.md

    Occurrences: 102

    Eye. 102 NT occurrences. In Revelation: 10 occurrences concentrated in christological visions. Rev 5:6: ophthalmous hepta — seven eyes of the Lamb = seven Spirits. Rev 1:14, 19:12: eyes as a flame of fire (christophany descriptions).

    Semantic categories: physical organ of sight, spiritual perception, divine omniscience, envy/desire

    Key verses: Rev 5:6, Rev 1:14, Rev 19:12, Mat 6:22-23

    Relevance: Greek counterpart to Hebrew ayin (H5869). Rev 5:6 uses ophthalmos for the Lamb's seven eyes, translating the concept from Zech 3:9/4:10 into Greek apocalyptic vocabulary.

    G2033 — hepta¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2033-heptá.md

    Occurrences: 87

    Seven. 87 NT occurrences. Revelation alone accounts for 54 (62%), making it the most seven-saturated book in the Bible (55.1 per 100 verses). The number structures nearly every major symbol: churches, spirits, lampstands, stars, seals, trumpets, bowls, thunders, heads, horns, eyes, plagues, mountains, kings.

    Semantic categories: completeness/divine fullness, apocalyptic structuring, narrative patterns in Gospels, leadership/institutional order

    Key verses: Rev 1:4, Rev 4:5, Rev 5:6, Rev 8:2, Rev 10:3, Rev 15:1

    Relevance: The organizing principle of Revelation. 54 of 87 NT occurrences are in Revelation — the number seven is not merely a motif but the book's architectural framework.

    G3466 — mysterion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3466-mystērion.md

    Occurrences: 27

    Mystery — divine secret requiring revelation for disclosure. 27 NT occurrences. In Revelation: 4 occurrences (1:20, 10:7, 17:5, 17:7). At Rev 10:7: the mystery of God 'should be finished' and 'hath been declared to his servants the prophets' — this is REVEALED content, unlike the sealed thunders.

    Semantic categories: mysteries of the kingdom, God's pre-temporal secret now disclosed, mystery of Christ and Gentile inclusion, apocalyptic mystery to be completed

    Key verses: Rev 10:7, Rev 1:20, Rev 17:5, Rev 17:7

    Relevance: Critical for distinguishing the mystery of God (Rev 10:7, revealed and being completed) from the sealed thunders (Rev 10:4, sealed and not disclosed). The text does NOT equate these.

    G731 — arrhetos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G731-árrhētos.md

    Occurrences: 1

    Unspeakable. NT hapax at 2 Cor 12:4. Alpha-privative + rhetos ('spoken/stated') = 'not-to-be-spoken.' Paul heard arreta rhemata (unspeakable words) in paradise — the prohibition is on speaking, not on understanding. Parallels Rev 10:4: both involve comprehensible divine communication deliberately withheld.

    Semantic categories: divine prohibition of speech, sealed/restricted revelation

    Key verses: 2 Cor 12:4

    Relevance: Closest NT analogy to the sealed thunders. Both Paul and John comprehended divine speech and were forbidden to communicate it. Both use laleo (G2980) for the prohibited speech.

    H4501 — menorah¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4501-mᵉnôwrâh.md

    Occurrences: 44

    Lampstand. 44 OT occurrences, concentrated in Exodus (22) for the tabernacle lampstand design and construction. In Zech 4:2: menorat zahab — golden lampstand with seven lamps. The LXX renders menorah with lychnia (G3087), establishing the Hebrew-Greek translation chain.

    Semantic categories: tabernacle/temple furnishing, ritual service and maintenance, visionary lampstand (Zechariah)

    Key verses: Exo 25:31, Exo 37:17, Zech 4:2, Zech 4:11

    Relevance: The Hebrew source for the lampstand imagery. LXX menorah → lychnia (G3087, PMI 9.56 per revs-43). The seven lamps ON the menorah use ner (H5216), rendered by LXX as lampas (G2985) — connecting directly to Rev 4:5.

    G3107 — makarios¶

    Key verses: Rev 1:3, Rev 14:13, Rev 16:15, Rev 19:9, Rev 20:6, Rev 22:7, Rev 22:14

    Relevance: One of the 'subtler sevens' — exactly 7 makarios beatitudes in Revelation, forming a heptadic spine across the book.

    H7481 — raam¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7481-raam.md`

    Occurrences: 17

    Thunder/roar. 17 OT occurrences. Hiphil stem used exclusively for God as subject — He 'causes thunder.' In Psa 29:3: hir'im (Hiphil perfect 3ms) — 'the God of glory thundered,' explicitly equating the 'voice of the LORD' (qol YHWH) with thunder.

    Semantic categories: divine thunder/theophanic voice, creation's roar responding to God, agitation and distress

    Key verses: Psa 29:3, 1 Sam 2:10, 1 Sam 7:10, 2 Sam 22:14, Job 37:4-5

    Relevance: The Hiphil at Psa 29:3 bridges the 'voice of the LORD' and thunder, establishing the OT pattern that the seven thunders of Rev 10 inherit.

    H5216 — ner/niyr¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5216-niyr.md

    Occurrences: 53

    Lamp (the burner). 53 OT occurrences. Primary usage: tabernacle/temple lamps on the menorah (Exo 25:37, 27:20, 30:7-8). Also used metaphorically for the Davidic dynasty ('a lamp for David'). In Zech 4:2: shib'ah neroteha — 'his seven lamps' on the golden lampstand.

    Semantic categories: tabernacle/temple lamp service, dynastic promise/covenant continuity, human life and prosperity, divine illumination

    Key verses: Exo 25:37, Zech 4:2, 2 Sam 22:29, 1 Ki 11:36, Psa 18:28

    Relevance: The Hebrew lamp term in Zech 4:2. The LXX renders this with lampas (G2985) in Exodus 25:37, creating the lexical bridge to Rev 4:5's lampades = seven Spirits.
