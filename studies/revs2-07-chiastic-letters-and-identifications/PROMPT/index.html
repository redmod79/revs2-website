    Question¶

    Do the seven letters form a chiastic arrangement (Ephesus-Laodicea, Smyrna-Philadelphia, Pergamos-Sardis, with Thyatira at center), and how do Christ's self-identifications at the opening of each letter connect back to the vision of the glorified Christ in Rev 1:12-20? Document the specific element from the Rev 1 vision that each letter draws on.

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Eight prior studies provide foundational data. revs-10 established the overall chiasm as Moderate (SP024), with the B-B' pair (Smyrna-Philadelphia) rated Strong (VP046, 8 shared elements), A-A' (Ephesus-Laodicea) rated Weak (VP048, 1 thematic link), and C-C' (Pergamos-Sardis) rated Minimal (VP047, 2 verbal links). revs-11 mapped all seven self-IDs to Rev 1, establishing SP1 (self-ID/problem correspondence, Strong), SP2 (progressive source-divergence, Moderate), and SP3 (functional-vs-appearance selection, Strong). revs2-06 established the eight-element template (SP021 Strong), ear/overcomer reversal (SP022 Strong), symmetric rebuke-absence (SP023 Moderate), and Thyatira centrality (TM044). Web research identified scholarly proposals from Hoskins, Paulien, Wahlen, Strand, Beale/Snyder, Ramsay, and Mueller. Key leads include: Paulien's menorah-chiasm connection, Ramsay's compositional argument (Rev 1 composed FROM the letters), Wahlen's observation that Philadelphia/Laodicea titles point FORWARD not back to Rev 1, and the tade legei LXX background (350 occurrences, 320 with YHWH as subject).

    Primary Verses (35 — full analysis required)¶

    Rev 1 vision passage: Complete vision of glorified Christ — source for self-IDs - Rev 1:11 — Alpha and Omega reiteration, command to write to seven churches - Rev 1:12-13 — Vision foundation: seven golden candlesticks, Son of man in midst, garment, golden girdle - Rev 1:14 — Vision: white hair like wool (Dan 7:9 Ancient of Days merger), eyes as flame of fire - Rev 1:15 — Vision: feet like fine brass (chalkolibanon), voice as many waters - Rev 1:16 — Vision: seven stars in right hand, sharp twoedged sword from mouth, countenance as sun - Rev 1:17-18 — Vision: first and last, living one who was dead, alive forevermore, keys of hell and death - Rev 1:20 — Explicit interpretation: seven stars = angels of churches, seven candlesticks = seven churches

    Rev 1 prologue titles: Prologue Christological titles — source for Sardis and Laodicea self-IDs - Rev 1:4-5 — Prologue titles: seven Spirits, faithful witness, first begotten of dead, prince of kings — source for Sardis and Laodicea self-IDs - Rev 1:8 — Alpha and Omega, beginning and ending — potential source for Laodicea's 'beginning of creation'

    Seven self-ID openings: All tade legei self-identification sections - Rev 2:1 — Ephesus self-ID: holdeth seven stars, walketh in midst of candlesticks — maps to 1:13+1:16 with verbal intensification - Rev 2:8 — Smyrna self-ID: first and last, was dead and is alive — maps to 1:17-18 - Rev 2:12 — Pergamos self-ID: sharp sword with two edges — maps to 1:16 - Rev 2:18 — Thyatira self-ID: Son of God, eyes like flame of fire, feet like fine brass — maps to 1:14-15, with Son of Man replaced by Son of God - Rev 3:1 — Sardis self-ID: seven Spirits of God and seven stars — maps to 1:4 (Spirits) + 1:16 (stars), mix of vision and prologue - Rev 3:7 — Philadelphia self-ID: holy, true, key of David, openeth/shutteth — NOT in Rev 1:12-20; key concept from Isa 22:22, holy/true from Rev 19:11 - Rev 3:14 — Laodicea self-ID: the Amen, faithful and true witness, beginning of creation of God — NOT in Rev 1:12-20; maps to 1:5 (faithful witness) and possibly 1:8 (beginning/ending)

    OT theophany sources: Daniel vision passages underlying Rev 1 imagery - Dan 7:9 — OT source: Ancient of Days with white-as-wool hair — merged with Son of Man in Rev 1:14 - Dan 7:13-14 — OT source: Son of man coming to Ancient of Days, receiving dominion — Rev 1:13 'one like unto the Son of man' - Dan 10:5-6 — OT source: theophany composite — linen garment, gold girdle, eyes like lamps of fire, feet like polished brass, voice like a multitude

    Isaiah source texts: Isaianic sources for first/last title and key of David - Isa 44:6 — OT source: YHWH as 'the first, and the last; and beside me there is no God' — applied to Christ in Rev 1:17, 2:8

    Additional Primary Verses: - Rev 2:2-6 — Ephesus body: works, labour, patience, tested false apostles, hates Nicolaitans — but left first love - Rev 2:7 — Ephesus overcomer promise: tree of life in paradise — ear BEFORE overcomer (position 1 of 3) - Rev 2:9-10 — Smyrna body: tribulation, poverty (but rich), synagogue of Satan, faithful unto death/crown of life - Rev 2:11 — Smyrna overcomer promise: not hurt of second death — ear BEFORE overcomer (position 2 of 3) - Rev 2:13-16 — Pergamos body: Satan's seat, Antipas martyr, Balaam doctrine, Nicolaitans, sword threat - Rev 2:17 — Pergamos overcomer promise: hidden manna, white stone, new name — ear BEFORE overcomer (position 3 of 3) - Rev 2:19-25 — Thyatira body: charity, service, faith, patience, last more than first, Jezebel, depths of Satan - Rev 2:26-29 — Thyatira overcomer promise: power over nations, morning star — overcomer BEFORE ear (position 4, pivot) - Rev 3:2-4 — Sardis body: name that livest but dead, works not perfect, few names not defiled, white garments - Rev 3:5-6 — Sardis overcomer promise: white raiment, book of life, confess name — overcomer BEFORE ear - Rev 3:8-10 — Philadelphia body: open door, little strength, kept word, synagogue of Satan, hour of temptation - Rev 3:11-13 — Philadelphia overcomer promise: pillar in temple, name of God, new Jerusalem, new name — overcomer BEFORE ear - Rev 3:15-19 — Laodicea body: lukewarm, self-deceived about riches, wretched/blind/naked, gold/white raiment/eyesalve counsel, rebuke and chasten - Rev 3:20-22 — Laodicea overcomer promise: throne sharing, door knocking — overcomer BEFORE ear; bookend with Ephesus - Isa 22:22 — OT source: key of house of David — 'he shall open, and none shall shut; and he shall shut, and none shall open' — near-verbatim in Rev 3:7
