    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    Do the seven letters form a chiastic arrangement (Ephesus-Laodicea, Smyrna-Philadelphia, Pergamos-Sardis, with Thyatira at center), and how do Christ's self-identifications at the opening of each letter connect back to the vision of the glorified Christ in Rev 1:12-20? Document the specific element from the Rev 1 vision that each letter draws on.

    G2902 — krateo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2902-krateo.md

    Occurrences: 50

    To use strength, seize, hold firmly. In Rev 2:1, Christ 'holdeth' (krateo, V-PAP-NSM) the seven stars — a verbal intensification from echon (having, G2192) in Rev 1:16. krateo implies firm sovereign grasp, not mere possession. 8 occurrences in Revelation.

    Semantic categories: physical seizing, firm retention, sovereign authority

    Key verses: Rev 2:1, Rev 1:16, Rev 2:13, Rev 2:14, Rev 2:15, Rev 2:25

    Relevance: The echon→krateo intensification (TM047) is the primary verbal evidence for compositional transformation between the Rev 1 vision and the Ephesus self-ID. krateo also appears in Pergamos (2:13 'holdest fast my name') and Thyatira context (2:25 'hold fast till I come').

    G4043 — peripateo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4043-peripateo.md

    Occurrences: 97

    To walk about, tread all around. From peri (around) + pateo (to tread). Used literally for physical walking and figuratively for conduct/manner of life. 5 occurrences in Revelation. In Rev 2:1, Christ 'walketh' (ho peripateon, V-PAP-NSM) in the midst of the lampstands — a verbal intensification from static en meso in Rev 1:13.

    Semantic categories: physical walking, moral/ethical conduct, priestly ministry

    Key verses: Rev 2:1, Rev 3:4, Rev 21:24

    Relevance: The static en meso→active peripateo intensification (TM048) transforms Christ from a figure standing among lampstands to one actively walking among them. The LXX maps peripateo to Hebrew halak (H1980, PMI 5.17). EGW interprets this as 'untiring wakefulness, unremitting vigilance.' The priestly lamp-tending background (Exo 27:20-21) connects walking among lampstands to ongoing sanctuary ministry.

    G792 — aster¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G792-aster.md

    Occurrences: 24

    Star. 13 occurrences in Revelation (62% of NT total). The seven stars in Christ's right hand (Rev 1:16, 1:20, 2:1, 3:1) are explicitly identified as 'the angels of the seven churches' (1:20). The stars appear in the Ephesus self-ID (2:1, with krateo) and Sardis self-ID (3:1, with echon).

    Semantic categories: symbolic authority, angelic beings, celestial objects

    Key verses: Rev 1:16, Rev 1:20, Rev 2:1, Rev 3:1

    Relevance: The seven stars create a structural link between the Rev 1 vision, the Ephesus self-ID, and the Sardis self-ID. Ephesus uses krateo (holds firmly), while Sardis uses echon (having) — the verbal difference is the opposite of what might be expected from TM047.

    G3087 — lychnia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3087-lychnia.md

    Occurrences: 12

    Lampstand/candlestick. From lychnos (lamp). The seven golden lampstands = the seven churches (Rev 1:20). Appears in the Rev 1 vision (1:12, 1:13, 1:20), the Ephesus self-ID (2:1), and the Ephesus threat (2:5 lampstand removal). Also in Rev 11:4 (two witnesses as lampstands).

    Semantic categories: sanctuary furniture, church symbol, light-bearing

    Key verses: Rev 1:12, Rev 1:13, Rev 1:20, Rev 2:1, Rev 2:5

    Relevance: The lampstand provides the menorah connection for Paulien's structural proposal. Christ walks among lampstands (2:1) — priestly lamp-tending function. The threat to remove Ephesus' lampstand (2:5) uses the self-ID imagery as the instrument of judgment, paralleling the sword threat in Pergamos (2:16).

    G4501 — rhomphaia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4501-rhomphaia.md

    Occurrences: 7

    A large sword/sabre. Distinguished from the shorter machaira (G3162). 6 of 7 NT occurrences are in Revelation. In Rev 1:16 the sharp two-edged sword proceeds from Christ's mouth; this is quoted in the Pergamos self-ID (2:12) and becomes the instrument of judgment (2:16). Also appears in Rev 19:15, 19:21.

    Semantic categories: judicial authority, divine word as weapon, Christological attribute

    Key verses: Rev 1:16, Rev 2:12, Rev 2:16, Rev 19:15, Rev 19:21

    Relevance: The sword connects the Rev 1 vision to the Pergamos self-ID and to the eschatological judgment in Rev 19. The Bohr observation about eyes/sword extending to Rev 19:12-15 is verified: Rev 19:12 has 'eyes as a flame of fire' (from 1:14) and 19:15 has 'sharp sword out of his mouth' (from 1:16) — both vision elements reappear together in the final battle.

    G1366 — distomos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1366-dístomos.md

    Occurrences: 3

    Two-edged, double-mouthed. From dis (twice) + stoma (mouth). Only 3 NT occurrences: Rev 1:16, Rev 2:12, and Heb 4:12. Always collocated with oxys (sharp). In Revelation, paired with rhomphaia; in Hebrews, with machaira.

    Semantic categories: divine word as weapon, judicial authority

    Key verses: Rev 1:16, Rev 2:12, Heb 4:12

    Relevance: The three-occurrence distribution makes distomos a marker linking the Rev 1 vision to the Pergamos self-ID. Its presence in Heb 4:12 connects the concept of God's penetrating word to the Revelation imagery.

    G5474 — chalkolibanon¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5474-chalkolibanon.md

    Occurrences: 2

    A compound of chalkos (copper/brass) and libanos (frankincense/whiteness). A metal of uncertain composition — burnished bronze or fine brass. Only 2 NT occurrences, both in Revelation: 1:15 (vision) and 2:18 (Thyatira self-ID). A lexical hapax outside Revelation entirely.

    Semantic categories: theophanic radiance, judgment and purity, OT theophanic background

    Key verses: Rev 1:15, Rev 2:18

    Relevance: The exclusivity of chalkolibanon to Rev 1:15 and 2:18 makes it one of the strongest lexical links between any self-ID and the vision (TM046). The OT background is Dan 10:6 nechoshet qalal (polished brass). The furnace-refined quality evokes judgment — feet of glowing brass treading in judicial authority.

    G4413 — protos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4413-protos.md

    Occurrences: 105

    First — foremost in time, place, order, or importance. Contracted superlative of pro (before). In Rev 1:17 and 2:8, ho protos kai ho eschatos ('the first and the last') — a title drawn from YHWH's self-declaration in Isa 41:4, 44:6, 48:12. Also 'first love' (proten agapen, 2:4) and 'first works' (prota erga, 2:5) in Ephesus.

    Semantic categories: temporal priority, ordinal sequence, rank/primacy

    Key verses: Rev 1:17, Rev 2:8, Rev 2:4, Rev 2:5, Rev 22:13

    Relevance: protos appears in the Smyrna self-ID as part of the 'first and last' divine title, and in the Ephesus letter as 'first love' and 'first works' — different semantic domains (rank vs. temporal) in different contexts.

    G2807 — kleis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2807-kleis.md

    Occurrences: 6

    Key. Symbol of authority. 4 of 6 NT occurrences in Revelation. Rev 1:18 has 'keys of hell and of death' (kleis tou thanatou kai tou Haidou); Rev 3:7 has 'key of David' (kleis Daueid) from Isa 22:22. Different keys, different sources.

    Semantic categories: authority/access, keys held by the risen Christ, key of the abyss

    Key verses: Rev 1:18, Rev 3:7, Rev 9:1, Rev 20:1

    Relevance: The two key references in the letters use different vocabulary: 1:18 'keys of hell and death' (plural, sovereignty over death) vs. 3:7 'key of David' (singular, from Isa 22:22, Davidic authority). Philadelphia's key draws from OT (Isa 22:22), not from the vision — supporting SP2 source-divergence.

    G228 — alethinos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G228-alethinos.md

    Occurrences: 27

    True, genuine — not merely factually accurate (alethes, G227) but ontologically real vs. counterfeit. 10 of 27 occurrences in Revelation (37%). Used in Philadelphia self-ID (3:7 'he that is true'), Laodicea self-ID (3:14 'faithful and true witness'), and the rider title (19:11 'Faithful and True'). Primary Hebrew equivalent: emeth (H571, PMI 16.05).

    Semantic categories: genuine vs. copy, God as true God, divine character: faithful and true, true judgments

    Key verses: Rev 3:7, Rev 3:14, Rev 6:10, Rev 19:11

    Relevance: alethinos links Philadelphia (3:7), Laodicea (3:14), and the eschatological rider (19:11). Its absence from Rev 1:12-20 confirms that the last two letters draw from sources outside the vision. The pairing with pistos (G4103) in 3:14 and 19:11 suggests these titles may point forward.

    G4103 — pistos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4103-pistos.md

    Occurrences: 67

    Faithful, trustworthy. 8 occurrences in Revelation. Rev 1:5 'the faithful witness' (ho martys ho pistos) and Rev 3:14 'the faithful and true witness' (ho martys ho pistos kai alethinos). Also 'faithful unto death' (2:10, exhortation to Smyrna) and Antipas 'my faithful martyr' (2:13). Primary Hebrew source: aman (H539, PMI 24.22) — the root of 'amen.'

    Semantic categories: character quality of persons, divine attribute, faithful sayings formula, believing class

    Key verses: Rev 1:5, Rev 2:10, Rev 2:13, Rev 3:14, Rev 19:11

    Relevance: pistos connects the prologue title (1:5 faithful witness) to the Laodicea self-ID (3:14 faithful and true witness) and forward to 19:11 (Faithful and True). The Hebrew root aman connects to the personal title 'the Amen' (3:14) — pistos and Amen share the same etymological foundation.

    G3144 — martys¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3144-martys.md

    Occurrences: 34

    Witness. In Rev 1:5, Christ is ho martys ho pistos (the faithful witness). In Rev 3:14, the expanded form: ho martys ho pistos kai alethinos. Antipas is 'my faithful martyr' (2:13, ho martys mou ho pistos mou). The word carries both judicial witness and death-for-testimony senses.

    Semantic categories: judicial testimony, faithful witness, martyr (death for testimony)

    Key verses: Rev 1:5, Rev 2:13, Rev 3:14

    Relevance: martys creates a direct verbal link between Rev 1:5 (prologue) and Rev 3:14 (Laodicea self-ID). Antipas as pistos martys (2:13) echoes Christ's own title — the disciple mirrors the master. This is NOT from the vision (1:12-20) but from the prologue (1:5).

    G746 — arche¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G746-arche.md

    Occurrences: 58

    Beginning, ruler, principality, first principle. Two semantic poles: (1) temporal/ordinal 'beginning' and (2) positional 'rule/authority.' Both from archo (to rule, to begin). In Rev 3:14, he arche tes ktiseos tou theou — 'the beginning of the creation of God.' Could mean temporal origin, active source/ruler, or causal originator. Col 1:18 uses arche for Christ as 'the beginning, the firstborn from the dead.'

    Semantic categories: temporal origin, active source/ruler, supernatural powers, basic principles

    Key verses: Rev 3:14, Rev 1:8, Rev 21:6, Rev 22:13, Col 1:18

    Relevance: The arche title in Laodicea's self-ID (3:14) is theologically significant. In Rev 1:8 arche appears as 'beginning AND ending' (temporal endpoints). In 3:14 it appears as 'beginning OF the creation OF God' (genitive construction suggesting origin/source). Col 1:18 provides the closest parallel usage. The word's semantic ambiguity between 'beginning' and 'ruler' is deliberate.

    G2937 — ktisis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2937-ktisis.md

    Occurrences: 19

    Creation — the act of creating or the thing created. From ktizo (G2936, to create). In Rev 3:14, 'the beginning of the creation of God' (he arche tes ktiseos tou theou). Col 1:15 has 'firstborn of every creature' (prototokos pases ktiseos) — a parallel construction.

    Semantic categories: act of creating, the created order, creature

    Key verses: Rev 3:14, Col 1:15, Mark 10:6, Rom 8:19-22

    Relevance: ktisis in Rev 3:14 is qualified by 'of God' (tou theou) — God's creation. Combined with arche, the phrase identifies Christ as the source/origin of God's creative work, paralleling Col 1:15-18 where Christ is 'before all things' and 'by him all things consist.'

    G3528 — nikao¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3528-nikao.md

    Occurrences: 28

    To overcome, conquer, prevail. 17 of 28 NT occurrences in Revelation (61%). All seven overcomer promises use the present active participle ho nikon/to nikeonti — identifying the overcomer by characteristic, ongoing action (imperfective aspect). Rev 3:21 provides the clearest aspectual contrast: ho nikon (present, believer's ongoing overcoming) vs. enikesa (aorist, Christ's completed historical victory).

    Semantic categories: eschatological victory, cosmic victory (Christ), moral victory, military conquest

    Key verses: Rev 2:7, Rev 2:11, Rev 2:17, Rev 2:26, Rev 3:5, Rev 3:12, Rev 3:21, Rev 21:7

    Relevance: The nikao participle is invariable across the chiastic arrangement — it appears in every letter regardless of chiastic position. The present tense aspect (ongoing, characteristic action) is theologically significant: the overcomer is identified by habitual pattern, not a single event.

    G4864 — synagoge¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4864-synagōgḗ.md

    Occurrences: 57

    Assembly, congregation, synagogue. 2 of 57 NT occurrences are the 'synagogue of Satan' phrases in Rev 2:9 (Smyrna) and 3:9 (Philadelphia). The word is exclusively shared between these two letters within the seven-letter section, making it one of the strongest chiastic pair markers.

    Semantic categories: physical building for worship, gathered assembly, hostile institution

    Key verses: Rev 2:9, Rev 3:9

    Relevance: synagoge tou Satana appears ONLY in the B-B' pair (Smyrna-Philadelphia), making it the most exclusive vocabulary link in the proposed chiasm. The identical formula with 8+ shared Greek words (VP046 Strong) is the primary evidence for chiastic pairing.

    G3592 — hode (tade)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3592-hode.md

    Occurrences: 11

    This, these things. Near demonstrative pronoun. 7 of 11 NT occurrences are in the tade legei formula opening each letter (Rev 2:1, 2:8, 2:12, 2:18, 3:1, 3:7, 3:14). The only other NT use of tade legei is Acts 21:11 (Agabus). LXX background: ~350 occurrences with ~320 having YHWH as subject.

    Semantic categories: Revelation letter formulas, near demonstrative reference

    Key verses: Rev 2:1, Rev 2:8, Rev 2:12, Rev 2:18, Rev 3:1, Rev 3:7, Rev 3:14, Acts 21:11

    Relevance: The tade legei formula is INVARIABLE across the chiastic arrangement — it appears identically in all seven letters. Its LXX background as the prophetic messenger formula (with YHWH as speaker) makes it an implicit deity claim when Christ uses it. The formula does NOT show chiastic variation.

    G3340 — metanoeo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3340-metanoeo.md

    Occurrences: 36

    To repent, change one's mind. Present in 5 of 7 letters (Ephesus 2:5, Pergamos 2:16, Thyatira 2:21-22, Sardis 3:3, Laodicea 3:19). ABSENT from Smyrna and Philadelphia. This symmetric absence marks the B-B' pair (SP023 Moderate).

    Semantic categories: change of behavior, divine demand for turning

    Key verses: Rev 2:5, Rev 2:16, Rev 2:21, Rev 2:22, Rev 3:3, Rev 3:19

    Relevance: metanoeo is a structural marker for the chiastic arrangement: its absence from BOTH Smyrna and Philadelphia (the proposed B-B' pair) is symmetric and supports SP023. Present in ALL other letters.

    G3531 — Nikolaites¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3531-nikolaïtēs.md

    Occurrences: 2

    Nicolaitans — followers of Nicolaus. Only 2 NT occurrences: Rev 2:6 (Ephesus, 'deeds' of Nicolaitans) and Rev 2:15 (Pergamos, 'doctrine' of Nicolaitans). The progression from erga (deeds) to didache (doctrine) suggests formalization.

    Semantic categories: condemned teaching in early church, association with Balaam's error

    Key verses: Rev 2:6, Rev 2:15

    Relevance: Nikolaiton links Ephesus (A) to Pergamos (C), NOT to Laodicea (A') — a cross-link that complicates clean chiastic pairing. This is one of several vocabulary links that cross the proposed pairs.

    G281 — amen¶

    Occurrences: 152

    Amen — used to affirm statements and prayers. In Rev 3:14, ho Amen (with the article) is used as a PERSONAL TITLE for Christ — the only NT instance of this usage. Background: Isa 65:16 'God of truth/amen' (elohei amen). 2 Cor 1:20 'in him Amen.' The Hebrew amen (H543) is the root of aman (H539), the primary source for pistos (G4103).

    Semantic categories: affirmation formula, personal divine title, liturgical response

    Key verses: Rev 3:14, Rev 1:18, 2 Cor 1:20, Isa 65:16

    Relevance: The personal title ho Amen (TM050) is unique in the NT. It connects the Laodicea self-ID to the OT 'God of amen' (Isa 65:16) and to the etymological foundation of pistos/faithful. The Amen title is NOT from Rev 1:12-20 — it is an entirely non-vision element.

    G5207 — huios¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5207-huios.md

    Occurrences: 382

    Son. The Thyatira self-ID uses 'Son of God' (ho Huios tou Theou, 2:18) — the ONLY occurrence of this exact title in the seven letters. The Rev 1 vision has 'one like unto the Son of man' (homoion huion anthropou, 1:13) from Dan 7:13. The switch from Son of Man to Son of God is TM049.

    Semantic categories: kinship term, divine Son, Christological title

    Key verses: Rev 2:18, Rev 1:13, Rev 21:7

    Relevance: The Son of Man → Son of God switch at Thyatira (TM049) is one of the most distinctive structural features of the letter center. Rev 21:7 'he shall be my son' (huios) connects the overcomer to the Thyatira Christological title.

    G4151 — pneuma¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4151-pneuma.md

    Occurrences: 385

    Spirit/wind/breath. In the letter section: 'seven Spirits of God' in Sardis self-ID (3:1, from prologue 1:4); 'what the Spirit saith unto the churches' in the ear formula of all 7 letters. The seven Spirits come from the PROLOGUE, not the vision — marking Sardis as the beginning of source-divergence (SP2).

    Semantic categories: Holy Spirit, seven Spirits, human spirit, breath

    Key verses: Rev 1:4, Rev 3:1, Rev 2:7, Rev 4:5, Rev 5:6

    Relevance: pneuma in the ear formula is INVARIABLE across all letters. In the Sardis self-ID, 'seven Spirits of God' draws from the prologue (1:4) rather than the vision (1:12-20) — the first letter to mix prologue and vision sources.

    G1492 — oida¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1492-oida.md

    Occurrences: 334

    To know (defective perfect with present force). 'I know thy works' (oida sou ta erga) appears in all seven letters as the invariable template element opening the assessment section. Morphologically perfect (V-RAI-1S) but functionally present tense — settled, comprehensive knowledge.

    Semantic categories: settled knowledge, divine omniscience, comprehensive awareness

    Key verses: Rev 2:2, Rev 2:9, Rev 2:13, Rev 2:19, Rev 3:1, Rev 3:8, Rev 3:15

    Relevance: oida is an INVARIABLE template element — it does not vary across the chiastic arrangement. Its perfect-with-present-force grammar emphasizes that Christ's knowledge of each church is settled and comprehensive, not acquired or developing.

    G26 — agape¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G26-agape.md

    Occurrences: 116

    Love — noun form of agapao. In the letters: Ephesus has 'left thy first love' (agape, 2:4 — complaint); Thyatira has 'charity/love' (agape, 2:19 — commendation). Laodicea uses phileo (G5368, 3:19 'as many as I love') not agape — a different Greek love word.

    Semantic categories: divine love, self-sacrificial love, love-feast

    Key verses: Rev 2:4, Rev 2:19

    Relevance: agape distribution in the letters: Ephesus (complaint context), Thyatira (commendation context). The A-A' pair uses DIFFERENT love words: agape in Ephesus (2:4) vs. phileo in Laodicea (3:19). This lexical difference weakens the 'love deficit' thematic parallel between A and A'.

    G3466 — mysterion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3466-mysterion.md

    Occurrences: 27

    Mystery — a divine secret requiring revelation for disclosure. In Rev 1:20, 'the mystery of the seven stars' frames the vision-to-letters connection as something requiring divine interpretation. The stars = angels, the lampstands = churches.

    Semantic categories: divine secret, revealed truth, hidden wisdom

    Key verses: Rev 1:20, Rev 10:7, Rev 17:5, Rev 17:7

    Relevance: mysterion in 1:20 governs the interpretive framework: the vision symbols have specific referents that Christ himself explains. This explicit interpretation grounds all subsequent letter self-IDs in the vision.

    G1577 — ekklesia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1577-ekklesia.md

    Occurrences: 118

    Church, assembly. The seven churches frame the letter section. ekklesia appears in the ear formula of every letter ('what the Spirit saith unto the churches') and in the epilogue (22:16 'in the churches'), creating an inclusio from 1:4 to 22:16.

    Semantic categories: called-out assembly, local congregation, universal church

    Key verses: Rev 1:4, Rev 1:11, Rev 1:20, Rev 22:16

    Relevance: The sevenfold 'unto the churches' (plural) in the ear formula extends each letter's message beyond its specific addressee to ALL churches — supporting the universal-application reading.

    G3498 — nekros¶

    Occurrences: 132

    Dead. In Rev 1:17 Christ 'was dead' (egenomyn nekros, aorist); in 2:8 'which was dead and is alive' (egeneto nekros kai ezesen); in 3:1 Sardis 'art dead' (nekros ei, present). nekros connects the vision to both the Smyrna and Sardis letters.

    Semantic categories: physical death, spiritual death, death and resurrection

    Key verses: Rev 1:17, Rev 1:18, Rev 2:8, Rev 3:1

    Relevance: nekros creates cross-pair links: Christ's death/resurrection (Smyrna self-ID, B) and Sardis' spiritual death (C'). These are different senses of the same word but create a verbal echo across non-paired letters.

    G2288 — thanatos¶

    Occurrences: 119

    Death. In the letters: 'keys of death' (1:18, vision), 'faithful unto death' (2:10, Smyrna exhortation), 'second death' (2:11, Smyrna overcomer promise), 'kill her children with death' (2:23, Thyatira threat).

    Semantic categories: physical death, second death, keys of death

    Key verses: Rev 1:18, Rev 2:10, Rev 2:11, Rev 2:23

    Relevance: thanatos vocabulary concentrates in Smyrna (death/crown/second death) and connects to the eschatological resolution in Rev 21:8 (the second death). The 'keys of death' from 1:18 are relevant to a church facing martyrdom.

    G2078 — eschatos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2078-éschatos.md

    Occurrences: 54

    Last — in time, place, or rank. From eschaton (farthest, extreme). In Rev 1:17 and 2:8, ho protos kai ho eschatos ('the first and the last') — a title drawn from YHWH's self-declaration in Isa 41:4, 44:6, 48:12. Also 'the last to be more than the first' in Thyatira (2:19). 7 occurrences in Revelation.

    Semantic categories: temporal finality, ordinal sequence, rank/extremity, eschatological

    Key verses: Rev 1:17, Rev 2:8, Rev 2:19, Rev 22:13

    Relevance: eschatos paired with protos forms the 'first and last' divine title in the Smyrna self-ID (2:8), drawn from YHWH's Isaianic declarations. In Thyatira (2:19), eschatos appears in the growth trajectory ('the last more than the first') — a different semantic domain. The title's OT background (Isa 41:4, 44:6, 48:12) makes it a YHWH-to-Christ title transfer.

    G4567 — Satanas¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4567-satanas.md

    Occurrences: 36

    Satan — the adversary. Transliteration of Hebrew satan (H7854). In the seven letters: Smyrna 'synagogue of Satan' (2:9), Pergamos 'Satan's seat' and 'where Satan dwelleth' (2:13), Thyatira 'depths of Satan' (2:24), Philadelphia 'synagogue of Satan' (3:9). 8 occurrences in Revelation.

    Semantic categories: personal name for the adversary, source of opposition, cosmic enemy

    Key verses: Rev 2:9, Rev 2:13, Rev 2:24, Rev 3:9, Rev 12:9, Rev 20:2

    Relevance: Satanas distribution in the letters creates cross-links: 'synagogue of Satan' exclusively in Smyrna (2:9) and Philadelphia (3:9) — B-B' pair evidence. 'Satan's seat/dwelling' in Pergamos (2:13) and 'depths of Satan' in Thyatira (2:24) link the C-D positions. Satan vocabulary spans 4 of 7 letters, absent from Ephesus, Sardis, and Laodicea.

    G4735 — stephanos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4735-stephanos.md

    Occurrences: 18

    Crown — a wreath or garland awarded for victory. Distinguished from diadema (G1238, royal/imperial crown). In the seven letters: Smyrna 'crown of life' (2:10, stephanos tes zoes) and Philadelphia 'thy crown' (3:11, ton stephanon sou). Only these two letters use stephanos within Rev 2-3.

    Semantic categories: victor's wreath, reward for faithfulness, symbolic authority

    Key verses: Rev 2:10, Rev 3:11, 1 Cor 9:25, 2 Tim 4:8, Jas 1:12

    Relevance: stephanos appears exclusively in the B-B' pair (Smyrna 2:10 and Philadelphia 3:11) within the seven letters, making it one of the strongest pair-exclusive vocabulary markers alongside synagoge tou Satana. In Smyrna, the crown is promised ('I will give thee a crown of life'); in Philadelphia, it is possessed but at risk ('that no man take thy crown').
