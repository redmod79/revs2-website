    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    How does Revelation use the Old Testament? What are the primary source books, their relative contributions, and the hierarchy of allusion types? Produce a source map, tally AN-tier allusions by OT book and strength, classify transformation types, and rank which OT books are most deeply embedded vs. superficially used.

    G2342 — thērion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2342-therion.md

    Occurrences: 46

    Dangerous wild animal; diminutive of thēr. 46 NT occurrences; 38 in Revelation (84%). Outside Revelation always literal animal (Mark, Acts, Hebrews). In Revelation the apocalyptic beast figure. Distinction from zōon critical: zōon = living creature (G2226, life-rooted); thērion = dangerous beast (harm-rooted). Rev 4 deliberately uses zōon not thērion for the heavenly living creatures — the lexical split signals that Revelation's throne-room creatures are not the apocalyptic beast.

    Semantic categories: dangerous wild animal, apocalyptic beast figure, Dan 7 beast → Rev 13 beast

    Key verses: Rev 13:1-2, Rev 13:5, Rev 14:9, Mark 1:13

    Relevance: Central to Daniel-primacy analysis; Dan 7's four beasts (thēria) combine into Rev 13's composite beast; G2342 vs G2226 lexical split confirms deliberate transformation of throne-room creatures vs. beast figure

    G1404 — drakōn¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1404-drakon.md

    Occurrences: 13

    Dragon; from dérkomai (to look). 13 NT occurrences, all in Revelation. Exclusive to Revelation — most lexically constrained Greek word in NT. Concentrated in Rev 12-13, 16, 20. Identified explicitly with Satan, devil, ancient serpent (Rev 12:9; 20:2). LXX Isa 27:1 uses drakōn for sea-dragon YHWH will slay — the Gen 3 serpent, Isa 27:1 dragon, Rev 12 dragon form an OT-to-NT identification chain.

    Semantic categories: cosmic adversary, ancient serpent identification, Gen 3 serpent → Isa 27:1 dragon → Rev 12 dragon

    Key verses: Rev 12:3, Rev 12:9, Rev 20:2

    Relevance: Genesis bookend analysis; serpent pair (Gen 3 ophis → Rev 12:9/20:2 drakōn + ho archaios); confirms Revelation's own self-commentary on Gen 3 identity; distinct from ophis (the serpent form)

    G3789 — ophis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3789-ophis.md

    Occurrences: 14

    Serpent; 14 NT occurrences across 7 books. 5 in Revelation. Gen 3:1 LXX = ho ophis; same term in Rev 12:9 (ho ophis ho archaios = that old serpent). The ho archaios addition is Revelation's own self-commentary explicitly identifying the dragon as the Genesis 3 serpent. ho ophis ho archaios formula appears identically in Rev 12:9 and Rev 20:2 — Revelation-internal inclusio on dragon's eschatological career.

    Semantic categories: literal serpent, wisdom/danger symbol, Genesis 3 identification chain

    Key verses: Rev 12:9, Rev 20:2, Gen 3:1 LXX

    Relevance: Bookend pair verification; ophis shared between Gen 3:1 LXX and Rev 12:9/20:2; B-VERBAL ECHO classification; ho archaios confirms intentional back-reference

    G2226 — zōon¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2226-zoon.md

    Occurrences: 23

    Living creature; from zaō (to live). 20 unique verses / 23 tokens. 17 in Revelation. KJV translates as 'beasts' creating confusion with G2342 thērion. Life-rooted rather than harm-rooted. Deliberate substitution for cherubim (H3742 kerubim) in Rev 4 — bypasses Eden-guardian and fallen-cherub associations of Ezek 28 in favor of life-creature identity. Term choice is transformation marker: chayyot (H2416) root rather than kerub.

    Semantic categories: living creature, heavenly throne attendants, deliberate lexical substitution for cherubim

    Key verses: Rev 4:6-8, Ezek 1:5-10 (kerubim in Ezek 10:20)

    Relevance: Ezekiel-to-Revelation transformation analysis; term choice signals deliberate change from Ezek 1's kerubim vocabulary; zōon vs thērion split confirms two distinct entity types in Revelation

    G2362 — thronos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2362-thronos.md

    Occurrences: 61

    Throne; 61 NT occurrences in 50 unique verses; Revelation accounts for 36 of 50 unique verses (72%). Appears in every major division of Revelation: prologue through New Jerusalem. Organizing thread across entire book. Adversarial throne thread: Satan's throne (2:13), dragon gives beast his throne (13:2), beast's throne darkened (16:10). Trajectory: throne 'set in heaven' (4:2) → 'throne of God and Lamb' among humanity (22:1,3).

    Semantic categories: divine authority seat, organizing structural motif, political power (adversarial throne)

    Key verses: Rev 4:2, Rev 22:1, Rev 22:3

    Relevance: Source map analysis: throne terminology from Dan 7:9-10 (Ancient of Days) distributed to Rev 4-5 (throne-setting) and Rev 20:11-12 (judgment); organizing thread linking all Revelation sections

    G721 — arnion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G721-arnion.md

    Occurrences: 30

    Lamb; diminutive of arēn. 30 tokens in 28 unique verses across 2 books. 29 of 30 NT occurrences in Revelation. Introduced at Rev 5:6 with three permanent perfect participles defining permanent simultaneous states. Hear/see contrast at 5:5-6: hears Lion (Gen 49:9) but sees Lamb. Threads from 5:6 to 22:3 connecting the book's entire arc.

    Semantic categories: sacrificial Lamb, Christological title, throne-room figure

    Key verses: Rev 5:6, Rev 22:3, John 21:15

    Relevance: Christological elevation analysis: Zech 3:9 atonement stone → Rev 5:6 slain Lamb with same seven eyes; Lamb threads throughout entire book from throne room to New Jerusalem

    G3087 — lychnia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3087-lychnia.md

    Occurrences: 12

    Lampstand (stand that holds the lamp). 12 NT occurrences, 6 in Revelation (50%). Zechariah chain: Zech 4's single lampstand → Rev 1:12 seven lampstands (multiplication, each = a church). HP furniture from Exo 25:31-40. Churches section distribution (Rev 1-2). Distinct from lampas (G2985, torch) in throne room and lychnos (G3088) in eschatological resolution — non-overlapping distribution signals deliberate lexical planning.

    Semantic categories: lampstand/candlestand, churches as lights, HP furniture

    Key verses: Rev 1:12-13, Rev 2:5, Rev 11:4, Zech 4:2

    Relevance: Zechariah contribution analysis: Zech 4 lampstand → Rev 1:12 seven lampstands (multiplication transformation); Rev 11:4 merges olive trees + lampstands (merger transformation)

    G2985 — lampas¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2985-lampas.md

    Occurrences: 9

    Torch/lamp. 9 NT occurrences. 2 in Revelation (Rev 4:5 seven torches = seven Spirits; Rev 8:10 star burning as lamp). Distinct from lychnia (lampstand) and lychnos (portable lamp). In Rev 4:5 specifically: hepta lampades pyros = seven Spirits of God. From Zech 4:2,6 (seven lamps on lampstand = Spirit). The throne room term for the heavenly spiritual reality vs lychnia for the earthly institutional churches.

    Semantic categories: torch/burning lamp, Spirit identification, throne room lighting

    Key verses: Rev 4:5, Zech 4:2,6

    Relevance: Zechariah lampstand chain: lampas in throne room = seven Spirits = Zech 4 decoder; distinct from lychnia (churches section) and lychnos (eschatological resolution)

    G5357 — phialē¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5357-phiale.md

    Occurrences: 12

    Bowl/vessel. 12 NT occurrences, all in Revelation. Same Greek construction: phialas chrysas gemousas at Rev 5:8 (incense/prayers) and Rev 15:7 (wrath). Phonetic/etymological link: thymiama (incense, from thyō) → thymos (wrath, from thyō). Living creatures hold prayer bowls in throne room (5:8) and distribute wrath bowls before bowls (15:7) — same entities, same vessels, same adjectives, connecting throne room to bowl judgments.

    Semantic categories: bowl/vessel, vessel transformation arc, incense→wrath transformation

    Key verses: Rev 5:8, Rev 15:7, Rev 16:1

    Relevance: Exodus/Leviticus contribution: vessel transformation from prayer (5:8) to wrath (15:7) is Revelation's strongest structural evidence for sanctuary progression and connects throne room to bowl judgments

    G5457 — phōs¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5457-phos.md

    Occurrences: 70

    Light. Isa 60:19-20 → Rev 21:23; 22:5 new creation light chain. No more sun/moon needed; God's glory as light. Part of three-stage prophetic arc: Genesis states problem (no light after Fall) → Isa 60:19-20 previews solution → Rev 22:5 executes. AN102.

    Semantic categories: physical light, divine illumination, new creation light

    Key verses: Rev 21:23, Rev 22:5, Isa 60:19-20

    Relevance: Isaiah contribution: new creation light chain (Isa 60 → Rev 21-22); lychnos eschatological negation (no more candle); God as direct source of light transcending solar system

    G1668 — helkos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1668-hélkos.md

    Occurrences: 3

    Boils/sores. Bowl 1 (Rev 16:2) from Exo 9:8-12 P6. LXX helkos translates Hebrew shechyin (H7822). PMI 10.64. 3 NT occurrences: Luke 16:21, Rev 16:2, Rev 16:11. Direct LXX vocabulary chain — no prophetic intermediary needed. Selective application: only those with mark of the beast receive the sore (Goshen principle transformation).

    Semantic categories: boils/sores, Exodus plague 6 vocabulary

    Key verses: Rev 16:2, Exo 9:9 LXX

    Relevance: Exodus plague matrix: direct LXX vocabulary chain confirming bowls closer to Exodus than trumpets; PMI 10.64 confirms strong lexical co-occurrence; Goshen principle applied inversely

    G5464 — chalaza¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5464-chalaza.md

    Occurrences: 4

    Hail. Rev 16:21 from Exo 9:23-24 P7. PMI 29.60 — strongest PMI in the plague vocabulary chain. LXX chalaza translates Hebrew barad (H1259). Appears in both trumpet 1 (Rev 8:7) and bowl 7 (Rev 16:21). The Exo 9 superlative ('none like it since Egypt became a nation') echoed by Rev 16:18 superlative ('since men were upon the earth').

    Semantic categories: hail, Exodus plague 7 vocabulary

    Key verses: Rev 16:21, Rev 8:7, Exo 9:23-24 LXX

    Relevance: Exodus plague matrix: highest PMI in the vocabulary chain (29.60); appears in both trumpet and bowl sequences; superlative echo confirms deliberate parallel

    G944 — batrachos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G944-bátrachos.md

    Occurrences: 1

    Frog. NT hapax legomenon — appears only at Rev 16:13. LXX translates Hebrew tsephardea (H6854). Exo 8:2-6 P2. Function-changed to demonic spirits emerging from mouths of dragon, beast, false prophet. Three frogs as inverted three angels (Rev 14:6-12) — structural inversion at four levels.

    Semantic categories: frog, Exodus plague 2 vocabulary, NT hapax

    Key verses: Rev 16:13, Exo 8:2-6 LXX

    Relevance: Exodus plague matrix: NT hapax confirming intentional vocabulary link; function-change transformation (plague frogs → demonic spirits); structural inversion of three angels

    G2563 — kalamos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2563-kalamos.md

    Occurrences: 12

    Reed/measuring rod. LXX qaneh H7070 = kalamos G2563; 16 co-occurrences; PMI 9.09. Ezek 40 measuring rod vocabulary → Rev 11:1 (mortal reed, selective exclusion) → Rev 21:15 (golden reed, total inclusion). Arc: mortal reed (persecution conditions) → golden reed (eschatological glory). AN092/AN093.

    Semantic categories: measuring rod, reed instrument, Ezek 40 LXX vocabulary chain

    Key verses: Rev 11:1, Rev 21:15, Ezek 40:3-42

    Relevance: Ezekiel macro-structural parallel: LXX vocabulary chain confirms deliberate Ezek 40 allusion; mortal→golden reed arc documents transformation type (intensification)

    G4087 — pikriainō¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4087-pikraino.md

    Occurrences: 6

    Make bitter. 4 of 6 NT occurrences in Revelation. Scroll-eating transformation: Ezek 3:14 external emotional aftermath (mar = emotional state of Ezekiel) → Rev 10:10 intrinsic bodily content (koilia as grammatical subject; belly undergoes bitterness as completed past event). Deepens rather than changes direction of Ezekiel's original.

    Semantic categories: bitterness, scroll eating transformation

    Key verses: Rev 10:10, Ezek 3:14

    Relevance: Ezekiel parallel: scroll eating bitterness transformation is paradigm example of intensification + internalization transformation type; pikrianō concentration in Revelation confirms deliberate Ezek vocabulary

    H3742 — kᵉrûwb¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3742-kruwb.md

    Occurrences: 105

    Cherubim. Ezek 1 → Rev 4 via zōon substitution. Ezek 10:20 names the Ezek 1 creatures as kerubim. Rev 4 uses zōon (G2226), bypassing the cherubim term entirely. The Gen 3:24 cherubim guard the tree of life; Ezek 28 uses kerub for the fallen guardian. Rev 4's zōon substitution avoids these associations in favor of life-creature identity.

    Semantic categories: cherubim, throne guardian, OT → NT term substitution

    Key verses: Ezek 1:5-10, Ezek 10:20, Gen 3:24, Rev 4:6-8

    Relevance: Ezekiel transformation analysis: deliberate term substitution (kerubim → zōon) signals transformation type (term-shift/function-change); life-rooted vocabulary replaces guardian-rooted vocabulary

    H1823 — dᵉmûwth¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1823-dᵉmûwth.md

    Occurrences: 26

    Likeness/resemblance. Ezek 1 vision formula: 'as the likeness of' (kᵉdᵉmût). The repeated demut formula in Ezek 1 maintains distance between the vision imagery and the divine reality — 'the appearance of the likeness of the glory of the LORD' (Ezek 1:28). LXX: homoiōma (G3667).

    Semantic categories: likeness, vision formula, distance marker

    Key verses: Ezek 1:5, Ezek 1:28

    Relevance: Ezekiel vision vocabulary: demut formula establishes the 'as/like' distance between Ezek 1 imagery and divine reality; Rev 4 preserves this via homoiōma and comparison phrases

    H4758 — marʼeh¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4758-mareh.md

    Occurrences: 110

    Appearance/sight. Ezek 1 vision vocabulary alongside demut. marʼeh = what can be seen; the visual appearance of a thing. LXX: horaō/horaō family. Together with demut, establishes the epistemological modesty of Ezekiel's vision: appearances and likenesses, not direct identity.

    Semantic categories: appearance, vision vocabulary, visual perception

    Key verses: Ezek 1:4-28

    Relevance: Ezekiel vision vocabulary chain: mareh + demut together create the distinctive 'appearance of the likeness' formula that Rev 4 inherits and adapts

    G3667 — homoiōma¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3667-homoioma.md

    Occurrences: 6

    Likeness/resemblance. NT bridge word connecting Ezek 1 demut vocabulary to NT 'as/like' vision formula. Rev 9:7 locust-like-horses use homoion. LXX renders demut as homoiōma. The 'as the likeness of' formula preserved in Revelation's vision descriptions maintains the visual-distance epistemology of Ezekiel's merkavah.

    Semantic categories: likeness, comparison formula, LXX bridge term

    Key verses: Rev 9:7, Ezek 1:28 LXX

    Relevance: Connects Ezekiel's demut formula to Revelation's vision language; confirms LXX mediates the vocabulary chain
