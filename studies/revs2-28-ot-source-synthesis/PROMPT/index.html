    Question¶

    How does Revelation use the Old Testament? What are the primary source books, their relative contributions, and the hierarchy of allusion types? Produce a source map, tally AN-tier allusions by OT book and strength, classify transformation types, and rank which OT books are most deeply embedded vs. superficially used.

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Six prior revs2 studies (revs2-03, revs2-13, revs2-22, revs2-23, revs2-24, revs2-25) provide the core data. The structural DB contains 194 AN-tier entries with 14 Explicit, 68 Strong, 39 Probable, 4 Echo, 69 Unclassified. Top-four OT sources by raw count: Exodus/Leviticus (~29 entries), Ezekiel (~25), Daniel (~23), Zechariah (~12), Isaiah (~12), Genesis (~9). revs2-24 catalogued 8 transformation types. revs2-23 confirmed stoma laloun megala as verbatim LXX quotation (near-quotation tier). Web research: Moyise/Huffman scholarly consensus confirms zero formal OT quotations in Revelation. Beale prioritizes Daniel; Moyise/Bøe prioritize Ezekiel. Fekkes documents 40 virtually-certain Isaiah allusions. The synthesis must formalize the allusion hierarchy, source map, and transformation taxonomy using all 194 AN entries and the 6 prior study CONCLUSION.md files as primary data.
