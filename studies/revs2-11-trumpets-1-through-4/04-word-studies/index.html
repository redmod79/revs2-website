    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    What is the structural pattern of trumpets 1-4 — the '1/3 destruction' pattern? Document what is struck, what fraction is affected, and what the result is for each trumpet. How do trumpets 1-4 parallel the Exodus plagues? Use Exodus 7-12 as the source text and document the element-by-element mapping. What structural distinction separates the first four trumpets from the three woes (trumpets 5-7)?

    G5154 — tritos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5154-tritos.md

    Occurrences: 57

    tritos (third) — the key structural word of the trumpet sequence. Appears as to triton (the third part) 11 times in Rev 8:7-12, with 5 concentrated in 8:12 forming the rhetorical climax. The construction to triton + partitive genitive consistently expresses a quantitative fraction. The nominative/accusative neuter substantive form (to triton) functions as the subject of the fractional destruction.

    Semantic categories: quantitative fraction, ordinal number, rhetorical marker

    Key verses: Rev 8:7, Rev 8:8, Rev 8:9, Rev 8:10, Rev 8:11, Rev 8:12

    Relevance: The defining structural marker (TM075) of trumpets 1-4. The 11 occurrences and 5-in-one-verse climax pattern constitute the most concentrated vocabulary repetition in the trumpet sequence.

    G4537 — salpizo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4537-salpizo.md

    Occurrences: 13

    salpizo (to sound a trumpet) — the formulaic verb. The construction ho [ordinal] aggelos esalpisen uses the aorist active indicative 3S consistently across all seven trumpets. Parser output confirms identical morphology (V-AAI-3S) at Rev 8:7, 8:8, 8:10, 8:12, 9:1, 9:13, 11:15.

    Semantic categories: formulaic verb, trumpet-sounding, judgment herald

    Key verses: Rev 8:7, Rev 8:8, Rev 8:10, Rev 8:12, Rev 9:1, Rev 9:13, Rev 11:15

    Relevance: The formulaic trumpet-sounding pattern (TM076) — identical morphology across all seven trumpets confirms a deliberate syntactic template.

    G4536 — salpinx¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4536-salpinx.md

    Occurrences: 11

    salpinx (trumpet, noun) — 11 NT occurrences. Heavily concentrated in Revelation (6 of 11, 55%). The Greek conflates both distinct OT instruments (showphar H7782 and chatsotsrah H2689) in LXX translation. Semantic categories span eschatological gathering, resurrection signal, divine voice/communication, and the seven apocalyptic trumpets.

    Semantic categories: musical instrument, eschatological signal, divine communication, apocalyptic judgment

    Key verses: Rev 8:2, Rev 8:6, Rev 8:13, 1Co 15:52, 1Th 4:16

    Relevance: The noun complement to salpizo (G4537). The seven trumpets (hepta salpinges) define the structure of the entire judgment sequence.

    G5464 — chalaza¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5464-chalaza.md

    Occurrences: 4

    chalaza (hail) — 4 NT tokens in 3 verses, all in Revelation (8:7, 11:19, 16:21). Exclusively a Revelation word used only in judgment contexts. The primary Hebrew equivalent is barad (H1259, 21 LXX co-occurrences, PMI 29.60). The LXX of Exo 9:24 uses chalaza and pyr together — the identical pairing in Rev 8:7.

    Semantic categories: theophanic judgment, Exodus plague echo, divine wrath

    Key verses: Rev 8:7, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:21

    Relevance: Links trumpet 1 (Rev 8:7) to Exodus plague 7 (Exo 9:23-24) through the LXX vocabulary chain. Also creates a verbal inclusio bracketing the trumpet sequence: chalaza at 8:7 and chalaza megale at 11:19.

    G4442 — pyr¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4442-pyr.md

    Occurrences: 73

    pyr (fire) — 73 unique verses (74 tokens) across 14 NT books. Revelation dominates with 25 occurrences (34%). Appears in trumpets 1 and 2 (8:7 'hail and fire'; 8:8 'mountain burning with fire') and in the incense prelude (8:5 'fire of the altar'). The primary Hebrew equivalent is esh (H784, 316 LXX co-occurrences). Semantic categories: eschatological punishment, judgment parables, divine theophany, cosmic plagues, testing/refining.

    Semantic categories: eschatological punishment, theophany, cosmic plague, refining

    Key verses: Rev 8:5, Rev 8:7, Rev 8:8

    Relevance: Appears in both the incense prelude (fire from altar) and the first two trumpets. The fire of 8:5 transitions from the altar to the earth, then reappears as destructive agent in 8:7 and 8:8.

    G129 — haima¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G129-haima.md

    Occurrences: 99

    haima (blood) — 99 tokens in 92 verses across 14 NT books. Appears in trumpet 1 (8:7 'mingled with blood') and trumpet 2 (8:8 'sea became blood'). The Hebrew equivalent dam (H1818, 269 LXX co-occurrences) is the same word used in Exodus Plague 1 (7:17-21). Semantic categories: atoning blood of Christ, sacrificial blood, bloodshed/violent death, eschatological judgment blood.

    Semantic categories: eschatological judgment, plague imagery, sacrificial system

    Key verses: Rev 8:7, Rev 8:8, Rev 16:3, Rev 16:4

    Relevance: The blood element in trumpet 1 has no parallel in the Exodus hail plague (P7) — it comes from Plague 1 (blood), creating a composite allusion. In trumpet 2, the sea becoming blood directly echoes Plague 1.

    G2281 — thalassa¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2281-thalassa.md

    Occurrences: 92

    thalassa (sea) — trumpet 2's primary target. The sea domain is struck by the burning mountain, and the triple 1/3 (sea to blood, creatures died, ships destroyed) makes this the most elaborate single trumpet. The sea is also a creation domain (Gen 1:10) and part of the Rev 14:7 creation formula.

    Semantic categories: creation domain, judgment target, ecological domain

    Key verses: Rev 8:8, Rev 8:9, Rev 14:7, Gen 1:10

    Relevance: The sea domain is the second trumpet target, the second bowl target (Rev 16:3), and part of both creation formulae (Gen 1:10, Rev 14:7).

    G4077 — pege¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4077-pege.md

    Occurrences: 12

    pege (fountain/spring) — trumpet 3's target alongside potamos (rivers). The freshwater domain is distinct from the sea domain of trumpet 2. Pege appears in Rev 14:7's creation formula ('fountains of waters'), linking the trumpet target directly to the creation framework.

    Semantic categories: freshwater source, creation domain, judgment target

    Key verses: Rev 8:10, Rev 14:7, Rev 16:4

    Relevance: Part of the four-term creation formula in Rev 14:7. The freshwater domain (rivers + fountains) is targeted by trumpet 3 and bowl 3.

    G792 — aster¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G792-aster.md

    Occurrences: 24

    aster (star) — appears in both trumpet 3 (8:10, 'great star from heaven burning as a lamp') and trumpet 4 (8:12, '1/3 of the stars smitten'). The falling star of trumpet 3 is personified (given the name Wormwood). Stars as part of the celestial triad (helios, selene, aster) match Genesis Day 4 creation.

    Semantic categories: celestial body, personified agent, creation domain

    Key verses: Rev 8:10, Rev 8:12, Rev 9:1

    Relevance: Stars appear in two distinct roles: as a personified agent (trumpet 3, falling star Wormwood) and as a smitten celestial body (trumpet 4, 1/3 of stars darkened).

    G4655 — skotos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4655-skotos.md

    Occurrences: 32

    skotos (darkness, noun) — the result of the fourth trumpet. The verb form skotizo (G4654, 10 occurrences) is used in Rev 8:12 (skotisthe, aorist passive subjunctive). The cosmic/eschatological darkening category (Mat 24:29, Mark 13:24, Rev 8:12) links Revelation to the prophetic tradition.

    Semantic categories: cosmic darkening, eschatological sign, divine judgment

    Key verses: Rev 8:12, Rev 9:2, Rev 16:10

    Relevance: Darkness is the fourth trumpet's result and echoes Exodus Plague 9. The prophetic tradition (Isa 13:10, Joel 2:10, Ezek 32:7-8) mediates between the Exodus event and Revelation's usage.

    G894 — apsinthos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G894-ápsinthos.md

    Occurrences: 2

    apsinthos (wormwood) — 2 NT occurrences, both in Rev 8:11. NT hapax by passage. Used exclusively to name the falling star and describe the substance the waters become. The article variation is structurally notable: ho Apsinthos (with article, N-NSM — named entity) vs. apsinthon (without article, N-ASF — substance). OT source is laanah (H3939), linked to apostasy and divine judgment in Jer 9:15 and 23:15.

    Semantic categories: bitterness and judgment, poisoned waters, named entity

    Key verses: Rev 8:11

    Relevance: The named-entity construction (TM077) is unique among trumpet agents — only the third trumpet's agent receives a proper name. The gender shift (masculine for the star-name, feminine for the substance) marks the distinction between agent and effect.

    G4087 — pikraino¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4087-pikraino.md

    Occurrences: 6

    pikraino (to make bitter) — 6 NT occurrences across Colossians and Revelation. In Rev 8:11 (epikranthesan, V-API-3P, aorist passive indicative 3P), the waters 'were made bitter.' Also used in Rev 10:9-10 for the bitter experience of absorbing prophetic revelation. The verb connects the trumpet judgment (bitter waters) to the prophetic internalization (bitter scroll).

    Semantic categories: judgment on waters, prophetic bitterness, relational embitterment

    Key verses: Rev 8:11, Rev 10:9, Rev 10:10

    Relevance: The verb describing the third trumpet's effect. The connection to Rev 10:9-10 (bitter scroll) within the same trumpet sequence suggests thematic coherence between the wormwood judgment and the prophetic commission.

    G4127 — plege¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4127-plege.md

    Occurrences: 21

    plege (plague/blow) — the text of Rev 9:20 retroactively labels the trumpet judgments as 'plagues' (plegais, dative plural), explicitly connecting the trumpet sequence to the Exodus plague tradition. The LXX uses plege to translate Hebrew makkah/negeph in Exodus plague narratives, creating a direct vocabulary bridge.

    Semantic categories: Exodus plague bridge, judgment label, retroactive identification

    Key verses: Rev 9:20, Rev 16:21, Rev 11:6

    Relevance: The retroactive plague label (TM201) is critical: by calling the trumpets 'plagues,' the text itself makes the Exodus connection explicit rather than leaving it as scholarly inference.

    G3321 — mesouranema¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3321-mesouranema.md

    Occurrences: 3

    mesouranema (mid-heaven) — 3 NT occurrences, all in Revelation (8:13, 14:6, 19:17). An exclusively Revelation word marking the aerial broadcast position. In 8:13, the eagle flies in mid-heaven announcing the three woes. In 14:6, the angel with the everlasting gospel flies in mid-heaven. In 19:17, the angel summons birds in mid-heaven.

    Semantic categories: aerial broadcast position, universal announcement, Revelation-exclusive

    Key verses: Rev 8:13, Rev 14:6, Rev 19:17

    Relevance: The three mid-heaven announcements share a common function: universal proclamation. The eagle's triple ouai (8:13) is the structural hinge dividing the trumpet sequence.

    G796 — astrape¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G796-astrapē.md

    Occurrences: 9

    astrape (lightning) — 9 NT occurrences. Appears exclusively in the four Sinai-theophany passages within Revelation (4:5, 8:5, 11:19, 16:18). Always paired with bronte (G1027) and phone (G5456) in the theophanic triad. The Hebrew source is baraq (H1300, 16 LXX co-occurrences, PMI 28.55). Also used for Christ's return visibility (Mat 24:27, Luk 17:24) and supernatural radiance.

    Semantic categories: theophanic manifestation, suddenness of Christ's coming, supernatural radiance

    Key verses: Rev 4:5, Rev 8:5, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:18

    Relevance: The structural marker of the Sinai-theophany escalation (TM057). Its exclusive appearance in these four passages constitutes one of the most rigorous distribution patterns in Revelation.

    G3031 — libanotos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3031-libanotos.md

    Occurrences: 2

    libanotos (censer) — only Rev 8:3 and 8:5 in NT. The same censer carries prayers upward (8:3-4) then fire downward (8:5), marking the pivot from intercession to judgment. Analyzed in revs2-10 as the transitional mechanism between the sanctuary intercession and the trumpet judgments.

    Semantic categories: sanctuary furniture, intercession-to-judgment pivot, NT exclusive to Revelation

    Key verses: Rev 8:3, Rev 8:5

    Relevance: The censer reversal establishes the sanctuary context for the trumpet judgments — they emanate from the place of worship, not from abstract cosmic forces.

    G1093 — ge¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1093-gē.md

    Occurrences: 252

    ge (earth) — trumpet 1's primary target domain. The earth is also the first element in the Rev 14:7 creation formula and the first bowl target (Rev 16:2). As a creation domain, ge maps to Genesis 1:9-10 (Day 3). The 'inhabitants of the earth' (katoikountas epi tes ges) in 8:13 are the addressees of the woe-trumpet judgments.

    Semantic categories: creation domain, trumpet 1 target, inhabited realm

    Key verses: Rev 8:7, Rev 8:13, Rev 14:7

    Relevance: The first trumpet target and the first creation formula element. The 'earth' domain spans both ecological (vegetation) and inhabited (earth-dwellers) meanings.

    G4215 — potamos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4215-potamós.md

    Occurrences: 16

    potamos (river) — trumpet 3 target alongside pege (fountains). The freshwater domain (rivers and springs) is distinct from the sea domain (trumpet 2). Rivers become the carrier of the wormwood effect, being made bitter by the falling star.

    Semantic categories: freshwater domain, trumpet 3 target, creation domain

    Key verses: Rev 8:10, Rev 16:4

    Relevance: Part of the freshwater domain targeted by trumpet 3 and bowl 3. The rivers + fountains pairing creates a complete freshwater category.

    G2246 — helios¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2246-hḗlios.md

    Occurrences: 31

    helios (sun) — 31 NT occurrences, with 12 in Revelation (highest concentration at 12.3 per 100 verses). In trumpet 4 (Rev 8:12), the sun is smitten and 1/3 darkened. In bowl 4 (Rev 16:8), the sun is empowered to scorch — opposite effects on the same target. Collocates with skotizo (G4654) 4 times and with selene (G4582) 6 times. The Hebrew source is shemesh (H8121).

    Semantic categories: celestial body, eschatological sign, glory metaphor, creation domain

    Key verses: Rev 8:12, Rev 16:8, Mat 24:29

    Relevance: The sun is the first celestial body struck in trumpet 4 and the target of bowl 4. The darkening/scorching contrast between trumpet 4 and bowl 4 is a key structural observation.

    G3759 — ouai¶

    Semantic categories: judgment exclamation, structural marker, prophetic warning

    G3340 — metanoeo¶

    Relevance: The negated metanoeo forms the structural bookend of the trumpet sequence. The same 'repented not' appears in the bowl sequence (16:9, 16:11), creating a parallel failure across both judgment series.

    H7992 — shliyshiy¶

    Occurrences: 109

    shliyshiy/shliyshith (third/third part, Hebrew) — 109 OT occurrences. The majority (about 87%) are ordinal ('the third day/year/time'). Only about 13% are partitive ('a third part'). The partitive usage is concentrated in Ezekiel 5 (shliyshith, the noun form 'third part') and Zechariah 13:8-9. The LXX translates the partitive form with to triton — the same Greek construction used 11 times in Rev 8:7-12. The one-third-trumpets study confirmed that the fraction is semantically neutral — it gains meaning from context.

    Semantic categories: ordinal number, partitive fraction, tripartite division

    Key verses: Ezek 5:2, Ezek 5:12, Zech 13:8-9

    Relevance: The OT source for the 1/3 fraction concept. Ezekiel 5 provides the tripartite destruction background; Zechariah 13 inverts it with a surviving-and-refined 1/3. Revelation synthesizes both traditions.
