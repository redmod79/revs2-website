    Question¶

    How does Revelation present events as both already accomplished and still future? Key tensions: dragon cast down (12:9-10) but still active (12:13-17); Christ overcame (3:21, 5:5) but final battle remains (19:11-21); kingdoms 'have become' Lord's (11:15 aorist) but beast still reigns (13:5-7). How do 'quickly/shortly' vocabulary (en tachei G1722+5034, tachu G5035) and Daniel sealed/Revelation unsealed contrast (Dan 12:4 vs Rev 22:10) illuminate the temporal framework?

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Two predecessor revs-series studies (revs-38 and revs-39) have directly investigated this topic. revs-38 cataloged the nikao tense system (TM195: aorist for Christ/heaven-perspective saints; present participle for ongoing overcoming; future for eschatological victory; infinitive for beast's permitted overcoming — 17 occurrences), the ballo three-stage Satan displacement (TM196), the ginomai tense progression from prophetic aorist to perfect (TM197), and the basileuo/basileia multi-tense arc (TM198). revs-39 documented the en tachei/tachu dual semantic range (TM188-189), the hypomonē co-occurrence pattern (TM190), and the Daniel-Revelation seal progression (SP090: four-step sealed → opened → only thunders sealed → not sealed). The IC077 hybrid model from revs2-20 (telescoping + overlapping + escalating resolution) is the established series chronological framework within which this study operates. 50+ structural DB elements are already registered and must be cited, not re-registered, when encountered. This study should direct fresh investigation especially on: (1) specific Greek parsing of the proleptic aorists across the catalog, (2) the John 12:31-33 / Rev 12:9 cross vocabulary, (3) the arti (G737) temporal marker at Rev 12:10, (4) the Rev 16:5 and Rev 11:17 divine-name reduction as temporal marker, and (5) chronos at Rev 10:6.
