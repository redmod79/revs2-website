    Verses¶

    Question¶

    How does Revelation construct its already/not-yet temporal framework through verb tense, vocabulary, and structural markers — and what does this reveal about the book's eschatological logic?

    En Tachei — The Book's Opening Temporal Claim¶

    The Revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave unto him, to shew unto his servants things which must shortly come to pass; and he sent and signified [it] by his angel unto his servant John:

    Context: Prologue. The entire book's mandate — 'things which must shortly come to pass' — uses en tachei (G5034, dative of tachos). Sets temporal expectation for everything that follows.

    Notes: τάχει [N-DSN] G5034 dative of tachos. BDAG glosses en tachei as 'soon, in a short time' at this verse. Debate: does it mean temporal imminence (Mounce, BDAG, Farrar) or manner of execution — swiftness (Walvoord, Thomas)? The identical phrase recurs at Rev 22:6, forming a prologue-epilogue frame. LXX Dan 2:28 uses 'ha dei genesthai' (what must come to pass) — Rev 1:1 echoes this verbatim, linking Revelation's mandate to Daniel's.

    Kairos Engus — First Inclusio Marker¶

    Blessed [is] he that readeth, and they that hear the words of this prophecy, and keep those things which are written therein: for the time [is] at hand.

    Context: First beatitude of Revelation. 'The time is at hand' = ho kairos engus. Opens the book's temporal inclusio bracket.

    Notes: καιρὸς [N-NSM] G2540 + ἐγγύς [ADV] G1451. This exact phrase 'ho kairos engus' recurs at Rev 22:10, forming an inclusio that brackets the entire book. Engus from G1451 word study: 30 NT occurrences; Rev has 2 (1:3, 22:10) — both in this inclusio. Hebrew source H7138 (PMI 7.99). G1448 eggizō is the verbal cognate (process vs. state distinction).

    Three-Element Divine Formula — Full Form¶

    John to the seven churches which are in Asia: Grace [be] unto you, and peace, from him which is, and which was, and which is to come; and from the seven Spirits which are before his throne;

    Context: Epistolary prescript. The three-element divine self-description — ho ōn kai ho ēn kai ho erchomenos — appears here in its full form.

    Notes: ὢν [V-PAP-NSM] G1510 Pres Act Ptcp — 'which is' (present). ἦν [V-IAI-3S] G1510 Impf Act Ind — 'which was' (past). ἐρχόμενος [V-PNP-NSM] G2064 Pres M/P Ptcp — 'which is to come' (future-oriented). Three-element formula: present + past + coming. Recurs at 1:8 and 4:8 in full form. Reduced to two elements at 11:17 and 16:5 (dropping ho erchomenos) — the reduction signals that 'the coming' has been realized.

    Three-Element Divine Formula with Pantokrator¶

    I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the ending, saith the Lord, which is, and which was, and which is to come, the Almighty.

    Context: Divine self-declaration. Full three-element formula + Pantokrator (Almighty/all-sovereign).

    Notes: Same three-element formula as 1:4. Παντοκράτωρ [N-NSM] G3841 added. This is the full form — all three elements present. Compare to reduced form at 11:17 and 16:5 where ho erchomenos is absent.

    Hypomonē as Covenant Condition — Philadelphia¶

    Because thou hast kept the word of my patience, I also will keep thee from the hour of temptation, which shall come upon all the world, to try them that dwell upon the earth.

    Context: Letter to Philadelphia. The church's past hypomonē (keeping 'my patience') conditions a future divine keeping during the hour of testing.

    Notes: G5281 hypomonē = 'my patience/endurance.' The church has matched Christ's own hypomonē (see Rev 1:9 where John is co-partaker). Note tense structure: kept (aorist) → I will keep (future). Endurance in the present tribulation connects to future preservation.

    Nikao Aorist/Present Contrast — Christ's Victory Grounds Believer's Ongoing Overcoming¶

    To him that overcometh will I grant to sit with me in my throne, even as I also overcame, and am set down with my Father in his throne.

    Context: Letter to Laodicea, seventh and climactic overcomer promise. Christ's completed victory grounds the promise to ongoing overcomers.

    Notes: νικῶν [V-PAP-NSM] G3528 Pres Act Ptcp — ongoing 'he that overcometh.' ἐνίκησα [V-AAI-1S] G3528 Aor Act Ind 1S — Christ's completed 'I overcame.' The tense contrast is structural: Christ's aorist (past completed) grounds the believer's present participle (ongoing process). This is TM195 nikao taxonomy: Christ's self-originated aorist vs. believer's present participle.

    Three-Element Divine Formula in Throne Room¶

    And the four beasts had each of them six wings about [him]; and [they were] full of eyes within: and they rest not day and night, saying, Holy, holy, holy, Lord God Almighty, which was, and is, and is to come.

    Context: Throne room vision (Rev 4). The living creatures declare the divine formula without ceasing.

    Notes: Note word order here: ὁ ἦν (was) + ὁ ὢν (is) + ὁ ἐρχόμενος (is to come) — same three elements but past listed first. All three elements present. The incessant praise = the formula describes God's eternal nature across all time.

    Sealed Scroll and the Lion Who Has Prevailed — Nikao Aorist¶

    And I saw in the right hand of him that sat on the throne a book written within and on the backside, sealed with seven seals. [2-4 — no one worthy]... And one of the elders saith unto me, Weep not: behold, the Lion of the tribe of Juda, the Root of David, hath prevailed to open the book, and to loose the seven seals thereof.

    Context: Throne room. The sealed scroll of Rev 5:1 echoes Dan 12:4. The Lion has 'prevailed' (aorist) — completed act at the cross. The unveiling of a sealed scroll mirrors Daniel's sealed vision now opened.

    Notes: ἐνίκησεν [V-AAI-3S] G3528 Aor Act Ind 3S — 'hath prevailed' at 5:5. Cross-event aorist — Christ's victory already accomplished. The sealed scroll (sphragís) at Rev 5:1 = the sealed revelation given to Daniel (Dan 12:4 satam + chatham). Christ's opening of the seals = the epistemological unsealing. See revs2-10 for the unsealing framework.

    New Song — Proleptic Aorist Redemption and Future Reign¶

    And they sung a new song, saying, Thou art worthy to take the book, and to open the seals thereof: for thou wast slain, and hast redeemed us to God by thy blood out of every kindred, and tongue, and people, and nation; And hast made us unto our God kings and priests: and we shall reign on the earth.

    Context: Throne room worship (Rev 5). The four living creatures and 24 elders sing. ēgorasas (redeemed) and epoiēsas (made) are aorists referring to completed cross-work. basileusousin (shall reign) is future.

    Notes: ἠγόρασας [V-AAI-2S] G59 Aor Act Ind 2S — 'hast redeemed' (completed act). ἐποίησας [V-AAI-2S] G4160 Aor Act Ind 2S — 'hast made' (completed act). βασιλεύσουσιν [V-FAI-3P] G936 Fut Act Ind 3P — 'shall reign' (future). Tense pattern: past acts of redemption ground a future reign. The 'already' of the cross funds the 'not yet' of the reign.

    Mighty Angel, Open Book, and Chronos No Longer¶

    And I saw another mighty angel come down from heaven, clothed with a cloud: and a rainbow [was] upon his head, and his face [was] as it were the sun, and his feet as pillars of fire: And he had in his hand a little book open: and he set his right foot upon the sea, and [his] left [foot] on the earth, [3] And cried with a loud voice, as [when] a lion roareth: and when he had cried, seven thunders uttered their voices. [4] And when the seven thunders had uttered their voices, I was about to write: and I heard a voice from heaven saying unto me, Seal up those things which the seven thunders uttered, and write them not. [5] And the angel which I saw stand upon the sea and upon the earth lifted up his hand to heaven, [6] And sware by him that liveth for ever and ever, who created heaven, and the things that therein are, and the earth, and the things that therein are, and the sea, and the things which are therein, that there should be time no longer: [7] But in the days of the voice of the seventh angel, when he shall begin to sound, the mystery of God should be finished, as he hath declared to his servants the prophets.

    Context: Interlude between trumpets 6 and 7. The little book is open (contrast sealed scroll of Rev 5). The oath declares chronos ouketi estai. Rev 10:4 seals the seven thunders — the ONLY thing sealed in Revelation (per G4972 word study).

    Notes: χρόνος [N-NSM] G5550 + οὐκέτι [ADV-N G3765] + ἔσται [V-FDI-3S] G1510 Fut Mid Ind 3S. 'Time shall be no longer' — chronos = measured elapsed duration (not kairos = appointed season). The open book contrasts with the sealed scroll of Dan 12:4 and Rev 5:1. The mystery to be 'finished' (G5055 teleō) at the seventh trumpet links to Rev 11:15 kingdom announcement. The oath structure (lifting hand to heaven) echoes Dan 12:7.

The 'no more hunger/thirst/tears' anticipations (7:16-17) are expressed in future tenses from the earthly perspective but as present realities in the heavenly vision. Classic dual-perspective structure.

    Seal the Book — Daniel's Sealed Contrast¶

    But thou, O Daniel, shut up the words, and seal the book, [even] to the time of the end: many shall run to and fro, and knowledge shall be increased.

    Context: Daniel's closing command. God commands the sealing of the words until 'the time of the end.'

    Notes: סְתֹ֥ם (satam H5640) Verb.Qal.Impv.2ms — 'shut up' (imperative). חֲתֹ֣ם (chatham H2856) Verb.Qal.Impv.2ms — 'seal' (imperative). Both commands in sequence. Time marker: עַד עֵת קֵץ = 'until the time of the end.' This is the OT sealing command that Rev 22:10 inverts (mē sphragisēs — do NOT seal). The contrast signals the shift from sealed anticipation to open revelation.

    Words Closed and Sealed — Participial State¶

    And he said, Go thy way, Daniel: for the words [are] closed up and sealed till the time of the end.

    Context: Daniel's dismissal. The commands of Dan 12:4 (imperatives) are now described as accomplished state (participles).

    Notes: סְתֻמִ֧ים (satam H5640) Verb.Qal.Ptcp.mp — 'are closed/stopped' (participle — state). חֲתֻמִ֛ים (chatham H2856) Verb.Qal.Ptcp.mp — 'are sealed' (participle — state). The imperatives of v.4 are now accomplished states in v.9. Same 'until time of end' marker. Top OT parallel for Rev 22:10 (0.337 score) and second parallel (0.303).

    Seal the Vision — Many Days Future¶

    And the vision of the evening and the morning which was told [is] true: wherefore shut thou up the vision; for it [shall be] for many days.

    Context: End of the 2,300-day vision. Command to seal with explicit distance marker ('many days').

    Notes: סְתֹ֣ם (satam H5640) Verb.Qal.Impv.2ms — 'shut up' (same root as Dan 12:4). Time marker: לְיָמִ֥ים רַבִּֽים = 'for many days.' Contrast with Rev 22:10's engus (near) — the sealed distant future → the unsealed near present. The distance-to-nearness inversion is the core structural shift.

    Now Is the Judgment — Satan Cast Out (Future Indicative)¶

    Now is the judgment of this world: now shall the prince of this world be cast out. And I, if I be lifted up from the earth, will draw all [men] unto me. This he said, signifying what death he should die.

    Context: Jesus' declaration at his triumphal entry. 'Now' (nyn) + future 'shall be cast out.' The cross is the moment of cosmic judgment.

    Notes: ἐκβληθήσεται [V-FPI-3S] G1544 ekballō — Fut Pass Ind 3S — 'shall be cast out' (FUTURE — not aorist). CRITICAL: this is G1544 ekballō, NOT G906 ballo (used in Rev 12:9 eblēthē). Cognate but distinct root. The cross event is the pivot point for Satan's defeat — John 12:31 uses future, while Rev 12:9 looks back on the completed casting with aorist. The casting is the SAME event viewed from different temporal vantages.

    Prince of This World Judged — Perfect State¶

    Nenikeka — Christ's Perfect Victory Declaration¶

    Context: Farewell discourse. Christ's declaration of victory over the world — PERFECT tense, not aorist.

    Notes: νενίκηκα [V-RAI-1S] G3528 Perf Act Ind Sg 1S — 'I have overcome' (PERFECT — completed with ongoing relevance). Contrast: Christ uses PERFECT here (ongoing validity emphasized), while at Rev 3:21 the aorist enikesan marks the completed act without emphasis on ongoing state. The perfect in John 16:33 grounds the disciples' present peace (present indicative: 'ye have peace') despite future tribulation.

    Engus — Lord's Nearness in Pauline Context¶

    Let your moderation be known unto all men. The Lord [is] at hand.

    Context: Philippians 4. Paul uses engus of the Lord's nearness — temporal, relational, or both.

    Notes: ἐγγύς [ADV] G1451 — 'at hand' (engus). Per G1451 word study: Php 4:5 sits on the boundary between temporal nearness and relational proximity — the Lord's nearness is both imminent return and present oversight. 30 NT occurrences of engus; Revelation has 2 (1:3, 22:10) in the inclusio.

    Aorist Redemption — Completed Cross-Work Declared¶

    And from Jesus Christ, [who is] the faithful witness, [and] the first begotten of the dead, and the prince of the kings of the earth. Unto him that loved us, and washed us from our sins in his own blood, And hath made us kings and priests unto God and his Father; to him [be] glory and dominion for ever and ever. Amen.

    Context: Epistolary doxology. The aorists 'washed' (luonti) and 'made' (epoiēsen) describe completed cross-work.

    Notes: luonti = participle from luō (G3089), washed/loosed — aorist active participle; epoiēsen [V-AAI-3S] G4160 Aor Act Ind 3S — 'hath made.' Both completed acts: the cross has already accomplished redemption and already constituted believers as a kingdom of priests. The 'already' of the redemptive work grounds the epistolary context.

    Hypomonē — John's Co-Participation, First Occurrence¶

    I John, who also am your brother, and companion in tribulation, and in the kingdom and patience of Jesus Christ, was in the isle that is called Patmos, for the word of God, and for the testimony of Jesus Christ.

    Context: John's self-introduction to the seven churches. He shares in 'the tribulation, kingdom, and patience of Jesus Christ.'

    Notes: ὑπομονή [G5281] — first Revelation occurrence, positioned alongside thlipsis (tribulation) and basileia (kingdom). The triad 'tribulation + kingdom + patience' is the already/not-yet formula in miniature: tribulation is present reality, kingdom is partially realized, patience is the required posture of the interval. John's own exile models the hypomonē he calls the churches to.

    Hypomonē in the Letters — Ephesus's Structural Endurance¶

    I know thy works, and thy labour, and thy patience, and how thou canst not bear them which are evil: and thou hast tried them which say they are apostles, and are not, and hast found them liars: And hast borne, and hast patience, and for my name's sake hast laboured, and hast not fainted.

    Context: Letter to Ephesus (Rev 2:1-7). Hypomonē commended twice in three verses.

    Notes: ὑπομονή [G5281] appears twice. 'Hast borne' (ebastasas, aorist — specific incidents of bearing) and 'hast patience' (hypomonēn echeis, present indicative — ongoing state). The repeated commendation establishes hypomonē as a virtue the churches should exemplify during the tribulation interval.

    Tachu — Conditional Disciplinary Coming¶

    Remember therefore from whence thou art fallen, and repent, and do the first works; or else I will come unto thee quickly, and will remove thy candlestick out of his place, except thou repent.

    Context: Letter to Ephesus. Conditional tachu — 'I will come quickly' as disciplinary threat contingent on non-repentance.

    Notes: ταχύ [ADV] G5035 — 'quickly' (tachy). Conditional structure: 'or else I will come quickly' = disciplinary intervention threatened, not the Parousia. Distinct from the eschatological tachy declarations of 22:7,12,20. This use constrains the semantic range — tachy can refer to local/specific divine actions, not only the final coming.

    Tachu — Conditional-Disciplinary, Pergamos¶

    Repent; or else I will come unto thee quickly, and will fight against them with the sword of my mouth.

    Context: Letter to Pergamos. Second conditional tachu threat — same structure as 2:5.

    Notes: ταχύ [ADV] G5035 — 'quickly.' Same conditional disciplinary use as 2:5. 'Sword of my mouth' = verbal judgment, connecting to the Christophany in Rev 1:16. The tachy in the seven letters (2:5, 2:16) functions as conditional imminence; the tachy in the epilogue (3:11, 22:7, 22:12, 22:20) functions as eschatological promise.

    Tachu — Transitional Promise, Hold Fast¶

    Behold, I come quickly: hold that fast which thou hast, that no man take thy crown.

    Context: Letter to Philadelphia (transitional use — encouragement in the context of the 'hour of temptation' promise of 3:10).

    Notes: ταχύ [ADV] G5035 — 'quickly.' This tachy appears mid-letters, before the epilogue cluster. It bridges the letters and the visions — the 'I come quickly' here is the first non-conditional tachy, functioning as promise rather than threat. 'Hold fast' = imperative response required during the interval before the coming.

    Nikao Aorist — Lion Has Prevailed, Cross Victory¶

    And one of the elders saith unto me, Weep not: behold, the Lion of the tribe of Juda, the Root of David, hath prevailed to open the book, and to loose the seven seals thereof.

    Context: Throne room. The Lion of Judah is identified as the only worthy one. His prevailing (enikesan) is a completed act that qualifies him to open the sealed scroll.

    Notes: ἐνίκησεν [V-AAI-3S] G3528 Aor Act Ind 3S — 'hath prevailed' (nikao aorist). This is the second christological nikao aorist (alongside 3:21 enikesan 1S). The cross-victory is complete — the Lion has already prevailed. The sealed scroll requires a prevailing one to open it; Christ's already-completed victory is the qualification.     Chronos Ouketi Estai — Time No Longer¶

    And sware by him that liveth for ever and ever, who created heaven, and the things that therein are, and the earth, and the things that therein are, and the sea, and the things which are therein, that there should be time no longer:

    Context: The mighty angel's oath (Rev 10:5-6). The cessation of chronos marks the end of the delay interval.

    Notes: χρόνος [N-NSM] G5550 + οὐκέτι [ADV-N G3765] + ἔσται [V-FDI-3S] G1510 Fut Mid Ind 3S. 'Time shall be no longer' = the measured delay interval is declared complete. Chronos = elapsed measured duration (contrast kairos = appointed season). The cessation of chronos is the transitional declaration between the sixth and seventh trumpet — the remaining 'not yet' interval is closing. Rev 10:7 explains: 'the mystery of God should be finished' when the seventh angel sounds.

    Egeneto Aorist Kingdom — Prophetic Perfect at the Seventh Trumpet¶

    And the seventh angel sounded; and there were great voices in heaven, saying, The kingdoms of this world are become [the kingdoms] of our Lord, and of his Christ; and he shall reign for ever and ever.

    Context: Seventh trumpet. The kingdom announcement using the proleptic/prophetic aorist egeneto — declared accomplished from the heavenly vantage point.

    Notes: Ἐγένετο [V-2ADI-3S] G1096 Aor Mid Ind 3S — 'are become' (prophetic/proleptic aorist). βασιλεύσει [V-FAI-3S] G936 Fut Act Ind 3S — 'he shall reign' (future). The tense contrast within the same announcement: the transfer is declared accomplished (aorist) while the endless reign is stated as future. NT parallel: Rev 12:10 (highest parallel, 0.507). OT background: Dan 2:44 and 7:27.

    Temple Opened — Theophany Escalation, Naos Access Contrast¶

    And the temple of God was opened in heaven, and there was seen in his temple the ark of his testament: and there were lightnings, and voices, and thunderings, and an earthquake, and great hail.

    Context: Immediate sequel to the seventh trumpet kingdom announcement (11:15-18). The temple opens — contrast with 15:8 where it closes.

    Notes: The naos (temple) OPENS at 11:19 — access to the ark of the covenant. This contrasts with Rev 15:8 where the naos is CLOSED (filled with smoke) until the bowls are finished. The pattern: open (11:19) → closed (15:8) → complete (16:17 gegonen) tracks the judgment timeline. From revs2-20 theophany formula escalation.

    Oligon Kairon — Dragon's Temporal Compression¶

    Therefore rejoice, [ye] heavens, and ye that dwell in them. Woe to the inhabiters of the earth and of the sea! for the devil is come down unto you, having great wrath, because he knoweth that he hath but a short time.

    Context: Heaven's dual announcement: celebration for heaven, woe for earth. The dragon's awareness of his limited time.

    Notes: κατέβη [V-2AAI-3S] G2597 Aor Act Ind 3S — 'is come down' (aorist = completed descent). ἔχων [V-PAP-NSM] — 'having' great wrath (present). εἰδὼς [V-RAP-NSM] G1492 Perf Act Ptcp — 'knoweth' (perfect participle = knowing with certainty). ἔχει [V-PAI-3S] G2192 — 'hath' (present). ὀλίγον καιρὸν [G2540, G3398] = 'a short time/season' (kairos, not chronos — appointed season, not measured duration). The dragon's knowledge of his limited kairos intensifies his rage — the not-yet interval that the church must endure is the dragon's short-but-intense season.

And the beast which I saw was like unto a leopard, and his feet were as [the feet] of a bear, and his mouth as the mouth of a lion: and the dragon gave him his power, and his seat, and great authority. And I saw one of his heads as it were wounded to death; and his deadly wound was healed: and all the world wondered after the beast. And they worshipped the dragon which gave power unto the beast: and they worshipped the beast, saying, Who [is] like unto the beast? who is able to make war with him?

    Context: Beast introduction (Rev 13:1-4). The dragon's derivative authority transfer to the beast — active giving (ἔδωκεν) by the dragon, contrasted with the passive edothē (V-API-3S) at vv.5,7 where unnamed divine sovereignty limits the beast.

    Notes: ἔδωκεν [V-AAI-3S] G1325 Aor Act Ind 3S — v.2 'dragon GAVE' (active — dragon as named subject). Contrast with v.5,7 ἐδόθη [V-API-3S] G1325 Aor Pass Ind 3S (divine passive — unnamed divine subject). The distinction is structural: the dragon's active giving of power to the beast stands behind the passives that limit that power. 'Who is like unto the beast?' = parody of the divine 'who is like God?' (mi khamocha). From revs2-19.

    Naos Closed — Temple Sealed Until Bowls Complete¶

    And the temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God, and from his power; and no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues of the seven angels were fulfilled.

    Context: Transition to the bowl judgments. The naos is closed — no entry until the seven plagues are finished.

    Notes: The naos (G3485) closed with smoke — same structure as Exodus 40 and 1 Kings 8 (divine glory filling the sanctuary, preventing entry). Contrast with Rev 11:19 (naos opened).     Three Temporal Categories — Fallen/Is/Not Yet Come in One Verse¶

    And there are seven kings: five are fallen, and one is, [and] the other is not yet come; and when he cometh, he must continue a short space.

    Context: Angel's interpretation of the seven heads (Rev 17:10). Three temporal categories within a single verse.

    Notes: 'Five are fallen' = past (epesan, aorist). 'One is' = present (estin, present indicative). 'Not yet come' = future (oupō ēlthen, oupō = not yet + aorist = has not come yet). Three tenses in one verse creating a past/present/future temporal map. 'A short space' = oligon dein autom menein — the final king's reign is bounded and brief.

    And I saw heaven opened, and behold a white horse; and he that sat upon him [was] called Faithful and True, and in righteousness he doth judge and make war.     Context: Epilogue. Third tachu declaration in Rev 22 (following 22:7). Reward language added.

    Notes: ταχύ [ADV] G5035 — 'quickly.' The reward formula 'to give every man according as his work shall be' echoes the judgment/accountability language of 11:18 and 20:12-13. The tachy here marks the imminence of the final accounting, not just the arrival of the person.

    Tachu — Final and Definitive Declaration, Community Response¶

    He which testifieth these things saith, Surely I come quickly. Amen. Even so, come, Lord Jesus.

    Context: Final words of Christ in Revelation. The community's liturgical response: 'Even so, come, Lord Jesus.'

    Notes: ταχύ [ADV] G5035 — 'quickly' (nai, erchomai tachy = 'yes, I come quickly'). The church's response 'Amen.     Ha Dei Genesthai — Daniel's LXX Source for Rev 1:1¶

    But there is a God in heaven that revealeth secrets, and maketh known to the king Nebuchadnezzar what shall be in the latter days. Thy dream, and the visions of thy head upon thy bed, are these;

    Context: Daniel interpreting Nebuchadnezzar's dream (Dan 2). The LXX of this verse contains 'ha dei genesthai' — the exact phrase repeated in Rev 1:1.

    Notes: LXX Dan 2:28: 'ha dei genesthai ep' eschatōn tōn hēmerōn' = 'what must come to pass in the last days.' Rev 1:1: 'ha dei genesthai en tachei' = 'what must shortly come to pass.' Rev 1:1 echoes Dan 2:28 verbatim except for the time modifier: 'in the last days' → 'shortly.' This substitution is the key temporal shift — Daniel's distant 'latter days' becomes Revelation's imminent 'shortly.'

    Naphal Naphal — Hebrew Prophetic Perfect Doublet Source¶

    Context: Isaiah's 'burden of Babylon' oracle. The doublet 'is fallen, is fallen' = Hebrew prophetic perfect (qatal used for future certainty).

    Surely he hath borne our griefs, and carried our sorrows: yet we did esteem him stricken, smitten of God, and afflicted. But he [was] wounded for our transgressions, [he was] bruised for our iniquities: the chastisement of our peace [was] upon him; and with his stripes we are healed.

    Context: Isaiah's fourth Servant Song. All Hebrew verbs are past-tense qatal forms describing the Servant's future suffering as accomplished fact.

    Notes: Hebrew qatal (perfect) throughout vv.4-5 describes future events as completed — the prophetic perfect in its most concentrated OT form.     Pauline Prophetic Aorist — Glorified as Past¶

    Destroy Devil Through Death — Cross Already¶

    Forasmuch then as the children are partakers of flesh and blood, he also himself likewise took part of the same; that through death he might destroy him that had the power of death, that is, the devil;

    Tachu in Vision — Speed/Urgency Meaning¶
