    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    What is the seven-element template that structures each letter to the seven churches (address, self-identification, commendation, rebuke, exhortation, promise, ear formula)? Document the template elements in each letter. What does the ear/overcomer reversal between the first three and last four letters reveal — in letters 1-3 the ear formula comes before the overcomer promise; in letters 4-7 the overcomer promise comes before the ear formula? What does this 3+4 structural division signify? Does it correspond to any other 3+4 or 4+3 patterns in Revelation?

    G3528 — nikao¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3528-nikao.md

    Occurrences: 28

    To conquer, overcome, prevail, gain the victory. 28 NT occurrences, 17 in Revelation (61%). Present active participle ho nikon in all seven letter promises (ongoing overcoming). Aorist enikesen at 5:5 (Christ's completed victory) and 12:11 (believers' realized victory). Future nikesei at 17:14 (decisive eschatological victory). Adversarial uses at 11:7 and 13:7 (beast overcomes). KJV translates 77% as overcome/overcometh/overcame.

    Semantic categories: eschatological victory, cosmic victory, moral victory, military conquest, forensic vindication

    Key verses: Rev 2:7, Rev 2:11, Rev 2:17, Rev 2:26, Rev 3:5, Rev 3:12, Rev 3:21, Rev 5:5, Rev 12:11, Rev 21:7

    Relevance: Core verb in all seven overcomer promises. Tense distribution (present in letters, aorist at pivot, mixed in conflict) is a primary structural observation. The nikao thread connects the letters to the rest of the book.

    G3529 — nike¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3529-nike.md

    Occurrences: 1

    Victory (noun). Hapax legomenon at 1 John 5:4: 'this is the victory (nike) that overcometh (nikao) the world, even our faith.' Cognate of nikao (G3528) and nikos (G3534).

    Semantic categories: victory, conquest

    Key verses: 1 John 5:4

    Relevance: Completes the nikao word family. Nike (noun, 1x) + nikao (verb, 28x) + nikos (noun, 4x) form the complete victory vocabulary.

    G3534 — nikos¶

    Occurrences: 4

    Victory, conquest (neuter noun). 4 NT occurrences: Matt 12:20, 1 Cor 15:54, 1 Cor 15:55, 1 Cor 15:57. All KJV translations: 'victory.' Part of the nikao word family. 1 Cor 15:54-57 uses nikos three times in the death/resurrection victory context: 'Death is swallowed up in victory... O death, where is thy sting? O grave, where is thy victory? ...thanks be to God, which giveth us the victory through our Lord Jesus Christ.'

    Semantic categories: victory, conquest, eschatological triumph

    Key verses: 1 Cor 15:54, 1 Cor 15:55, 1 Cor 15:57, Matt 12:20

    Relevance: Completes the nikao word family (G3528 nikao + G3529 nike + G3534 nikos). The 1 Cor 15 victory-over-death context connects to the second death promise at Rev 2:11.

    G3004 — lego¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3004-lego.md

    Occurrences: 1349

    To say, speak, declare. Most common verb of speech in the NT (1244+ verses). In the letters, tade legei ('these things saith') is the invariable formula introducing each Christological self-identification. The formula echoes the LXX prophetic formula tade legei Kurios (thus saith the LORD), rendering Hebrew koh amar YHWH. Over 400 OT occurrences of the prophetic messenger formula.

    Semantic categories: speech, declaration, prophetic formula, naming

    Key verses: Rev 2:1, Rev 2:8, Rev 2:12, Rev 2:18, Rev 3:1, Rev 3:7, Rev 3:14

    Relevance: The tade legei formula is present in all seven letters and is the key to genre classification. It parallels the OT prophetic oracle formula, Aune's royal edict preamble, and Shea's treaty preamble.

    G1125 — grapho¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1125-grapho.md

    Occurrences: 216

    To write. In the letters, grapson (aorist active imperative, 2nd person singular) appears in all seven addresses as the 'write command': 'unto the angel of the church in [city] write.' This is one of the three invariable template elements.

    Semantic categories: writing, inscription, divine command

    Key verses: Rev 2:1, Rev 2:8, Rev 2:12, Rev 2:18, Rev 3:1, Rev 3:7, Rev 3:14

    Relevance: The write command (grapson) is an invariable element in the letter template. Its aorist imperative form conveys urgency — a single decisive command.

    G1492 — oida¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1492-oida.md

    Occurrences: 334

    To know intuitively, to perceive (perfect tense functioning as present). Distinguished from ginosko (G1097, experiential knowledge). In the letters, oida introduces the 'I know' assessment formula: 'oida ta erga sou' (I know thy works) in all seven letters. The formula's function shifts: commendatory in Ephesus-Thyatira, transitioning to accusatory in Sardis and Laodicea.

    Semantic categories: intuitive knowledge, divine omniscience, assessment

    Key verses: Rev 2:2, Rev 2:9, Rev 2:13, Rev 2:19, Rev 3:1, Rev 3:8, Rev 3:15

    Relevance: One of three invariable template elements. Its valence shift from commendatory to accusatory across the seven letters is a structural observation.

    G3340 — metanoeo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3340-metanoeo.md

    Occurrences: 34

    To repent, change one's mind. 34 NT occurrences (32 unique verses, 36 tokens). 10 in Revelation (highest density at 2.5 per 100 verses). In the letters, appears in exactly 5 of 7: Ephesus (2:5, 2x), Pergamos (2:16), Thyatira (2:21-22, reference to Jezebel), Sardis (3:3), Laodicea (3:19). Absent from Smyrna and Philadelphia — the two letters without rebuke. TM039 from revs-08: metanoeo distribution perfectly correlates with rebuke presence.

    Semantic categories: kingdom proclamation, eschatological urgency, hardened refusal

    Key verses: Rev 2:5, Rev 2:16, Rev 2:21, Rev 3:3, Rev 3:19

    Relevance: Metanoeo in exactly 5 of 7 letters, correlating with rebuke presence (TM039). Its absence from Smyrna and Philadelphia confirms these as the two rebuke-free letters. The verb also appears in judgment contexts (9:20-21, 16:9,11) where repentance is refused.

    G4151 — pneuma¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4151-pneuma.md

    Occurrences: 385

    Spirit, breath, wind. In the ear formula, 'to Pneuma' (articular, nominative, personal agent) — 'what THE Spirit saith unto the churches.' This is grammatically distinct from the en pneumati structural markers (anarthrous, dative, visionary domain) per revs2-01. The Spirit is the speaking agent in the ear formula, addressing all churches collectively.

    Semantic categories: Holy Spirit, prophetic speech, divine agent

    Key verses: Rev 2:7, Rev 2:11, Rev 2:17, Rev 2:29, Rev 3:6, Rev 3:13, Rev 3:22

    Relevance: The Spirit/churches clause (ti to Pneuma legei tais ekklesiais) is confined to the seven ear formula occurrences in chs 2-3. It is ABSENT from Rev 13:9's modified formula. This confinement is a structural marker of the letter section.

    G1577 — ekklesia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1577-ekklesia.md

    Occurrences: 118

    Church, assembly, congregation. 115 unique verses, 118 tokens, 19 books. In Revelation: 19 occurrences in chs 1-3, 1 in epilogue (22:16), completely absent from Visions 2-4. This distribution is a major structural marker per revs2-01. Derives from ek (out of) + kaleo (to call) — 'called out assembly.' LXX translates Hebrew qahal with ekklesia.

    Semantic categories: local congregation, universal church, prophetic address

    Key verses: Rev 1:4, Rev 1:11, Rev 1:20, Rev 2:1, Rev 2:7, Rev 2:23, Rev 22:16

    Relevance: The ekklesia distribution (19x in chs 1-3, absent from Visions 2-4, reappearing at 22:16) creates an inclusio framing the entire book. The word appears in every ear formula ('unto the churches') and vanishes once the letters end.

    G228 — alethinos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G228-alethinos.md

    Occurrences: 27

    True, genuine, real. 27 tokens in 25 verses, 80% in Johannine literature. In the letters: Philadelphia 'he that is true' (3:7, ho alethinos) and Laodicea 'faithful and true witness' (3:14, pistos kai alethinos). Also at 6:10 ('holy and true') in the martyrs' cry. Semantic categories: genuine vs copy (type/antitype), divine character (faithful and true), true judgments.

    Semantic categories: genuine, faithful, divine character, type/antitype contrast

    Key verses: Rev 3:7, Rev 3:14, Rev 6:10, Rev 19:11, Rev 21:5

    Relevance: Two letter self-IDs use alethinos: Philadelphia (3:7) and Laodicea (3:14). The word connects to the 'faithful and true' thread across Revelation (1:5, 3:14, 19:11, 21:5, 22:6).

    G5207 — huios¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5207-huios.md

    Occurrences: 382

    Son. Thyatira's self-ID: 'ho huios tou theou' (the Son of God, 2:18) — the ONLY letter using this title. All other letters use descriptive phrases from Rev 1:12-18. The Son of God title connects to Ps 2:7 ('Thou art my Son') which is also the source of Thyatira's rod-of-iron overcomer promise (2:27).

    Semantic categories: divine sonship, messianic title, Christological designation

    Key verses: Rev 2:18, Rev 21:7

    Relevance: Thyatira's unique Son of God title (TM044) is one of the markers of its structural centrality. The title connects Thyatira's self-ID (2:18) to Thyatira's overcomer promise (2:27 via Ps 2:7-9) and to the universal promise (21:7 'he shall be my son').

    G2222 — zoe¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2222-zoe.md

    Occurrences: 134

    Life. Appears in three letter promises: tree of life (Ephesus 2:7, xulon tes zoes), crown of life (Smyrna 2:10, stephanos tes zoes), book of life (Sardis 3:5, biblos tes zoes). Three distinct 'of life' constructs using the same genitive tes zoes.

    Semantic categories: eternal life, reward imagery, eschatological promise

    Key verses: Rev 2:7, Rev 2:10, Rev 3:5, Rev 20:12, Rev 22:2

    Relevance: Zoe connects three of the seven overcomer promises through the genitive construction 'tes zoes.' The tree of life arc (Gen 2:9 -> Rev 2:7 -> Rev 22:2) is a complete narrative cycle.

    G2288 — thanatos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2288-thanatos.md

    Occurrences: 119

    Death. In the letters: Smyrna self-ID 'was dead and is alive' (2:8); Smyrna promise 'not hurt of the second death' (2:11, thanatos deuteros); Sardis accusation 'thou art dead' (3:1); Thyatira judgment 'kill her children with death' (2:23). Second death (thanatos deuteros) at 2:11, 20:6, 20:14, 21:8.

    Semantic categories: physical death, second death, spiritual death

    Key verses: Rev 2:8, Rev 2:11, Rev 2:23, Rev 3:1, Rev 20:6, Rev 20:14, Rev 21:8

    Relevance: The second death (thanatos deuteros) connects Smyrna's overcomer promise (2:11) to its fulfillment at 20:6 and the universal antithesis at 21:8.

    G3686 — onoma¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3686-onoma.md

    Occurrences: 228

    Name. Appears across multiple letters: new name on white stone (Pergamos 2:17); name that lives yet dead (Sardis 3:1); not blot out name from book of life (Sardis 3:5); confess name (Sardis 3:5); name of God + name of city + new name (Philadelphia 3:12). Names thread connects promises to fulfillment at 22:4 ('his name in their foreheads').

    Semantic categories: identity, divine name, new identity, eschatological naming

    Key verses: Rev 2:17, Rev 3:1, Rev 3:5, Rev 3:12, Rev 22:4

    Relevance: Onoma is one of the most distributed words across the letter promises, appearing in 4 of 7 letters. The name thread connects to 22:4 fulfillment.

    G976 — biblos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G976-biblos.md

    Occurrences: 13

    Book, scroll. In the letters: 'book of life' (biblos tes zoes) at Sardis 3:5. Fulfillment at 20:12,15 (book of life in the judgment scene) and 21:27 (Lamb's book of life).

    Semantic categories: book, scroll, book of life

    Key verses: Rev 3:5, Rev 20:12, Rev 20:15, Rev 21:27

    Relevance: Biblos tes zoes connects Sardis promise to its judgment fulfillment. The OT background is Exod 32:32-33 (Moses' intercession).

    G2937 — ktisis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2937-ktisis.md

    Occurrences: 19

    Creation, creature. In the letters: Laodicea's self-ID 'the beginning of the creation of God' (he arche tes ktiseos tou theou, 3:14). This title is NOT drawn from Rev 1:12-18 — it stands outside the commissioning vision.

    Semantic categories: creation, creature, cosmological origin

    Key verses: Rev 3:14

    Relevance: Part of Laodicea's unique triple self-ID (Amen + faithful/true witness + beginning of creation). The phrase's meaning (source/ruler of creation vs. first created being) is a classic interpretive question.

    G3144 — martys¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3144-martys.md

    Occurrences: 34

    Witness, martyr. In the letters: Laodicea self-ID 'faithful and true witness' (ho martys ho pistos kai alethinos, 3:14); Antipas 'my faithful martyr' (ho martys mou ho pistos, 2:13). Christ as faithful witness at 1:5. The same vocabulary (martys + pistos) applied to both Christ and Antipas.

    Semantic categories: witness, testimony, martyrdom

    Key verses: Rev 1:5, Rev 2:13, Rev 3:14

    Relevance: The martys+pistos formula links Laodicea's self-ID (3:14) to Christ's introduction (1:5) and to Antipas in Pergamos (2:13). The witness vocabulary threads through the letter section.

    G4567 — satanas¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4567-satanas.md

    Occurrences: 36

    Satan, adversary. In the letters: 'synagogue of Satan' (Smyrna 2:9, Philadelphia 3:9 — exclusive to this pair, TM041); 'Satan's seat/throne' (Pergamos 2:13); 'depths of Satan' (Thyatira 2:24). The synagoge tou Satana phrase is one of 8 shared vocabulary items in the Smyrna-Philadelphia VP (VP046 Strong).

    Semantic categories: adversary, accuser, eschatological opponent

    Key verses: Rev 2:9, Rev 2:13, Rev 2:24, Rev 3:9

    Relevance: Satan appears in 4 of 7 letters. The synagogue of Satan formula (G4864+G4567) is exclusive to the Smyrna-Philadelphia pair, providing the strongest VP evidence for their chiastic pairing (B-B').

    G746 — arche¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G746-arche.md

    Occurrences: 58

    G281 — amen¶

    Occurrences: 152

    Amen, truly, verily. 152 NT occurrences: 64 'Verily,' 51 'Amen,' 37 'verily.' In the letters: Laodicea's self-ID 'the Amen' (ho Amen, 3:14) — Christ as the personified Amen, the embodiment of divine faithfulness. The only letter using a Hebrew loanword as a Christological title. Also at 1:6,7 (doxological amens) and 22:20,21 (closing amens).

    Semantic categories: affirmation, divine faithfulness, Christological title

    Key verses: Rev 3:14, Rev 1:6-7, Rev 22:20-21

    Relevance: Laodicea's unique 'the Amen' title (ho Amen) is structurally notable — it uses a title NOT found in the Rev 1 commissioning vision. This is part of the evidence that Laodicea's self-ID is the most independent of the seven.

    G191 — akouo¶

    G3775 — ous¶

    Ear (neuter noun). 37 NT occurrences. In Revelation: 8 occurrences (7 in ear formula + 1 at 13:9). Notably SINGULAR (ous, accusative neuter singular) in Revelation vs PLURAL (ota, accusative neuter plural) in the Synoptic parallels (Matt 11:15, 13:9, etc.). This is a deliberate Johannine modification.

    Key verses: Rev 2:7, Rev 2:11, Rev 2:17, Rev 2:29, Rev 3:6, Rev 3:13, Rev 3:22, Rev 13:9

    Relevance: The singular ous vs plural ota distinction between Revelation and the Synoptics is a textual marker (TM). The singular may emphasize individual responsibility: each person individually called to hear.

    G32 — aggelos¶

    Angel, messenger. 186 NT occurrences: 71 'angel,' 61 'angels,' 3 'messenger.' In the letters: 'to the angel of the church in [city]' (to aggelo tes en [city] ekklesias) — address formula in all seven letters. The identity of the 'angel' is debated: guardian angel, human messenger/bishop, or heavenly representative of the church.

    Relevance: The aggelos address is an invariable element in the letter template. The dative 'to aggelo' identifies the recipient of each letter. Per 1:20, the seven stars = the seven angels of the seven churches.

    G4864 — synagoge¶
