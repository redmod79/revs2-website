    Verses¶

    Question¶

    How does the Genesis-Revelation bookend structure work? Map the seven parallel pairs (tree of life, river, no more curse, face of God, very good/all things new, serpent, marriage) with LXX vocabulary chains. Does Matthew 24 provide a structural key to the seal sequence (Rev 6)? Does Paul's man of lawlessness (2 Thess 2:3-12) parallel Revelation's beast, using Daniel 7:25 as the common source?

    God's Verdict: Very Good¶

    And God saw every thing that he had made, and, behold, [it was] very good. And the evening and the morning were the sixth day.

    Context: The concluding verdict of creation week. tov meod (very good) is the authoritative divine assessment before the fall. Bookend pair: Gen 1:31 original goodness -> Rev 21:5 eschatological renewal.

    Notes: Hebrew: tov meod (very good). LXX: kalon lian. kalon (G2570) replaced by kainos (G2537) in Rev 21:5.

    Tree of Life in Eden¶

    And out of the ground made the LORD God to grow every tree that is pleasant to the sight, and good for food; the tree of life also in the midst of the garden, and the tree of knowledge of good and evil.

    Context: The etz hachayyim (tree of life) at the center of Eden. LXX: xylon tes zoes (G3586 + G2222) - identical phrase at Rev 22:2 and 22:14. A-VERBAL IDENTITY.

    Notes: Hebrew: etz hachayyim (H6086 + H2416). LXX: xylon tes zoes (G3586 + G2222). Appears at Rev 2:7, 22:2, 22:14. Classification: A-VERBAL IDENTITY.

    River Flowing from Eden¶

    And a river went out of Eden to water the garden; and from thence it was parted, and became into four heads.

    Context: The nahar (river) flowing from Eden. Second bookend pair with Rev 22:1. LXX: potamos (G4215) for nahar (H5104).

    Notes: Hebrew: nahar (H5104). LXX: potamos (G4215). PMI 32.49. Rev 22:1 adds hydatos zoes via Ezek 47 intermediary.

    The First Marriage: Adam and Eve¶

    And Adam said, This [is] now bone of my bones, and flesh of my flesh: she shall be called Woman, because she was taken out of Man. Therefore shall a man leave his father and his mother, and shall cleave unto his wife: and they shall be one flesh.

    Context: Institution of marriage at creation. Seventh bookend pair: Gen 2:23-24 (first marriage) -> Rev 19:7-9 (marriage of the Lamb). Hebrew ishshāh (H802) -> LXX gynē (G1135).

    Notes: Hebrew: ishshāh (H802), ish (H376). LXX: gynē (G1135). Eph 5:31-32 quotes Gen 2:24 LXX verbatim and calls it a mystery concerning Christ and the church.

    The Serpent's Entrance¶

    Now the serpent was more subtil than any beast of the field which the LORD God had made. And he said unto the woman, Yea, hath God said, Ye shall not eat of every tree of the garden?

    Context: The nachash (H5175) makes its first appearance. Fifth bookend anchor: Gen 3:1 (serpent enters) -> Rev 12:9 and 20:2 (serpent identified as devil and Satan). LXX ophis (G3789).

    Notes: Hebrew: nachash (H5175). LXX: ophis (G3789). PMI 31.32. Rev 12:9: ho ophis ho archaios ho kaloumenos Diabolos kai ho Satanas.

    The Temptation and Fall¶

    But of the fruit of the tree which [is] in the midst of the garden, God hath said, Ye shall not eat of it, neither shall ye touch it, lest ye die. And the serpent said unto the woman, Ye shall not surely die: For God doth know that in the day ye eat thereof, then your eyes shall be opened, and ye shall be as gods, knowing good and evil. And when the woman saw that the tree [was] good for food, and that it [was] pleasant to the eyes, and a tree to be desired to make [one] wise, she took of the fruit thereof, and did eat, and gave also unto her husband with her; and he did eat.

    Context: The serpent's lie directly contradicts God's word. 2 Cor 11:3 connects this event to satanic deception of the church.

    Notes: Hebrew: lo mut temutun (ye shall not surely die). Three-fold appeal (good for food, pleasant to eyes, desired for wisdom) anticipates 1 John 2:16. 2 Cor 11:3: exapataō (G1818, thoroughly deceive).

    Hiding from the Face of God¶

    And they heard the voice of the LORD God walking in the garden in the cool of the day: and Adam and his wife hid themselves from the presence of the LORD God amongst the trees of the garden.

    Context: Fourth bookend anchor: Gen 3:8 (hiding from God's face) -> Rev 22:4 (his servants shall see his face). Hebrew panim (H6440) -> LXX prosopon (G4383).

    Notes: Hebrew: panim (H6440), mithallekh (Hithpael participle). LXX: prosopon (G4383). Rev 22:4 opsetai to prosopon autou - direct reversal.

    Curse on the Serpent and Protoevangelium¶

    And the LORD God said unto the serpent, Because thou hast done this, thou [art] cursed above all cattle, and above every beast of the field; upon thy belly shalt thou go, and dust shalt thou eat all the days of thy life: And I will put enmity between thee and the woman, and between thy seed and her seed; it shall bruise thy head, and thou shalt bruise his heel.

    Context: The protoevangelium: Gen 3:15 seed-of-the-woman language fulfilled in Rev 12:1-5 (woman gives birth to male child) and Rev 20:2 (serpent bound).

    Notes: Hebrew: shuph (H7779, bruise/crush), rosh (H7218, head), aqev (H6119, heel), eybah (H342, enmity), zera (H2233, seed). Same root H7779 for both bruising occurrences - protoevangelium symmetry.

    The Curse on the Ground¶

    And unto Adam he said, Because thou hast hearkened unto the voice of thy wife, and hast eaten of the tree, of which I commanded thee, saying, Thou shalt not eat of it: cursed [is] the ground for thy sake; in sorrow shalt thou eat [of] it all the days of thy life; Thorns also and thistles shall it bring forth to thee; and thou shalt eat the herb of the field;

    Context: Third bookend anchor: arur ha'adamah (H779 + H127) -> Rev 22:3 no more curse (katathema, G2652). LXX renders arur as epikataratos (G1944).

    Notes: Hebrew: arur (H779, Qal passive participle), ha'adamah (H127), atzavon (H6093). LXX: epikataratos (G1944). Rev 22:3 uses katathema (G2652, NT hapax) - comprehensive reversal.

    Expulsion and the Tree of Life Barred¶

    And the LORD God said, Behold, the man is become as one of us, to know good and evil: and now, lest he put forth his hand, and take also of the tree of life, and eat, and live for ever: Therefore the LORD God sent him forth from the garden of Eden, to till the ground from whence he was taken. So he drove out the man; and he placed at the east of the garden of Eden Cherubims, and a flaming sword which turned every way, to keep the way of the tree of life.

    Context: The tree of life blocked by cherubim after the fall. Rev 22:2 and 22:14 restore full access.

    Notes: Hebrew: keruvim (H3742), lahat (H3858, flame), cherev (H2719, sword), hithpakekhet (turning, Hithpael participle), lishmor derek etz hachayyim.

    River of Water of Life from the Throne¶

    And he shewed me a pure river of water of life, clear as crystal, proceeding out of the throne of God and of the Lamb.

    Context: River bookend resolution (pair 2): potamon hydatos zoes from the throne of God and the Lamb. Source shifts from geographic Eden to the divine throne. Ezek 47:1-12 is the OT intermediary.

    Notes: Greek: potamon (G4215) hydatos (G5204) zoes (G2222) ekporeuomenon (G1607, proceeding) ek tou thronou tou theou kai tou arniou (G721).

    Tree of Life Restored¶

    In the midst of the street of it, and on either side of the river, [was there] the tree of life, which bare twelve [manner of] fruits, [and] yielded her fruit every month: and the leaves of the tree [were] for the healing of the nations.

    Context: Tree of life bookend resolution (pair 1): xylon tes zoes - A-VERBAL IDENTITY with Gen 2:9 LXX. The tree blocked by cherubim (Gen 3:24) is now fully accessible.

    Notes: Greek: xylon (G3586) tes zoes (G2222). Identical phrase in LXX Gen 2:9, Rev 2:7, Rev 22:2, Rev 22:14. Adds karpon dodeka (twelve fruits) kata mena, phyla eis therapeian tōn ethnōn echoing Ezek 47:12.

    No More Curse; They Shall See His Face¶

    And there shall be no more curse: but the throne of God and of the Lamb shall be in it; and his servants shall serve him: And they shall see his face; and his name [shall be] in their foreheads. And there shall be no night there; and they need no candle, neither light of the sun; for the Lord God giveth them light: and they shall reign for ever and ever.

    Context: Curse reversal (pair 3) and face-of-God reversal (pair 4). Rev 22:3: pan katathema ouk estai eti abolishes Gen 3:17 curse. Rev 22:4: opsetai to prosopon autou reverses Gen 3:8 hiding.

    Notes: Rev 22:3: katathema (G2652, NT hapax). Rev 22:4: opsetai (G3708, future middle 3pl) to prosopon (G4383). Rev 22:5: ouk estin eti (no more night). TM216 ouk eti cluster.

    New Heaven and New Earth; All Things New¶

    And I saw a new heaven and a new earth: for the first heaven and the first earth were passed away; and there was no more sea. And I John saw the holy city, new Jerusalem, coming down from God out of heaven, prepared as a bride adorned for her husband. And I heard a great voice out of heaven saying, Behold, the tabernacle of God [is] with men, and he will dwell with them, and they shall be his people, and God himself shall be with them, [and be] their God. And God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes; and there shall be no more death, neither sorrow, nor crying, neither shall there be any more pain: for the former things are passed away. And he that sat upon the throne said, Behold, I make all things new. And he said unto me, Write: for these words are true and faithful.

    Context: Bookend pair 6 resolution: Gen 1:31 tov meod -> Rev 21:5 kaina poiō panta. kainos (G2537, qualitative newness). TM216 ouk eti negation cluster. Covenant formula fulfilled in direct divine dwelling.

    Notes: Greek: kainos (G2537) ouranos kai gē. hē skēnē (G4633) tou theou meta tōn anthrōpōn. idou kaina poiō panta: present tense poiō. TM216 cluster. Isa 65:17 LXX: poiō kainos ouranos kai gē.

    The Ancient Serpent Identified¶

    And the great dragon was cast out, that old serpent, called the Devil, and Satan, which deceiveth the whole world: he was cast out into the earth, and his angels were cast out with him.

    Context: Serpent pair explicit identification: ho ophis ho archaios = Devil and Satan. archaios (G744, primeval/from the beginning) is the direct verbal link to the Genesis serpent.

    Notes: Greek: ho drakon (G1404) ho megas, eblēthē (G906, aorist passive), ho ophis (G3789) ho archaios (G744), kaloumenos (G2564) Diabolos (G1228) kai ho Satanas (G4567). Four-fold identification formula.

    The Serpent Bound¶

    And he laid hold on the dragon, that old serpent, which is the Devil, and Satan, and bound him a thousand years,

    Context: Serpent identification formula repeated. The thousand-year binding is the eschatological fulfillment of Gen 3:15 seed crushing the serpent's head.

    Notes: Greek: ton drakonta (G1404, accusative) ton ophin (G3789, accusative) ton archaion (G744, accusative). ekratēsen (seized), edēsen (G1210, bound), chilia etē (thousand years).

    The Devil Cast into the Lake of Fire¶

    And the devil that deceived them was cast into the lake of fire and brimstone, where the beast and the false prophet [are], and shall be tormented day and night for ever and ever.

    Context: Final destruction of the dragon - ultimate fulfillment of Gen 3:15. The lake of fire is the permanent end of the deceiver who began in Eden.

    Notes: Greek: ho diabolos (G1228) ho planōn (G4105) autous, eblēthē eis tēn limnēn tou pyros (G4442) kai theiou (G2303). basanisthēsontai (G928) hēmeras kai nyktos eis tous aionas tōn aiōnōn.

    The Marriage of the Lamb¶

    Let us be glad and rejoice, and give honour to him: for the marriage of the Lamb is come, and his wife hath made herself ready.     For the stars of heaven and the constellations thereof shall not give their light: the sun shall be darkened in his going forth, and the moon shall not cause her light to shine.

    Notes: Hebrew: kochevei hashamayim lo yahu orram; hashemesh (H8121) batzetho chashakh (H2821); hayareah (H3394) lo yahil orah. Same sun/moon/stars vocabulary as Matt 24:29 and Rev 6:12-13.

    Persecution Before the Signs¶

    But before all these, they shall lay their hands on you, and persecute [you], delivering [you] up to the synagogues, and into prisons, being brought before kings and rulers for my name's sake.

    Context: Luke's parallel to Matt 24:9 (SP114 position 5) - persecution comes 'before all these' (cosmic signs), consistent with seal 5 martyrdom preceding seal 6 cosmic signs.

    Notes: Greek: pro de toutōn panton (before all these) epibalousin eph' hymas tas cheiras kai diōxousin (G1377) paradidountes eis synagōgas kai phylakas. basileis kai hēgemonas (G2232) heneken tou onomatos mou.

    Mark's Olivet Cosmic Signs¶

    But in those days, after that tribulation, the sun shall be darkened, and the moon shall not give her light, And the stars of heaven shall fall, and the powers that are in heaven shall be shaken. And then shall they see the Son of man coming in the clouds with great power and glory. And then shall he send his angels, and shall gather together his elect from the four winds, from the uttermost part of the earth to the uttermost part of heaven.

    Context: Mark's version confirms the three-gospel SP114 pattern for positions 6-7. The four-winds gathering of the elect anticipates Rev 7 sealing of 144,000.

    Notes: Greek: ho hēlios skotisthēsetai (G4654), hē selēnē ou dōsei to phengos autēs, hoi asteres tou ouranou esontai piptuntes. Son of man erchomenon en nephelais (G3507) meta dynameōs pollēs kai doxēs. apostelei tous angelous episynaxai (G1996) tous eklektous (G1588).

    The Beast That Was, Is Not, and Shall Be¶

    The beast that thou sawest was, and is not; and shall ascend out of the bottomless pit, and go into perdition: and they that dwell on the earth shall wonder, whose names were not written in the book of life from the foundation of the world, when they behold the beast that was, and is not, and yet is.

    Context: The beast's 'was, is not, and yet is' is a parody of the divine 'who is and was and is to come.' Ultimately consigned to apōleian (G684) - same word as 2 Thess 2:3 'son of perdition.'

    Notes: Greek: ēn kai ouk estin kai mellei anabainein ek tēs abyssou (G12) kai eis apōleian (G684) hypagei. Parodies Rev 1:4 (ho ōn kai ho ēn kai ho erchomenos). apōleian connects to 2 Thess 2:3 (ho huios tēs apōleias).

    The Sharp Sword from Christ's Mouth¶

    And out of his mouth goeth a sharp sword, that with it he should smite the nations: and he shall rule them with a rod of iron: and he treadeth the winepress of the fierceness and wrath of Almighty God.

    Context: The sharp sword from Christ's mouth connects to 2 Thess 2:8 where the Lord 'shall consume [the lawless one] with the spirit of his mouth.' Same stoma vocabulary creates the verbal link.

    Notes: Greek: ek tou stomatos (G4750) autou ekporeuetai (G1607) rhomphaia (G4501) oxeia hina en autē pataxē (G3960) ta ethnē. poimanei en rhabdō sidēra (G4464 + G4603). stoma ties: Dan 7:8, Rev 13:5, 2 Thess 2:8, Rev 19:15.

    The Aaronic Blessing: Face of God¶

    The LORD make his face shine upon thee, and be gracious unto thee: The LORD lift up his countenance upon thee, and give thee peace.

    Context: The Aaronic priestly blessing anticipates Rev 22:4's direct face-vision. The panim (H6440) of God shining upon his people is the OT intermediate expression between Gen 3:8 hiding and Rev 22:4 beatific vision.

    Notes: Hebrew: ya'er (H215, Hiphil, cause to shine) YHWH panav (H6440) elecha vichuneka (H2603). yisa YHWH panav elecha veyasem lecha shalom (H7965). panav -> prosopon (LXX). Rev 22:4 fulfills: opsetai to prosopon autou.

    No Man Can See God's Face and Live¶

    And he said, Thou canst not see my face: for there shall no man see me, and live.

    Context: Exod 33:20 establishes the intermediate-state rule: no human can see God's face directly and survive. Rev 22:4's 'they shall see his face' is the definitive eschatological reversal.

    Notes: Hebrew: lo tukhal lir'ot et panai (you cannot see my face) ki lo yir'ani ha'adam vachai (for no man shall see me and live). Rev 22:4 resolves this: the redeemed see his face because fully conformed to his image.

    New Heavens and New Earth of Righteousness¶

    Nevertheless we, according to his promise, look for new heavens and a new earth, wherein dwelleth righteousness.

    Context: Peter's anticipation grounds the new creation in 'his promise' - pointing to Isa 65:17 and 66:22. The dwelling of dikaiosynē (righteousness) reverses the corruption introduced by the fall.

    Notes: Greek: kainous ouranous (G2537 + G3772) kai gēn kainēn kata to epangelma autou (G1862, promise) prosdokōmen (G4328). en hois dikaiosynē (G1343) katoikei (G2730). Points to Isa 65:17; 66:22.

    New Creation Permanence¶

    For as the new heavens and the new earth, which I will make, shall remain before me, saith the LORD, so shall your seed and your name remain.

    Context: Second major OT prophecy of new heavens and new earth (with Isa 65:17), confirming the permanence of the new creation. Rev 21:1 fulfills this.

    Notes: Hebrew: hashamayim hachadashim wha'arets hachadashah asher ani oseh omedim (standing, present participle) lefanai. LXX: ho ouranos ho kainos kai hē gē hē kainē ha egō poiō diamenei enōpion mou.

    God's Dwelling and End of Suffering¶

    And I heard a great voice out of heaven saying, Behold, the tabernacle of God [is] with men, and he will dwell with them, and they shall be his people, and God himself shall be with them, [and be] their God. And God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes; and there shall be no more death, neither sorrow, nor crying, neither shall there be any more pain: for the former things are passed away.

    Context: Covenant formula fulfilled in direct divine dwelling. Rev 21:4 ouk eti cluster (no more death, tears, crying, pain) comprehensively reverses the fall's consequences (Gen 3:16-19).

    Notes: Greek: hē skēnē (G4633) tou theou meta tōn anthrōpōn. skenoo (G4637). Covenant formula: autoi laoi autou esontai - echoes Jer 31:33, Ezek 37:27. ouk eti: thanatos (G2288), penthos (G3997), kraugē (G2906), ponos (G4192).

    Worthy to Receive Glory: Creator God¶

    Thou art worthy, O Lord, to receive glory and honour and power: for thou hast created all things, and for thy pleasure they are and were created.

    Context: The throne-room doxology establishes creation as the basis of God's worthiness. The same creator who made all things very good (Gen 1:31) will make all things new (Rev 21:5).

    Notes: Greek: axios ei (G514) ho kyrios kai ho theos hēmōn labein tēn doxan (G1391) kai tēn timēn (G5092) kai tēn dynamin (G1411). hoti sy ektisas (G2936) ta panta kai dia to thelēma sou (G2307) ēsan kai ektisthēsan.

    Come, I Will Show You the Bride¶

    And there came unto me one of the seven angels which had the seven vials full of the seven last plagues, and talked with me, saying, Come hither, I will shew thee the bride, the Lamb's wife.

    Context: The angel's announcement 'I will show you the bride' - then shows John the holy city Jerusalem (Rev 21:10-11). Provides the interpretive key: the bride = the city = the people of God.

    Notes: Greek: tēn nymphēn (G3565) tēn gynaika tou arniou (G1135). Then shows the holy city Jerusalem - bride = New Jerusalem = covenant people. Compare Rev 17:1-3 where same formula introduces harlot Babylon.

    Your Maker is Your Husband¶

    For thy Maker [is] thine husband; the LORD of hosts [is] his name; and thy Redeemer the Holy One of Israel; The God of the whole earth shall he be called.

    Context: OT basis for the marriage metaphor applied to Israel/the church: YHWH is husband (ba'al, H1166) to his people. Grounds the Rev 19:7-9 marriage of the Lamb in OT covenant theology.

    Notes: Hebrew: bo'alaik (H1166, your husband) oshaik (H6213, your maker) YHWH Tzvaot (H6635) shemo. LXX: ho kyrios ho poiōn se kyrios Sabaōth onoma autō.

    The Land Married: Covenant Restoration¶

    Thou shalt no more be termed Forsaken; neither shall thy land any more be termed Desolate: but thou shalt be called Hephzibah, and thy land Beulah: for the LORD delighteth in thee, and thy land shall be married. For [as] a young man marrieth a virgin, [so] shall thy sons marry thee: and [as] the bridegroom rejoiceth over the bride, [so] shall thy God rejoice over thee.

    Context: The marriage metaphor for covenant restoration between God and his people. The bridegroom-rejoicing-over-bride image directly prepares for Rev 19:7.

    Notes: Hebrew: Beulah (H1166, married) as new name. kechatan yasis al-kallah yasis alayik Elohayik. LXX: nymphiou (G3566) chairōn epi nymphēn (G3565) - direct LXX source for Rev 19:7 marriage imagery.

    One Flesh: The Great Mystery¶

    For we are members of his body, of his flesh, and of his bones. For this cause shall a man leave his father and mother, and shall be joined unto his wife, and they two shall be one flesh. This is a great mystery: but I speak concerning Christ and the church.

    Context: Paul's theological mediation making the Genesis 2 marriage (bookend 7) explicitly about Christ and the church. Eph 5:31 quotes Gen 2:24 LXX verbatim, then Eph 5:32 declares: 'This is a great mystery: but I speak concerning Christ and the church.'

    Notes: Greek: mysterion (G3466) mega (G3173) touto estin. Gen 2:24 quoted in Eph 5:31. Paul: alla egō legō eis Christon kai eis tēn ekklēsian. Interpretive key for the entire marriage bookend.

    Serpent's Beguiling of Eve: Warning to the Church¶

    But I fear, lest by any means, as the serpent beguiled Eve through his subtilty, so your minds should be corrupted from the simplicity that is in Christ.

    Context: Paul's direct reference to Gen 3:1-6: the ophis (G3789) that beguiled Eve is the type of satanic deception threatening the church. Connects Genesis serpent to ongoing SP115 dynamic.

    Notes: Greek: ho ophis (G3789) exēpatēsen (G1818, thoroughly deceived) Heuan (G2096, Eve) en tē panourgia autou (G3834, craftiness). phthartē ta noēmata (G3540) apo tēs haplotētos (G572) tēs eis Christon. ophis here identical to LXX Gen 3 serpent and Rev 12:9/20:2.

    The Everlasting Gospel to All Nations¶

    And I saw another angel fly in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel to preach unto them that dwell on the earth, and to every nation, and kindred, and tongue, and people,

    Context: The first angel of Rev 14 carries the everlasting gospel (euangelion aiōnion) to every nation. Connects to Matt 24:14 (gospel preached in all the world, then the end come).

    Notes: Greek: euangelion (G2098) aiōnion (G166, everlasting) euangelisai (G2097) tous kathēmenous epi tēs gēs. Four-fold universal formula: epi pan ethnos kai phylēn (G5443) kai glōssan (G1100) kai laon (G2992).
