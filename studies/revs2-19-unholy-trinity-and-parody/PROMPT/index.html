    Question¶

    Dragon/Beast/False Prophet as systematic parody of Father/Son/Spirit in Revelation 12-13. Document every verbal parallel between the parody system and its divine counterpart. Trace Greek vocabulary links. Does the parody structure operate at verse, scene, or macro-structural level?

    Prior Research Summary¶

    revs-35 (2026-03-07) registered 19 structural elements documenting the trinitarian parody system — SP086-088, TM116-118, TM181-187, VP168-170, AN081-082, IC068-069. All must be verified fresh against the Greek text and assigned new revs2 codes. revs2-05 documented the six positive threads (pantokrator, nikao, martyria, pistos kai alethinos, logos tou theou, kings-and-priests) that the parody inverts; revs2-03 established the thronos ratio (~12:1 divine vs. beast); revs2-04 linked the forehead inscription to a three-way system (God's seal, beast's mark, Babylon's name). revs2-12 traced the abyssos chain and three unclean spirits/three angels inversion; revs2-13 documented the three unclean spirits from the mouths of all three unholy trinity members as parody of three angels (14:6-12). revs2-15 recorded the diadema trajectory (12:3→13:1→19:12) and the beast temporal formula vs. divine formula comparison. Key Greek terms documented in references: sphazo (G4969), thronos (G2362), exousia (G1849), proskuneo (G4352), pneuma (G4151), charagma (G5480), sphragis (G4973), metopon (G3359), eikon (G1504), diadema (G1238). Web research confirmed broad scholarly consensus (Beale, Caird, Fee, Beasley-Murray, Mounce, Stefanovic, Paulien) on the parody framework; the Stefanovic/Gentry debate on 17:8 is the main open interpretive question (parodies divine eternality vs. Christ's resurrection).

    Primary Verses (38 — full analysis required)¶

    Unholy Trinity Core Text: The primary narrative of the three-member parody system - Rev 12:3-4 — Dragon's first appearance — seven heads, ten horns, seven diadems; tail sweeps third of stars; compare divine throne-room numerology - Rev 12:7-9 — War in heaven; dragon identified as 'that old serpent, called the Devil, and Satan' — explicit symbol decoding (Rev 12:9 uses participial naming-copula ὁ καλούμενος) - Rev 12:10-12 — Proclamation of salvation and kingdom; dragon cast down; Rev 12:11 three-thread convergence (nikao + logos + martyria) — the positive thread convergence that precedes the parody narrative - Rev 12:17 — Dragon makes war with remnant; textual variant estathē / estathēn at 12:17/13:1 junction — verify whether dragon stands actively summoning beasts - Rev 16:13 — Three unclean spirits (pneumata G4151 akatharton) from mouths of dragon, beast, false prophet — pneuma vocabulary explicitly linking all three unholy trinity members; parody of three angels (14:6-12) - Rev 19:20 — Beast and false prophet cast into lake of fire — the parody system's final judgment; false prophet identified by miracles and beast-mark enforcement - Rev 20:10 — Devil/dragon joins beast and false prophet in lake of fire — all three members of unholy trinity receive identical judgment; confirms the triad is a unified system

    Divine Throne-Room Counterparts (Parodied Originals): The divine positive that Rev 12-13 systematically inverts - Rev 1:4 — Divine formula 'who is, and who was, and who is to come' (ho ōn kai ho ēn kai ho erchomenos) — the formula the beast's 17:8 formula inverts; from seven Spirits before his throne - Rev 1:8 — Alpha and Omega / the Almighty (pantokrator G3841); divine formula repeated with 'ho erchomenos' — compare to beast's formula which replaces erchomenos with mellei - Rev 4:8 — Four living creatures: 'Holy, holy, holy, Lord God Almighty, which was, and is, and is to come' — third occurrence of divine formula (order differs: past-present-future vs. 1:4,8 present-past-future); pantokrator G3841 - Rev 4:11 — Doxology: 'Thou art worthy to receive glory and honour and power (dunamis G1411)' — dunamis in divine doxology; contrast with dragon giving his dunamis to beast (13:2) - Rev 5:5-6 — Lamb introduced: hōs esphagmenon (G4969, perfect passive participle, neuter) — THE verbal anchor for the parody; Lamb has seven horns and seven eyes which are the seven Spirits; divine seven vs. beast's 666 - Rev 5:12-13 — Doxology to Lamb: 'power (dunamis), riches, wisdom, strength, honour, glory, blessing'; all creatures worship Lamb and him on the throne — universal worship in divine context contrasts 13:3-4 - Rev 7:2-3 — Seal of living God; angels seal servants in their foreheads (metopon G3359) — the divine seal contrasted with beast's mark in metopon (13:16); sphragis/sphragizō vocabulary - Rev 7:9-10 — Great multitude before throne and Lamb; worship of God — contrast with beast-worship of all dwellers on earth (13:8)

    Beast Temporal Formula Chain: All occurrences of divine formula and beast's inverted formula - Rev 1:4 — Divine formula 'who is, and who was, and who is to come' (ho ōn kai ho ēn kai ho erchomenos) — the formula the beast's 17:8 formula inverts; from seven Spirits before his throne - Rev 1:8 — Alpha and Omega / the Almighty (pantokrator G3841); divine formula repeated with 'ho erchomenos' — compare to beast's formula which replaces erchomenos with mellei - Rev 4:8 — Four living creatures: 'Holy, holy, holy, Lord God Almighty, which was, and is, and is to come' — third occurrence of divine formula (order differs: past-present-future vs. 1:4,8 present-past-future); pantokrator G3841 - Rev 17:3 — Scarlet beast with seven heads and ten horns — same description as dragon (12:3) and sea beast (13:1); third appearance of unholy trinity head; full of names of blasphemy (G987) - Rev 17:8 — Beast temporal formula 'was, and is not, and yet is' (ēn kai ouk estin kai parestai/mellei anabainein) — systematic inversion of divine formula; Stefanovic/Gentry debate: parodies divine eternality (present tense IS) vs. parodies Christ's death-resurrection
