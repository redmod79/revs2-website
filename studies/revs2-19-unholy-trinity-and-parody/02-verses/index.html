    Verses¶

    Question¶

    Dragon/Beast/False Prophet as systematic parody of Father/Son/Spirit in Revelation 12-13. Document every verbal parallel. Trace Greek vocabulary links. Does the parody operate at verse, scene, or macro-structural level?

    Divine temporal formula: Is/Was/Is-to-Come¶

    John to the seven churches which are in Asia: Grace [be] unto you, and peace, from him which is, and which was, and which is to come; and from the seven Spirits which are before his throne;

    Context: Prologue salutation; divine name given in threefold temporal formula — present, past, future

    Notes: ὁ ὤν (V-PAP-NSM present) + ὁ ἦν (V-IAI-3S past) + ὁ ἐρχόμενος (V-PNP-NSM coming). Order: IS/WAS/IS-TO-COME. Beast formula in Rev 17:8 inverts: WAS/IS-NOT/WILL-COME-UP.

    Divine name with Pantokrator¶

    I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the ending, saith the Lord, which is, and which was, and which is to come, the Almighty.

    Context: God speaks directly; same threefold formula with addition of Pantokrator

    Notes: Same IS/WAS/IS-TO-COME as 1:4, plus ὁ Παντοκράτωρ (G3841 N-NSM). The Almighty formula brackets divine incomparability that Rev 13:4 parodies.

    Christ's resurrection self-declaration¶

    [I am] he that liveth, and was dead; and, behold, I am alive for evermore, Amen; and have the keys of hell and of death.

    Context: Christophany to John; Christ declares his death-to-life status

    Notes: ὁ Ζῶν (V-PAP-NSM living) + ἐγενόμην νεκρός (became dead) + ζῶν εἰμι (am living). The authentic resurrection formula — contrasted with Rev 13:3 beast parody (ἐσφαγμένην V-RPP-ASF + ἐθεραπεύθη V-API-3S).

    Heavenly throne-room: Trisagion and divine formula¶

    And the four beasts had each of them six wings about [him]; and [they were] full of eyes within: and they rest not day and night, saying, Holy, holy, holy, Lord God Almighty, which was, and is, and is to come.

    Context: Throne-room vision; four living creatures worship continuously

    Notes: ORDER DIFFERS from 1:4 and 1:8 — WAS/IS/IS-TO-COME (ὁ ἦν V-IAI-3S + ὁ ὤν V-PAP-NSM + ὁ ἐρχόμενος V-PNP-NSM). Same three elements, different arrangement. Confirms the formula is not rigidly sequential but identifies the eternal one.

    Slain Lamb standing — original sphazō perfect passive¶

    And I beheld, and, lo, in the midst of the throne and of the four beasts, and in the midst of the elders, stood a Lamb as it had been slain, having seven horns and seven eyes, which are the seven Spirits of God sent forth into all the earth.

    Context: Throne-room vision; the Lamb is introduced as the scroll's worthy recipient

    Notes: ἑστηκὸς (V-RAP-ASN perfect active — standing as a completed state) ὡς ἐσφαγμένον (V-RPP-ASN perfect passive — having been slain, neuter agreeing with arnion G721 neuter). The paradigm: death-state + living presence. Rev 13:3 mirrors with ἐσφαγμένην (V-RPP-ASF feminine, agreeing with kephalē G2776). Seven Spirits (πνεύματα G4151 N-NPN) = authentic spirit connection, contrasted with false-prophet's pneuma animation in 13:15.

    Heavenly throne-room worship: new song and universal praise¶

    And they sung a new song, saying, Thou art worthy to take the book, and to open the seals thereof: for thou wast slain, and hast redeemed us to God by thy blood out of every kindred, and tongue, and people, and nation; And hast made us unto our God kings and priests: and we shall reign on the earth. And I beheld, and I heard the voice of many angels round about the throne and the beasts and the elders: and the number of them was ten thousand times ten thousand, and thousands of thousands; Saying with a loud voice, Worthy is the Lamb that was slain to receive power, and riches, and wisdom, and strength, and honour, and glory, and blessing. And every creature which is in heaven, and on the earth, and under the earth, and such as are in the sea, and all that are in them, heard I saying, Blessing, and honour, and glory, and power, [be] unto him that sitteth upon the throne, and unto the Lamb for ever and ever. And the four beasts said, Amen. And the four [and] twenty elders fell down and worshipped him that liveth for ever and ever.

    Context: Throne-room vision; universal worship directed to the Lamb who was slain

    Notes: The authentic proskuneo (G4352) scene — contrasted with Rev 13:4,8,12 beast worship. Worship is addressed to the genuinely slain Lamb (ἐσφαγμένον G4969 V-RPP-ASN). The power/riches/wisdom/strength sevenfold doxology in 5:12 parallels the triple dunamis/thronos/exousia transfer in 13:2.

    Divine seal on the servants of God¶

    And I saw another angel ascending from the east, having the seal of the living God: and he cried with a loud voice to the four angels, to whom it was given to hurt the earth and the sea, Saying, Hurt not the earth, neither the sea, nor the trees, till we have sealed the servants of our God in their foreheads.

    Context: Interlude before the seventh seal; protective sealing of 144,000

    Notes: σφραγίς (G4973) — God's seal in foreheads (μέτωπα). Directly contrasted with χάραγμα (G5480) beast mark in foreheads (Rev 13:16, 14:9, 20:4). Both seal and mark are placed in the forehead — the parallel is deliberate and spatial.

    Divine formula: Is/Was — without Is-to-Come¶

    Saying, We give thee thanks, O Lord God Almighty, which art, and wast, and art to come; because thou hast taken to thee thy great power, and hast reigned.

    Context: Seventh trumpet; 24 elders worship as God's kingdom is declared

    Notes: KJV renders 'art to come' but the critical Greek text drops ὁ ἐρχόμενος — confirmed: ὁ ὤν (V-PAP-NSM) + ὁ ἦν (V-IAI-3S) only. The coming has arrived — the kingdom has been taken. This structural change marks narrative progress.

    Dragon's diadems — seven crowns on seven heads¶

    And there appeared another wonder in heaven; and behold a great red dragon, having seven heads and ten horns, and seven crowns upon his heads.

    Context: Heavenly sign — the great red dragon; war in heaven narrative

    Notes: hepta diademata (G1238 N-APN) on kephalas (G2776 N-APF — heads, feminine plural). Dragon: 7 heads, 10 horns, 7 diadems on heads. Contrast: Beast (Rev 13:1) 7 heads, 10 horns, 10 diadems on horns. Christ (Rev 19:12) many diadems (unquantified). Diadem trajectory tracks the escalation of the parody and its ultimate resolution.

    Dragon identified as ancient serpent, Devil, Satan¶

    And the great dragon was cast out, that old serpent, called the Devil, and Satan, which deceiveth the whole world: he was cast out into the earth, and his angels were cast out with him.

    Context: War in heaven; defeat of dragon and ejection to earth

    Notes: ὁ καλούμενος (V-PPP-NSM participial naming-copula) identifies dragon as Diabolos and Satanas. The dragon = serpent of Gen 3 = the deceiver of the world. This grounds the entire parody in the original cosmic adversary. OT parallels: Isa 27:1 (great sea serpent/leviathan, 0.352), Gen 3:14 (serpent, 0.316).

    Heavenly proclamation: accuser cast down, saints overcome¶

    And I heard a loud voice saying in heaven, Now is come salvation, and strength, and the kingdom of our God, and the power of his Christ: for the accuser of our brethren is cast down, which accused them before our God day and night. And they overcame him by the blood of the Lamb, and by the word of their testimony; and they loved not their lives unto the death.

    Context: War in heaven aftermath; heavenly voice announces victory

    Notes: 12:10 — ὁ κατήγωρ (accuser) = dragon's defining activity. Contrasts with Rev 13:7 where beast ἐδόθη (divine passive) authority to overcome (νικῆσαι G3528) saints. 12:11 — ἐνίκησαν (G3528 V-AAI-3P) through blood of Lamb + word of martyria (G3141). The saints' nikao uses same verb as beast's nikao in 13:7 and 11:7, but through opposite means: blood and testimony vs. conquest. NT parallels: Rev 17:14 (0.372 — Lamb and his overcome beast), 1 Pet 1:19 (0.370 — blood of lamb).

    Dragon makes war with remnant¶

    And the dragon was wroth with the woman, and went to make war with the remnant of her seed, which keep the commandments of God, and have the testimony of Jesus Christ.

    Context: End of woman-dragon narrative; dragon turns to persecute the remnant

    Notes: The remnant is defined by martyria Iēsou (G3141) — the testimony of Jesus. This same phrase recurs in Rev 19:10 where it equals the spirit of prophecy. Contrasted with Rev 13:7 where the beast overcomes the saints.

    Beast from the sea — ten diadems on horns¶

    And I stood upon the sand of the sea, and saw a beast rise up out of the sea, having seven heads and ten horns, and upon his horns ten crowns, and upon his heads the name of blasphemy.

    Context: New vision unit (kai eidon formula); first beast emerges from sea

    Notes: deka diademata (G1238 N-APN) on keratōn (G2768 N-GPN — horns). Progression: Dragon 7 diadems on 7 heads → Beast 10 diadems on 10 horns → Christ many on head. Name of blasphemy (βλασφημία G988) on heads = inverse of God's name on servants' foreheads (7:3, 14:1, 22:4). kai eidon = vision-introduction formula marking new visionary unit parallel to Rev 4-5 throne room.

    Dragon grants triple authority to the beast¶

    And the beast which I saw was like unto a leopard, and his feet were as [the feet] of a bear, and his mouth as the mouth of a lion: and the dragon gave him his power, and his seat, and great authority.

    Context: Description of first beast; dragon as source of authority

    Notes: ἔδωκεν (G1325 V-AAI-3S) — ACTIVE, dragon named subject. Triple transfer: δύναμιν (G1411 N-ASF power) + θρόνον (G2362 N-ASM throne) + ἐξουσίαν (G1849 N-ASF authority). This is the dragon's active conferral — parody of Rev 4-5 where Father's authority rests on the Lamb. OT parallels: Hosea 13:7 (leopard, 0.392), Dan 7:6 (composite beast, 0.365). Note: From v.5 onward, the same verb shifts to divine passive ἐδόθη — God's unnamed sovereignty bounds the dragon's active grant.

    Slain head healed — sphazō parody¶

    And I saw one of his heads as it were wounded to death; and his deadly wound was healed: and all the world wondered after the beast.

    Context: Description of first beast; pseudo-death-resurrection draws universal wonder

    Notes: ἐσφαγμένην (G4969 V-RPP-ASF — perfect passive, feminine agreeing with kephalē G2776 head, feminine).     Notes: 12:10: ἡ ἐξουσία τοῦ Χριστοῦ αὐτοῦ — authority of his Christ proclaimed precisely when the dragon falls. The dragon's ejection enables Christ's authority proclamation. 12:11: ἐνίκησαν (G3528 — overcame) by blood of Lamb + word of martyria (G3141). The three-part victory formula (blood + testimony + self-sacrifice) is the anti-structure to the beast's granted nikao (13:7). 12:12: dragon has 'short time' (ὀλίγον καιρόν) = the 42 months of Rev 13:5 — bounded divine permission.

    Dragon persecutes woman; serpent casts flood¶

    And when the dragon saw that he was cast unto the earth, he persecuted the woman which brought forth the man [child]. And to the woman were given two wings of a great eagle, that she might fly into the wilderness, into her place, where she is nourished for a time, and times, and half a time, from the face of the serpent. And the serpent cast out of his mouth water as a flood after the woman, that he might cause her to be carried away of the flood. And the earth helped the woman, and the earth opened her mouth, and swallowed up the flood which the dragon cast out of his mouth.

    Context: Dragon persecutes the remnant community after failing to destroy the man child

    Notes: ἐδόθησαν (divine passive — were given) two eagle wings. God's protection is coded in divine passives even here. The dragon/serpent casts flood from his mouth (στόμα G4750) — contrasted with the beast's στόμα speaking blasphemy (13:5-6) and the false prophet's στόμα from which unclean spirits proceed (16:13). Dragon's mouth = persecution flood; beast's mouth = blasphemy; false prophet's mouth = deceiving spirits.

    Saints overcome by blood of Lamb and word of testimony¶

    And they overcame him by the blood of the Lamb, and by the word of their testimony; and they loved not their lives unto the death.

    Context: Heavenly proclamation; the saints' victory formula

    Notes: ἐνίκησαν (G3528 V-AAI-3P) αὐτόν — they overcame him. Three-part formula: (1) blood of the Lamb, (2) word of their testimony (λόγος + martyria G3141), (3) love of life not unto death. This is the saints' nikao that the beast's nikao (13:7) temporarily interrupts but cannot permanently defeat. NT parallels: Rev 17:14 (0.372 — Lamb and called/chosen overcome beast), 1 Pet 1:19 (0.370 — blood of lamb).

    Heavenly doxology: worthy to receive glory, honour, power¶

    Thou art worthy, O Lord, to receive glory and honour and power: for thou hast created all things, and for thy pleasure they are and were created.

    Context: Throne-room vision; 24 elders cast crowns and declare God's worthiness

    Notes: ἄξιος (worthy) + δόξαν + τιμήν + δύναμιν (G1411 N-ASF) — the creation doxology directed to the Creator. Rev 13:4 beast worship says 'Who is able to make war with him?' (δύναμιν implied). The same noun (dunamis G1411) appears in 4:11 (God's creation power), 13:2 (dragon grants beast his power), and 5:12 (Lamb receives power/riches/wisdom). The doxology vocabulary circulates through the parody structure.

    Scroll sealed with seven seals; no one worthy to open it¶

    And I saw in the right hand of him that sat on the throne a book written within and on the backside, sealed with seven seals. And I saw a strong angel proclaiming with a loud voice, Who is worthy to open the book, and to loose the seals thereof? And no man in heaven, nor in earth, neither under the earth, was able to open the book, neither to look thereon. And I wept much, because no man was found worthy to open and to read the book, neither to look thereon.

    Context: Throne-room; the sealed scroll creates the crisis of worthiness that only the Lamb resolves

    Notes: σφραγίς (G4973) seals — seven of them. The crisis of worthiness (ἄξιος) is resolved only by the slain Lamb (5:5-6). The beast in Rev 13:4 demands a different worthiness question: 'Who is able (δύναται) to make war?' — replacing moral/redemptive worthiness with military capability. The Lamb's worthiness is established by his slaying (5:9); the beast's 'worthiness' is claimed through terror.

    Lamb introduced as Lion of Judah, Root of David; slain but standing¶

    And one of the elders saith unto me, Weep not: behold, the Lion of the tribe of Juda, the Root of David, hath prevailed to open the book, and to loose the seven seals thereof. And I beheld, and, lo, in the midst of the throne and of the four beasts, and in the midst of the elders, stood a Lamb as it had been slain, having seven horns and seven eyes, which are the seven Spirits of God sent forth into all the earth.

    Context: The Lamb is introduced as the only worthy one; his identity is paradoxical — Lion announced, Lamb seen

    Notes: ἐνίκησεν (G3528 — he overcame/prevailed) to open the scroll. This is the foundational Lamb nikao from which all subsequent overcomer promises derive. ἑστηκὸς (V-RAP-ASN perfect — standing as completed state) ὡς ἐσφαγμένον (V-RPP-ASN perfect passive — having been slain). The double perfect: both the standing and the slaying are stative — permanent conditions, not past events only. Rev 13:11: false prophet has two horns homoios (like) arnion — the comparison noun is from this exact introduction.

    Sevenfold doxology to the Lamb and to the one on the throne¶

    Saying with a loud voice, Worthy is the Lamb that was slain to receive power, and riches, and wisdom, and strength, and honour, and glory, and blessing. And every creature which is in heaven, and on the earth, and under the earth, and such as are in the sea, and all that are in them, heard I saying, Blessing, and honour, and glory, and power, [be] unto him that sitteth upon the throne, and unto the Lamb for ever and ever.

    Context: Universal heavenly doxology; seven attributes given to the Lamb

    Notes: δύναμιν (G1411) + πλοῦτον + σοφίαν + ἰσχύν + τιμήν + δόξαν + εὐλογίαν — sevenfold doxology to the Lamb. Compare Rev 13:2: dragon gives beast δύναμιν + θρόνον + ἐξουσίαν — a threefold (not sevenfold) transfer of derived (not inherent) attributes. The Lamb receives seven authentic attributes by universal acclamation; the beast receives three derived attributes from the defeated dragon.

    Great multitude before throne and Lamb in white robes¶

    After this I beheld, and, lo, a great multitude, which no man could number, of all nations, and kindreds, and people, and tongues, stood before the throne, and before the Lamb, clothed with white robes, and palms in their hands; And cried with a loud voice, saying, Salvation to our God which sitteth upon the throne, and unto the Lamb.

    Context: Interlude between sixth and seventh seals; redeemed multitude in heavenly worship

    Notes: proskuneo context — universal worship (πᾶσα φυλή/γλώσσα/λαός) directed to God on throne and the Lamb. Directly contrasted with Rev 13:7-8 where the beast is given authority over 'every kindred, and tongue, and nation' and all earth worships him. The same universal scope vocabulary (nations/kindreds/tongues/peoples) marks both scenes; the direction of worship is inverted.

    Ear formula and patience of the saints¶

    If any man have an ear, let him hear. He that leadeth into captivity shall go into captivity: he that killeth with the sword must be killed with the sword. Here is the patience and the faith of the saints.

    Context: Between the beast's war on saints (13:7-8) and the false prophet introduction (13:11); structural pivot

    Notes: Εἴ τις ἔχει οὖς ἀκουσάτω — the same ear formula used in the seven letters (2:7, 2:11, etc.) appears here as the structural marker separating the beast's conquest from the false prophet section. ὑπομονή (patience/endurance) and πίστις (faith) of the saints — the two virtues that sustain the saints under the beast's 42-month mandate. This mirrors the letter-pattern: overcomer promised + ear formula. Here the ear formula appears AFTER the persecution, not the promise.

    Scarlet beast with seven heads and ten horns — beast as throne¶

    So he carried me away in the spirit into the wilderness: and I saw a woman sit upon a scarlet coloured beast, full of names of blasphemy, having seven heads and ten horns.

    Context: Vision of the great harlot riding the scarlet beast; angel interprets both

    Notes: The scarlet beast reuses the full description of Rev 13:1 (seven heads, ten horns, blasphemy names) but now in a different role — as throne for the harlot. The beast's θρόνον (received from dragon, 13:2) becomes itself a throne for another. The blasphemy names now fill the beast rather than being written on one head. Rev 17:8 will provide the beast's temporal formula. NT parallels to Rev 5:6: Rev 17:3 scores 0.518 — highest NT parallel for the Lamb scene (horn/seven imagery connecting the two).

    Beast and false prophet cast into lake of fire¶

    And the beast was taken, and with him the false prophet that wrought miracles before him, with which he deceived them that had received the mark of the beast, and them that worshipped his image. These both were cast alive into a lake of fire burning with brimstone.

    Context: Final judgment; the first two members of the unholy trinity are destroyed together

    Notes: Beast + false prophet — the two of Rev 13 — are taken together. The false prophet is identified by: (1) miracles before the beast, (2) deception of mark-receivers, (3) deception of image-worshippers. All three identifying marks reference Rev 13:13-15. ἐβλήθησαν ζῶντες (were cast alive — note: they are 'alive' when cast in, parodying the Lamb who was dead and is alive). Rev 20:10 will complete the unholy trinity's fate by adding the dragon/devil.

    Devil joins beast and false prophet in lake of fire¶

    And the devil that deceived them was cast into the lake of fire and brimstone, where the beast and the false prophet [are], and shall be tormented day and night for ever and ever.

    Context: After Satan's final release and defeat; dragon joins the other two in final destruction

    Notes: The dragon/devil is cast into the lake of fire where ὁ θηρίον and ὁ ψευδοπροφήτης already are — completing the unholy trinity's shared fate. 'Tormented day and night for ever and ever' mirrors the torment of beast-worshippers in Rev 14:11 ('they have no rest day nor night'). The unholy trinity shares its worshippers' eternal consequence. This scene closes the parody arc: the imitation trinity is destroyed together while the authentic Trinity reigns.

    Father gives all judgment to Son; authority to execute judgment¶

    For the Father judgeth no man, but hath committed all judgment unto the Son: [...] And hath given him authority to execute judgment also, because he is the Son of man.

    Context: Jesus' discourse on his authority; Johannine theology of delegated divine authority

    Notes: ἐδόθη αὐτῷ ἐξουσία (divine passive — authority given to him, v.27). The pattern: Father → Son via delegated authority (ἐξουσία G1849). Compare Rev 13:2: dragon → beast via ἔδωκεν (active). In John 5, the authority transfers from the greater to the lesser via divine passive; in Rev 13:2, it transfers from the dragon (via active verb) but is still bounded by the divine passives. The Son's authority is genuine (all judgment); the beast's authority is partial (every tribe, earth only, 42 months).

    Father gives Son power over all flesh¶

    As thou hast given him power over all flesh, that he should give eternal life to as many as thou hast given him.

    Context: High Priestly Prayer; Christ's universal authority over humanity

    Notes: ἔδωκας αὐτῷ ἐξουσίαν πάσης σαρκός — the Father's grant of universal authority over all flesh. Compare Rev 13:7: beast given ἐξουσία ἐπὶ πᾶσαν φυλήν (over every tribe). Both claim universal authority. Christ's use of that authority: to give eternal life. Beast's use: to wage war and demand worship. The scope overlaps; the purpose is diametrically opposed.

    Grieve not the Holy Spirit by whom ye are sealed¶

    And grieve not the holy Spirit of God, whereby ye are sealed unto the day of redemption.

    Context: Paul to Ephesians; the Spirit as the seal of redemption

    Notes: ἐσφραγίσθητε (V-API-2P Aor Pass — you were sealed, divine passive) ἐν ᾧ (by whom = the Spirit). The Spirit is the sealing agent of redemption. Contrasted with Rev 13:16: false prophet causes all to receive χάραγμα (charagma — beast's mark). Authentic sealing: by the Spirit to the day of redemption; false marking: by the false prophet to the beast. The agent (Spirit vs. false prophet) and the purpose (redemption vs. commerce/allegiance) are the inversions.

    Pergamos — where Satan's throne is¶

    I know thy works, and where thou dwellest, [even] where Satan's seat [is]: and thou holdest fast my name, and hast not denied my faith, even in those days wherein Antipas [was] my faithful martyr, who was slain among you, where Satan dwelleth.

    Context: Letter to Pergamos; the church endures where Satan's throne is located

    Notes: ὁ θρόνος τοῦ Σατανᾶ — Satan's throne at Pergamos. The dragon possesses a throne (θρόνος G2362) which he gives to the beast (Rev 13:2). The letter to Pergamos identifies an earthly locus of that throne. The church at Pergamos holds fast Christ's name (ὄνομα) even where Satan's throne is — inverting the beast's pattern where his name/mark replaces God's name on the beast-worshippers.

    Fifth bowl poured on beast's throne — kingdom darkened¶

    And the fifth angel poured out his vial upon the seat of the beast; and his kingdom was full of darkness; and they gnawed their tongues for pain,

    Context: Fifth bowl judgment; directed specifically at the beast's throne

    Notes: τὸν θρόνον τοῦ θηρίου (G2362 — the throne of the beast) targeted by divine judgment. The beast's received throne (from dragon, 13:2) is now the target of the bowl. The darkening (σκότος) contrasts with Rev 4:5's lightnings and thunders from God's throne (authentic throne as source of light and power). The beast's throne produces only darkness when judged.

    Locust army cannot harm those with God's seal in forehead¶

    And it was commanded them that they should not hurt the grass of the earth, neither any green thing, neither any tree; but only those men which have not the seal of God in their foreheads.

    Context: Fifth trumpet; locust-scorpions exempted from harming the sealed

    Notes: σφραγίς (G4973) of God on μέτωπα (G3359 — foreheads). The divine seal in foreheads is protective — the sealed are exempt from the locust judgment. This is the functional distinction between the divine seal and the beast's mark: God's seal protects; the beast's mark (in the same location, 13:16) grants commercial access but leads to judgment (14:9-11). The forehead (metopon G3359) is the consistent spatial marker for both divine ownership and beast allegiance.

    Four beasts from the sea — OT source for Rev 13 chimeric beast¶

    And four great beasts came up from the sea, diverse one from another. The first [was] like a lion, and had eagle's wings: I beheld till the wings thereof were plucked, and it was lifted up from the earth, and made stand upon the feet as a man, and a man's heart was given to it. And behold another beast, a second, like to a bear, and it raised up itself on one side, and [it had] three ribs in the mouth of it between the teeth of it: and they said thus unto it, Arise, devour much flesh. After this I beheld, and lo another, like a leopard, which had upon the back of it four wings of a fowl; the beast had also four heads; and dominion was given to it. After this I saw in the night visions, and behold a fourth beast, dreadful and terrible, and strong exceedingly; and it had great iron teeth: it devoured and brake in pieces, and stamped the residue with the feet of it: and it [was] diverse from all the beasts that [were] before it; and it had ten horns.

    Context: Daniel's four-beast vision; four successive world empires depicted as composite creatures

    Notes: Rev 13:2's chimeric beast (leopard + bear + lion) is the four Danielic beasts combined into one. Daniel's beasts are sequential (four kingdoms); Rev 13's beast synthesizes all their characteristics into one end-time entity. 'Dominion was given to it' (7:6) = divine passive — the same construction that governs the beast's authority in Rev 13:5,7. The ten horns of Daniel's fourth beast (7:7) reappear on the Rev 13 beast (13:1). OT genesis of the sea-beast imagery confirmed.

    Elijah — fire from the LORD falls on Mount Carmel¶

    Then the fire of the LORD fell, and consumed the burnt sacrifice, and the wood, and the stones, and the dust, and licked up the water that [was] in the trench.

    Context: Contest on Mount Carmel; YHWH's fire authenticates the true prophet against 450 Baal prophets

    Notes: The authentic sign: fire falling from heaven from YHWH. Rev 13:13 parodies: the false prophet performs σημεῖα and 'maketh fire come down from heaven on the earth in the sight of men.' Same description, different source. Elijah's fire vindicates the true prophet (1 Kgs 18:36-40); the false prophet's fire deceives. Acts 2:3 provides a NT parallel: the Pentecostal fire from heaven. Both are parodied by Rev 13:13.

    Tav mark on foreheads of mourners — OT forehead-mark prototype¶

    And the LORD said unto him, Go through the midst of the city, through the midst of Jerusalem, and set a mark upon the foreheads of the men that sigh and that cry for all the abominations that be done in the midst thereof.

    Context: Ezekiel's vision of Jerusalem's judgment; the faithful marked for protection before slaughter begins

    Notes: תָּו (tav — the last letter of the Hebrew alphabet, literally meaning 'mark') placed on foreheads (מְצָח — metsach) of the faithful mourners as protective identification before judgment. This is the earliest biblical forehead-mark for divine protection — the structural prototype for both Rev 7:3 (seal of God on foreheads) and 22:4 (name in foreheads). The beast's charagma in foreheads (Rev 13:16) inverts the Ezekiel tav: both use the forehead as the site of identification, but one marks God's faithful for protection while the other marks beast-allegiance.
