    Question¶

    How does sanctuary/temple typology run through the entire book of Revelation? Trace every sanctuary element, identify its OT compartment (Exod 25-40), and determine whether the progression outer court → Holy Place → Most Holy Place correlates with the judgment sequences. How does DOA typology (Lev 16) function as a structural marker?

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Prior revs2 studies (03, 09, 10, 11, 12, 13) and dedicated studies sanc-28 and sanc-29 have established the core framework: naos (G3485) 16x in Revelation, hieron (G2411) 0x — a deliberate lexical choice. Compartmental progression documented: OC (6:9 thysiastērion unqualified) → HP (8:3 thysiastēriou tou chrysou + golden altar + incense) → MHP (11:19 kibotos, 15:5-8 tabernacle of testimony). Five DOA indicators at 15:5-8 (triple-genitive 15:5; linen garments 15:6; smoke 15:8; access exclusion 15:8; 'until' clause 15:8). Vessel transformation: phiale with prayers (5:8) → phiale with wrath (15:7). Altar vindication arc: 6:10 → 16:7 → 19:2. Theophany escalation 3→4→5→5+ at 4:5/8:5/11:19/16:18. Feast calendar: Trumpets→DOA→Tabernacles. Showbread table gap confirmed real. New Jerusalem cube = MHP typology (21:16, 22; 1 Kgs 6:20). Exo 25:8 → Rev 21:3 → 21:22 theological spine. PRIMARY OPEN QUESTION: Does naos carry compartmental specificity (Bohr: naos = MHP exclusively) or does naos mean 'inner sanctuary' generally with compartmental progression emerging from context and associated elements?

    Focus Areas¶

    1. The naos/hieron distinction and what naos means in Revelation: The naos/hieron distinction governs the entire study. READ G3485-naos.md and G2411-hieron.md from the library — these canonical entries will have occurrence counts, translation distributions, and the lexical argument. The key claim to verify: John used hieron 11x in his Gospel (referring to Herod's temple complex where crowds gather, money-changers work, etc.) but uses it zero times in Revelation. Instead, he uses naos exclusively, which in Greek (Josephus, Philo, Trench) refers to the inner sanctuary building containing the HP and MHP. This means every naos reference in Revelation points to the inner shrine — never the courts or outer areas.
