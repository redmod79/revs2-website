    Verses¶

    Question¶

    What are the primary structural markers in the book of Revelation? Trace the four "in the Spirit" (en pneumati) markers at Rev 1:10, 4:2, 17:3, and 21:10 — how do they divide the book into four major visions? Trace the "and I saw" (kai eidon) transitions — where do they occur, what do they introduce, and how do they subdivide the visions into scenes? Trace the "after these things" (meta tauta) indicators. How do these three marker systems work together to create Revelation's structural skeleton? For each marker, document the Greek form, every occurrence, and what structural boundary it signals. Note where the markers' structural signals converge and where they create ambiguity.

    Prologue: Book's self-identification and transmission chain¶

    The Revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave unto him, to shew unto his servants things which must shortly come to pass; and he sent and signified [it] by his angel unto his servant John: Who bare record of the word of God, and of the testimony of Jesus Christ, and of all things that he saw. Blessed [is] he that readeth, and they that hear the words of this prophecy, and keep those things which are written therein: for the time [is] at hand.

    Context: Opening of the book. Establishes the transmission chain: God → Christ → angel → John → servants. Self-identifies as 'prophecy' and 'things which must shortly come to pass.' Part of the Prologue-Epilogue inclusio.

    Notes: Verbal parallels with Rev 22:6 (angel, servants, things which must shortly be done). The word 'signified' (esēmanen, G4591 from sēmaion) connects to the semeion markers at 12:1, 12:3, 15:1.

    Prologue greeting: seven churches, seven Spirits, Alpha and Omega¶

    John to the seven churches which are in Asia: Grace [be] unto you, and peace, from him which is, and which was, and which is to come; and from the seven Spirits which are before his throne; And from Jesus Christ, [who is] the faithful witness, [and] the first begotten of the dead, and the prince of the kings of the earth. Unto him that loved us, and washed us from our sins in his own blood, And hath made us kings and priests unto God and his Father; to him [be] glory and dominion for ever and ever. Amen. Behold, he cometh with clouds; and every eye shall see him, and they [also] which pierced him: and all kindreds of the earth shall wail because of him. Even so, Amen. I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the ending, saith the Lord, which is, and which was, and which is to come, the Almighty.

    Context: Prologue greeting addressed to the seven churches. Contains 'Alpha and Omega' (inclusio with 22:13), 'seven Spirits' (structural marker: Spirits before the throne), 'Behold, he cometh' (parallels 22:7, 12, 20).

    Notes: Multiple verbal elements shared with the Epilogue (22:6-21): Alpha/Omega, seven churches, testimony, faithful witness, coming quickly.

    Space/time referent: Patmos setting, trumpet voice¶

    I John, who also am your brother, and companion in tribulation, and in the kingdom and patience of Jesus Christ, was in the isle that is called Patmos, for the word of God, and for the testimony of Jesus Christ. I was in the Spirit on the Lord's day, and heard behind me a great voice, as of a trumpet, Saying, I am Alpha and Omega, the first and the last: and, What thou seest, write in a book, and send [it] unto the seven churches which are in Asia; unto Ephesus, and unto Smyrna, and unto Pergamos, and unto Thyatira, and unto Sardis, and unto Philadelphia, and unto Laodicea.

    Context: First en pneumati marker. Establishes the single visionary episode from Patmos. The trumpet voice recurs at 4:1. Korner's 'single space/time referent' — the en pneumati at 1:10 inaugurates a vision continuing through 22:5.

    Notes: egenomen en Pneumati (V-2ADI-1S of ginomai G1096 + PREP G1722 + N-DSN of pneuma G4151). Anarthrous pneumati. Trumpet voice (salpinx G4536) parallels 4:1.

    First vision content: seven lampstands, Son of Man description¶

    And I turned to see the voice that spake with me. And being turned, I saw seven golden candlesticks; And in the midst of the seven candlesticks [one] like unto the Son of man, clothed with a garment down to the foot, and girt about the paps with a golden girdle. His head and [his] hairs [were] white like wool, as white as snow; and his eyes [were] as a flame of fire; And his feet like unto fine brass, as if they burned in a furnace; and his voice as the sound of many waters. And he had in his right hand seven stars: and out of his mouth went a sharp twoedged sword: and his countenance [was] as the sun shineth in his strength. And when I saw him, I fell at his feet as dead. And he laid his right hand upon me, saying unto me, Fear not; I am the first and the last: [I am] he that liveth, and was dead; and, behold, I am alive for evermore, Amen; and have the keys of hell and of death. Write the things which thou hast seen, and the things which are, and the things which shall be hereafter; The mystery of the seven stars which thou sawest in my right hand, and the seven golden candlesticks. The seven stars are the angels of the seven churches: and the seven candlesticks which thou sawest are the seven churches.

    Context: Vision 1 content begins. Sanctuary imagery: seven golden lampstands (menorah). Rev 1:17 contains 'when I saw him' (kai hote eidon auton) — eidon with intervening words, classified as concurrent description by Korner. Rev 1:19 contains meta tauta in temporal framework: past/present/future division.

    Notes: Rev 1:19 Greek: graphon oun ha eides kai ha eisin kai ha mellei genesthai meta tauta. The meta tauta here is part of the temporal framework (what shall be hereafter), not a structural transition formula. Paulien identifies this as a sanctuary introduction scene (lampstands = Holy Place furniture).

    Spirit saith to the churches formula (first of seven)¶

    He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith unto the churches; To him that overcometh will I give to eat of the tree of life, which is in the midst of the paradise of God.

    Context: First occurrence of the 'Spirit saith unto the churches' formula. Uses articular to Pneuma (the Spirit, G4151 with definite article G3588 T-NSN) — contrasting with the anarthrous en Pneumati of the visionary-state formula. Occurs identically at 2:11, 2:17, 2:29, 3:6, 3:13, 3:22.

    • τὸ (G3588) — T-NSN — Nom Sg N
    • Πνεῦμα (G4151) — N-NSN — Nom Sg N
    • λέγει (G3004) — V-PAI-3S — Pres Act Ind Sg thirdPers
    • ἐκκλησίαις (G1577) — N-DPF — Dat Pl F

    Notes: Greek: to Pneuma legei tais ekklesiais. The Spirit (articular, nominative) is the subject speaking. This is functionally different from en Pneumati (anarthrous, dative) where pneuma describes John's visionary state. Seven occurrences confined to Vision 1.

    Spirit saith to the churches formula (remaining six occurrences)¶

    He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith unto the churches; He that overcometh shall not be hurt of the second death. [2:11] | He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith unto the churches; To him that overcometh will I give to eat of the hidden manna... [2:17] | He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith unto the churches. [2:29] | He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith unto the churches. [3:6] | He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith unto the churches. [3:13] | He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith unto the churches. [3:22]

    Context: All seven occurrences are verbatim identical in Greek formula. All within Vision 1 (Rev 1:10-3:22). After 3:22, this formula never appears again — suggesting Vision 1 has a unique structural character (direct communication to the churches).

    Notes: TM004 from revs-01: strict confinement to Vision 1. The formula creates internal cadence within the seven churches section. ekklesia (G1577) confined to chs. 1-3 and 22:16.

    Major structural boundary: meta tauta eidon + en pneumati convergence¶

    After this I looked, and, behold, a door [was] opened in heaven: and the first voice which I heard [was] as it were of a trumpet talking with me; which said, Come up hither, and I will shew thee things which must be hereafter.     Meta tauta: post-millennium release — non-formulaic usage¶

    Context: Millennium binding scene. Meta tauta in the phrase 'after that he must be loosed' — but this is not meta tauta eidon. It refers to a future event (post-millennium release), not a vision transition. Larsen includes it in his meta tauta count; most scholars distinguish it as non-formulaic.

    • meta (G3326) — PREP — tauta houtos G3778 [D-APN] Acc Pl N

    Notes: Disputed meta tauta occurrence. The Greek meta tauta dei lythenai auton — 'after these things it is necessary for him to be loosed.' Distinguishing formulaic from non-formulaic meta tauta is critical for structural analysis.

    Sinai theophany source: thunders, lightnings, voice, earthquake¶

    And it came to pass on the third day in the morning, that there were thunders and lightnings, and a thick cloud upon the mount, and the voice of the trumpet exceeding loud; so that all the people that [was] in the camp trembled. And Moses brought forth the people out of the camp to meet with God; and they stood at the nether part of the mount. And mount Sinai was altogether on a smoke, because the LORD descended upon it in fire: and the smoke thereof ascended as the smoke of a furnace, and the whole mount quaked greatly.

    Context: Sinai theophany — the OT source text for Revelation's theophany formula. Elements present: thunders (qolot), lightnings (beraqim), voice of trumpet, thick cloud, smoke, fire, earthquake (quaked greatly). These same elements recur at Rev 4:5, 8:5, 11:19, 16:18.

    Notes: Primary OT source for the Sinai-theophany formula in Revelation. The vocabulary maps: thunders -> brontai (G1027), lightnings -> astrapai (G796), voice -> phonai (G5456), earthquake -> seismos (G4578). Revelation adds hail (chalaza G5464) as a fifth element not present in the Exodus account.

    Context: The people's experience of the Sinai theophany. They 'saw' the thunderings and lightnings — the same vocabulary as the Revelation theophany formula. The people's fearful withdrawal contrasts with John's Spirit-transported approach.

    Notes: Supporting OT text for the Sinai-theophany formula. The people 'saw' (ra'ah) the theophanic manifestation. In Revelation, John also 'sees' (horao/eidon) the theophany at each occurrence. The people stood afar off; John is brought near by the Spirit.

    Notes: Ezekiel Spirit-transport parallel. Double Spirit reference: 'the spirit took me up' and 'by the Spirit of God' — the ruach functions both as transport agent and as the visionary medium. Parallels the en pneumati formula in Revelation where pneuma serves both functions.
