    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    What are the primary structural markers in the book of Revelation? Trace the four "in the Spirit" (en pneumati) markers at Rev 1:10, 4:2, 17:3, and 21:10 — how do they divide the book into four major visions? Trace the "and I saw" (kai eidon) transitions — where do they occur, what do they introduce, and how do they subdivide the visions into scenes? Trace the "after these things" (meta tauta) indicators. How do these three marker systems work together to create Revelation's structural skeleton? For each marker, document the Greek form, every occurrence, and what structural boundary it signals. Note where the markers' structural signals converge and where they create ambiguity.

    G4151 — pneuma¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4151-pneuma.md

    Occurrences: 385

    Neuter noun meaning spirit, breath, wind. 385 NT occurrences across all books. In Revelation (25 occurrences, 6.2 per 100 verses): functions both as the visionary-state domain (en pneumati, anarthrous dative) and as the speaking Holy Spirit (to pneuma, articular nominative). The en pneumati formula at Rev 1:10, 4:2, 17:3, 21:10 uses pneuma in the dative singular neuter WITHOUT the definite article — marking the sphere/means of John's visionary experience. The 'Spirit saith to the churches' formula uses TO pneuma WITH the article — marking the Holy Spirit as personal speaker.

    Semantic categories: Holy Spirit / Spirit of God, visionary state/sphere, unclean/evil spirits, human spirit

    Key verses: Rev 1:10, Rev 2:7, Rev 4:2, Rev 4:5, Rev 14:13, Rev 17:3, Rev 21:10

    Relevance: Core term in the en pneumati structural formula. The anarthrous/articular distinction (en pneumati vs to pneuma) separates two structural functions within Vision 1.

    G3708 — horao¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3708-horao.md

    Occurrences: 415

    Verb meaning to see, perceive, experience. 415 NT occurrences. The 2nd aorist active indicative 1st person singular form eidon (V-2AAI-1S) is the key structural marker in Revelation. kai eidon ('and I saw') occurs approximately 35 times as a scene-opening formula. The suppletive aorist stem (eid-) replaces the present stem (hora-); the form is morphologically identical whether catalogued under G3708 (horao) or G1492 (oida/eido). Only the 2nd aorist active indicative 1st singular qualifies as a technical structural marker; other forms (blepo G991, theoreo G2334) serve descriptive functions.

    Semantic categories: physical sight, visionary perception, experiential knowledge, divine appearing (passive)

    Key verses: Rev 5:1, Rev 6:1, Rev 13:1, Rev 19:11, Rev 20:1, Rev 21:1

    Relevance: Root verb for the kai eidon structural marker system — the most frequent structural device in Revelation.

    G1096 — ginomai¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1096-ginomai.md

    Occurrences: 702

    Verb meaning to become, come into being, happen. 631 NT occurrences (668 parsed forms). In the en pneumati formula of Visions 1-2: egenomen en Pneumati (V-2ADI-1S, aorist middle deponent indicative 1st singular). John 'became in Spirit' — he enters the visionary state as his own experience. This contrasts with Visions 3-4 where apophero (G667) replaces ginomai — the angel carries John rather than John entering the state himself.

    Semantic categories: becoming/change of state, happening/occurrence, existence, narrative transition marker

    Key verses: Rev 1:10, Rev 4:2, Rev 8:5, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:18

    Relevance: Verb in Visions 1-2 en pneumati formula. The shift from ginomai to apophero at Vision 3 marks a structural pair division.

    G667 — apophero¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G667-apopherō.md

    Occurrences: 7

    Verb meaning to bear off, carry away. Only 7 NT occurrences (3 in Revelation: 17:3, 21:10, and 17:3's textual context). The formula apenengken me en Pneumati (V-AAI-3S, aorist active indicative 3rd singular of apophero) occurs only at Rev 17:3 and 21:10 — a unique verbal pair in the entire NT (TM005). The shift from ginomai (John enters the state) to apophero (angel carries John) distinguishes Visions 1-2 from Visions 3-4.

    Semantic categories: visionary/spiritual relocation, judicial/administrative transport, post-mortem conveyance

    Key verses: Rev 17:3, Rev 21:10, Mark 15:1, Luke 16:22

    Relevance: Verb in Visions 3-4 en pneumati formula. The apophero + en Pneumati combination is unique to Rev 17:3 and 21:10.

    G3326 — meta¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3326-metá.md

    Occurrences: 472

    Preposition meaning 'with' (genitive case, accompaniment) or 'after' (accusative case, temporal succession). 472 NT occurrences. In Revelation (49 occurrences, 12.1 per 100 verses — highest per-verse density of any book): meta + accusative tauta = 'after these things,' functioning as a macro-level structural transition marker when followed by eidon. The meta tauta eidon formula creates major vision-cycle boundaries. The same preposition with genitive case ('with') appears frequently in non-structural contexts.

    Semantic categories: accompaniment (with genitive), temporal succession (with accusative), spatial relationship

    Key verses: Rev 4:1, Rev 7:1, Rev 7:9, Rev 15:5, Rev 18:1, Rev 19:1

    Relevance: Preposition in the meta tauta structural formula. The accusative case use ('after') creates temporal-structural markers.

    G5023 — tauta¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5023-tauta.md

    Occurrences: 243

    Nominative or accusative neuter plural demonstrative pronoun meaning 'these things.' 243 NT occurrences. In Revelation (16 occurrences): functions as the pronoun in meta tauta ('after these things') structural transitions. Distinguished from the singular touto (G5124) which appears at Rev 7:1 (meta touto). The plural tauta references a series of preceding events; the singular touto references a single preceding event.

    Semantic categories: discourse referential, narrative transition marker, eschatological referent

    Key verses: Rev 4:1, Rev 7:9, Rev 15:5, Rev 18:1, Rev 19:1, Rev 20:3

    Relevance: Demonstrative pronoun in meta tauta formula. The plural/singular distinction (tauta vs touto at 7:1) is a grammatical marker within the structural system.

    G2532 — kai¶

    Occurrences: 9101

    Conjunction meaning 'and,' 'also,' 'even.' 9101 NT occurrences — the most frequent word in the NT. In the kai eidon formula, kai functions as a COPULATIVE conjunction linking scenes, NOT as a temporal adverb ('then'). The kai is structurally neutral on chronological sequence — it connects what John saw next in his visionary experience, not necessarily what happens next historically. This is confirmed by Rev 13:11 where kai eidon introduces a contemporaneous beast.

    Semantic categories: copulative conjunction, additive, emphatic

    Key verses: Rev 5:1, Rev 6:1, Rev 13:1, Rev 19:11

    Relevance: Conjunction in kai eidon. The copulative (not temporal) function is critical to the chronological neutrality of the kai eidon system.

    G2400 — idou¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2400-idou.md

    Occurrences: 213

    Particle/interjection meaning 'behold!, look!, see!' 213 NT occurrences. In Revelation (30 occurrences, 7.4 per 100 verses — highest density): functions as an attention-demand marker that elevates structural significance when combined with kai eidon (kai eidon kai idou double formula). The double formula occurs at Rev 4:1; 5:6; 6:2; 6:5; 6:8; 7:9; 14:1; 14:14; 19:11 — marking the most structurally significant scene transitions.

    Semantic categories: narrative introduction, divine announcement, prophetic declaration, exclamation

    Key verses: Rev 1:7, Rev 4:1, Rev 5:6, Rev 6:8, Rev 14:1, Rev 21:3

    Relevance: Interjection in the kai eidon kai idou double formula. Marks elevated structural significance above standard kai eidon.

    G796 — astrape¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G796-astrapē.md

    Occurrences: 9

    Feminine noun meaning lightning, bright shining. 9 NT occurrences (4 in Revelation). All 4 Revelation occurrences are in the Sinai-theophany formula at 4:5, 8:5, 11:19, 16:18. Hebrew source: H1300 baraq (PMI 10.08). The word appears at every occurrence of the theophany formula, making it a constant element alongside bronte and phone.

    Semantic categories: theophanic manifestation, suddenness of Christ's coming, supernatural radiance

    Key verses: Rev 4:5, Rev 8:5, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:18

    Relevance: Constant element in all four Sinai-theophany formula occurrences. Lightning component of the structural marker.

    G1027 — bronte¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1027-bronte.md

    Occurrences: 12

    Feminine noun meaning thunder. 12 NT tokens in 11 unique verses (9 in Revelation). Thunder appears in the Sinai-theophany formula at 4:5, 8:5, 11:19, 16:18 — a constant element. Also appears as the seven thunders (10:3-4, personified), as voice of majesty (6:1, 14:2, 19:6), and as epithet Boanerges (Mark 3:17). Virtually synonymous with Revelation (82% of occurrences).

    Semantic categories: throne-room theophanic formula, seven thunders (personified), voice of majesty, epithet

    Key verses: Rev 4:5, Rev 8:5, Rev 10:3-4, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:18, Rev 19:6

    Relevance: Constant element in all four Sinai-theophany formula occurrences. Thunder component of the structural marker.

    G5456 — phone¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5456-phone.md

    Occurrences: 141

    Feminine noun meaning voice, sound, tone. 141 NT tokens in 129 unique verses. Revelation dominates: 44 occurrences (10.9 per 100 verses, 34% of all NT uses). In the Sinai-theophany formula, phonai (voices) appears at all four occurrences (4:5, 8:5, 11:19, 16:18) — a constant element. Also prominent throughout Revelation's narrative as 'loud voice' (phone megale), trumpet voice, and heavenly announcements.

    Semantic categories: divine/heavenly voice, loud voice exclamation, theophanic formula element

    Key verses: Rev 1:10, Rev 4:1, Rev 4:5, Rev 8:5, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:18

    Relevance: Constant element in all four Sinai-theophany formula occurrences. Voice component; also the 'trumpet voice' linking 1:10 to 4:1.

    G4578 — seismos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4578-seismos.md

    Occurrences: 14

    Masculine noun meaning earthquake, commotion. 14 NT tokens in 12 unique verses (5 in Revelation). In the Sinai-theophany formula: ABSENT at 4:5 (3 elements), PRESENT at 8:5, 11:19, 16:18. The escalation from absence to presence demonstrates progressive intensification. At 16:18 qualified with superlatives: 'such as was not since men were upon the earth, so mighty an earthquake, and so great.'

    Semantic categories: apocalyptic theophany, eschatological warning, divine deliverance

    Key verses: Rev 6:12, Rev 8:5, Rev 11:13, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:18

    Relevance: Escalation element in the Sinai-theophany formula. Absent at 4:5, present at 8:5+, marks progressive intensification.

    G5464 — chalaza¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5464-chalaza.md

    Occurrences: 4

    Feminine noun meaning hail. Appears in the Sinai-theophany formula ONLY at the final two occurrences: 11:19 (chalaza megale, great hail) and 16:21 (chalaza megale, hail of a talent's weight). Absent at 4:5 and 8:5. The escalation pattern: absent → absent → present → present with superlative. Functions as the final escalation element added to the formula.

    Semantic categories: theophanic judgment element, escalation marker

    Key verses: Rev 11:19, Rev 16:21

    Relevance: Final escalation element in the Sinai-theophany formula. Only at the last two of four occurrences.

    G4592 — semeion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4592-semeion.md

    Occurrences: 77

    Neuter noun meaning sign, indication, miracle, token. 77 NT tokens in 69 unique verses (7 in Revelation). In Revelation: the semeion ophthe construction at 12:1 and 12:3 uses passive voice (ophthe, V-API-3S of horao) — distinct from the active kai eidon. At 15:1 the construction shifts to active: kai eidon allo semeion. The word's root (semaino G4591) appears at 1:1 ('signified'), connecting the semeion markers to the book's self-description.

    Semantic categories: authenticating signs, demanded sign, eschatological sign, heavenly wonder/portent

    Key verses: Rev 12:1, Rev 12:3, Rev 15:1, Rev 13:13-14, Rev 16:14, Rev 19:20

    Relevance: Sign-marker vocabulary distinct from kai eidon. Passive ophthe vs active eidon is a structural observation.

    G1492 — oida¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1492-oida.md

    Occurrences: 334

    Defective verb (perfect with present meaning) meaning to know, perceive. 334 NT tokens. Morphologically related to the 2nd aorist eidon of horao (G3708) — both derive from the same PIE root *weid-. The perfect oida ('I know') has shifted from visual to cognitive meaning. In Revelation, oida appears in the seven churches ('I know thy works' — Rev 2:2, 2:9, 2:13, 2:19, 3:1, 3:8, 3:15). The structural eidon form is catalogued under G3708 (horao), not G1492.

    Semantic categories: settled knowledge, perceptive awareness, divine omniscience

    Key verses: Rev 2:2, Rev 2:9, Rev 3:1, Rev 3:8, Rev 3:15

    Relevance: Related to horao (G3708) etymologically. The 'I know' formula (oida) is confined to Vision 1 churches, paralleling the 'Spirit saith' formula's confinement.

    G3700 — optanomai¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3700-optanomai.md

    Occurrences: 60

    Middle voice verb meaning to gaze at, appear, be seen. 60 NT occurrences (5 in Revelation). Prolonged form of horao (G3708). The aorist passive ophthe (was seen/appeared) is used at Rev 11:19 (ark 'was seen'), 12:1 and 12:3 (semeion ophthe — sign 'appeared'). These passive constructions shift agency from the seer to the thing seen — the sign presents itself rather than John actively seeing it.

    Semantic categories: divine/supernatural appearance, post-resurrection appearances, visionary appearances (signs)

    Key verses: Rev 1:7, Rev 11:19, Rev 12:1, Rev 12:3, Rev 22:4

    Relevance: The ophthe (passive) form at 12:1, 12:3 creates the semeion ophthe marker system distinct from active kai eidon.

    G1577 — ekklesia¶

    Occurrences: 118

    Feminine noun meaning church, assembly, congregation. 118 NT occurrences (20 in Revelation). Structural distribution marker: ekklesia appears 19 times in chs. 1-3 (Vision 1) and exactly once in 22:16 (Epilogue). It is COMPLETELY ABSENT from Vision 2 (chs. 4-16), Vision 3 (chs. 17-21:8), and Vision 4 (21:9-22:5). This distribution pattern confirms Vision 1's unique structural character as direct communication to the churches.

    Semantic categories: local church/congregation, universal church/body, assembly

    Key verses: Rev 1:4, Rev 1:11, Rev 1:20, Rev 2:1, Rev 22:16

    Relevance: Distribution confined to Vision 1 + Epilogue. Structural evidence for the distinctiveness of the churches section.

    G3485 — naos¶

    Occurrences: 46

    Masculine noun meaning temple (inner sanctuary, as distinguished from hieron G2411 which includes the entire temple complex). 46 NT occurrences (16 in Revelation). In Revelation: structural appearances at 11:19 (temple opened, ark seen), 14:15,17 (angels come out of temple), 15:5 (temple of tabernacle of testimony opened), 15:6,8 (no man able to enter temple), 16:1,17 (voice from temple), 21:22 (no temple — 'the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are the temple'). Marks sanctuary progression from opened temple to no temple.

    Semantic categories: heavenly temple/sanctuary, divine dwelling, body as temple

    Key verses: Rev 11:19, Rev 15:5, Rev 15:8, Rev 16:17, Rev 21:22

    Relevance: Temple vocabulary tracks sanctuary progression as a structural marker system. The arc from temple opened (11:19) to no temple (21:22) traces the narrative's resolution.

    G2776 — naos¶

    Occurrences: 76

    G2776 kephale (head) — 76 occurrences in NT. Translations: head (44x), heads (17x), the head (9x). In Revelation, kephale appears in beast descriptions (seven heads — Rev 12:3, 13:1, 13:3, 17:3, 17:7, 17:9), dragon imagery (12:3), and the 6th trumpet horses (9:17, 9:19). The seven heads are interpreted as seven mountains and seven kings (17:9-10). The wounded head at 13:3 is structurally significant. Listed in prompt.json as naos (temple); note that the actual temple word is G3485 naos, already cataloged separately.

    Semantic categories: head, authority, beast imagery, seven heads

    Key verses: Rev 12:3, Rev 13:1, Rev 13:3, Rev 17:3, Rev 9:17

    Relevance: Required word study per prompt.json. Kephale (head) appears in structural beast descriptions and dragon imagery, linking the semeion markers (12:1, 12:3) to the kai eidon beast passages (13:1-3). The seven-heads motif connects Visions 2 and 3.
