    Verses¶

    Question¶

    How do trumpets 5-7 (the three "woes") differ structurally from trumpets 1-4? What is the role of the trumpet interlude (the little book and the two witnesses, Rev 10:1-11:14)? How does the seventh trumpet's kingdom announcement (Rev 11:15-18) function as both climax and transition? What is the relationship between the seventh trumpet and the bowls?

    Trumpets 1-4: Formulaic nature judgments with one-third fraction¶

    The first angel sounded, and there followed hail and fire mingled with blood, and they were cast upon the earth: and the third part of trees was burnt up, and all green grass was burnt up. And the second angel sounded, and as it were a great mountain burning with fire was cast into the sea: and the third part of the sea became blood; And the third part of the creatures which were in the sea, and had life, died; and the third part of the ships were destroyed. And the third angel sounded, and there fell a great star from heaven, burning as it were a lamp, and it fell upon the third part of the rivers, and upon the fountains of waters; And the name of the star is called Wormwood: and the third part of the waters became wormwood; and many men died of the waters, because they were made bitter. And the fourth angel sounded, and the third part of the sun was smitten, and the third part of the moon, and the third part of the stars; so as the third part of them was darkened, and the day shone not for a third part of it, and the night likewise.

    Context: Trumpets 1-4 follow the seven-trumpet distribution scene (8:2,6). Brief, formulaic, nature-targeting, with the one-third fraction appearing 11 times. These serve as the structural baseline for comparison with trumpets 5-7.

    Notes: TM075 documents the one-third fraction (to triton, G5154) appearing 11 times in these 6 verses. TM076 documents the formulaic trumpet-sounding formula. SP055 (Strong) documents that trumpets 1-4 and bowls 1-4 target the same domains in the same order: earth, sea, rivers, sun.

    Fifth trumpet / First woe: star fallen, key to abyss, locust-army restrictions¶

    And the fifth angel sounded, and I saw a star fall from heaven unto the earth: and to him was given the key of the bottomless pit. And he opened the bottomless pit; and there arose a smoke out of the pit, as the smoke of a great furnace; and the sun and the air were darkened by reason of the smoke of the pit. And there came out of the smoke locusts upon the earth: and unto them was given power, as the scorpions of the earth have power. And it was commanded them that they should not hurt the grass of the earth, neither any green thing, neither any tree; but only those men which have not the seal of God in their foreheads.     Fifth trumpet: locust description and king Abaddon/Apollyon¶

    And the shapes of the locusts [were] like unto horses prepared unto battle; and on their heads [were] as it were crowns like gold, and their faces [were] as the faces of men. And they had hair as the hair of women, and their teeth were as [the teeth] of lions. And they had breastplates, as it were breastplates of iron; and the sound of their wings [was] as the sound of chariots of many horses running to battle. And they had tails like unto scorpions, and there were stings in their tails: and their power [was] to hurt men five months. And they had a king over them, [which is] the angel of the bottomless pit, whose name in the Hebrew tongue [is] Abaddon, but in the Greek tongue hath [his] name Apollyon.

    Context: The locust description transforms Joel 2's divine army into a demonic force. The bilingual naming (Abaddon H11 / Apollyon G623) is unique in Revelation. Apollyon = active present participle of apollymi G622, 'the one who destroys.'

    Notes: AN031 (Strong) documents the Joel 2 allusion. Key transformation: Joel 2:25 identifies the locusts as 'my great army which I sent among you' — God's army. Rev 9's locusts emerge from the abyss under a destroyer-king — a demonic inversion. Shared elements: horse-like appearance (Joel 2:4 / Rev 9:7), chariot noise (Joel 2:5 / Rev 9:9), battle array, fire. Rev 9:11 has the bilingual naming: Hebraisti Abaddon (G3, H11) and en te Hellenike Apollyon (G623).
