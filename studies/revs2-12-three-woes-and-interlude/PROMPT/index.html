    Question¶

    How do trumpets 5-7 (the three "woes") differ structurally from trumpets 1-4? What is the role of the trumpet interlude (the little book and the two witnesses, Rev 10:1-11:14)? How does the seventh trumpet's kingdom announcement (Rev 11:15-18) function as both climax and transition? What is the relationship between the seventh trumpet and the bowls?

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Three prior revs studies (revs-19, -20, -21) already cataloged 40+ structural elements covering the woe trumpets, interlude, and seventh trumpet. Key findings include: SP038 (Strong) confirming the 4+3 nature-humanity division; TM070-TM074 documenting woe markers, abyss vocabulary, and chronos plural in Rev 10:7; VP082/VP084/VP096 establishing verbal parallels between the seventh trumpet and bowls; SP042-SP043 analyzing the two-part interlude and the expanding-interlude pattern; AN036-AN039 documenting the dense OT allusion network in the interlude; and IC033/IC040 presenting the telescoping reading with competing alternatives. The revs2-10 study established the Sinai-theophany formula escalation (TM057) and the seventh-seal/seventh-trumpet structural parallel (VP070). The revs2-11 study documented the one-third fraction pattern (TM075), formulaic trumpet-sounding (TM076), creation-domain targeting (SP041), and trumpet-bowl target correspondence (SP055). Web research identified scholarly positions from Hoskins, Mueller, Paulien, Lambrecht, Strand, Aune, Wallace, Bauckham, Storms, and Brown on the telescoping/recapitulation debate, the little scroll identity, the two witnesses, and the proleptic aorist in the kingdom announcement.
