    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    How do trumpets 5-7 (the three "woes") differ structurally from trumpets 1-4? What is the role of the trumpet interlude (the little book and the two witnesses, Rev 10:1-11:14)? How does the seventh trumpet's kingdom announcement (Rev 11:15-18) function as both climax and transition? What is the relationship between the seventh trumpet and the bowls?

    G3759 — ouai¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3759-ouai.md

    Occurrences: 47

    Primary exclamation of grief meaning 'woe' or 'alas.' In Revelation, ouai shifts from prophetic denunciation to cosmic lament and becomes a structural marker. The triple ouai at Rev 8:13 designates trumpets 5-7 as 'woe trumpets.' The woe-closure formula (he ouai he [ordinal] apelthen) appears at Rev 9:12 and 11:14. Ouai is treated as a feminine noun with the definite article in these formulae.

    Semantic categories: prophetic denunciation, eschatological lament, structural marker

    Key verses: Rev 8:13, Rev 9:12, Rev 11:14, Rev 12:12, Rev 18:10, Rev 18:16, Rev 18:19

    Relevance: The structural divider between trumpets 1-4 and trumpets 5-7. The woe-closure formula and the missing third-woe closure are key structural evidence for the telescoping thesis.

    G12 — abyssos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G12-abyssos.md

    Occurrences: 9

    Depthless place, translated 'bottomless pit' (5x) or 'deep' (2x). Revelation-concentrated (7 of 9 NT occurrences). Functions as both source of demonic power (9:1-2, 9:11, 11:7, 17:8) and prison for Satan (20:1-3). The vocabulary chain links the woe-trumpet sequence to the beast narrative and final judgment.

    Semantic categories: apocalyptic pit, demonic source, place of confinement

    Key verses: Rev 9:1, Rev 9:2, Rev 9:11, Rev 11:7, Rev 17:8, Rev 20:1, Rev 20:3

    Relevance: Creates a vocabulary chain spanning the entire book: opened (9:1-2), locust-king's domain (9:11), beast's origin (11:7, 17:8), Satan's prison (20:1-3). Links the woe trumpets to subsequent narratives.

    G5421 — phrear¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5421-phrear.md

    Occurrences: 7

    A hole in the ground — literally a well or cistern, apocalyptically the shaft/opening giving access to the abyss. 4 of 7 occurrences are in Revelation 9:1-2 in the phrase to phrear tes abyssou (the pit of the abyss). G5421 is the shaft or opening; G12 (abyssos) is the depthless space itself.

    Semantic categories: literal well, apocalyptic pit

    Key verses: Rev 9:1, Rev 9:2, John 4:11-12, Luke 14:5

    Relevance: Provides the physical metaphor for the abyss entry point in the fifth trumpet. The distinction between phrear (shaft) and abyssos (space) matters for the spatial imagery.

    G623 — Apollyon¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G623-apollýōn.md

    Occurrences: 1

    Active present participle of apollymi (G622), meaning 'the one who destroys.' A proper name/title for the angel-king of the bottomless pit at Rev 9:11. Unique in the NT. The bilingual naming (Hebrew Abaddon + Greek Apollyon) ensures both linguistic audiences grasp the meaning.

    Semantic categories: personification of destruction, bilingual naming

    Key verses: Rev 9:11

    Relevance: The bilingual naming is unique in Revelation and emphasizes that this is a demonic king — 'the Destroyer.' H11 (Abaddon) appears 6x in OT as the realm of the dead (Job 26:6, 28:22, 31:12, Psa 88:11, Pro 15:11, 27:20); Revelation personalizes it as a ruler.

    G936 — basileuo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G936-basileuo.md

    Occurrences: 23

    Verb: to reign, to be king. 18 unique verses (23 tokens). Revelation-dominant (39% of all occurrences). The key contrast: basileusei (V-FAI-3S, future) at Rev 11:15 = 'he shall reign forever' vs. ebasileusas (V-AAI-2S, aorist) at Rev 11:17 = ingressive/proleptic 'you have begun to reign.' The aorist in doxological context is distinctive to Revelation (11:17, 19:6).

    Semantic categories: eschatological reign, saints reigning with Christ, personified forces reigning

    Key verses: Rev 11:15, Rev 11:17, Rev 19:6, Rev 20:4, Rev 22:5, 1 Cor 15:25

    Relevance: The basileuo tense distinction between Rev 11:15 (future) and 11:17 (aorist) is central to understanding the kingdom announcement as proleptic/ingressive.

    G3340 — metanoeo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3340-metanoeo.md

    Occurrences: 36

    Verb: to change one's mind, to repent. 32 unique verses (36 tokens). Heaviest concentration in Revelation (10 of 32). The failed-repentance motif: ou metanoesan (Rev 9:20-21) marks the structural bookend of the woe trumpets. The same formula recurs in bowls (16:9, 16:11). Cognitive/volitional reorientation, not merely emotional remorse.

    Semantic categories: kingdom proclamation, universal divine command, hardened refusal under judgment

    Key verses: Rev 9:20, Rev 9:21, Rev 16:9, Rev 16:11, Matt 3:2, Acts 17:30

    Relevance: The failed-repentance motif at Rev 9:20-21 is the narrative logic for the warning-to-final-judgment transition. If trumpets are warnings and humanity does not repent, bowls escalate to total judgment.

    G4536 — salpinx¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4536-salpinx.md

    Occurrences: 11

    Feminine noun: trumpet. 11 NT occurrences concentrated in Revelation (6, or 55%). Conflates both OT instruments (shofar H7782 and chatsotsrah H2689) in LXX translation. Semantic categories: eschatological gathering, resurrection signal, divine voice, seven apocalyptic trumpets.

    Semantic categories: eschatological signal, divine communication, apocalyptic judgment

    Key verses: Rev 8:2, Rev 8:6, Rev 8:13, Rev 9:14, Matt 24:31, 1 Cor 15:52

    Relevance: The instrument of the seven-trumpet series. TM076 documents the formulaic trumpet-sounding across all seven.

    G974 — biblaridion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G974-bibliaridion.md

    Occurrences: 4

    Double diminutive of biblion: a very small book/scroll. Appears ONLY in Rev 10:2,8,9,10 (4 occurrences). TM078: hapax-like distribution. Contextually distinguished from biblion (G975): different word, different state (open vs. sealed), different handler (angel vs. God/Lamb).

    Semantic categories: diminutive scroll, prophetic content

    Key verses: Rev 10:2, Rev 10:8, Rev 10:9, Rev 10:10

    Relevance: Central to the scroll-identity debate. The biblaridion/biblion distinction and the sealed-to-unsealed vocabulary arc are structural evidence for identifying the Rev 10 scroll with Daniel's sealed prophecy.

    G975 — biblion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G975-biblion.md

    Occurrences: 32

    Neuter noun: book/scroll. Used for the seven-sealed scroll of Rev 5:1 and the book of life. Rev 10:8 uses biblion while John uses biblaridion (10:2,9,10) — the vocabulary alternation is significant for the scroll-identity question.

    Semantic categories: sealed scroll, book of life, prophetic writing

    Key verses: Rev 5:1, Rev 10:8, Rev 20:12

    Relevance: Contrasts with biblaridion (G974). The heavenly voice uses biblion at 10:8 while John uses biblaridion — if they were the same scroll, why does the vocabulary switch? This may indicate the biblaridion is the Danielic subset of the larger biblion.

    G3466 — mysterion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3466-mysterion.md

    Occurrences: 27

    Neuter noun: mystery, secret. Rev 10:7's to mysterion tou theou etelesthe (the mystery of God was finished) forms the raz-mysterion chain from Dan 2:28. The mystery is completed (aorist passive of teleo G5055) at the seventh trumpet's sounding.

    Semantic categories: divine secret revealed, prophetic completion

    Key verses: Rev 10:7, Rev 1:20, Rev 17:5, Dan 2:28

    Relevance: Rev 10:7 declares the mystery of God finished at the seventh trumpet. The same verb (etelesthe) appears at Rev 15:1 for the wrath of God being finished — linking mystery-completion to wrath-completion.

    G5055 — teleo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5055-teleo.md

    Occurrences: 30

    Verb: to finish, complete, bring to an end. VP084 (Minimal, 2 links) connects Rev 10:7 and Rev 15:1 via identical etelesthe (V-API-3S, aorist passive) + tou Theou. Rev 10:7 = mystery of God finished; Rev 15:1 = wrath of God finished.

    Semantic categories: prophetic completion, judgment completion

    Key verses: Rev 10:7, Rev 15:1, John 19:30

    Relevance: The identical etelesthe in both passages is one of the verbal parallels linking the seventh trumpet to the bowls.

    G3709 — orge¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3709-orge.md

    Occurrences: 36

    Feminine noun: wrath as settled, judicial anger. Distinct from thymos (G2372, burning fury/passion). SP045: the 7th trumpet announces orge (Rev 11:18), the bowls pour thymos (Rev 15:1,7), at the climax both combine (tou thymou tes orges at Rev 16:19 and 19:15).

    Semantic categories: divine settled judgment, judicial wrath

    Key verses: Rev 11:18, Rev 6:17, Rev 16:19, Rev 19:15

    Relevance: The orge-thymos distinction is central to the wrath continuum thesis (SP045). orge = the decision to judge; thymos = the execution of that judgment.

    G2372 — thumos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2372-thumos.md

    Occurrences: 18

    Masculine noun: fury, burning wrath, passionate anger. Distinct from orge (G3709). Used for the bowl judgments (Rev 15:1,7, 16:1) and combined with orge at the climax (Rev 16:19, 19:15). Also used for the dragon's wrath at Rev 12:12 (thymos megas).

    Semantic categories: burning fury, divine wrath poured out

    Key verses: Rev 12:12, Rev 14:10, Rev 15:1, Rev 15:7, Rev 16:1, Rev 16:19, Rev 19:15

    Relevance: The bowl vocabulary. SP045's complementary distribution: orge announced at 7th trumpet, thymos poured in bowls, both combined at climax.

    G932 — basileia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G932-basileia.md

    Occurrences: 162

    Feminine noun: kingdom, reign, royal power. Rev 11:15: Egeneto he basileia tou kosmou tou Kyriou hemon — 'The kingdom of the world has become [the kingdom] of our Lord.' The kingdom-transfer declaration at the seventh trumpet.

    Semantic categories: kingdom of God, world-kingdom, kingdom transfer

    Key verses: Rev 11:15, Rev 12:10, Dan 2:44, Dan 7:14, Dan 7:27

    Relevance: The subject of the seventh trumpet's proleptic announcement. basileia tou kosmou (kingdom of the world) becomes basileia tou Kyriou (kingdom of the Lord).

    G1096 — ginomai¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1096-ginomai.md

    Occurrences: 702

    Verb: to become, to arise, to come to be. The most common Greek verb (631 NT occurrences). In Rev 11:15, Egeneto (V-2ADI-3S, aorist) functions as a proleptic aorist declaring kingdom transfer as accomplished. In Rev 16:17, Gegonen (V-2RAI-3S, perfect) declares 'It is done' with permanent results. The aorist-to-perfect shift between the two passages marks the completion of what was proleptically announced.

    Semantic categories: narrative transition, change of state, proleptic declaration

    Key verses: Rev 11:15, Rev 16:17, Rev 12:10

    Relevance: VP096 strongest parallel: Egeneto (aorist, Rev 11:15) vs. Gegonen (perfect, Rev 16:17). The tense shift tracks the progress from announcement to completion.

    G796 — astrape¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G796-astrapē.md

    Occurrences: 9

    Feminine noun: lightning. In Revelation, astrape occurs exclusively in the four theophany-formula passages: Rev 4:5, 8:5, 11:19, 16:18. This exclusive distribution makes it a structural marker for the Sinai-theophany formula.

    Semantic categories: theophanic phenomenon, structural marker

    Key verses: Rev 4:5, Rev 8:5, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:18

    Relevance: TM057: astrape's exclusive distribution in the four theophany passages confirms the Sinai-theophany formula as a deliberate structural device.

    G5464 — chalaza¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5464-chalaza.md

    Occurrences: 4

    Feminine noun: hail. Appears in Revelation at 8:7 (trumpet 1), 11:19 (7th trumpet theophany), and 16:21 (7th bowl). Creates an inclusio bracketing the entire trumpet sequence: hail at trumpet 1, hail at trumpet 7.

    Semantic categories: theophanic element, judgment plague, Exodus allusion

    Key verses: Rev 8:7, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:21

    Relevance: chalaza inclusio: trumpet 1 (8:7) → 7th trumpet (11:19) → 7th bowl (16:21). The escalation from mixed-with-blood hail (8:7) to great hail (11:19) to talent-weight hail (16:21) demonstrates progressive intensification.

    G3485 — naos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3485-naos.md

    Occurrences: 46

    Masculine noun: temple, inner sanctuary, holy place. Distinguished from hieron (G2411, the broader temple complex). In Revelation, naos refers to the heavenly sanctuary. Key structural connections: naos opened at 11:19 and 15:5; naos measured at 11:1; naos as voice origin at 16:1,17.

    Semantic categories: heavenly sanctuary, divine dwelling, judgment source

    Key verses: Rev 11:1, Rev 11:19, Rev 15:5, Rev 15:8, Rev 16:1, Rev 16:17

    Relevance: VP082 (Moderate, 3 links): naos + anoigo + en to ourano shared between Rev 11:19 and 15:5, linking 7th trumpet to bowl introduction. The naos as voice origin in both 11:15 and 16:17 supports VP096.

    G2787 — kibotos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2787-kibotos.md

    Occurrences: 6

    Feminine noun: ark, chest, box. In Revelation, kibotos tes diathekes autou (the ark of his covenant/testament) appears ONLY at Rev 11:19. Its visibility evokes the Day of Atonement when the high priest entered the Most Holy Place.

    Semantic categories: covenant symbol, Most Holy Place, Day of Atonement

    Key verses: Rev 11:19

    Relevance: The unique appearance of the ark at Rev 11:19 signals the opening of the Most Holy Place — evoking Lev 16's Day of Atonement ritual and supporting AN040.

    G4127 — plege¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4127-plege.md

    Occurrences: 21

    Feminine noun: plague, blow, wound. Rev 9:20 uses plege to retroactively label the trumpet judgments as 'plagues' — confirming the Exodus-plague typology. The same word describes the bowls at Rev 15:1,6,8 and 16:9,21.

    Semantic categories: divine judgment, Exodus plague, eschatological woe

    Key verses: Rev 9:20, Rev 11:6, Rev 15:1, Rev 16:9, Rev 16:21

    Relevance: The shared vocabulary (plege) linking trumpets and bowls as plague-sequences strengthens the trumpet-bowl connection.

    G5550 — chronos¶

    Semantic categories: duration of time, eschatological time-termination

    Key verses: Rev 10:6, Rev 20:3

    Relevance: chronos ouketi estai at Rev 10:6 marks the end of the delay/waiting period — no more time for warnings (trumpets); final judgment (bowls) follows immediately.

    G2540 — kairos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2540-kairos.md

    Occurrences: 84

    Masculine noun: time as season, appointed time, opportunity. Rev 11:18: ho kairos ton nekron krithenai = 'the time/season of the dead to be judged.' Distinct from chronos (G5550, time as duration). kairos marks divinely appointed moments; chronos measures ongoing duration.

    Key verses: Rev 11:18, Rev 12:12, Rev 12:14

    Relevance: Rev 11:18's kairos identifies the five-element program as a divinely appointed eschatological season. Rev 12:14 uses kairos for the 3.5-year period (kairon kai kairous kai hemisy kairou).
