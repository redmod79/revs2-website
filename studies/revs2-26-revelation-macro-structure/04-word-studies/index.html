    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    Synthesizing all 25 prior revs2 studies, what is the macro-structure of the book of Revelation? How do the four en pneumati visions, the 1:19 threefold frame, the prologue-epilogue inclusio, the three judgment sequences with interludes, the sanctuary compartmental progression, the Babylon-bride mirror, the structural threads, the IC077 hybrid model, the overcomer arc, and the theophany formula progression combine into one coherent literary architecture?

    G4151 — πνεῦμα (pneuma)¶

    Occurrences: 385

    385 NT occurrences, 23 in Revelation (6.2 per 100 verses). The en pneumati formula (G1722 + G4151) marks all four structural vision entries (1:10, 4:2, 17:3, 21:10). Key distinction: egenomēn en pneumati (V1/V2, aorist middle from ginomai G1096, self-initiated) vs. apēnegken me en pneumati (V3/V4, aorist active from apophero G667, angel-transports John). The verb shift is the primary grammatical basis for the fourfold structural division. Pneuma also appears at Rev 2:7/11/17/29/3:6/13/22 (seven times in ear-formula) and Rev 22:17 (Spirit and bride say 'Come').

    Semantic categories: Spirit of God / Holy Spirit, En pneumati vision-entry formula, Spirit and bride (22:17), Seven Spirits of God (1:4, 3:1, 4:5, 5:6)

    Key verses: Rev 1:10, Rev 4:2, Rev 17:3, Rev 21:10, Rev 4:5, Rev 22:17

    Relevance: En pneumati is the primary macro-structural marker of the fourfold vision division. The grammatical shift from ginomai to apophero at the V3 boundary is the strongest textual basis for treating Rev 17:1–22:5 as structurally distinct from Rev 4:1–16:21. The seven Spirits (4:5) = Holy Spirit (Zechariah chain); the Spirit and bride invitation at 22:17 closes the consummation vision.

    G3485 — ναός (naos)¶

    Occurrences: 46

    40-43 NT occurrences, 16 in Revelation (highest density 4.0 per 100 verses). Zero hieron in Revelation — deliberate inner-shrine perspective throughout. The naos trajectory begins in Vision 2 (naos at 7:15, sanctuary sections at 8:3-5, 9:13, 11:1-2, 11:19) and culminates at 21:22 (God and Lamb ARE the naos). The outer court (aulē) appears only at 11:2 and is excluded from measurement. Two-altar grammar: chrysoun qualifier distinguishes HP golden altar (8:3, 9:13) from OC bronze altar (6:9, 14:18, 16:7).

    Semantic categories: Inner sanctuary / Holy of Holies, Heavenly sanctuary, New Jerusalem as cosmic naos

    Key verses: Rev 7:15, Rev 11:1, Rev 11:2, Rev 11:19, Rev 15:5, Rev 15:8, Rev 21:22

    Relevance: The 16 naos occurrences trace the sanctuary compartmental progression (IC155): from HP perspective (trumpets/golden altar) through MHP DOA (15:5-8) to the cosmic naos of NJ (21:22) where the sanctuary category is transcended. The absence of hieron (temple complex) confirms John maintains the inner-shrine perspective throughout, reflecting the heavenly sanctuary's Most Holy Place as the referent.

    G1096 — γίνομαι (ginomai)¶

    Occurrences: 702

    631 NT occurrences; egeneto is the dominant form (233x, 34.9%). Three-stage arc in Revelation (TM197): (1) proleptic aorist egeneto (Rev 11:15) — heaven declares kingdom accomplished before earthly execution; (2) perfect singular gegonen (Rev 16:17, V-2RAI-3S) — judgment permanently consummated; (3) perfect plural gegonan (Rev 21:6, V-2RAI-3P) — all things of new creation accomplished. The progressive morphology (aorist → perfect singular → perfect plural) constitutes the temporal backbone of the entire book. Also at Rev 1:10/4:2 as egenomēn (aorist middle indicative, vision-entry form).

    Semantic categories: Temporal backbone arc, Vision-entry formula (egenomēn), Completion declarations (gegonen/gegonan), Proleptic aorist declarations

    Key verses: Rev 1:10, Rev 4:2, Rev 11:15, Rev 16:17, Rev 21:6

    Relevance: The ginomai arc provides Revelation's temporal spine: proleptic aorist (11:15, heaven's anticipatory declaration) → perfect singular (16:17, judgment consummated) → perfect plural (21:6, new creation accomplished). SP048: this arc parallels John 19:30 (tetelestai = cross completion). The three stages together trace the 'It is done' declarations from anticipation through judgment to consummation.

    G3841 — παντοκράτωρ (pantokrator)¶

    Occurrences: 10

    10 NT occurrences, 9 in Revelation (90%). All 9 nominative or genitive. Distribution across all four visions: V1 at 1:8 (ego eimi declaration, prologue); V2 at 4:8 (throne room hymn), 11:17 (7th trumpet hymn), 15:3 (Song of Moses), 16:7 (altar vindication), 16:14 (Armageddon); V3 at 19:6 (allelouia), 19:15 (parousia); V4 at 21:22 (God IS the naos). Pantokrator is the ONLY vocabulary thread spanning all four en pneumati visions (IC102). It also bridges two Christological systems: ego eimi declarations (1:8) and doxological hymns (4:8/11:17 etc.).

    Semantic categories: Divine sovereignty title, Hymnic doxology marker, Book-spanning structural thread, Ego eimi declaration vocabulary

    Key verses: Rev 1:8, Rev 4:8, Rev 11:17, Rev 15:3, Rev 16:7, Rev 19:6, Rev 21:22

    Relevance: IC102 (revs2-05): pantokrator and the kings-and-priests thread are the only two vocabulary threads spanning all four en pneumati visions. As the lexical bridge between ego eimi declarations (structural boundary points) and hymns (sequence transitions), pantokrator provides vertical cohesion across the horizontal narrative. Its V4 appearance at 21:22 ('the Lord God the Almighty is the naos of it') is structurally climactic — pantokrator appears in the verse that declares God himself is the temple.

    G3528 — νικάω (nikaō)¶

    Occurrences: 28

    28 NT occurrences, 17 in Revelation (61%). Tense taxonomy (revs2-21): (1) Christ: aorist indicative (eniikēsa/eniikēsen, completed past victory — 3:21, 5:5); (2) Believer: present participle (tō nikōnti, ongoing — 2:7/11/17/26/3:5/12/21); (3) Beast: aorist infinitive under edothē divine passive (permitted-dependent, temporary — 13:7); (4) Lamb: future indicative (nikēsei, eschatological — 17:14). IC117: overcomer arc bridges Rev 2-3 (letters) to Rev 19-22 (NJ/consummation). Five of seven promises converge at Rev 22:2-5. Nikao inversion: overcomer promises → beast's temporary nikao → saints' genuine nikao (12:11) → Lamb overcomes (17:14).

    Semantic categories: Overcomer arc, Christ's completed victory, Believer's ongoing overcoming, Beast's temporary permitted victory

    Key verses: Rev 2:7, Rev 3:21, Rev 5:5, Rev 12:11, Rev 13:7, Rev 17:14, Rev 21:7

    Relevance: The nikao tense taxonomy is one of the most precise grammatical arguments in Revelation: the four different grammatical forms used for the four different subjects encode the theological relationships. Christ's aorist = self-originated completed victory; believer's present = ongoing participation; beast's infinitive under divine passive = permitted only, derivative; Lamb's future = eschatological triumph. IC117 maps the overcomer arc from V1 (letters) through V2 (conflict/beast's temporary nikao) to V3-4 (resolution).

    G796 — ἀστραπή (astrapē)¶

    Occurrences: 9

    9 NT occurrences; 4 in Revelation exclusively at the four TM081 theophany formula passages (4:5, 8:5, 11:19, 16:18). PMI H1300 = 10.08 (strongest alignment to Hebrew baraq = lightning). Astrapē in non-Revelation NT: Matt 24:27 (coming of Son of Man as lightning), Luke 10:18 (Satan as lightning), Luke 17:24 (parousia), Matt 28:3 (angel's appearance). In Revelation: exclusively marks the four structural boundary passages, never in narrative sections. Element count at each station: 3 (4:5) → 4 (8:5, adds seismos) → 5 (11:19, adds chalaza) → 5+ superlative (16:18).

    Semantic categories: Theophany formula marker, Structural boundary signal, OT Sinai theophany echo

    Key verses: Rev 4:5, Rev 8:5, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:18

    Relevance: TM081 (revs2-20): astrapē's exclusive confinement to the four theophany formula passages is one of the most precise structural markers in Revelation. Because it appears nowhere else in the book, every astrapē occurrence marks a structural boundary. The escalating element count (3→4→5→5+) across the four stations encodes the intensification of the judgment narrative — each new sequence adds one element. The Sinai theophany (Exod 19:16-19) provides the three base elements that Revelation's formula builds upon.

    G4637 — σκηνόω (skēnoō)¶

    Occurrences: 5

    5 NT occurrences, 4 in Revelation. John 1:14 (aorist — completed incarnation, the Word tabernacled among us); Rev 7:15 (future — God will dwell over them); Rev 12:12 (present imperative — let heavens rejoice, he that dwells in them); Rev 13:6 (present participle — blaspheming those dwelling in heaven); Rev 21:3 (future — God will dwell with them). The arc moves from completed incarnation (John 1:14) through eschatological promise (7:15) to realized consummation (21:3) — where the divine dwelling with humanity is permanently achieved. Terminal point at Rev 21:22 (God IS the naos) transcends even the skēnoō category.

    Semantic categories: Divine dwelling arc, Incarnation (John 1:14), Eschatological consummation (Rev 21:3), Heavenly combat section (Rev 12/13)

    Key verses: Rev 7:15, Rev 12:12, Rev 13:6, Rev 21:3

    Relevance: The skēnoō arc traces the trajectory from Exod 25:8 (divine dwelling intent), through John 1:14 (incarnation = first fulfillment), to Rev 21:3 (cosmic consummation = final fulfillment). This arc spans the entire canonical program of divine dwelling with humanity. Rev 21:22 transcends even skēnoō: the constructed sanctuary is eliminated, and God himself becomes the dwelling-place (IS the naos). The arc provides the narrative logic of the sanctuary progression.

    G4352 — προσκυνέω (proskyneō)¶

    Occurrences: 65

    54 unique verses, 22 in Revelation (41% of NT total — highest single-book concentration). True worship (God/Lamb): Rev 4:10, 5:14, 7:11, 11:1, 11:16, 14:7, 15:4, 19:4, 19:10 (angel refusal), 22:8-9 (angel refusal). False worship (beast/image): Rev 9:20, 13:4 (×2), 13:8, 13:12, 13:15, 14:9, 14:11, 16:2, 19:20, 20:4. The worship divide — true vs. false — maps directly onto the Babylon/New Jerusalem structural opposition. The two angel-refusal scenes (19:10 and 22:8-9) create the internal bracket within Visions 3-4.

    Semantic categories: True worship (God/Lamb), False worship (beast/image), Worship as structural theme, Angel-refusal scenes

    Key verses: Rev 4:10, Rev 13:4, Rev 13:8, Rev 14:7, Rev 19:10, Rev 22:8

    Relevance: The 22 proskyneō occurrences (41% of NT total) make worship the dominant theological-structural theme of Revelation. The true/false worship divide maps onto the macro-structure: Vision 2 (throne room: true worship established) → Vision 3 (Babylon: false worship system exposed and judged) → Vision 4 (NJ: pure worship consummated). The two angel-refusal scenes (19:10 and 22:8-9) are the structural brackets that close Visions 3 and 4 respectively, using the same scene to enforce the correct object of worship.

    G1238 — διάδημα (diádēma)¶

    Occurrences: 3

    3 NT occurrences, ALL in Revelation. All three as plural diademata (never singular). Diadema arc: Rev 12:3 (dragon: 7 diadems on seven heads); Rev 13:1 (sea beast: 10 diadems on ten horns); Rev 19:12 (Christ: polla/many diadems — uncounted). The escalation 7→10→polla (many) then transcends enumeration. Diadema = royal diadem (sovereign authority bound to head); contrasts with stephanos = victor's wreath (earned, awarded). In Revelation: diadema reserved exclusively for the sovereignty contest (dragon, beast, Christ); no human figure wears diadema. Stephanos is given to elders (4:4, 4:10), the woman (12:1), locusts (9:7), and the conquering rider (6:2).

    Semantic categories: Sovereign authority symbol, Parody crown arc (dragon/beast), Christ's unlimited sovereignty (polla)

    Key verses: Rev 12:3, Rev 13:1, Rev 19:12

    Relevance: The diadema arc (revs2-19, IC155 class element) is one of the three-occurrence book-spanning macro-structural arcs in Revelation. The deliberate use of diadema (not stephanos) for all three figures ensures the progression is visible: dragon claims 7 domains, beast claims 10, Christ's polla surpasses and subsumes both claims. This arc supports the macro-structural observation that Rev 12-13 (Unholy Trinity/Parody section) deliberately mirrors the divine sovereignty claims of Rev 4-5 and 19.

    G91 — ἀδικέω (adikeō)¶

    Occurrences: 35

    35 tokens, 23 unique NT verses. 9 in Revelation. The sealing chain (IC147 Linear, Strong, revs2-12/20): Rev 7:3 ('Hurt not the earth, neither the sea, nor the trees, till we have sealed') → Rev 8:7-9 (T1-T2 harm earth/sea/trees as the exact domains released) → Rev 9:4 (locusts commanded 'hurt not those who have the seal of God'). The achri ('until') temporal clause at 7:3 makes the protection explicitly conditional-sequential. Three-link chain: (1) seal prohibits harm to earth/sea/trees (7:3); (2) trumpets execute harm on these domains (8:7-9); (3) seal-possession exempts from locust harm (9:4).

    Semantic categories: Harm/injury verb, IC147 sealing chain, Temporal protection via seal

    Key verses: Rev 7:3, Rev 8:7, Rev 9:4, Rev 9:10, Rev 9:19, Rev 11:5

    Relevance: IC147 (Linear, Strong): the G91 sealing chain is the strongest single sequential argument in the structural database for telescoping/linear reading of the judgment sequences. The chain demonstrates that the seal chapter (Rev 7) anticipates the very domains the trumpet sequence attacks (Rev 8:7-9), and that the seal of God at 7:3 provides protection from the locust attack at 9:4. The achri temporal clause at 7:3 makes the sequence explicitly temporal, not merely thematic — prohibiting recapitulation interpretations that treat Rev 7 and Rev 9 as covering the same events from different angles.

    G3107 — μακάριος (makarios)¶

    Occurrences: 49

    49 NT occurrences (50 tokens), 7 in Revelation — all seven beatitudes. All seven: B1 Rev 1:3 (prologue: readeth/heareth/keepeth), B2 Rev 14:13 (die in the Lord), B3 Rev 16:15 (watcheth, keepeth garments), B4 Rev 19:9 (called to marriage supper, Grapson pair), B5 Rev 20:6 (first resurrection participant, makarios kai hagios), B6 Rev 22:7 (keepeth prophecy words, Idou-erchomai pair), B7 Rev 22:14 (doeth commandments, tree of life). Six of seven at structural seam positions; B3 (16:15) is the structural exception embedded inside a judgment sequence (between bowl 5 and 6).

    Semantic categories: Seven beatitudes, Structural spine, Inclusio pairs (B1-B6, Grapson B2/B4, Idou B3/B6)

    Key verses: Rev 1:3, Rev 14:13, Rev 16:15, Rev 19:9, Rev 20:6, Rev 22:7, Rev 22:14

    Relevance: The seven beatitudes form a book-spanning structural spine across all four visions: B1 (V1/prologue), B2 (V2 cosmic conflict), B3 (V2 bowls, exception), B4 (V3 hinge), B5 (V4 millennium), B6 (V4/epilogue), B7 (V4/epilogue). The pairing system (B1-B6 inclusio, Grapson B2/B4, Idou-erchomai B3/B6) creates a sub-bracket network within the book-level structure. B5 (20:6) is the convergence node: makarios kai hagios, first resurrection, second death immunity, kings-and-priests — multiple structural threads intersect at this beatitude.

    G906 — βάλλω (ballō)¶

    Occurrences: 127

    106 verses (127 tokens), 23 in Revelation. The ballo arc (TM element, revs2-21): heaven→earth eblēthē passive (Rev 12:9, Satan cast out); earth→abyss ébalen active (Rev 20:3, angel casts Satan in); abyss→lake of fire eblēthē passive (Rev 20:10, beast/false prophet cast in). Verbal unity across chapters 12 and 20 via same G906 root. Also: ébalen at Rev 8:5 (censer cast to earth, seal→trumpet hinge), ébalen at Rev 14:19 (sickle cast into earth, harvest scene).

    Semantic categories: Casting arc (satan's expulsion/binding/final doom), Censer transformation (8:5), Harvest imagery (14:19)

    Key verses: Rev 8:5, Rev 12:9, Rev 14:19, Rev 20:3, Rev 20:10

    Relevance: The ballo arc constitutes a micro-structural proof of linear reading within the macro-structure: Satan is cast from heaven (12:9) → is cast into the abyss (20:3) → is cast into the lake of fire (20:10). These three events cannot be the same event viewed from different angles — they are sequential stages of Satan's imprisonment. The arc also connects the 8:5 censer-casting (hinge between seal and trumpet sequences) to the larger ballo pattern, tying the sanctuary transition scene to the Satan-binding narrative.

    G2064 — ἔρχομαι (erchomai)¶

    G5064 — τέσσαρες (tessares)¶

    Occurrences: 42

    42 NT occurrences, 30 in Revelation (71% — highest concentration of any NT book). Tessares represents creation/universality in Revelation's numerical symbolism. Four living creatures mediate the 4→7 interaction (distribute first four seals, distribute seven bowls at 15:7). Fourfold ethnic formula (phyle/glossa/laos/ethnos): exactly 7 occurrences (creating 4×7=28 total terms). 4+3 division of judgment sequences (explicit in triple ouai at Rev 8:13): first four target creation/ecology; last three target humanity.

    Semantic categories: Creation/universality number, Four living creatures as mediators, Fourfold ethnic formula

    Key verses: Rev 4:6, Rev 5:9, Rev 6:1, Rev 7:1, Rev 13:7, Rev 15:7

    Relevance: The 4+3 structural division of the trumpet sequence (marked by the triple ouai at 8:13) is the clearest example of tessares as a structural number: first four trumpets attack creation (earth/sea/rivers/sun), last three ('woe-trumpets') attack humanity. This same 4+3 pattern appears in the seal sequence (four horsemen distributed by four living creatures, then fifth/sixth seals target the spiritual and cosmic). The fourfold ethnic formula's exact 7 occurrences (7×4=28 terms) demonstrates the deliberate integration of the two key structural numbers (4 and 7) in Revelation's design.

Major heptads: seven churches (1:4), seven spirits (1:4), seven lampstands (1:12), seven seals, seven trumpets, seven bowls, seven beatitudes (G3107), seven ego eimi declarations, seven letters, seven hymnic units, seven overcomer promises, seven beatitudes.     Key verses: Rev 1:4, Rev 1:12, Rev 5:1, Rev 8:2, Rev 15:1, Rev 17:9

    Relevance: Heptadic saturation (55.1 per 100 verses, revs2-14) is the clearest structural signature of Revelation. The three major judgment sequences (7 seals/7 trumpets/7 bowls) are the primary macro-structural heptads, but the structural integration of hepta pervades every layer: the seven churches, seven beatitudes, seven ego eimi declarations, and seven hymnic scenes all operate as structural markers at different scales. The sealed thunders (Rev 10:4) are the only hidden heptad — a deliberate anomaly within a book built on disclosure.

    G3108 — μακαρισμός (makarismos)¶

    Occurrences: 3

    3 NT occurrences (all tokens). The abstract noun form of the makarios adjective. Occurrences: Rom 4:6 (David's pronouncement of blessedness for the forgiven), Rom 4:9 (the blessedness — does it come upon the circumcised?), Gal 4:15 (Where is then the blessedness you spoke of?). Zero occurrences in Revelation. As the abstract noun for 'blessedness/beatitude,' makarismos captures the concept of being-in-a-blessed-state rather than the declarative pronouncement (makarios). The seven beatitudes in Revelation use the adjective G3107 exclusively; G3108 does not appear.

    Semantic categories: Abstract blessedness concept, Pauline usage (Rom 4), Complement to makarios (G3107)

    Key verses: Rom 4:6, Rom 4:9, Gal 4:15

    Relevance: Makarismos (G3108) has no direct Revelation occurrences. Its relevance to revs2-26 is negative-confirmatory: Revelation's seven beatitudes use only makarios (G3107 adjective), never the abstract noun form. This confirms that the seven beatitudes function as direct pronouncements ('Blessed is he that...') rather than theological abstractions. The concept of blessedness is active and participatory in Revelation — addressed to specific groups at specific structural seam points.
