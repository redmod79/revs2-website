    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    SHORT STUDY: What does IC077 mean for readers? How do the three judgment sequences (seals, trumpets, bowls) relate? This synthesizes established Part 1 findings — NOT a re-investigation.

    G1096 — ginomai¶

    Source: word-studies/G1096-ginomai.md

    Occurrences: 702

    631 NT occurrences. Primary verb of becoming/happening. The ginomai arc (SP048/TM197) provides Revelation's temporal backbone: egeneto (proleptic aorist, 11:15) announces the kingdom as accomplished; gegonen (perfect singular, 16:17) declares judgment completed with permanent state; gegonan (perfect plural, 21:6) extends completion to all things made new. Aspectual escalation from aorist through perfect singular to perfect plural.

    Semantic categories: Narrative transition, Happening/occurring, Becoming/change of state, Being made/existence, Emphatic denial (me genoito)

    Key verses: Rev 11:15, Rev 16:17, Rev 21:6, Rev 6:12, John 1:3

    Relevance: Core of the ginomai arc (SP048/TM197): the three-point aspectual escalation from egeneto through gegonen to gegonan constitutes Revelation's grammatical backbone across the judgment sequences

    G3709 — orge¶

    Source: word-studies/G3709-orge.md

    Occurrences: 36

    36 NT occurrences (6 in Revelation). Settled judicial wrath/displeasure, distinguished from thumos (passionate fury). The elthen he orge formula appears identically at Rev 6:17 and 11:18 (VP174), providing overlapping eschatological orientation evidence — both seal and trumpet endpoints use the same wrath-arrival language.

    Semantic categories: Divine eschatological wrath, Divine judicial wrath, Wrath and the law, Deliverance from wrath, Human anger as vice

    Key verses: Rev 6:16, Rev 6:17, Rev 11:18, Rev 16:19, Rev 19:15

    Relevance: VP174 identical elthen he orge at Rev 6:17 and 11:18; combined with thumos in bowl judgments (16:19, 19:15) for maximal intensity

    G5357 — phiale¶

    Source: word-studies/G5357-phiale.md

    Occurrences: 12

    12 NT occurrences, ALL in Revelation. Broad shallow bowl (vial). Central to vessel transformation arc (SP119): golden bowls hold incense-prayers at 5:8 (phialas chrysas gemousas thymiamaton) then wrath at 15:7 (phialas chrysas gemousas tou thymou tou theou). Identical grammatical construction with content reversal. OT source: mizraq (H4219) sanctuary sprinkling bowl.

    Semantic categories: Golden vials of incense-prayers, Seven golden vials of wrath, Poured-out vials (plagues), Vial-bearing angels as guides

    Key verses: Rev 5:8, Rev 15:7, Rev 16:1, Rev 16:17, Rev 17:1, Rev 21:9

    Relevance: Central to vessel transformation arc (SP119): the same bowls that hold prayers become vessels of wrath, demonstrating judgment as consequence of rejected intercession

    G4536 — salpinx¶

    Source: word-studies/G4536-salpinx.md

    Occurrences: 11

    11 NT occurrences (6 in Revelation). Trumpet instrument. OT background: conflates showphar (ram's horn, covenantal/eschatological) and chatsotsrah (silver priestly trumpet). The trumpet's primary function is WARNING and ALARM (Joel 2:1, Num 10:9), underlying the trumpet sequence's purpose as divine warnings calling for repentance.

    Semantic categories: Eschatological gathering/second coming, Resurrection signal, Voice of God/divine communication, Seven apocalyptic trumpets

    Key verses: Rev 8:2, Rev 8:6, 1 Cor 15:52, Matt 24:31, 1 Thess 4:16

    Relevance: Structural marker for the middle judgment sequence; OT warning-alarm function (Joel 2:1, Num 10:1-10) underlying the trumpet sequence's warning purpose

    G3340 — metanoeo¶

    Source: word-studies/G3340-metanoeo.md

    Occurrences: 36

    32 unique verses (10 in Revelation — highest concentration). Repent: cognitive/volitional re-orientation. Negated twice at Rev 9:20-21 (ou metanoesan), marking the trumpet sequence's failed warning. In bowls, metanoeo appears at 16:9 and 16:11 (both negated with blasphemy added), then disappears entirely at 16:21 where only blasphemy remains. This impenitence escalation (SP056) shows progressive character fixation.

    Semantic categories: Kingdom proclamation, Universal divine command, Response to preaching, Seven Churches urgency, Hardened refusal under judgment

    Key verses: Rev 9:20, Rev 9:21, Rev 16:9, Rev 16:11, Rev 2:5, Rev 3:19

    Relevance: Negated twice at Rev 9:20-21 marking the trumpet's failed warning; disappears after 16:11 showing character fixation in the bowl sequence

    G1325 — didomi¶

    Source: word-studies/G1325-didomi.md

    Occurrences: 430

    377 unique verses (49 in Revelation — highest density at 12.1 per 100 verses). The aorist passive edothe (V-API-3S) is the divine passive signature in Revelation: God grants authority to horsemen (6:2,4,8), abyss key (9:1), locust power (9:3), beast's 42-month mandate (13:5), and the angel's incense (8:3). All judgment agents act only within divinely granted scope.

    Semantic categories: Material giving, Delegated authority/power, God's gifts, Father giving through Son, Self-giving/sacrifice, Divine passive permission

    Key verses: Rev 6:2, Rev 6:4, Rev 6:8, Rev 8:3, Rev 9:1, Rev 13:5, Rev 13:7

    Relevance: Divine passive edothe analysis in seal and trumpet sequences: all judgment unfolds under divine permission, not autonomous power

    G5055 — teleo¶

    Source: word-studies/G5055-teleo.md

    Occurrences: 30

    26 unique verses (8 in Revelation). Complete/finish. The etelesthe verbal bridge (VP084) connects 10:7 ('mystery finished') and 15:1 ('wrath filled up'). Also 15:8 achri telesthosin ('until the seven plagues were fulfilled'). Links the 7th trumpet completion to the bowl sequence initiation.

    Semantic categories: Narrative closure, Fulfillment of Scripture, It is finished (cross), Fulfilling the law, Eschatological completion

    Key verses: Rev 10:7, Rev 15:1, Rev 15:8, Rev 20:3, Rev 20:7, John 19:30

    Relevance: etelesthe verbal bridge at 10:7 and 15:1 connecting 7th trumpet to bowl sequence; achri telesthosin at 15:8 matching Lev 16:17's 'until' clause

    G4127 — plege¶

    Source: word-studies/G4127-plege.md

    Occurrences: 21

    21 NT occurrences (14 in Revelation — 64%). Plague/stroke/wound. Used of the bowl judgments (Rev 15:1,6,8; 16:9,21; 22:18), connecting Revelation's final judgments to the Exodus plague tradition. The bowls are called 'the seven last plagues' (eschatas plegas, 15:1) — the only sequence explicitly marked as final.

    Semantic categories: Physical blows/stripes, Apostolic suffering, Beast's deadly wound, Seven last plagues, Divine retribution/warning

    Key verses: Rev 15:1, Rev 15:6, Rev 15:8, Rev 16:9, Rev 16:21, Rev 22:18

    Relevance: Bowl judgments explicitly designated as 'plagues' connecting to Exodus tradition; eschatas plegas at 15:1 marks bowls as terminally final

    G3485 — naos¶

    Source: word-studies/G3485-naos.md

    Occurrences: 46

    40 NT occurrences (16 in Revelation — 37%). Inner sanctuary/temple. Critical for DOA hinge: naos OPENED at 11:19 (ark visible, VP082) then naos FILLED with smoke at 15:8 (access barred). The open/close contrast marks the mercy-to-execution transition. Distinguished from hieron (temple precinct): naos = inner sanctuary where deity dwells.

    Semantic categories: Physical inner sanctuary, Christ's body as temple, Believers as God's dwelling, Heavenly temple, Eschatological/prophetic temple

    Key verses: Rev 11:19, Rev 15:5, Rev 15:8, Rev 21:22, Rev 7:15, Rev 11:1

    Relevance: DOA hinge vocabulary: naos opened at 11:19 (ark visible) vs. naos barred at 15:8 (smoke filling); states that cannot coexist prove these are not the same event

    G796 — astrape¶

    Source: word-studies/G796-astrapē.md

    Occurrences: 9

    9 NT occurrences (4 in Revelation). Lightning. Appears EXCLUSIVELY in the NT at the four theophany passages: Rev 4:5, 8:5, 11:19, 16:18 — and nowhere else in Revelation or the NT epistles. This exclusive distribution makes astrape one of the most robust structural markers linking the judgment sequences (TM081).

    Semantic categories: Theophanic manifestation, Suddenness of Christ's coming, Supernatural radiance

    Key verses: Rev 4:5, Rev 8:5, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:18

    Relevance: Exclusive NT distribution at four theophany passages makes it one of the strongest structural markers linking the judgment sequences

    G4578 — seismos¶

    Source: word-studies/G4578-seismos.md

    Occurrences: 14

    12 unique verses (5 in Revelation). Earthquake. Added at the second theophany station (Rev 8:5) and escalated to 'unprecedented since men were upon the earth' at the fourth (Rev 16:18). Part of the theophanic tetrad (seismos + astrape + bronte + phone). Marks structural junctions between judgment sequences.

    Semantic categories: Physical sea storm, Eschatological warning signs, Earthquake at the Passion, Divine deliverance, Apocalyptic theophany

    Key verses: Rev 6:12, Rev 8:5, Rev 11:13, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:18

    Relevance: Added at 2nd theophany station (8:5), escalated to superlative at 4th (16:18); marks structural junctions

    G2078 — eschatos¶

    Source: word-studies/G2078-éschatos.md

    Occurrences: 54

    54 NT occurrences (7 in Revelation). Last/final. The bowls are called eschatas plagues (Rev 15:1, 'the seven last plagues') — the only sequence explicitly marked as terminally final. Also used as divine self-designation 'the first and the last' (Rev 1:17, 2:8, 22:13). Superlative adjective: farthest, final.

    Semantic categories: Divine self-designation (First and Last), The last day (resurrection), The last days/times, Social reversal (last shall be first), Eschatological finality (last events)

    Key verses: Rev 15:1, Rev 21:9, Rev 1:17, Rev 22:13, 1 Cor 15:52

    Relevance: The bowls are the only sequence explicitly designated as 'last' (eschatas), supporting the linear/escalating component of IC077

    G3031 — libanotos¶

    Source: word-studies/G3031-libanotos.md

    Occurrences: 2

    2 NT occurrences, both in Rev 8:3-5. Censer/frankincense. The same vessel carries prayers upward (intercession, 8:3) then fire downward (judgment, 8:5). Its ABSENCE from the bowl prelude (Rev 15:5-8) confirms the cessation of intercession — the censer that mediated prayers never appears again after the trumpet introduction.

    Semantic categories: Heavenly censer — offering incense with prayers, Heavenly censer — filled with fire and cast to earth

    Key verses: Rev 8:3, Rev 8:5

    Relevance: Only 2 NT occurrences both in Rev 8; absence from bowl prelude confirms cessation of intercession after DOA hinge

    G2379 — thysiastērion¶

    Source: word-studies/G2379-thysiastērion.md

    Occurrences: 23

    23 NT occurrences (8 in Revelation). Altar. Central to the altar vindication arc (SP037): passive at 6:9, mediating at 8:3-5, commanding at 9:13, authoritative at 14:18, personified speaking at 16:7 (masculine participle legontos with neuter noun — grammatical personification), resolved at 19:2. Threads across ALL three sequences.

    Semantic categories: Temple altar of sacrifice, Altar of incense, Scene of bloodshed/martyrdom, Oath and sanctification, Heavenly/eschatological altar, Christological/theological

    Key verses: Rev 6:9, Rev 8:3, Rev 8:5, Rev 9:13, Rev 14:18, Rev 16:7

    Relevance: Altar vindication arc (SP037) threads across all three judgment sequences with the same vocabulary and theological question, providing one of the strongest structural arguments for unified narrative

    G5464 — chalaza¶

    Source: word-studies/G5464-chalaza.md

    Occurrences: 4

    4 tokens in 3 unique verses, all in Revelation. Hail. Added at theophany station 3 (Rev 11:19 'great hail') and escalated to superlative at station 4 (Rev 16:21 talent-weight hail). At 16:21, humanity blasphemes God 'because of the plague of the hail' — blasphemy replacing any possibility of repentance.

    Semantic categories: Hail as theophanic judgment (trumpet plague), Hail as extreme divine wrath (bowl plague)

    Key verses: Rev 8:7, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:21

    Relevance: Added at 3rd theophany station (11:19), superlative at 4th (16:21); theophany formula escalation evidence

    G2372 — thumos¶

    Source: word-studies/G2372-thumos.md

    Occurrences: 18

    18 NT occurrences (10 in Revelation — 56%). Passionate fury. Distinguished from orge (settled judicial wrath) as the more volatile, boiling eruption. Concentrated in bowl-judgment wrath vocabulary. Phonetic near-identity with thymiama (incense) reinforces the vessel transformation arc: the bowls that held thymiama now hold thymos.

    Semantic categories: Divine eschatological fury, Corrupting/intoxicating passion (Babylon's wine), Satanic rage, Human passionate outburst, Vice/work of the flesh

    Key verses: Rev 14:10, Rev 15:1, Rev 15:7, Rev 16:1, Rev 16:19, Rev 19:15

    Relevance: Bowl-judgment wrath vocabulary; phonetic near-identity thymiama/thymos reinforces vessel transformation arc where worship vessels become judgment vessels

    G2368 — thymiama¶

    Source: word-studies/G2368-thymiama.md

    Occurrences: 6

    6 NT verses (4 in Revelation). Incense. Explicitly equated with prayers of saints at Rev 5:8 and offered WITH prayers at 8:3-4. Phonetic near-identity with thymos (wrath) in Greek reinforces that judgment is not arbitrary violence but the judicial consequence of rejected intercession.

    Semantic categories: Temple liturgy — hour of incense, Heavenly worship — incense as prayers, Commercial commodity

    Key verses: Rev 5:8, Rev 8:3, Rev 8:4, Luke 1:10

    Relevance: Incense = prayers of saints; phonetic reversal thymiama (incense/prayers) -> thymos (wrath) marks the vessel transformation arc's theological logic

    G455 — anoigo¶

    Source: word-studies/G455-anoigo.md

    Occurrences: 78

    78 tokens in 75 verses (25 in Revelation — 33%). Open. VP082: shared naos-opening vocabulary at 11:19 and 15:5 (anoigo + naos). The verb connects the seal-opening sequence (Rev 5-8, Lamb as agent) with the sanctuary transitions that link judgment sequences.

    Semantic categories: Opening sealed documents/scrolls, Opening eyes, Opening heaven/cosmic access, Opening doors/gates, Opening the mouth, Opening earth/graves/temple/abyss

    Key verses: Rev 5:2, Rev 6:1, Rev 8:1, Rev 11:19, Rev 15:5, Rev 19:11

    Relevance: VP082 shared naos-opening formula at 11:19 and 15:5; structural marker for sanctuary transitions between judgment sequences

    G1027 — bronte¶

    Source: word-studies/G1027-bronte.md

    Occurrences: 12

    11 unique verses (9 in Revelation — 82%). Thunder. Part of the theophanic tetrad formula with astrape, phone, and seismos. Appears at all four theophany stations (Rev 4:5, 8:5, 11:19, 16:18). Always paired with astrape and phone.

    Semantic categories: Throne-room theophanic formula, Seven thunders (personified/sealed), Thunder as voice of majesty, Epithet (Boanerges)

    Key verses: Rev 4:5, Rev 8:5, Rev 11:19, Rev 16:18, Rev 10:3-4

    Relevance: Theophany formula distribution; part of the escalating structural marker at each judgment sequence juncture

    G4442 — pyr¶

    Source: word-studies/G4442-pyr.md

    Occurrences: 74

    73 unique verses (25 in Revelation — 34%). Fire. Central to the censer reversal at Rev 8:5 (fire from altar cast to earth). Fire appears in judgment imagery throughout all three sequences.

    Semantic categories: Eschatological punishment, Judgment parables, Divine theophany, Cosmic/apocalyptic plagues, Testing/refining/purifying

    Key verses: Rev 8:5, Rev 8:7, Rev 9:17-18, Rev 14:10, Rev 20:14-15

    Relevance: Censer reversal at 8:5 (fire cast to earth); fire imagery threads through all three judgment sequences

    G2246 — helios¶

    Source: word-studies/G2246-hḗlios.md

    Occurrences: 31

    Key verses: Rev 8:7, Rev 8:8, Rev 8:10, Rev 8:12, Rev 9:1, Rev 9:13, Rev 11:15

    Relevance: Verb form complementing salpinx; morphologically identical formula across all seven trumpets
