    Verses¶

    Question¶

    What does structural and OT-typological evidence identify about the beast of Revelation 13? Evaluate four models: individual eschatological figure, political-religious system, symbolic-universal, preterist.

    Daniel 7:3-7¶

    Daniel 7:3 And four great beasts came up from the sea, diverse one from another. Daniel 7:4 The first [was] like a lion, and had eagle's wings: I beheld till the wings thereof were plucked, and it was lifted up from the earth, and made stand upon the feet as a man, and a man's heart was given to it. Daniel 7:5 And behold another beast, a second, like to a bear, and it raised up itself on one side, and [it had] three ribs in the mouth of it between the teeth of it: and they said thus unto it, Arise, devour much flesh. Daniel 7:6 After this I beheld, and lo another, like a leopard, which had upon the back of it four wings of a fowl; the beast had also four heads; and dominion was given to it. Daniel 7:7 After this I saw in the night visions, and behold a fourth beast, dreadful and terrible, and strong exceedingly; and it had great iron teeth: it devoured and brake in pieces, and stamped the residue with the feet of it: and it [was] diverse from all the beasts that [were] before it; and it had ten horns.

    Notes: Source imagery for Rev 13:1-2 composite beast. Sequential beasts: lion (1 head, Babylon), bear (1 head, Medo-Persia), leopard with 4 heads (Greece), terrible beast with 10 horns (Rome). Cumulative heads: 1+1+4+1 = 7.

    Daniel 7:8¶

    Daniel 7:8 I considered the horns, and, behold, there came up among them another little horn, before whom there were three of the first horns plucked up by the roots: and, behold, in this horn [were] eyes like the eyes of man, and a mouth speaking great things.

    Notes: Little horn with 'mouth speaking great things' — LXX Theodotion renders as stoma laloun megala, the exact phrase reproduced verbatim in Rev 13:5. Proof of deliberate literary dependence (TM205).

    Daniel 7:17¶

    Daniel 7:17 These great beasts, which are four, [are] four kings, [which] shall arise out of the earth.

    Notes: EXPLICIT DECODER #1: beast = king/kingdom. The text's OWN interpretive key. Interchangeability of malkin (king) and malkuw (kingdom) reflects corporate solidarity in prophetic symbolism. Any interpretive model must account for this within-text explanation.

    Daniel 7:23¶

    Daniel 7:23 Thus he said, The fourth beast shall be the fourth kingdom upon earth, which shall be diverse from all kingdoms, and shall devour the whole earth, and shall tread it down, and break it in pieces.

    Notes: EXPLICIT DECODER #2: substitutes 'kingdom' (malkuw H4438) for 'king' (malkin H4430), confirming that beast = political power structure, not merely individual leader.

    Daniel 7:25¶

    Daniel 7:25 And he shall speak [great] words against the most High, and shall wear out the saints of the most High, and think to change times and laws: and they shall be given into his hand until a time and times and the dividing of time.

    • מִלִּין () — Noun.fp.Abs — word
    • לְצַד () — Prep + Noun.ms.Cst — to/against the side of
    • עליא () — Adj.ms — highest / Most High
    • יְמַלִּל () — Verb.pael.Impf.3ms — speak
    • קַדִּישֵׁי () — Adj.mp.Cst — holy ones
    • עֶלְיוֹנִין () — Adj.mp.Abs — Most High
    • יְבַלֵּא () — Verb.pael.Impf.3ms — wear out
    • יִסְבַּר () — Verb.peal.Impf.3ms — intend/think
    • הַשְׁנָיָה () — Verb.haf.InfCon — to change
    • זִמְנִין () — Noun.mp.Abs — times/seasons
    • דָת () — Noun.fs.Abs — law
    • יִתְיַהֲבוּן () — Verb.htpe.Impf.3mp — be given (passive)
    • בִּידֵהּ () — Prep + Noun.fs.Abs + 3ms suffix — into his hand
    • עִדָּן וְעִדָּנִין וּפְלַג עִדָּן () — Noun.ms + Noun.mp + Noun.ms.Cst + Noun.ms — a time and times and half a time

    Notes: Little horn profile embedded in Rev 13:5-7. Near point-for-point parallel: (1) speak against Most High = Rev 13:5 blasphemies; (2) wear out saints = Rev 13:7 war with saints; (3) given into his hand = Rev 13:5,7 edothe; (4) time-times-half = Rev 13:5 forty-two months. SP115 tripartite verbal chain: Dan 7:25 → 2 Thess 2:3-4 → Rev 13:5-7. The Aramaic yithyahabun (htpe.Impf.3mp = passive 'they shall be given') is the Aramaic antecedent of Greek edothe (divine passive).

    Exodus 15:11¶

    Exodus 15:11 Who [is] like unto thee, O LORD, among the gods? who [is] like thee, glorious in holiness, fearful [in] praises, doing wonders?

    Notes: Original incomparability formula: LXX tis homoios soi en theois Kyrie. Rev 13:4 reproduces the same Greek structure (tis homoios to therio) for a creature — blasphemous inversion of the Exodus worship hymn. The name Michael (mi-kha-el = 'who is like God?') directly answers this formula.

    Revelation 13:1-2¶

    Revelation 13:1 And I stood upon the sand of the sea, and saw a beast rise up out of the sea, having seven heads and ten horns, and upon his horns ten crowns, and upon his heads the name of blasphemy. Revelation 13:2 And the beast which I saw was like unto a leopard, and his feet were as [the feet] of a bear, and his mouth as the mouth of a lion: and the dragon gave him his power, and his seat, and great authority.

    Notes: Composite beast description: leopard body (Greece), bear feet (Medo-Persia), lion mouth (Babylon) — REVERSED order from Daniel (TM204). Dragon's threefold active delegation: dynamis + thronos + exousia megale. Seven heads = mathematical sum of Daniel's four beasts (1+1+4+1=7, TM206). Ten horns from Dan 7:7 fourth beast.

    Revelation 13:3-4¶

    Revelation 13:3 And I saw one of his heads as it were wounded to death; and his deadly wound was healed: and all the world wondered after the beast. Revelation 13:4 And they worshipped the dragon which gave power unto the beast: and they worshipped the beast, saying, Who [is] like unto the beast? who is able to make war with him?

    Notes: Death-wound parody: sphazo perfect passive pair — Rev 5:6 hos esphagmenon (neuter, Lamb) vs Rev 13:3 hos esphagmenen (feminine, head). Incomparability formula: 'Who is like unto the beast?' (tis homoios to therio) parodies Exod 15:11 'Who is like unto thee, O LORD?' Same Greek structure (tis homoios + dative) applied to creature = blasphemous inversion.

    Revelation 13:5-7¶

    Revelation 13:5 And there was given unto him a mouth speaking great things and blasphemies; and power was given unto him to continue forty [and] two months. Revelation 13:6 And he opened his mouth in blasphemy against God, to blaspheme his name, and his tabernacle, and them that dwell in heaven. Revelation 13:7 And it was given unto him to make war with the saints, and to overcome them: and power was given him over all kindreds, and tongues, and nations.

    Notes: The grammatical backbone of the passage. stoma laloun megala = verbatim LXX Dan 7:8 (TM205). edothe divine passive architecture: God permits, dragon grants. 42-month hostile-subject formula (SP079). Threefold blasphemy target (13:6): God's name, tabernacle (skene G4633 = Christ's heavenly ministry per Heb 8:2), heaven-dwellers. Dan 7:25 profile point-for-point: speak against Most High, wear out saints, given into hand, time-times-half.

    Revelation 13:8¶

    Revelation 13:8 And all that dwell upon the earth shall worship him, whose names are not written in the book of life of the Lamb slain from the foundation of the world.

    Notes: Universal scope of beast worship (pasa phyle kai glossa). Book of life of the Lamb slain from foundation of world — cosmic context. The Lamb's sacrificial death is eternal foundation; beast's wound-healing is temporal parody.

    Revelation 13:11-15¶

    Revelation 13:11 And I beheld another beast coming up out of the earth; and he had two horns like a lamb, and he spake as a dragon. Revelation 13:12 And he exerciseth all the power of the first beast before him, and causeth the earth and them which dwell therein to worship the first beast, whose deadly wound was healed. Revelation 13:13 And he doeth great wonders, so that he maketh fire come down from heaven on the earth in the sight of men, Revelation 13:14 And deceiveth them that dwell on the earth by [the means of] those miracles which he had power to do in the sight of the beast; saying to them that dwell on the earth, that they should make an image to the beast, which had the wound by a sword, and did live. Revelation 13:15 And he had power to give life unto the image of the beast, that the image of the beast should both speak, and cause that as many as would not worship the image of the beast should be killed.

    Notes: Second beast (false prophet) as Spirit-parody. Six documented Spirit-function parallels: (1) lamb-like appearance vs dragon speech; (2) fire from heaven (counterfeiting Elijah/Pentecost); (3) pneuma to image (counterfeiting Spirit's life-giving); (4) directing worship to another (counterfeiting Spirit glorifying Christ per John 16:14); (5) marking followers (counterfeiting Spirit's sealing per Eph 1:13); (6) sign-working for authentication. arnion anomaly: 29/30 Christological uses, sole exception here. elalei imperfect = habitual dragon speech.

    Revelation 13:16-18¶

    Revelation 13:16 And he causeth all, both small and great, rich and poor, free and bond, to receive a mark in their right hand, or in their foreheads: Revelation 13:17 And that no man might buy or sell, save he that had the mark, or the name of the beast, or the number of his name. Revelation 13:18 Here is wisdom. Let him that hath understanding count the number of the beast: for it is the number of a man; and his number [is] Six hundred threescore [and] six.

    Notes: Charagma (G5480) on forehead/hand contrasts sphragis (G4973) seal in Rev 7:3. Inverts Deut 6:6-8 loyalty pattern. 666 as parody of divine triple completeness (IC184). Anarthrous anthropou = qualitative 'human-ness' rather than specific individual identification. Economic coercion (buy/sell) as enforcement mechanism.

    Revelation 5:6¶

    Revelation 5:6 And I beheld, and, lo, in the midst of the throne and of the four beasts, and in the midst of the elders, stood a Lamb as it had been slain, having seven horns and seven eyes, which are the seven Spirits of God sent forth into all the earth.

    Notes: The Christological original for Rev 13:3 parody. Same verb (sphazo G4969), same hos construction, same perfect passive aspect — but neuter gender (matching arnion) vs feminine gender in 13:3 (matching kephale). Lamb's wound is permanent redemptive identity; beast's wound was healed (etherapeuthē = divine passive). Seven horns = complete power vs beast's ten horns = derivative power.

    Revelation 12:3¶

    Revelation 12:3 And there appeared another wonder in heaven; and behold a great red dragon, having seven heads and ten horns, and seven crowns upon his heads.

    Notes: Dragon with seven heads, ten horns, seven diadems ON HEADS. Same shared anatomy as beast (13:1) — but diadem distribution shifts: dragon has 7 on heads, beast has 10 on horns. Diadema arc: dragon 7 on heads → beast 10 on horns → Christ polla ('many') on head (19:12). Parody escalates in counted quantities; divine transcends counting.

    Revelation 17:8-11¶

    Revelation 17:8 The beast that thou sawest was, and is not; and shall ascend out of the bottomless pit, and go into perdition: and they that dwell on the earth shall wonder, whose names were not written in the book of life from the foundation of the world, when they behold the beast that was, and is not, and yet is. Revelation 17:9 And here [is] the mind which hath wisdom. The seven heads are seven mountains, on which the woman sitteth. Revelation 17:10 And there are seven kings: five are fallen, and one is, [and] the other is not yet come; and when he cometh, he must continue a short space. Revelation 17:11 And the beast that was, and is not, even he is the eighth, and is of the seven, and goeth into perdition.

    Notes: Inverted temporal formula: divine (is-was-is coming, Rev 1:4) vs beast (was-is not-shall come up from abyss). Beast cannot sustain the divine present tense (ouk estin). Triple identification: heads = mountains = kings (17:9-10). Five fallen, one is, one not yet come. Eighth king ek ton hepta = qualitative transformation. Beast's formula includes its own destruction (goes to perdition) — divine formula has no end clause.

    2 Thessalonians 2:3-4¶

    2 Thessalonians 2:3 Let no man deceive you by any means: for [that day shall not come], except there come a falling away first, and that man of sin be revealed, the son of perdition; 2 Thessalonians 2:4 Who opposeth and exalteth himself above all that is called God, or that is worshipped; so that he as God sitteth in the temple of God, shewing himself that he is God.

    Notes: SP115 tripartite verbal chain: Dan 7:25 → 2 Thess 2:3-4 → Rev 13:5-7. Paul condenses the little horn profile into a single figure (man of lawlessness). John distributes the same profile across TWO figures: sea beast (political blasphemer, Rev 13:1-8) and earth beast (sign-working deceiver, Rev 13:11-15). Present participles antikeimenos and hyperairomenos indicate habitual, ongoing opposition and self-exaltation — consistent with a system rather than a momentary individual act.

    2 Thessalonians 2:9-10¶

    2 Thessalonians 2:9 [Even him], whose coming is after the working of Satan with all power and signs and lying wonders, 2 Thessalonians 2:10 And with all deceivableness of unrighteousness in them that perish; because they received not the love of the truth, that they might be saved.

    Notes: Parousia of the lawless one kata energeian tou Satana — 'according to the working of Satan.' With all power (dynamis), signs (semeia), and lying wonders (terata pseudous). Parallels false prophet's sign-working in Rev 13:13-14. The Satanic source matches Rev 13:2 dragon delegation.

    Revelation 14:7¶
