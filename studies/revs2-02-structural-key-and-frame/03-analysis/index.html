    Verse-by-Verse Analysis — Structural Key and Frame (revs2-02)¶

    Verse-by-Verse Analysis¶

    Rev 1:1¶

    Context: Opening verse of Revelation. John introduces the book's origin and transmission chain: God gave the revelation to Jesus Christ, who sent and signified (esemainen, from semaino G4591) it through his angel to his servant John. Direct statement: Establishes the transmission chain (God -> Christ -> angel -> John -> servants) and the Daniel allusion ha dei genesthai en tachei ("things which must shortly come to pass"). The verb esemainen signals that the mode of communication is through signs/symbols. Original language: Parser confirms: apokalypsis (G602, N-NSF), deixai (G1166, V-AAN, aorist infinitive "to show"), dei (G1163, V-PAI-3S), genesthai (G1096, V-2ADN, aorist middle infinitive), en tachei (G5034, N-DSN). The five-word phrase ha dei genesthai en tachei reappears verbatim at 22:6. Cross-references: Dan 2:28 LXX provides the source formula: ha dei genesthai ep' eschatwn ton hemeron ("in the latter days"). Revelation transforms "in the latter days" to "en tachei" (shortly). Rev 22:6 echoes the transmission chain and verbatim formula, creating the strongest inclusio element. Relationship to other evidence: Anchors the ha dei genesthai chain (1:1 -> 4:1 -> 22:6) and the transmission chain inclusio (1:1 / 22:6 / 22:16). Establishes the mode of communication (signs) that supports the epexegetical reading of 1:19's eisin.

    Rev 1:2¶

    Context: John's credential statement — he "bare record" of the word of God, the testimony of Jesus Christ, and all things he saw. Direct statement: Introduces the martyreo/martyria vocabulary chain. John emartyesen (aorist of martyreo G3140) the word of God and the martyria (G3141) of Jesus Christ. Original language: The aorist eiden ("he saw," from horao G3708) parallels the aorist eides in 1:19. Three objects of testimony: word of God, testimony of Jesus, all things seen. Cross-references: The witness vocabulary returns at 22:16 (martyresai), 22:18 (summartyro), 22:20 (ho martyron). Rev 19:10 provides the defining statement: "the testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy." Relationship to other evidence: Part of TM022 (witness chain spanning prologue-epilogue). The shared vocabulary creates a Moderate VP between 1:2 and the epilogue witness references.

    Rev 1:3¶

    Context: First of seven makarios beatitudes in Revelation. Pronounces blessing on the reader, hearers, and keepers of "the words of this prophecy." Direct statement: Three present participles: ho anaginoskon (reading), hoi akouontes (hearing), terountes (keeping). Concludes with ho kairos eggys ("the time is at hand," kairos G2540). Original language: Parser: makarios (G3107, A-NSM), kairos (G2540, N-NSM), eggys (G1451, ADV). The phrase logous tes propheteias ("words of the prophecy") recurs at 22:7, 22:10, 22:18. Cross-references: Rev 22:7 echoes: makarios + teron (keeping) + logous tes propheteias = 3 shared elements (Moderate VP). Rev 22:10 echoes ho kairos eggys (TM017). The 1:3/22:10 kairos echo creates an inclusio element asserting prophetic imminence at both ends. Relationship to other evidence: Establishes the sevenfold beatitude pattern (TM019). Three of seven makarios occurrences fall in the prologue-epilogue frame (1:3, 22:7, 22:14).

    Rev 1:4¶

    Context: Epistolary greeting from John to the seven churches in Asia. Direct statement: Charis hymin kai eirene ("Grace to you and peace") matching Pauline convention. Contains the temporal divine title: ho on kai ho en kai ho erchomenos — "which is, and which was, and which is to come." Mentions the seven Spirits (hepta pneumaton G4151). Original language: Parser: charis (G5485, N-NSF), on (G1510, V-PAP-NSM, present participle), en (G1510, V-IAI-3S, imperfect), erchomenos (G2064, V-PNP-NSM, present middle participle). The order is present-past-future. Cross-references: The charis at 22:21 creates the epistolary frame (TM021, SP010). The temporal title reappears at 1:8 (same order), 4:8 (reversed to past-present-future), 11:17 (possibly shortened), 16:5 (variant). The seven Spirits connect to the en pneumati formula (1:10, 4:2, 17:3, 21:10). Relationship to other evidence: The temporal title's appearance in 1:4 and its recurrence in characterological contexts (4:8 living creatures, 11:17 praise, 17:8 beast parody) supports reading it as a characterological device rather than a structural organizer.

    Rev 1:5-6¶

    Context: Christ described with three titles: ho martys ho pistos (the faithful witness, martys G3144), ho prototokos ton nekron (firstborn of the dead), ho archon ton basileon tes ges (ruler of the kings of the earth). Followed by doxology. Direct statement: Establishes Christ as martys (G3144) — the word appears 5 times in Revelation (1:5, 2:13, 3:14, 11:3, 17:6). The "faithful witness" title reappears at 3:14. Original language: Martys (G3144, 34 NT occurrences). The title "faithful witness" connects to the "faithful and true" (pistos kai alethinos) chain: 1:5 -> 3:14 -> 19:11 -> 22:6. Cross-references: The martys vocabulary is part of TM022. The doxology format does not recur in the epilogue, making this a prologue-only element. Relationship to other evidence: Contributes to the witness vocabulary chain but is not directly paralleled in the epilogue. The "faithful and true" thread spans the entire book.

    Rev 1:7¶

    Context: Prophetic oracle: "Behold, he cometh with clouds." Composite allusion to Dan 7:13 + Zech 12:10. Direct statement: Erchetai (present tense, 3rd person, from erchomai G2064) — "he comes." The parallels tool confirms Dan 7:13 as the top OT parallel (0.411 hybrid score). Original language: The erchomai is 3rd person here. In the epilogue, it shifts to 1st person: erchomai (22:7, 12, 20, "I come quickly"). The person-shift from 3rd to 1st is documented as a structural feature of the prologue-epilogue frame. Cross-references: Dan 7:13 ("one like the Son of man came with the clouds of heaven") + Zech 12:10 ("they shall look upon me whom they have pierced"). The composite allusion creates a Strong AN (two sources, 3+ shared elements with shared context). Relationship to other evidence: The erchomai person-shift is part of the prologue-epilogue inclusio (VP for coming language). The 1:7 oracle sets up the eschatological expectation that the epilogue intensifies through the triple tachy (22:7, 12, 20).

    Rev 1:8¶

    Context: Alpha and Omega self-declaration by "the Lord God." Direct statement: First Alpha-Omega occurrence: ego eimi to Alpha kai to O. Includes the temporal title (ho on kai ho en kai ho erchomenos) and ho Pantokrator. Original language: Parser: Alpha (G1, N-LI), O (G5598, N-LI), on (V-PAP-NSM), en (V-IAI-3S), erchomenos (V-PNP-NSM), Pantokrator (G3841, N-NSM). Cross-references: Rev 22:13 provides the maximum expansion: Alpha/Omega + first/last (protos G4413, eschatos G2078) + beginning/end (arche G746, telos G5056). The OT source for "first and last" is Isa 41:4, 44:6, 48:12. Relationship to other evidence: Anchors the Alpha-Omega expansion pattern (TM018). The expansion from 1:8 (partial) to 22:13 (complete) reinforces the prologue-epilogue frame by placing the climactic title at the end.

    Rev 1:9-10¶

    Context: John's self-identification and the first en pneumati marker. Direct statement: "I John" (ego Ioannes) on Patmos. Egenomyn en pneumati (V-2ADI-1S from ginomai G1096) — "I became in the Spirit." Heard a great voice "as of a trumpet" (hos salpingos). Original language: The ginomai verb with John as subject characterizes the first two en pneumati markers (1:10, 4:2). At 17:3 and 21:10 the verb shifts to apophero (G667) with the angel as subject. Cross-references: Rev 22:8 echoes "I John" (kago Ioannes), creating a personal-frame inclusio. The trumpet voice (salpingos) reappears at 4:1 ("the first voice which I heard was as it were of a trumpet"), linking the two passages. Relationship to other evidence: Establishes Vision 1 of the fourfold en pneumati division. The phrase "for the word of God and for the testimony of Jesus Christ" uses the same martyria vocabulary from 1:2 (TM022).

    Rev 1:11¶

    Context: First graphon command: "write in a book, and send it unto the seven churches." Direct statement: The KJV/TR includes "I am Alpha and Omega, the first and the last" — but the text comparison confirms this is a TR addition absent from the N1904/critical text (31 words in N1904 vs. 36 in TR). The graphon (V-AAM-2S, aorist imperative) begins the 12-occurrence write-command series. Original language: The textual variant is significant: if Alpha-Omega at 1:11 is original, there are 4 occurrences; if it is a TR addition, there are 3 (1:8, 21:6, 22:13). The expansion pattern works with either count. Cross-references: Rev 1:19 provides the second general write command. The seven church-specific commands follow in 2:1-3:14. The distribution continues at 10:4 (prohibition), 14:13, and 21:5 (TM014). Relationship to other evidence: The write-command distribution spanning all four visions is evidence that John's writing activity covers the entire visionary experience, not just one section.

    Rev 1:12-18 (Christophany)¶

    Context: The vision that constitutes "things which thou hast seen" (ha eides) under the threefold reading. Direct statement: Christ among the lampstands, holding seven stars. Description draws from Dan 7:9,13 (white hair, Son of man) and Dan 10:5-6 (golden girdle). "I am the first and the last" (ho protos kai ho eschatos, 1:17) — first occurrence of this Isaiah-derived title. Original language: Protos (G4413) and eschatos (G2078) at 1:17 appropriate the YHWH title from Isa 41:4, 44:6, 48:12. The title expands through 2:8, 21:6, and reaches maximum at 22:13. Cross-references: Christ's countenance "as the sun shineth in his strength" (1:16) creates a thematic parallel with 21:23 ("the city had no need of the sun... the Lamb is the light thereof"). This is thematic rather than lexical — different Greek words are used. Relationship to other evidence: Under the threefold scheme, this is the referent of "things you saw." Under the epexegetical reading of 1:19, the Christophany is what John saw, 1:20 explains what it means, and everything from 4:1 onward is what is about to happen.

    Rev 1:19¶

    Context: THE crux verse. Christ's commission to John following the Christophany. Direct statement: Three clauses: ha eides (aorist — things seen), ha eisin (present — things which are/what they mean), ha mellei genesthai meta tauta (present + aorist infinitive — things about to happen after these things). Original language: Parser confirms: grapson (G1125, V-AAM-2S), eides (G3708, V-2AAI-2S), eisin (G1510, V-PAI-3P), mellei (G3195, V-PAI-3S), genesthai (G1096, V-2ADN), meta tauta (G3326+G3778). The oun ("therefore") connects the writing command to the preceding Christophany — write because of what you have seen. Cross-references: Rev 4:1 uses ha dei genesthai meta tauta — blending the dei from 1:1 with the meta tauta from 1:19. Rev 1:20 immediately uses both eides and eisin in its mystery explanation. Dan 2:28 provides the source formula. Relationship to other evidence: The verse is consistent with multiple readings: (1) threefold structural outline (ch.1/chs.2-3/chs.4-22), (2) twofold commission with epexegetical middle clause, (3) general content-scope description covering the entire visionary experience. The text does not contain a disambiguation marker.

    Rev 1:20¶

    Context: Immediate explanation of the Christophany's symbols. Direct statement: The mysterion (G3466) of the stars and lampstands is decoded. Uses eides (same form as 1:19) and eisin (same form as 1:19) in the identifications: "the seven stars ARE (eisin) the angels... the seven candlesticks ARE (eisin) the seven churches." Original language: Eisin appears twice as V-PAI-3P — both times as a copula of identification, not a temporal marker. The stars ARE (represent) the angels; the lampstands ARE (represent) the churches. Cross-references: The eisin-as-identification pattern continues at 4:5, 5:8, 17:9, 17:12, 17:15. Relationship to other evidence: Strongest evidence for the epexegetical reading of 1:19's ha eisin. If 1:20 fulfills the "what they are" clause of 1:19 by explaining the mystery, then ha eisin means "what these things signify" rather than "things in the present era."

    Rev 4:1¶

    Context: Major structural transition — the strongest boundary in Revelation. Direct statement: Meta tauta eidon + "the first voice" (he phone he prote, protos G4413) echoing 1:10's trumpet voice + "I will shew thee things which must be hereafter" (deixo soi ha dei genesthai meta tauta, using dei G1163 + genesthai G1096). Original language: Parser: meta tauta eidon (G3326+G3778+G3708), prote (G4413, A-NSF-S), deixo (G1166, V-FAI-1S, future), dei (G1163, V-PAI-3S), genesthai (G1096, V-2ADN). The 4:1 formula blends 1:1's dei genesthai with 1:19's meta tauta. Cross-references: This is the ONLY point where meta tauta eidon and en pneumati (4:2) co-occur at the same boundary. The phrase "the first voice which I heard" (he phone he prote hen ekousa) uses protos to link explicitly to 1:10's trumpet voice. Relationship to other evidence: Every structural model recognizes this transition. Under the threefold reading, it begins "things hereafter." Under the fourfold reading, it begins Vision 2. Under Bauckham's single-vision reading, it is an internal transition. The markers converge but their interpretation diverges.

    Rev 4:2¶

    Context: Second en pneumati marker. Direct statement: Eutheos egenomyn en pneumati — "immediately I was in the Spirit." Same verb form (egenomyn, V-2ADI-1S from ginomai G1096) as 1:10. Throne-room vision begins. Original language: The eutheos (G2112, "immediately") adds urgency absent from 1:10. The ginomai verb with John as subject matches 1:10, distinguishing the first two markers from the apophero pair at 17:3 and 21:10. Cross-references: This is the only verse where meta tauta (from 4:1) and en pneumati co-occur at the same boundary, making it the strongest structural marker convergence in Revelation. Relationship to other evidence: Begins Vision 2 in the fourfold system. Under the threefold system, begins "things hereafter." The convergence of both marker systems at a single boundary confirms its structural weight.

    Rev 4:8¶

    Context: Living creatures' Trisagion with temporal title. Direct statement: "Holy, holy, holy, Lord God Almighty, which was (ho en), and is (ho on), and is to come (ho erchomenos)." Order differs from 1:4,8: past-present-future rather than present-past-future. Original language: Parser: en (V-IAI-3S, imperfect), on (V-PAP-NSM, present participle), erchomenos (V-PNP-NSM, present middle participle). Same forms as 1:4,8 but reordered. Cross-references: Part of TM016 (5 temporal title instances). The reversed order suggests the formula describes an attribute of God rather than imposing a structural template. Relationship to other evidence: Supports reading the temporal triad as characterological rather than organizational. If the same formula appears in different orders and is applied to different entities (God, beast, kings), it characterizes rather than structures.

    Rev 11:17¶

    Context: Temporal title during the seventh trumpet — "which art, and wast, and art to come." Direct statement: Some manuscripts omit "and art to come" (ho erchomenos), leaving only ho on kai ho en. If the omission is original, the "coming" element drops because the divine arrival has occurred. Original language: The textual variant is theologically loaded. A two-element version at the seventh trumpet would signal the realization of the "coming" component. Cross-references: Part of TM016. Contrasts with 17:8 where the beast's parody retains all three elements. Relationship to other evidence: The possible shortening of the title at 11:17 is consistent with the formula being characterological — its elements can be adjusted to fit the narrative moment rather than rigidly maintaining a fixed form.

    Rev 16:5¶

    Context: Angel of waters' declaration: "Thou art righteous, O Lord, which art, and wast, and shalt be." Direct statement: The KJV reads "shalt be" rather than "is to come." Some textual traditions have ho hosios ("the holy one") rather than ho esomenos or ho erchomenos. Original language: Textual variant — the third element differs across manuscripts. Cross-references: Part of TM016. The variation in the third element across 1:4, 1:8, 4:8, 11:17, and 16:5 further supports reading the formula as flexible characterization rather than fixed structural template. Relationship to other evidence: Consistent with the characterological reading of the temporal triad.

    Rev 17:1-3¶

    Context: Third en pneumati marker. One of the seven bowl angels introduces the Babylon vision. Direct statement: The verb shifts from egenomyn (1:10, 4:2) to apenengken (G667, apophero, V-AAI-3S) — the angel carries John to the wilderness. The invitation formula ("Come hither, I will shew thee") and bowl-angel identification match 21:9-10 exactly. Original language: Parser: apenengken (G667, V-AAI-3S). The apophero + en pneumati combination occurs only at 17:3 and 21:10 in the NT — a unique verbal pair. Cross-references: VP001 (Strong, 6+ shared elements) with 21:9-10. The deiknymi verb ("I will shew thee") connects to the transmission chain at 1:1 and 22:6. Relationship to other evidence: Under the threefold scheme, this falls within "things hereafter" with no 1:19 signal at this boundary. The en pneumati system places a primary division here that the threefold scheme does not recognize.

    Rev 17:8¶

    Context: Beast's anti-temporal formula: "was, and is not, and yet is." Direct statement: En kai ouk estin kai parestai — inverts the divine title pattern. Where God "is" (ho on), the beast "is not" (ouk estin). Original language: The parody confirms the temporal triad is a characterological device. If it characterizes the beast (not a structural entity), it also characterizes God in 1:4,8 rather than organizing the book. Cross-references: Part of TM016. Connected to 17:10 ("five are fallen, one is, the other is not yet come") — another temporal triad applied to a non-structural entity (seven kings). Relationship to other evidence: Strongest evidence that the temporal triad is characterological rather than organizational. If multiple entities are described in temporal triads, the device is not unique to 1:19's structural question.

    Rev 19:10¶

    Context: First worship refusal. John falls to worship the angel; the angel redirects to God. Direct statement: "Worship God" (to theo proskyneson). Contains the defining statement: "the testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy" (he martyria Iesou estin to pneuma tes propheteias). Original language: Martyria (G3141), pneuma (G4151), propheteias (G4394). The martyria connects to the witness chain (1:2, 1:9, 12:17, 19:10, 20:4). Cross-references: Parallel to 22:8-9 — same worship-refusal pattern, same syndoulos identification, same "worship God" conclusion. The pair creates structural brackets at the Babylon/Jerusalem vision conclusions (Bauckham). Relationship to other evidence: The 19:10/22:8-9 parallel is independent of both the threefold and fourfold divisions. It creates internal symmetry within the visionary body.

    Rev 21:9-10¶

    Context: Fourth en pneumati marker. Bowl-angel introduces the New Jerusalem vision. Direct statement: Same formula as 17:1-3: bowl angel + "Come hither, I will shew thee" + apenengken me en pneumati + location (great and high mountain) + female figure (bride) + city (holy Jerusalem descending from heaven). Original language: Parser: apenengken (G667, V-AAI-3S), edeixen (G1166, V-AAI-3S from deiknymi). The deiknymi verb at 21:10 connects to the transmission chain (1:1 deixai, 22:6 deixai). Cross-references: VP001 with 17:1-3 (Strong). The antithetical parallel: wilderness/harlot/Babylon vs. mountain/bride/Jerusalem. Relationship to other evidence: The fourth en pneumati marker creates the final structural division. Under the threefold scheme, this boundary does not exist — 21:10 falls within the undifferentiated "things hereafter" section.

    Rev 22:6¶

    Context: Epilogue opening. Echoes 1:1's transmission chain. Direct statement: "These sayings are faithful and true" (pistoi kai alethinoi). The Lord God "sent his angel to shew unto his servants the things which must shortly be done" — verbatim ha dei genesthai en tachei. Original language: Parser: apestellen (G649, V-AAI-3S), angelon (G32, N-ASM), deixai (G1166, V-AAN), doulois (G1401, N-DPM), dei (G1163, V-PAI-3S), genesthai (G1096, V-2ADN), tachei (G5034, N-DSN). Every element matches 1:1. Cross-references: Verbatim 5-word match with 1:1 (ha dei genesthai en tachei). The "faithful and true" connects to 1:5 (martys pistos) and 19:11 (Pistos kai Alethinos). Relationship to other evidence: The strongest single inclusio element. Anchors SP009 (Strong validation).

    Rev 22:7¶

    Context: First of three "I come quickly" (erchomai tachy) statements in the epilogue. Direct statement: Makarios (G3107) + keeping (teron) + words of prophecy of this book (logous tes propheteias tou bibliou toutou). Erchomai (V-PNI-1S, first person) + tachy (G5035, ADV). Original language: The erchomai is first person, contrasting with 1:7's third person erchetai. The beatitude echoes 1:3 (Moderate VP, 3 shared elements). Cross-references: Part of TM020 (triple tachy: 22:7, 12, 20). Part of TM019 (sevenfold beatitude). Part of the erchomai person-shift pattern. Relationship to other evidence: Demonstrates the escalating eschatological intensity in the epilogue — from promise (22:7) to reward (22:12) to liturgical exchange (22:20).

    Rev 22:8-9¶

    Context: Second worship refusal, paralleling 19:10. Direct statement: "I John" (kago Ioannes) echoes 1:9 (ego Ioannes). Worship refusal with "worship God" conclusion matches 19:10. Original language: Shared vocabulary with 19:10: proskynesai, syndoulos, to theo proskyneson. Cross-references: The I-John inclusio (1:9/22:8) and the worship-refusal pair (19:10/22:8-9) create overlapping structural brackets. Relationship to other evidence: Part of the Bauckham Babylon-Jerusalem structural pair and the prologue-epilogue personal frame.

    Rev 22:10¶

    Context: Seal reversal: "Seal not the sayings of the prophecy of this book." Direct statement: Me sphragises (V-AAS-2S, prohibitive subjunctive from sphragizo G4972) — prohibits the sealing that Daniel was commanded to perform. Ho kairos gar eggys estin echoes 1:3 (TM017). Original language: Parser: me (PRT-N, G3361), sphragises (V-AAS-2S, G4972), kairos (G2540, N-NSM), eggys (G1451, ADV). The prohibitive subjunctive form prohibits initiating the act of sealing. Cross-references: Reverses Dan 12:4 ("shut up the words and seal the book"). Part of the five-stage seal vocabulary chain (Dan 8:26 -> 12:4 -> Rev 5:1 -> 6-8 -> 22:10). The kairos eggys echo links to 1:3 (TM017). Relationship to other evidence: The sealed-to-unsealed arc spans from the Daniel allusion in the prologue (1:1) to the explicit reversal in the epilogue (22:10), integrating the body (seal-breaking sequence) into the frame.

    Rev 22:12-13¶

    Context: Second "I come quickly" with reward. Maximum Alpha-Omega title expansion. Direct statement: Erchomai tachy (second occurrence). The combined triple title: to Alpha kai to O (Alpha/Omega), ho protos kai ho eschatos (first/last), he arche kai to telos (beginning/end). Original language: Parser: protos (G4413, A-NSM-S), eschatos (G2078, A-NSM-S), arche (G746, N-NSF), telos (G5056, N-NSN). All three title pairs combined for the first and only time. Cross-references: Parallels: 1:8 (Alpha/Omega + temporal title), 1:17 (first/last), 21:6 (Alpha/Omega + beginning/end). OT source: Isa 41:4, 44:6, 48:12 (first/last as YHWH self-identification). Relationship to other evidence: The maximum expansion at 22:13 places the climactic divine self-identification in the epilogue, reinforcing the prologue-epilogue frame. The title expansion moves from partial (1:8) to complete (22:13).

    Rev 22:14¶

    Context: Sixth beatitude — commandments, tree of life, gates. Direct statement: Makarios (G3107) + "do his commandments" + "right to the tree of life" (xylon tes zoes) + "gates into the city." Original language: The "tree of life" connects to 2:7 (Ephesus overcomer promise) and 22:2 (New Jerusalem vision). Cross-references: Part of Strand's chiastic churches/New Jerusalem parallels: tree of life (2:7/22:2/22:14). Part of the sevenfold beatitude pattern (TM019). Relationship to other evidence: Creates a chiastic link between the church letters and the epilogue, supporting concentric architecture.

    Rev 22:16¶

    Context: Ekklesia return — only occurrence outside chapters 1-3. Direct statement: "I Jesus have sent mine angel to testify unto you these things in the churches" (tais ekklesiais, G1577). Transmission chain echo with Christ as explicit sender. Original language: Martyresai (aorist infinitive from martyreo G3140) — connects to the witness chain. Epempsa ton angelon mou — "I sent my angel." Cross-references: Echoes 1:1 (transmission chain) but with Christ as the named sender. The ekklesia absence from Visions 2-4 and its return here creates a literary bracket (TM). Relationship to other evidence: The 19-chapter absence of ekklesia followed by its epilogue return is one of the strongest arguments for the prologue-epilogue frame enclosing the visionary body.

    Rev 22:17¶

    Context: Spirit and bride invitation — "Come." Direct statement: The pneuma (G4151) and bride say erchou (imperative from erchomai G2064, "Come!"). The invitation shifts erchomai from a divine promise ("I come") to a human/divine invitation ("Come!"). Original language: Erchou is the imperative form — the third stage of the erchomai person-shift: 3rd person (1:7) -> 1st person (22:7,12,20) -> imperative (22:17,20). Cross-references: The pneuma in the epilogue connects to the en pneumati formula and the seven Spirits of 1:4. The "water of life" connects to 21:6 and 22:1. Relationship to other evidence: Completes the erchomai person-shift pattern spanning the book's frame sections.

    Rev 22:18-19¶

    Context: Canon warning against adding or removing from "the words of the prophecy of this book." Direct statement: Summartyro ("I testify," from martyreo G3140) introduces the warning. The phrase "words of the prophecy of this book" (logous tes propheteias tou bibliou toutou) echoes 1:3, 22:7, 22:10. Original language: The biblion (G975) in "book of this prophecy" and "book of life" creates a double reference. The Deut 4:2 and 12:32 parallel (adding/removing from the law) provides the OT model. Cross-references: Part of TM022 (witness chain). The "words of this prophecy" phrase creates a thread spanning 1:3, 22:7, 22:10, 22:18-19. Relationship to other evidence: Reinforces the frame's function as literary bracket — the book's integrity is asserted in both the first beatitude (1:3) and the closing warning (22:18-19).

    Rev 22:20¶

    Context: Third and final "I come quickly" with Maranatha response. Direct statement: Ho martyron ("the one testifying," present participle from martyreo G3140) + nai erchomai tachy ("surely I come quickly") + John's response erchou kyrie Iesou ("Come, Lord Jesus"). Original language: The triple tachy (G5035) in the epilogue (22:7, 12, 20) creates TM020. The Maranatha formula (cf. 1 Cor 16:22) appears in John's response. Cross-references: Climax of the erchomai pattern and the witness chain. The progression: 22:7 (promise + beatitude), 22:12 (promise + reward), 22:20 (promise + liturgical response). Relationship to other evidence: Final element of both the triple tachy (TM020) and the witness chain (TM022) in the epilogue.

    Rev 22:21¶

    Context: Final verse. Epistolary grace benediction. Direct statement: He charis tou kyriou Iesou meta panton — "The grace of the Lord Jesus with all." Original language: Parser: charis (G5485, N-NSF), meta (G3326, PREP), panton (G3956, A-GPM). The N1904 is shorter than the KJV/TR (which adds "Christ" and "Amen"). Cross-references: Creates the epistolary frame with 1:4 (TM021, SP010 Moderate validation). Matches Pauline closing convention (cf. 2 Thess 3:18). Relationship to other evidence: The charis frame wraps the entire book — apocalypse, prophecy, and visions — in an epistolary container. The genre hybridity (letter + prophecy + apocalypse) is structurally significant.

    Dan 2:28-29¶

    Context: Daniel interprets Nebuchadnezzar's dream — "what shall be in the latter days." Direct statement: Source for Revelation's ha dei genesthai formula. Dan 2:28 LXX: ha dei genesthai ep' eschatwn ton hemeron. Dan 2:29 adds "what should come to pass hereafter" (meta tauta). Original language: The three shared Greek words (ha, dei, genesthai) plus the relative pronoun create a Strong allusion. The temporal shift from "in the latter days" to "en tachei" (Revelation) signals John views the fulfillment as imminent. Cross-references: Rev 1:1, 4:1, 22:6 all use the dei genesthai formula. Dan 2:45 also uses meta tauta. Relationship to other evidence: Anchors the Daniel-Revelation structural bridge. The verbal chain from Daniel through the prologue, transition, and epilogue may be more structurally significant than 1:19's threefold scheme.

    Dan 2:45¶

    Context: "The great God hath made known to the king what shall come to pass hereafter." Direct statement: Uses meta tauta for "hereafter" in the LXX — the direct source for Rev 4:1's meta tauta. Original language: The Dan-Rev verbal chain includes both dei genesthai (Dan 2:28/Rev 1:1,22:6) and meta tauta (Dan 2:45/Rev 4:1). Cross-references: Rev 4:1 combines both formulas: ha dei genesthai meta tauta. Relationship to other evidence: Confirms the Danielic origin of both key formulas used at Revelation's major structural transition.

    Dan 7:13¶

    Context: "One like the Son of man came with the clouds of heaven." Direct statement: Source for Rev 1:7 "he cometh with clouds." Combined with Zech 12:10 at Rev 1:7. Original language: The Son of man title from Dan 7:13 appears in Rev 1:13 and 14:14. The "clouds of heaven" imagery enters the Revelation structural frame through 1:7. Cross-references: Confirmed as top OT parallel for Rev 1:7 (0.411 hybrid score). Relationship to other evidence: Part of the composite allusion at Rev 1:7 (AN, Strong). Connects the Daniel prophetic framework to Revelation's prologue.

    Dan 12:4¶

    Context: "Shut up the words, and seal the book, even to the time of the end." Direct statement: The sealing command to Daniel using satham (shut up) + chatham (seal). Reversed at Rev 22:10 (me sphragises, "seal not"). Original language: Hebrew satham and chatham correspond to Greek sphragizo. The five-stage vocabulary chain spans Dan 8:26 -> 12:4 -> Rev 5:1 -> 6-8 -> 22:10. Cross-references: Rev 5:1 (katasphragizo, hapax — intensified sealing). Rev 22:10 (prohibitive subjunctive — explicit reversal). Relationship to other evidence: Creates the sealed-to-unsealed structural arc spanning the entire Daniel-Revelation relationship.

    Isa 41:4¶

    Context: First of three Isaiah "first and last" passages. YHWH: "I the LORD, the first, and with the last." Direct statement: Hebrew rishon (first) and acharon (last) correspond to Greek protos (G4413) and eschatos (G2078). YHWH self-identification title. Cross-references: Repeated at Isa 44:6 and 48:12. Applied to Christ at Rev 1:17, 2:8, 22:13. Relationship to other evidence: Provides the OT source for the first/last title in the Alpha-Omega expansion pattern. The threefold repetition in Isaiah establishes it as a core YHWH claim.

    Isa 44:6¶

    Context: Second first/last passage: "I am the first, and I am the last; and beside me there is no God." Direct statement: The uniqueness claim ("beside me there is no God") makes the christological appropriation in Revelation striking — Rev 22:13 applies this title to Christ. Cross-references: Confirmed as OT parallel for Rev 22:13 by the parallels tool. Relationship to other evidence: Strengthens the AN for the Alpha-Omega title chain. The exclusivity language in Isaiah makes the Revelation application a christological identification with YHWH.

    Isa 48:12¶

    Context: Third first/last passage: "I am he; I am the first, I also am the last." Direct statement: The formula "I am he" (ani hu) is a divine self-identification formula. The threefold repetition (41:4, 44:6, 48:12) establishes "first and last" as an exclusive YHWH title. Cross-references: All three Isaiah passages are classified as Strong allusion for the Rev 1:17/22:13 title appropriation. Relationship to other evidence: Completes the OT source documentation for the Alpha-Omega expansion pattern.

    Zech 12:10¶

    Context: "They shall look upon me whom they have pierced." Direct statement: Combined with Dan 7:13 at Rev 1:7 to create a composite allusion. The piercing element comes from Zechariah; the clouds element from Daniel. Original language: The Hebrew hevittu elay et asher-daqaru ("looked upon me whom they have pierced") enters Revelation in adapted form. Cross-references: John 19:37 also cites this passage at the crucifixion. Relationship to other evidence: Part of the two-source composite allusion at Rev 1:7 (AN, Strong).

    Rev 2:7, 2:10, 3:5, 3:10, 3:12, 3:16, 3:21 (Church Letter Passages)¶

    Context: Supporting verses from the church letters relevant to structural analysis. Direct statement: Rev 2:7 (tree of life overcomer promise), 2:10 (mello in Smyrna — future suffering), 3:5 (book of life), 3:10 (mello in Philadelphia — future trial), 3:12 (New Jerusalem + name), 3:16 (mello in Laodicea — future spewing), 3:21 (throne promise). Original language: The three mello occurrences (2:10, 3:10, 3:16) are the key TM013 evidence — future vocabulary within the "present" section of the threefold reading. The overcomer promises (nikao G3528) create chiastic parallels with New Jerusalem (Strand). Cross-references: Each overcomer promise has a corresponding fulfillment in Vision 4 (21:7, 22:2, 21:27, 21:10, 22:4, 22:3). Relationship to other evidence: The mello leakage (TM013) and the overcomer chiasm (Strand) both complicate the strict threefold reading of 1:19 — the first by breaking temporal boundaries, the second by creating structural connections across the entire book.

    Rev 5:1, 10:4, 14:13, 21:5 (Body Passages)¶

    Context: Supporting verses from the book's body relevant to the write-command distribution and seal vocabulary. Direct statement: Rev 5:1 — katasphragizo hapax ("sealed with seven seals"), intensified sealing. Rev 10:4 — graphon prohibition + sphragison (seal command for thunder sayings) + mello (emellon, "was about to"). Rev 14:13 — graphon command in Vision 2 with third beatitude. Rev 21:5 — graphon command in Vision 4 with "true and faithful" declaration. Original language: The graphon distribution across all visions (TM014) and the seal vocabulary spanning the body (katasphragizo -> sphragis openings -> me sphragises) demonstrate that both writing and sealing vocabulary cross-cut proposed structural divisions. Cross-references: The 10:4 internal seal command contrasts with the 22:10 global unseal command. The 14:13 and 21:5 write commands show the writing mandate extends beyond the prologue. Relationship to other evidence: Both the write-command span and the body's seal vocabulary integrate with the prologue-epilogue frame, supporting the inclusio as the primary structural feature.

    Exod 3:14, Hab 2:2, Isa 30:8, Dan 8:26 (OT Background)¶

    Context: OT supporting passages providing background for Revelation's structural features. Direct statement: Exod 3:14 ("I AM THAT I AM") — possible background for the temporal divine title. LXX ego eimi ho on may echo in Rev 1:4's ho on. Hab 2:2 ("Write the vision, and make it plain") — prophetic writing command parallel to Rev 1:19. Isa 30:8 ("Write it before them in a table, and note it in a book") — prophetic inscription command. Dan 8:26 ("shut thou up the vision") — first stage of the seal vocabulary chain. Original language: The connections range from Echo (Isa 30:8, 1 shared element) to Probable allusion (Hab 2:2) to Strong allusion (Dan 8:26 within the seal chain). Cross-references: These passages establish OT precedent for the prophetic writing commission and the sealed/unsealed prophecy motif. Relationship to other evidence: Supporting background rather than primary structural evidence.

    Patterns Identified¶

    1. The prologue-epilogue inclusio is the dominant structural feature. Nine verified verbal parallels (2 Strong, 5 Moderate, 2 Minimal) create a literary bracket that frames the four en pneumati visions. The verbatim ha dei genesthai en tachei at 1:1/22:6 is the most precise match. The charis frame, Alpha-Omega expansion, witness chain, kairos eggys echo, beatitude pattern, and ekklesia bracket all reinforce this frame.

    2. The temporal triad is characterological, not organizational. Five instances of the "was/is/coming" formula (1:4, 1:8, 4:8, 11:17, 16:5) apply the pattern to God. The beast's parody at 17:8 and the king sequence at 17:10 apply temporal triads to non-structural entities. The variation in order and element count across occurrences supports reading the device as descriptive of entities rather than prescriptive of structure.

    3. The mello leakage undermines strict temporal boundaries. Three occurrences of mello within the church letters (2:10, 3:10, 3:16) place future content in the "present" section. The temporal boundary between the threefold scheme's second and third sections is not maintained by the text.

    4. The Daniel allusion chain connects frame and body. Ha dei genesthai runs from Dan 2:28 through Rev 1:1, 4:1, to 22:6. The sealed-to-unsealed arc runs from Dan 8:26/12:4 through Rev 5:1, 6-8, to 22:10. Both chains integrate the prologue-epilogue frame with the body through verifiable Greek vocabulary.

    5. The write-command distribution spans all visions. Graphon appears 12 times across Vision 1 (9 occurrences), Vision 2 (2 occurrences), and Vision 4 (1 occurrence). The writing mandate is not confined to one section.

    6. Strand's chiastic parallels are real but uneven. The tree of life, New Jerusalem, and name-of-God pairs involve shared Greek vocabulary. The throne pair involves only one shared word. Taken collectively, the pattern of church-promise / New-Jerusalem-fulfillment correspondences supports concentric architecture linking Visions 1 and 4.

    Word Study Integration¶

    The eisin (G1510) ambiguity at 1:19 is illuminated by its distribution pattern: the same form serves as an identification copula at 1:20, 4:5, 5:8, 17:9, 17:12, and 17:15. In each of these passages, eisin decodes what a symbolic element represents rather than marking temporal presence. The grammar reference confirms that ha eisin is syntactically identical to these symbol-identification clauses.

    The mello (G3195) distribution — appearing both in the structural verse (1:19) and within the church letters (2:10, 3:10, 3:16) — creates a paradox for the threefold reading: the same word that defines the boundary between "present" and "future" appears on the "present" side of that boundary.

    The erchomai (G2064) person-shift (3rd person at 1:7 -> 1st person at 22:7,12,20 -> imperative at 22:17) creates a movement from prophetic announcement to personal promise to liturgical invitation within the frame sections.

    The deiknymi (G1166) distribution (1:1, 4:1, 17:1, 21:9-10, 22:1, 22:6) threads through all four visions and both frame passages, making it one of the most structurally pervasive verbs. Its presence at the vision introductions (17:1, 21:9) connects the en pneumati boundaries to the transmission chain.

    Cross-Testament Connections¶

    The Daniel-Revelation connection is the most extensively documented: - Ha dei genesthai: Dan 2:28 LXX -> Rev 1:1, 4:1, 22:6 (verbal chain with temporal transformation) - Meta tauta: Dan 2:45 LXX -> Rev 4:1 (direct source for the transitional formula) - Sealed-to-unsealed: Dan 8:26, 12:4 -> Rev 5:1, 6-8, 22:10 (five-stage vocabulary chain) - Son of man with clouds: Dan 7:13 -> Rev 1:7, 1:13, 14:14

    The Isaiah-Revelation connection for the Alpha-Omega title: - First and last: Isa 41:4, 44:6, 48:12 -> Rev 1:17, 2:8, 22:13

    The Zechariah connection at Rev 1:7: - Piercing: Zech 12:10 -> Rev 1:7 (composite with Dan 7:13)

    The Ezekiel Spirit-transport model (documented in revs2-01) provides the intertextual template for the en pneumati/apophero pattern.

    Difficult or Complicating Passages¶

    Rev 1:19 — Genuine grammatical ambiguity¶

    Both the temporal and epexegetical readings of ha eisin are syntactically valid. The context supports the epexegetical reading (1:20 uses eisin as identification copula immediately after). The three-kai list structure supports the temporal reading. Neither reading can be excluded on grammatical grounds. The ambiguity may be inherent to the text.

    Rev 4:1 — The nature of the transition¶

    Every model recognizes 4:1 as a structural boundary, but its nature is debated. It is the only convergence of meta tauta eidon and en pneumati. The "first voice" echo links back to 1:10, which could support either a new vision or a resumed element. The dei genesthai meta tauta formula blends 1:1 and 1:19, suggesting the verse serves as a structural hinge rather than a clean break.

    The disproportionality of the threefold reading¶

    The 5%/13%/82% verse distribution under the threefold reading leaves the majority of the book in an undifferentiated "future" category. This alone does not disprove the reading — a structural key can designate unequal sections — but the extreme asymmetry means 1:19 provides minimal structural differentiation for the book's main content.

    Beale's whole-book reading¶

    If all three clauses cover the entire book from different temporal perspectives, the three-kai list becomes a set of overlapping categories rather than sequential sections. This avoids both the disproportionality problem and the mello-leakage problem but reads the list in an unusual way.

    Preliminary Synthesis¶

    The weight of evidence points toward Rev 1:19 functioning as a general content-scope description rather than a strict structural outline. The prologue-epilogue inclusio is the stronger structural system — more extensively verified, more precisely documented in shared Greek vocabulary, and more compatible with the fourfold en pneumati division.
