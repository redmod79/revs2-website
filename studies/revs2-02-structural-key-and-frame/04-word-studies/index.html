    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    Does Revelation 1:19 ("write the things which thou hast seen, and the things which are, and the things which shall be hereafter") provide a structural key for the entire book, and how does each clause map to sections of the text? How does the prologue-epilogue inclusio (Rev 1:1-8 / Rev 22:6-21) frame the visions — what verbal parallels connect the opening and closing, and how do they create a literary bracket? What is the relationship between the 1:19 threefold division and the fourfold en pneumati division from study 01?

    G1125 — grapho¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1125-grapho.md

    Occurrences: 216

    Primary verb meaning 'to write.' 197 occurrences in 26 NT books. Revelation has 28 occurrences (6.9 per 100 verses), the highest density of any book. The dominant form is the perfect passive gegrapai ('it is written,' 52% of all uses). In Revelation, the aorist active imperative grapson ('Write!') appears 12 times: 2 general commands (1:11, 1:19), 7 church-specific (2:1,8,12,18; 3:1,7,14), 1 prohibition (10:4 me grapses), 2 later (14:13, 21:5). The distribution spans all proposed structural divisions.

    Semantic categories: written revelation, prophetic inscription, scriptural authority

    Key verses: Rev 1:11, Rev 1:19, Rev 10:4, Rev 14:13, Rev 21:5

    Relevance: The graphon distribution (TM014) is critical evidence that writing commands span the entire book, cross-cutting the threefold division of 1:19. If the writing activity covers all visions, the writing mandate of 1:19 cannot be confined to one section.

    G3195 — mello¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3195-mello.md

    Occurrences: 109

    Verb meaning 'to be about to, to intend, to be destined.' 106 unique verses, 109 tokens across 18 NT books. Revelation has 12 occurrences (3.0 per 100 verses). Forms: present active indicative (33%), present active participle (47%), imperfect active indicative (18%). The extreme translation variety (60+ renderings) reflects mello's function as an auxiliary verb. In Revelation: 1:19 (mellei genesthai, structural), 2:10 (melleis paschein, Smyrna), 3:10 (mellouses erchesthai, Philadelphia), 3:16 (mello se emesai, Laodicea), 10:4 (emellon graphein), and others.

    Semantic categories: imminence, futurity, divine necessity, intention

    Key verses: Rev 1:19, Rev 2:10, Rev 3:10, Rev 3:16, Rev 10:4

    Relevance: The presence of mello within the church letters (2:10, 3:10, 3:16 — TM013) is one of the strongest arguments against a strict threefold structural reading of 1:19. Future-oriented vocabulary within the 'present' section makes the temporal boundaries porous.

    G1096 — ginomai¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1096-ginomai.md

    Occurrences: 702

    Primary verb meaning 'to become, to happen, to come into being.' 631 occurrences (668 parsed forms) — one of the most common NT verbs. The aorist dominates (73%). In Revelation's structural formulas: genesthai (aorist middle infinitive) appears in ha dei genesthai en tachei (1:1, 22:6) and ha mellei genesthai meta tauta (1:19, 4:1). Also egenomyn en pneumati (1:10, 4:2) uses the aorist middle indicative 1st sg. The verb serves dual structural functions: the dei genesthai formula (Danielic allusion) and the en pneumati formula (vision markers).

    Semantic categories: becoming, happening, transition, narrative marker

    Key verses: Rev 1:1, Rev 1:10, Rev 1:19, Rev 4:1, Rev 4:2, Rev 22:6

    Relevance: Ginomai appears in both the ha dei genesthai chain (linking prologue-epilogue) and the en pneumati markers (creating the fourfold division). Its dual structural role makes it a key lexical thread connecting the two structural systems this study examines.

    G1510 — eimi¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1510-eimi.md

    Occurrences: 155

    The verb 'to be.' In Rev 1:19, eisin (V-PAI-3P, present active indicative 3rd plural) is the crux of the epexegetical vs. temporal debate. The grammar reference documents that ha eisin is syntactically identical to Revelation's symbol-identification clauses (4:5, 5:8, 17:9, 17:12, 17:15) where eisin functions as a timeless copula of identification. In the temporal divine title, eimi appears as ho on (present participle, 'the one who is') and ho en (imperfect, 'the one who was').

    Semantic categories: existence, identity, copula, divine being

    Key verses: Rev 1:4, Rev 1:8, Rev 1:19, Rev 1:20, Rev 4:8

    Relevance: The eisin in 1:19 is the linguistic crux of the entire study — whether it functions temporally ('things which are now') or epexegetically ('what they mean'). The grammar reference shows both readings are syntactically valid.

    G602 — apokalypsis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G602-apokalypsis.md

    Occurrences: 18

    Noun meaning 'revelation, unveiling, disclosure.' Opening word of the book (Apokalypsis Iesou Christou). The genitive 'of Jesus Christ' is ambiguous — objective ('revelation about Jesus Christ') or subjective ('revelation from/by Jesus Christ'). Rev 1:1 favors the subjective reading: God gave the revelation to Christ to show to his servants.

    Semantic categories: divine disclosure, unveiling, apocalyptic genre

    Key verses: Rev 1:1

    Relevance: The opening word establishes the book's genre and purpose. The transmission chain (God -> Christ -> angel -> John -> servants) embedded in 1:1 reappears at 22:6 and 22:16, forming the inclusio's structural spine.

    G1163 — dei¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1163-dei.md

    Occurrences: 105

    Impersonal verb meaning 'it is necessary, one must.' Expresses divine necessity. In Revelation: appears in the ha dei genesthai formula (1:1, 4:1, 22:6) — always V-PAI-3S (present active indicative 3rd singular). The formula echoes Dan 2:28 LXX, where dei genesthai introduces God's disclosure of future events. The shift from Daniel's 'in the latter days' to Revelation's 'en tachei' (shortly) signals inaugurated eschatology.

    Semantic categories: divine necessity, prophetic disclosure, eschatological compulsion

    Key verses: Rev 1:1, Rev 4:1, Rev 22:6, Dan 2:28

    Relevance: The dei genesthai formula creates a three-point structural thread: 1:1 (prologue) -> 4:1 (major transition) -> 22:6 (epilogue). This chain, rooted in Daniel 2:28, may be more structurally significant than 1:19's threefold scheme.

    G3107 — makarios¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3107-makarios.md

    Occurrences: 50

    Adjective meaning 'blessed, happy, fortunate.' Occurs exactly 7 times in Revelation (1:3, 14:13, 16:15, 19:9, 20:6, 22:7, 22:14) — the sevenfold beatitude pattern. Three of seven (43%) fall in the prologue-epilogue frame (1:3, 22:7, 22:14). TM019 documents this as a structural pattern spanning the entire book.

    Semantic categories: blessedness, beatitude, eschatological promise

    Key verses: Rev 1:3, Rev 14:13, Rev 22:7, Rev 22:14

    Relevance: The sevenfold makarios distribution with concentration in the frame (3 of 7) supports the inclusio structure. The 1:3/22:7 pair shares vocabulary (makarios + keeping + words of prophecy), forming a VP element.

    G5035 — tachy¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5035-tachy.md

    Occurrences: 12

    Adverb meaning 'quickly, shortly, without delay.' 12 NT occurrences, 6 in Revelation (50% — highest concentration). Used in the 'I come quickly' (erchomai tachy) formula at Rev 2:16, 3:11, 22:7, 22:12, 22:20. Also at 11:14 ('the third woe cometh quickly'). The epilogue triple (22:7, 12, 20) is documented as TM020.

    Semantic categories: eschatological imminence, divine promise, temporal urgency

    Key verses: Rev 22:7, Rev 22:12, Rev 22:20, Rev 2:16, Rev 3:11

    Relevance: The triple tachy in the epilogue (TM020) creates escalating eschatological urgency. Distinct from G5034 tachos (en tachei in 1:1, 22:6) — tachy is used for Christ's personal coming promise, while tachos describes the broader scope of events.

    G5485 — charis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5485-charis.md

    Occurrences: 156

    Noun meaning 'grace, favor, kindness.' In Revelation: 1:4 (charis hymin kai eirene, 'grace to you and peace') and 22:21 (he charis tou kyriou Iesou, 'the grace of the Lord Jesus'). These two occurrences create the epistolary frame (SP010, Moderate validation). The opening formula matches Pauline convention (cf. Rom 1:7, 1 Cor 1:3). The closing matches 2 Thess 3:18.

    Semantic categories: divine favor, epistolary convention, blessing

    Key verses: Rev 1:4, Rev 22:21

    Relevance: The charis frame (TM021) is the strongest evidence for Revelation's epistolary character. The grace opening/closing wraps apocalyptic and prophetic content in an epistolary frame, establishing the book's genre hybridity.

    G4151 — pneuma¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4151-pneuma.md

    Occurrences: 385

    Neuter noun meaning 'spirit, breath, wind.' 385 NT occurrences. Revelation has 25 occurrences (6.2 per 100 verses). Key structural uses: en pneumati (1:10, 4:2, 17:3, 21:10) creating the fourfold division; 'seven Spirits' (1:4, 3:1, 4:5, 5:6); 'Spirit saith unto the churches' (7 occurrences in chs. 2-3); 'Spirit and the bride' (22:17). The en pneumati formula marks the highest-level structural divisions documented in revs2-01.

    Semantic categories: Holy Spirit, prophetic vision state, divine presence, spiritual realm

    Key verses: Rev 1:4, Rev 1:10, Rev 4:2, Rev 17:3, Rev 21:10, Rev 22:17

    Relevance: The en pneumati fourfold division is one of the two primary structural systems this study examines. The relationship between the fourfold en pneumati system and the threefold 1:19 system is the central unresolved question.

    G3140 — martyreo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3140-martyreo.md

    Occurrences: 86

    Verb meaning 'to bear witness, to testify.' Part of the martyr- word family with martyria (G3141) and martys (G3144). In the prologue: 1:2 (emartyesen, John bore record). In the epilogue: 22:16 (martyresai, to testify), 22:18 (summartyro, I testify), 22:20 (ho martyron, the one testifying). TM022 documents this as a vocabulary chain spanning prologue and epilogue.

    Semantic categories: testimony, witness-bearing, prophetic attestation

    Key verses: Rev 1:2, Rev 22:16, Rev 22:18, Rev 22:20

    Relevance: The witness vocabulary chain (TM022) is a key inclusio element — testimony/witness words concentrate in the prologue and epilogue but also appear at structural boundaries (19:10 'testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy').

    G3141 — martyria¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3141-martyria.md

    Occurrences: 37

    Noun meaning 'testimony, witness, evidence.' In Revelation: 1:2 ('testimony of Jesus Christ'), 1:9 ('testimony of Jesus Christ'), 6:9, 11:7, 12:11, 12:17, 19:10 (x2), 20:4. The phrase 'testimony of Jesus' (he martyria Iesou) recurs as a structural thread. At 19:10 'the testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy' provides a defining statement.

    Semantic categories: divine testimony, prophetic witness, faithful confession

    Key verses: Rev 1:2, Rev 1:9, Rev 12:17, Rev 19:10

    Relevance: The martyria chain links the prologue (1:2,9) to the body (12:11,17; 19:10) to the epilogue (implicit via the martyreo verb). It provides evidence for structural continuity beyond the prologue-epilogue frame.

    G3144 — martys¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3144-martys.md

    Occurrences: 34

    Noun meaning 'witness, martyr.' In Revelation: 1:5 (ho martys ho pistos, 'the faithful witness'), 2:13 (ho martys mou ho pistos, Antipas), 3:14 (ho martys ho pistos kai alethinos), 11:3 (my two witnesses), 17:6 (blood of the martyrs of Jesus). The 'faithful witness' title brackets the prologue (1:5) and the letter to Laodicea (3:14).

    Semantic categories: faithful witness, prophetic testimony, martyrdom

    Key verses: Rev 1:5, Rev 2:13, Rev 3:14, Rev 11:3

    Relevance: The martys vocabulary connects to the broader witness word family chain (TM022) and provides evidence for the 'faithful and true' vocabulary thread spanning the book (1:5 -> 3:14 -> 19:11 -> 22:6).

    G746 — arche¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G746-arche.md

    Occurrences: 58

    Noun meaning 'beginning, origin, first cause, ruler.' In Revelation: part of the Alpha-Omega expansion pattern at 21:6 and 22:13 ('the beginning and the end,' he arche kai to telos). Also at 3:14 ('the beginning of the creation of God'). The arche/telos pair supplements Alpha/Omega and protos/eschatos.

    Semantic categories: beginning, origin, authority, primacy

    Key verses: Rev 21:6, Rev 22:13, Rev 3:14

    Relevance: Part of the Alpha-Omega title expansion pattern documented in rev-23. The triple title at 22:13 (Alpha/Omega + first/last + beginning/end) represents the maximum expansion, combining all three pairs.

    G2078 — eschatos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2078-eschatos.md

    Occurrences: 54

    Adjective meaning 'last, final.' In Revelation: part of 'the first and the last' (ho protos kai ho eschatos) at 1:17, 2:8, and 22:13. This title is sourced from Isa 41:4, 44:6, 48:12 where YHWH identifies himself as 'the first and the last.'

    Semantic categories: finality, eschatology, divine title

    Key verses: Rev 1:17, Rev 2:8, Rev 22:13, Isa 44:6

    Relevance: Part of the first/last title pair within the Alpha-Omega expansion pattern. The Isaiah source passages make this an YHWH self-identification appropriated by Christ in Revelation.

    G5056 — telos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5056-telos.md

    Occurrences: 42

    Noun meaning 'end, completion, goal.' In Revelation: part of 'the beginning and the end' (he arche kai to telos) at 21:6 and 22:13. Pairs with arche (G746) as the second title pair in the Alpha-Omega expansion.

    Semantic categories: completion, goal, terminus

    Key verses: Rev 21:6, Rev 22:13

    Relevance: Part of the arche/telos pair in the Alpha-Omega expansion. The combination at 22:13 brings all three divine self-identification pairs together in a single climactic declaration.

    G4413 — protos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4413-protos.md

    Occurrences: 105

    Adjective meaning 'first, foremost, chief.' In Revelation: part of 'the first and the last' (ho protos kai ho eschatos) at 1:17, 2:8, 22:13. Also at 4:1 'the first voice' (he phone he prote), linking back to the trumpet voice of 1:10. And at 21:1 'the first heaven and the first earth' (ho protos ouranos kai he prote ge).

    Semantic categories: primacy, sequence, divine title

    Key verses: Rev 1:17, Rev 4:1, Rev 22:13

    Relevance: The protos at 4:1 ('the first voice which I heard') explicitly links the throne-room transition back to 1:10's trumpet voice, creating a verbal thread across the 1:19 boundary.

    G2064 — erchomai¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2064-erchomai.md

    Occurrences: 664

    Verb meaning 'to come, to go.' In Revelation's structural frame: forms part of the temporal title (ho erchomenos, 'the coming one' at 1:4, 1:8, 4:8). In the prologue: erchetai (3rd person, 'he comes' at 1:7). In the epilogue: erchomai (1st person, 'I come' at 22:7, 12, 20) + erchou (imperative, 'Come!' at 22:17, 20). This person-shift creates an escalating eschatological progression.

    Semantic categories: coming, arrival, eschatological advent, divine approach

    Key verses: Rev 1:4, Rev 1:7, Rev 22:7, Rev 22:12, Rev 22:17, Rev 22:20

    Relevance: The erchomai person-shift (3rd person prologue -> 1st person epilogue -> imperative invitation) is a structural observation documenting the book's movement from announcement to personal promise to liturgical response.

    G4972 — sphragizo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4972-sphragizo.md

    Occurrences: 28

    Verb meaning 'to seal, to mark with a seal.' In Revelation: 7:3-4,8 (sealing of the 144,000), 10:4 (sphragison, 'seal up' the thunder sayings), 20:3 (sealed the pit), 22:10 (me sphragises, 'seal not' — prohibitive subjunctive). The 22:10 usage reverses Dan 12:4's sealing command.

    Semantic categories: sealing, authentication, closure, protection

    Key verses: Rev 7:3, Rev 10:4, Rev 22:10, Dan 12:4

    Relevance: The seal/unseal vocabulary arc from Dan 12:4 to Rev 22:10 creates a structural bridge spanning the entire Daniel-Revelation canonical relationship. The prohibitive subjunctive me sphragises at 22:10 is the grammatically precise reversal of Daniel's sealing command.

    G4973 — sphragis¶

    Key verses: Rev 5:1, Rev 6:1, Rev 7:2, Rev 8:1

    Relevance: Part of the five-stage seal vocabulary chain. The progressive seal-breaking (Rev 6-8) connects Dan 12:4's sealed book to Rev 22:10's unsealed prophecy, with Rev 5:1 (katasphragizo, hapax) as the intensified sealing at the midpoint.

    G1577 — ekklesia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1577-ekklesia.md

    Occurrences: 118

    Noun meaning 'church, assembly, congregation.' In Revelation: 20 occurrences, all but one confined to Vision 1 (chs. 1-3). The seven churches are named at 1:4,11 and addressed individually in chs. 2-3. The 'Spirit saith unto the churches' formula appears 7 times. The sole exception: 22:16 'I Jesus have sent mine angel to testify unto you these things in the churches.'

    Semantic categories: Christian assembly, local congregation, addressees

    Key verses: Rev 1:4, Rev 1:11, Rev 2:1, Rev 22:16

    Relevance: The ekklesia distribution is structurally significant: confined to Vision 1 except for the epilogue return at 22:16. This creates a bracket — the churches are addressed in the opening vision, disappear for 19 chapters, and reappear only when the epilogue reconnects to the prologue. The 22:16 return supports the prologue-epilogue inclusio as enclosing the body of visions.

    G2540 — kairos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2540-kairos.md

    Occurrences: 84

    Noun meaning 'time, season, appointed time.' In Revelation: 1:3 and 22:10 both contain 'ho kairos eggys' ('the time is near/at hand'). This creates a prologue-epilogue inclusio element (TM017). Also at 11:18 and 12:12,14.

    Semantic categories: appointed time, eschatological season, prophetic timing

    Key verses: Rev 1:3, Rev 22:10

    Relevance: The ho kairos eggys echo between 1:3 and 22:10 (TM017) is one of the clearest inclusio elements — identical vocabulary in both locations asserting the nearness of the prophesied events.

    G3466 — mysterion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3466-mysterion.md

    Occurrences: 27

    Noun meaning 'mystery, secret.' In Revelation: 1:20 ('the mystery of the seven stars'), 10:7 ('the mystery of God'), 17:5,7 ('mystery, Babylon'). At 1:20, the mystery is decoded using the eisin construction — 'the seven stars ARE the angels' — immediately after 1:19's ha eisin clause, supporting the epexegetical reading.

    Semantic categories: divine secret, symbolic meaning, apocalyptic disclosure

    Key verses: Rev 1:20, Rev 10:7, Rev 17:5

    Relevance: The mysterion at 1:20 is directly relevant to the epexegetical reading of 1:19. If 1:19's middle clause (ha eisin) asks 'what they mean,' then 1:20 provides the immediate answer: the mystery (what they mean) of the stars and lampstands. This sequential connection supports Michaels' reading.

    G3528 — nikao¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3528-nikao.md

    Occurrences: 29

    Verb meaning 'to overcome, to conquer, to prevail.' In Revelation: 17 occurrences. The overcomer promises in churches (2:7,11,17,26; 3:5,12,21) parallel the overcomer inheritance at 21:7 ('he that overcometh shall inherit all things'). This creates a structural link between Vision 1 (churches) and Vision 4 (New Jerusalem).

    Semantic categories: victory, overcoming, spiritual conquest

    Key verses: Rev 2:7, Rev 3:21, Rev 21:7

    Relevance: The nikao distribution supports Strand's chiastic reading — overcomer promises in churches correspond to overcomer fulfillments in the New Jerusalem vision, creating concentric architecture.

    G32 — aggelos¶

    Occurrences: 186

    Masculine noun meaning 'angel, messenger.' 186 NT occurrences across 15 translations. KJV: 'angel' (71), 'angels' (61), 'an angel' (11), 'the angel' (10), 'messengers' (3). In Revelation's structural frame: 1:1 (dia tou angelou autou, 'by his angel' — transmission chain), 1:20 (angels of the seven churches), 22:6 (apestellen ton angelon autou, 'sent his angel'), 22:16 (epempsa ton angelon mou, 'I have sent mine angel'). Revelation uses aggelos more than any other book.

    Semantic categories: heavenly messenger, divine intermediary, church representative

    Key verses: Rev 1:1, Rev 1:20, Rev 22:6, Rev 22:16

    Relevance: The angel as mediator in the transmission chain (1:1/22:6/22:16) is a key inclusio element. The shift from dia tou angelou autou (through his angel, 1:1) to apestellen ton angelon autou (sent his angel, 22:6) to epempsa ton angelon mou (I sent my angel, 22:16) shows variation within the transmission chain formula.

    G1166 — deiknymi¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1166-deiknyo.md

    Occurrences: 31

    Verb meaning 'to show, to exhibit, to make visible.' 31 NT occurrences, 8 in Revelation (1.8 per 100 verses). In Revelation's structural frame: 1:1 (deixai, aorist infinitive, 'to show'), 4:1 (deixo, future indicative, 'I will show'), 17:1 (deixo, 'I will show' — Babylon), 21:9 (deixo, 'I will show' — bride), 22:1 (edeixen, aorist, 'he showed'), 22:6 (deixai, aorist infinitive, 'to show'). The verb connects the transmission chain (1:1/22:6), the major vision introductions (17:1/21:9), and the throne-room transition (4:1).

    Semantic categories: divine revelation, apocalyptic disclosure, visual display

    Key verses: Rev 1:1, Rev 4:1, Rev 17:1, Rev 21:9, Rev 22:6

    Relevance: Deiknymi threads through all four visions and both frame passages, making it one of the most structurally pervasive verbs in Revelation.     G5034 — tachos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5034-tachos.md

    Occurrences: 7

    Neuter noun meaning 'speed, swiftness.' 7 NT occurrences, all in the fixed idiom en tachei (in speed = shortly/quickly). In Revelation: 1:1 and 22:6 both contain ha dei genesthai en tachei. The idiom functions adverbially — 'what must shortly come to pass.'

    Semantic categories: swiftness, imminence, eschatological timing

    Key verses: Rev 1:1, Rev 22:6

    Relevance: The en tachei at 1:1 and 22:6 is distinct from tachy (G5035) used in 'I come quickly.' Tachos describes the timeframe of the prophesied events; tachy describes the speed/certainty of Christ's personal coming.

    G5030 — tacheos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5030-tacheos.md

    Occurrences: 10

    Adverb meaning 'shortly, quickly, soon.' 10 NT occurrences, none in Revelation. Used by Paul in travel plans and warnings against rashness. Part of the tach- word family with tachos (G5034) and tachy (G5035).

    Semantic categories: temporal urgency, speed of action

    Relevance: Context for the tach- word family — tacheos is the Pauline form (epistolary), tachy is the Johannine/apocalyptic form (direct discourse), tachos is the LXX-influenced form (en tachei). Revelation uses tachy and tachos but not tacheos.

    G4592 — semeion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4592-semeion.md

    Occurrences: 77

    Neuter noun meaning 'sign, miracle, token.' 77 tokens in 69 verses across 11 books. Root of semaino (G4591, 'to signify'). In Revelation: 7 occurrences. Critically, Rev 1:1 uses esemainen (aorist of semaino) — 'he signified it' — indicating the mode of revelation is through signs/symbols. Revelation's content is sign-mediated communication, not direct literal description.

    Semantic categories: sign, meaning-bearing act, semiotic communication

    Key verses: Rev 1:1, Rev 12:1, Rev 12:3, Rev 13:13-14, Rev 15:1, Rev 16:14, Rev 19:20

    Relevance: The esemainen at 1:1 establishes the hermeneutical principle for the entire book: the content is 'signified' (communicated through signs/symbols). This connects to the eisin as identification (explaining what the signs mean) in the epexegetical reading of 1:19.

    G3708 — horao¶

In Revelation: eides (aorist active indicative 2nd sg, 'you saw') appears in 1:19 (ha eides, 'things which thou hast seen') and 1:20 (hous eides, 'which thou sawest').
