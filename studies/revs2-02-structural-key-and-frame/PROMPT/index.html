    Question¶

    Does Revelation 1:19 ("write the things which thou hast seen, and the things which are, and the things which shall be hereafter") provide a structural key for the entire book, and how does each clause map to sections of the text? How does the prologue-epilogue inclusio (Rev 1:1-8 / Rev 22:6-21) frame the visions — what verbal parallels connect the opening and closing, and how do they create a literary bracket? What is the relationship between the 1:19 threefold division and the fourfold en pneumati division from study 01?

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Seven prior studies provide directly relevant findings. revs-03 documented the threefold division of Rev 1:19 (TM012, TM013, TM016, SP008 Moderate validation) and concluded IC007: Rev 1:19 provides a general content-scope description rather than a strict structural outline. revs-04 documented 9 verbal parallels between prologue and epilogue (SP009 Strong validation) including ha dei genesthai (TM015), ho kairos eggys (TM017), Alpha-Omega title expansion (TM018), sevenfold makarios (TM019), tachy triple (TM020), charis grace-frame (TM021), martyreo word family (TM022), and the Dan 12:4 seal reversal (TM023). revs2-01 established the fourfold en pneumati division with two verb-pairs (ginomai vs. apophero), kai eidon catalog (~35 structural uses), meta tauta catalog (9 occurrences), and the Sinai-theophany formula. Web research identified three competing scholarly camps: traditional threefold (McLean), eclectic idealist (Beale — all three clauses describe the whole book), and chiastic/concentric (Strand, Schussler Fiorenza). Key unresolved question: Michaels' epexegetical reading of ha eisin — if it means 'what they are/mean' rather than 'things which are now,' the threefold-division reading is fundamentally undermined.

    Primary Verses (34 — full analysis required)¶

    Rev 1:19 and its immediate context: The crux verse with surrounding material — Christophany, threefold command, mystery explanation - Rev 1:19 — THE crux text — threefold temporal command (eides aorist / eisin present / mellei genesthai future) - Rev 1:20 — Mystery of seven stars/lampstands — eides echo from 1:19; explanatory clause blurs 'seen'/'are' boundary

    Prologue (Rev 1:1-8): Complete prologue for word-by-word inclusio comparison with epilogue - Rev 1:1 — Prologue opening — transmission chain (God → Christ → angel → John → servants); ha dei genesthai en tachei - Rev 1:2 — John's testimony — martyria word; links testimony/witness vocabulary chain - Rev 1:3 — First beatitude — makarios; 'words of this prophecy'; 'the time is at hand' (ho kairos eggys) - Rev 1:4 — Epistolary greeting — charis kai eirene; temporal title 'which is, was, is to come'; seven Spirits - Rev 1:5-6 — Christ as faithful witness (martys pistos); doxology - Rev 1:7 — Coming with clouds — Dan 7:13 + Zech 12:10 allusion; 'every eye shall see him' - Rev 1:8 — Alpha and Omega self-declaration; 'beginning and ending'; temporal title 'which is, was, is to come, the Almighty'

    Epilogue (Rev 22:6-21): Complete epilogue for word-by-word inclusio comparison with prologue - Rev 22:6 — Epilogue opening — 'faithful and true'; transmission chain echoes 1:1; ha dei genesthai en tachei - Rev 22:7 — Second epilogue beatitude — 'I come quickly'; 'keepeth sayings of the prophecy of this book' - Rev 22:8-9 — 'I John' self-identification; worship refusal parallel to 19:10; 'worship God' - Rev 22:10 — 'Seal not the sayings' — reversal of Dan 12:4; 'the time is at hand' echoes 1:3 - Rev 22:12-13 — 'I come quickly' + reward; Alpha and Omega expanded title — first/last + beginning/end - Rev 22:14 — Sixth beatitude — 'do his commandments'; tree of life; gates of the city - Rev 22:16 — 'I Jesus have sent mine angel to testify... in the churches' — transmission chain + ekklesia return - Rev 22:17 — Spirit and bride invitation — 'Come'; water of life; pneuma in epilogue - Rev 22:18-19 — Canon warning — adding/taking away from 'words of the prophecy of this book' - Rev 22:20 — 'He which testifieth... surely I come quickly'; response 'Even so, come, Lord Jesus' - Rev 22:21 — Epistolary grace benediction — charis closing; matches 1:4 grace opening

    En pneumati markers: All four en pneumati passages for fourfold division analysis - Rev 1:9-10 — First en pneumati marker; 'I John' self-identification; Patmos setting; 'word of God and testimony of Jesus' - Rev 17:1-3 — Third en pneumati marker — apenengken me en pneumati (wilderness); Babylon vision introduction - Rev 21:9-10 — Fourth en pneumati marker — apenengken me en pneumati (mountain); New Jerusalem vision; verbal parallel to 17:1-3

    Temporal title variations: All occurrences of the 'was, is, is to come' formula and its variants - Rev 1:4 — Epistolary greeting — charis kai eirene; temporal title 'which is, was, is to come'; seven Spirits - Rev 1:8 — Alpha and Omega self-declaration; 'beginning and ending'; temporal title 'which is, was, is to come, the Almighty'

    Daniel allusion passages: OT source passages for the ha dei genesthai and seal/unseal chains - Dan 2:28-29 — OT source for ha dei genesthai — 'what shall be in the latter days'; LXX verbal parallel - Dan 2:45 — 'What shall come to pass hereafter' — meta tauta echo; same prophetic disclosure formula - Dan 7:13 — 'One like the Son of man came with the clouds' — source for Rev 1:7 coming-with-clouds - Dan 12:4 — 'Shut up the words and seal the book' — command reversed at Rev 22:10

    Isaiah first/last passages: OT source for the Alpha/Omega first/last title - Isa 41:4 — 'I the LORD, the first, and with the last' — source for Alpha/Omega first/last title - Isa 44:6 — 'I am the first, and I am the last; beside me there is no God' — YHWH self-identification - Isa 48:12 — 'I am he; I am the first, I also am the last' — third Isaiah first/last passage

    Worship God parallel: Bauckham's Babylon/Jerusalem structural pair conclusions - Rev 22:8-9 — 'I John' self-identification; worship refusal parallel to 19:10; 'worship God'

    Additional Primary Verses: - Rev 1:11 — First graphon command — 'write in a book'; Alpha and Omega (TR); seven churches listed - Rev 4:1 — Major structural transition — meta tauta eidon + 'first voice'; 'things which must be hereafter' echoes 1:19 - Rev 4:2 — Second en pneumati marker — eutheos egenomyn en pneumati; only verse where meta tauta and en pneumati co-occur - Zech 12:10 — 'They shall look upon me whom they have pierced' — combined with Dan 7:13 at Rev 1:7

    Focus Areas¶

    1. The Greek clause structure of Revelation 1:19: This is the foundational linguistic question. Run greek_parser.py --verse 'REV 1:19' and greek_parser.py --clause 'REV 1:19' to get the exact morphological data. Three verb forms create the temporal framework: aorist eides ('you saw'), present eisin ('are'), and present mellei + aorist infinitive genesthai ('about to happen'). READ $WORD_STUDIES/G3195-mello.md for mello's semantic range — does mello + infinitive always denote futurity, or can it denote imminence or inevitability? READ $WORD_STUDIES/G1096-ginomai.md for ginomai's range. The critical question from Michaels is whether ha eisin can be read as 'what they are' (epexegetical, explaining what was seen) rather than 'things which are now' (temporal, referring to present realities). If epexegetical, 1:19 collapses from threefold to twofold: 'write what you saw — namely what they mean — and what is about to happen.' Compare Rev 1:20's immediate use of eides to explain the mystery of the lampstands — does 1:20 function as the fulfillment of the middle clause? Verses: Rev 1:19, Rev 1:20, Rev 4:1 Strong's: G1125, G3195, G1096, G1510
