    Verses¶

    Question¶

    How does Ezekiel serve as the most extensive OT structural template for Revelation? Map the parallel sequence: throne vision (Ezek 1 / Rev 4), scroll eating (Ezek 2:9-3:3 / Rev 10:8-10), temple measuring (Ezek 40-42 / Rev 11:1-2), Gog and Magog (Ezek 38-39 / Rev 20:7-10), river of life (Ezek 47:1-12 / Rev 22:1-2). What does Zechariah contribute? Map: horses and colors (Zech 1:8, 6:1-8 / Rev 6:1-8), lampstand (Zech 4:2 / Rev 1:12, 4:5), olive trees (Zech 4:3,11-14 / Rev 11:4), stone with seven eyes (Zech 3:9 / Rev 5:6), measuring line (Zech 2:1-2 / Rev 11:1). Where does Revelation transform rather than simply repeat its source?

    The Throne-Chariot Vision (Full Chapter): Four Living Creatures, Wheels, Firmament, Divine Glory¶

    See sub-entries Ezek 1:4-6, Ezek 1:7-14, Ezek 1:15-21, Ezek 1:22-28 for detailed verse analysis.

    Context: Ezekiel 1 — Ezekiel's inaugural merkavah/throne-chariot vision by the river Chebar in Babylon (593 BC). Full chapter overview entry.

    Notes: VP187 Strong (8 elements shared with Rev 4). See sub-entries for detailed per-section analysis. Sirach 49:8 confirms merkavah status. Source merger for Rev 4: Ezek 1 + Isa 6 = composite.

    Opening Theophany: Stormy Wind, Cloud, Fire; Four Living Creatures Appear¶

    Ezekiel 1:4 And I looked, and, behold, a whirlwind came out of the north, a great cloud, and a fire infolding itself, and a brightness [was] about it, and out of the midst thereof as the colour of amber, out of the midst of the fire. 1:5 Also out of the midst thereof [came] the likeness of four living creatures. And this [was] their appearance; they had the likeness of a man. 1:6 And every one had four faces, and every one had four wings.

    Context: Ezekiel 1 — Opening of the merkavah vision. Theophanic storm from the north; four living creatures (chayyot) emerge. The foundational throne-chariot vision (593 BC, by river Chebar).

    Notes: H2416 chay root: chayyot (living creatures) — the term identifying these beings before Ezek 10:20 names them kerubim. Four creatures with four faces (v.6) — face-count is the key transformation marker for Rev 4:7 (one face each). LXX renders chayyot as zōa (G2226) — Revelation adopts this term, deliberately bypassing the cherub identity confirmed in Ezek 10.

    Creatures' Feet, Hands, Faces; Fire and Lightning Between Them¶

    Ezekiel 1:7 And their feet [were] straight feet; and the sole of their feet [was] like the sole of a calf's foot: and they sparkled like the colour of burnished brass. 1:8 And [they had] the hands of a man under their wings on their four sides; and they four had their faces and their wings. 1:9 Their wings [were] joined one to another; they turned not when they went; they went every one straight forward. 1:10 As for the likeness of their faces, they four had the face of a man, and the face of a lion, on the right side: and they four had the face of an ox on the left side; they four also had the face of an eagle. 1:11 Thus [were] their faces: and their wings [were] stretched upward; two [wings] of every one [were] joined one to another, and two covered their bodies. 1:12 And they went every one straight forward: whither the spirit was to go, they went; [and] they turned not when they went. 1:13 As for the likeness of the living creatures, their appearance [was] like burning coals of fire, [and] like the appearance of lamps: it went up and down among the living creatures; and the fire was bright, and out of the fire went forth lightning. 1:14 And the living creatures ran and returned as the appearance of a flash of lightning.

    Context: Ezekiel 1 — Creature morphology: calf-foot, burnished brass, four faces (man/lion/ox/eagle), four wings. Fire and lightning circulate among them.

    Notes: 1:10 face order: man/lion (right) / ox (left) / eagle — four faces per creature simultaneously. Rev 4:7 assigns ONE face per creature (lion/calf/man/eagle) — the ox becomes calf (moschos G3448). H6440 paniym (faces) is the morphological key: each creature has a paniym-set of four orientations. 1:13 fire/lightning → Rev 4:5 lightnings from the throne. 4 wings here; Rev 4:8 has 6 wings (source: Isa 6:2, not Ezek 1).

    Wheels Full of Eyes Alongside the Creatures¶

    Ezekiel 1:15 Now as I beheld the living creatures, behold one wheel upon the earth by the living creatures, with his four faces. 1:16 The appearance of the wheels and their work [was] like unto the colour of a beryl: and they four had one likeness: and their appearance and their work [was] as it were a wheel in the middle of a wheel. 1:17 When they went, they went upon their four sides: [and] they turned not when they went. 1:18 As for their rings, they were so high that they were dreadful; and their rings [were] full of eyes round about them four. 1:19 And when the living creatures went, the wheels went by them: and when the living creatures were lifted up from the earth, the wheels were lifted up. 1:20 Whithersoever the spirit was to go, they went, thither [was their] spirit to go; and the wheels were lifted up over against them: for the spirit of the living creature [was] in the wheels. 1:21 When those went, [these] went; and when those stood, [these] stood; and when those were lifted up from the earth, the wheels were lifted up over against them: for the spirit of the living creature [was] in the wheels.

    Context: Ezekiel 1 — The ophanim/wheels: beryl-colored, wheel-within-a-wheel, rings full of eyes. The wheels move in perfect synchrony with the creatures because the spirit of the creature is in the wheels.

    Notes: 1:18 eye-location: the WHEELS (rings/rims) are full of eyes — NOT the creature bodies. Hebrew: gabbot (rims/rings) + mele'ot einayim (full of eyes). This is the critical eye-location difference from Rev 4:6 where eyes are on creature bodies ('full of eyes before and behind'). Revelation RELOCATES eyes from the wheels to the creatures themselves — a deliberate transformation. Hebrew parser: gabbot from gab (G-N-mp-Abs) = rims/curves; mele'ot (Qal Ptcp fp) = being full.

    Crystal Firmament Above Creatures; Divine Throne; Rainbow of Glory¶

    Ezekiel 1:22 And the likeness of the firmament upon the heads of the living creature [was] as the colour of the terrible crystal, stretched forth over their heads above. 1:23 And under the firmament [were] their wings straight, the one toward the other: every one had two, which covered on this side, and every one had two, which covered on that side, their bodies. 1:24 And when they went, I heard the noise of their wings, like the noise of great waters, as the voice of the Almighty, the voice of speech, as the noise of an host: when they stood, they let down their wings. 1:25 And there was a voice from the firmament that [was] over their heads, when they stood, [and] had let down their wings. 1:26 And above the firmament that [was] over their heads [was] the likeness of a throne, as the appearance of a sapphire stone: and upon the likeness of the throne [was] the likeness as the appearance of a man above upon it. 1:27 And I saw as the colour of amber, as the appearance of fire round about within it, from the appearance of his loins even upward, and from the appearance of his loins even downward, I saw as it were the appearance of fire, and it had brightness round about. 1:28 As the appearance of the bow that is in the cloud in the day of rain, so [was] the appearance of the brightness round about. This [was] the appearance of the likeness of the glory of the LORD. And when I saw [it], I fell upon my face, and I heard a voice of one that spake.

    Context: Ezekiel 1 — The raqia (firmament) above the creatures like terrible crystal; the divine throne above the firmament; the kavod surrounded by rainbow. This is the climax of the merkavah vision.

    Notes: 1:22 raqia (firmament/crystal expanse) is ABOVE the creatures. Rev 4:6 has the sea of glass like crystal BEFORE/BELOW the throne — positional transformation: Ezek firmament above → Rev sea below. 1:28 rainbow = Ezek 1:28 → Rev 4:3 rainbow around the throne, Rev 10:1 rainbow on the angel's head. 1:24 'voice like the noise of great waters' → Ezek 43:2 repeats this phrase for the returning kavod; Rev 1:15 applies it to Christ. VP187 Strong (8 elements). Sirach 49:8: 'It was Ezekiel who saw the glorious vision, which was shewed him upon the chariot of the cherubims' — confirms merkavah identity.

    The Scroll Written on Front and Back¶

    Ezekiel 2:9 And when I looked, behold, an hand [was] sent unto me; and, lo, a roll of a book [was] therein; 2:10 And he spread it before me; and it [was] written within and without: and [there was] written therein lamentations, and mourning, and woe.

    Context: Ezekiel 2 — The scroll-eating commission. Before eating, Ezekiel is shown the scroll. Its content: lamentations, mourning, and woe.

    Notes: Scroll morphology identical to Rev 5:1 ('a book written within and on the backside'). Content of Ezek's scroll is pure judgment (lamentations, mourning, woe); Rev 5:1 scroll has seven seals. The two-sided writing is the lexical link. This precedes the eating command in Ezek 3:1-3.

    Eat the Scroll — Sweet as Honey¶

    Ezekiel 3:1 Moreover he said unto me, Son of man, eat that thou findest; eat this roll, and go speak unto the house of Israel. 3:2 So I opened my mouth, and he caused me to eat that roll. 3:3 And he said unto me, Son of man, cause thy belly to eat, and fill thy bowels with this roll that I give thee. Then did I eat [it]; and it was in my mouth as honey for sweetness.

    Context: Ezekiel 3 — The eating command. Honey sweetness is the ONLY taste experience. No bitterness is mentioned here.

    Notes: AN037 Strong — core parallel to Rev 10:9-10. Key finding: Ezekiel's scroll is purely sweet ('as honey for sweetness'). No bitterness in the scroll itself or its eating. The transformation in Rev 10:10 adds intrinsic belly bitterness. Hebrew: debash (honey) — LXX uses glykasmos/meli. G1099 glykys chain confirmed.

    Bitterness of Spirit After Eating¶

    Ezekiel 3:14 So the spirit lifted me up, and took me away, and I went in bitterness, in the heat of my spirit; but the hand of the LORD was strong upon me.

    Context: Ezekiel 3 — After eating the scroll and receiving his commission, Ezekiel describes his emotional state as he goes to the captives.

    Notes: CRITICAL for transformation analysis: bitterness here is (1) EMOTIONAL (bitterness of spirit/ruach), (2) SEQUENTIAL — comes AFTER the eating, not during, (3) describes the PROPHET'S STATE not the scroll's property.     Notes: AN037 Strong — Ezek 2:9-3:3 primary source. KEY TRANSFORMATION: Ezek 3:3 scroll is purely sweet ('as honey for sweetness'); Rev 10:10 adds belly bitterness (absent from Ezek 3:3).     Reed Like a Rod; Measure Naos; Exclude Outer Court; Two Witnesses = Two Olive Trees and Two Lampstands¶

    Revelation 11:1 And there was given me a reed like unto a rod: and the angel stood, saying, Rise, and measure the temple of God, and the altar, and them that worship therein. 11:2 But the court which is without the temple leave out, and measure it not; for it is given unto the Gentiles: and the holy city shall they tread under foot forty [and] two months. 11:3 And I will give [power] unto my two witnesses, and they shall prophesy a thousand two hundred [and] threescore days, clothed in sackcloth. 11:4 These are the two olive trees, and the two candlesticks standing before the God of the earth.

    Context: Revelation 11 — Temple measuring and two witnesses. The outer court (aulē) is excluded. The witnesses are identified as both olive trees AND lampstands simultaneously.

    Notes: 11:1 'kalamos homoios rabdō' (reed like a rod) — kalamos (G2563) = same word as Ezek 40:3 LXX (qaneh → kalamos). Strong lexical link. THE CRITICAL TRANSFORMATION: Rev 11:2 explicitly EXCLUDES the outer court ('leave out, measure it not') — Ezekiel measures EVERYTHING including outer court (Ezek 40:6-16). Measuring becomes theologically charged: what is measured = protected (naos + altar + worshippers); what is excluded = given to Gentiles for trampling (42 months). aulē (G833) — only occurrence in Revelation. 11:4 'hai duo elaiai kai hai duo lychniai' — AN038 Explicit, near-verbatim Zech 4:14 LXX. MERGER: in Zech 4, olive trees and lampstand are SEPARATE; in Rev 11:4 witnesses ARE BOTH simultaneously. TM214 constructio ad sensum: hestotes (masculine participle) with feminine subjects elaiai/lychniai — witnesses are PERSONS not objects.
