    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    How does Ezekiel serve as the most extensive OT structural template for Revelation? Map the parallel sequence: throne vision (Ezek 1 / Rev 4), scroll eating (Ezek 2:9-3:3 / Rev 10:8-10), temple measuring (Ezek 40-42 / Rev 11:1-2), Gog and Magog (Ezek 38-39 / Rev 20:7-10), river of life (Ezek 47:1-12 / Rev 22:1-2). What does Zechariah contribute? Map: horses and colors (Zech 1:8, 6:1-8 / Rev 6:1-8), lampstand (Zech 4:2 / Rev 1:12, 4:5), olive trees (Zech 4:3,11-14 / Rev 11:4), stone with seven eyes (Zech 3:9 / Rev 5:6), measuring line (Zech 2:1-2 / Rev 11:1). Where does Revelation transform rather than simply repeat its source?

    G2226 — ζῶον¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2226-zoon.md

    Occurrences: 23

    Living creature/animal. Neuter derivative of zaō (to live). 17 of 20 verses in Revelation (85%), all in the throne-room complex. KJV translates 'beast/beasts' (ambiguous — also used for G2342 thērion, the apocalyptic beast). Modern translations distinguish: G2226 = living creature, G2342 = beast. In Revelation, the four zōa surround the throne with one face each, six wings, full of eyes, and lead worship (Rev 4:6-8). They announce the seal judgments (Rev 6:1ff) and hold vials (Rev 5:8).

    Semantic categories: heavenly throne-attendants, supernatural living beings, worship leaders, sacrificial animals

    Key verses: Rev 4:6-8, Rev 5:6, Rev 5:8, Heb 13:11

    Relevance: The LXX renders H2416 chay (living creature) as zōon. In Ezek 1, the creatures are called chayyot (H2416). In Ezek 10, they are identified as kerubim (H3742), LXX cheroubim (G5502). Revelation uses zōon (G2226) — NOT cheroubim. This deliberate terminological shift away from the cherub-term while preserving the creature-imagery is a key transformation indicator.

    G2563 — κάλαμος¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2563-kalamos.md

    Occurrences: 12

    Reed — the plant or its stem; by implication a pen. 12 NT occurrences: 8 in Gospels (weakness/fragility; mockery in Passion), 3 in Revelation (measuring instrument at Rev 11:1, 21:15, 21:16), 1 in 3 John (pen). The LXX renders H7070 qaneh as G2563 kalamos (count 16, PMI 9.09) — the primary lexical chain linking Ezek 40:3 to Rev 11:1. Rev 11:1 'kalamos homoios rabdō' (reed like a rod) draws from this chain.

    Semantic categories: measuring instrument, symbol of weakness, writing instrument, mockery in Passion

    Key verses: Rev 11:1, Rev 21:15-16, Ezek 40:3 (LXX)

    Relevance: The LXX chain H7070 qaneh → G2563 kalamos establishes a Strong lexical link between Ezek 40:3 (measuring rod) and Rev 11:1 (reed like a rod). Rev 21:15 upgrades to 'golden reed' (chrysoun kalamon) — deliberate contrast with Rev 11:1's mortal reed. Zech 2:1 uses a different measuring instrument (line/cord), not kalamos — confirming Ezek 40 as the primary measuring source.

    G1636 — ἐλαία¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1636-elaia.md

    Occurrences: 15

    Olive tree or olive fruit. 15 NT occurrences: 11 in Synoptics as toponym (Mount of Olives), 2 in Romans (metaphor for Jew/Gentile relation), 1 in James (olive berries), 1 in Revelation (symbolic: two olive trees = two witnesses, Rev 11:4). LXX chain: H2132 zayith → G1636 elaía (23 co-occurrences, PMI 9.24 — strong alignment). Rev 11:4 uses hai duo elaiai — SAME Greek word as LXX Zech 4:3,11-14.

    Semantic categories: olive tree (literal), olive grove/mountain toponym, metaphor for Israel/Gentile, prophetic witnesses (Rev 11:4)

    Key verses: Rev 11:4, Zech 4:3, Zech 4:11-14, Rom 11:17-24

    Relevance: The LXX chain H2132 zayith → G1636 elaía (PMI 9.24) establishes the lexical bridge from Zech 4:3,11-14 to Rev 11:4. Rev 11:4 uses the identical Greek word as LXX Zechariah. This is an Explicit allusion (AN038): near-verbatim LXX vocabulary. Revelation's single symbolic use of elaía (Rev 11:4) stands apart from all other NT occurrences — Revelation is the only NT book to use elaía for persons/symbols.

    H7070 — קָנֶה¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7070-qaneh.md

    Occurrences: 87

    Reed, rod, measuring rod, branch. 87 tokens across 10 OT books. Primary senses: (1) water-reed/plant (fragility imagery); (2) aromatic calamus (spice compound qaneh-bosem); (3) menorah arm/branch (Exodus); (4) measuring rod (Ezekiel 40-42). Ezekiel and Exodus share the highest density (0.9/100v). LXX map: H7070 → G2563 kalamos (count 16, PMI 9.09, score 25.76) — highest-ranking LXX translation. Also maps to G3087 lychnia (5x, PMI 6.85) for the menorah-branch sense.

    Semantic categories: measuring rod (Ezek 40-42), menorah arm/branch (Exod 25-37), aromatic calamus (Exod 30:23), water reed (fragility)

    Key verses: Ezek 40:3, Ezek 40:5, Exod 25:31-36, Exod 30:23

    Relevance: Ezek 40:3 'measuring reed' is the Hebrew source for Rev 11:1 measuring scene. LXX renders qaneh as kalamos (G2563) — confirmed by lxx-map data (16 occurrences, PMI 9.09). This is the lexical chain establishing AN092 Strong for Ezek 40:3 / Rev 11:1. The menorah-branch sense (G3087 lychnia) adds an unexpected connection: the same Hebrew word underlies both the measuring rod and the lampstand branches.

    H5104 — נָהָר¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5104-nahar.md

    Occurrences: 132

    River, stream, flood. 132 tokens across 25 OT books. Dominant rendering: 'the river' (39x, 29.5%). Named rivers: Euphrates, Nile, Tigris, Eden's four rivers (Gen 2:10-14). Theological uses: rivers of divine judgment, the Eden-river as life source, Ezek 47 healing river. LXX map: H5104 → G4215 potamos (101 co-occurrences, PMI 7.03, score 32.49) — dominant and confirmed. The Ezek 47:1 nahar that flows from the sanctuary becomes the Rev 22:1 potamos from the throne.

    Semantic categories: named geographic rivers, Eden life-source river, rivers as divine judgment, eschatological healing river (Ezek 47)

    Key verses: Gen 2:10, Ezek 47:1, Rev 22:1

    Relevance: The H5104 nahar → G4215 potamos LXX chain (101 co-occurrences, PMI 7.03) is the strongest lexical link in the river-of-life parallel. Ezek 47:1 nahar → LXX potamos → Rev 22:1 potamos: SAME Greek word. This confirms AN094 Strong for Ezek 47 / Rev 22. The source-transformation (temple threshold → throne) is a theological shift while the vocabulary chain remains intact.

    G4087 — πικραίνω¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4087-pikraino.md

    Occurrences: 6

    To embitter — literally or figuratively. 6 NT occurrences: 4 in Revelation, 2 in Colossians. Rev uses: Rev 8:11 (waters made bitter by Wormwood), Rev 10:9 ('it shall make thy belly bitter'), Rev 10:10 ('my belly was bitter'). Col 3:19 (husbands not to be bitter toward wives). Primary LXX antecedent: H4843 marar (to be bitter, PMI 9.18). Ezek 3:14 uses a cognate concept (bitterness of spirit) but with different Hebrew vocabulary (mar/chemah).

    Semantic categories: prophetic bitterness — internalized revelation (Rev 10:9-10), judgment on waters (Rev 8:11), relational embitterment (Col 3:19)

    Key verses: Rev 10:9, Rev 10:10, Rev 8:11

    Relevance: G4087 pikraino at Rev 10:9-10 marks the key transformation from Ezek 3:3 (sweet only) to the sweet-and-bitter scroll. In Ezekiel, bitterness is emotional/sequential (Ezek 3:14, spirit/ruach). In Revelation, pikraino describes bodily digestion — the bitterness is immediate and intrinsic to the scroll itself. This is the transformation verb: Ezek has no pikraino in the scroll-eating scene; Revelation adds it.

    G5515 — χλωρός¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5515-chlōrós.md

    Occurrences: 4

    Greenish — verdant (living vegetation) or pale/death-colored. 4 NT occurrences: Mark 6:39 (green grass), Rev 8:7 and 9:4 (green vegetation judgment), Rev 6:8 (pale horse — death-pallor sense). The death-pallor use at Rev 6:8 is unique in NT. LXX Zechariah does NOT use chloros for the grisled/bay horses (barod/'amots) — confirmed. LXX uses psaroi kai poikiloi ('speckled and many-colored') for Zech 6:3's grisled horses.

    Semantic categories: living vegetation (green), pallor of death (Rev 6:8)

    Key verses: Rev 6:8, Rev 8:7, Mark 6:39

    Relevance: chloros does NOT appear in LXX Zechariah for the horse colors. The fourth horseman's color (chloros, Rev 6:8) is Revelation's own addition with no Zechariah LXX counterpart. This proves the fourth horse color represents a deliberate editorial choice by John — the deathly pallor (chloros in death sense) combined with Death and Hades as riders. Revelation transforms Zechariah's patrol horses into judgment-executors and adds a fourth color not present in its source.

    G4215 — ποταμός¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4215-potamós.md

    Occurrences: 16

    River, stream, flood, current. 16 NT occurrences across 7 books. Revelation has 8 (50%) — the most of any NT book (0.8/100v). Rev uses: Euphrates (9:14, 16:12), dragon's flood against the woman (12:15-16), John's water of life vision (22:1-2). LXX primary source: H5104 nahar → G4215 potamos (101 co-occurrences, PMI 7.03) — the dominant and confirmed LXX chain for nahar.

    Semantic categories: literal rivers (Jordan, Euphrates), destructive floodwaters, spiritual life/provision (river of life), perils and hardship

    Key verses: Rev 22:1, John 7:38, Rev 12:15

    Relevance: The H5104 → G4215 LXX chain (PMI 7.03) is the lexical backbone of the Ezek 47 / Rev 22 parallel. Both use potamos for the eschatological healing river. Rev 22:1 potamos proceeds from the throne — the source transformation preserves the vocabulary while changing the source from temple to throne.

    G86 — ᾅδης¶

    Occurrences: 11

    Hades — the realm of the dead. 11 NT occurrences, KJV translates 'hell' (7x), 'of hell' (2x), 'O grave' (1x), 'Hell' (1x). Hades appears paired with Death at Rev 6:8 (on the pale horse) and Rev 20:13-14 (cast into lake of fire). This pairing is absent from Zechariah. Hades is Revelation's own theological addition to the horse-colors complex.

    Semantic categories: realm of the dead, paired with Death in judgment scenes

    Key verses: Rev 6:8, Rev 20:13-14, Matt 16:18

    Relevance: Death and Hades paired on the pale horse (Rev 6:8) — this pairing is absent from Zechariah. Rev 6:8 is Revelation's own editorial addition beyond its Zechariah source material, adding eschatological weight (Hades as the destination of the dead) to the fourth horseman's domain.

    H3742 — כְּרוּב¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3742-keruwb.md

    Occurrences: 105

    Cherub/cherubim. 105 OT occurrences; 41 in Ezekiel (highest concentration). Three main contexts: (1) Eden guardian (Gen 3:24); (2) ark/tabernacle cherubim (Exod 25; 1 Kgs 6-8); (3) Ezekiel's throne-chariot beings (Ezek 1, 10, 28). LXX renders H3742 as cheroubim (G5502 — transliteration, not translation). Ezek 10:20 explicitly identifies the Ezek 1 chayyot as kerubim. Revelation uses zōon (G2226) for the same beings, deliberately bypassing the cherub term.

    Semantic categories: Eden guardian cherub, ark/sanctuary cherubim, throne-chariot cherubim (Ezek 1/10), covering cherub (Ezek 28)

    Key verses: Ezek 1:5, Ezek 10:20, Ezek 28:14-16, Gen 3:24

    Relevance: H3742 kerub is the Hebrew identity-term for the Ezek 1/10 creatures, confirmed by Ezek 10:20 ('I knew that they were the cherubims'). The LXX transliterates as cheroubim (G5502) — NOT translating. Revelation's deliberate choice to use zōon (G2226, 'living creature') rather than cheroubim marks a terminological transformation: the throne-room beings are presented through their vitality (living) rather than their identity as sanctuary-cherubim. This avoids conflating the heavenly beings with the earthly ark-cover figures.

    H2416 — חַי¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2416-chay.md

    Occurrences: 531

    Living, alive, life. 531 tokens across 449 verses; 51 in Ezekiel. Root of chayyot (living creatures, Ezek 1) and LXX zōon (G2226). Three main senses: (1) adjective: alive/living; (2) noun: life; (3) construct plural chayyot = living creatures/beasts. Ezekiel uses chay extensively — both as the adjective 'living' and as chayyot (living creatures). LXX: H2416 → G2226 zōon (primary rendering for 'living creature').

    Semantic categories: alive/living (adjective), life (noun), living creatures (chayyot plural construct), divine epithet 'the living God'

    Key verses: Ezek 1:5, Ezek 1:13-14, Rev 4:6-8

    Relevance: H2416 chay is the root of chayyot, Ezekiel's term for the four creatures of the throne-chariot. The LXX renders chayyot as zōa (plural of G2226 zōon). Revelation adopts this LXX term: the four zōa of Rev 4:6-8 are linguistically identified with the chayyot of Ezek 1 through the LXX vocabulary chain. This is why Revelation uses zōon and NOT cheroubim — it follows the LXX's rendering of the chayyot-term (H2416-rooted) rather than the kerub-term (H3742-rooted) as applied in Ezek 10.

    G3354 — μετρέω¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3354-metréō.md

    Occurrences: 10

    To measure. 10 NT occurrences; 5 in Revelation (Rev 11:1, 11:2, 21:15, 21:16, 21:17). The measuring verb in the two Revelation temple/city measuring scenes. In Rev 11:1 the command 'measure' (metrison, Aor Act Impv) applies to the naos, altar, and worshippers. In Rev 11:2 'do not measure' (mē metrisēs, Aor Act Subj) applies to the outer court. In Rev 21:15-17 an angel measures the New Jerusalem with a golden reed.

    Semantic categories: physical measurement, theological measurement (protection/judgment), eschatological city measurement

    Key verses: Rev 11:1, Rev 11:2, Rev 21:15-17

    Relevance: G3354 metreo is the verb linking the Ezek 40-42 measuring scene to Rev 11:1-2 and Rev 21:15-17. In Ezek 40, the measuring function is comprehensive (all precincts). Rev 11:1-2 introduces a theological selectivity: what is measured (naos + altar + worshippers) = protected; what is NOT measured (outer court) = given to Gentiles. The measuring verb becomes a marker of divine claim and protection, not merely architectural survey. Rev 21:15-17 completes the arc with the full glorious measurement of the eternal city.

    G3087 — λυχνία¶

    G2985 — λαμπάς¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2985-lampas.md

    Lamp, torch, lantern. 9 NT occurrences: Matt 25 (5 wise/5 foolish virgins' lamps, 6x), John 18:3 (Judas' torches), Acts 20:8 (lamps in upper room), Rev 4:5 (seven lamps of fire before the throne), Rev 8:10 (great star burning as a lamp). G2985 is the LAMP itself (the burning implement), whereas G3087 lychnia is the STAND that holds the lamp. LXX rendering of Hebrew ner (tabernacle lamps) and nerot (multiple lamps).

    Semantic categories: individual burning lamp/torch, tabernacle lamp (LXX ner → lampas), virginal parable lamps, heavenly throne-room lamps (Rev 4:5)

    Key verses: Rev 4:5, Matt 25:1-8

    Relevance: G2985 lampas at Rev 4:5 ('seven lamps of fire burning before the throne') = the individual burning flames identified as 'the seven Spirits of God.' This is the Zechariah verbal chain applied: Zech 4:2 seven lamps (lampades = nerot in LXX) → Zech 4:6 'my Spirit' → Zech 4:10 'eyes of the LORD' → Rev 4:5 seven lampades = seven Spirits → Rev 5:6 seven eyes = seven Spirits. Lampas (individual lamp) is distinct from lychnia (lampstand): the lampstand holds the lamps; Rev 4:5 focuses on the burning lamps themselves as symbols of the Spirit.

    H6440 — פָּנִים¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6440-paniym.md

    Occurrences: 2145

    Face, faces, presence. 2145 tokens; always plural in form. 129 occurrences in Ezekiel (among highest density books). Primary use: spatial preposition 'before/in front of' (~61%). As noun: face/faces (~23%), presence (~3%), countenance (~2%). Ezek 1:10 uses paniym for the four faces of each creature: 'the likeness of their faces (penihem) — faces of a man and faces of a lion on the right side... faces of an ox... faces of an eagle.' The four-faces-per-creature morphology is encoded in repeated paniym constructs.

    Semantic categories: face (physical), divine presence (paniym YHWH), spatial preposition (before), face-count in throne-chariot beings

    Key verses: Ezek 1:10, Ezek 10:14, Exod 33:14-20

    Relevance: H6440 paniym at Ezek 1:10 encodes the four-face-per-creature structure: each creature simultaneously shows the face of man, lion, ox, and eagle. This is the morphological source for Rev 4:7's one-face-per-creature individualization. Hebrew paniym is always plural (morphologically) but refers to the single face of a person or being. In Ezek 1:10 the four-faces-of-each-creature are expressed through repeated paniym-construct phrases. Rev 4:7 assigns one face to each of the four creatures (lion/calf/man/eagle) — the COLLECTION of four faces is distributed among four beings rather than concentrated in each being. This is the transformation at the paniym level.

    G4394 — προφητεία¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4394-propheteia.md

    Occurrences: 19

    Prophecy, prophesying. 19 NT occurrences; 7 in Revelation (highest count per book). Revelation self-designates as 'the prophecy of this book' (Rev 1:3; 22:7,18,19) and the two witnesses 'shall prophesy' (G4395 prophēteuō, Rev 11:3). The noun propheteia appears in Rev 11:6 'days of their prophecy' referring to the two witnesses' ministry. The scroll-eating commission (Rev 10:11) generates the prophetic ministry (Rev 11:3).

    Semantic categories: book of Revelation as 'this prophecy', two witnesses' prophetic ministry, spiritual gift, OT prophetic scripture

    Key verses: Rev 1:3, Rev 11:6, Rev 22:7, Rev 22:18

    Relevance: G4394 propheteia connects the scroll-eating commission (Rev 10:11 'thou must prophesy again' — G4395 prophēteuō) to the two-witness ministry (Rev 11:3,6). The chain: Ezek 2-3 scroll-eating → commission to speak; Rev 10:9-11 scroll-eating → 'prophesy again'; Rev 11:3 'they shall prophesy 1,260 days' — the commission of Rev 10:11 is embodied in the two witnesses. Rev 1:3 identifies the entire book as a propheteia, framing all subsequent visions within the prophetic word tradition. Rev 22:18-19's closing warnings treat 'the words of the prophecy of this book' as a canonical unit.
