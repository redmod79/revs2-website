    Question¶

    How does the bowl prelude (Rev 15:1-8) establish a Day of Atonement typology? How do the seven bowls compare systematically with the seven trumpets — shared target domains and escalation from 1/3 to total? What does the seventh bowl's gegonen ("It is done") declare? How does Rev 16:15 and the Armageddon gathering establish the bowls as pre-advent events?

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Nine prior studies provide extensive relevant findings. Three studies being absorbed (revs-22, revs-23, revs-24) document 111 structural elements including the 7-element trumpet-bowl parallel sequence (SP055, Strong), fraction escalation from 1/3 to total (TM075/TM107), the three completion declarations (SP048), and the superlative earthquake (TM094). Three revs2 studies (03, 10, 11) establish the sanctuary progression, censer reversal, vessel transformation arc (SP119), and theophany formula escalation. The Lev 16:17 vs. Exo 40:34-35 source question for Rev 15:8 is documented with the DOA reading structurally stronger given the absence of exclusion in Rev 4-5 (revs2-03). Web research identified Leithart's 'inverted atonement' thesis, Peerbolte's JSNT article on bowls as penultimate pre-parousia stage, Paulien's bowl-Exodus plague mappings, and the gegonen/tetelestai distinction. Most traditional commentators default to Exo 40 or Isa 6 for Rev 15:8 rather than Lev 16:17.

    Focus Areas¶

    1. Day of Atonement typology in the bowl prelude (Rev 15:1-8): This is the central typological question. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G3485-naos.md for the naos vs. hieron distinction — naos specifies the inner sanctuary. Parse Rev 15:5 for the unique triple genitive naos tes skenes tou martyriou (TM100) — this phrase never appears elsewhere in the NT. Compare Rev 15:8 'no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues...were fulfilled' with Lev 16:17 'there shall be no man in the tabernacle of the congregation when he goeth in to make an atonement...until he come out.' Note the structural parallel: both have (a) exclusion of persons, (b) from the sanctuary space, (c) during a specific ritual action, (d) with a temporal limit (until).
