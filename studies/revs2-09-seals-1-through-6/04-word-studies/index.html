    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    What is the internal pattern of the four horsemen (seals 1-4)? Document the formula each horseman follows (living creature says 'Come,' horse appears, rider described, effect stated). How does the fifth seal's altar cry ('How long, O Lord?') introduce the theodicy question that drives the rest of the book? What role does the interlude between seals 6 and 7 (the 144,000 and the great multitude, Rev 7) play — how does it answer the question of 6:17 ('who shall be able to stand?')? What is the structural relationship between the seals and the Olivet Discourse (Matt 24)?

    G2462 — hippos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2462-hippos.md

    Occurrences: 16

    Horse — central image in seals 1-4. Used for all four horsemen plus the heavenly armies in Rev 19:14.

    Semantic categories: apocalyptic imagery, military/warfare, divine judgment

    Key verses: Rev 6:2, Rev 6:4, Rev 6:5, Rev 6:8, Rev 19:11, Rev 19:14

    Relevance: The four horses define the first four seals. The color vocabulary (leukos, pyrrhos, melas, chloros) distinguishes each seal.

    G3528 — nikao¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3528-nikao.md

    Occurrences: 28

    To overcome, conquer, prevail. 17 of 28 NT uses in Revelation. The nikao arc runs from overcomer promises in letters → pivot at 5:5 (enikesen) → first seal rider (6:2, nikon) → conflict uses → culmination at 17:14 and 21:7.

    Semantic categories: eschatological victory, cosmic victory, military conquest, moral victory

    Key verses: Rev 6:2, Rev 5:5, Rev 12:11, Rev 17:14, Rev 2:7

    Relevance: The first horseman 'goes forth conquering and to conquer' (nikon kai hina nikese). The nikao vocabulary is a structural thread (revs2-05) bearing on the first horseman identity question.

    G4973 — sphragis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4973-sphragis.md

    Occurrences: 16

    Seal — 13 of 16 NT uses in Revelation. Functions as authentication mark, closure device on the scroll, and divine ownership mark on foreheads.

    Semantic categories: authentication, divine concealment, sequential judgment, divine ownership

    Key verses: Rev 5:1, Rev 6:1, Rev 7:2, Rev 9:4

    Relevance: The sphragis concept defines both the scroll's seal-closures and the 144,000's protective seal. The semantic range (authentication → protection → judgment sequence) spans the entire seal narrative.

    G721 — arnion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G721-arnion.md

    Occurrences: 30

    Lamb — 27 of 30 NT uses in Revelation. Nearly exclusive christological title. The Lamb opens the seals, is worshipped, judges, and marries.

    Semantic categories: enthroned sovereign, slain redeemer, object of worship, warrior-judge, bridegroom

    Key verses: Rev 5:6, Rev 6:1, Rev 6:16, Rev 7:9, Rev 7:14, Rev 7:17

    Relevance: The Lamb's dual identity (slain yet enthroned, redeeming yet judging) is the theological axis of the seal sequence. 'Wrath of the Lamb' (6:16) is paradoxical — the sacrificial victim exercises judgment.

    G2379 — thysiastērion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2379-thysiastērion.md

    Occurrences: 23

    Altar — 8 of 23 NT uses in Revelation. Distinguished between unqualified altar (6:9, burnt offering) and qualified 'golden altar' (8:3, incense). The altar vindication arc tracks through 6:9 → 8:3 → 16:7.

    Semantic categories: temple sacrifice, altar of incense, martyrdom scene, heavenly sanctuary

    Key verses: Rev 6:9, Rev 8:3, Rev 9:13, Rev 16:7

    Relevance: The unqualified altar in Rev 6:9 identifies as the burnt offering altar (AN027 Strong). The altar that receives martyrs' blood later confirms God's justice (16:7) and its vindication completes at 19:2.

    G5590 — psyche¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5590-psyche.md

    Occurrences: 104

    Soul/life — the 'souls under the altar' (psychas ton esphagmenon) in Rev 6:9. Used in the OT tradition of blood-as-life (Gen 9:4, Lev 17:11).

    Semantic categories: life/self, blood-life tradition, martyrdom

    Key verses: Rev 6:9, Rev 20:4

    Relevance: The 'souls under the altar' are interpreted within the blood-cry personification tradition (Gen 4:10) — the martyrs' blood cries like Abel's. The sphazo vocabulary places their death in sacrificial register.

    G4969 — sphazo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4969-sphazo.md

    Occurrences: 10

    To butcher/slaughter — 8 of 10 NT uses in Revelation. The perfect passive participle esphagmenon links Abel, the Lamb, and the martyrs in a sacrificial vocabulary chain.

    Semantic categories: christological slaughter, martyrdom, literal violence, beast parody

    Key verses: Rev 5:6, Rev 6:9, Rev 6:4, Rev 13:3, Rev 18:24, 1 John 3:12

    Relevance: The sphazo chain (Abel → Lamb → martyrs → Babylon) is the primary vocabulary thread linking the fifth seal to both the throne room (5:6) and the vindication at Babylon's fall (18:24).

    G1203 — despotes¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1203-despotēs.md

    Occurrences: 10

    Sovereign master — appears only in Rev 6:10 within Revelation. The martyrs address God with this unique title emphasizing absolute sovereign authority (TM063).

    Semantic categories: divine sovereignty, master-slave relationship

    Key verses: Rev 6:10

    Relevance: The unique title despotes in the martyrs' address reinforces the theodicy dimension: they appeal to God's absolute sovereignty as the basis for expecting vindication.

    G228 — alethinos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G228-alethinos.md

    Occurrences: 27

    True/genuine — the martyrs address God as 'holy and true' (ho hagios kai alethinos, 6:10). The vindication declarations use 'true and righteous' (alethinai kai dikaiai, 16:7, 19:2).

    Semantic categories: divine attribute, vindication vocabulary

    Key verses: Rev 6:10, Rev 16:7, Rev 19:2

    Relevance: The alethinos vocabulary creates a verbal thread from the altar cry (6:10) through the vindication arc to its resolution (19:2). The martyrs appeal to God's truthfulness; the resolution confirms it.

    G1325 — didomi¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1325-didomi.md

    Occurrences: 430

    To give — the divine passive edothe ('was given') pattern appears in seals 1, 2, and 4 (6:2, 6:4 x2, 6:8). Absent from seal 3. Each horseman's authority is divinely granted.

    Semantic categories: divine passive, authority granting

    Key verses: Rev 6:2, Rev 6:4, Rev 6:8, Rev 6:11

    Relevance: The edothe pattern is a structural marker identifying divine authorization behind the horsemen's destructive activity. Its absence from seal 3 is a notable formula variation.

    G3022 — leukos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3022-leukos.md

    Occurrences: 25

    White — 15 of 25 NT uses in Revelation. Used for the first horse (6:2), martyrs' robes (6:11), great multitude's robes (7:9, 7:13), and the returning Christ's horse (19:11).

    Semantic categories: purity, victory, divine glory

    Key verses: Rev 6:2, Rev 6:11, Rev 7:9, Rev 7:13, Rev 19:11, Rev 3:4-5

    Relevance: The leukos thread connects the first horseman, the fifth seal martyrs' robes, the interlude multitude, and the returning Christ — a vocabulary chain spanning the entire seal sequence.

    G4450 — pyrrhos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4450-pyrrhós.md

    Occurrences: 2

    Fire-red — only 2 NT occurrences: Rev 6:4 (red horse) and Rev 12:3 (red dragon). From pyr (fire). The exclusive connection between the red horse and the red dragon is a TM-level observation.

    Semantic categories: apocalyptic color symbolism, bloodshed and warfare, satanic imagery

    Key verses: Rev 6:4, Rev 12:3

    Relevance: The pyrrhos connection between the red horse (6:4) and the red dragon (12:3) is significant — the only two entities described with this color in the NT. Whether this implies the second horseman is satanically driven is an IC question.

    G5515 — chloros¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5515-chlōrós.md

    Occurrences: 4

    Green/pale — 4 NT occurrences. Normal meaning: verdant/green (Mark 6:39, Rev 8:7, 9:4). In Rev 6:8: sickly pallor/death color. The semantic shift from 'living green' to 'death pallor' is unique to the fourth horseman.

    Semantic categories: living vegetation, pallor of death

    Key verses: Rev 6:8, Mark 6:39, Rev 8:7

    Relevance: The semantic shift in chloros from 'verdant green' to 'sickly death pallor' is contextually determined by the rider's name (Thanatos/Death). This is a unique apocalyptic application of an otherwise natural-world adjective.

    G3189 — melas¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3189-melas.md

    Occurrences: 3

    Black — 3 NT occurrences: Rev 6:5 (black horse), Rev 6:12 (sun black as sackcloth), Matt 5:36 (hair color). The double appearance in the sixth seal chapter connects the third horseman's color to the cosmic judgment.

    Semantic categories: natural description, apocalyptic symbolism

    Key verses: Rev 6:5, Rev 6:12, Matt 5:36

    Relevance: Melas appears twice in Rev 6: the black horse (6:5) and the blackened sun (6:12). The third horseman's color reappears in the cosmic signs, creating an internal color echo within the chapter.

    G2288 — thanatos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2288-thanatos.md

    Occurrences: 119

    Death — the only named horseman (Rev 6:8, onoma auto ho Thanatos). Paired with Hades as a companion figure. Death appears as a personified power in Revelation: 1:18 (keys of death and hades), 20:13-14 (death and hades deliver up dead, cast into lake of fire).

    Semantic categories: personified power, judgment agent, defeated enemy

    Key verses: Rev 6:8, Rev 1:18, Rev 20:13-14

    Relevance: The fourth horseman is the only named rider (TM065). Death is personified as a rider executing divine judgment, but is ultimately defeated — cast into the lake of fire at 20:14.

    G86 — hades¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G86-háidēs.md

    Occurrences: 11

    Hades — realm of the dead, the unseen world. Paired with Death as companion of the fourth horseman (Rev 6:8). Christ holds keys of hades and death (1:18). Death and hades are cast into the lake of fire (20:14).

    Semantic categories: realm of the dead, personified power, defeated enemy

    Key verses: Rev 6:8, Rev 1:18, Rev 20:13-14, Matt 16:18

    Relevance: Hades follows (ekolouthei, imperfect — continuous following) the fourth horseman Death. The Death-Hades pairing is unique to the fourth seal and creates a personified duo that reappears at Rev 20:13-14 when both are destroyed.

    G4735 — stephanos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4735-stephanos.md

    Occurrences: 18

    Crown/wreath — 8 of 18 NT uses in Revelation. Victor's wreath (not royal diadem). The first horseman receives a stephanos (6:2); the returning Christ wears diadema (19:12). This crown distinction is a key contrastive element (VP073).

    Semantic categories: athletic victor's wreath, eschatological reward, heavenly crowned beings

    Key verses: Rev 6:2, Rev 2:10, Rev 4:4, Rev 14:14

    Relevance: The stephanos/diadema distinction between Rev 6:2 and Rev 19:12 is one of four contrastive differences that complicate the identification of the first horseman with the returning Christ.

    G1238 — diadema¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1238-diadema.md

    Occurrences: 3

    Royal crown/diadem — used only 3x in NT (all Revelation): 12:3 (dragon's 7 crowns), 13:1 (beast's 10 crowns), 19:12 (Christ's many crowns). Never used for the first horseman.

    Semantic categories: royal sovereignty, contrastive with stephanos

    Key verses: Rev 19:12, Rev 12:3, Rev 13:1

    Relevance: The diadema/stephanos distinction is critical. Rev 6:2 uses stephanos (winner's wreath); Rev 19:12 uses diademata polla (many royal crowns). This argues against simple identification of the first horseman with the parousia Christ.

    G975 — biblion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G975-biblion.md

    Occurrences: 32

    Book/scroll — the sealed scroll in God's right hand (Rev 5:1). The Lamb alone is worthy to open it, and the seal openings release the horsemen and subsequent visions.

    Semantic categories: divine document, sealed revelation

    Key verses: Rev 5:1, Rev 5:5, Rev 5:9

    Relevance: The sealed scroll is the object whose opening drives the entire seal narrative. Its identity (divine plan, title deed, covenant document) informs how the horsemen are understood.

    G4972 — sphragizo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4972-sphragizo.md

    Occurrences: 28

    To seal — the verb form of sphragis (G4973). Used for sealing the 144,000 (Rev 7:3-4) and for sealing Satan in the abyss (Rev 20:3).

    Semantic categories: divine protection, authentication, confinement

    Key verses: Rev 7:3, Rev 7:4, Rev 7:5-8

    Relevance: The sealing of the 144,000 uses sphragizo — the protective/ownership sense of the verb. The sealing precedes the judgment trumpets, establishing a protection-before-judgment pattern (Ezek 9 parallel, AN020).

    G3359 — metopon¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3359-metopon.md

    Occurrences: 8

    Forehead — 8x in Revelation only (TM054). Always marks identification/allegiance: God's seal/name (7:3, 9:4, 14:1, 22:4) or beast's mark/name (13:16, 14:9, 17:5, 20:4).

    Semantic categories: allegiance marking, divine identification, antithetical marking

    Key verses: Rev 7:3, Rev 9:4, Rev 14:1, Rev 13:16

    Relevance: The forehead (metopon) is the exclusive location for both God's seal and the beast's mark in Revelation — creating an allegiance binary. The 144,000 are sealed on their foreheads (7:3), and their Father's name appears there (14:1).

    G1556 — ekdikeo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1556-ekdikeo.md

    Occurrences: 6

    To avenge/vindicate — the verb of the vindication arc. Rev 6:10 (ekdikeis, present — 'dost thou not avenge?') → Rev 19:2 (exedikesen, aorist — 'hath avenged'). Tense shift marks arc resolution.

    Semantic categories: divine vindication, justice

    Key verses: Rev 6:10, Rev 19:2

    Relevance: The ekdikeo tense shift (present → aorist) from 6:10 to 19:2 is the grammatical backbone of the vindication arc (VP076 Strong, 5 links).

    G1557 — ekdikesis¶
