    Question¶

    What is the internal pattern of the four horsemen (seals 1-4)? Document the formula each horseman follows (living creature says 'Come,' horse appears, rider described, effect stated). How does the fifth seal's altar cry ('How long, O Lord?') introduce the theodicy question that drives the rest of the book? What role does the interlude between seals 6 and 7 (the 144,000 and the great multitude, Rev 7) play — how does it answer the question of 6:17 ('who shall be able to stand?')? What is the structural relationship between the seals and the Olivet Discourse (Matt 24)?

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Eight prior studies provide extensive groundwork: revs-13 documented the four-horsemen formula (SP035 Moderate), color vocabulary, divine passive edothe pattern, Ezekiel 14:21 allusion (AN024 Strong), and Zechariah 1/6 allusion (AN025 Probable). revs-14 documented the 4+3 structural break at seal 5, the altar identification (burnt offering, AN027), the sphazo chain linking Abel/Lamb/martyrs/Babylon, the heos pote lament formula (TM064), temporal markers in Rev 6:11, and the vindication arc (VP076). revs-15 documented the hear/see pattern (VP069), meta touto/meta tauta transition formulas, tribal list anomalies (TM052), the 12x12x1000 structure (TM053), and the interlude pattern (SP031). revs2-03 traced the altar vindication arc through seals/trumpets/bowls and the Sinai-theophany escalation. revs2-05 tracked the nikao arc through the first seal and the martyria/logos pairing at 6:9. revs-45 documented the Olivet-Seals parallel (SP114 Moderate). Web research found scholarly work on the covenant-curse framework (Lev 26/Deut 28/Ezek 14:21), Allet's dissertation on the fifth seal delay, and the Hellholm Doppelurkunde structural division proposal.

    Primary Verses (32 — full analysis required)¶

    First four seals — horsemen formula: Retrieve each seal with full chapter context for Rev 6 - Rev 6:1-2 — First seal: Lamb opens, first living creature thunders 'Come,' white horse with bow and stephanos crown, conquering and to conquer - Rev 6:3-4 — Second seal: second living creature says 'Come,' red horse, rider given power to take peace, given great sword - Rev 6:5-6 — Third seal: third living creature says 'Come,' black horse, rider with balances, voice announces prices, oil and wine spared - Rev 6:7-8 — Fourth seal: fourth living creature says 'Come,' pale/chloros horse named Death, Hades follows, authority over 1/4 of earth with sword/hunger/death/beasts

    Fifth seal — altar cry and theodicy: The altar scene, martyrs' cry, and divine response - Rev 6:9-11 — Fifth seal: altar scene, souls of slain martyrs, 'How long O Lord' cry, white robes given, told to rest until fellow-martyrs complete - Rev 8:3-5 — Golden altar — incense with prayers of saints, fire cast to earth — altar vindication arc second stage - Rev 16:5-7 — Vindication arc midpoint: altar personified speaks ('Even so, Lord God Almighty, true and righteous are thy judgments') — answers the fifth seal cry - Rev 19:1-2 — Vindication arc resolution: 'true and righteous are his judgments... hath avenged (exedikesen) the blood of his servants' — echoes 6:10 ekdikeis

    Sixth seal — cosmic signs and climactic question: Cosmic disruption and wrath-of-the-Lamb climax - Rev 6:12-14 — Sixth seal: great earthquake, sun black as sackcloth, moon as blood, stars fall, heaven departs as scroll, mountains and islands moved - Rev 6:15-17 — Sixth seal response: seven social classes hide, cry to mountains to fall on them, 'who shall be able to stand?' — climactic question

    Seal interlude — 144,000 and great multitude: The sealing and celebration scenes answering 6:17 - Rev 7:1-3 — Four angels holding four winds, sealing angel from east with seal of living God, 'hurt not till we have sealed' - Rev 7:4-8 — 144,000 sealed from 12 tribes — tribal listing with anomalies (Judah first, Dan absent, Levi included) - Rev 7:9-10 — Great multitude from all nations, white robes, palms, 'Salvation to our God' — hear/see pattern counterpart - Rev 7:13-14 — Elder identifies great multitude: 'came out of great tribulation, washed robes white in blood of Lamb' — present participle erchomenoi - Rev 7:15-17 — Throne service, skenosei dwelling, no hunger/thirst/heat, Lamb leads to living fountains, God wipes tears — answers 6:17

    Lion/Lamb hear/see pattern: Structural parallel to the 144,000/great multitude pattern - Rev 5:5-6 — Lion/Lamb hear/see pattern — structural parallel to 144,000/great multitude hear/see in Rev 7 - Rev 5:9 — Lamb's worthiness song — 'redeemed us by thy blood out of every kindred, tongue, people, nation' — parallels Rev 7:9 multitude

    White horse contrastive parallel: Rev 19 Christ on white horse — compare with Rev 6:2 - Rev 19:11-16 — White horse rider comparison — Faithful and True, diadema crowns (not stephanos), sword from mouth, named, armies follow — contrastive parallel to Rev 6:2

    OT horsemen sources: Zechariah colored horse visions and Ezekiel fourfold judgment - Zech 1:8-12 — OT source for colored horse vision: red horse, patrol function, 'how long' cry at Zech 1:12 — dual allusion to horsemen AND altar cry - Zech 6:1-8 — OT source for four colored horse-chariots from between mountains of brass — red, black, white, grisled — patrol/judgment function - Ezek 14:21 — OT source for fourfold judgment taxonomy: sword, famine, noisome beast, pestilence — matches fourth horseman's killing means

    OT covenant-curse framework: The four covenant curses underlying the horsemen - Lev 26:21-26 — Covenant-curse framework: sword, pestilence, famine, wild beasts — same four judgments as fourth horseman, covenant context

    OT 'how long' lament tradition: Psalms and prophets asking God 'how long?' - Ps 13:1-2 — OT 'how long' lament: fourfold 'how long' paralleling Rev 6:10 — lament tradition - Ps 79:5-6 — OT 'how long' lament with request for divine vengeance poured on nations — structural parallel to Rev 6:10 - Ps 94:1-3 — OT vengeance psalm: 'God to whom vengeance belongeth... how long shall the wicked triumph?' — theological framework for altar cry - Hab 1:2-4 — OT 'how long' cry with theodicy: violence, injustice, why does God not act? — closest conceptual parallel to fifth seal
