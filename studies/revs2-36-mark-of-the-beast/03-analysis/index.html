    03-analysis.md — Verse-by-Verse Analysis by Focus Area¶

    Question¶

    How do image, worship, and number elements connect structurally in the mark of the beast?

    Focus Area 1: Charagma (G5480) — Mark Vocabulary and Every Occurrence¶

    Acts 17:29¶

    The only non-Revelation use of charagma. Paul tells the Athenians that the Godhead "is not like unto gold, or silver, or stone, graven by art and man's device." Here charagma carries its baseline meaning: a human engraving, a product of craftsmanship. This establishes the word's semantic floor before John repurposes it. The term itself is neutral in Acts — it describes what humans make. What John does with it in Revelation is invert the direction: the beast's charagma is not something humans make but something imposed on them.

    Rev 13:16¶

    The mark-imposition verse. The false prophet (earth beast) "causeth all" to receive a charagma on the right hand or on the forehead. The Greek parsing reveals three grammatically significant features. First, poiei (G4160, present active indicative) functions as a causative — the false prophet compels. Second, the hina clause introduces dosin (aorist active subjunctive, 3rd plural) — an impersonal subject shift. The false prophet causes, but unnamed agents administer. Third, the disjunction e (or, G2228) between cheir (hand) and metopon (forehead) is theologically loaded: the beast accepts either location, whereas God's seal appears on the forehead alone (Rev 7:3, 9:4, 14:1, 22:4). The six meristic pairs (small/great, rich/poor, free/bond) exhaust all social categories. No one is exempt from the demand.

    Rev 13:17¶

    Economic enforcement. Three components of identification are listed: the mark, the name of the beast, or the number of his name. These are connected by e (or), indicating they are not interchangeable synonyms but interconnected facets of one allegiance system. The mark is the visible identifier; the name is the authority acknowledged; the number is the calculated identity. The hina clause of v.17 is a continuation from v.16 — both purpose clauses depend on the same main verb poiei. The mark-imposition and the economic sanction are two purposes of one causative action.

    Rev 14:9¶

    The third angel's warning binds three elements into one compound condition: "If any man worship the beast and his image, and receive his mark." The Greek uses present active indicatives for both proskunei (worships) and lambanei (receives), indicating habitual, ongoing action — not a one-time event. The coordinate kai links worship-of-beast-and-image with mark-reception into a single conditional protasis. These are grammatically inseparable.

    Rev 14:11¶

    The consequence: no rest day or night for "those worshipping the beast and his image, and whosoever receiveth the mark of his name." The present participle proskunountes (worshipping) continues the habitual-action grammar. The triad appears again — beast, image, mark — bound as one in judgment.

    Rev 15:2¶

    Victory over the beast. The overcomers have victory "over the beast, and over his image, and over his mark, and over the number of his name." Four elements listed with the repetition of ek (out of / over): beast, image, mark, number. This is the only verse that lists all four components as distinct objects of victory. The text proves the mark was resistible — nikao (overcome) requires a genuine conflict that could be won.

    Rev 16:2¶

    First bowl judgment. Painful sores fall on those with the mark of the beast "and upon them which worshipped his image." Mark and worship are again coordinate in judgment — the mark-bearers and the image-worshippers are one group receiving one plague.

    Rev 19:20¶

    Final capture of beast and false prophet. Those deceived are described as having "received the mark of the beast, and them that worshipped his image." The linkage of deception, mark-reception, and worship is maintained to the end. The false prophet's signs deceived people into the mark; the mark expressed their worship.

    Rev 20:4¶

    Martyrs who refused the entire system. The triple negation (ou prosekunesan... oude ten eikona... ouk elabon to charagma) treats refusal of worship, rejection of the image, and non-reception of the mark as one comprehensive act of resistance. The ou/oude/ouk sequence is grammatically significant: oude ("and not even") coordinates the image-refusal with the worship-refusal, and ouk introduces the mark-refusal as a parallel clause. The three negations cover one decision.

    Focus Area 2: Sphragis (G4973) vs. Charagma — Seal/Mark Binary Competition¶

    Rev 7:2-3¶

    The angel ascending from the east carries "the seal of the living God" and commands: seal the servants of God "in their foreheads." The verb sphragisomen (aorist active subjunctive, 1st plural) indicates divine initiative — "let us seal." The location is specified: epi ton metopon — on the forehead. No hand option. The forehead-only pattern for God's seal begins here and never deviates.

    Rev 9:4¶

    The locust plague cannot harm those with "the seal of God in their foreheads." The seal functions as divine protection. The phrase sphragis tou theou epi ton metopon confirms the forehead-only pattern. The seal's protective function contrasts with the mark's economic function (Rev 13:17) — one protects from judgment, the other enables commercial participation.

    Rev 14:1¶

    The counter-image to the mark scene. The Lamb stands on Mount Zion with 144,000 having "his Father's name written in their foreheads." The same anatomical site (metopon), opposite allegiance. Where the beast's mark claims the forehead or hand, the Father's name claims the forehead alone. The content of the seal is identified here as the Father's name (onoma).

    Rev 22:4¶

    The eschatological terminus. In the new earth, the redeemed "shall see his face; and his name shall be in their foreheads." This is metopon's final occurrence. The seal's permanence extends into eternity. The allegiance binary resolves: those who bore God's name on their foreheads stand in his presence forever.

    Eph 1:13¶

    The Spirit as sealing agent. Believers "were sealed with that holy Spirit of promise" (esphragisthete, aorist passive indicative). The sealing is God's act through the Spirit. This establishes the pattern: the Spirit seals, the content is divine ownership, the result is eschatological security.

    Eph 4:30¶

    "Grieve not the holy Spirit of God, whereby ye are sealed unto the day of redemption." The sealing has an eschatological terminus — it holds until redemption's consummation. The Spirit is both agent and ongoing presence.

    2 Cor 1:22¶

    God "sealed us, and given the earnest of the Spirit in our hearts." Three elements: God seals, the Spirit is the earnest (guarantee), the location is "in our hearts" — internal, not merely external. This matches the Deuteronomic pattern where God's words must be in the heart first (Deut 6:6) before they become external signs.

    Rom 4:11¶

    Paul calls circumcision both semeion (sign, G4592) and sphragis (seal, G4973). This dual designation proves that a sign can simultaneously function as a seal. The precedent matters: if circumcision — a physical covenant marker — is both sign and seal, then the Sabbath — designated as a sign (owth) four times — can also function as a seal.

    Focus Area 3: Metopon (G3359) — Forehead as the Allegiance Site¶

    All 8 NT occurrences of metopon are in Revelation. Zero non-Revelation uses. The distribution:

    Verse Allegiance Location
    Rev 7:3 Seal of God Forehead
    Rev 9:4 Seal of God Forehead
    Rev 14:1 Father's name Forehead
    Rev 22:4 God's name Forehead
    Rev 13:16 Mark of beast Forehead OR hand
    Rev 14:9 Mark of beast Forehead OR hand
    Rev 20:4 Mark of beast (refused) Forehead AND hand
    Rev 17:5 Babylon's name Forehead

    God's seal/name: 4 forehead references, 0 hand references, 0 exceptions. Beast's mark: 3 references, each including "or hand" via the e disjunction. Babylon: 1 reference (forehead).

    The asymmetry is theologically significant. The Deuteronomic source texts (Deut 6:6-8, 11:18) require words in the heart first, then binding as signs on hand and forehead. The sequence — internal conviction before external sign — explains why God's seal appears only on the forehead: divine allegiance requires the engagement of mind and will. The beast accepts forehead or hand because the counterfeit system is satisfied with either genuine conviction or mere external compliance.

    Focus Area 4: Eikon (G1504) — Image of the Beast and Daniel 3 Parallel¶

    Gen 1:26-27¶

    Humanity created in God's eikon. The LXX uses eikon for the divine image in which humans are made. This is the origin point of image theology — authentic, divine, life-giving.

    Col 1:15¶

    Christ IS "the image of the invisible God." The eikon chain elevates: Christ is not made in God's image but IS the image. The authentic divine imprint in its fullest expression.

    Rev 13:14¶

    The earth beast commands humanity to "make an image to the beast" (poiesai eikona to therio). The dative to therio marks the beast as the beneficiary — the image is made FOR the beast. This is the construction phase. The eikon chain degrades: God's image in humanity (Gen 1:26) to Christ as God's image (Col 1:15) to a manufactured likeness of a counterfeit system (Rev 13:14).

    Rev 13:15¶

    The case shifts from dative to genitive: "the image OF the beast" (eikoni tou theriou). The grammatical shift marks a stage transition. The image was made FOR the beast (v.14, dative of advantage); now it belongs TO and represents the beast (v.15, genitive of possession). It has become an extension of the beast's authority, not merely an instrument dedicated to it.

    The double-didomi construction (edothe auto dounai pneuma te eikoni) is unique in Rev 13. Permission-to-give-permission — a second-order delegation that still traces back to the unnamed divine source. The pneuma given to the image is not zoe, psyche, bios, or pnoe. It is pneuma — functional animation, institutional capacity for speech and enforcement. Not genuine divine life.

    The image receives exactly two functions: laleo (speech) and the power to cause death (apoktathosin, aorist passive subjunctive). Speech and enforcement — institutional authority, not biological existence.

    Daniel 3:1-6, 14-18¶

    The structural parallel is five elements in sequence: (1) state-mandated image, (2) universal worship command, (3) death penalty for non-compliance, (4) faithful remnant refusal, (5) divine vindication. Revelation escalates beyond Daniel 3: Daniel's image was silent gold; the beast's image has pneuma, speech, and enforcement capacity. The institutional authority represented by the image has been upgraded from passive monument to active agent.

    Dan 3:1 provides a further numerical link: the image's dimensions are 60 cubits by 6 cubits. The sixfold numerical pattern connects to 666.

    Focus Area 5: Proskuneo (G4352) — Worship Vocabulary and the Allegiance Conflict¶

    Proskuneo occurs 65 times in the NT, with the highest concentration in Revelation. The distribution divides cleanly into true and false worship:

    True worship (toward God and the Lamb): - Rev 4:10 — Elders worship the one on the throne - Rev 5:14 — Elders worship "him that liveth for ever and ever" - Rev 7:11 — Angels, elders, and living creatures worship God - Rev 11:16 — Elders worship God at the seventh trumpet - Rev 15:4 — "All nations shall come and worship before thee" - Rev 19:4 — Elders and living creatures worship God - Rev 19:10 — Angel corrects John: "worship God" - Rev 22:8-9 — Angel corrects John again: "worship God"

    False worship (toward dragon and beast): - Rev 13:4 — Dragon and beast worshipped simultaneously - Rev 13:8 — All earth-dwellers worship the beast (except those in the Lamb's book) - Rev 13:12 — Earth beast causes worship of the first beast - Rev 13:15 — Image enforces worship under death penalty

    Warning/judgment on false worship: - Rev 14:9, 11 — Third angel warns against worshipping beast and image - Rev 16:2 — Plague on those worshipping the beast's image - Rev 19:20 — Those who worshipped the image are captured - Rev 20:4 — Martyrs who refused worship reign with Christ

    The worship binary is exhaustive. Rev 13:8 states that "all that dwell upon the earth shall worship him, whose names are not written in the book of life." Every person worships either the beast or the Lamb. No neutral ground exists.

    Rev 14:7¶

    The first angel's counter-command uses proskunesate (aorist imperative): "worship him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters." The creation verb is poiesanti (aorist active participle of poieo, G4160) — not ktizo (G2936). This verb switch is significant. John uses ktizo in Rev 4:11 and 10:6 for creation contexts. But here in 14:7, he switches to poieo — which matches the LXX of Exodus 20:11: "for in six days the LORD made [epoiesen, poieo] heaven and earth, the sea, and all that in them is." The first angel's worship command echoes the fourth commandment's creation language.

    Rev 13:4¶

    The homoios incomparability formula — "Who is like unto the beast?" — parodies divine incomparability. Exodus 15:11: "Who is like unto thee, O LORD?" Isaiah 40:18, 25: "To whom then will ye liken God?" The beast-worshippers use the same rhetorical form for the beast that Scripture reserves for God.

    Focus Area 6: Deut 6:6-8 / 11:18 — Hand/Forehead as Covenant Loyalty Markers¶

    Deut 6:6-8¶

    The foundational text. Verse 6: "these words... shall be in thine heart." Verse 7: teach them diligently. Verse 8: "bind them for a sign [owth, H226] upon thine hand, and they shall be as frontlets [totaphoth] between thine eyes." The sequence is critical: heart first (v.6), then teaching (v.7), then external sign (v.8). Internal conviction precedes external display.

    The Hebrew parsing confirms: owth (sign) on yad (hand); totaphoth (appendages/frontlets) between einayim (eyes). The covenant words become visible markers of allegiance — but only after they have been internalized.

    Deut 11:18¶

    Second Deuteronomic hand/forehead text: "lay up these my words in your heart and in your soul, and bind them for a sign upon your hand." Same pattern. Heart and soul first, then external binding. The parallel reinforces that the hand/forehead allegiance pattern originates in covenant internalization.

    Exod 13:9, 16¶

    The two earliest hand/forehead texts. Exod 13:9: "for a sign [owth] upon thine hand, and for a memorial [zikaron] between thine eyes." Exod 13:16: "for a token [owth] upon thine hand, and for frontlets [totaphoth] between thine eyes." These establish the original covenant-loyalty marking pattern.

    The Hebrew owth (H226) used in these passages is the same word used four times for the Sabbath sign — a vocabulary link connecting the hand/forehead loyalty system to the Sabbath.

    Focus Area 7: Exod 31:13-17 — Sabbath as Sign/Seal and the Owth Vocabulary¶

    Exod 31:13¶

    "Verily my sabbaths ye shall keep: for it is a sign [owth] between me and you throughout your generations; that ye may know that I am the LORD that doth sanctify you." The Hebrew parsing confirms: owth (sign), beni (between me), ubenekem (and between you), ledorotekem (for your generations). The Sabbath is the sign; its communicative purpose is sanctification — "that ye may know."

    Exod 31:17¶

    "It is a sign [owth] between me and the children of Israel for ever [le-olam]: for in six days the LORD made heaven and earth." The sign is permanent (owth le-olam) and grounded in creation. The creation language ("made heaven and earth") connects directly to Rev 14:7's worship command using poieo.

    Ezek 20:12¶

    Third Sabbath-sign text: "I gave them my sabbaths, to be a sign between me and them, that they might know that I am the LORD that sanctify them." Repeats the sanctification purpose from Exod 31:13.

    Ezek 20:20¶

    Fourth Sabbath-sign text: "hallow my sabbaths; and they shall be a sign between me and you, that ye may know that I am the LORD your God." Fourth owth designation. No other Decalogue commandment receives this four-fold designation.

    The Sabbath-Seal Composite Argument (8 lines of evidence)¶

    1. Owth designation: The Sabbath alone among the Ten Commandments is called owth (sign) — four times (Exod 31:13, 31:17, Ezek 20:12, 20:20). No other commandment receives this designation even once.

    2. Sign-seal precedent: Romans 4:11 calls circumcision both semeion (sign) and sphragis (seal). If a physical covenant marker can be both sign and seal, the Sabbath sign can function as a seal.

    3. Three seal elements: The fourth commandment uniquely contains the three elements of an ancient seal: the ruler's name ("the LORD thy God"), title ("who made" / Creator), and domain ("heaven, earth, sea, and all that in them is"). No other Decalogue commandment identifies the Lawgiver with all three.

    4. Poieo verb switch: Rev 14:7 uses poieo (not ktizo) to match Exod 20:11 LXX, linking the first angel's worship command directly to the fourth commandment.

    5. Commandment-keeping identity: Rev 14:12 identifies the sealed group by "the commandments of God, and the faith of Jesus." Commandments link back to the Sabbath-sign vocabulary through the first angel's creation language.

    6. Spirit as agent, Sabbath as content: The Spirit seals believers (Eph 1:13, 4:30). The Sabbath is the seal's content — what the seal identifies and authenticates. These are complementary, not competing.

    7. Ezekiel 8-9 template: The escalating abomination sequence in Ezek 8:5-16 climaxes in sun worship (8:16), immediately followed by the tav on the faithful (9:4). Sun worship is the trigger for the seal/judgment response.

    8. Perpetual covenant: The Sabbath is called berit olam (perpetual covenant, Exod 31:16) and owth le-olam (sign forever, Exod 31:17). Its permanence parallels the seal's eschatological duration (Eph 4:30, "sealed unto the day of redemption"; Rev 22:4, God's name on foreheads in eternity).

    Focus Area 8: 666 and the Parody Math¶

    Rev 13:18¶

    "Here is wisdom. Let him that hath understanding count the number of the beast: for it is the number of a man; and his number is Six hundred threescore and six."

    The parsing confirms: psephisato (G5585, aorist active imperative, 3rd person singular) — "let him calculate." This verb appears only twice in the NT: here and Luke 14:28 (counting the cost of discipleship). The rarity is itself significant — John chose a specific, uncommon word for this calculation.

    The phrase arithmos gar anthropou estin contains the anarthrous genitive anthropou — no article before anthropou. The grammar-reference entry on anthropos-individual-vs-type classifies this as qualitative: "a human number" rather than "the number of the man." The absence of the article leaves the genitive semantically open toward "characteristic of humanity" rather than pointing to a specific individual.

    Structural reading (IC184, SP225): Within Revelation's number theology, seven signifies divine completeness. The beast's number is 666 — three sixes, each falling one short of seven. The triple repetition parodies the divine triple pattern (the thrice-holy of Isa 6:3). The structural reading sees 666 as threefold falling-short of divine sevenfold completeness — perpetual incompleteness intensified.

    Gematria reading: Nero Caesar in Hebrew transliteration (nron qsr) yields 666; the Latin form (nro qsr) yields 616, matching the P115 papyrus variant. This historical identification does not compete with the structural reading but can operate alongside it.

    Rev 15:2¶

    Victory "over the beast, and over his image, and over his mark, and over the number of his name." The number is listed as a separate element alongside beast, image, and mark — confirming it as a distinct component of the allegiance system, not merely a calculation tool.

    Focus Area 9: Mark-Seal as Counterfeit-Genuine Pair (IC182)¶

    The parody operates at every level:

    Vocabulary level: The charasso root generates three terms in a spectrum: charakter (Heb 1:3, Christ as authentic divine imprint), sphragis (covenant seal applied by the Spirit), and charagma (counterfeit brand imposed by the false prophet). Authentic to protective to counterfeit.

    Agency level: The Spirit seals believers (Eph 1:13, 4:30, 2 Cor 1:22). The false prophet imposes the mark (Rev 13:16). The false prophet is the counterfeit Spirit — the third member of the unholy trinity parodies the third person of the divine Trinity.

    Location level: God's seal is forehead-only (4 references, 0 exceptions). The beast's mark is forehead-or-hand (3 references, each with the e disjunction).

    Mode level: The seal is received through conviction — internal, Spirit-mediated. The mark is imposed through economic coercion — external, institutionally enforced.

    Economic level: The mark enables buying and selling (Rev 13:17). The seal has no economic component; it operates on trust in God.

    Eschatological level: The mark promises economic survival but delivers divine wrath (Rev 16:2). The seal appears to mark for persecution but delivers eternal protection (Rev 7:3, 9:4) and resurrection reign (Rev 20:4).

    Heb 1:3¶

    Christ as charakter (G5481) — "the express image of his person." The charakter is the authentic divine imprint, sharing the charasso root with charagma. Christ IS the genuine imprint of God; the beast's charagma is the counterfeit. The spectrum: charakter (authentic) → sphragis (protective covenant) → charagma (imposed counterfeit).

    Focus Area 10: Three Angels' Warning Against the Mark (Rev 14:6-12)¶

    Rev 14:6-7 — First Angel¶

    The first angel carries "the everlasting gospel" to every nation, kindred, tongue, and people. The message: fear God, give him glory, worship the Creator. The three aorist imperatives (phobethete, dote, proskunesate) are decisive summons — respond as a whole. The creation catalogue (heaven, earth, sea, fountains of waters) matches and extends Exod 20:11.

    Rev 14:8 — Second Angel¶

    Babylon is fallen. The double aorist epesen epesen announces completed judgment. The perfect pepotiken (has made to drink) presents Babylon's intoxicating influence as a completed action with an ongoing result — the nations stand as those whom Babylon has made to drink.

    Rev 14:9-11 — Third Angel¶

    The compound condition: ei tis proskunei to therion kai ten eikona autou, kai lambanei charagma. Present tense proskunei and lambanei treat these as habitual, ongoing activities. The marked person is one who continually worships the beast and continually maintains the mark. This is a settled allegiance pattern, not a one-time event.

    The mirrored genitive chains between v.8 (wine of the wrath of her fornication) and v.10 (wine of the wrath of God) create a reciprocal judgment pattern. Those who drank Babylon's cup drink God's cup. The grammar carries the reversal.

    Rev 14:12¶

    "Here is the patience of the saints: here are they that keep the commandments of God, and the faith of Jesus." The articular present participle hoi terountes (the ones keeping) defines the sealed community by ongoing identity and practice. Two markers: commandments of God and faith of Jesus. The commandments link back to the Sabbath-sign vocabulary through the first angel's creation language.

    Focus Area 11: Economic Exclusion (Rev 13:17)¶

    Economic restriction is structurally subordinate to worship. The hina clause of v.17 is the second purpose clause under the same main verb poiei — the first hina (v.12) establishes worship as the ultimate purpose. Economic control is an enforcement mechanism deployed to achieve worship compliance.

    The three-part identification system (mark, name, number) presents three interconnected components: the charagma is the visible allegiance marker, the onoma is the authority under which one operates, and the arithmos is the calculated identity. The three-deep genitive chain (arithmon → onomatos → autou) binds these into one system.

    The six meristic pairs of v.16 cover all social strata. No one is exempt. The economic exclusion applies universally — the beast's system allows no neutral participants.

    Focus Area 12: Edothe Divine Passive in Rev 13¶

    The divine passive (edothe, aorist passive indicative of didomi) governs every beast mandate in Rev 13:

    Verse Construction Content
    13:5a edothe auto stoma Mouth speaking great things
    13:5b edothe auto exousia poiesai Authority to act 42 months
    13:7a edothe auto poiesai polemon To make war with saints
    13:7b edothe auto exousia Authority over all nations
    13:14 edothe auto poiesai To perform signs
    13:15 edothe auto dounai pneuma To give breath to the image

    The v.15 instance is unique: the double-didomi (edothe auto dounai) — "it was given to him to give." Permission-to-give-permission. The same root appears in passive finite form and active infinitive form in the same clause. This meta-giving construction underscores that the earth beast cannot autonomously animate; it is given to give.

    The voice pivot from active edoken (dragon as agent, v.2) to passive edothe (agent unnamed, vv.5-15) is structurally significant. The dragon openly gives; God silently permits. Every beast mandate — including the mark — exists under bounded divine permission. The 42-month temporal limit (v.5) adds a chronological boundary to the grammatical one. The beast's entire operational portfolio is bounded in time and authorized by an unnamed greater power.

    Supporting Verses (Context Notes)¶

    Ezek 9:4-6¶

    The tav (H8420, only 3 OT occurrences) placed on the foreheads of the faithful who "sigh and cry for all the abominations." Five structural elements shared with Rev 7: divine command, marking on forehead, protective function, judgment on unmarked, faithful remnant preserved. This is the OT template for Revelation's sealing.

    Ezek 8:16¶

    The climactic abomination: sun worship at the temple entrance. The escalating sequence (Ezek 8:5-16, four stages) climaxes in sun worship, immediately followed by the tav marking in 9:4. The trigger for the seal/judgment binary is a worship violation.

    Dan 7:8, 25¶

    The little horn speaking great things — stoma laloun megala in the LXX of 7:8 is verbatim quoted at Rev 13:5 (TM205). Dan 7:25 specifies: "think to change times and laws." The time-and-law-changing mandate establishes that the beast's mark involves alteration of divine ordinances.

    2 Thess 2:3-4, 9-12¶

    The man of sin / judicial delusion pattern. Truth offered, truth rejected, God sends strong delusion, they believe the lie. Mark reception follows truth rejection, not ignorance. The deception is judicial — it is the consequence of rejecting known truth.

    Gal 6:17¶

    Paul bears stigmata (G4742) — a different mark-word from charagma. Stigma = wounds borne voluntarily for Christ. Charagma = brand imposed by coercion for the beast. The vocabulary distinction reinforces the voluntary/coerced contrast between genuine and counterfeit marking.

    Lev 19:28¶

    The only OT prohibition directly addressing body-marking: "nor print any marks upon you." The Hebrew kethobeth (H3793, "letter or mark branded on skin") appears only here. Imposed marks violate covenant boundaries.

    Isa 8:16¶

    "Bind up the testimony, seal the law among my disciples." Testimony and law sealed together — connecting sealing to Torah preservation. Anticipates Rev 14:12 where the sealed group is identified by commandments plus faith of Jesus.

    Gen 4:15¶

    The LORD set an owth (sign, H226) upon Cain. The same word used for the Sabbath sign. The earliest use of owth as a protective mark — though on an offender rather than the faithful. The marking vocabulary runs from Genesis to Revelation.
