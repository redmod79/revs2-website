    Question¶

    What is the structural significance of Revelation's time periods? Why does the 3.5-year period appear in three different expressions — 42 months (Rev 11:2, 13:5), 1,260 days (Rev 11:3, 12:6), and 'a time, and times, and half a time' (Rev 12:14)? Use Daniel 7:25 and 12:7 as the source texts. Do the three expressions map to different subjects or perspectives? What does the text of Revelation 20 explicitly state about the millennium — what happens before, during, and after the thousand years? Document the sequence of events in Rev 20:1-15 without importing external frameworks.

    Prior Research Summary¶

Additional studies on time-times-half-time, prophetic-time-units, prophetic-month-year-calculation, and day-for-year-principle-grounding. Absorbed studies revs-33 and revs-34 registered 40+ structural DB items; this study integrates them into Module 5 symbolic numbers framework.

    Primary Verses (31 — full analysis required)¶

    3.5-year expression passages: The five Revelation occurrences of the 3.5-year period in three different expressions - Rev 11:2 — 42 months — Gentiles trample holy city (hostile subject) - Rev 11:3 — 1260 days — two witnesses prophesy (preservation subject) - Rev 12:6 — 1260 days — woman nourished in wilderness (preservation subject) - Rev 12:14 — time-times-half — woman's wilderness period; LXX quotation of Dan 7:25 - Rev 13:5 — 42 months — beast given authority to act (hostile subject); poiesai accusative duration

    Daniel source texts: The Aramaic and Hebrew source texts for the time-times-half formula - Dan 7:25 — Aramaic source text — iddan (H5732) + zimnin (H2166); horn speaks, wears out saints, changes times - Dan 12:7 — Hebrew source text — moed (H4150); oath formula for time-times-half - Dan 7:21-22 — Horn makes war with saints; judgment given TO saints — parallel to Rev 20:4 krima edothe - Dan 7:9-10 — OT judgment scene — thrones, Ancient of Days, books opened; parallel to Rev 20:11-12 - Dan 4:16 — Seven times (iddan) = seven years — establishes iddan = year - Dan 4:25 — Seven times pass over Nebuchadnezzar — confirmed year-meaning of iddan

    Pre-millennium (Rev 20:1-3): The binding of Satan — confinement sequence and purpose clause - Rev 20:1-3 — Pre-millennium sequence: angel descends, binds Satan, casts into abyss, seals; triple aorist confinement

    During millennium (Rev 20:4-6): Thrones, judgment, first resurrection, reign with Christ - Rev 20:4-6 — During millennium: thrones, judgment, souls lived (ezesan), first resurrection, priests-and-kings

    Ezesan consistency check: All four occurrences of the identical aorist form ezesan (V-AAI-3P) in Revelation - Rev 2:8 — Ezesan of Christ — same aorist form as Rev 20:4; bodily resurrection consistency check - Rev 13:14 — Ezesan of beast — same form; wound/revival consistency check

    Second death occurrences: All four NT occurrences of deuteros thanatos - Rev 2:11 — Second death — promise to overcomer; first of four occurrences - Rev 20:14 — Second death defined — lake of fire is the second death - Rev 21:8 — Second death — final occurrence; lake of fire for the faithless

    Day-year foundation texts: OT passages establishing the day-for-year ratio - Num 14:34 — Day-year formula: yom lashshanah — foundational for symbolic time interpretation - Ezek 4:6 — Day-year formula: yom lashshanah — second occurrence of ratio formula

    OT millennium parallels: OT passages paralleling the Rev 20 millennium sequence - Isa 24:21-22 — OT parallel to Rev 20:1-3,7 — supernatural beings imprisoned, released after many days - Ezek 38:2 — OT Gog/Magog prophecy — source text for Rev 20:8 allusion - Ezek 39:6 — Fire from heaven on Magog — parallel to Rev 20:9 fire from heaven - Dan 7:9-10 — OT judgment scene — thrones, Ancient of Days, books opened; parallel to Rev 20:11-12

    Additional Primary Verses: - Rev 20:7-10 — Post-millennium: Satan released, deceives nations (Gog/Magog), fire from heaven, lake of fire - Rev 20:11-15 — Great white throne judgment: books opened, book of life, death/Hades cast into lake of fire (second death) - Rev 13:7 — Beast given authority over saints — verbal parallel to Dan 7:25 (edothe + war with saints) - Rev 11:9 — 3.5 days — compressed echo of 3.5-year period (hemera + hemisu) - Rev 11:11 — 3.5 days — witnesses raised; resurrection/revival vocabulary - Dan 12:11-12 — Extended periods: 1290 days, 1335 days — same unit system, extended beyond 1260 - Rev 19:20 — Beast and false prophet cast into lake of fire — presupposed by Rev 20:10 (hopou) - Rev 10:6 — Chronos (G5550) — 'delay no longer'; chronos vs kairos distinction in Revelation
