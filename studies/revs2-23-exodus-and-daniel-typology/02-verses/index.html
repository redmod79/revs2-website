    Verses¶

    Question¶

    How does Revelation draw on Exodus plague typology in the trumpet and bowl sequences, how does Rev 13:2 composite the four Danielic beasts in reverse order with Dan 7 as the explicit decoder, and how does Dan 7:9-10 serve as the structural template for the great white throne judgment in Rev 20:11-13?

    Plague 1 — Blood: The Nile turned to blood¶

    Exo 7:14 — And the LORD said unto Moses, Pharaoh's heart [is] hardened, he refuseth to let the people go. | Exo 7:17 — Thus saith the LORD, In this thou shalt know that I [am] the LORD: behold, I will smite with the rod that [is] in mine hand upon the waters which [are] in the river, and they shall be turned to blood. | Exo 7:20 — And Moses and Aaron did so, as the LORD commanded; and he lifted up the rod, and smote the waters that [were] in the river, in the sight of Pharaoh, and in the sight of his servants; and all the waters that [were] in the river were turned to blood. | Exo 7:21 — And the fish that [was] in the river died; and the river stank, and the Egyptians could not drink of the water of the river; and there was blood throughout all the land of Egypt.

    Context: First plague. Moses strikes the Nile with his rod; all waters of Egypt become blood. Fish die, river stinks, Egyptians cannot drink. LXX vocabulary: haima (G129) for dam (H1818). Revelatory echo: Rev 8:8-9 (second trumpet — sea becomes blood), Rev 16:3-4 (bowls 2-3 — sea and rivers become blood).

    Notes: LXX bridge H1818 dam → G129 haima (PMI 34.72). The blood-water transformation is the most frequently echoed plague in Revelation. Rev 16:3-4 expands scope: sea + rivers, not just the Nile.

    Plague 2 — Frogs: Covering the land¶

    Exo 8:2 — And if thou refuse to let [them] go, behold, I will smite all thy borders with frogs. | Exo 8:6 — And Aaron stretched out his hand over the waters of Egypt; and the frogs came up, and covered the land of Egypt. | Exo 8:13 — And the LORD did according to the word of Moses; and the frogs died out of the houses, out of the villages, and out of the fields.

    Context: Second plague. Frogs rise from the Nile and cover Egypt. LXX: tsephardea (H6854) → batrachos (G944), perfect one-to-one correspondence (13 co-occ, PMI 10.77). NT hapax at Rev 16:13 — three unclean spirits like frogs emerge from the dragon, beast, and false prophet (Bowl 6). This is the only NT use of batrachos; its exclusivity marks it as a deliberate Exodus allusion.

    Notes: H6854 tsephardea is the sole OT word for frog; G944 batrachos is its only NT occurrence (Rev 16:13). Bowl 6 frog-spirits = direct LXX allusion to Plague 2.

    Plague 3 — Lice/Gnats: No echo in Revelation¶

    Exo 8:16 — And the LORD said unto Moses, Say unto Aaron, Stretch out thy rod, and smite the dust of the land, that it may become lice throughout all the land of Egypt. | Exo 8:19 — Then the magicians said unto Pharaoh, This [is] the finger of God: and Pharaoh's heart was hardened, and he hearkened not unto them; as the LORD had said.

    Context: Third plague: lice/gnats from dust. Magicians acknowledge 'the finger of God' — their power is broken. No direct vocabulary echo in Revelation trumpet or bowl sequences. Plague 3 is among the two plagues (with Plague 5, cattle murrain) that have no clear Revelation counterpart.

    Notes: Plague 3 (lice) and Plague 5 (murrain) are the two plagues with no clear Revelation echo. This is part of the evidence that bowl coverage is 6/7 not 10/10.

    Plague 4 — Flies: Goshen separation begins¶

    Exo 8:21 — Else, if thou wilt not let my people go, behold, I will send swarms [of flies] upon thee, and upon thy servants, and upon thy people, and into thy houses. | Exo 8:22 — And I will sever in that day the land of Goshen, in which my people dwell, that no swarms [of flies] shall be there; to the end thou mayest know that I [am] the LORD in the midst of the earth. | Exo 8:23 — And I will put a division between my people and thy people: to morrow shall this sign be.

    Context: Fourth plague: flies. First plague with explicit Goshen distinction — YHWH separates his people from Egyptians. This Goshen principle (divine protection/exemption during judgment) is the theological background for Rev 7:2-3 (sealing before trumpet judgments) and Rev 9:4 (locusts cannot harm those with the seal of God).

    Notes: Exo 8:22-23 establishes the Goshen principle: divine discrimination between peoples during judgment. Rev 9:4 and Rev 7:3 echo this structure. Also: Rev 16:2 inverts it — the bowl falls only on those with the mark of the beast, not on God's people.

    Plague 5 — Murrain on cattle: No echo in Revelation¶

    Exo 9:4 — And the LORD shall sever between the cattle of Israel and the cattle of Egypt: and there shall nothing die of all [that is] the children's of Israel. | Exo 9:6 — And the LORD did that thing on the morrow, and all the cattle of Egypt died: but of the cattle of the children of Israel died not one.

    Context: Fifth plague: disease on Egyptian livestock; Israel's livestock untouched. Goshen principle applied to cattle. No direct Revelation echo. The explicit separation (v.4) mirrors Exo 8:22-23.

    Notes: Plague 5 with Plague 3 are the two without clear Revelation counterparts. The cattle/Goshen distinction here (v.4) repeats the Plague 4 pattern.

    Plague 6 — Boils: LXX shechin → helkos links to Bowl 1¶

    Exo 9:9 — And it shall become small dust in all the land of Egypt, and shall be a boil breaking forth [with] blains upon man, and upon beast, throughout all the land of Egypt. | Exo 9:10 — And they took ashes of the furnace, and stood before Pharaoh; and Moses sprinkled it up toward heaven; and it became a boil breaking forth [with] blains upon man, and upon beast. | Exo 9:11 — And the magicians could not stand before Moses because of the boils; for the boil was upon the magicians, and upon all the Egyptians.

    Context: Sixth plague: boils. Hebrew shechin (H7822) → LXX helkos (G1668, PMI 10.64). Rev 16:2 Bowl 1: 'there fell a noisome and grievous sore (helkos kakon kai poneron) upon the men which had the mark of the beast.' This is the strongest single LXX vocabulary chain between the plagues and the bowls — exact same Greek word.

    Notes: H7822 shechin → G1668 helkos: 11 co-occ, PMI 10.64. Rev 16:2 uses helkos kakon kai poneron — the adjectives 'evil and grievous' intensify beyond the Exodus original. The magicians' incapacity (v.11) echoes at Rev 13:15 — no one can resist the beast's image.

    Plague 7 — Hail full passage: fire mingled with hail¶

    Exo 9:18 — Behold, to morrow about this time I will cause it to rain a very grievous hail, such as hath not been in Egypt since the foundation thereof even until now. | Exo 9:23 — And Moses stretched forth his rod toward heaven: and the LORD sent thunder and hail, and the fire ran along upon the ground; and the LORD rained hail upon the land of Egypt. | Exo 9:24 — So there was hail, and fire mingled with the hail, very grievous, such as there was none like it in all the land of Egypt since it became a nation. | Exo 9:25 — And the hail smote throughout all the land of Egypt all that [was] in the field, both man and beast; and the hail smote every herb of the field, and brake every tree of the field. | Exo 9:26 — Only in the land of Goshen, where the children of Israel [were], was there no hail. | Exo 9:33 — And Moses went out of the city from Pharaoh, and spread abroad his hands unto the LORD: and the thunders and hail ceased, and the rain was not poured upon the earth. | Exo 9:34 — And when Pharaoh saw that the rain and the hail and the thunders were ceased, he sinned yet more, and hardened his heart, he and his servants.

    Context: Seventh plague full narrative. Hail mixed with fire. Hebrew barad (H1259) → LXX chalaza (G5464). Rev 8:7 Trumpet 1 and Rev 16:21 Bowl 7 both echo this vocabulary. Goshen exemption at v.26. Pharaoh hardening at v.34 parallels impenitence refrain in Rev 16:9,11,21.

    Notes: H1259 barad → G5464 chalaza. Goshen exemption explicitly stated at v.26. Pharaoh hardening = bowl impenitence pattern.

    Plague 8 — Locusts: LXX arbeh → akris links to Trumpet 5¶

    Exo 10:4 — Else, if thou refuse to let my people go, behold, to morrow will I bring the locusts into thy coast. | Exo 10:14 — And the locusts went up over all the land of Egypt, and rested in all the coasts of Egypt: very grievous [were they]; before them there were no such locusts as they, neither after them shall be such. | Exo 10:15 — For they covered the face of the whole earth, so that the land was darkened; and they did eat every herb of the land.

    Context: Eighth plague: locusts that darken the land. Hebrew arbeh (H697) → LXX akris (G200, PMI 9.52). Rev 9:3 Trumpet 5: 'there came out of the smoke locusts upon the earth' — same akris. Critical distinction: Exodus locusts eat vegetation (v.15); Rev 9:4 locusts are commanded NOT to hurt grass/trees but only men without the seal of God. This reversal marks the Goshen principle operating in the trumpet sequence.

    Notes: H697 arbeh → G200 akris (19 co-occ, PMI 9.52). The Revelation locust trumpet inverts the Exodus mandate: Exo locusts destroy vegetation; Rev 9:4 locusts are forbidden from harming vegetation. The inversion is deliberate — emphasizing the seal/Goshen distinction.

    Song of Moses: Exodus anchor for Rev 15:3¶

    Exo 15:1 — Then sang Moses and the children of Israel this song unto the LORD, and spake, saying, I will sing unto the LORD, for he hath triumphed gloriously: the horse and his rider hath he thrown into the sea. | Exo 15:3 — The LORD [is] a man of war: the LORD [is] his name. | Exo 15:11 — Who [is] like unto thee, O LORD, among the gods? who [is] like thee, glorious in holiness, fearful [in] praises, doing wonders?

    Context: The Song of Moses after the Red Sea crossing — the victory song celebrating YHWH's defeat of Pharaoh's army. Rev 15:3 explicitly calls the bowls prelude song 'the song of Moses the servant of God, and the song of the Lamb.' The question 'Who is like unto thee?' (v.11) echoes in Rev 13:4 'Who is like unto the beast?' — a deliberate anti-doxology in the mouth of the beast's worshippers.

    Notes: Rev 15:3 is the explicit Exodus anchor for the entire bowl sequence. The Song of Moses frames the bowls as the antitypical Exodus judgment. Rev 13:4 'who is like the beast?' directly parodies Exo 15:11 'who is like thee, O LORD?'

    The four beasts from the sea: lion, bear, leopard, terrible beast¶

    Context: Tenth plague announced: death of all firstborn; Israel explicitly protected. V.7 is the most comprehensive theological statement of the Goshen distinction principle.

    Notes: V.7 ('the LORD doth put a difference between the Egyptians and Israel') is the clearest Exodus statement of divine discrimination during judgment. No trumpet or bowl maps directly to firstborn death via LXX vocabulary. Rev 18:8 ('death in one day') is the closest conceptual echo.

    Plague 10 — Firstborn: executed at midnight¶

    And it came to pass, that at midnight the LORD smote all the firstborn in the land of Egypt, from the firstborn of Pharaoh that sat on his throne unto the firstborn of the captive that [was] in the dungeon; and all the firstborn of cattle. [12:30] And Pharaoh rose up in the night, he, and all his servants, and all the Egyptians; and there was a great cry in Egypt; for [there was] not a house where [there was] not one dead.

    Context: Tenth plague executed at midnight — total scope (not a house without one dead). No LXX vocabulary chain to any trumpet or bowl, confirming P10 is absent from Revelation's plague typology sequence.

    Notes: 'Not a house where there was not one dead' — totality language. Rev 18:8 ('death and mourning and famine in one day') echoes the sudden-death-stroke concept but without shared LXX vocabulary.

    Goshen exemption — first explicit divine severance¶

    And I will sever in that day the land of Goshen, in which my people dwell, that no swarms [of flies] shall be there; to the end thou mayest know that I [am] the LORD in the midst of the earth. [8:23] And I will put a division between my people and thy people: to morrow shall this sign be.

    Context: First explicit Goshen exemption (Plague 4, flies). YHWH uses H6395 palah (to distinguish/separate) — the same root recurs at Exod 9:4 (cattle), 11:7 (firstborn). This Goshen severance is the prototype for Rev 9:4 (seal-exemption from locusts) and Rev 16:2 (mark-recipients receive sores).

    Notes: Five Exodus Goshen-protection texts (8:22-23; 9:4; 9:26; 10:23; 11:7) all use language of divine severance. Revelation transforms geographic protection into personal seal protection.

    Goshen protection from hail — no hail in Israel's land¶

    Only in the land of Goshen, where the children of Israel [were], was there no hail.

    Context: Plague 7 (hail): Goshen exemption explicitly stated. The hail (H1259 barad → G5464 chalaza) falls throughout all Egypt but not in Goshen.

    Notes: Goshen principle applied to Plague 7 — the same plague whose vocabulary (chalaza) echoes in Rev 8:7 (T1) and Rev 16:21 (B7). The exemption is geographic in Exodus; Revelation transforms it to seal-based (Rev 9:4) or mark-based (Rev 16:2).

    Goshen light during Plague 9 darkness¶

    They saw not one another, neither rose any from his place for three days: but all the children of Israel had light in their dwellings.

    Context: Plague 9 (darkness): Israel explicitly had light while Egypt was in three days of palpable darkness. This is the explicit Goshen-light verse.

    Notes: Rev 16:10 (B5) targets the beast's kingdom with darkness but does not state God's people have light. The Exodus Goshen-light principle is present in the source but is not explicitly echoed in Revelation's bowl sequence.

    Passover blood on doorposts — personal protection typology¶

    And they shall take of the blood, and strike [it] on the two side posts and on the upper door post of the houses, wherein they shall eat it. [12:12] For I will pass through the land of Egypt this night, and will smite all the firstborn in the land of Egypt, both man and beast; and against all the gods of Egypt I will execute judgment: I [am] the LORD. [12:13] And the blood shall be to you for a token upon the houses where ye [are]: and when I see the blood, I will pass over you, and the plague shall not be upon you to destroy [you], when I smite the land of Egypt.

    Context: Passover institution: blood on doorposts = personal/household protection during P10. This transforms geographic Goshen protection (land-level) into household-level blood-mark protection.

    Notes: Passover blood on doorpost prefigures Rev 7:3 forehead seal and Rev 13:16-17 mark-of-beast on forehead/hand. The blood-mark as protection/identification token is the Exodus source for both the seal of God and the mark of the beast in Revelation.

    Psalm 78 plague recitation — non-sequential OT precedent¶

    How he had wrought his signs in Egypt, and his wonders in the field of Zoan: [78:44] And had turned their rivers into blood; and their floods, that they could not drink. [78:45] He sent divers sorts of flies among them, which devoured them; and frogs, which destroyed them. [78:46] He gave also their increase unto the caterpiller, and their labour unto the locust. [78:47] He destroyed their vines with hail, and their sycomore trees with frost. [78:48] He gave up their cattle also to the hail, and their flocks to hot thunderbolts. [78:51] And smote all the firstborn in Egypt; the chief of [their] strength in the tabernacles of Ham:

    Context: Psalm 78's plague recitation: blood, flies, frogs, locust, hail, firstborn — in non-Exodus order, omitting lice/gnats (P3), livestock (P5), boils (P6), darkness (P9). This OT liturgical reordering is the precedent for Revelation's selective non-sequential use.

    Notes: Psalm 78 omits exactly the three plagues Revelation also omits (P3 gnats, P5 livestock) plus P6 (boils) and P9 (darkness), while Revelation does echo P6 and P9. The non-sequential liturgical tradition establishes that selective and reordered plague use is standard OT practice.

    Psalm 105 plague recitation — different non-sequential order¶

    He sent darkness, and made it dark; and they rebelled not against his word. [105:29] He turned their waters into blood, and slew their fish. [105:30] Their land brought forth frogs in abundance, in the chambers of their kings. [105:32] He gave them hail for rain, [and] flaming fire in their land. [105:33] He smote their vines also and their fig trees; and brake the trees of their coasts. [105:34] He spake, and the locusts came, and caterpillers, and that without number, [105:35] And did eat up all the herbs in their land, and devoured the fruit of their ground. [105:36] He smote also all the firstborn in their land, the chief of all their strength.

    Context: Psalm 105 begins with darkness (P9), then blood (P1), frogs (P2), hail (P7), locusts (P8), firstborn (P10) — completely reordered from Exodus. This is the second OT precedent for plague reordering.

    Notes: Psalm 105 starts with P9 (darkness), not P1 (blood). Both Psalms 78 and 105 omit P3 (gnats/lice) and P5 (livestock) — the same two plagues Revelation consistently omits. This correlation is structural: these three plagues operate at a domestic/agricultural scale that does not fit the cosmic register of either the Psalms' praise or Revelation's judgments.

    Hail and fire in Psalm 105 plague recitation¶

    He gave them hail for rain, [and] flaming fire in their land.

    Context: Psalm 105's brief mention of Plague 7 hail: barad + esh (fire) paired — same combination as Rev 8:7 (chalaza + pyr). This is the Psalmic precedent for the hail-fire combination.

    Notes: H1259 barad + H784 esh (fire) in Psalm 105:32 = LXX chalaza + pyr = Rev 8:7 chalaza + pyr. The hail-fire pairing appears in Exod 9:23-24, Psalm 105:32, and Rev 8:7 — confirming the LXX vocabulary chain across all three.

    Psalm 135 plague recitation — liturgical use¶

    Who smote the firstborn of Egypt, both of man and beast. [135:9] [Who] sent tokens and wonders into the midst of thee, O Egypt, upon Pharaoh, and upon all his servants.

    Context: Brief praise reference to Egypt plagues: firstborn death (P10) and general 'signs and wonders.' Part of the liturgical tradition using the plagues as evidence of divine sovereignty.

    Notes: semeia kai terata (signs and wonders) — the vocabulary Stephen uses in Acts 7:36. Revelation inverts this: the false prophet performs terata (wonders) to deceive (Rev 13:13-14). The true signs validate YHWH; the counterfeit signs validate the beast.

    Beast slain; Son of Man receives everlasting kingdom¶

    I beheld then because of the voice of the great words which the horn spake: I beheld [even] till the beast was slain, and his body destroyed, and given to the burning flame. [7:13] I saw in the night visions, and, behold, [one] like the Son of man came with the clouds of heaven, and came to the Ancient of days, and they brought him near before him. [7:14] And there was given him dominion, and glory, and a kingdom, that all people, nations, and languages, should serve him: his dominion [is] an everlasting dominion, which shall not pass away, and his kingdom [that] which shall not be destroyed.

    Context: Consequence of the little horn's great words: beast slain and burned. Then Son of Man comes to Ancient of Days and receives an everlasting kingdom (yehibh — Peil passive, divinely bestowed).

    Notes: Dan 7:11 'beast was slain' = Rev 19:20 (beast cast into the lake of fire). Dan 7:13-14 Son of Man kingdom reception = Rev 5:5-7 (Lamb receives scroll from throne). Dan 7:14 'all people, nations, and languages shall serve him' = Rev 5:9; 7:9; 14:6 (every tribe, tongue, people, nation). The Christological merger in Rev 1:14 applies the Ancient of Days' description to the Son of Man figure.

    Judgment sits; beast's dominion removed; kingdom given to saints¶

    But the judgment shall sit, and they shall take away his dominion, to consume and to destroy [it] unto the end. [7:27] And the kingdom and dominion, and the greatness of the kingdom under the whole heaven, shall be given to the people of the saints of the most High, whose kingdom [is] an everlasting kingdom, and all dominions shall serve and obey him. [7:28] Hitherto [is] the end of the matter. As for me Daniel, my cogitations much troubled me, and my countenance changed in me: but I kept the matter in my heart.

    Context: The judgment formally seated: beast's dominion consumed, kingdom transferred to saints of Most High. V.27 everlasting kingdom = Dan 7:14 kingdom of Son of Man. Direct parallel to Rev 20:4 (thrones, judgment given to saints).

    Notes: Dan 7:26 'judgment shall sit' (dina yetib) → Rev 20:4 'judgment was given unto them' (krima edothē autois). Dan 7:27 kingdom given to saints → Rev 20:6; 22:5 saints reign. Rev 11:15 'kingdoms of this world are become the kingdoms of our Lord' = Dan 7:27 kingdom transfer formula.

    T3 — Wormwood star: Jeremiah tradition (not Exodus)¶

    And the third angel sounded, and there fell a great star from heaven, burning as it were a lamp, and it fell upon the third part of the rivers, and upon the fountains of waters; [8:11] And the name of the star is called Wormwood: and the third part of the waters became wormwood; and many men died of the waters, because they were made bitter.

    Context: Third trumpet: star named Wormwood poisons rivers and springs. No Exodus plague equivalent — source tradition is Jer 9:13-15 (wormwood as covenant-curse judgment). The to triton (one-third) fraction continues.

    Notes: Wormwood (G894 apsinthos) = H3939 la'anah in LXX. Source: Jer 9:13-15 (God feeds Israel wormwood for unfaithfulness), Lam 3:15,19, Amos 5:7. T3 demonstrates Revelation draws on multiple OT judgment traditions, not only Exodus. No chalaza, haima, or akris vocabulary — confirms T3 is non-Exodus.

    T4 — One-third darkened: Plague 9 echo via Joel 2:10¶

    And the fourth angel sounded, and the third part of the sun was smitten, and the third part of the moon, and the third part of the stars; so as the third part of them was darkened, and the day shone not for a third part of it, and the night likewise.

    Context: Fourth trumpet: one-third of sun, moon, and stars smitten and darkened. to triton appears 5x in this single verse — highest concentration. Echo of Plague 9 (darkness) mediated through Joel 2:10 (sun and moon dark, stars withdraw).

    Notes: T4 vs B5 contrast: T4 = partial (to triton, one-third); B5 (Rev 16:10) = total (beast's kingdom full of darkness, no fraction). This fraction escalation (1/3 trumpets → total bowls) mirrors Revelation's overall intensification sequence. Joel 2:10 mediates between Exod 10:21-23 and Rev 8:12.

    T5 seal exemption — Goshen principle in trumpet sequence¶

    And it was commanded them that they should not hurt the grass of the earth, neither any green thing, neither any tree; but only those men which have not the seal of God in their foreheads.

    Context: Fifth trumpet locust command: do not harm vegetation; harm only those without the seal. This is the Goshen principle applied to the trumpet sequence — the seal of God functions as the Goshen severance marker.

    Notes: Inversion of Plague 8 mandate: Exod 10:15 locusts ate every herb; Rev 9:4 locusts explicitly commanded NOT to hurt grass/trees. The Goshen geographic exemption (Exod 8:22-23) is replaced by individual seal protection. Rev 9:4 sphragi-da (G4973) = the same seal as Rev 7:3.

    Retroactive plague label — plagues applied to all trumpets¶

    And the rest of the men which were not killed by these plagues yet repented not of the works of their hands, that they should not worship devils, and idols of gold, and silver, and brass, and stone, and of wood: which neither can see, nor hear, nor walk: [9:21] Neither repented they of their murders, nor of their sorceries, nor of their fornication, nor of their thefts.

    Context: After the sixth trumpet: survivors of the plagues (plegais, G4127 dative plural) did not repent. The text itself retroactively labels the entire trumpet sequence as 'these plagues.' The refusal-to-repent formula echoes Pharaoh's heart-hardening.

    Notes: plegais (G4127, dative plural) applied retroactively to all trumpet judgments — the author's own label for the sequence. Pharaoh pattern: Exod 7:22, 8:19, 9:12, 9:34, 10:20 — each refusal is followed by the next escalated plague. Rev 9:20-21 mirrors this pattern; no repentance = bowls follow.

    Two witnesses — power over water-to-blood and all plagues¶

    These have power to shut heaven, that it rain not in the days of their prophecy: and have power over waters to turn them to blood, and to smite the earth with all plagues, as often as they will.

    Context: Two witnesses: power to turn waters to blood (Moses P1 typology) and to smite with all plagues (pasais tais pleges). Elijah typology (no rain) + Moses typology (blood + plagues) = the Exodus plague tradition as actively operative in the prophetic witness.

    Notes: pasais tais pleges (all the plagues) — strongest explicit Exodus-plague connection outside the bowl sequence. The two witnesses are themselves a Moses/Elijah composite, confirming that the plague tradition is not merely typological background but actively deployed authority in Revelation's narrative.

    Jerusalem called Egypt — typological designation confirmed¶

    And their dead bodies [shall lie] in the street of the great city, which spiritually is called Sodom and Egypt, where also our Lord was crucified.

    Context: The great city called Egypt and Sodom pneumatikos (spiritually/typologically). The author explicitly signals that Egypt is a typological designation for spiritual oppression — confirming the Exodus plague typology operates at authorial-intent level.

    Notes: pneumatikos (G4152, adverb) = the explicit authorial signal that the typological reading is intentional. Egypt as a type of spiritual oppression validates reading the trumpet/bowl sequence as antitypical Exodus plagues. The great city encompasses both Jerusalem and Egypt — typological overlap.

    Song of Moses before the bowls — explicit Exodus anchor¶

    And I saw as it were a sea of glass mingled with fire: and them that had gotten the victory over the beast, and over his image, and over his mark, [and] over the number of his name, stand on the sea of glass, having the harps of God. [15:3] And they sing the song of Moses the servant of God, and the song of the Lamb, saying, Great and marvellous [are] thy works, Lord God Almighty; just and true [are] thy ways, thou King of saints. [15:4] Who shall not fear thee, O Lord, and glorify thy name? for [thou] only [art] holy: for all nations shall come and worship before thee; for thy judgments are made manifest.

    Context: Sea-glass victors sing Song of Moses + Song of the Lamb immediately before the seven bowls are poured. Sea of glass = transformed Red Sea imagery. The sequence mirrors Exod 15 (Song of Moses) → wilderness → conquest.

    Notes: Rev 15:4 'who shall not fear thee' (tis ou me phobethei) echoes Exod 15:11 'who is like thee' (mi khamocha). Rev 13:4 'who is like the beast' (tis homoios to therio) parodies YHWH's incomparability hymn. The Song of Moses immediately before the bowls is the most explicit programmatic anchor for reading the bowl sequence through the Exodus lens.

    B3 — Rivers and fountains to blood; lex talionis¶

    And the third angel poured out his vial upon the rivers and fountains of waters; and they became blood. [16:5] And I heard the angel of the waters say, Thou art righteous, O Lord, which art, and wast, and shalt be, because thou hast judged thus. [16:6] For they have shed the blood of saints and prophets, and thou hast given them blood to drink; for they are worthy. [16:7] And I heard another out of the altar say, Even so, Lord God Almighty, true and righteous [are] thy judgments.

    Context: Third bowl: all rivers and springs become blood (no fraction). Angel of waters declares lex talionis: they shed saints' blood, so receive blood to drink. Completes the total blood-of-waters coverage (B2 sea + B3 rivers + springs).

    Notes: egenonto haima (they became blood) — no fraction, total. B2 + B3 together cover all water bodies. Exodus P1 only affected the Nile; Revelation universalizes to all waters. Angel of waters interprets B3 as poetic justice — echoing YHWH's rationale in Exod 7:17 ('in this thou shalt know that I am the LORD').

    Hosea — lion, leopard, bear as divine judgment triad (pre-Daniel)¶

    Therefore I will be unto them as a lion: as a leopard by the way will I observe [them]: [13:8] I will meet them as a bear [that is] bereaved [of her whelps], and will rend the caul of their heart, and there will I devour them like a lion: the wild beast shall tear them.

    Context: YHWH uses lion, leopard, and bear as divine judgment metaphors against Israel — all three Daniel beast animals combined in a single passage, pre-dating Daniel.

    Notes: Hosea 13:7-8 contains lion (H738 ari), leopard (H5246 namer), and bear (H1677 dob) — all three animals of Rev 13:2's composite beast — in a single judgment passage. This confirms the three-animal combination was established OT judgment imagery before Daniel. Revelation's composite beast inherits both the Daniel sequential tradition and the Hosea simultaneous-judgment tradition.

    And in the midst of the seven candlesticks [one] like unto the Son of man, clothed with a garment down to the foot, and girt about the paps with a golden girdle. [1:14] His head and [his] hairs [were] white like wool, as white as snow; and his eyes [were] as a flame of fire;

    Context: Exalted Christ described with the Ancient of Days' white-wool hair (Dan 7:9) while appearing as Son of Man (Dan 7:13). Deliberate Christological merger: Christ is simultaneously both Daniel figures.

    Notes: erion (G2053, N-NSN) appears here — one of only 2 NT occurrences (Heb 9:19; Rev 1:14). Dan 7:9 Aramaic: amar neqa (pure wool). Rev 1:14 leukos hos erion (white like wool) = direct LXX Theodotion echo of Dan 7:9. The Great White Throne judgment (Rev 20:11) is performed by this same figure — the Christ who is both Son of Man and Ancient of Days.

    Context: Seventh trumpet proleptic kingdom announcement: egeneto (G1096, aorist = became) marks kingdom transfer as completed event. Fulfillment of Dan 7:14 (everlasting kingdom) and Dan 7:27 (kingdom given to saints).

    Notes: egeneto (aorist inceptive) = kingdom transfer happened. Dan 7:14 everlasting dominion + Dan 7:27 kingdom given to saints = fulfilled in Rev 11:15. The missing title 'ho erchomenos' (he who is coming) in Rev 11:17 signals fulfillment — God has come, so the coming-title no longer applies.

    Millennial thrones — judgment given to saints (Dan 7:22)¶

    Context: Thrones and judgment given to the saints — direct parallel to Dan 7:22 (judgment was given to the saints of the Most High). Those who refused the mark of the beast are specifically those who receive the judgment/reign.

    Dragon — seven heads, seven diadems (diadema arc)¶

    And there appeared another wonder in heaven; and behold a great red dragon, having seven heads and ten horns, and seven crowns upon his heads.

    Notes: Diadema arc: dragon (7 crowns on 7 heads) → beast (10 crowns on 10 horns) → Christ (Rev 19:12, many diadems). Moses' true signs validate YHWH; the false prophet's counterfeit signs validate the beast. Acts 7:36 confirms the NT treats the Exodus plagues as historically foundational signs.
