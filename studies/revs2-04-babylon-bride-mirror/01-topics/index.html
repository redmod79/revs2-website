    Topics Research¶

    Question¶

    How do the Babylon/harlot (Rev 17-18) and New Jerusalem/bride (Rev 21-22) sections form a deliberate structural mirror? Document every verbal parallel between Rev 17:1-3 and Rev 21:9-10 (the angel introduction formula). Trace the antithetical contrasts: harlot vs. bride, wilderness vs. mountain, jewels adorning each, what fills each (abominations vs. glory), the fate of each. How does this structural mirror connect to the en pneumati markers (17:3 and 21:10 are visions 3 and 4)?

    • GEN 11:1-9
    • DAN 4:30
    • 2KI 25:13
    • ISA 45:1-2
    • JER 51:44,58
    • ISA 14:4
    • 1PE 5:13
    • REV 14:8
    • REV 16:19
    • REV 17
    • REV 18

    BRIDE — FIGURATIVE¶

    • REV 21:2
    • REV 21:9
    • REV 22:17
    • ISA 62:5
    • JER 2:2
    • HOS 2:19-20

    Topic retrieved via naves_semantic.py. Bride used figuratively for the people of God and for the New Jerusalem in Revelation. Key references: Rev 21:2 (new Jerusalem as bride adorned), Rev 21:9 (bride, the Lamb's wife), Rev 22:17 (Spirit and bride say Come), Isa 62:5 (as bridegroom rejoices over bride), Jer 2:2 (Israel's bridal devotion), Hos 2:19-20 (betrothal).

    JERUSALEM — NEW JERUSALEM¶

    • REV 21:2
    • REV 21:10
    • REV 21:14-21
    • REV 3:12
    • GAL 4:26
    • HEB 11:10
    • HEB 12:22
    • HEB 13:14

    The heavenly city, the holy Jerusalem descending from God out of heaven. Rev 21:2 (holy city, new Jerusalem), Rev 21:10 (shown from great high mountain), Rev 21:14-21 (twelve foundations, gates of pearl, streets of gold), Rev 3:12 (name of the city written on overcomer), Gal 4:26 (Jerusalem which is above), Heb 11:10 (city which hath foundations), Heb 12:22 (mount Sion, the heavenly Jerusalem), Heb 13:14 (we seek one to come).

    HARLOT (PROSTITUTE) — GENERAL / FIGURATIVE¶

    • REV 17:1
    • REV 17:5
    • REV 17:15-16
    • REV 19:2
    • ISA 1:21
    • JER 3:1
    • EZK 16:15
    • EZK 23:4-5
    • HOS 2:5

    Harlot used figuratively for apostate religious communities. Rev 17:1 (judgment of the great whore), Rev 17:5 (MOTHER OF HARLOTS), Rev 17:15-16 (waters = peoples; horns hate whore), Rev 19:2 (judged the great whore). OT: Isa 1:21 (faithful city become harlot), Jer 3:1 (thou hast played the harlot), Ezek 16:15 (Jerusalem played the harlot), Ezek 23:4-5 (Oholah and Oholibah as sister harlots), Hos 2:5 (mother hath played the harlot).

    ABOMINATION — THINGS THAT ARE TO GOD¶

    • REV 17:4
    • REV 17:5
    • REV 21:27
    • PRO 6:16-19
    • DEU 7:25-26
    • LEV 18:22

    Things detestable to God. Rev 17:4 (cup full of abominations), Rev 17:5 (MOTHER OF ABOMINATIONS), Rev 21:27 (nothing that worketh abomination enters NJ). OT: Pro 6:16-19 (seven things are abomination), Deu 7:25-26 (abomination to the LORD thy God), Lev 18:22.

    GLORY — OF GOD / ETERNAL¶

    • REV 21:11
    • REV 21:23
    • REV 21:24
    • REV 21:26
    • ISA 60:1-3
    • ISA 60:19
    • EZK 43:2

    The glory of God filling the New Jerusalem. Rev 21:11 (having the glory of God), Rev 21:23 (glory of God did lighten it), Rev 21:24 (kings bring their glory), Rev 21:26 (bring glory and honour of nations). OT parallels: Isa 60:1-3 (glory of the LORD risen upon thee), Isa 60:19 (the LORD shall be thine everlasting light), Ezk 43:2 (the earth shined with his glory).

    TABERNACLE — GENERAL¶

    • REV 21:3
    • LEV 26:11-12
    • EZK 37:27
    • EXO 25:8
    • JOH 1:14

    The tabernacle of God with men. Rev 21:3 (Behold, the tabernacle of God is with men). OT sources: Lev 26:11-12 (I will set my tabernacle among you; I will walk among you), Ezk 37:27 (My tabernacle shall be with them), Exo 25:8 (let them make me a sanctuary that I may dwell among them). NT: John 1:14 (the Word was made flesh, and dwelt [eskēnōsen] among us).

    PRECIOUS STONES — BREASTPLATE / NJ FOUNDATIONS¶

    • REV 21:19-20
    • EXO 28:17-20
    • EZK 28:13
    • ISA 54:11-12

    Precious stones in the high priest's breastplate and New Jerusalem foundations. Rev 21:19-20 (12 foundation stones: jasper, sapphire, chalcedony, emerald, sardonyx, sardius, chrysolite, beryl, topaz, chrysoprasus, jacinth, amethyst). Exo 28:17-20 (breastplate: sardius, topaz, carbuncle; emerald, sapphire, diamond; ligure, agate, amethyst; beryl, onyx, jasper). Ezk 28:13 (9 stones of the covering cherub). Isa 54:11-12 (foundations with sapphires, windows of agates, gates of carbuncles).

    WILDERNESS — GENERAL¶

    • REV 17:3
    • DEU 32:10
    • MAT 4:1
    • EZK 20:35-36

    Wilderness as place of testing, judgment, and separation. Rev 17:3 (carried away into the wilderness to see the harlot). OT backgrounds: Deu 32:10 (found him in a desert land, in the howling wilderness), Ezk 20:35-36 (I will bring you into the wilderness of the people and plead with you face to face). NT: Mat 4:1 (led into the wilderness to be tempted).

    MOUNTAIN — GENERAL / SYMBOLIC¶

    • REV 21:10
    • EZK 40:2
    • EXO 19:18-20
    • ISA 2:2-3
    • PSA 48:1-2

    Mountain as place of divine revelation, covenant, and the city of God. Rev 21:10 (carried to a great and high mountain to see the holy Jerusalem). OT: Ezk 40:2 (set upon a very high mountain, saw frame of a city), Exo 19:18-20 (the LORD came down upon mount Sinai), Isa 2:2-3 (mountain of the LORD's house established in the top of the mountains), Psa 48:1-2 (mount Zion, the city of the great King).

    LIGHT — FIGURATIVE AND SYMBOLICAL¶

    • REV 21:23
    • REV 22:5
    • REV 18:23
    • ISA 60:19-20
    • JOH 8:12

    Light as symbol of divine presence. Rev 21:23 (glory of God did lighten it, the Lamb is the light thereof), Rev 22:5 (no night, the Lord God giveth them light). Contrasted with Babylon: Rev 18:23 (light of a candle shall shine no more). OT: Isa 60:19-20 (the LORD shall be thine everlasting light). NT: Joh 8:12 (I am the light of the world).

    LAMP — FIGURATIVE¶

    • REV 18:23
    • REV 21:23
    • REV 22:5
    • MAT 5:15
    • PSA 119:105

    Lamp/candle as symbol of life and divine illumination. Rev 18:23 (light of a candle shall shine no more at all in thee — extinguished in Babylon). Rev 21:23 (the Lamb is the light/lamp thereof). Rev 22:5 (they need no candle, neither light of the sun). Contrast between the extinguished lamp of Babylon and the eternal Lamb-lamp of New Jerusalem.

    FORNICATION — GENERAL / FIGURATIVE¶

    • REV 17:2
    • REV 17:4
    • REV 18:3
    • REV 18:9
    • REV 19:2
    • EZK 16:15-16
    • HOS 4:12

    Fornication as metaphor for spiritual unfaithfulness. Rev 17:2 (kings of the earth committed fornication), Rev 17:4 (filthiness of her fornication), Rev 18:3 (all nations drunk of the wine of her fornication), Rev 18:9 (kings committed fornication with her), Rev 19:2 (corrupted the earth with her fornication). OT background: Ezk 16:15-16 (playedst the harlot, pouredst out thy fornications), Hos 4:12 (spirit of whoredoms caused them to err).

    DEFILEMENT — LAWS RELATING TO¶

    • REV 21:27
    • REV 17:4
    • LEV 11:44
    • ISA 6:5

    Defilement and uncleanness excluded from God's presence. Rev 21:27 (there shall in no wise enter into it any thing that defileth). Rev 17:4 (the unclean things of her fornication). OT: Lev 11:44 (ye shall be holy; for I am holy), Isa 6:5 (I am a man of unclean lips).

    PROVERBS — TWO-WOMEN TOPOS¶

    • PRO 7:10-12
    • PRO 9:1-6
    • PRO 9:13-18

    The two-women topos from Proverbs. Pro 7:10-12 (strange woman with attire of a harlot), Pro 9:1-6 (Lady Wisdom builds her house, invites guests), Pro 9:13-18 (foolish woman sits at her door, her guests are in the depths of Sheol). Rossing traces the harlot/bride contrast in Revelation to this OT template.

    EZEKIEL — VISIONS OF / SPIRIT-TRANSPORT¶

    • EZK 8:3
    • EZK 37:1
    • EZK 40:2
    • EZK 16:8-14
    • EZK 16:15-16
    • EZK 23:4-5

    Ezekiel's Spirit-transport visions providing the template for Revelation's en Pneumati markers. Ezk 8:3 (the spirit lifted me up, brought me to Jerusalem in visions of God), Ezk 37:1 (carried me out in the spirit of the LORD, set me in the valley), Ezk 40:2 (in visions of God brought me to Israel, set me upon a very high mountain — the closest parallel to Rev 21:10). Also the city-woman imagery: Ezk 16:8-14 (Jerusalem as bride adorned by God), Ezk 16:15-16 (Jerusalem turned harlot), Ezk 23:4-5 (Oholah and Oholibah as sister harlots).
