    Question¶

    How do the Babylon/harlot (Rev 17-18) and New Jerusalem/bride (Rev 21-22) sections form a deliberate structural mirror? Document every verbal parallel between Rev 17:1-3 and Rev 21:9-10 (the angel introduction formula). Trace the antithetical contrasts: harlot vs. bride, wilderness vs. mountain, jewels adorning each, what fills each (abominations vs. glory), the fate of each. How does this structural mirror connect to the en pneumati markers (17:3 and 21:10 are visions 3 and 4)?

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Eight prior studies contain directly relevant findings. revs-06 already cataloged 22 structural elements (11 VPs, 4 TMs, 3 SPs, 2 ANs, 2 ICs) for this mirror. revs2-01 documented the apophero + en Pneumati verb pair unique to 17:3 and 21:10 (TM005) and the four-vision division. revs2-02 verified Strand's chiastic pairs and the prologue-epilogue inclusio. 3am-10 established the harlot's priestly-garment parody and zanah/porneia covenant vocabulary. 3am-12 documented 12 verbal parallels between Jer 50-51 and Rev 17-18. Web research identified Bauckham's parallel-sections model, Paulien's detailed verbal-parallel table, Rossing's Proverbs two-women topos, Zerbe's economic-contrast analysis, and Campbell's claim that the antithetical parallelism is engineered from chapter 14 onward.

    Primary Verses (33 — full analysis required)¶

    Angel introduction formulas: The near-verbatim parallel openings of the two city-woman visions - Rev 17:1-3 — Angel introduction formula (vision 3) — harlot shown in wilderness via apophero + en Pneumati - Rev 21:9-10 — Angel introduction formula (vision 4) — bride shown on mountain via apophero + en Pneumati

    Harlot description and identification: The harlot's appearance, contents, and angelic explanation - Rev 17:4 — Harlot's external adornment: purple, scarlet, gold, precious stones, pearls; golden cup full of abominations - Rev 17:5 — Forehead inscription: MYSTERY BABYLON THE GREAT MOTHER OF HARLOTS — contrast with 22:4 God's name on foreheads - Rev 17:6 — Harlot drunk with blood of saints — antithesis to NJ's river of life - Rev 17:15 — Angel interprets the waters: peoples, multitudes, nations, tongues — contrasts NJ's divine river - Rev 17:16-17 — Fate of harlot: burned with fire, made desolate by the ten horns - Rev 17:18 — Woman identified as the great city — city-woman identification principle shared with bride

    Babylon's fall and judgment: The destruction sequence and sevenfold removal - Rev 18:2 — Babylon becomes habitation of devils (katoiketerion daimonion) — antithesis to NJ as tabernacle of God (21:3) - Rev 18:4 — Come out of her my people — call to separate; antithesis to 22:17 Come invitation to enter - Rev 18:7-8 — Babylon self-glorifies as queen; plagues come in one day — fire judgment - Rev 18:11-13 — Cargo list ending with bodies and souls of men — Ezekiel 27 allusion; antithesis to NJ's free life-giving resources - Rev 18:21-23 — Sevenfold 'no more' of life-elements removed from Babylon; lamp extinguished; voice of bridegroom silenced - Rev 18:24 — Blood of prophets and saints found in her — blood-guilt climax

    Structural brackets: The hallelujahs, marriage supper, and angel-worship refusals framing both sections - Rev 19:1-6 — Four hallelujahs responding to Babylon's fall — structural conclusion of Babylon section - Rev 19:7-9 — Marriage of the Lamb announced; bride made herself ready; fine linen = righteousness of saints — antithesis to harlot's garments - Rev 19:10 — Angel-worship refusal (first occurrence) — one end of VP022 bracket - Rev 22:6-9 — Angel-worship refusal (second occurrence) — other end of VP022 bracket; parallel conclusion to 19:10 - Rev 22:17 — Spirit and bride say Come — bride's invitation; antithesis to 18:4 Come out of Babylon

    NJ description and identification: The bride-city's appearance, composition, and divine presence - Rev 21:2 — New Jerusalem descending as bride adorned for husband — first identification of city-as-bride - Rev 21:3-4 — Tabernacle of God with men; no more tears/death/sorrow — antithesis to Babylon's habitation of devils and suffering - Rev 21:11 — NJ has the glory of God; light like jasper, clear as crystal — antithesis to harlot's counterfeit adornment - Rev 21:18-21 — NJ composed of precious stones: wall of jasper, city of pure gold, 12 foundation stones, pearl gates — antithesis to harlot's external decoration - Rev 21:22-23 — No temple; God and Lamb are the temple and light — antithesis to Babylon's lamplight extinguished - Rev 21:24-26 — Kings of earth bring glory into NJ; nations walk in its light — antithesis to kings committing fornication with harlot - Rev 21:27 — Nothing unclean (koinon), no abomination (bdelygma), no lie enters — explicit exclusion vocabulary matching harlot's cup contents

    NJ interior and eternal life: The river, tree of life, and eternal state - Rev 22:1-2 — River of water of life; tree of life with healing leaves — antithesis to Babylon's blood and poisoned waters - Rev 22:3-5 — No more curse; God's name on foreheads; no night; Lamb is lamp; they reign forever — comprehensive antithesis to harlot's fate

    OT city-woman and Spirit-transport sources: Ezekiel and Isaiah passages providing the OT template - Ezek 40:2 — OT source for Rev 21:10 mountain transport: Spirit brought me to very high mountain, showed me city - Ezek 16:8-14 — Jerusalem as bride adorned by God — clothed with fine linen, silk, jewels, crown; became exceedingly beautiful

    OT Babylon and harlot sources: Jeremiah and Ezekiel passages behind the harlot/Babylon imagery - Jer 51:7 — Babylon a golden cup in the LORD's hand making nations drunk — direct OT source for Rev 17:4 golden cup - Exod 28:5-6 — High priest's garments: gold, blue, purple, scarlet, fine linen — harlot's garments match except blue (3am-10 finding) - Exod 28:17-20 — High priest's breastplate stones: 12 gems — comparison with NJ's 12 foundation stones
