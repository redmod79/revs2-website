    Question¶

    How do the seven heads/mountains/kings of the beast (Rev 17:9-11) function as a structural and symbolic element? What does the "five are fallen, and one is, and the other is not yet come" formula (17:10) reveal about the beast's temporal structure? How do the seven beatitudes (makarios — Rev 1:3, 14:13, 16:15, 19:9, 20:6, 22:7, 22:14) create a structural spine distributed through the entire book? Do the beatitudes form a chiastic or progressive pattern? Document each beatitude's position in the book's narrative and its thematic content.

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Five prior studies have directly relevant findings. revs-26 catalogs the core structural data for seven heads (TM111-TM120, VP122-VP128, SP064-SP067, AN059-AN063, IC051-IC054), including temporal formula analysis, three-entity continuity, and sphazo/hos parody construction. revs-27 tested and rejected chiastic arrangement of beatitudes, confirmed the 1-6 inclusio, documented internal pairing network (2-4 Grapson, 3-6 Idou erchomai), and classified seam-placement pattern. revs2-05 documented basileus (G935) vocabulary thread across Revelation (23 occurrences) including the kings-and-priests arc. rev-17-great-harlot-beast found Daniel composite head arithmetic (1+1+4+1=7) and seven-forms-of-Roman-government interpretation. revs-41 documented reversed beast order and stoma laloun megala quotation. Web research identified three major interpretive schools for seven heads (preterist/emperor, historicist/empire, idealist/symbolic), Bauckham's graded numerical saying concept, Gentry's ek ton hepta genitive-of-relationship analysis, and the six-plus-one chiastic model for beatitudes with sabbath capstone.

    Primary Verses (30 — full analysis required)¶

    Seven heads primary passages: All three entities bearing seven heads, plus the interpretive angel speech - Rev 12:3 — dragon with seven heads and ten horns — first appearance of seven-headed entity - Rev 13:1-2 — sea beast with seven heads — composite Daniel beast with reversed order (leopard-bear-lion) - Rev 13:3 — one head 'as it were wounded to death' — sphazo/hos construction paralleling Lamb in 5:6 - Rev 17:3 — scarlet beast with seven heads and ten horns — primary location of the seven-headed beast

    Seven beatitudes: All seven makarios occurrences in Revelation - Rev 1:3 — beatitude 1 — 'Blessed is he that readeth, and they that hear the words of this prophecy, and keep those things' - Rev 14:13 — beatitude 2 — 'Blessed are the dead which die in the Lord from henceforth' with Grapson imperative - Rev 16:15 — beatitude 3 — 'Blessed is he that watcheth, and keepeth his garments' parenthetical within bowl sequence - Rev 19:9 — beatitude 4 — 'Blessed are they which are called unto the marriage supper of the Lamb' with Grapson imperative - Rev 20:6 — beatitude 5 — 'Blessed and holy is he that hath part in the first resurrection' convergence node with kings-and-priests - Rev 22:7 — beatitude 6 — 'Blessed is he that keepeth the sayings of the prophecy of this book' inclusio pair with 1:3 - Rev 22:14 — beatitude 7 — 'Blessed are they that do his commandments / wash their robes' potential sabbath capstone

    Divine existence formula: All occurrences of the 'is, was, is to come' formula and its beast parody - Rev 1:4 — seven spirits — heptadic structure opening the book, context for beatitude 1 - Rev 1:8 — 'which is, and which was, and which is to come' — divine existence formula that 17:8 inverts - Rev 4:8 — divine existence formula repeated — 'was, is, is to come'

    OT mountain = kingdom passages: Testing whether mountain consistently symbolizes kingdom/empire in OT prophecy - Dan 2:34-35 — stone strikes image, becomes great mountain — mountain = kingdom symbolism in Daniel - Dan 2:44-45 — God sets up kingdom (the mountain that fills the earth) — mountain = kingdom confirmed - Jer 51:25 — 'O destroying mountain' addressed to Babylon — OT mountain = kingdom/empire symbolism

    King = kingdom equivalence in Daniel: Daniel's interchangeable use of kings and kingdoms - Dan 7:17 — four kings = four kingdoms — king/kingdom equivalence governing Rev 17 interpretation - Dan 7:23 — fourth beast = fourth kingdom — confirms king/kingdom interchangeability in Daniel

    Graded numerical saying parallels: OT X/X+1 pattern testing Bauckham's claim - Prov 30:15-16 — graded numerical saying 'three...four' — Hebrew literary pattern for X/X+1 (Bauckham parallel) - Prov 30:18-19 — graded numerical saying 'three...four things' — further X/X+1 literary pattern example - Amos 1:3 — 'for three transgressions...and for four' — prophetic use of graded numerical saying - Mic 5:5 — 'seven shepherds and eight principal men' — OT seven/eight pattern parallel to Rev 17:11

    Multi-headed beast OT background: Leviathan and chaos-monster imagery - Isa 27:1 — Leviathan the piercing/crooked serpent — dragon/sea-beast OT background

    Number eight / new creation: Eight as covenant/new-beginning marker - Mic 5:5 — 'seven shepherds and eight principal men' — OT seven/eight pattern parallel to Rev 17:11

    Additional Primary Verses: - Rev 17:7-8 — angel's interpretation begins — beast 'was, is not, yet is' temporal formula inverting divine existence formula - Rev 17:9-11 — core text — seven heads = seven mountains = seven kings, temporal formula 'five are fallen, one is, one not yet come', eighth king ek ton hepta - Ps 74:14 — Leviathan's heads broken — ancient multi-headed beast archetype in OT - Rev 5:6 — Lamb 'as it had been slain' — sphazo/hos construction that Rev 13:3 parodies - Rev 17:12-14 — ten horns = ten kings, contemporaneous (contrasting the seven consecutive heads) - Rev 20:4 — first resurrection context — immediate environment of beatitude 5, kings-and-priests fulfillment
